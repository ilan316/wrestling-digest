🔥 AEW Dynamite May 27 Double or Nothing Follow-up

AEW Dynamite on May 27 aired live from the Liacouras Center in Philadelphia as a special three-hour combined episode with AEW Collision, serving as the direct fallout from AEW Double or Nothing. The event provided immediate follow-ups to several major storylines while also introducing new feuds and returning wrestlers to active competition.

Prior to the live broadcast, AEW taped dark matches for the live crowd featuring several wrestlers. Steven Borden defeated Kiran Grey, Action Andretti picked up a victory over Joey Kets, and Daniel Garcia teamed with Wheeler Yuta to defeat LSG and Matt Mako in tag team action.

Chris Jericho finally ended his long-running feud with Ricochet by defeating him in a hard-fought singles match with all other wrestlers banned from ringside. The match lasted over 14 minutes and saw both men trade multiple near-falls and signature moves. Ricochet came out aggressively with quick attacks and aerial offense early on, even targeting Jericho's leg by driving him into the announce desk and steel steps. Jericho fought back and locked Ricochet in the Walls of Jericho on top of the announce table. In the closing moments, Ricochet attempted to cheat with a low blow while the referee was distracted before hitting the Ricosault, but Jericho kicked out. Ricochet then went for a 630 senton, but Jericho evaded it and hit a Codebreaker. After Ricochet still kicked out, Jericho connected with the Judas Effect followed by a Lionsault to secure the pinfall victory. This marked Jericho's first win over Ricochet after weeks of rivalry between the two competitors.

However, Jericho's celebration was immediately cut short when Tommaso Ciampa blindsided him with a surprise attack. Ciampa beat Jericho down, choked him with his own shirt, and delivered his signature Knee Trembler while standing over Jericho's body mockingly. The attack established Ciampa as Jericho's next major rival and set up what could potentially become a bigger faction feud involving Jericho, Adam Copeland, and Christian Cage against Ciampa and other competitors.

Adam Copeland and Christian Cage, fresh off their brutal Double or Nothing victory over FTR for the AEW World Tag Team Championships, came to the ring to celebrate with their newly won titles. The champions began joking around about bringing back their classic five-second pose from 25 years ago and even handed out disposable cameras to fans so they could capture the moment. Their celebration was completely derailed when David Finlay and Clark Connors rushed the ring out of nowhere and began beating down both champions with intensity. The attack quickly became vicious, with Finlay and Connors at one point trapping Christian's hand inside the steel steps and smashing it before continuing their assault. By the end of the attack, both Copeland and Christian were completely laid out as Finlay and Connors stood tall in the ring. This angle immediately set up Finlay and Connors as the next title challengers for Copeland and Cage's tag team championships, wasting no time in establishing new challengers after the Cage's war with FTR.

Jake Doyle made his long-awaited return to AEW television after four months away from a torn biceps injury suffered in January. Doyle appeared during the Superstation Showcase four-way match featuring RUSH, Brian Cage, Orange Cassidy, and Lio Rush. The match began to break down when members of the Don Callis Family, Rocky Romero and Lance Archer, appeared at ringside to target Orange Cassidy. While Cassidy was distracted dealing with Romero and Archer, Doyle blindsided him from behind in his surprise return. Roderick Strong, Cassidy's Conglomeration ally, immediately ran out to confront Doyle and the two began brawling before fighting their way to the backstage area, with Lance Archer subsequently carrying Cassidy to the back. With the field cleared, RUSH capitalized and won the match by pinning Lio Rush. Doyle's return immediately placed him back into the ongoing issues involving Orange Cassidy and The Conglomeration while also bringing the Don Callis Family closer to full strength as a faction.

QT Marshall had experienced a scare during the AEW Double or Nothing Zero Hour pre-show when he fell hard to the floor after losing his balance on the top rope when the ropes appeared to be slippery from Vita Coco Water that had been splashed around during his 10-man tag team match. The landing looked painful and Marshall was seen grabbing his knee afterward, struggling to put weight on the leg as officials and medical staff helped him backstage. However, the latest update sounded much more positive. Marshall addressed the situation himself on social media, noting that he expected to be okay following the scare. According to backstage sources within AEW, Marshall was reportedly doing fine after the show and was moving around without any major issues. There is currently no expectation that he will miss any in-ring time or upcoming bookings as a result of the incident. Marshall even joked about his next feud being with Vita Coco on social media.

Kevin Knight surprised fans by turning heel and attacking Darby Allin at the closing moments of AEW Double or Nothing, and during a recent interview, the TNT Champion spoke about his early defining moment in AEW. Knight cited his pay-per-view debut against Will Ospreay as his welcoming moment in AEW, noting that facing one of the top wrestlers in the world just weeks into his AEW tenure was a baptism by fire. Knight also praised Speedball Mike Bailey, calling him to pro wrestling what Albert Einstein was to science, highlighting Bailey's encyclopedic knowledge of wrestling and his ability to put wrestlers in the best position to get the best reactions from fans. Knight explained his philosophy that pro wrestling is a team sport where if you make others look good, success comes back to you tenfold, noting his own success including becoming a two-time Trios Champion and current TNT Champion within the year.

Wheeler Yuta appeared on local Philadelphia media to promote the Dynamite and Collision show and discussed his evolution from the Blackpool Combat Club to the Death Riders faction. Yuta reflected on his childhood dream of becoming a professional wrestler, which was inspired by his cousins introducing him to wrestling when he was very young. He discussed joining AEW with support from the Best Friends group before transitioning to the Blackpool Combat Club with William Regal, Bryan Danielson, and Jon Moxley, which eventually formed into the Death Riders. Yuta also touched on his competitive relationship with Daniel Garcia, noting that they always felt like modern contemporaries on the independents and even wrestled to an hour-long draw for the IWTV Championship. Though they ended up on rival teams initially with Garcia in the Jericho Appreciation Society and Yuta in Blackpool Combat Club, their rivalry has made both wrestlers better, and now that they're on the same side in the Death Riders, Yuta suggested they may develop an even closer friendship while continuing to motivate each other competitively. Yuta also mentioned playing steel drums for weddings before becoming a wrestler and briefly discussed his cat Petunia.

MJF's first AEW World Title defense was announced for the following week as part of the loaded Dynamite and Collision special that closed out the Double or Nothing fallout programming. The three-hour event overall provided significant follow-up to the pay-per-view while establishing multiple new directions for various wrestlers and factions heading forward.

🔥 AEW Samoa Joe Hiatus

Samoa Joe has announced an extended hiatus from AEW programming, stepping away for a few months to pursue outside opportunities in Hollywood. The announcement came during a backstage segment on the May 27 episode of AEW Collision, where Joe addressed his faction, The Opps, which also includes Katsuyori Shibata, HOOK, and Anthony Bowens.

During the segment, Joe reflected on the week The Opps had experienced, acknowledging that it had not been the best, but emphasizing that their core mission would remain unchanged. He took the opportunity to reinforce the foundational principles of the organization he created. "When I founded the ops, this was an organization of opposition. But more importantly, opportunity," Joe explained. "We stand in opposition of any who stand in our way. And we provide opportunities for men who deserve them."

Joe then addressed individual members of the group. He praised Hook for recently finishing work on a film project and expressed confidence in Anthony Bowens' future, noting that Bowens would soon receive the standing ovation he deserves. However, the biggest announcement came when Joe revealed his own immediate plans. "But once again, opportunity calls and Hollywood's ringing. Gentlemen, I'll be stepping away for a few months, but you all know how this works. I expect you to hold it down."

With Joe's departure, leadership of The Opps immediately fell to Hook, who repositioned himself between Shibata and Bowens to reestablish his role as the group's leader during his mentor's absence. This mirrors the situation that occurred during Joe's previous hiatus when he suffered a concussion in January, during which Hook also took charge of the faction and even brought Anthony Bowens into the group.

While Joe did not officially confirm his specific Hollywood project, all available reporting strongly indicates that he will be filming the third season of Peacock's Twisted Metal series. Joe portrays the physical performance for the masked character Sweet Tooth, with Will Arnett providing voice work for the character. The series has already been renewed for a third season and is expected to premiere in 2027. Twisted Metal has become one of Peacock's biggest hits in terms of viewership minutes, making it a significant commitment for Joe's time away from wrestling.

This is Joe's second hiatus of 2026, having recently returned from a concussion suffered in January. Since returning to action, Joe has wrestled only five times this year, with his most recent match coming at Double or Nothing when he faced Will Ospreay in an Owen Hart tournament bout, which he lost. Despite his limited in-ring activity in 2026, his role as the leader and patriarch of The Opps has kept him prominently featured in AEW storylines.

Joe's departure leaves questions about how The Opps will continue to operate and whether Hook, Shibata, and Bowens can maintain the group's relevance and momentum during his absence. However, Joe made it clear before leaving that he has confidence in his team to maintain order and continue the organization's mission of standing in opposition to those who threaten them while creating opportunities for deserving talent.

🔥 Kyle Fletcher AEW Return

Kyle Fletcher Made His Shocking Return At AEW Double Or Nothing

AEW star Kyle Fletcher made a surprise return at Double or Nothing, appearing in the aftermath of Kazuchika Okada's International Championship match against Konosuke Takeshita. At first, it appeared Fletcher was coming out to support his Don Callis Family stablemate Takeshita. However, he shocked the audience by turning on him, setting up one of the major storyline developments heading into the following week's programming.

Fletcher Had Been Sidelined Since March With Multiple Serious Injuries

Fletcher had been out of action for weeks dealing with several serious injuries. He initially suffered the injuries on March 28 during an AEW Collision episode when he was teaming with Mark Davis against The Rascalz. Despite the injury, Fletcher and Davis managed to win the match. At first, it was believed Fletcher had only torn his meniscus, but it was later revealed the injury was significantly worse than initially thought.

The extent of Fletcher's injuries included a broken leg in two places and a torn meniscus. However, what made his recovery remarkable was that he avoided surgery entirely. Initially, there was a 90 percent chance he would require surgery, but after tests revealed the injuries didn't necessitate the procedure, Fletcher was able to pursue a non-surgical recovery path instead.

Fletcher's Recovery Process Involved Rest, Physical Therapy, And Conditioning

Fletcher revealed details about his recovery process, explaining that he had to rest for a couple of weeks before beginning physical therapy along with gym and cardio work. This intensive rehabilitation allowed him to be technically cleared on May 15, just days before his in-ring return at Double or Nothing.

Fletcher Officially Announced His Medical Clearance On May 27

Fletcher officially confirmed his medical clearance during the May 27 episode of AEW Dynamite and Collision in Philadelphia. The announcement came during a segment following Mark Davis defeating Jack Perry in the Owen Hart Foundation Tournament quarterfinals. Don Callis grabbed the microphone and declared that Konosuke Takeshita was never truly "the chosen one" in the Don Callis Family, instead claiming it was always Fletcher.

Fletcher then took over the microphone and addressed his incredible recovery. He called himself the "Picture Perfect Performer" and a "genetic anomaly" while announcing his status. Fletcher stated: "You broke your leg in two places, and you tore your meniscus, and you're back in under two months. How? And the answer is I am a genetic anomaly. I am the picture-perfect performer. And I am happy to announce that as of today, I am 100% clear for in-ring competition."

Fletcher Explained His Betrayal Of Takeshita

Fletcher elaborated on his shocking betrayal of Takeshita, explaining that Takeshita had become "dead weight" in The Don Callis Family. He accused Takeshita of causing problems within the family while Fletcher was injured. Fletcher revealed that fixing his relationship with Takeshita was impossible and stated he wanted to reach the top alongside him but had zero regrets about turning on him. He declared that his motivation to return was to "be the man to cut loose the dead weight of the Don Callis Family."

Takeshita Immediately Confronted Fletcher

Fletcher's clearance announcement immediately brought Takeshita out to confront him. Takeshita was flanked by members of The Conglomeration and Mark Briscoe, showing that the issue between the former allies was far from over. The segment made it clear that Takeshita wanted Fletcher, and with Fletcher now healthy enough to compete again, AEW has set up a major feud between two of the company's hardest-hitting stars.

Fletcher Also Had An Earlier Confrontation With TNT Champion Kevin Knight

Fletcher's second confrontation of the night came earlier in the episode when he eyed up current TNT Champion Kevin Knight before Don Callis made a pitch to Knight to join the Don Callis Family. This added another layer to the Don Callis Family's ongoing expansion and storyline development.

Jake Doyle Also Returned After Four-Month Absence

In another return storyline, Jake Doyle officially resurfaced on May 27 after being sidelined since January with a torn bicep. Doyle made his comeback during a chaotic Superstation Showcase four-way match featuring Brian Cage, Lio Rush, Orange Cassidy, and Lio Rush. The match had already spiraled into chaos after Rocky Romero and Lance Archer appeared through the crowd to target Orange Cassidy. As Archer blocked an Orange Punch attempt, Doyle suddenly re-emerged and attacked Cassidy from behind.

Doyle's surprise appearance shifted the momentum completely, with the Don Callis Family members overwhelming Cassidy and Roderick Strong before dragging them away from ringside. 🔥 WCW 30 Year Anniversary

On This Day in Pro Wrestling History: 30 Years Ago Scott Hall Shocked the World and Changed Wrestling Forever

On May 27, 1996, Scott Hall made his shocking return to World Championship Wrestling on WCW Monday Nitro, launching the New World Order storyline that changed the trajectory of professional wrestling forever. Hall's appearance marked a pivotal moment that would influence the entire industry for decades to come.

Before this historic date, WCW was doing adequately but struggling creatively. The company was largely rehashing WWE's old storylines with acquired stars like Hulk Hogan and "Macho Man" Randy Savage. Hogan in particular was being booed by WCW fans because it was the same red and yellow babyface act audiences had already seen for years in WWE. Other major problems plagued the company, including poorly received feuds and the disastrous Dungeon of Doom faction, which was largely viewed as a joke despite introducing the young star "The Giant" (Paul Wight, later known as Big Show). The Uncensored 1996 Pay Per View exemplified WCW's creative struggles with its terrible "Doomsday Cage" main event.

While WCW Monday Nitro itself was a great concept as a platform to compete with WWE's RAW, the storylines and older wrestlers being pushed simply weren't working. WCW executive producer Eric Bischoff recognized the problem and wanted to incorporate "reality-based" storylines that would feel authentic to fans. His inspiration came from observing New Japan Pro Wrestling, which was drawing massive crowds by creating storylines that felt very real and making audiences feel part of the action. New Japan's hostile takeover angle particularly influenced Bischoff's thinking.

The catalyst for change came when two major WWE wrestlers became free agents. Scott Hall, who had played Razor Ramon in WWE, felt his pay had plateaued and wanted more guaranteed money plus higher percentages of merchandise sales. When Vince McMahon refused his requests, Hall began discussing a return to WCW through his longtime friend Diamond Dallas Page, who served as an intermediary. Through DDP, Hall received a verbal offer from Bischoff that provided a substantial guaranteed amount while requiring him to work far fewer dates than WWE's grueling schedule demanded.

Kevin Nash, who played Diesel in WWE, initially had reservations about leaving the company despite fond memories being overshadowed by bad gimmicks and storylines from his earlier WCW tenure as "Oz" and "Vinnie Vegas." However, the breaking point came when Bret Hart was petty over a match finish before WrestleMania 12. Nash immediately signed with WCW after hearing Hall's generous offer, as his contract was set to expire one week after Hall's deal.

On their way out of WWE, Hall and Nash wrestled their last matches at Madison Square Garden. They, along with clique buddies Shawn Michaels and Triple H, performed the infamous "Curtain Call" salute, where they broke character and acknowledged the crowd. This incident had major ramifications, resulting in Triple H being depushed and creating the opportunity for Steve Austin's rise.

After the MSG event, Hall was free to begin working with WCW. He laughed about the irony of going from working at Madison Square Garden one night to working in Macon, Georgia the next, though both events would create long-lasting impacts on the business forever.

On May 27, 1996, Scott Hall made his unexpected debut on WCW Monday Nitro, which was broadcasting its first-ever two-hour episode. This timing was significant as the Monday Night Wars between WCW and WWE were intensifying, and WCW was searching for bigger stars to help compete. Hall's arrival turned out to be one of the smartest moves in wrestling history.

Hall interrupted a match between Steve Doll and The Mauler and delivered the famous line: "You people know who me, but you don't know why I'm here." What made the segment so shocking was how real it felt, blurring the line between storyline and reality in a way never truly seen before in professional wrestling.

The segment worked brilliantly because it felt believable and authentic. Hall didn't come out through the entrance ramp as a typical wrestler would. Instead, he walked through the crowd, speaking like he was genuinely invading the company for real and hinting at WWE without actually naming it. He called out Ted Turner by addressing him as "Billionaire Ted" and Randy Savage as "The Nacho Man," adding to the invasion angle's credibility.

Before Hall's debut, WWE and WCW had already been taking shots at each other for years. WWE mocked stars who left for WCW in their programming, while WCW often spoiled WWE RAW results during Nitro broadcasts. However, Hall's debut took the rivalry to an entirely different level. Fans genuinely wondered if this was part of the scripted show or an actual takeover happening before their eyes.

A few weeks later, Kevin Nash arrived in WCW and joined Hall as "The Outsiders." Together, they claimed they were invading WCW on behalf of a mystery company and targeting the company's top stars. The intrigue and mystery of the angle captivated wrestling audiences everywhere.

The storyline changed even more dramatically at Bash at the Beach 1996 when Hulk Hogan shocked the world by turning heel and joining Hall and Nash. The three men formed the New World Order, or nWo. This heel turn was revolutionary, transforming Hogan from the red and yellow babyface into a dangerous villain alongside two invading forces.

The nWo storyline helped turn WCW into the biggest wrestling company in America during a significant period. WCW even beat WWE in television ratings for 83 straight consecutive weeks, demonstrating the enormous popularity of the angle. The nWo storyline also changed wrestling storytelling forever, moving the industry toward more realistic, edgy stories that would later define the Attitude Era and influence how wrestling was presented for years to come.

Ironically, while the nWo helped WCW become hugely successful and the most dominant wrestling promotion, the company later struggled because it became over-reliant on the faction. Management continued pushing the angle long after its novelty had worn off, and this dependency eventually became one of the primary reasons WCW collapsed in 2001.
