🔥 CM Punk WWE Raw Promos and Ticket Prices

CM Punk opened the April 6, 2026 episode of WWE RAW from the Toyota Center in Houston, Texas with a lengthy, aggressive promo that immediately drew comparisons to his legendary 2011 pipe bomb segment. Much like that historic moment, Punk was provided with a general outline but ultimately veered off-script to deliver a far more personal and emotionally charged message that extended well beyond his upcoming WrestleMania 42 main event against World Heavyweight Champion Roman Reigns.

The segment featured Punk attacking multiple targets. He called Roman Reigns a "buck-toothed nepo baby" who ate dog food for Vince McMahon, whom Punk described as a "weird old man." He also took aim at The Rock, calling him a "former" Hollywood star and describing him as "bloated." Pat McAfee was dubbed "MAGAfee," drawing criticism for his insertion into the Cody Rhodes versus Randy Orton storyline. Throughout the promo, Punk made clear references to TKO Group Holdings and TKO CEO Ari Emanuel, criticizing them for their involvement in creative decisions.

One of the most significant parts of Punk's promo addressed WrestleMania ticket pricing directly. Punk said, "You want to talk about ticket sales? Do me a favor, call up that agent that was foolish enough to shoehorn you into this business and tell him to lower the ticket prices. Houston, lower the ticket prices because I want ALL these families to come at WrestleMania and watch me stand on Roman Reigns' throat." He also referenced wrestling history, mentioning Harley Race, which led to Houston fans performing "What?" chants that drew criticism from Bronson Reed on social media, who felt the legendary wrestler deserved more respect.

According to reports, backstage reaction has been positive overall, with no confirmed fallout from the promo itself. However, the most striking aspect of the segment is that much of what Punk said mirrors genuine frustrations within the WWE locker room. Several talents have expressed concerns about WrestleMania ticket prices being set too high while many seats remain unsold. Additionally, performers are reportedly only being allotted two complimentary tickets for their respective nights, requiring them to purchase additional tickets out of pocket. Punk's words appear to have echoed these real grievances, with sources noting that the promo struck a nerve because it reflected actual locker room sentiment.

The YouTube upload of Punk's promo was delayed compared to other segments, with those familiar with the situation believing the hold-up may have been due to internal clearance concerns given the controversial nature of his comments. Interestingly, once the video was ultimately released on YouTube, it did not appear to contain significant edits, preserving the full promo including the ticket price commentary.

However, WWE's official social media strategy told a different story. When WWE posted the promo clip to their official X/Twitter account, they edited out the section where Punk explicitly called for lower ticket prices. The omission did not go unnoticed. Fans quickly flagged the clip with a Community Note pointing out the edit and providing context: "The official WWE clip is edited and omits the end of CM Punk's promo where he calls on TKO (WWE's parent company) to lower ticket prices, saying he should tell his agent Ari Emanuel to do so." This Community Note went viral, drawing significantly more attention to the missing segment and raising questions about WWE's filtering of critical corporate commentary.

The editing decision sparked considerable debate within the wrestling media. Many observers noted that WWE's attempt to sanitize the official clip actually backfired, as the Community Note ensured even more visibility for the ticket price criticism than would have occurred if the full clip had been posted originally. Tommy Dreamer, appearing on Busted Open, called the promo one of the best "blur the lines" moments in wrestling history, praising Punk for delivering something that genuinely blurred the line between storyline and reality while adding authentic intrigue to his WrestleMania match with Reigns.

Behind the scenes, sources indicated that WWE and TKO are well aware of ongoing social media discussions about ticket pricing, particularly as WrestleMania approaches. Reports suggest that WWE may be seriously considering lowering ticket prices for events through the summer. Bryan Alvarez noted that "if it wasn't obvious from Punk's promo, it appears WWE is looking to lower ticket prices through the summer." While WWE has already offered several discounts for WrestleMania, ticket sales are reportedly trailing behind last year's pace, making the pricing issue a genuine business concern rather than purely a creative one.

Several top superstars have reportedly been advocating internally for lower ticket prices, believing that die-hard fans are being priced out of the opportunity to attend shows in person. This grassroots talent sentiment, combined with the public criticism delivered by Punk on live television, appears to have created pressure on WWE management to address the pricing structure.

Mixed reactions emerged to the promo itself. While figures like Tommy Dreamer praised it as great wrestling storytelling that successfully blurred kayfabe and reality, others were more critical. One reviewer noted that while the promo had edge and aimed for the impact of Punk's 2011 pipe bomb, it lacked clear direction and became difficult to follow, with shots fired at numerous targets making it unclear what the overall message was supposed to be. Still, the promo achieved significant buzz and became one of the most discussed segments from the show.

The broader context surrounding the promo adds layers of complexity. Beyond the Roman Reigns feud, Punk's comments about Pat McAfee's involvement in wrestling stories reflected genuine creative disagreements within the company. Some talent feel McAfee's insertion into the Cody Rhodes-Randy Orton program is unnecessary, arguing that the historical depth between Rhodes and Orton is compelling enough without additional elements. McAfee's recent unscripted comments on SmackDown only added fuel to this internal fire. While Rhodes reportedly did not push back against the creative direction, there is a sense among his peers that he is being asked to "carry additional weight" heading into WrestleMania, and several talents aligned with Punk's assessment that McAfee's involvement felt like an unnecessary complication.

As for the match itself, CM Punk will defend his World Heavyweight Championship against Roman Reigns in what has been confirmed as the main event of the second and final night of WrestleMania 42 on April 19, 2026, at Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas. The explosive promo and subsequent discussion about ticket prices, corporate decision-making, and booking choices have only intensified anticipation for this championship match, with observers noting that the segment succeeded in creating genuine "big fight feel" heading into the spectacle.

The incident ultimately highlighted the power of worked-shoot promos in modern professional wrestling, where Punk's willingness to address real concerns from both his character's perspective and from actual locker room frustration created a moment that transcended typical storyline development. Whether intentional on WWE's part or stemming from Punk's departure from the planned script, the promo achieved what Punk's original pipe bomb accomplished years earlier: it made audiences genuinely uncertain about where the line between entertainment and reality actually fell.

🔥 Liv Morgan WWE Raw Injury and Feud with Stephanie Vaquer

During the April 6 episode of WWE Raw, an intense backstage segment involving Liv Morgan and Roxanne Perez took a dangerous turn when Women's World Champion Stephanie Vaquer launched a surprise attack. Morgan and Perez had been discussing Perez's recent return from surgery and her appreciation for Finn Balor bringing her into The Judgment Day faction when Vaquer stormed in from behind and blindsided Morgan.

The attack escalated quickly. Vaquer shoved Morgan directly into Perez, causing the two women to collide heads hard. The impact was audible and immediately concerning, with both women dropping to the floor. The assault continued as Vaquer grabbed Morgan and threw her head-first into a nearby TV monitor, leaving visible damage in its wake.

Following the incident, both Morgan and Perez were placed in concussion protocol for medical evaluation. Bryan Alvarez of Wrestling Observer Radio confirmed the status, stating both women had "clonked skulls HARD" and expressing concern about potential implications for WrestleMania. Perez had only recently returned to WWE programming the previous week after undergoing surgery to remove a benign mass from her back, making the timing of the injury particularly unfortunate.

Morgan documented the aftermath by sharing photos on social media showing a substantial hematoma on her forehead. The bump was visible and impressive in size, with her Judgment Day teammates visible in some of the backstage photos. Despite the severity of the injury, Morgan responded with characteristic defiance, tweeting at Vaquer: "You're mine now bitch" with a middle finger emoji.

When Vaquer mockingly responded by posting that Morgan looked "like a doll with its head caved in," Morgan shot back with intense resolve. In a follow-up post showing the swelling had subsided somewhat but the bruise remained, Morgan declared: "Hahahha u wish. Ur gonna have to kill me puta." The message was unmistakable: she was not backing down from her WrestleMania commitment.

Despite the medical concerns, Morgan's Women's World Championship match against Vaquer on night one of WrestleMania 42 remained on the card as announced. The match represents the culmination of an intense rivalry that began when Morgan won the Royal Rumble and chose to challenge Vaquer. The feud has been marked by repeated attacks on nearly every edition of Raw, with Morgan also making personal remarks targeting Vaquer's mother, which prompted previous aggressive responses from the champion.

At the time of reporting, WWE had not made an official statement regarding the medical clearance status of either Morgan or Perez. However, the fact that the match was included in the announced WrestleMania 42 lineup suggested Morgan was expected to be cleared for competition. Medical protocols like ImPACT testing would be used to assess whether either woman suffered a concussion and whether they could be cleared to compete.

The segment that caused the injuries was designed to further build the WrestleMania storyline between Morgan and Vaquer. Perez's involvement stemmed from her recent return and her alignment with Morgan against the champion. The angle also touched on the recent removal of Finn Balor from The Judgment Day faction, adding emotional layers to the confrontation.

Morgan's defiant social media responses indicated she remained focused on her championship opportunity despite the physical damage. The intensity of the feud and the personal nature of the attacks from both sides suggested the WrestleMania encounter would be highly charged if Morgan received medical clearance. With less than two weeks until WrestleMania 42, the wrestling world waited for official updates on both women's medical status and whether the match would proceed as scheduled.

🔥 WWE WrestleMania 42 Card and Lineup Announcement

WWE has officially announced the complete lineups for WrestleMania 42, ending weeks of speculation about how the company would divide its marquee matches across the two-night event. The announcement came during an appearance by SmackDown play-by-play announcer Joe Tessitore on ESPN's Get Up on the morning of April 7, 2026, just over a week and a half before the show begins.

Night One of WrestleMania 42, taking place Saturday April 18, will be headlined by Undisputed WWE Champion Cody Rhodes defending his title against Randy Orton, who will have Pat McAfee in his corner. The card also features Women's World Champion Stephanie Vaquer defending against Liv Morgan, Women's Intercontinental Champion AJ Lee defending against Becky Lynch, and a highly anticipated matchup between Seth Rollins and GUNTHER. Additionally, Night One will see the Women's Tag Team Champions Nia Jax and Lash Legend defend their titles in a fatal four-way match against Charlotte Flair and Alexa Bliss, Bayley and Lyra Valkyria, and The Bella Twins.

The first hour of Night One will air on ESPN2 and features two matches designed to draw mainstream attention. Drew McIntyre will face Jacob Fatu in an Unsanctioned Match, while LA Knight and The Usos will team up to face The Vision, a group consisting of Austin Theory, Logan Paul, and streamer IShowSpeed.

Night Two, taking place Sunday April 19, will conclude with CM Punk defending his World Heavyweight Championship against Roman Reigns in what is expected to be the marquee main event. The card also includes WWE Women's Champion Jade Cargill defending against Rhea Ripley, United States Champion Sami Zayn defending against Trick Williams, and "The Demon" Finn Balor facing Dominik Mysterio.

The opening hour of Night Two will air on ESPN and features Brock Lesnar taking on Oba Femi as well as a highly competitive six-way ladder match for Penta's Intercontinental Championship. The ladder match will include Je'Von Evans, Dragon Lee, JD McDonagh, Rusev, and Rey Mysterio competing for the title.

With 13 matches currently confirmed, there remains the possibility of one additional match being added to the card. Reports indicate that Asuka and IYO Sky have been building toward a WrestleMania 42 encounter, with IYO Sky confirming her intentions to face Asuka at the event. This match would likely be added to Night Two's lineup, which currently has one fewer match than Night One, bringing both nights to seven matches each.

The card reveals WWE's strategy of balancing star power and mainstream appeal alongside traditional wrestling matchups. The inclusion of Logan Paul and IShowSpeed represents the company's continued effort to draw crossover viewership through celebrity involvement, a strategy that has been both praised and criticized by longtime wrestling fans. Former WWE star Baron Corbin commented that the main event of WrestleMania does not need celebrity or outside involvement, reflecting some of the skepticism surrounding these bookings.

The event will take place at Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas, Nevada, the same venue that hosted WrestleMania 41 the previous year. Both nights will be available to watch on the ESPN App in the United States, while international viewers will be able to watch on Netflix, with shows starting at 6 PM ET/3 PM PT on both days.

The announcement of the full lineup comes amid reports of lower-than-expected ticket sales for the event. During an episode of Raw, CM Punk himself called for WWE to reduce ticket prices, suggesting the company price the event more competitively. Ticket prices had been reported as extraordinarily high in initial sales, with some premium seating reaching nearly nine thousand dollars. The pricing controversy represents a significant concern for WWE heading into the event, particularly given that WrestleMania 41 at the same venue drew 124,693 fans across two nights.

The match order within each night has not yet been fully confirmed, though WWE has designated which matches will air during the first hour of each night for ESPN broadcast purposes. The company has maintained some flexibility with the presentation while locking in which matches appear on which night.

🔥 Pat McAfee WWE SmackDown Actions and Randy Orton Storyline

Pat McAfee's involvement in the Randy Orton and Cody Rhodes storyline heading into WrestleMania 42 has become one of the most divisive angles in recent WWE history. The ESPN personality was revealed as the mysterious voice on the other end of Orton's phone calls during the April 3, 2026 episode of SmackDown, setting off a chain reaction of controversy both internally within WWE and among the fanbase.

On The Pat McAfee Show, McAfee addressed his involvement in character, declaring that he and Orton were out to "save the business" from the likes of Cody Rhodes and what he perceives as a declining WWE product. He revealed that he and Orton first concocted the plan after meeting at the NFL combine, and that McAfee's motivations stemmed in part from wanting his three-year-old daughter to become a wrestling fan—something he felt wouldn't happen unless WWE was forced to change. McAfee refused to fully reveal his plans but strongly suggested that what transpired on SmackDown was merely the beginning, with major surprises planned for WrestleMania 42 in Las Vegas.

"Just know that we've got some things cooking, and you're going to want to be in Las Vegas," McAfee said on his show. "It's going to be spectacular, it's going to be special. And future wrestling generations, fans, will have to look back on the night that was in Las Vegas and say 'That was the moment that that GOAT saved the entire business by becoming 15 time World Champion.' Shout out to Randy Orton. Thank you Randy for doing this."

Throughout his commentary, McAfee took shots at the Internet Wrestling Community, claiming they didn't understand the angle despite it being designed to help them. He criticized Rhodes and repeatedly brought up WWE's lower-than-usual ticket sales for WrestleMania as justification for the storyline. McAfee spent considerable time putting over Orton, describing him as the ideal wrestler who should be at the top of WWE's hierarchy. He also became cryptic when discussing whether he and Orton were acting alone or if others could become involved in the future.

McAfee's comments on The Pat McAfee Show were adamant that he was not the only one frustrated with WWE's direction, once again hinting at more surprises to come at WrestleMania. "I'm not the only one who feels this way," he stated. "We'll see how it all goes, we'll see how it all goes. But we're certainly on the Road to WrestleMania. And it is fantastic."

Behind the scenes, however, the reception to McAfee's involvement has been decidedly negative. According to reports, several WWE talents expressed frustration over McAfee being inserted into the Orton versus Rhodes match, with concerns extending beyond storyline logic to the fundamental question of whether such a high-profile bout between two wrestlers with deep history even needed the additional element of outside involvement. Multiple sources indicated that while Cody Rhodes didn't personally "sell" the pivot to anyone, there was a sense of sympathy for him having to "carry that additional weight" heading into WrestleMania.

The situation became more complicated when CM Punk delivered what was reportedly an off-script promo on WWE Raw, where he went off the cuff and delivered a heartfelt speech instead of following the planned material. In this segment, Punk took multiple shots at various WWE figures and corporate decisions, including calling McAfee "MAGAfee" and criticizing ticket prices. McAfee responded quickly on Twitter, firing back at Punk while redirecting the conversation toward Orton's accomplishments and legacy.

Backstage frustrations revealed in reports showed that talent had concerns building over several weeks, with performers voicing issues about high ticket prices and the limited number of complimentary tickets being provided to wrestlers for the event they would be performing on. The sense was that while WrestleMania had many available seats, performers were being offered minimal comps and forced to purchase additional tickets themselves.

The uncertainty about whether McAfee's angle was a coordinated corporate decision or an instance of him going into business for himself has created additional tension backstage. WWE talent have expressed concern that if the angle was planned by WWE management, it buries the work of the rest of the roster and makes everyone look bad. Conversely, if McAfee went off-script, that presents an even more problematic scenario. This ambiguity has led to genuine worry among performers about setting a precedent for corporate interference in creative decisions.

"I spoke to a WWE talent who was at that SmackDown TV taping, and they expressed the following on record. They essentially told me that if this was a work and WWE was in on it, it buries everything that they as talent do on the show, and it just makes everybody look bad. But if this is instead Pat McAfee going into business for himself, that is even worse," according to one report from a wrestling insider.

Reports have suggested that McAfee's involvement in the storyline was a corporate-driven move tied to TKO CEO Ari Emanuel, which has raised fears among talent about what this could mean for creative direction going forward. There is genuine concern backstage that if business continues to slip or ticket sales remain soft, Ari Emanuel could pop in and demand changes at short notice, establishing a troubling precedent of corporate interference overriding creative autonomy.

McAfee has doubled down on his criticisms of the current WWE product and the Internet Wrestling Community in subsequent appearances. He criticized the IWC for not understanding the angle, claiming it was designed for their benefit. McAfee continued to push the narrative that Orton is the solution WWE needs, highlighting his extensive accomplishments including being a 14-time world champion on the verge of his 15th title reign, being the youngest world champion ever, a 10-time WWE Grand Slam champion, and the only WWE Superstar to have entered WrestleMania as a champion with five different titles.

The storyline has also pulled in celebrity elements that have added complexity to the narrative. Jelly Roll, who shares a friendship with Orton after teaming with him at SummerSlam, attempted to calm down Orton during a recent SmackDown episode but was attacked with an RKO for his troubles. McAfee subsequently commented on Jelly Roll's involvement, suggesting that the musician made a strategic error by placing himself in Orton's path without understanding the magnitude of what Orton is attempting to accomplish.

As WrestleMania 42 approaches, McAfee has made bold proclamations about the event. He tweeted that the night would "live on in documentaries and wrestling folklore FOREVER" and that it would be "the night that @RandyOrton wins his 15th WORLD TITLE and in doing so SAVES THE BUSINESS." McAfee added a dramatic stipulation to his own involvement, stating that if Orton does not win the championship, he will be "GONE from wrestling FOREVER."

The match is confirmed as the Night One main event on April 18, 2026, at Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas, Nevada. Whether Orton emerges victorious and claims his 15th world title as McAfee predicts, or whether Rhodes retains the Undisputed WWE Championship, remains to be seen. What is clear, however, is that McAfee's insertion into the storyline has created significant internal strife within WWE, raised questions about corporate decision-making in creative matters, and generated considerable backlash from both the fanbase and performers, making this one of the most controversial angles heading into the company's biggest annual event.
