🔥 WrestleMania Hall of Fame Inductions

WWE has announced that the legendary Hulk Hogan vs. Andre the Giant match from WrestleMania III will be inducted into the 2026 WWE Hall of Fame as the Immortal Moment Award recipient. The announcement was made on March 31, 2026, recognizing one of the most iconic matches in professional wrestling history.

The match took place on March 29, 1987, at the Pontiac Silverdome in Michigan and saw Hogan defeat Andre to retain the WWF World Heavyweight Championship. The bout is forever remembered for "the slam heard around the world," when Hogan body-slammed the 500-pound, seven-foot-four Andre the Giant before finishing him with a leg drop. According to WWE Chief Content Officer Triple H, Gorilla Monsoon's commentary describing the matchup as "the irresistible force meets the immovable object" represents some of the greatest commentary in wrestling history. Triple H stated that the main event of WrestleMania III is more than just a piece of WWE history but is in the DNA of every bit of the industry.

This induction marks only the second time a match has been inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame. The Immortal Moment Award was introduced in 2025, with Steve Austin vs. Bret Hart from WrestleMania 13 becoming the inaugural recipient. At that induction ceremony, Hart and Austin accepted the award and spoke about the match. The award exists to recognize the most important and influential matches in WWE history that have changed the business.

The timing of this induction carries significant weight as it serves as a posthumous tribute to Hulk Hogan, who passed away in July 2025. This marks Hogan's third induction into the WWE Hall of Fame, setting a record for multiple inductions. He was first inducted individually in 2005 and again as part of the New World Order in 2020. Andre the Giant, who passed away in January 1993, was recognized as the first-ever Hall of Fame inductee that same year. The company honors Andre through the Andre the Giant Memorial Trophy, which has been contested regularly in a Battle Royal since WrestleMania 30.

The 2026 WWE Hall of Fame ceremony is scheduled to take place on Friday, April 17, at 9 PM PT at Dolby Live inside Park MGM in Las Vegas. The ceremony will occur during WrestleMania 42 weekend, with WrestleMania 42 itself taking place on April 18 and April 19 at Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas, Nevada.

The full 2026 WWE Hall of Fame class includes several notable inductees. In the Individual category are Stephanie McMahon and AJ Styles. Demolition has been recognized as a Group inductee. Sid Vicious is being posthumously inducted in the Legacy wing, while Bad News Brown is also receiving a posthumous Legacy Award induction. Dennis Rodman represents the Celebrity wing of the Hall of Fame.

One editor offered critical commentary on the practice of inducting matches into the Hall of Fame. The critic questioned whether the award holds meaningful value, noting that there is no physical Hall of Fame building that fans can visit. The editor argued that fans ultimately determine what matches and moments are truly immortal through their viewing and discussion, rather than WWE designations. The critic also pointed out a historical inaccuracy in the marketing, noting that the "slam heard around the world" was not Hogan's first time slamming Andre, nor was it the first time Andre had ever been slammed. Despite acknowledging that the moment is undeniably iconic, the critic maintained that inducting it into the Hall of Fame is unnecessary.

Bret Hart offered his perspective on the Hall of Fame process when discussing the posthumous induction of Bad News Brown. Hart expressed happiness at Brown's induction, describing him as one of his father's wrestlers from Stampede Wrestling. Hart characterized Brown as an intense, well-educated, and disciplined individual who was honorable and never told a lie. Brown was a 1976 Olympic bronze medalist in judo who learned wrestling in Japan before breaking into the profession in Canada and the United States. Hart noted that Brown's Japanese-style wrestling psychology made him difficult to work with, describing him as a "meat chopper" who simply wanted to beat his opponent and get his hand raised. Hart described Brown as fearless and arguably one of the toughest men ever to put on boots in professional wrestling, calling him a legitimate badass capable of fighting anybody. Hart recommended against putting himself and Brown in a program together when Vince McMahon wanted to bring Brown to the WWF, but McMahon paired them as the final two in the WrestleMania IV battle royal that Brown won. Hart believed some in the industry saw Brown as "a bit of a racist" because of his outspokenness about his Blackness, though Hart himself would have been a big fan of Brown outside of the business context due to his honesty. Brown passed away at age 63 in 2007 due to a heart attack.

Hart also reflected on his historic WrestleMania 13 match against Steve Austin when discussing the torch-passing moment. Hart explained that his goal was to deliver a really good story for Austin because he wanted him to be the guy and knew he was destined for that role. Hart stated he was not going to let Shawn Michaels and Triple H steal that position from Austin and that he intentionally wanted to pass the torch to him. The No Disqualification Submission match between Hart and Austin has long been regarded as one of the greatest bouts in professional wrestling history.

🔥 Trick Williams White Coat and WWE Gold

Trick Williams has quickly become one of WWE's most compelling rising stars since his main roster debut, and much of that success owes itself to his distinctive presentation and personal drive heading into WrestleMania 42. The SmackDown superstar will challenge Sami Zayn for the United States Championship on April 18 and 19, marking his debut on the grandest stage of them all.

Williams' white mink coat and white shoes, which he calls his "lemon pepper steppers," have become synonymous with his WWE presentation. However, the signature look was not initially intended to be a permanent fixture. Williams explained that when he debuted on SmackDown, he wore the white mink coat to make a statement as he interrupted Sami Zayn, an action that sparked the rivalry between the two that continues to this day. The following Friday, Williams decided to wear a different outfit, but that decision was quickly questioned by WWE management.

Triple H approached Williams asking where his coat was, and Williams initially dismissed the question, thinking it was just a one-time piece of attire rather than something that should become a regular part of his presentation. However, the message became clearer when Bruce Prichard also asked about the coat's absence. The final push came from Michael Hayes, who delivered a comparison that would stick with Williams. Hayes told him, "I ain't never seen Elvis Presley without his coat." That comment was the turning point. Williams realized that the coat had already become so synonymous with his character that its absence was immediately noticeable to the creative team.

Williams has since embraced the coat as an integral part of his character, particularly in relation to his self-proclaimed moniker "The Anointed One." He noted that wearing all white every time fans see him is entirely fitting for someone who calls himself by that name. The coat has become a visual representation of his character and presence on the main roster, much like the iconic outfits worn by legends of the past.

Beyond the coat, Williams is carrying significant emotional weight into his WrestleMania match. His mother is making the journey to Las Vegas despite being in recovery from knee replacement surgery. She is rehabbing every single day to be physically capable of attending his first WrestleMania, telling her son that she could not miss the event for anything in the world. Williams acknowledged the gravity of this sacrifice, stating that it means everything to him and that he is determined to put on a show like never before.

Williams is also on the verge of making history with his fiancee Lash Legend. The two were called up to the main roster around the same time and have become engaged. Legend has already captured WWE Women's Tag Team Championship gold alongside Nia Jax, and Williams is hopeful that he can win the United States Championship from Zayn at WrestleMania, allowing them both to hold WWE gold simultaneously. He emphasized that it would mean everything to achieve that milestone with someone who understands his background, shares his cultural identity as an SEC athlete from the South, and can relate to his journey from NXT to the main roster.

Williams' recent media appearances on ESPN's First Take have showcased his personality and articulate nature, further building his profile beyond the wrestling community. These appearances have highlighted not only his ambitions within WWE but also his character as someone who values family, understands the significance of representation, and appreciates the opportunity he has been given on the main roster.

The stage is set for Williams to make a significant statement at WrestleMania 42. Whether he wins or loses against Zayn, this marks a pivotal moment in his career with his mother watching, his fiancee also competing on the same card, and his signature white coat serving as a visual reminder of how far he has come since his SmackDown debut. The combination of personal motivation, family support, and his distinctive presentation has created a compelling narrative heading into the event.

THE PATH TO THE MATCH: INJURIES FORCING WWE TO ADJUST

The Rollins vs. Gunther matchup did not materialize in a vacuum.
