🔥 TNA Stars and Updates

TL;DR: TNA Wrestling continues to attract major talent and build momentum with positive testimonials from returning WWE/NXT stars about the company's creative freedom, supportive locker room environment, and attractive work schedules.

## TNA Wrestling Attracting Major Talent with Creative Freedom and Supportive Environment

TNA Wrestling is establishing itself as an attractive destination for professional wrestlers, with multiple recent signings praising the company's approach to creative control, locker room culture, and work-life balance. Recent interviews reveal a pattern of talent—both established veterans and rising stars—choosing TNA as their preferred promotion.

### Hardy Boys Highlight Creative Freedom and Family Time

Matt Hardy recently discussed why The Hardy Boys have found success in TNA, emphasizing the promotional freedoms they've been granted. "TNA allowed us to do things that were out of the box, take risks, and change our characters," Hardy said in an interview. "When I was 'Broken' Matt and Jeff was Brother Nero, we were white-hot again."

Beyond creative opportunities, Hardy highlighted the practical benefits of working with TNA. "TNA allows me a great schedule," he explained. "It allows me so much more creative freedom, and it also allows me more time to be with my kids." The schedule has even enabled opportunities for additional work, as The Hardy Boys were recently revealed to be part of the WWE2K26 video game roster while maintaining their TNA contracts.

Hardy also spoke about working with younger talent in TNA. "We work with so many people who said, 'Oh, my God, I grew up idolizing you guys, and this is my dream,'" he noted. He specifically highlighted X Division champion Leon Slater as an example, noting that Slater was extremely excited to work with The Hardy Boys and had idolized them throughout his career.

The Hardy Boys are currently the second longest-reigning TNA Tag Team champions and were featured on the card for the TNA Sacrifice PLE.

### Elayna Black: Mental Health Recovery and Locker Room Support

Elayna Black (formerly Cora Jade in WWE/NXT) took a different path to TNA, prioritizing mental health after her WWE release in July 2025. Black initially had no plans to sign with any promotion immediately following her departure from WWE. However, after taking time away from wrestling, she reconsidered her career when reflecting on her previous experience in the TNA locker room during the NXT-TNA partnership.

"I loved the locker room environment, and it felt like they creatively trusted me," Black explained. "Tommy Dreamer would genuinely ask my opinion on things, and if I wasn't okay with something or didn't like something, I didn't have to do it. He wanted to hear my ideas, and that was very different for me."

Black signed with TNA in January 2026, with her contract running through the end of the year. She made her debut on the TNA AMC broadcast on January 15. "I have friends there, the locker room is great, and I was trying not to hate wrestling — I wanted to fall back in love with it," she said. "So I thought about what would help me do that, and TNA checked all those boxes. It's a great environment, very creatively free, and they trust me. I also had a great relationship with Tommy when I was there, so I felt like TNA was the right place. And it's been great."

Black is scheduled to face Mara Sade in a No Disqualification match at the TNA Sacrifice PLE.

### Indi Hartwell's Path: Legend Recruitment

Indi Hartwell, another former NXT talent, came to TNA through a personal outreach from TNA legend Gail Kim. "She's no longer with the company, but she's done so much for TNA, and she's someone that I grew up watching on TNA," Hartwell explained. "She reached out to me and asked if it's something that I'd want to do, and I was so glad she did because I was such a huge fan of TNA growing up."

Hartwell made her in-ring debut for TNA in May 2025 against Kelsey Heather at the Rebellion PPV and has been pursuing opportunities for the TNA Knockouts World Title since then.

### Behind-the-Scenes Development: Steve Maclin's Producing Role

Steve Maclin, a former TNA World Champion, is expanding his involvement with the promotion beyond in-ring work. According to his wife, AEW's Deonna Purrazzo, Maclin has been working with the company in a producing capacity alongside Lance Storm and Ace Steel. Purrazzo noted that Maclin's wrestling mind is constantly active, drawing on training from some of the industry's best minds including Robbie Brookside, Norman Smiley, and Terry Taylor.

Maclin was scheduled to challenge current TNA World Champion Mike Santana at the TNA Sacrifice PLE.

ROH Studio Tapings and Changes

TL;DR: Ring of Honor has shifted to dedicated studio tapings in Jacksonville, and Billie Starkz credits the changes as "baby steps" that are significantly improving the product's quality and creative opportunities.

Ring of Honor has undergone a significant operational change over the last month, moving away from their previous model of taping matches before and after AEW Dynamite and AEW Collision. The promotion has now transitioned to holding dedicated studio tapings at WJCT Studios in Jacksonville, Florida, a shift that talent members say is yielding positive results.

Billie Starkz, who has been one of the prominent faces of the ROH women's division since February 2023, spoke about the impact of these changes. Starkz has held the distinction of being the first-ever ROH Women's World Television Champion and has challenged for both the ROH Women's Title and ROH Women's Pure Title during her tenure with the promotion. In an interview, she expressed her enthusiasm for the new direction, stating, "It's been a great experience so far. Having it truly focused on ROH and our roster has been so helpful for everyone who needed that extra minute and extra time to figure out our lives through ROH."

One of the notable differences in the new studio taping format is the consistency of the crowd attending each taping session. Rather than performing in front of different audiences each week as part of AEW programming, ROH talent now performs in front of the same audience across multiple weeks at the studio location. When asked if this consistency presents a different dynamic compared to performing before varied crowds, Starkz acknowledged the distinction. She noted that while her experience performing in front of many different crowds means the change doesn't negatively impact her personally, she has observed a clear shift in atmosphere. "I do feel the atmosphere shift because it is a different type of crowd than you would get after Dynamite or Collision," she explained. "These crowds showed up just for ROH, and they've been here for a couple of weeks, so we can't be predictable and give them something we gave them last week. We have to give them something new, and having this time in Jacksonville where it's just about ROH is giving us that time to do that."

ROH held its first set of studio tapings on March 1, followed by a second set on March 22. Between these two tapings, noticeable improvements were evident in production quality and presentation. The second taping session featured more character development and distinction, with various additions that enhanced the overall product. Reflecting on these improvements, Starkz observed, "It was very noticeable. I was getting off the bus with somebody and we both had that moment of, 'Oh, this has changed so much.' It's taken baby steps to get there, but all these little baby steps are adding up to make the product so much better."

At the time of the interview, Starkz was actively involved in ROH's championship picture. She had recently challenged Deonna Purrazzo for the ROH Women's Pure Title on the March 19 episode of ROH TV. Looking ahead, she was scheduled to compete at ROH x MLP Global Wars, which took place on Friday, March 27.

Steve Maclin TNA Marine Gimmick

TL;DR: Steve Maclin has consistently rejected pitches to build his wrestling character around his Marine background, viewing such exploitation as inauthentic and disrespectful to his military service.

---

Steve Maclin is pushing back against the expectation that he should constantly lean on his real-life military background as part of his wrestling persona. The former TNA World Champion revealed in an interview that fans and promoters have repeatedly pitched him the idea of playing up the "Marine" identity in his character, particularly early in his career and during his time in WWE.

"All the time. My time in WWE, even when I started on the independents, that's what you people wanted. It's like, no, that's not authentic. That's not what a veteran does. Especially being a Marine, you're not out there flaunting it," Maclin said, making clear his philosophical stance on the matter.

While Maclin is proud of his service to his country, he refuses to exploit that chapter of his life for entertainment purposes. "My time in service is my time in service. That was a part of my life. I got to do exactly what I wanted to do during my time there, and that was to go fight for and serve my country," he explained, drawing a firm boundary between his personal achievements and his professional wrestling character.

Maclin emphasized that while he's willing to allow wrestling promotions to reference his military background if they choose, he will not aggressively promote it himself. "I'm not someone who likes to push that agenda. They can use my background if they want, but I'll never push it down anybody's throat because I don't feel like that's right or authentic. That's what I've always tried to be, no matter what, in the ring. I try to be myself and as authentic as I can, whether I'm a babyface or a heel."

Looking forward, the TNA star hinted at a more aggressive and unfiltered version of his character that could emerge in his current storyline. "For me, it's however you want to look at it and perceive it. And like we said earlier about layers, we're getting to a layer now where I'm just going to become a belligerent Marine. And that's the worst thing you could ever want a Marine to be, belligerent and on your head, because now I don't care," he said.

Referencing his recent unceremonious firing from TNA Wrestling, Maclin suggested he's now operating without the usual concerns that typically constrain professional wrestlers. "What are you going to do, fire me? I've already been fired. I've been fired from WWE. I've been fired from TNA. And look what happened when I got fired from TNA. I got my job back because I used everybody else," he explained, indicating a newfound freedom in his approach to his character and career decisions.

Once they removed the chaos and distractions that had built up during their separation, things between them started to feel natural again. "And it really made a difference. When we both got rid of all that extra stuff, it was like, 'Oh yeah, we do like being around each other,'" Perry said.

Her comments add significant weight to their story. This wasn't a simple breakup and reunion — it was a relationship that reached the point of legal separation and divorce proceedings, only to be pulled back before it was finalized. The fact that things got as far as attempted paperwork delivery, including the birthday incident, shows just how close they came to ending things completely. Instead, they chose to reset, work on themselves individually, and ultimately find their way back together.

Jacob Fatu Calls Out Internet For Twisting His Words Over Release Remarks

TL;DR: Jacob Fatu clarified that his earlier comments about getting released weren't hypothetical — he was stating he WILL be released if he goes too far with Drew McIntyre because the situation is personal and not a scripted storyline.

---

Jacob Fatu isn't backing down from his intense rivalry with Drew McIntyre, but he is setting the record straight on what he actually meant when discussing the possibility of getting released.

During an appearance on the Babyfaces podcast on March 26, 2026, Fatu addressed the confusion surrounding his recent comments head-on. He emphasized that the situation with McIntyre is rooted in reality and personal conflict, not a promotional angle leading toward a WrestleMania program.

"So what I'm tapping into right now with Drew is — man, it's week by week. This isn't no buildup. These interviews that we're doing, I'm not doing it to build up WrestleMania matches. This isn't no buildup, okay? This is just me speaking and talking," Fatu explained.

The primary point Fatu wanted to clarify involved his earlier statement about getting released. According to him, the internet had twisted his words into something he never intended to say. Rather than speaking hypothetically, Fatu made clear that his statement was definitive.

"And when I was talking and I said that, you know — no, I didn't mean if — I didn't mean I would get released if me and Drew go too far. No, I was saying I will get released, okay? Because I don't play with it. I don't play this. He's taking food off my family's table," Fatu stated.

This distinction is crucial to understanding Fatu's mindset. His intensity stems from a place of genuine conviction, rooted in his work ethic and the sacrifices he made before joining WWE. He elaborated on this perspective, drawing from his 12 years of grinding in the industry prior to signing with the company.

"Before WWE, for 12 years, man, I did anything and everything I had to do. I never went online, I never complained, I never bitched, I never said, 'Oh, I'm supposed to be in WWE.' No, I grinded for mine. Okay, so this is what makes me different. And I keep telling cats, I'm not hot at y'all — don't worry, we still all good. But they've got to be very clear, because the internet is a powerful thing. They'll take anything you say, twist your words, and make it something else," Fatu said.

Rather than cooling tensions with McIntyre, Fatu's clarification only intensified his message. He made his intentions unmistakably clear, leaving no room for further misinterpretation.

"So therefore, Drew, take this down and write this down for the internet: Drew, every time I see you, I'MMA BEAT YOUR ASS. Okay? That's all it comes down to," Fatu declared.

This latest statement does nothing to de-escalate one of WWE's most compelling rivalries. If anything, it sharpens the edge and demonstrates that Fatu is committed to his position. He isn't walking back anything — he's simply making his stance even more explicit and unambiguous for those who may have misunderstood his initial remarks.

WWE Hall Of Famer To Have Retirement Match At 83 Years Old

TL;DR: WWE Hall of Famer Jimmy Valiant will wrestle his final match at age 83 on April 25, 2025, in Lancaster, South Carolina, concluding a 62-year professional wrestling career.

---

"The Boogie Woogie Man" Jimmy Valiant is ready to dance one last time. The WWE Hall of Famer will have his final match on April 25 in Lancaster, South Carolina at an event called "Boogie's Last Dance" for NAWA Championship Wrestling. The 83-year-old wrestling legend has not wrestled since September 23, 2023, but will return to the squared circle one last time next month.

"Yes, this will be my final dance. I've retired before but this is truly my last dance," Valiant said in a statement via press release. "I'm honored to hang up my boots after 62 years of wrestling pro. I had over 15,000 matches and drove 6 million miles on U.S. highways, plus flying getting to those matches."

Valiant expressed his desire to make wrestling across seven decades a reality for himself. "I want to dance down that aisle to my theme song, 'Boy From New York City,' one last time to make wrestling in 7 decades, from 1964 to 2026, a reality for me," he added.

NAWA promoter Michael Elliott emphasized the significance of hosting this historic event. "We are honored to have this moment be a part of NAWA. This will truly be a historic evening," Elliott said. Event tickets can be purchased through official channels.

Valiant was inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame (then WWF Hall of Fame) in 1996 alongside Johnny Valiant. His retirement match joins a tradition of legendary wrestlers who continue performing well into their advanced years. Fellow WWE Hall of Famer Bushwhacker Luke continues to wrestle at age 79 and recently garnered attention after performing a stinkface during a match in Florida. Ric Flair also wrestled his final match at 73 years old in 2022, demonstrating the enduring passion many wrestling legends maintain for the sport.
