🔥 AEW Dynamite Coverage and News

Darby Allin's rise to the AEW World Championship continues to generate significant discussion within the wrestling community. The young star captured the prestigious title from Maxwell Jacob Friedman during the April 15, 2026 edition of Dynamite, defeating the long-time champion in a shocking upset that immediately prompted response from fellow roster members.

Brian Cage took to social media to address mounting criticism regarding Allin's championship win. In his statement, Cage offered a strong endorsement of Darby's credentials and work ethic, noting that while he himself is billed as "the" bodyguy, Darby has proven his legitimacy through consistent hard work and creative performance. Cage emphasized that Allin has earned respect through his willingness to put his body on the line week after week with new and destructive offense, all while maintaining strong fan support. The veteran wrestler highlighted that Darby is not only a phenomenal in-ring worker who bleeds for AEW, but also represents the ultimate example of someone who gets themselves over through sheer determination and believability in matches. Cage also gave particular praise to the finish of the title change, specifically calling out a callback headlock takeover that garnered significant crowd reaction.

Darby's championship reign is already shaping up to be an active one. His first defense as champion comes on April 29 during the Dynamite broadcast from Fairfax, Virginia, where he will face Brody King, who earlier defeated Lio Rush on the same show where Allin won the title. During this same April 29 episode, MJF will compete in a title match of his own, as the former world champion challenges TNT Champion Kevin Knight. Knight apparently attempted to goad MJF into a match earlier in the week but was trolling the situation, with the actual challenge being set for the following Wednesday. The dynamic between Allin and MJF appears far from over, with commentary suggesting these two competitors may have additional encounters in their future, particularly given their history of trading signature headlock takedown exchanges.

AEW's television schedule is undergoing some adjustments for May programming due to external sports commitments. The May 9 edition of Collision will shift from its usual TNT home to TBS, maintaining its regular 8 PM ET / 5 PM PT time slot. The network change is necessary because TNT will be airing NHL playoff coverage that evening. The episode, branded as "Fairway to Hell," will take place at the SoFi Center in West Palm Beach, Florida, a high-tech indoor golf facility where AEW plans to incorporate golfers and golf influencers into the special-themed presentation. This timing creates interesting counter-programming circumstances, as the same evening will see WWE Backlash streaming live on ESPN Unlimited from Tampa, Florida at 6 PM ET, meaning there will be partial overlap between the two major wrestling broadcasts, though not a complete head-to-head competition. AEW has not officially confirmed the network switch despite the program already appearing on TBS scheduling information.

Dr. Britt Baker Outside WWE

Dr. Britt Baker, D.M.D. made a hometown appearance in a big way during a major sports weekend in Pittsburgh. With the NFL Draft taking place in the Steel City, the AEW star was on hand representing her roots, sporting full Pittsburgh Steelers gear along with a classic New World Order hat. Baker has long been vocal about her Steelers fandom, even incorporating the iconic Terrible Towels into her entrance whenever she performs in Pittsburgh, affectionately referring to the city as "Brittsburgh."

A familiar face made an unexpected return to television this week, though not inside an AEW ring, as Britt Baker resurfaced during a major mainstream broadcast amid her ongoing absence from the company. The appearance didn't come with entrance music or a D.M.D. promo. However, it still caught attention, especially given how long she's been off AEW programming.

While attending the Draft festivities, Baker had a memorable moment when she caught a Terrible Towel tossed by Steelers legend Ben Roethlisberger. The moment marked her first notable TV appearance in quite some time, even if it came outside the squared circle. Taking to social media, Baker noted that the moment doubled as a perfect birthday gift, adding another personal highlight to her already strong connection with her hometown crowd. She wrote via X after the cameo appearance: "My fav player ever @_BigBen7 just threw me his terrible towel. Best birthday gift #HereWeGo @steelernation @steelers #nfldraft."

Britt Baker has not appeared on AEW television since November 2024, following her last in-ring outing on Dynamite. Since then, speculation about her status has remained stuck in a prolonged hold. In March, Baker indicated that her return timeline is "not up to [her]," suggesting decisions remain with AEW management. Meanwhile, AEW president Tony Khan has maintained that she is still part of the company. He stated earlier this year that she is "highly regarded and respected" and expected back at some point, though no timeline has been confirmed.

Recently, All Elite Wrestling took to X to wish Britt Baker a happy birthday as she turned 35 on April 23. She happens to share the date with John Cena, who has already confirmed his return for an upcoming PLE. As it stands, there's still no confirmed update on Baker's AEW future.

With no official update on her return, Baker's latest appearance serves as a reminder that while she may be off AEW TV, she hasn't faded from the spotlight entirely. For now, fans continue to wait, and like a delayed comeback angle, the payoff remains to be seen.

Brian Cage Addresses Criticism Over Darby Allin Being AEW Champion

Brian Cage has stepped into the conversation surrounding Darby Allin's AEW World Championship win, directly pushing back on the wave of criticism questioning whether the title change was deserved or if it could ultimately lead to a transitional reign.

Cage addressed the discourse on social media, making it clear he strongly disagrees with the negative reaction and instead highlighted Allin's value to AEW. "I've noticed some criticism over Darby Allin winning the AEW title," Cage wrote. "First and foremost, this guy absolutely deserves it. He's not only a phenomenal in ring worker and bleeds aew, but this guy is the epitome of getting yourself over. Darby kills himself week after week, in a new, creative, and destructive way. Yet somehow gets back up, again and again."

Cage did not stop there, doubling down on his support and pointing to Allin's credibility, work ethic, and connection with fans. He continued, "I'm 'the' body guy, and yes, Darby is very small. But he has fought harder than anyone to get the people behind him, and has become very believable in his matches. So huge stamp of a approval from me to have him as world champion. And bonus points for the shock pop and the call back headlock takeover finish."

With his championship reign underway, Darby Allin now moves forward with his next defense already scheduled. He will put the AEW World Championship on the line for the second time on the April 29 episode of AEW Dynamite, where he faces Brody King.

Kenny Omega on TNA Talent Pulls

AEW's Kenny Omega has waded into the ongoing controversy surrounding TNA Wrestling's decision to pull its stars from matches with AEW wrestlers. TNA, which maintains a partnership with WWE, recently requested that its roster avoid facing AEW talent on independent promotions, resulting in the cancellation of several high-profile matches, including one between Nic Nemeth and MJF.

Speaking with The Takedown on SI, Omega explained that these territorial disputes stem from promotions operating in self-contained environments without recognizing the broader wrestling landscape. "I think whether people would like to realize it or not, and I think that if you're in a bubble for too long and you fail to realize what exists outside your bubble and how small your bubble is — and in this case, we're talking about professional wrestling — I think you start to run into problems like this, where someone will think that they're better than somebody else, and that there is no reason to work together, and they don't see the upside," he explained.

Nemeth, who was directly affected by the booking cancellation, indicated that TNA President Carlos Silva cited "deep contractual obligations" as the reason for preventing the match. These obligations likely stem from TNA's partnership agreement with WWE, which apparently restricts cross-promotional talent exchanges with AEW.

Despite his frustration with the current situation, Omega expressed optimism about the potential benefits of inter-promotional cooperation. He believes that if wrestling companies could overcome their competitive anxieties, the entire industry would experience growth and development. "And I still think that we are in a place where professional wrestling can still be more than what it is, and I do think that if there were situations where we could all play nice with each other, then I really do think that business as a whole would move in towards more of a positive direction," he stated.

Omega acknowledged the legitimate concerns that promotions face when considering cross-promotional matches. The financial implications are substantial, and there are strategic considerations regarding how such matches should be promoted and which promotion should benefit from a victory. "I understand with the amount of money that's at play, that's a very sensitive subject. It's a very sensitive issue. And if you get to a point where we're starting to cross-promote or we're having AEW wrestlers wrestle against talent that's affiliated with WWE, for example, it becomes a sensitive topic as to how do we promote that? Who wins? Who loses? I don't say that specifically as a who wins, who loses on paper, but I mean, who wins, who loses as a promotion. Who's going to come out looking like the stronger promotion," he said.

Despite these obstacles, Omega remains convinced that solutions exist if promotions are willing to negotiate in good faith. "If everyone were to really just sit down and figure out a way to make it happen, it's more than doable," he added.

AEW has already demonstrated the viability of cross-promotional partnerships through its established relationships with Japanese promotion NJPW and Mexican promotion CMLL. These collaborations have proven commercially successful, spawning several annual pay-per-view events that benefit both parties involved.
