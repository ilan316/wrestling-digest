🔥 AEW Forbidden Door 2026

Will Ospreay continues to dominate the headlines heading into AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door 2026, the cross-promotional event scheduled for Sunday, June 28 from the SAP Center in San Jose, California. The British wrestler finds himself in the Owen Hart Foundation Cup final against Swerve Strickland, where the winner will challenge for the AEW World Championship at AEW All In.

According to the final betting odds released by BetOnline.ag, Ospreay is positioned as the biggest favorite on the entire card at -5000 against Strickland's +1200. This represents an extremely lopsided matchup from a wagering perspective. Other major favorites include Jon Moxley defending the AEW Continental Championship against Bandido at -1100, Thekla retaining the AEW Women's World Championship against Starlight Kid at -2500, Adam Copeland and Christian Cage defending the AEW World Tag Team Titles at -3000, Kenny Omega facing Zack Sabre Jr. at -1500, and Team Briscoe in a steel cage match against Team MJF at -600.

Away from the betting odds, Ospreay has been making the media rounds promoting the event and addressing recent controversy. On the June 17 edition of AEW Dynamite, Ospreay delivered a promo discussing his recent marriage to fellow wrestler Alex Windsor that attracted significant backlash on social media. During an appearance on 107.7 The Bone with Baby Huey, Ospreay clarified his comments and explained what he meant during the controversial segment.

Ospreay stated that he had been describing how amazing his wife is as a person and how she has encouraged him to follow his wrestling dreams. The British wrestler explained that the provocative language that sparked outrage was actually a playful reference to video games. "I said she's a good wife. I was explaining how amazing of a person she is. She's encouraged me to follow my dream. Then I said I took her upstairs and smashed the life out of her, at Mario Kart. Like, I beat her so bad at Mario Kart, and when I said to her, 'Clean yourself up, love. I'm going to Houston,' she was sweating, mate. She was sweating trying to play this game. It's 38 degrees over in England right now. It's so hot," Ospreay explained.

He emphasized that the comments were intended humorously and reflected his authentic personality. "I don't get why everyone was so mad about it, I'll be honest with you guys. People got angry, and honestly, I attract anger everywhere I go, but I can't please all you guys. This is me. I'm authentically myself. I don't mean any harm. I'm just being funny," Ospreay stated. He further emphasized that those closest to him, including his wife, his boss, Warner Bros., and Paramount Pictures, had no issues with his remarks.

"Here's the thing that I'll always say about this. My boss didn't care. Warner Bros. didn't care. Paramount Pictures didn't care. My wife didn't care. So on that note, I politely disagree with all of you if you have a problem with it. There are so many other things going on in the world that really need our attention, our love, and our care. Don't come to me with mine and my wife's relationship. F*** off," he concluded.

In separate interviews promoting Forbidden Door, Ospreay has spoken positively about his marriage to Windsor and shared his most memorable moments in AEW. During an appearance on Kron 4, he discussed how Windsor supports him through the pressures of being an active performer. "I mean, the pressure always does get to me. But thankfully, I've got an incredible wife that I can air my anxiety and I can air my thoughts and my feelings. She gives me a pat on the back and she says, 'what's the worst that could happen? You could poo yourself in the ring,' and I go, 'don't say that.' You can't be saying that. She's amazing. I found a good girl, thank God. One that can take a joke," Ospreay explained.

When asked about his two most memorable moments in AEW, Ospreay named his match against Bryan Danielson at AEW Dynasty and a collaboration with the Assassin's Creed franchise. "I've got two. When I wrestled Bryan Danielson at Dynasty, I couldn't even imagine that match was going to happen because he got medically disqualified. I generally thought I'd never wrestle him. So when he showed up to AEW, I was like, 'Aha, there's a chance.' And the other one, we had a collaboration deal with Assassin's Creed and I got to do an entrance that featured Ezio, who was the main character for the Assassin's Creed franchise for so long, and he got to do a voiceover for me, and I essentially got inducted into the Brotherhood, so like, that wouldn't happen without AEW, do you know what I mean? Assassin's Creed was like a way to portray the character that I wanted to do in wrestling, so then to finally have them pay me money to do an entrance, like, this is a dream come true, it was wild," he explained.

🔴 WWE News

🔥 Sami Zayn WWE Championship Victory Reactions

Sami Zayn has finally reached the pinnacle of WWE. At Night of Champions 2026, the Canadian superstar captured the Undisputed WWE Championship in a triple threat match against defending champion Cody Rhodes and Gunther. This victory represents the culmination of months of character progression that saw Zayn transition from a rule-following babyface to a frustrated, increasingly delusional competitor who felt he had been repeatedly overlooked despite his consistent performances.

In the weeks leading up to the event, Zayn's relationship with Rhodes deteriorated significantly. After being inserted as special guest referee for Rhodes and Gunther's title match on June 19, Zayn's actions drew criticism from commentators and fellow wrestlers who felt he overstepped his boundaries. However, Zayn disputed these claims during an appearance on ESPN's SportsCenter before Night of Champions. He acknowledged that he had not been himself in recent months following his Royal Rumble loss but maintained that he was pushed to his limit in his referee role just as anyone else would have been.

Zayn's character development centered on his belief that having the championship had changed Rhodes, while simultaneously acknowledging that not having the championship had not always made him act his best. He described the situation as a "full circle" opportunity, a chance at redemption after failing to capture the world championship six months prior in Saudi Arabia. This narrative resonated with audiences who had watched Zayn continue to deliver exceptional performances despite repeated title shortfalls.

The victory at Night of Champions sends Zayn forward with significant momentum heading into SummerSlam 2026, which takes place in Minneapolis on August 1 and 2. His first challenger is expected to be either Oba Femi or Jey Uso, determined by their King of the Ring final match at the same event. Both competitors represent formidable obstacles, setting up compelling matchups that will test Zayn's ability to maintain his new status as WWE Champion.

Kevin Owens, Zayn's longtime best friend and frequent rival, celebrated the achievement despite being unable to attend in person. Owens, who has been sidelined for over fifteen months recovering from serious neck surgery, expressed genuine emotion about missing the event in person. In a lengthy message posted to social media, Owens stated that Zayn's championship victory was the moment he most wished he had been able to witness during his injury absence. He praised Zayn's consistency and talent, noting that when someone is consistently that good for that long, they become undeniable. Owens emphasized that Zayn becoming WWE Champion was inevitable and that he had deserved the moment for a very long time.

Owens' tribute included humorous personal anecdotes and photographs documenting their decades-long friendship and wrestling relationship spanning various promotions and eras. He referenced an incident in Texas where he had to help Zayn put on his socks and boxers after injuring his back, highlighting the depth of their connection both as allies and occasional competitors. The message served as a powerful testament to their bond and demonstrated that despite their on-screen rivalries, the two men shared a genuine friendship that transcended the business of professional wrestling.

Zayn's ascension to championship status also comes amid broader changes to WWE's television programming. The company announced that SmackDown will be returning to a two-hour format beginning July 3, 2026, after operating with a three-hour runtime since the start of 2025. This schedule adjustment reflects WWE's ongoing evaluation of programming structure and pacing decisions that affect how stories are told and developed.

The championship victory represents not only a personal milestone for Zayn but also a significant moment for character development in WWE storytelling. His journey from overlooked competitor to world champion through gradual frustration and character evolution demonstrated the company's willingness to invest in complex narrative arcs that reflect genuine emotional progression. With SummerSlam looming, Zayn's reign promises to be a focal point of WWE's championship program heading into one of the company's biggest events of the summer.

🔥 WWE Night of Champions 2026

SAMI ZAYN FINALLY REACHES THE MOUNTAINTOP AT WWE NIGHT OF CHAMPIONS 2026

For the first time in his career spanning more than two decades in professional wrestling, Sami Zayn achieved the pinnacle of WWE success by capturing the Undisputed WWE Championship at Night of Champions on Saturday, June 27, 2026, at Kingdom Arena in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia. Zayn defeated defending champion Cody Rhodes and former World Heavyweight Champion Gunther in a Triple Threat Match, with the finish coming when he reversed a Cross Rhodes from Rhodes into a tight roll-up for the three-count.

The victory proved to be an emotional moment for Zayn, who has been chasing the WWE Championship for more than ten years without success. In an interview following his win, an overwhelmed Zayn addressed the crowd: "A lot has been said about me. Some good, and lately, some bad. A lot of folks said I would never win the WWE Championship. But I stand here tonight in front of my people, the WWE Champion. It is hard to believe. Oh my God."

Responding to chants from the crowd that he deserved the title, Zayn fired back passionately: "You know what? You're damn right I deserve it. Twenty-plus years in the game at the highest level, never got the recognition. Ten-plus years chasing this championship, never got the recognition. I don't care what happens to me tomorrow, next week, next month, or next year. Forever, I will be Sami Zayn, WWE Champion."

According to reports, WWE had originally planned to give Zayn his world title moment at WrestleMania 43 in Saudi Arabia next year, making the decision to move his coronation to Night of Champions a significant creative shift. With the victory, Zayn becomes the 26th Grand Slam Champion in WWE history, joining the ranks of legends like Rey Mysterio, Eddie Guerrero, Daniel Bryan, and Roman Reigns. Prior to Zayn's achievement, John Cena was the last wrestler to become a Grand Slam Champion when he won the Intercontinental Championship in 2025.

REACTIONS FROM THE WRESTLING WORLD

The moment resonated strongly with Zayn's longtime friend and ally Kevin Owens, who has been sidelined with a neck injury for the past fifteen months. Owens shared an emotional message on social media: "I've missed a lot of things I wish I hadn't in the last 15 months because of this injury but today is, by far, the one I wish I had been there for the most. He has brought out the best in me as both an ally and a foe. That's because he is THE best. When you're consistently that good for that long, you become undeniable. Sami becoming WWE Champion was inevitable. He has deserved that moment for a very long time and I am so happy to see him finally get it."

Owens accompanied his message with several photos documenting their friendship over the years, including a particularly touching moment from a hotel room in Texas when Zayn had to help him put on his socks and boxers after Owens thought he had broken his back.

Killer Kross, who competed in the backend of his WWE run against Zayn in three consecutive singles matches, was captured on video reacting to news of Zayn's title win by jumping up in excitement and yelling "Finally!"

Triple H, WWE's Chief Creative Officer, posted a congratulatory message on social media stating: "From underdog to undisputed. Congrats to the NEW WWE Undisputed Champion Sami Zayn! Never say never." The message included photos and video of Triple H with Zayn backstage after the match, with Zayn holding the Undisputed WWE Championship.

However, not everyone celebrated Zayn's achievement. Val Venis took to social media to express his displeasure with the title change, saying both Zayn and Rhodes would have been beneath legendary figures like Hulk Hogan, The Rock, Steve Austin, The Undertaker, and Triple H. Venis claimed that Triple H had "lowered the bar to dumpster on fire levels so the less fortunate could get a shot." He further attacked Zayn in a separate post, calling him "a communist who could not lace my boots on his best day."

THE MAIN EVENT MATCH ITSELF

The Triple Threat Match was pure chaos from start to finish. Rhodes and Zayn initially worked together to neutralize Gunther early on, but the Austrian Athlete soon took control of the match. The action spilled to the outside, with Rhodes sending Zayn crashing through the announce table. Gunther capitalized on the chaos moments later, delivering two brutal powerbombs onto the announce desk, with the second sending Rhodes crashing through in spectacular fashion.

Throughout the contest, both Zayn and Rhodes repeatedly pulled the referee out of the ring to break pin attempts, showing they would do whatever necessary to win or survive. Gunther demonstrated his dominance by locking Rhodes in a sleeper hold, nearly forcing the champion into submission before Zayn intervened to save his rival from losing consciousness.

The closing sequence saw Rhodes land a double Cross Rhodes on both Gunther and Zayn simultaneously, nearly retaining his championship. When Rhodes attempted a second Cross Rhodes on Zayn, however, Zayn countered at the last second and rolled him up for the pinfall victory. The entire match was a physical war that had all three competitors pushing themselves to their limits.

OBA FEMI CROWNED KING OF THE RING

In the opening match of Night of Champions, Oba Femi won the King of the Ring tournament finals, defeating Jey Uso in approximately ten minutes. Femi, known as "The Ruler," kicked out of multiple top rope splashes from Uso and powered out of Uso's sleeper hold before finishing with his Fall From Grace sit-out powerbomb.

With this victory, Femi earned the right to challenge for a world championship at SummerSlam 2026, scheduled for August 1 and 2 at U.S. Bank Stadium in Minneapolis, Minnesota. The decision of which champion Femi will face at SummerSlam remains to be announced, with the possibilities being either the newly crowned Undisputed WWE Champion Sami Zayn or World Heavyweight Champion Roman Reigns. According to reports, Femi's decision may be revealed on the June 29 episode of Monday Night Raw in Atlantic City, New Jersey.

Jey Uso, despite losing the match, later took to Instagram Stories claiming that Femi had tapped out when he had him locked in the sleeper hold, though he was referring to when Femi banged his arm on the mat while attempting to power out of the submission hold, which was clearly not an actual tap-out. This loss came after Roman Reigns had tasked Uso with winning the King of the Ring Tournament and bringing the Undisputed WWE Championship back to The Bloodline, making Uso's failure significant within the ongoing faction storyline.

IYO SKY BECOMES QUEEN OF THE RING

In the Queen of the Ring finals, IYO SKY defeated WWE Women's World Champion Liv Morgan to become the 2026 Queen of the Ring. The match took an interesting turn before the opening bell when Danhausen appeared on stage and cursed Morgan before the match got underway, essentially telegraphing the winner given Danhausen's established success rate with curses.

Immediately following her victory, Sky made an in-ring announcement declaring her intentions, directly stating to Morgan: "I'll see you at SummerSlam." This confirmed that the first official SummerSlam match will be a rematch between Sky and Morgan for the Women's World Championship, despite the immediate back-to-back losses for the champion.

SETH ROLLINS DEFEATS BRON BREAKKER IN STEEL CAGE MATCH

Seth Rollins successfully concluded his feud with Bron Breakker by winning a brutal steel cage match at Night of Champions. The two men, who had been locked in an intense rivalry throughout 2026, had split their previous three singles encounters, with Rollins defeating Breakker in a singles match on June 1 and in five street fights during WWE's European summer tour, while Breakker had won their Backlash encounter on May 9.

The cage match was filled with weapons, including chairs, kendo sticks, and tables that both men brought into the ring. The finish came when Breakker gave Rollins a moment of respect by holding up his hand and telling him to stop, but once both men stared at each other, Rollins capitalized by hitting a devastating Stomp off the second rope to secure the victory.

In his post-match interview, Rollins reflected on the emotional weight of finally putting away Breakker and the struggles he has faced since returning from injury. "It was a fight out there man. It was a war. That's what I expected to be honest with you. There's a lot that can be said about Bron. There's a lot I've said and a lot I will say but what I will say tonight is that I hoped that he will be when I brought him into The Vision. He was a worthy opponent, a hell of a warrior, he went out on his shield there."

Rollins admitted that landing the finishing blow brought a sense of relief more than celebration, especially given the struggles he faced since returning from injury earlier in 2026. "When I got that super stomp on him, I think mostly a relief, you know, to feel like I have finally finished the job. I have finally put The Vision behind me. It's been a tough road man. It didn't get off to a good start at WrestleMania. This comeback has been very difficult for me. It wasn't straightforward."

He explained that rebuilding momentum after injury required patience, matches on RAW, and the right support system around him as he regained confidence. "You just want to bam bam bam get right to it. It just hadn't been that for me. Took me getting my feet under me, having the right people check me at the right time. When I hit that stomp and I got that cover it was a real relief, like, I can breathe a little."

With The Vision seemingly behind him and a major victory over Breakker secured, Rollins now awaits to see which direction his career takes next, noting that his potential SummerSlam match against World Heavyweight Champion Roman Reigns depends on Oba Femi's decision regarding which champion he will challenge.

TRICK WILLIAMS RETAINS US TITLE, LASH LEGEND WEDDING

Trick Williams successfully retained his WWE United States Championship against Ricky Saints at Night of Champions. The match featured controversial moments when Saints attempted to deceive the referee by making it appear that Lil Yachty had struck him with a kendo stick, when Saints had actually hit the steel steps with the stick before tossing it to Yachty. This deception led to Yachty being ejected from ringside.

Williams finished the match by hitting his Trick Shot knee strike finisher to retain the title. After the match, Yachty returned to ringside and helped Williams fend off a post-match attack from Saints. The segment concluded with Yachty delivering his version of The People's Elbow to Saints.

During the Night of Champions post-show, Williams opened up about his real-life wedding to fellow WWE Superstar Lash Legend, which took place just days before the event on June 20, 2026, in Jamaica at The Trident Castle in Portland. "Hey, look, man, it was crazy. No lie. We was in a castle on the Portland called The Trident Castle. I had 120 guests, family, friends. Boat was there. It was beautiful. Most importantly, my queen, the Legend herself, had the wedding of her dreams. I'm just glad that I can bring that into fruition. It was a special day for us, man. I'm still on cloud nine."

Williams and Legend had started dating in 2023 before getting engaged in late 2025. The couple officially tied the knot this month, making Williams' title retention and subsequent celebration part of a larger personal milestone.

TIFFANY STRATTON RETAINS WOMEN'S US CHAMPIONSHIP

Tiffany Stratton successfully defended her Women's United States Championship against Jade Cargill at Night of Champions. The match featured multiple run-ins, with Michin and B-Fab interfering on behalf of Cargill, while Chelsea Green emerged to even the odds for Stratton. Charlotte Flair also made an appearance during the match.

The finish came when Cargill attempted to use the U.S. title belt as a weapon, but Flair intercepted her and hit Cargill with the title instead. This allowed Stratton to hit the Prettiest Moonsault Ever for the victory. This was Stratton's third successful title defense since defeating Giulia for the championship on April 24, 2026.

PIPER NIVEN CRITICIZES JADE CARGILL'S TECHNIQUE

After the match, Piper Niven, who has been recovering from neck surgery since August 2025, critiqued Cargill's execution of the Black Hole Slam on Stratton during their match. Niven responded to Cargill's social media post celebrating the move, writing: "5/10 a bit sloppy, you didn't transfer her weight onto your chest so you never really had her and that's why she slipped out and you got that double bump effect and her head verberated on the mat."

Niven went further, sarcastically suggesting that Cargill cared more about how she looked than whether her opponent was injured: "But you looked great, and who cares who you hurt so long as you're alright, right?" She later followed up with another facetious post, thanking Cargill for paying homage to her and keeping her moves alive, while adding that once she relearns how to use her right arm, she will show Cargill how to properly transfer weight to get the pin.

When someone on social media challenged Niven's claim that the move was hers, she pushed back by pointing out that Abyss, the original user of the move in TNA, is retired and that she personally got his approval to use it. She also made it clear that despite her injury, she does not consider herself retired, keeping herself relevant in locker room discussions.

GUNTHER GETS PHYSICAL WITH NICK ALDIS

After losing the main event, Gunther's frustration boiled over during the post-show as he confronted SmackDown General Manager Nick Aldis backstage. The Austrian grappler immediately began accusing Aldis of bias, telling him: "You screwed me over in Europe, you screwed me over here. You're jealous. You would love to be in my position. You're jealous because you are a failed wrestler yourself! You're miserable!"

Gunther poked his finger into Aldis' chest, prompting Aldis to shove him back while saying: "Get the f*** off of me." When Gunther grabbed at Aldis, WWE officials and security had to intervene and separate the two men before the altercation could escalate further.

Gunther's grievances stemmed from his previous losses in the WWE Championship picture. He had believed he was robbed at Clash in Italy when his foot was on the ropes, and then again on SmackDown when Sami Zayn's involvement as a special referee cost him another title opportunity. The physical confrontation with Aldis has fueled speculation about a potential future storyline between the two, with some wrestling observers suggesting this could lead to a match at SummerSlam if Aldis returns to in-ring competition, though the former NWA World Heavyweight Champion has not competed for WWE since becoming on-screen General Manager in 2023.

WWE has faced similar production challenges during previous Saudi Arabia broadcasts, making this a recurring concern that needs addressing before the company's flagship WrestleMania event takes place there next year.

The upcoming broadcast will air this Sunday at 11 ET/10 CT on A&E as part of WWE's Greatest Moments.

Rhodes was a major name in pro wrestling before he went to work for WWF, giving him the "Common Man" gimmick highlighted by yellow polka-dotted attire. Rhodes went on to film vignettes where he took on other blue-collar roles before he was eventually recognized by others who said, "Hey! Aren't you...?" Lady Shani has returned to AAA on Fox after nearly six months of absence. Last seen wrestling in January 2026, the 33-year-old wrestler made her television return on the June 27 episode. Following Lola Vice's victory over La Hiedra in the main event, Las Toxicas attacked Vice and La Catalina until Lady Shani made her dramatic comeback, evening the numbers game by joining forces with Vice and Catalina to take down the group. Shani was previously the AAA Reina de Reinas Champion and will look to continue her comeback against Lady Flammer in a non-title match on the July 4 episode.

The next episode of AAA on Fox on July 4, 2026, features several significant announcements made by the promotion's new general manager Rey Mysterio. In addition to Lady Shani's in-ring return against Lady Flammer, a singles match between El Fiscal and Abismo Negro has been announced. Most notably, Mysterio announced the arrival of a former WWE World Champion who will be appearing on the show, though the wrestler's identity was not revealed in the announcements.

Although a specific date has not been officially set, Mysterio made the announcement during the June 27 episode of AAA on Fox. Just 29 days into his title reign, this championship defense on NXT will mark Vikingo's first televised title defense. The announcement came after WWE NXT General Manager Robert Stone previously stated that AAA luchadores would be appearing at the NXT Great American Bash Premium Live Event on June 28, 2026, though no specific names were revealed at that time.

AAA has officially announced the return of Copa Bardahl for TripleMania 34.
