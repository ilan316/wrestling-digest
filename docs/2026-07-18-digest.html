🔥 AEW Dynamite Viewership and Programming

AEW DYNAMITE VIEWERSHIP AND PROGRAMMING

As All Elite Wrestling builds toward its major summer pay-per-views, the promotion is juggling multiple storylines across weekly programming with mixed creative reception. The Redemption event scheduled for July 26 in Montreal features several championship matches headlined by Kenny Omega defending the AEW World Championship against Kevin Knight, though reports indicate this match was planned long-term despite the surprise nature of the announcement. Additional title defenses include Mark Davis against Andrade El Idolo for the National Championship, Kyle Fletcher against Bandido for the International Championship, Thekla against Willow Nightingale for the Women's World Championship, and a World Tag Team Championship bout between champions Adam Copeland and Christian Cage facing Claudio Castagnoli and PAC of the Death Riders. The card also features Chris Jericho in a non-title match against Tommaso Ciampa, with a pre-show celebrating the Rougeau wrestling dynasty.

The Don Callis Family storyline continues to generate intrigue as multiple competitors are targeting the faction's championships. Speedball Mike Bailey, speaking on his YouTube vlog during AEW Dynamite Beach Break, expressed his intention to take gold away from the Don Callis Family and capture titles for himself. Bailey previously challenged Mark Davis for the National Championship on AEW Collision but was unsuccessful, with Andrade El Idolo earning the next shot at Davis at Redemption. Bailey appears to be positioned toward a collision course with Kevin Knight for a shot at the TNT Championship. Knight executed a heel turn at Double or Nothing, attacking Darby Allin after Allin's World Championship loss to MJF, and has since aligned with Don Callis. The former tag team partners have engaged in several segments since Knight's turn in early June, setting the stage for a future championship program.

However, creative direction for Redemption has drawn criticism from observers. One analyst noted that while the weekly Dynamite shows feature strong in-ring wrestling, the overall direction feels disjointed with big matches announced at the last minute and major storyline decisions lacking context. The criticism particularly centered on Will Ospreay's title shot at All In being built around his relationship with Jon Moxley, the trainer who injured Ospreay's neck the previous year, rather than focusing on Ospreay's immediate future. Kenny Omega's title match against Kevin Knight on Dynamite was cited as an unusual placement for such a significant bout, especially considering it essentially sets up the Redemption title match on a secondary PPV rather than a major event. Additionally, a major title defense between Claudio Castagnoli and PAC versus the World Tag Team Champions was announced via digital exclusive after Dynamite concluded rather than being promoted during television programming.

Hangman Page's return from a three-month absence received particular criticism for its handling. Tony Khan announced Page's return during the main event of WWE's SmackDown on a Friday, described as the worst possible timing for the announcement. When Page returned to AEW television and cut a vague promo mentioning he might pursue other titles, no follow-up was provided during the subsequent Wednesday Dynamite episode, with zero mention of his comments or future direction.

Bryan Danielson's extended absence from AEW television has been explained by Tony Khan as a health and recovery situation rather than a creative decision. During a charity livestream, Khan revealed that Danielson's body requires more time to recover from the physical toll the wrestling business has taken. Rather than bringing Danielson in for commentary or occasional appearances, Khan emphasized that keeping him off the road is critical for his recuperation. Khan explained that even sitting behind the commentary desk and moving his head around a monitor is physically taxing for Danielson. The strategy of allowing Danielson to remain home with his family rather than traveling each week is designed to provide optimal recovery conditions. Khan stated that when Danielson is pain-free and able to travel more comfortably, he will return to television. Danielson last competed at WrestleDream 2024, where he lost the AEW World Championship to Jon Moxley. While he has not officially announced retirement, recurring neck and spinal issues have created uncertainty about his wrestling future.

Kyle Fletcher's path to capturing the AEW International Championship came with significant physical challenges. Fletcher defeated Konosuke Takeshita at the Beach Break edition of Dynamite to win the title and has already defended it once against Komander on the July 15 Dynamite, with another defense scheduled against Bandido at Redemption. However, the match took place in extreme humidity conditions in Clearwater, Florida. Fletcher documented the day on his Protolog YouTube channel, expressing stress about competing against one of the world's best wrestlers in oppressive heat that left him sweating before entering the ring. He called the venue perhaps the hottest place he has ever wrestled, noting the difficulty of the environmental conditions. After his victory, Fletcher expressed gratitude for his opportunity while acknowledging the physical toll of the extreme heat and humidity.

During his July 2 match against El Phantasmo on Collision, Fletcher experienced what appeared to be a serious injury concern, as he was seen on all fours under the ring while medical personnel poured water on his head attempting to cool him down. However, Fletcher clarified that he was actually experiencing heat-related stress rather than an actual injury. He explained to doctors that the episode was an accumulation of stress, fatigue, not eating enough, and not drinking enough water, resulting in his body's involuntary response of dry heaving. Given the humidity forecast for the Takeshita match and it being his first singles match back from injury, Fletcher was concerned the same situation might occur again. During and immediately after his match with Takeshita, he experienced moments where he felt the involuntary reflux sensation again but was able to push through both instances. After consulting with AEW doctors, Fletcher indicated that his recent reactions might be more psychological than indicative of any physical problem.

Will Ospreay continues to prepare for what he describes as the biggest match of his career facing Kenny Omega at All In at Wembley Stadium. Ospreay departed New Japan Pro Wrestling after eight years with the company, a promotion where he had achieved his dream of being a contracted wrestler in 2016 by turning down a WWE CWC tournament opportunity. In 2023, Ospreay decided it was time to move to the next stage of his career, partly motivated by his personal situation. Having begun dating Alex Windsor and becoming a father to her son, Ospreay wanted to be more present and there for his family. AEW provided the opportunity to move forward in his career while maintaining connections to Japan. When Ospreay informed Gedo of his intention to leave, Gedo initially opposed the departure but eventually agreed to allow his final match to be a cage match with his best mates in United Empire.

That final NJPW match became notable for an unexpected injury during its production. During the cage match, Gedo threw a table Sabu-style that accidentally struck HENARE in the head, creating a significant gash. With spots already planned that required HENARE's participation, Ospreay had to communicate changes to the other competitors while HENARE was being attended to by trainers. HENARE eventually returned to finish the match, though he subsequently went to the hospital. The group later reconvened for dinner together. Kenny Omega publicly defended Ospreay on social media after a fan posted an inappropriate comparison between Ospreay and late political activist Charlie Kirk, with the comment also mocking Ospreay's career-threatening neck injury. Omega responded critically to the post, stating it was inappropriate to mock a dear friend battling back from a career-threatening injury while comparing him to someone who was murdered, arguing that such comparisons should be kept separate from jokes.

Rhodes lost the TNT Championship to Kyle Fletcher in a Street Fight on the July 31, 2025 edition of Collision and underwent surgery following the match. He stated his intention to return soon for what he humorously called his "One Last Ride," a farewell run that has already lasted seven years. Rhodes emphasized that despite his decades-long career, becoming a World Champion remains his number one goal and primary motivation. He expressed confidence that he remains capable of achieving this goal, stating "I don't think its too late for me, because I am damn good at what I do and will continue to strive for that." 🔥 Willow Nightingale Travel Issues

Willow Nightingale was set to make her return to Tokyo Joshi Pro Wrestling this weekend, but unexpected travel problems have changed those plans. A last-minute issue forced the AEW star to pull out of Summer Sun Princess, leaving fans waiting longer for her anticipated return to Japan. The change came just days after Nightingale regained momentum in AEW, making the timing of the setback particularly unfortunate.

TJPW confirmed that Nightingale would no longer compete at Summer Sun Princess on July 18 at Korakuen Hall after the cancellation of her scheduled flight to Japan. The promotion explored alternative travel arrangements but could not get her to the venue before the event began. In its statement, TJPW said, "Willow Nightingale, who was scheduled to compete in 'SUMMER SUN PRINCESS '26'… has had her flight to Japan cancelled. We have been working to arrange an alternative flight, but it has become difficult for her to arrive in time for the start of the event, so she will unfortunately have to withdraw."

The promotion announced that Chris Brookes would make an emergency appearance and face Mizuki in a singles match instead. Brookes, a veteran of the Japanese wrestling scene, has previously competed for TJPW and other major promotions in Japan. He first competed in TJPW at Grand Princess in March 2024, teaming with Emi Sakura in a loss to Magical Sugar Rabbits. Brookes has been a fixture in Japanese wrestling since 2019, signed to DDT Pro-Wrestling and bringing his creativity to various shows. Standing at six feet four inches and weighing 207 pounds, Brookes has held championships in many promotions he has graced with his presence, including being a two-time DDT Universal and Extreme Champion, a six-time Ironman Heavymetalweight Champion, and the current KO-D Tag Team Champion alongside Harashima.

Nightingale later addressed the situation herself, explaining that she had done everything possible to make the trip. "I tried to exhaust every option, but a flight issue in the middle of my travel day has prevented me from making it to Tokyo in time. I'm sorry to fans who were looking forward to our match. Heck, I was really looking forward to it but I still want to face Mizuki one day!" she wrote on social media.

Following the announcement, Mizuki took to social media to express hope that the match with Nightingale can still happen in the future, tweeting that she misses Willow and hopes to face her again. Nightingale echoed those sentiments, with both wrestlers clearly disappointed about missing the opportunity but optimistic about rescheduling their encounter.

The cancelled match would have marked Nightingale's first TJPW appearance since defeating Miu Watanabe at Grand Princess 2025. While this chapter has been delayed, it doesn't appear to be closed, as both Nightingale and Mizuki have expressed interest in facing each other in the future.

Despite missing the Japan event, Nightingale still has a major opportunity ahead. After returning from a shoulder injury on the July 8 edition of AEW Dynamite, she earned an AEW Women's World Championship match against Thekla at AEW Redemption on July 26. The match takes place in Montreal and could impact the road to All In, where Mercedes Mone is set to challenge whoever the champion is. Nightingale had been forced to vacate the TBS Championship and withdraw from the Owen Hart Tournament due to her shoulder injury before her recent return, making the upcoming championship opportunity a significant moment in her career.

Summer Sun Princess takes place on July 18 from Korakuen Hall and streams live on Wrestle Universe.

Darby Allin Fan Appreciation

Before competing for indie promotion 1FW on Friday night, AEW star Darby Allin helped bring joy to a longtime wrestling fan with special needs named Buddy Divins. Divins believed he was simply visiting a news studio when Allin surprised him there, creating an unforgettable moment captured by 11Alive News in Georgia.

Allin met Divins and presented him with tickets to the 1FW Summer Stage 2 event scheduled for July 17. The AEW star then took the surprise further by inviting Divins to visit his home for a private tour. According to 11Alive's Kaitlyn Ross, the meaningful experience extended well beyond the television cameras.

"The story didn't end when the cameras stopped rolling," Ross wrote. "Darby invited Buddy back to his home, where he showed him his practice wrestling ring, his skate park and his ATVs. There were no interviews. No cameras. No audience. Just two people spending time together."

Allin's motivation for creating this meaningful experience stemmed from his own life experiences. Ross explained that Allin knows what it feels like to grow up searching for a place to belong, which is why he wants every fan who walks into a wrestling show to feel welcome. 🔥 Former TNA Star Shelly Martinez Departure

Former TNA Knockout Shelly Martinez has revealed the difficult circumstances that led to her departure from the company, shedding light on the financial hardships that some wrestlers faced while performing on national television. Speaking on her Too Much Information podcast, Martinez responded to comments made by Awesome Kong on Dark Side of the Ring, where Kong discussed the notoriously low pay received by members of TNA's Knockouts division.

Martinez explained that Kong's story resonated deeply because the two women had regularly discussed their financial struggles while working together in TNA. "Kong and I go way back, and we were able to reconnect by working with each other regularly at TNA. We would talk about these things. We were two broke-ass bitches just trying," Martinez recalled. She stressed that the financial crisis extended far beyond just herself and Kong. "We weren't the only ones. It was rough. Going back to why I quit—to refresh the listeners and viewers out there—I was just struggling."

The physical toll of her poverty became a daily reality for Martinez. She described the routine humiliation of having her utilities shut off. "I constantly was getting things shut off in the place I was living. The gas would be shut off a lot, so I had to take cold showers. I was grateful to be able to take a shower, but it's rough when that shower is cold and there's no temperature." These conditions were the backdrop against which she worked for TNA, appearing in matches and representing one of the company's featured acts while simultaneously facing the threat of homelessness.

The immediate catalyst for Martinez's departure came when she received an opportunity that seemed like a potential lifeline. An independent film production offered her a role that would only require approximately one week of her time. Martinez planned to use the money from this gig to pay her rent, which was coming due with an eviction notice already looming. Her TNA contract included a promised $100 increase if the company renewed her for another year, but that renewal was not yet finalized. To Martinez, every dollar mattered for her survival.

Martinez reached out to TNA official Terry Taylor with a proposal. She explained her situation and asked if TNA could write her character off television temporarily so she could complete the film. "I told him, 'Look, I've got this gig coming up. I need it to pay my rent so I don't get kicked out. Can I just go through a table at the pay-per-view, and then I'll be out for TV so I can go do this movie?'" Taylor indicated they would discuss the matter when Martinez arrived at the next event, which she interpreted as a rejection of her request.

Facing this setback, Martinez offered an alternative solution. She would abandon the film opportunity if TNA would provide the $100 contractual increase ahead of schedule. "I said, 'Look, I'll flake on this gig if you give me my $100 bump now.'" This modest request—for a one hundred dollar advance on money already promised to her—was denied by the company. With no other options available, Martinez had no choice but to leave TNA.

The decision to quit was not driven by anger, ego, or a loss of passion for wrestling. Martinez emphasized that it was born from panic and the raw necessity of survival. "That's why I quit. It was out of fear. It was out of, 'What am I going to do?' I needed something that would buy me another month to figure it out," she explained. She was counting on the film money to pay her rent because she was already receiving an eviction notice. Without either the film income or the early contract increase, she had no way to keep a roof over her head.

"That's when I said, 'Well, then I'm going to have to quit because I was counting on that money from the film to pay my rent, because I was already getting an eviction notice.' I had to have that money. I felt like if I got at least that bump, I could go to the landlord and be like, 'Look, I just got a raise.' I had something to work with."

Martinez's situation represents a stark reality for professional wrestlers, even those working at the national television level. While fans saw her performing in physical matches and featuring prominently in TNA programming, she was simultaneously one month away from losing her home. Her exit was not a dramatic wrestling storyline or a character development. It was a real-life financial emergency that forced an impossible choice.

Beyond the immediate circumstances of her departure, Martinez has carried emotional wounds from her TNA experience that have persisted for years. She has acknowledged ongoing hurt regarding her relationship with company owner Dixie Carter. During her time at TNA, Martinez always sensed that Carter did not like her, but she never understood the reason for this apparent animosity. "With Dixie Carter, I always felt she didn't like me, and I didn't understand why. It bothered me. I really should be like, 'So what? You don't like me,' and then move on."

The situation was complicated by the fact that Martinez was in survival mode during her TNA tenure. She was still dealing with the heartbreak of her WWE departure while struggling financially, which meant she was not always aware of everything happening backstage. She either missed much of what was occurring behind the scenes or only heard fragmented accounts through whispers and gossip. However, Carter's apparent dislike of her was direct and unmistakable enough that it made an impression.

The Dixie Carter situation became what Martinez describes as a lasting wrestling wound. "For some reason, that Dixie wound remained for so long. I'm better with it now, for sure, but at the same time, I still want to know." She hopes that reviewing the Dark Side of the Ring series examining TNA's history might provide answers and context that would allow her to finally achieve closure on this chapter of her life.

Martinez made it clear that her goal is not to maintain anger or resentment toward Carter indefinitely. Instead, she wants to reach a place of emotional peace where Carter is simply another former boss from a previous chapter in her professional life. "I want to be able to look at Dixie and just be like, 'That was a woman I used to work with.' Boom. But that doesn't happen. I'd really like to get to that place, and I'm almost there."

The emotional impact of the Carter situation has proven more durable than Martinez expected or desired. "No matter how much I've evolved in not caring about Dixie, it still does affect me. Every time I see her on my feed, one way or another, I get triggered. I don't want that." She is working toward healing from this emotional response but acknowledges that she is not yet at her destination.

Despite her personal feelings toward Carter, Martinez acknowledged that the company owner did bring something of value to TNA. She believes Carter's involvement helped the company grow during her tenure. However, Martinez also noted that Carter lacked the wrestling expertise necessary to maintain the company's success long-term. "Whether you like her or not—and I'm a person who does not like her—I'm here to say her involvement still did do something positive for that company."

Martinez's story serves as a sobering reminder of the disparity that can exist between a wrestler's visibility on television and their actual financial circumstances. It also highlights the potential human cost when companies prioritize contractual technicalities over the immediate survival needs of their talent. For Martinez, her journey from TNA includes not only the lasting impact of financial desperation but also unresolved emotional wounds that continue to affect her years after her departure.
