🔥 Becky Lynch Offers Legal Help to MJF

On the April 15, 2026 episode of AEW Dynamite, MJF suffered a shocking loss of his AEW World Championship to Darby Allin. The match was remarkably brief, lasting under five minutes as Allin connected with a Scorpion Death Drop followed by four Coffin Drops before securing victory with a headlock pin. What made the loss even more controversial was that MJF was reportedly unaware he even had a title defense scheduled that night. He was forced to compete or lose the title by default, leaving him frustrated with how the situation was handled.

Following his defeat, MJF took to Instagram to air his grievances. In his social media rant, he blamed multiple individuals for his loss, including AEW President Tony Khan, Bryan Danielson, referee Aubrey Edwards, Renee Paquette, and Darby Allin himself. MJF maintained that he had been cheated out of his championship reign and demanded justice for what he viewed as an unfair circumstance.

Becky Lynch, a WWE performer, responded to MJF's post in a way that caught the attention of wrestling fans everywhere. Staying true to her on-screen character, Lynch replied with a supportive message that included humor: "Let me know, I'll put you in contact with the BEST lawyers!" The comment stood out as a notable moment of camaraderie between employees of rival wrestling companies.

The friendship between Lynch and MJF runs deeper than kayfabe. The two met while working together on the film Happy Gilmore 2, where Lynch discovered that MJF, contrary to her expectations, was actually very kind and talented in person. MJF has also publicly praised Lynch in the past, saying "If you don't like Becky Lynch, go f*ck yourself" just months before this exchange. This existing relationship explains why Lynch felt comfortable engaging in friendly banter with MJF during his moment of frustration.

The situation highlights how professional wrestling personalities can maintain genuine friendships despite working for competing organizations. While MJF continues to demand a rematch and justice for his title loss, with no official rematch yet announced by AEW, Lynch's public show of support has only added to the ongoing drama surrounding the AEW World Championship picture. Whether her offer of legal help is purely friendly banter or a sign of something larger remains to be seen, but the interaction demonstrates that rivalries in the wrestling business sometimes take a backseat to personal relationships.

DARBY ALLIN GETS TOUGH ASSIGNMENT FOR FIRST TITLE DEFENSE ON 4/22 AEW DYNAMITE IN PORTLAND

The main event for next week's AEW Dynamite show has been announced, and it's a big one. In what will be his first official title defense following his shocking AEW World Championship squash match victory over MJF this past Wednesday night in Everett, Washington, newly crowned AEW World Champion Darby Allin is gearing up for a "Psycho Killer" in Portland.

All Elite Wrestling officially announced that Darby Allin will be defending his AEW World Championship against Tommaso Ciampa on the April 22 episode of AEW Dynamite at the Veterans Memorial Coliseum in Portland, Oregon.

The 4/22 episode of AEW Dynamite airs live at 8/7c on TBS and HBO Max. Also scheduled for this card are Mina Shirakawa vs. Hikaru Shida and Will Ospreay vs. Mark Davis.

This Wednesday, April 22, AEW will broadcast another episode coast-to-coast. Fans are encouraged to set their DVRs for 8e/5p on TBS Network and Sports on Max for Wednesday Night AEW Dynamite live from Portland, Oregon.

Wardlow AEW Return

Wardlow says his long-awaited return to the ring may finally be close after more than two years away from consistent competition. The AEW powerhouse has been sidelined since March 2024, with only a brief appearance at AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door in August 2025. Reports later suggested he suffered a torn pectoral muscle, adding to a series of setbacks that also included a serious car accident.

Speaking with BCP+, Wardlow didn't hide how difficult the time away has been. "I miss wrestling so much I can't even put into words. It's been over two years since I've wrestled. So it has been a rough two years." Despite the extended absence, Wardlow made it clear that his recovery is nearing its end and that a return is within reach.

"I am so excited to get back to wrestling in front of a live crowd… And we're very, very close to being ready," Wardlow stated. He also promised that fans should expect a different version of him when he steps back into the ring. "We are most definitely going to come back the biggest baddest version of Wardlow that anybody's ever seen."

Wardlow's comments suggest that his return is not just a possibility but an imminent reality. After dealing with injuries and stalled momentum, he now appears focused on reestablishing himself as one of AEW's most dominant physical forces. His absence has been significant, especially considering how quickly he rose through the ranks before being sidelined.

A strong return could immediately position him back into a prominent role, particularly if AEW leans into his history as a destructive, hard-hitting performer. In a roster filled with established names and rising stars, Wardlow's comeback presents an opportunity to reintroduce a powerhouse presence that AEW has been missing. If he delivers on his promise, his return could shift the landscape and reinsert him into the conversation as a top-tier threat.

AEW New PPV Introduction 2026

AEW appears poised to expand its pay-per-view offerings in 2026 with the introduction of a new event that could reshape the company's annual calendar. According to recent reports, AEW has trademarked the name "AEW Redemption," and insider Andrew Zarian has indicated that this new property could be earmarked for a future slot on the company's growing roster of televised spectacles.

While official confirmation regarding a specific date or location has not yet been announced, early speculation within wrestling circles points to a potential summer debut for the event. The timing appears particularly strategic given AEW's current 2026 schedule, which features Double or Nothing in May, Forbidden Door in June, and All In scheduled for August. This layout creates a notable opening in July that has gone unused by the promotion in recent years, making it a logical candidate for the new event's inaugural running.

The July window represents a significant opportunity, as AEW has notably avoided scheduling major events during June and July over the past two years. If the company moves forward with launching AEW Redemption as a brand-new property, this would mark a notable departure from that recent trend and demonstrate a willingness to experiment with its calendar structure.

There remains another possibility worth considering regarding the "Redemption" trademark. Some speculation suggests that the name could serve as a rebrand for one of AEW's existing late-year events, such as All Out, WrestleDream, Full Gear, or Worlds End. However, sources have indicated that this scenario is considered unlikely at this current juncture, with a brand-new event appearing to be the more probable direction.

Should AEW Redemption come to fruition as an entirely new pay-per-view offering, it would represent a significant milestone for the promotion. This expansion would bring AEW's annual pay-per-view total to ten events for the first time in the company's history, reflecting the organization's continued growth and ambitions in the professional wrestling landscape.
