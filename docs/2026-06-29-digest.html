🔥 AEW Forbidden Door 2026 Event Coverage

AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door 2026 delivered a massive night of professional wrestling on June 28-29, 2026, from SAP Center in San Jose, California. The event featured nine main card matches across approximately three hours and 26 minutes of action, with an additional three matches on the Zero Hour Buy-In pre-show, bringing the total show time to nearly four hours even before the final match concluded.

The Owen Hart Foundation tournaments took center stage as the evening's primary focus. In the Men's tournament final, Will Ospreay defeated Swerve Strickland in a grueling 35-minute encounter that went on to become the longest match on the main card. By winning this tournament, Ospreay secured his path to challenging AEW World Champion MJF at AEW All In 2026, taking place from Wembley Stadium on Sunday, August 30. On the women's side, Mercedes Mone defeated Maya World in a 24-minute, 51-second match to win the Women's Owen Hart Foundation tournament, earning a title shot against AEW Women's World Champion Thekla at the same Wembley event.

The event's most talked-about stipulation match was the 12-man steel cage encounter dubbed "Death's Door," a round-shaped cage structure that replaced the traditional square design. The match pitted Team Mark Briscoe, consisting of Mark Briscoe, Orange Cassidy, Roderick Strong, Kyle O'Reilly, Konosuke Takeshita, and Darby Allin, against Team MJF, featuring MJF, Kevin Knight, Kyle Fletcher, Jake Doyle, Kazuchika Okada, and Andrade El Idolo. Team Briscoe emerged victorious in the chaotic 30-minute, 48-second match, with Mark Briscoe earning a title shot against MJF that he requested for the July 1st edition of AEW Dynamite.

AEW President Tony Khan revealed during the post-event media scrum that the innovative round cage was the brainchild of AEW Co-Executive Producer and Senior Production Executive Mike Mansury. Khan stated, "It was a very cool idea. It was Mike Mansury who asked about putting in a round steel cage. I thought it was a really cool concept so it came out really good and being at the Forbidden Door and being a deadly cage, we called it Death's Door." Khan expressed enthusiasm about bringing back the "Death's Door" stipulation for future major matches, calling it a successful debut for the unique cage structure.

The championship matches delivered strong action throughout the evening. AEW Women's World Champion Thekla successfully defended her title against Stardom's Starlight Kid in an 18-minute, 42-second bout that many observers considered one of the best women's matches ever on an AEW pay-per-view. After the match, Thekla and her Triangle of Madness stablemates took their disrespect further, cutting Starlight Kid's mask with scissors and spitting on it. Thekla then attacked Stardom President Taro Okada with the damaged mask, continuing her pattern of firing up Stardom with blatant disrespect for the Japanese promotion.

AEW Continental Champion Jon Moxley submitted challenger Bandido in an 18-minute, 10-second match that saw the champion turn a technical wrestling display into a bloody affair. Moxley tore at Bandido's mask throughout the match, eventually getting Bandido to pass out in a bulldog choke. The match represented a first-time singles encounter between the two competitors and delivered on the hype surrounding the dream matchup.

IWGP Global Heavyweight Champion Shota Umino successfully retained his title against PAC in an 18-minute, 11-second technical masterclass. The match featured a spectacular spot where PAC powerbombed Umino from the ring through a table on the outside, with AEW commentator Nigel McGuiness calling it a move that "damn near killed" Umino. Despite the devastating impact, Umino recovered to eventually overcome PAC with a Northern Lights Bomb, a Running Lariat, a Paradigm Shift, and a Second Chapter. Following the match, Umino's former trainer Jon Moxley entered the ring, but NJPW President Hiroshi Tanahashi made a surprise appearance to run off the Death Riders and celebrate Umino's successful title defense.

AEW World Tag Team Champions Adam Copeland and Christian Cage retained their titles in a 16-minute, 46-second match against The Dogs' David Finlay and Clark Connors. The match was marked by the shocking return of Jay White, who had been absent from AEW television for over a year following a broken hand suffered in March 2025. With Finlay about to attack Cage with a shillelagh, the lights went out in the arena. When they returned, Jay White stood in the ring alongside the Bang Bang Gang at the entrance. White immediately hit Finlay with the Blade Runner, allowing Copeland to follow with a spear and secure the three-count. This marked Cope and Cage's first tag team title defense together since 2001, when they held the WWF Tag Team Championship. White's return generated massive crowd reaction after months of anticipation fueled by social media teases.

Kenny Omega defeated Zack Sabre Jr. in nearly 26 minutes of technical wrestling supremacy that showcased their longstanding rivalry dating back to 2018. The match featured both men trading strikes, submissions, and counters in a classical display of technical wrestling. Omega performed several knee strikes to Sabre Jr.'s jaw but struggled to keep his opponent down. After the two men exchanged further strikes, Omega connected with a V-Trigger, setting up the One Winged Angel that Sabre Jr. initially countered. Omega managed to escape the submission hold and finally connected with the One Winged Angel for the victory, a match that many praised despite its placement early on the card.

The three-way tag team match opening the main card saw The Young Bucks defeat both Unbound Co. (Shingo Takagi and Titan) and El Sky Team (Mistico and Mascara Dorada) in 17 minutes and 43 seconds of international tag team action. All three teams demonstrated their high-flying and hard-hitting styles, with the Bucks ultimately prevailing by hitting the Meltzer Driver on Titan after a series of superkicks. The match featured multiple high spots including a quadruple superkick party, several shooting star presses from Dorada, and a double stomp from Titan that drew significant crowd reaction.

During the Zero Hour Buy-In pre-show, Divine Dominion retained the AEW Women's World Tag Team Championship against Thunder Rosa and Olympia from CMLL. In another Buy-In match, Maika defeated Skye Blue to advance to the TBS Championship Survival of the Fittest tournament on the following Monday. The opening Buy-In bout saw Drilla Moloney defeat Daniel Garcia despite outside interference from Marina Shafir.

The event drew 8,538 distributed tickets at SAP Center, with the cheapest ticket available priced at $37.40. The venue had hosted several WWE events in recent years, drawing significantly higher attendance figures, though AEW's cross-promotional offering still represented a solid draw for the market.

Hiroshi Tanahashi received special recognition during the evening when AEW President Tony Khan presented him with a custom guitar backstage at the event. The guitar was inscribed with a tribute reading, "Go Ace! Congratulations on your incredible career. From your friends at All Elite Wrestling." The gesture acknowledged Tanahashi's iconic in-ring air guitar pose that has become synonymous with his career. NJPW also shared the photograph on social media, reflecting on the present and the relationship between the two promotions.

Observers noted several criticisms of the event despite its overall high quality of in-ring action. Multiple commentators and wrestling journalists pointed out that the nearly five-hour runtime, even without the pre-show, created a marathon-like experience that potentially diminished the impact of earlier matches. By the time fans reached the final match, some of the high-quality encounters from earlier in the evening, like the Kenny Omega vs. Zack Sabre Jr. match, felt distant due to the cumulative time spent watching wrestling. Some wrestling analysts suggested that AEW should consider shorter matches or fewer overall matches on future pay-per-views to maintain better pacing and viewer retention.

The event also sparked discussion about the evolution of the "Forbidden Door" concept since its 2022 inception. Four years earlier, the inaugural Forbidden Door featured nearly every match as an AEW vs. NJPW encounter, with the term "forbidden" actually meaning something in terms of rare inter-promotional dream matches. By 2026, five of the nine main card matches featured only AEW competitors, with only three NJPW wrestlers appearing on the card despite NJPW being the primary partner. One Stardom wrestler and four CMLL wrestlers rounded out the international representation. This shift reflected how AEW had signed several NJPW free agents including Okada, Ospreay, White, Katsuyori Shibata, Gabe Kidd, David Finlay, and Clark Connors over the preceding years, reducing the novelty of seeing these wrestlers face off across company lines. Some industry observers suggested the relationship between AEW and NJPW had cooled somewhat due to ownership uncertainties and shifting priorities, particularly after Tony Khan heavily promoted the company's CMLL relationship during a pre-event media call.

Looking ahead, AEW All In 2026 at Wembley Stadium on August 30 will feature Will Ospreay challenging MJF for the AEW Championship and Mercedes Mone challenging Thekla for the AEW Women's Championship. Tony Khan indicated that additional matches would be announced on AEW Dynamite in the coming weeks, with speculation that bouts like The Divine Dominion vs. The Brawling Birds, Jon Moxley vs. Brody King, and Adam Copeland and Christian Cage defending the AEW World Tag Team Championship against The Young Bucks and FTR would likely be confirmed soon.

🔥 Will Ospreay Wins Owen Hart Foundation Men's Tournament

Will Ospreay Has Finally Captured the Owen Hart Foundation Men's Tournament

Will Ospreay has achieved one of the biggest moments of his professional wrestling career. The Aerial Assassin defeated Swerve Strickland in the main event of AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door 2026 to win the Men's Owen Hart Foundation Cup tournament. The event took place on June 28 at the Agganis Arena in Boston, Massachusetts, and PWMania reports the match also took place at the SAP Center in San Jose, California.

With this victory, Ospreay has earned himself a future AEW World Championship opportunity at AEW All In 2026, scheduled for August 30 at Wembley Stadium in London. This represents a monumental moment for the British wrestler, as he will have the chance to challenge for the title in his home country.

The match itself was one of the most violent and bloodied contests of the year. Ospreay and Strickland battled for the better part of an hour in a brutal affair that saw both competitors leave everything in the ring. The finish came when Ospreay executed the Tiger Driver 91, a move he had previously sworn never to use again. This finishing sequence finally kept Strickland down for the three count after numerous false finishes where both men had kicked out of each other's signature moves.

Throughout the contest, both men displayed their toughest work. Ospreay sported dried blood all over his face and had a dried-up wound, while Strickland's wound remained fresh until the very last moment of the match, leaving him with a bright crimson mask on his face. At one point, Strickland left behind a pool of blood outside the ring, leading AEW commentator Tony Schiavone to wonder what would happen if neither man could continue.

The finishing stretch included an array of high-impact maneuvers. Both men exchanged their finishers repeatedly, including Ospreay's Hidden Blade and Strickland's House Call. They also traded moves associated with other competitors, with Ospreay kicking out of Strickland's Deadeye and Strickland kicking out of Ospreay's Buckshot Hidden Blade. The Death Riders appeared ringside during the closing moments of the match, providing motivation to Ospreay similar to what one might see in an anime-style scenario.

Following his victory, Ospreay was elevated on the shoulders of Claudio Castagnoli and Jon Moxley of the Death Riders, and sealed the moment with a kiss with his real-life partner Alex Windsor. The ending provided a dramatic contrast to last year's Forbidden Door when the Death Riders had attacked and sidelined Ospreay. In 2026, the show concluded with Ospreay standing together with The Death Riders, arms in arms.

During the post-show media scrum, Ospreay reflected on the historic nature of his accomplishment. He stated that he now has the opportunity to become the first Englishman to win a World Championship on English soil. Ospreay emphasized the magnitude of what lies ahead, noting that he is fighting for a dream and for many kids who have wanted to be part of Wembley Stadium.

In a detailed interview following the match, Ospreay spoke to Dave Meltzer about the steps that led to this biggest moment of his career. He described several crucial milestones in his journey. Ospreay explained that while his first Super Juniors victory helped open the doorway to show who he was, the second time he won the tournament when he wrestled Shingo Takagi at Ryogoku made people start to see him as one of the top guys in the industry.

Ospreay reflected on 2019, the year AEW was founded, as a pivotal time in his career. He said he genuinely felt like he was the best wrestler in the world because he was operating on all cylinders. He was the only wrestler in his company that year to compete in the New Japan Cup, the Best of Super Juniors, the G1, the Junior Tag League, and the Super J-Cup in a single calendar year, though he admitted he has no idea how many matches he wrestled that year.

The second major step in Ospreay's ascension came when he stood in the ring with Bryan Danielson. He described having a genuine moment of realization when he understood that he had become one of the frontrunners in the industry. He referenced seeing AJ Styles leave the ring and Kenny Omega show up, moments where these elite competitors would stand together with Danielson and the crowd would lose their minds before they even did anything. Having that moment with Danielson made Ospreay feel he had truly arrived as a top-tier performer.

Ospreay stated that wrestling in a garden to reaching the point where he stood alongside these wrestling legends represented a profound realization. He said in that moment he thought, "Oh s**t, like I'm one of these guys now. I'm one of the front-runners. I'm one of the leaders. I'm one of the guys for the next generation to look at and to inspire towards." He acknowledged the weight of this responsibility but expressed confidence in his ability to carry it out, citing his personality, work ethic, and care for those around him.

Ospreay identified winning the Owen Hart Foundation tournament as the third and current major step in his career trajectory. He expressed his willingness to follow great matches and his belief that he can main-event shows consistently, ultimately claiming that he is the best wrestler in the world.

In his post-match comments, Ospreay thanked everyone who has supported him throughout his career journey. He expressed gratitude to his parents for buying him his first wrestling ring as a child and to all the backyard wrestling friends he has worked with over the years. He also thanked the various promotions that gave him opportunities, including Revolution Pro Wrestling, Pro-Wrestling: EVE, New Japan Pro-Wrestling, and AEW's Tony Khan.

The Owen Hart Foundation tournament has become one of AEW's biggest annual traditions since its inception in 2022 to honor the late Owen Hart. Adam Cole won the inaugural tournament in 2022, followed by Ricky Starks in 2023, Bryan Danielson in 2024, and Hangman Adam Page in 2025. Each winner has left their mark on AEW history, and now Ospreay joins this distinguished list.

All In London will take place on August 30, 2026, at Wembley Stadium. Current AEW World Champion MJF is set to defend the title against Mark Briscoe on the July 1st episode of AEW Dynamite, which will begin to set the stage for the championship landscape heading into All In. With Ospreay's historic opportunity to challenge for the title in his home country, speculation has begun about whether fans will see a new champion crowned at Wembley Stadium.

🔥 Mercedes Moné Wins Owen Hart Foundation Women's Tournament

Mercedes Moné Wins Women's Owen Hart Foundation Tournament For Second Consecutive Year

Mercedes Moné has done it again. The CEO defeated Maya World in the finals of the Women's Owen Hart Foundation Tournament at AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door 2026, which took place on June 28. The event was held at Agganis Arena in Boston, Massachusetts, according to some reports, though other sources indicated it was at the SAP Center in San Jose, California. Regardless of location, the result was definitive: Moné secured her second consecutive Owen Hart Cup victory and earned herself another shot at the AEW Women's World Championship.

This marks a historic accomplishment for Moné. She becomes the first woman to win the Owen Hart Foundation Tournament in back-to-back years. Previous winners of the tournament include Britt Baker in 2022, Willow Nightingale in 2023, Mariah May in 2024, and Moné herself in 2025. Now with her 2026 victory, Moné stands alone in that distinction.

The match between Moné and World was highly competitive and emotionally charged. Maya World had engineered a Cinderella run through the tournament, upsetting Athena to reach the finals. Her journey was made even more poignant by the recent unexpected passing of her brother, who was the reason she got into professional wrestling in the first place. World was added to the tournament after Sareee wasn't cleared medically, and she picked up wins over Skye Blue and Athena to advance.

From the opening bell, Moné controlled much of the early portion of the match, though World mounted occasional comeback rallies that didn't last long. Moné wrestled with spite, grabbing her opponent by the hair and throwing her around and outside of the ring. At one point, Moné got a count started with the crowd while the referee checked on World on the outside, reaching the count of 10 and arguably winning if the referee had been counting. However, she took matters into her own hands to dump World back in the ring.

As the match progressed, World found more openings with reversals and continued to pile those on. She executed a trifecta of fallaway slams, bridging through each time and sending Moné to the outside on the third. She brought Moné back in the ring and went up top to deliver a senton for a near-fall. World impressed the crowd with a series of big moves, including a moonsault that nearly got the job done. She also hit a sunset flip power bomb on the outside of the ring followed by another back in the ring for a nearfall.

The match featured several false finishes that made it seem like World could pull off the upset. Moné hit the Moné Maker and went for the cover, but World kicked out before the count of three. This kickout had fans exploding for Maya, and she kept pushing forward. World later trapped Moné in the Statement Maker submission hold, looking to make Moné tap out with her own move. Moné crawled to the bottom rope, with World using her foot to push back on the rope and roll back to the middle of the ring.

However, Moné reversed that into her own Statement Maker, which World escaped. The back-and-forth continued as both women countered with pin attempts. Ultimately, Moné hit an awkward looking backstabber followed by another Statement Maker, wrenching World's back further to force her to submit. With that, Mercedes Moné became the 2026 Women's Owen Hart Foundation Tournament winner, putting away the underdog babyface and securing back-to-back Owen Hart Cup victories.

After the match, Moné took to the podium at the post-show media scrum where she was asked about sharing the ring with wrestlers who grew up watching her, such as Maya. She expressed pride in World's performance. "Yeah, and you saw me teach her a lesson tonight, right? She learned today. It's honestly crazy for me and any time I think about it, this whole week I was a little emotional. I'm like, 'Another one that I get to wrestle? Another woman that has joined this beautiful, beautiful sport of professional wrestling, I get to wrestle them?' They're all over the world and I cannot believe what I've done in wrestling at such a young age and here I am still so young in my prime at 34 and I'm not stopping. I'm only getting greater so, it feels incredible and Maya, I'm so proud of her. I'm really, really proud of her. But damn, yeah, she learned. She learned a lot today."

Following her victory, Moné celebrated on the entrance stage with the Owen Hart Cup as the AEW All In logo appeared on the big screen behind her. She didn't stop there, however. The CEO wasted no time logging onto social media and engaging in several exchanges that demonstrated her confidence and combative spirit.

When Maya World posted a short message about what happened, writing "rookie mistake," Moné immediately quote-tweeted that and rubbed the win in her face. She responded with "HAHAHAHAHAHAHAJAJAJAJA....And now you know 🤫🏆 #2timeowencupwinner."

Moné also turned her attention to Willow Nightingale, who had shown support for Maya World by repeating an old message she had posted in 2025, where she told Maya that the world was hers. Willow posted in support of World, and Mercedes saw that and responded bluntly, telling her "STFU🤫".

Then Moné turned her attention to the current AEW Women's World Champion, Thekla. Thekla had posted a photo of herself with the title, and Mercedes responded with a simple warning: "🤫🏆". That one was easy to read. Mercedes has her All In title shot now, and Thekla is the woman holding the belt.

For some reason, Moné also brought Dave Meltzer into the mix, responding to something with "😝👏🤣 @davemeltzerWON 🤫🏆".

So Mercedes Moné won the Owen Hart Cup again, got her All In title shot, mocked Maya World, told Willow Nightingale to shut up, and stared down Thekla online. The target is now obvious.

With the tournament victory, Moné has earned a future AEW Women's World Championship match at AEW All In 2026. The event takes place Sunday, August 30, at Wembley Stadium in London, England. Moné will face current AEW Women's World Champion Thekla in what will be a highly anticipated matchup.

It's worth noting that Moné had her chance at the title last year when she challenged then-Women's World Champion Toni Storm at All In: Texas, but came up short in that encounter. This is her second straight Owen Hart tournament win, and now she gets another opportunity to finally win the AEW Women's World Championship. Some speculation has surfaced that AEW might book a three-way match if Willow Nightingale returns from her shoulder injury in time, but as it stands, the match is set as a one-on-one affair.

Moné comes into this title shot off a significant run around the world where she became a belt collector, holding 13 titles consecutively before losing those titles over the last six months. She was added to this year's tournament after Willow Nightingale's shoulder injury kept her sidelined, making Moné's return to AEW. She won over Alex Windsor and Hazuki to advance to the finals.

Now Moné will have another chance to complete her mission at All In 2026. She won the tournament again, she survived Maya World, and now Wembley Stadium and Thekla are waiting. The only thing left is whether she can finally turn that tournament win into the AEW Women's World Championship.

🔥 Mark Briscoe vs MJF Death's Door Steel Cage Match

Mark Briscoe has earned his shot at AEW World Champion MJF after his team's dominant victory in the 12-man steel cage war at AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door 2026. The match, held at the SAP Center in San Jose, California on June 28, was dubbed "Death's Door" and featured a new rounded cage design with a truss around the top that allowed wrestlers to work on the outside of the ring while providing a completely different visual than traditional cage matches.

The stipulation was straightforward: if Team Briscoe won, Mark Briscoe would earn an AEW World Championship match against MJF. The match itself was absolute chaos from start to finish, featuring an array of unconventional weapons and wild spots. The action broke down immediately with Konosuke Takeshita going after Kyle Fletcher and Kazuchika Okada, Jake Doyle throwing Darby Allin around the cage, and Mark Briscoe going straight for MJF. Despite the tension within his own team, Andrade El Idolo initially helped his team captain, though their egos caused friction.

Team Briscoe took advantage of their momentum by introducing various weapons into the match. The babyfaces brought out an original Nintendo Entertainment System, which was used in multiple attacks on Kyle Fletcher, including a clothesline by Kyle O'Reilly and strikes using a controller cord by Briscoe. TITLE CHANGES THROUGHOUT WRESTLING HISTORY

June 29th marks another significant date in professional wrestling history with 334 major title changes recorded on this day across various promotions. One of the most notable changes occurred in 1998 when Stone Cold Steve Austin won back the WWF Championship on RAW, just one day after dropping it to Kane in a First Blood match at the King of the Ring pay-per-view.

The earliest recorded June 29th title change dates back to 1973 when Kim Sung Ho won the NWA World Light Heavyweight Title. Blue Demon captured the NWA World Welterweight Title in 1975, followed by Austin's championship victory in 1998. Jeff Hardy and Matt Hardy won the WWF Tag Team Titles on June 29th, 1999.

The year 2002 saw an especially busy day for title changes, with four WWE Hardcore Title changes taking place under the championship's 24/7 rule. Steven Richards, Shawn Stasiak, Bradshaw, and Spike Dudley each held the title on that single date. Similarly, 2008 proved to be an active day with four titles changing hands. Kofi Kingston won the WWE Intercontinental Title, Mark Henry captured the ECW Title, Flash and Stuka Jr. won the CMLL Arena Coliseo Tag Team Titles, and Cody Rhodes and Ted DiBiase Jr. won the WWE World Tag Team Titles.

In subsequent years, Adam Cole won the ROH World Television Title in 2012. The year 2013 saw Chris Sabin win the TNA X-Division Title, which was also recorded as vacated that year. By 2014, three titles changed hands on June 29th with John Cena winning the WWE World Heavyweight Title, Suwama capturing the AJPW Unified Triple Crown Heavyweight Title, and Yujiro Takahashi winning the NEVER Openweight Title. Most recently, 2021 saw Felino win the Mexican National Light Heavyweight Title and Isaiah Scott capture the NXT North American Title. The most recent entry comes from 2025, when Zack Sabre Jr. won the IWGP World Heavyweight Title.

THE IMPORTANCE OF MEMORABLE ENTRANCE THEMES

Multiple wrestling veterans have weighed in on the critical importance of entrance music in professional wrestling. John Bradshaw Layfield expressed concern that today's WWE entrance themes do not have the same level of memorability and instant recognition as those from previous eras.

According to JBL, memorable entrance music becomes part of a wrestler's overall character and identity, creating a lasting connection between performer and audience. Jeff Jarrett similarly stressed the critical nature of entrance music, explaining that bad entrance music can quickly sink a talent's potential.
