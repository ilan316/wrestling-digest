🔥 AEW Forbidden Door Event

AEW Forbidden Door delivered significant developments across the wrestling landscape, with multiple wrestling personalities and outlets analyzing the event's impact on ongoing storylines.

Jay White made his return to in-ring competition at Forbidden Door after a lengthy absence since March 2025. On the July 2 special Thursday episode of Collision, White teamed with The Gunns and Rated R Superstar Adam Copeland in an 8-man tag team match against Shane Taylor Promotions. White secured the victory for his team by hitting the Blade Runner finisher. However, the celebration was short-lived as the match's aftermath became the real story.

After the match concluded, The Dogs emerged at the entrance ramp to confront the babyfaces. This confrontation was anticipated after White had screwed them over at Forbidden Door. What caught observers off guard was the arrival of Death Riders members PAC, Claudio Castagnoli, Daniel Garcia, Wheeler Yuta, and Marina Shafir, who ambushed the babyfaces from behind. The Dogs and Death Riders formed an unexpected alliance and brutalized Copeland, The Gunns, and White, with White being laid out with a shillelagh during the assault.

The angle raised questions about faction alignment and storytelling cohesion. Christian Cage, who has issues with White, was notably absent from the segment and failed to assist Copeland. Jon Moxley and Will Ospreay, fellow Death Riders members, were also not present to help their faction mates. Earlier in the episode, Gabe Kidd delivered a cryptic promo suggesting he holds dual membership in both The Dogs and the Death Riders, stating "Is he a Dog? Is he a Death Rider? I am BOTH!" This hints at deeper coordination between the supposedly opposing groups.

The confusing nature of these faction dynamics has left fans questioning Will Ospreay's alignment, as the Death Riders have been primarily heel, yet Ospreay is positioned as a top babyface. The question remains whether Moxley's henchmen acted independently or under orders from leadership.

In tournament news, Mercedes Mone defeated Maya World in the Owen Hart Foundation Women's Tournament Final at Forbidden Door, earning herself a world title opportunity in August at All In. Mone became the first AEW star to win the Owen Hart Foundation Tournament in back-to-back years. While Mone's achievement was significant, Maya World's performance drew praise from industry observers.

WWE Hall of Famer Booker T took to his podcast to specifically commend World on her performance and progression. Having trained World at his wrestling school, Reality of Wrestling, Booker T expressed pride in her journey from student to competitor standing toe-to-toe with Mone at the tournament's final. He stated: "I want to give a big shout out to my girl Maya World because she's put a lot of time in at Reality Of Wrestling trying to hone her craft ... she don't ask a lot of questions. She just go out there and get the job done to the to the best of her ability each and every time. She got to the final, man. That's huge for Maya World. That right there, that's a springboard. That tells you something else. That tell you that somebody believe in you. Somebody believe in you to put you in that spot. So, Maya, just keep doing what you're doing."

AEW President Tony Khan had previously commented on World's growth this year, noting that she makes the most of every opportunity provided and expressing no surprise at her tournament success.

Mone will likely challenge current AEW Women's World Champion Thekla at All In, assuming Thekla can retain the title over the next two months.

The Forbidden Door event has also drawn analysis from major wrestling media outlets, with Fight Game hosts discussing various aspects including Sami Zayn, Roman Reigns versus Seth Rollins, Gunther versus Nick Aldis, and the Oba Femi versus Brock Lesnar III match, indicating the event's significance in the broader wrestling landscape.

🔥 AEW Women's Championship and Casino Gauntlet

AEW has officially announced the first two participants in the Women's Casino Gauntlet match that will take place this Wednesday at Dynamite Beach Break. The gauntlet features a random number of competitors entering on a timed basis, with the match able to end at any point via pinfall or submission.

Athena, the ROH Women's World Champion, earned the coveted number one spot in the gauntlet by defeating Stardom's Rina in a competitive match on Thursday's AEW Collision. The finish came when Athena hit the O-Face for the pinfall victory. Later in the evening, Maya World secured the second spot by defeating Julia Hart after Hart missed a moonsault attempt, setting up World's Shining Wizard for the winning pin.

The rivalry between Athena and Maya World will intensify when they clash once more this Wednesday, as the two have recent history that extends beyond their upcoming encounter. World previously upset Athena during the second round of the Owen Hart Foundation Women's Tournament, advancing to the finals against Mercedes Mone at Forbidden Door. Following that loss, Athena mocked World in a backstage interview and foreshadowed more confrontations between them in the future.

The winner of the Women's Casino Gauntlet will earn an AEW Women's World Championship match against Thekla at Redemption on July 26 in Montreal. Thekla retained her title at Forbidden Door on June 28 against Starlight Kid in a controversial match where she cut off Kid's mask and attacked Stardom President Dumb Bitch Taro Okada after the bell. On the July 2 special episode of Collision, Thekla appeared wearing Starlight Kid's mask as a trophy and cut a promo declaring that Stardom is now dead. She also addressed the women entering the Casino Gauntlet, stating they could load up their guns but she would catch the bullet with her teeth and spit it right back in their faces.

The AEW Dynamite Beach Break episode on July 7 from Clearwater, Florida will also feature several other championship matches on the card, including AEW World Champion MJF defending against Kenny Omega and AEW International Champion Konosuke Takeshita defending against Kyle Fletcher. Additionally, Tommaso Ciampa will face Chris Jericho.

MyAEW Streaming Platform Launch

Tony Khan Elaborates On Motivation For Launching MyAEW Streaming Platform

MyAEW, the AEW-owned streaming service, is already beginning to make waves as it partners with several outside promotions, including promotional partner CMLL and indie promotions like PRODUCE and 1FW, to air their shows on the platform. That has people wondering if MyAEW could also become the home of video libraries from now defunct promotions like Pro Wrestling Guerilla or Lucha Underground, something AEW owner Tony Khan was asked about on last week's pre-Forbidden Door media call.

While Khan admitted he was open to the idea of adding video libraries, he clarified that his main goal for MyAEW content was not acquiring old content. Rather, his primary focus is being able to broadcast live content from other promotions in order to help those companies and their wrestlers expand their presence.

Khan elaborated on his vision for the platform, stating that he is interested in promoting many of these companies and believes AEW can work together with them to develop their wrestlers, develop their platforms, and grow their companies while getting more opportunities for them by featuring their wrestling shows on MyAEW.

Regarding the acquisition of company video libraries, Khan acknowledged that it would depend on the situation. He noted that the only library he has gone out and acquired in that manner was as part of a bigger sale when he purchased Ring of Honor from Sinclair. He described that library as incredible and one of the top wrestling promotions of the past 40 years, emphasizing that it was a major transaction.

Khan indicated that while there could absolutely be a great opportunity for MyAEW to acquire more libraries in the future, he remains very interested in working with promotions to feature their wrestling without necessarily having to buy them out or take over. He stressed that he believes it is good to be able to disseminate and stream a lot of the great wrestling all over the world and give these people a platform.

Historical Title Changes in Early July Wrestling

The early days of July have proven to be significant dates for professional wrestling title changes, though with varying degrees of activity depending on the specific date. Two consecutive days in this period show interesting patterns in championship activity across multiple wrestling promotions worldwide.

July 3rd has historically been a quieter day for major title changes compared to some other dates in the wrestling calendar. According to wrestling data archives, only 156 major and notable title changes have occurred on July 3rd throughout history. The earliest recorded July 3rd title change dates back to 1967, when Karloff Lagarde won the NWA World Welterweight Title. Throughout the 1970s, the date saw additional significant changes, with Johnny Valentine capturing the NWA United States Heavyweight Title in 1975 and Ringo Mendoza winning the NWA World Middleweight Title in 1977.

The 1980s and early 1990s brought more activity to July 3rd. Velvet McIntyre won the WWF Women's Title in 1986, followed by a busy 1987 that saw Jumbo Tsuruta and Tiger Mask #2 claim the AJPW and PWF World Tag Team Titles while Súper Halcón won the Mexican National Heavyweight Title. El Dandy captured the CMLL World Middleweight Title in 1992, and Mano Negra won the NWA World Middleweight Title in 1993.

The 2000s brought some of the most notable July 3rd title changes. In 2001, Billy Kidman won the WCW Cruiserweight Title. The year 2006 proved particularly significant, with Taiyo Kea winning the AJPW Unified Triple Crown Heavyweight Title and Edge capturing the WWE Championship in a triple threat match against Rob Van Dam and John Cena. This Edge victory stands as one of the biggest title changes to occur on July 3rd.

More recent July 3rd activity has been somewhat scattered. The ROH World Title was vacated in 2013, and the TNA Television Title was vacated in 2014. In 2016, Pentagón Jr. won the AAA Latin America Title and Matt Sydal, Ricochet, and Satoshi Kojima claimed the NEVER Openweight 6-Man Tag Team Titles.

July 2nd has shown more consistent activity, with 175 major and notable title changes recorded on this date throughout wrestling history. The earliest documented July 2nd title change occurred in 1970 when Bulldog Brower won the NWA North American Heavyweight Title. The following year, Bepo and Geeto won the WWWF International Tag Team Titles, and in 1980, Kengo Kimura captured the NWA World Junior Heavyweight Title in its Internationale Version. The 1990s saw Masato Yakushiji and Naohiro Hoshikawa claim the CMLL Japan Tag Team Titles in 1999.

The year 2000 marked a particularly active July 2nd, with three title changes recorded on that single day. Steve Blackman won the WWF Hardcore Title, the NWA World Heavyweight Title was vacated, and Crash Holly also won the WWF Hardcore Title. The early 2000s continued with Edge and Hulk Hogan winning the WWE World Tag Team Titles in 2002 and Umaga capturing the WWE Intercontinental Title in 2007. In 2010, Alex Koslov and Christina Von Eerie won the AAA World Mixed Tag Team Titles.

July 2nd, 2017 proved to be another particularly significant date for title changes, with three championships changing hands. Alberto El Patrón won the TNA World Heavyweight Title, Kenny Omega captured the IWGP United States Heavyweight Title, and Sienna won the TNA Knockouts Title. This date demonstrated the global nature of professional wrestling with changes spanning multiple major promotions.

The most recent July 2nd activity spans from 2020 to 2023. Brian Cage became the FTW World Heavyweight Champion in 2020. In 2022, Bobby Lashley won the WWE United States Title and Liv Morgan captured the WWE SmackDown Women's Title, representing significant changes in WWE's secondary championship picture. The most recent entries from 2023 include Yuma Aoyagi winning the AJPW Unified Triple Crown Heavyweight Title and El Lindaman claiming the AJPW World Junior Heavyweight Title.

Overall, while both July 2nd and 3rd have witnessed notable title changes throughout wrestling history, July 2nd has proven slightly more active with 175 documented changes compared to July 3rd's 156. The dates have featured champions from major promotions including WWE, WCW, AAA, CMLL, AJPW, TNA, ROH, and NJPW, demonstrating the international scope of professional wrestling title histories.
