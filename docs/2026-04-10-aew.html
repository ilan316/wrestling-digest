🔥 AEW star contract expiring soon

Lance Archer's future with All Elite Wrestling has become a topic of discussion as his current contract approaches expiration. According to the latest edition of the Wrestling Observer Newsletter, Archer's deal is scheduled to run out in July, putting his status with the company in question as the summer approaches.

Archer has been a fixture in AEW since his debut on the March 11, 2020 edition of Dynamite from Daily's Place in Jacksonville, Florida. He was introduced as a client of Jake Roberts and was immediately placed into a program that led to him facing Cody Rhodes in the finals of a tournament to determine the first-ever TNT Champion at Double or Nothing that year. Since then, Archer has carved out a role as a powerful presence on the AEW roster.

Most recently, Archer has been associated with the Don Callis Family, working alongside names like Brian Cage and other prominent members of the faction. He was previously teamed with Brian Cage until his Murder Machines teammate was sidelined through injury in April of last year, requiring two surgeries. Archer has remained active on AEW television and competes regularly in Ring of Honor, with his most recent match seeing him team with Josh Alexander, Rocky Romero, and Trent Beretta in a losing effort against the team of Claudio Castagnoli, Daniel Garcia, PAC, and Wheeler Yuta of Death Riders.

Before joining AEW, Archer built much of his reputation in New Japan Pro-Wrestling, where he found major success as both a singles competitor and tag team wrestler. He is a three-time IWGP Tag Team Champion with Davey Boy Smith Jr. as part of the Killer Elite Squad, a 2011 G1 Tag League winner with Minoru Suzuki, and a two-time IWGP United States Champion. He was voted "Most Improved" in the 2019 Wrestling Observer Newsletter Awards. Earlier in his career, Archer also spent several years in Total Nonstop Action Wrestling from 2004 to 2009, where he teamed with Jimmy Rave as part of The Rock 'n' Rave Infection. He later had a short stint in World Wrestling Entertainment from 2009 to 2010, competing under the name Vance Archer.

Despite his accomplishments across multiple promotions, Archer has yet to pick up a title in AEW. However, he has demonstrated consistent value as a member of prominent factions and has been on a winning streak in ROH over recent months. Currently separated from his tag team partner Brian Cage, there is an argument to be made that a singles run could benefit the 6-foot-8-inch powerhouse performer.

With his AEW contract set to expire in July, the coming months could determine whether Archer stays with the company or looks for a new opportunity elsewhere. There is potential for a return to NJPW, which enjoys a partnership with AEW, given his history with the promotion and established relationships there. Archer will also likely be seen on the big screen later this year, with him, Cage, and Chavo Guerrero Jr. involved in Steven Spielberg's upcoming sci-fi movie Disclosure Day.

Whether Archer and AEW can come to an arrangement on a new deal remains to be seen, with plenty of time for negotiations to take place before the July expiration date. His experience across multiple international wrestling promotions and championship history makes him a veteran talent who could draw interest if he becomes available. Regardless of how the negotiations end and where Archer's career moves forward, there is certainly more that the Murderhawk Monster can accomplish, whatever the future holds.

MJF addresses WWE jump rumors

MJF has directly addressed the ongoing speculation about a potential move to WWE, and he's making his commitment to All Elite Wrestling abundantly clear.

After recent confirmations from WWE officials that the company had expressed interest in bringing MJF into their fold multiple times, the AEW star took the opportunity to respond during an appearance on Klein Ally Show. Rather than leaving room for ambiguity, MJF laid out exactly where he stands both professionally and personally.

MJF first acknowledged the constant talking points surrounding WWE fans wanting to see him jump ship. However, he framed this desire in a very specific way, suggesting that the demand reflects what he represents rather than any actual likelihood of a move occurring.

"As far as people wanting me to go to WWE—WWE fans want me to go to WWE because they know that I'm the biggest thing in wrestling, and they're desperate to have me on their programming," MJF stated. He then made his current position crystal clear, shutting down any notion of departure and reinforcing exactly where he believes he belongs in the industry.

"But I'm AEW World Champion. I am in All Elite Wrestling," he declared. He doubled down on that stance, connecting his position directly to his philosophy about where top talent should perform. "It's where the best wrestle."

With this statement, MJF shifted the focus away from past discussions and hypothetical scenarios. While WWE's interest has been confirmed multiple times over, MJF is making it unambiguously clear that, at least for now, he is not entertaining the idea of leaving.

Despite having people he respects across the wrestling world, including WWE star Seth Rollins, whom he recently befriended, MJF appears fully invested in his current position. During an appearance on Games with Names, MJF was asked about who inspired him professionally. The AEW Champion named Rollins as someone he considers a "f*****g great professional wrestler" and "incredible," while also praising his friend's work as a football analyst.

However, MJF also expressed admiration for actor Timothee Chalamet, citing a sense of kinship with the young performer. "What he's been able to accomplish at such a young age, you want to talk about a student of the game, this guy lives and breathes and dies acting, clearly, and he takes his craft incredibly seriously. And he's so young," MJF said.

MJF drew parallels between Chalamet's path and his own trajectory in wrestling. "I feel like a kindred spirit to him in that sense, because in my industry, all the top guys, one of them which you met, my good friend Seth Rollins, f*****g great professional wrestler. Incredible. He's also a great football analyst. The guy's the man...He's killing it. But a lot of the guys that are in a top position are in their 40s and 50s. And it's the same thing in my sport. A lot of the guys are in their 40s and 50s. I'm 29, about to be 30. So I'm, by a wide margin, to be honest, the youngest top guy in my industry right now."

MJF reflected on the dedication required to achieve such status at a young age. "And that doesn't happen unless you just dedicate your life to something. So I respect Timothee Chalamet in that sense," he explained.

As a two-time AEW Men's World Champion and arguably the biggest star the promotion has ever homegrown, MJF's attention appears locked squarely on what lies directly ahead. With a major AEW World Championship defense against Kenny Omega scheduled at Dynasty, his focus is on defending his title rather than entertaining what-if scenarios about other promotions.

For now, the speculation about potential moves may continue in wrestling circles, but MJF himself has made it abundantly clear he isn't feeding into it. His commitment to All Elite Wrestling appears resolute, at least for the immediate future.

Adam Copeland Addresses Beth Phoenix AEW Return Talk And Why She Walked Away From Wrestling

Adam Copeland has been busy preparing for AEW Dynasty, but fans also wanted to know whether his wife, WWE Hall of Famer Beth Phoenix, could show up around the event.

During a conversation with TV Insider, Copeland addressed the speculation head-on and made it clear that Beth's life today looks very different from her days in the ring. Copeland was asked directly if Beth might appear again in AEW, possibly even around Dynasty weekend. While he joked about the idea at first, he quickly explained that her schedule and priorities outside wrestling make any return difficult right now.

"I don't know. Yes, she'll be here this weekend…Like I'll say that [laughs]. Nice try though. Really, for us, she is going to be in Portugal. So it will be difficult for her to make it. She is pretty busy."

Copeland went on to explain that Beth didn't step away from wrestling because of injury or circumstance. Instead, she made the choice herself to leave WWE and move on to other parts of her life. According to him, that kind of exit makes returning to the ring much harder than when someone is forced out.

"It's weird. Once you step away from this and walk past it, it's tough to take the steps back. For me, the first time being forced to it was easier to walk back to it if that makes sense."

He also made it clear that Beth is fully invested in life beyond wrestling. Between raising their family and launching new business ventures, her time is already spoken for, leaving little room for a return to the squared circle.

"She willingly walked away from all of it. She left WWE. She didn't want to renew and wanted to be done there. She is living life, being a mom. She is starting a company. She is pretty busy."

Even though Copeland didn't shut the door completely, his comments paint a clear picture of someone who has moved on by choice rather than necessity. Beth Phoenix remains one of the most respected names in women's wrestling, but for now, her focus appears to be firmly outside the ring. With Beth Phoenix choosing family life and new business ventures over wrestling, it raises real questions about whether fans will ever see her lace up her boots again, or if her in-ring career is truly finished for good.

An Important Thing Happened - Tony Khan Discusses AEW Changes Ahead Of 2025

Having co-founded AEW in 2019, Tony Khan has opened up about his growth as the owner and his previous moments of realization. Since its inception, AEW has gone from strength to strength, with Tony Khan leading the way and not being static in his approach.

AEW isn't the only element that has seen growth, either, with Tony Khan sharing an insight into his own development. Speaking on Ringer Wrestling, Khan revealed two major moments of reflection, five years apart. He explained that significant realizations occurred at the start of 2020 and then at the start of 2025, almost exactly five years apart, during the week leading into January 1, 2020 and January 1, 2025.

Khan stated: "An important thing that happened… It was about five years apart, at the start of 2020, and then at the start of 2025, almost exactly five years apart, Christmas 2019 and Christmas 2024, that week leading into January 1, 2020 and January 1, 2025. I had very similar reflections, almost exactly five years apart, like 'You can listen to a lot of people, but you're responsible for this, and you have to do the ideas you believe in. Even if you take someone else's idea, it's not their fault, it's my fault.'"

The AEW owner elaborated on the impact of these reflections, noting that he made substantial changes at both of these junctures. "I made a lot of changes at the start of 2020, and I made a lot of changes at the start of 2025, and both times they were really good changes," Khan said. He also acknowledged the evolution of AEW's approach and philosophy over time, adding: "Like you said, I think at the beginning of AEW it was good to have that outlaw, rebellious spirit, but now we're more established, and this is coming from more of a veteran place."

As part of these ongoing developments, Khan also shared his thoughts on bringing back Chris Jericho, the former World Champion, to the AEW fold.

AEW Is Where The Best Wrestle

More than just a slogan that is used on a frequent basis, AEW has become the home of some of the best talent in the world, some of whom have had interest from WWE. While it continues to have stars that have been part of AEW since the first Double or Nothing, the roster has seen growth, with Khan bringing in prominent names from the independent scene and through his partnerships with CMLL and NJPW.

AEW stars may occasionally take digs at WWE, but Khan has seemingly been backing away from that over recent months, further reinforcing his "more of a veteran place feel." This shift in tone reflects the company's maturation and Khan's more measured approach to competition.

Khan clearly has his eye out for the next star to bring into AEW, and has said as much in previous comments, stating he is "constantly evaluating the market." This ongoing assessment of talent demonstrates Khan's commitment to maintaining AEW's competitive position while remaining adaptable to the evolving landscape of professional wrestling.

MJF attempted abduction incident in Chicago

During a recent interview on The Adam Friedland Show, AEW World Champion MJF shared a disturbing story about the time a fake pilot attempted to kidnap him in Chicago at the All Out 2019 pay-per-view event.

MJF explained that he was attending the show during a particularly stressful time in his personal life. His father had recently suffered a heart attack, and while his mother encouraged him not to miss the company's important early pay-per-view, he was understandably distraught and worried about his father's health. Fortunately, his father would end up being okay.

While backstage at the event, a man claiming to be a pilot approached MJF and told him he would be flying him back home after the show. MJF found this gesture touching, as he had flown on a jet a couple of times with his boss previously. Grateful for what he believed was an act of kindness from his company's leadership, MJF decided to thank his boss Tony Khan for arranging the flight.

When MJF knocked on Khan's door to express his appreciation, Khan's reaction was immediate confusion. Khan had no idea what MJF was talking about and denied booking any jet to fly him home that night. This revelation alarmed MJF, who realized something was seriously wrong with the pilot's story.

MJF returned to confront the pilot with QT Marshall, one of AEW's executive vice presidents. When they asked the man who had hired him, he again claimed it was Tony Khan. After they pressed him on this, security moved in and apprehended the individual.

Before the man was escorted away, QT made one final request. He asked the alleged pilot to show them his pilot's license as a condition for allowing him to stay. The man then patted his pockets and claimed he had left his license at home, a excuse that immediately exposed the deception.

What made this incident even more disturbing was what happened next. When security questioned the fake pilot about his true intentions, he was honest about his plan. The man admitted that he intended to abduct MJF. An adult man had successfully infiltrated the backstage area with the premeditated goal of kidnapping the AEW World Champion.
