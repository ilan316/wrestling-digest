🔥 Nic Nemeth TNA Rebellion Challenge with NFL Legend

At TNA Sacrifice on March 27, Ryan Nemeth discovered his brother Nic Nemeth lying unconscious backstage after being attacked by a mystery assailant. The incident was captured in digital exclusive footage that TNA released following the event. Rather than wait for answers, Nic Nemeth decided to take matters into his own hands by issuing a direct challenge to whoever attacked him.

On Thursday, April 2, Nemeth posted a video message addressing the situation. In the video, he acknowledged that after 20-plus years in the wrestling business, he should have been more cautious. He stated, "To whom it may concern, I don't know who you are and I don't care, but after 20-plus years in this business, I should know better. This one's on me. I should have had eyes in the back of my head, but I didn't. I got blindsided. I got attacked." Rather than dwelling on the cheap shot, Nemeth made it clear that he wanted to settle the matter the right way, face-to-face in the ring.

Nemeth issued a direct challenge to his attacker, saying, "And if you've got the stomach for it, whoever you are, if you've got the guts to face me eye-to-eye, now that you got the cheap shot in, you made your mark, it got my attention, are you willing to go one step further? Face me eye-to-eye in that ring?" This challenge sets the stage for what promises to be a personal and intense confrontation at the upcoming Rebellion event.

Since TNA Rebellion is taking place in Cleveland, Ohio, Nemeth's hometown, he decided to invite a local legend to stand in his corner. He extended an invitation to Bernie Kosar, the legendary Cleveland Browns quarterback who played for the team from 1985 to 1993. Nemeth's words were emotional and respectful as he asked Kosar to be part of the match. "There's nobody that embodies the spirit of the city of Cleveland more than Cleveland and Cleveland Brown legend Bernie Kosar," Nemeth said. "Bernie, you're a hero to so many of us. You're a hero to the city of Cleveland. And I think you're the exact right person to be in my corner, to have my back, as you've had Cleveland's for all these years."

Bernie Kosar is widely regarded as a legend not only in Cleveland but throughout the NFL. During his 12-season career, he made the playoffs four times with the Browns and also played for the Dallas Cowboys in 1993, where he won Super Bowl XXVIII as a backup quarterback. Beyond his football accomplishments, Kosar recently underwent a life-saving liver transplant in November 2025. Following his recovery, he expressed excitement and gratitude for his second chance at life in an interview with Fox 8 News' I-Team.

The decision to have Kosar in Nemeth's corner adds significant emotional weight to the upcoming match. Kosar's presence will not only provide support to Nemeth but also give the hometown crowd in Cleveland a legendary figure to rally behind. The atmosphere at the Wolstein Center on April 11 is expected to be electric as fans witness this personal confrontation.

The identity of the mystery attacker remains unknown, leaving fans and analysts to speculate about who would target the former TNA World Champion. TNA has been strategic in keeping this mystery intact, which will help build anticipation for the Rebellion event. The company has not announced who Nemeth's opponent will be, instead allowing the mystery to develop naturally.

TNA Rebellion takes place on Saturday, April 11, 2026, at the Wolstein Center in Cleveland, Ohio. The updated card includes several championship matches. TNA World Champion Mike Santana defends his title against Eddie Edwards, X-Division Champion Leon Slater faces Cedric Alexander, International Champion Trey Miguel takes on Mustafa Ali, and TNA Tag Team Champions The Hardys defend against Bear Bronson and Brian Myers. Additionally, Elijah faces Frankie Kazarian, and The Elegance Brand faces ODB, Mickie James, and Taryn Terrell in a Hardcore Country match.

The card now prominently features Nic Nemeth in his mystery match with Bernie Kosar in his corner. This storyline has generated significant interest among wrestling fans, with many speculating about who the attacker could be and what the match will look like with a Browns legend at ringside. TNA appears to be booking Kosar's appearance carefully to ensure it enhances the storytelling rather than overshadowing the wrestling itself.

🔥 TNA Wrestling News

The promotion has added new stars like Ricky Sosa and Elayna Black while simultaneously bringing back former TNA wrestlers including Taryn Terrell, ODB, and Allie. Looking at the free agency market, several former TNA Wrestling stars could potentially make comebacks in the coming weeks and months.

The Hex, consisting of Allysin Kay and Marti Belle, represents a potential major acquisition for the women's division. The TNA Knockouts World Tag Team Title picture has been lacking in recent months since The Elegance Brand regained the championships. The former MLW World Heavyweight Champion, who held that title for 644 days, remains a free agent since departing the company. A return with a more important role could see him become a main event contender in the TNA World Title picture.

Amazing Red, one of the first standout talents in the X-Division when TNA Wrestling first started, continues his career as both an in-ring competitor and trainer at the HOG training school. Red, who began wrestling in 1998, has been an innovator for the high-flying style popularized today. His return to TNA Wrestling could provide a wonderful addition to the X-Division where he could challenge Leon Slater for the title he helped put on the map two decades earlier.

Mike Santana recently opened up about his AEW release in March 2024, calling it a turning point in both his career and personal life. Santana requested his own release after feeling stuck creatively and mentally, with burnout becoming a serious issue. He wanted to focus on building a singles career and began rebuilding himself step by step after leaving AEW.

Following his departure, Santana rebranded himself and moved to TNA Wrestling, making changes both inside and outside the ring. He focused on his personal struggles and achieved sobriety after hitting what he described as rock bottom. Over time, he became stronger both mentally and physically, reaching the top when he became TNA World Champion in 2026. Though that reign was disappointingly short, he again became TNA World Champion earlier this year and has been building momentum with every match.

On social media, Santana reflected on his journey, writing that two years ago he was granted his release and put a plan together to execute it. He stated it was the best thing he did for his life and career, noting that he had sacrificed too much and had a fire burning inside. He emphasized that above all, his daughter needed to eat and that she would by any means necessary. Two years later, Santana expressed amazement at what he has accomplished, calling it an amazing ride and noting that this is just the beginning.

Currently, Santana faces a major challenge as Eddie Edwards will challenge him for the TNA World Title. The match will headline the TNA Rebellion event in Cleveland, Ohio, on April 11.

She called the situation disappointing and mentioned that ever since her previous comment, the fan perception of the division has been very split, with only time telling how it plays out.

She suggested they state what they're thinking instead of telling her what to say.

In a follow-up exchange, Kim addressed critics telling her what to say and think, emphasizing that she has earned the right to have an opinion based on her experience and lived that life. She noted that anonymity on social media seems to give people a false sense of power.

🔥 NJPW Updates

The lineup for NJPW Best of the Super Juniors 33 is now almost fully set with 18 of the 20 entrants confirmed. NJPW has announced that junior heavyweight mainstays KUSHIDA and Ryusuke Taguchi will be participating in this year's tournament. Along with that, Dragon Gate wrestler Hyo has been added to the list of entrants as he makes his official NJPW debut. KUSHIDA and Taguchi are both former IWGP Junior Heavyweight Champions and BOSJ winners, with KUSHIDA having won the tournament in 2015 and 2017 and Taguchi winning in 2012. Though this is Hyo's NJPW debut, he won the promotion's bodybuilding contest in 2025. He's a two-time Open the Brave Gate Champion, which is Dragon Gate's version of a junior heavyweight title.

BOSJ 2026 begins on May 14 and runs through June 7. The final two entrants, along with the block lineups, are set to be revealed this Saturday at NJPW Sakura Genesis. A title shot will be awaiting the BOSJ winner if current IWGP Junior Heavyweight Champion DOUKI does not win the tournament himself. The updated list of confirmed entrants includes Kosei Fujita, DOUKI, El Desperado, Yoshinobu Kanemaru, SHO, Daisuke Sasaki, YOH, Master Wato, Jakob Austin Young, Francesco Akira, Valiente Jr., Robbie Eagles, Taiji Ishimori, Robbie X, Titan, KUSHIDA, Ryusuke Taguchi, and Hyo.

In separate news, Tiger Mask has set a date and revealed the location for his final NJPW show. It was already known that the fourth iteration of Tiger Mask would be retiring from the ring this July. After competing at NJPW's Road to Sakura Genesis event on Thursday, he revealed that his last NJPW date will be on July 7 at Korakuen Hall in Tokyo. It's the same venue where this version of Tiger Mask made his official in-ring debut in July 1995.

An opponent for Tiger Mask's retirement match is not yet known, with him saying he's not sure if he'll be wrestling at the show or if there will just be a retirement ceremony. It's possible that he'll be working some already scheduled indie dates overseas after this date, but July 7 will be his last NJPW event. Tiger Mask stated, "I don't even know if I'll have a retirement match. It might just be a ceremony. If someone I want to face comes along, I might be able to make an announcement at that time. My retirement is in July, but there were some overseas matches that were already scheduled after that, and I might not be able to cancel them, so there might be some matches overseas."

Following in the footsteps of Satoru Sayama, Mitsuharu Misawa, and Koji Kanemoto, Yoshihiro Yamazaki is retiring as the longest-serving Tiger Mask. He is a six-time IWGP Junior Heavyweight Champion, most recently holding the title in 2009-2010. Thursday's show saw Tiger Mask team up with Shota Umino for a victory over KUSHIDA and Masatora Yasuda. At the end of the night, Tiger Mask appeared again and issued a challenge to IWGP Junior Heavyweight Champion DOUKI, wanting to win the title again before retirement. While the future of the Tiger Mask character is unknown, it was recently announced that CMLL wrestler Magnus is taking over the Black Tiger gimmick, a character that was initially introduced as a Tiger Mask rival and has been portrayed by the likes of Mark Rocco, Eddie Guerrero, and Rocky Romero.

In retrospective news, former WWE Universal and NXT Champion Finn Balor recently opened up and reflected on his thoughts on leaving NJPW for WWE. An NJPW veteran and often known as the inaugural leader of Bullet Club, Balor left NJPW in 2014 to begin a fresh career in WWE NXT. Speaking on Talk'n Shop Live, Balor recalled how he had pictured spending his entire career in Japan before starting with WWE.

Balor stated, "I think what we had in Bullet Club when I was there was a real special time. Leaving when it was that hot was kind of bittersweet. I had known for a long time that I wanted to move on, but it's always bittersweet. And I cried, obviously, leaving New Japan. That was super emotional. I kind of always pictured my whole career in Japan up until that point. I just really wanted to roll the dice in WWE and give it one try. And if it's gonna be a year, it's gonna be a year, whatever, and then go back to Japan."

At NJPW, Balor was a three-time IWGP Junior Heavyweight Champion, six-time IWGP Junior Heavyweight Tag Team Champion, and even won the 2010 and 2013 editions of Best of the Super Juniors. He jumped ships to WWE in 2014 and has remained a vital part of the promotion since then.

Lady Frost Post-AEW Career

Lady Frost has moved on from AEW and found a new home in Major League Wrestling following the expiration of her contract. Frost became a free agent at midnight on April 1 after her time with the company came to an end. She confirmed her free agency by posting on social media, expressing gratitude to AEW President and CEO Tony Khan and mentioning that she is moving on to new opportunities.

MLW quickly capitalized on Frost's availability, signing her to a deal shortly after she became a free agent. Sources indicated that MLW had strong interest in Frost and communicated their excitement about finalizing the agreement. She is scheduled to participate in several tapings with the promotion. Details regarding the length of Frost's deal with MLW have not yet been disclosed, but updates will be provided as they become available.

In an interview, Frost reflected on her time with AEW and provided details about her contract history with the company. She began in 2022 on a per appearance deal while working Ring of Honor tapings in Orlando, receiving travel accommodations. She was subsequently signed to a full-time two-year contract with a three-year option, which was an AEW contract. Frost competed in a couple of AEW matches but was bounced around without any real direction for her character.

Despite the lack of direction and limited opportunities, Frost expressed that she is not bitter about her nearly four-year tenure with AEW. She noted that many people may not have even known she was signed, as she did not receive a graphic during her time with the company. However, she remained thankful for the experience and described it as a good chapter in her career.

Frost emphasized that she prefers to work actively rather than sit idle, whether in catering or at home collecting a paycheck. She expressed her passion for being in the ring doing what she loves. Her last match for AEW came in January when she lost to Mina Shirakawa on the January 10 episode of Collision. Prior to that, her last appearance for AEW or ROH was on the July 11 episode of ROH on HonorClub. Throughout her time with AEW and ROH, Frost remained active on the independent wrestling scene.

As an eight-year veteran of professional wrestling, Frost is excited about this next chapter of her career with MLW and the opportunity to compete more regularly.

Will Ospreay NJPW Return

Will Ospreay is set to make his long-awaited return to New Japan Pro Wrestling this weekend at NJPW Sakura Genesis on April 4, 2026. The AEW star will compete in a six-man tag team match at Ryogoku Sumo Hall, teaming with his former United Empire stablemates Great-O-Khan and HENARE against the team of Yuya Uemura, Taichi, and El Desperado. This marks Ospreay's first match in Japan since The New Beginning in Osaka on February 11, 2024.

Ahead of the show, Ospreay released an emotional statement on social media reflecting on his relationship with NJPW and Japan. "10 Years Ago. I boarded a flight to Tokyo to perform for NJPW. 22 years old I could barely speak English and I was so scared going to a country by myself that I couldn't speak the language," Ospreay began his statement, referencing his NJPW debut nearly a decade ago at Invasion Attack on April 10, 2016, where he unsuccessfully challenged KUSHIDA for the IWGP Junior Heavyweight title.

Ospreay went on to discuss his formative years in Japan and the profound impact the country had on his personal development. "I spent 8 years in Japan & it completely shaped me as a man. Japan taught me patience & etiquette. All I wanted to do was show my love & appreciation to a country that allowed me to be a kid & I got to grow up in a locker room full of men I had the utmost respect for," he explained. "I've not stopped thinking about you since I left 2 years ago. Can't wait to come home and wrestle in my favourite arena in wrestling."

The emotional tone of Ospreay's statement underscores the significance of his return. After spending eight years developing his craft in Japan, establishing himself as one of the most exciting wrestlers on the NJPW roster, Ospreay's two-year absence has made this homecoming particularly meaningful. His words suggest that despite his current commitments elsewhere, his connection to NJPW and Japan remains strong and deeply personal.

NJPW Sakura Genesis 2026 features a stacked card beyond Ospreay's match. The event will include several championship defenses, with IWGP Heavyweight Champion Yota Tsuji facing Callum Newman, IWGP Tag Team Champions The Knock Out Brothers defending against Zack Sabre Jr. and Ryohei Oiwa, and NJPW World Television Champion Konosuke Takeshita defending against Shota Umino. The NEVER Openweight Six-Man Tag Team Champions Hirooki Goto, YOSHI-HASHI, and Oleg Boltin will also defend their titles against Ren Narita, Yujiro Takahashi, and Chase Owens. Additional matches include bouts featuring Aaron Wolf, YOH, Toru Yano, Master Wato, Don Fale, DOUKI, SHO, Yoshinobu Kanemaru, Shingo Takagi, Taiji Ishimori, Robbie X, Jake Lee, Francesco Akira, Jakob Austin Young, Togi Makabe, Tiger Mask, Hartley Jackson, and Kosei Fujita, with a pre-show match featuring KUSHIDA and Masatora Yasuda against Tatsuya Matsumoto and Taisei Nakahara.

Mance Warner Open to WWE or AEW

Mance Warner is open to all options following his departure from TNA Wrestling in March. Both Warner and his partner Steph De Lander are now free agents and have quickly returned to active competition on the independent circuit, including a return to GCW just days after their TNA exit was announced.

When asked about the possibility of signing with a major promotion like WWE or AEW, Warner expressed his genuine passion for professional wrestling across all companies. "Here's how I look at everything. I'm a pro wrestler, I love all the companies. I watch too much pro wrestling on TV. I see that people argue about they like this and don't like that, I like it all. I turn on the TV until Steph goes, 'Alright, no more.' Then I go upstairs and I watch WCW from 2001. I'm addicted to wrestling," Warner stated during an interview with Muscle Man Malcolm.

Warner elaborated on the unique opportunities available at different promotions, noting that each company offers distinct advantages. "For me, WWE, NXT, AEW, I would go to any of them. There's things that you could do at one you probably can't do at another," he explained.

The free agent then outlined several dream matchups that excite him across both promotions. On the AEW side, Warner mentioned potential encounters with wrestlers including Darby Allin, Gabe Kidd, Eddie Kingston, and Jon Moxley. "I think ol' mancer versus Darby Allin would be pretty wild. I think ol' mancer and Gabe Kidd one more time would be pretty wild. ol' mancer and Eddie Kingston, Jon Moxley another one," he said.

Regarding WWE, Warner expressed interest in facing several notable names. "Then on the other side, you've got ol' mancer maybe against Kevin Owens. Sami Zayn. I would love to have a microphone against CM Punk," Warner noted, indicating his enthusiasm for potential programs with established WWE talent.

Ultimately, Warner's philosophy regarding his next destination is straightforward and flexible. "At the end of the day, whatever place wants ol' mancer to be ol' mancer, I'm cool with that," he concluded, emphasizing that his priority is finding a promotion that allows him to maintain his authentic character and wrestling style.

Brian Cage Provides Update On Recovery Following Injury And Surgery

Brian Cage recently appeared on the 1 Of A Kind podcast to discuss his recovery from a significant injury and the subsequent surgical complications that extended his time away from competition.

Cage expressed optimism about his current physical condition, stating that he has been cleared for a while. He explained his current performance level, noting that while he can perform all of his in-ring offense without issue, he has not yet returned to his pre-injury baseline in certain areas. "I feel great. I've been cleared for a while. I'm not 100% as far as performance-based. I'm not lifting as much weight on my leg lifts and I'm not jumping just as high just yet, but I can do everything I've done in the ring several times," Cage said. He added that he has been incorporating different dynamic jumping exercises into his training regimen on his off days and is feeling good and excited about his impending return.

The AEW/ROH star's return is happening sooner than some may have expected, with Cage noting that he has three matches scheduled on the same day during WrestleMania weekend. He also revealed a significant detail about his comeback, mentioning that he recently won a battle royal and eliminated the person responsible for his original injury. "I just won this battle royal, a year after I tore my quad, and the person I eliminated with Chris Masters, the guy who tore my quad."

Cage detailed the extent of his injuries and the complications that arose from his treatment. He suffered a torn quad, which would have typically sidelined him for approximately nine months. However, he chose to undergo right knee surgery and receive a special replacement during his recovery period. This decision, which was intended to address pre-existing knee issues and ensure a smoother return to competition, ultimately resulted in complications. "The quad would've had me out for probably nine months, and then I chose to get right knee surgery and get a special replacement on it. That's what kept me out longer. Long story short, they botched it."

According to Cage, the botched surgery forced him to undergo another procedure. He was originally scheduled to return in November, but had to return to surgery in that same month due to the complications from his June knee procedure. "I should've come back in November, but I had to go back under the knife in November, and now I'm cleared from that surgery. When I found that out in October, I was pretty devastated."

Cage explained the reasoning behind his decision to have the knee surgery in the first place. The goal was to ensure that by the time November arrived, he would be fully ready to return without any lingering issues with his right knee. "The whole point of getting what I got done on my right knee in June, was so that when November came around, I could be good to go instead of coming back and still having problems with my right knee."
