🔥 WrestleMania 42 Night One Coverage

WRESTLEMANIA 42 NIGHT ONE DELIVERS SURPRISES, TITLE CHANGES, AND DRAMATIC FINISHES

WrestleMania 42 Night One concluded with Cody Rhodes successfully defending his Undisputed WWE Championship against Randy Orton in a brutal main event match. The American Nightmare retained his title after hitting a Cross Rhodes on Orton, who had just inadvertently knocked out referee Charles Robinson with an RKO. Throughout the match, both men were busted open and traded each other's signature moves multiple times. Pat McAfee, who had aligned with Orton in the weeks leading to the event, attempted to help his partner by acting as a replacement referee after the original official was incapacitated, but Orton turned on him with another RKO when the count wasn't favorable. The match lasted just under 23 minutes and was marked by storytelling around Orton's back injury, which played into several near falls. After the match, Orton brutally attacked Rhodes with the championship belt and hit a devastating punt kick, standing tall to close the show while Rhodes was left with a visibly swollen eye requiring medical attention. This finish sets up a potential rematch, as Orton has been chasing his 15th world title reign to move closer to John Cena's record of 17 reigns.

Ric Flair, who has history with both men through his Evolution faction with Orton and his connections to the Rhodes family, took to social media to praise both performers. He wrote that the match was one of the greatest he had seen in recent years and expressed his respect for how both men represented the company at the highest level. Flair's endorsement added weight to what many in the wrestling community considered a standout performance, despite the controversial Pat McAfee subplot.

The women's division saw significant title changes throughout the night. Liv Morgan defeated Stephanie Vaquer to become the new Women's World Champion, capturing her second reign in the division. Morgan made a memorable entrance performing her new single "Trouble" live on stage with backup dancers, including a choreographed strip sequence that seamlessly transitioned from a music video into a live performance. The match itself lasted only six minutes and 50 seconds, drawing criticism from viewers who felt the talented performers deserved more time to develop their story. Vaquer had held the title since September when she defeated IYO SKY at Wrestlepalooza. Despite the shortened timeframe, Morgan's victory with the Judgment Day at ringside created the narrative she needed, with Roxanne Perez and Raquel Rodriguez providing crucial interference that distracted Vaquer at pivotal moments.

Becky Lynch reclaimed the Women's Intercontinental Championship from AJ Lee in another women's title change. Lynch had lost to Lee three consecutive times since Lee's return to the company in September, but tonight she broke that streak. The finish saw Lynch utilizing underhanded tactics, pulling referee Jessika Carr in front of herself as a human shield before slamming Lee into the exposed turnbuckle that Lynch had removed earlier in the match. This marked Lee's first WrestleMania in eleven years and served as the first time Lee defended the Women's Intercontinental Championship at the grandest stage. Lynch is now a three-time Women's Intercontinental Champion. On the post-show, Lee indicated this was not a permanent goodbye from WWE, stating she would see everyone again soon, hinting at a hiatus rather than a departure.

The Women's Tag Team Championships saw a shock victory as Paige and Brie Bella won the titles in a fatal four-way match. Nikki Bella was originally scheduled to team with her sister Brie against Nia Jax and Lash Legend, Charlotte Flair and Alexa Bliss, and Bayley and Lyra Valkyria. However, Nikki had suffered an ankle injury during a March 27 SmackDown match and was unable to compete. Rather than forfeit the match, Nikki announced she had called in an old friend, leading to Paige's shocking return to in-ring competition for the first time since December 2017. Paige had spent years away from active competition due to a neck injury that forced her retirement in 2017, worked in non-wrestling roles for WWE, departed for AEW in 2022 where she competed as Saraya after being medically cleared, and left AEW in March 2025. Her return generated an enormous reaction from the Las Vegas crowd. Paige pinned Alexa Bliss to secure the victory, mirroring her debut moment years earlier when she defeated AJ Lee on her main roster debut at the Raw after WrestleMania XXX. After the match, Paige appeared on the post-show to discuss her return, stating that WWE is her home and that she is a WWE girl forever. She expressed how magical and surreal the moment felt to be back at WrestleMania and in the ring with the Bella Twins. Nikki Bella revealed on the post-show that she had undergone surgery two weeks prior and had fractured, torn, and sprained her ankle. She explained that she encountered Paige in Birmingham while getting her injury checked and decided that Paige was the only person who could make the moment feel special for Brie while honoring their family bond.

Bianca Belair made a surprise return to announce she is pregnant with her and Montez Ford's first child together. Belair, who has been out of action since suffering a broken knuckle at WrestleMania 41 last year, appeared during John Cena's attendance announcement. After Cena announced 50,816 fans in attendance, Belair came to the ring and asked him to add one more to the total, then revealed her baby bump to a massive reaction from the crowd. Cena updated the attendance figure to 50,817, incorporating the unborn child into the announcement. Belair posted professional maternity photos with Ford afterward, captioning them "This means the world to me." Her fellow wrestlers, including Rhea Ripley, took to social media to congratulate the couple, with Ripley expressing emotional sentiments about losing Belair from the roster while celebrating the happy news.

Other championship matches on the card saw mixed results. GUNTHER defeated Seth Rollins in a hard-fought encounter that saw Bron Breakker's shocking return to the company. Breakker, who had been out since undergoing hernia surgery in February, appeared during the match and speared Rollins, allowing GUNTHER to lock in a sleeper hold and make Rollins pass out for the victory. Breakker then delivered another spear to Rollins post-match and embraced Paul Heyman, indicating that the orchestration of the attack came from Heyman's direction. On the post-show, Heyman confirmed he had set up Rollins from the beginning and warned of future consequences for the Visionary. This suggests the originally-planned Breakker versus Rollins program is back in motion.

The opening match saw LA Knight and the Usos defeat Logan Paul, Austin Theory, and IShowSpeed in a six-man tag team match. IShowSpeed made his WrestleMania debut in the match and impressed with his athleticism, including an impressive splash off the top rope. The post-match angle saw Logan Paul attack IShowSpeed over a spot that went wrong earlier in the match, but the babyfaces quickly returned to save Speed. Speed then delivered a massive splash off the ring post through the announce table onto Paul, a moment that immediately went viral. This sets up a potential future confrontation between Logan Paul and IShowSpeed, continuing their storyline.

Jacob Fatu defeated Drew McIntyre in an unsanctioned match that kicked off the first hour on ESPN2. The match was highly physical with weapons and tables coming into play. Fatu ultimately prevailed after hitting a Mighty Moonsault through a setup table for the victory. McIntyre, still sitting from his WrestleMania defeats, even took time during the match to record a selfie-style video where he laughed about being at WrestleMania again.

Several concerns emerged about the production and pacing of Night One. Many viewers noted that WrestleMania 42 was the first year to air primarily on ESPN Unlimited streaming through the ESPN app, with only the first hour airing on ESPN2 cable. This shift to a streaming platform with commercial breaks resulted in numerous interruptions throughout the show, leading some to describe the event as feeling rushed and overly commercial. Some matches that should have received more time to develop their stories were cut short, particularly the Women's World Championship match between Morgan and Vaquer, which lasted less than seven minutes. The attendance announced by Cena was 50,817 (including Belair's baby), down from last year's announced figure of 61,467, reflecting reported softer ticket sales for this year's event.

Other notable moments from the evening included John Cena's opening as host in a suit rather than wrestling gear, honoring his promise upon retirement. He delivered a heartfelt opening that connected with the Las Vegas crowd. Lin-Manuel Miranda, the creator of Hamilton, narrated the opening video package for WrestleMania. Becky Lynch had a special entrance featuring The Wonder Years performing her theme live, though the band faced criticism for apparent lip-syncing issues. The show also featured a drunk fan incident where a fan fell onto a woman seated below in the upper sections of the stadium after Seth Rollins' match, resulting in a confrontation between the fan and the woman he struck.

Outside the ring, there was significant fan and performer reaction to the event. Content creator GFed claimed WWE had him ejected from the stadium without explanation, with security stating the order came directly from WWE. Ric Flair's praise for the Orton versus Rhodes match helped underscore its quality, while Tony Khan posted immediately after Night One ended to promote AEW Dynamite, capitalizing on the wrestling attention of WrestleMania weekend. Various WWE Superstars reacted to Bianca Belair's announcement with genuine congratulations across social media.

Despite the production concerns and some negative feedback about match lengths and booking decisions, WrestleMania 42 Night One delivered several memorable moments, unexpected returns, and storyline developments that set up future programs heading into WWE's next premium live event at Backlash.

🔥 WWE Hall of Fame 2026

The 2026 WWE Hall of Fame ceremony held on Friday night proved to be a source of controversy both during and after the event. Sid Eudy, known professionally as Sycho Sid or Sid Vicious, was posthumously inducted as part of the Legacy Class, having been announced for the honor back in March. While his family was present at the event, they did not deliver a speech, and the company's handling of his induction drew significant criticism from his son.

Gunnar Eudy took to social media on Saturday to express his disappointment with how WWE presented his father's Hall of Fame induction. In a lengthy Facebook post, Gunnar stated that the induction was "straight up disappointing and disrespectful as hell." He noted that despite being in Las Vegas for the event, he saw no advertisements for his father's induction anywhere in the city. Most notably, he pointed out that the Hall of Fame gift shop featured no merchandise for Sycho Sid whatsoever, despite being "loaded with all the Dennis Rodman crap." Gunnar felt that WWE had essentially swept his father under the rug like a footnote, rushing to get him out of the way.

In his statement, Gunnar expressed frustration at what he perceived as jealousy from WWE colleagues who he believed could never match his father's athletic ability, looks, or his unwillingness to compromise his principles. He speculated that if they had waited another twenty years for those individuals to be gone, his father would have received the full induction he deserved. However, Gunnar attempted to remain positive, noting that he was able to bring his own son to see his grandfather honored, even though the child did not yet know about WWE's lack of support. He stated that the moment with his boy made the experience worthwhile. The family did receive an official WWE Hall of Fame ring, which Gunnar described as something solid they could hold onto, unlike what he characterized as a slap in the face from the company during the ceremony itself.

Another major point of controversy at the Hall of Fame emerged during Stephanie McMahon's induction speech. Stephanie acknowledged both of her parents during her remarks, stating that none of them would be there without her mother and father Vince McMahon. This mention prompted sections of the live crowd to chant "Thank you Vince," which sparked significant online backlash given the legal allegations and lawsuits surrounding Vince McMahon.

Social media users expressed strong criticism of the chants, with many arguing that praising Vince McMahon in light of his well-documented allegations of sexual abuse and harassment spanning decades, as well as his current involvement in a sex trafficking lawsuit, was inappropriate and disrespectful to the victims of such conduct. The reaction highlighted a significant divide within the wrestling fanbase regarding Vince McMahon's legacy and the broader conversation surrounding his impact on the industry. WWE issued no official comment regarding the chants or the online reaction.

A third controversy emerged from a social media post involving NXT talent Kendal Grey and her boss Shawn Michaels. Grey shared a photo on social media showing her hugging Michaels, with the caption "My boss is cooler than yours." The image drew swift criticism from former WWE trainer Sarah Stock, who publicly criticized the interaction and raised concerns about workplace professionalism. Stock characterized the physical contact as "perversely inappropriate" for a corporate environment and warned that women should not be subjected to full-body contact by male superiors. She advised that if women are just starting out in the industry, they should not be naive and should maintain physical boundaries with their bosses. Stock later clarified that she believed both individuals involved reflected poorly on the situation. Neither Michaels nor Grey publicly responded to Stock's remarks.

On a more positive note, CM Punk and Kevin Nash appeared to resolve their recent tensions. Video footage captured backstage at the Hall of Fame showed the two former rivals sharing a friendly moment, shaking hands and hugging while exchanging words. The interaction marked a notable shift from the tension that had built between them in recent months, including public critiques and back-and-forth commentary beginning in late December when Nash called out Punk's in-ring attire. Punk is set to defend the WWE World Heavyweight Championship against Roman Reigns in the main event of WrestleMania 42, which takes place on April 18 and 19 from Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas.

Paul Heyman Managing Jade Cargill

Paul Heyman, a WWE Hall of Famer with one of the most successful client lists in professional wrestling history, has never managed a female wrestler in WWE, but that could soon change. During a recent appearance on The Stephen A. Smith Show, Heyman addressed the prospect of managing Jade Cargill, a topic that has generated interest from both parties, as Cargill has previously expressed her desire to work with Heyman.

While Heyman expressed openness to eventually managing Cargill, he made clear that timing is essential to his decision. He emphasized that he wants Cargill to succeed on her own terms before they form a professional partnership. This measured approach reflects Heyman's philosophy about building legitimate credentials independent of his endorsement.

"I'm going to continue to let her ride the momentum by herself as a Women's Champion for all of humanity to be proud of. Let her get that solidified into the history books so that when Jade Cargill unites with Paul Heyman, you are seeing two superpowers of the universe come together instead of Paul Heyman endorsing a talent that at this time is not accepted by the public, though she should be, as a guaranteed first ballot Hall of Famer," Heyman explained.

When pressed about whether his involvement was conditional on Cargill achieving specific milestones, Heyman clarified his position, stressing that his reluctance to manage her immediately stems from his desire for her to build her legacy independently. He wants to avoid any narrative suggesting that Heyman's involvement was the catalyst for her rise to stardom.

"Oh, it's not willing to, it's that I want her to have that on her own. I never want it to be that, 'And then Paul Heyman stepped in, and that's when Jade Cargill really became a star,'" Heyman stated.

Currently, Cargill holds the Women's Championship and is scheduled to defend her title against Rhea Ripley at WWE WrestleMania 42, a major platform where she can continue to build her credentials as a main-event performer. This high-profile championship opportunity aligns with Heyman's vision of allowing Cargill to solidify her legacy through her own achievements before they potentially collaborate.

During the same interview appearance, Heyman also discussed other talent on his radar, including rising star Oba Femi, whom he highlighted as someone with the physical tools and mental fortitude to challenge even Brock Lesnar and push him to his limits.

AJ Lee Future After WrestleMania 42

AJ Lee's return to WWE after a decade away has been nothing short of remarkable, and the former Divas Champion has opened up extensively about her journey back to professional wrestling and what lies ahead.

Lee previously commented on her reasoning behind returning to WWE, stating that while she had been very comfortable in retirement, she had mused about a return for years. According to an interview on "The Takedown on SI," (h/t Wrestling Inc.), Lee expressed that her return was a surreal moment for her. "I had this like really picture-perfect career, and it was over," Lee stated. "For it to have kind of spiralized into a WarGames match and a title?...And now another WrestleMania match? It's all just been very unexpected and exciting. Fun!"

Lee further claimed that her return to WWE is for her fans and not just herself, although she enjoyed all her post-wrestling projects. However, a defining moment for her came when she saw fan turnout during signings. "That's when I realized, 'Oh, they're still here,'" she explained in the interview. "To me, it was at first I really fought them, and so to give them a title run and a 'Mania match has been, just really, incredibly meaningful!"

The momentum of Lee's return translated into championship gold when she captured the Women's Intercontinental Championship at WWE Elimination Chamber 2026. She held the prestigious title for approximately 49 days before facing her toughest challenge yet at WrestleMania 42.

On April 18, 2026, in Las Vegas, Nevada, Lee defended her Women's Intercontinental title against Becky Lynch in what would become one of the most memorable matches of her return. However, thanks to Lynch's shrewd tactics, Lee lost both the match and her title in a hard-fought contest that tested the returning superstar against one of WWE's top competitors.

Despite the title loss, Lee's perspective on the entire WrestleMania 42 experience remained positive and emotional. Speaking to Jackie Redmond at the WWE WrestleMania 42 post-show, Lee opened up about her special entrance and match. "It's been my favorite WrestleMania experience so far. Maybe my favorite match so far. Even though I lost it, I got to have my family here. For a lot of them, it was the first time ever, and what I wanted to do with that entrance was represent all the fans who have been with me from day one, who have been wearing my outfits, my shirts, my shorts, and my shoes, and skipping along with me the whole way. That was for you. I love you," she said.

The Women's Intercontinental Champion then noted that she saw herself as a kid during her previous WWE run but now views herself as a woman instead, which has been something she has personally enjoyed about her return.

When addressing her future with WWE, Lee left the door open for possibilities while providing closure in a way her first departure did not. Despite not confirming any upcoming hiatus, Lee left fans with a meaningful message about what comes next. "I never got to say goodbye last time, and I felt bad about that. So this is not goodbye. But I'll see you soon. I hope," she said.

Lee's comments suggest that while she may take time away from in-ring competition following WrestleMania 42, her relationship with WWE and her fans is far from over. Her approach to this potential departure differs significantly from her sudden exit over a decade ago, indicating that she has found closure and peace with her decision to step back, at least for now.

Stephanie McMahon briefly turned back the clock during her WWE Hall of Fame induction, stepping into her old on-screen persona from her days leading The Authority. While delivering her speech on Friday night in Las Vegas, Nevada, Stephanie leaned into a surprise heel-style promo in the middle of the ceremony.

Royce Keys WWE Debut

Royce Keys was nervous before making his WWE debut on the blue brand of SmackDown, very nervous. Thankfully, his new boss, WWE Chief Content Officer Paul "Triple H" Levesque, went above and beyond to put his mind at ease in the final moments he had to prepare for his debut showcase on the biggest stage his chosen profession has to offer.

During an interview in Las Vegas, Nevada for WrestleMania 42 Week, Royce Keys spoke with Adrian Hernandez of Unlikely about his experience. The newest addition to WWE SmackDown reflected on Triple H going out of his way to help him prepare for his WWE Royal Rumble debut in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia.

"I don't think I slept the night before," Keys admitted. "Just going to the airport, getting on that first flight. Okay, it's happening. Getting on that second flight and just landing in Saudi, then having someone wait for me… I had to stay in my hotel."

Keys continued explaining the lengths Triple H went to assist him. "I complained, and Hunter had a full gym delivered to me in my whole little living room. My living room was rearranged into a gym. I was pumping it and had to get that pump on. He's a gym guy, so he know how it is. But, that next morning, waking up, getting in the car, going in my room, it was crazy."

Reflecting on the surreal nature of his debut, Keys described watching himself perform. "I sit back and watch clips and I've watched the Rumble. I'm like, 'Man, golly, that's me? That's me walking down there and getting in Damian Priest's face? Getting in Cody's Rhodes' face?' It's wild."

Following his impressive debut, Royce Keys won the 2026 Andre The Giant Memorial Battle Royal on the WrestleMania 42 go-home episode of WWE SmackDown on April 17 in Las Vegas, Nevada, establishing himself as a rising star in the company.

Road Dogg WWE Departure

Road Dogg Brian James continues to open up about his unexpected departure from WWE earlier this year. During an appearance on Busted Open Radio on SiriusXM this week, the former WWE executive and SmackDown writer spoke candidly about how he felt "a little wronged by WWE."

James referenced the WWE Unreal behind-the-scenes show, suggesting that viewers who watched it would understand the circumstances of his exit without him needing to explain further. The veteran addressed his current feelings about the situation while acknowledging the complicated nature of speaking negatively about his former employer. "I have friends there, that are still going to be there. I have gripes, I do have gripes, but saying that even sounds shitty to say. 'Oh, you're going to gripe about stuff?' Why? James elaborated on what ultimately led him to depart WWE, emphasizing that his dissatisfaction stemmed from a lack of purpose rather than purely financial concerns. "I feel like I was done a little wrong and I feel like I was receiving a paycheck, and I didn't have any purpose. I was making a dollar, but I wasn't making any decisions. I think I can make a difference somewhere, where maybe I don't make as many dollars. In this phase in my life, that's what I'm thinking of."

The WWE departure of Road Dogg Brian James became public on March 5, 2026.

WWE Corporate Matters

WWE President Nick Khan is scheduled to appear before a Senate subcommittee to discuss proposed reforms to the Muhammad Ali Boxing Reform Act. US Senator Ted Cruz will chair the hearing, which is set for April 22 at 10 AM ET.

Khan will testify alongside other prominent figures in the boxing industry, including Golden Boy Promotions' CEO and Chairman Oscar De La Hoya, President of the Association of Boxing Commissions and Combative Sports and Executive Director for the Florida Athletic Commission Timothy Shipman, and Nico Ali-Walsh, the grandson of Muhammad Ali and a professional boxer himself.

According to the announcement, the hearing will assess the current state of boxing under the Ali Act and examine whether Congress should consider reforms to the legislation. Senator Cruz stated that boxing is a core part of American culture and that identifying avenues for its long-term success is critical for both fighters and fans. He expressed his intention to examine how the current structure has shaped the sport and determine if congressional action is warranted.

The Ali Act was introduced in 2000, building upon federal boxing standards established by the Professional Boxing Safety Act of 1996. The legislation was designed to address conflicts of interest within boxing and strengthen fighters' bargaining power in the sport.

Last month, the House passed the Muhammad Ali American Boxing Revival Act, which proponents claim is designed to make it easier for boxing to organize in the same manner as other professional sports leagues. However, this proposed reform has generated significant controversy within the boxing community.

Nico Ali-Walsh has been vocal in his opposition to the reforms. He argues that the proposed changes are anti-labor and serve purely to protect the interests of promoters and major organizations. Ali-Walsh contends that the reforms reverse the anti-monopoly protections that his family fought to establish through the original Ali Act. Following his public address and call-to-action, the World Boxing Commission announced its support for Ali-Walsh's position against the proposed reforms.

Khan will be representing TKO's interests in the matter as an executive working with Zuffa Boxing. The timing of Zuffa's entry into the boxing market has directly coincided with the proposal of these reforms, with Dana White reportedly seeking to create a unified promotion similar to the structure of the UFC.
