Mike Santana AJ Styles Torch

Mike Santana has opened up about AJ Styles' retirement from in-ring competition and the meaningful moment they shared when Styles passed the torch to him in TNA Wrestling.

AJ Styles officially retired from his in-ring career earlier this year at the WWE Royal Rumble event after losing to Gunther. The retirement was later confirmed at WWE's tribute night to Styles in Atlanta on February 23, when Styles left his gloves in the ring and was announced for the 2026 WWE Hall of Fame. This definitive retirement closed the door on many fan hopes for potential dream matches, including one between Styles and Mike Santana.

However, before his official retirement was cemented, Styles appeared on the January 15 TNA Impact premiere episode on AMC, where he had a significant interaction with Santana. During this encounter, which took place ahead of Santana's TNA World Title match against Frankie Kazarian, Styles passed the torch to Santana, telling him it was his turn to carry TNA. Styles said to Santana: "I held this company down as long as I could. Now it's your turn. So do what you do best: win."

During an interview with the Humble Roses Podcast, Santana was asked about the possibility of facing Styles in a future dream match following his retirement. While acknowledging that such a match would be amazing and that he would be honored if it happened, Santana expressed contentment with the torch-passing moment they already experienced.

Santana explained: "That'd be amazing man. When people say dream matches, not all dreams come true right? So they're called dream matches for a reason. If it happens, great. I'd be honored. If it doesn't, great. Because he left me, this business and so many others something to truly be inspired by. He left a body of work that will never be duplicated again, and for him to say that he's one of the very few in this business who inspired a generation, what more can you ask for right? So yeah, if it happens, great, if it doesn't, great! At least I got to share a moment with him and he got to legit like pass a torch over to me. So that felt good and I'm okay with that."

Santana's perspective reflects a mature understanding of the wrestling business and genuine appreciation for Styles' career and contributions to the industry. Rather than focusing on what could have been, Santana emphasized the significance of the actual moment he and Styles shared and the inspiration Styles has provided to him and an entire generation of wrestlers.

The likelihood of an AJ Styles in-ring return appears extremely slim. While speculation remained high after Styles didn't leave his gloves in the ring following his final WWE match at Royal Rumble, and he fueled further speculation by saying "never say never" to a potential return during an appearance on Stephanie McMahon's What's Your Story? podcast, his definitive retirement at WWE's Atlanta tribute event effectively closed that door. With Styles now in a behind-the-scenes role with WWE, a return to in-ring competition seems highly improbable, especially given that WWE would likely want any major return or appearance to be under their banner.

Currently, Mike Santana is leading the charge as the face of TNA Wrestling during one of the company's most successful periods in recent years, aided by their ongoing partnership with WWE and their new television deal with AMC. The torch has been officially passed from AJ Styles to Santana, and both men appear to view this transition as a meaningful moment in wrestling history.

Parker Boudreaux Japan Support

Parker Boudreaux has released a statement addressing the groundswell of support he has received while wrestling for GLEAT in Japan. The former WWE and AEW performer expressed his deep gratitude for the fanbase's embrace during his time with the Japanese promotion.

"I truly love professional wrestling from the bottom of my heart. I am deeply grateful for the recent support from Japan. Every day, in every moment, I feel your love with all my senses. From here on out, I will continue to give my all in every aspect. This is my dream. From now on, day by day, I will keep striving endlessly," Boudreaux stated in his message, which was translated from Japanese.

The statement comes following praise from GLEAT president Hiroyuki Suzuki, who called Boudreaux the "greatest fighter out there right now" last year. Suzuki cited the engagement numbers that Boudreaux's match videos generate on Instagram as evidence of his drawing power and popularity.

Boudreaux recently elaborated on the impact of Suzuki's comments in an interview. "The president of GLEAT, the company I am signed to in Japan, called me the greatest fighter out in the world right now," he said. "When he called me that, I nearly cried. All the hard work in the dojo and big matches throughout Japan paying off. They treat me like I am Taylor Swift out there."

The wrestler's journey to Japan represents a significant chapter in his professional career. Boudreaux originally signed with WWE in 2021 after playing college football at the University of Central Florida, where he performed under the ring name Harland before being released in April 2022. He subsequently debuted with AEW in December of that year, joining Swerve Strickland's Mogul Affiliates stable. After his AEW release in April 2024, Boudreaux made the decision to pursue opportunities in Japan, training at the GLEAT dojo before making his official promotion debut in October 2024.

His success in Japan appears to have reignited his passion for the sport and provided him with a platform where his physical presence and in-ring work have resonated with audiences in a significant way.

Rhea Ripley Says Proving Haters Wrong Fuels Her Success

Rhea Ripley isn't trying to silence the haters — she's using them as fuel, and she's making it clear that doubt is part of what drives her success.

During an appearance on The Ebro Laura Rosenberg Show, Ripley got real about how being doubted has followed her throughout her career, especially in a space that's still largely male-dominated. Instead of letting that pressure get to her, she made it clear it's exactly what pushes her forward. She pointed to doubters as a constant and something she actively uses as motivation.

"Yeah. Um, my main thing is something that's really driven me—to prove the doubters wrong. We're going to be doubted every step of the way. A lot of these sports are male-dominated, so there's going to be a lot of doubt, but you have to let that fuel that fire inside you and you have to prove the doubt wrong," Ripley said.

The former Women's World Champion didn't stop there. She expanded on that mindset, explaining that passion and belief in what you're doing matter more than anything else — no matter who you are.

"If you are so set on something and you love something so much, you can accomplish anything you want within that. Whatever you set your mind to, you can accomplish it. It doesn't matter if you're a female or a male. Go out there and live your dream—accomplish what you want to accomplish—and let the people talk, because there are going to be people that love you and people that hate you. But if they're talking about you, you're doing something," she explained.

As the conversation shifted to dealing with negativity, Ripley made it clear she's fully aware of the criticism that comes her way and she's learned to use it to her advantage. When it was suggested that critics often act like obsessed fans, Ripley leaned into that idea and doubled down on her approach.

"You're getting your name out there. The haters—they talk your name more than the people that like you. Believe me, I know—I've got a lot of haters. Right. So let that fuel you and just keep doing your thing, because they're getting your name out there for you," she stated.

Ripley's mindset is very clear — she's not worried about the hate, she's turning it into momentum, and if anything, the louder the criticism gets, the more it's helping her stay exactly where she wants to be.

Rhea Ripley Says She Doesn’t Need A Title To Feel Like A Champion

Rhea Ripley may not be carrying championship gold right now, but that hasn't changed how she sees herself when the lights come on and the music hits. While speaking on The Ebro, Laura & Rosenberg Show, Ripley was asked directly how it feels to be without a title after previously holding multiple championships, including a run with the WWE Women's Tag Team Titles alongside Iyo Sky.

The conversation started off lighthearted, with Ripley joking about one unexpected perk of not carrying gold everywhere she goes. She laughed about how traveling can actually be easier without a championship belt in her bag, pointing out that security checkpoints can become a hassle when you're carrying something that looks as valuable as a WWE title.

"I feel like it's easier to go through TSA. They don't want to open your bag just to see your title," Ripley said with humor.

After the joke, Ripley got more serious about what it actually feels like to be without a championship. She admitted that not holding a title does feel strange at times, especially after becoming used to walking around with one during previous title reigns. "But I don't know. I feel like kind of naked in a way without it," she reflected.

Even so, Ripley made it clear that her confidence hasn't taken a hit just because she isn't currently holding gold. She explained that her mindset hasn't changed when she walks through the curtain and faces the WWE crowd, because her presence alone still makes her feel like she belongs at the top of the division.

"But at the same time, like I don't mind not having a title because I still feel like — kind of what you were saying — every time I walk through the curtain and walk onto the stage in front of everyone, I feel like a champion," Ripley stated.

Ripley also made it clear that, in her mind, dominance in WWE isn't defined by holding a championship alone. She believes her reputation and performance speak louder than any belt she could carry.

"So, I feel like I don't need a title to represent how dominant I am in the WWE," she emphasized.

Ripley's comments reflect the mindset of a performer who knows her value inside WWE, whether she has gold around her waist or not. With major matches always on the horizon and her status as one of the company's most recognizable stars, her confidence without a championship may end up being just as important as the moment she holds one again.

Rhea Ripley Calls Out Eight-Year Gap Between WWE Evolution Events And Pushes For Annual Return

Rhea Ripley didn't hold back when discussing WWE's Evolution event, making it clear she doesn't want fans waiting years again to see another all-women's premium live event.

During her appearance on The Ebro, Laura & Rosenberg Show, Ripley spoke about how much the women's division has grown over the years and how today's roster continues to build momentum every time they step into the ring. She explained that the women take every opportunity seriously, no matter how short or long their matches might be, because every moment matters. While discussing that mindset, Ripley stressed how much effort the women put into making every appearance count, regardless of how much time they're given on screen.

"But I know all us women have really put in the time and the effort into everything that we get, whether it's 30 seconds or 20 minutes. Like it doesn't matter how long we get, we're going to make sure that we're talked about by the end of it," Ripley stated.

Ripley then turned her focus directly to the Evolution premium live event and the long delay between the original show and its return. She made it clear that waiting nearly a decade between events didn't make sense, especially after the roster proved what they could deliver.

"I know we had to wait — what was it — eight years for the Evolution too, which was a wild thing," she said.

Once Evolution 2 finally happened, the women's division made the most of the opportunity and showed exactly what they were capable of doing when given the spotlight.

"But I feel like after Evolution 2, which is an all-women's pay-per-view, I feel like we went out there and we showed everyone exactly what we can do," Ripley explained.

She didn't stop there. Ripley made it clear that she hopes WWE moves much faster when it comes to scheduling the next Evolution event, signaling that the long wait shouldn't happen again.

"And I'm hoping that it takes less time now for Evolution 3," she said.

She wrapped up her thoughts by making her strongest statement yet, pushing the idea that Evolution should become a regular event rather than a rare special attraction.

"I really hope that everyone watched that and went, 'Wow, like this needs to be a yearly thing,'" Ripley concluded.

Ripley's comments add more pressure to the ongoing conversation about how WWE handles major events for the women's division. With female stars consistently delivering big performances on premium live events, her remarks reinforce the idea that Evolution could easily become a staple on WWE's yearly calendar instead of something fans have to wait years to see again.

If WWE does decide to bring Evolution back more frequently, it could mark another step forward for the division and give rising talent more chances to shine on one of the company's biggest stages.

Ash By Elegance addresses health issue that led to brief retirement

Ash By Elegance has addressed the health crisis that led to her brief in-ring retirement announced at TNA Victory Road in September 2025. At the time, Elegance vacated the TNA Knockouts Championship with only a statement that she was "no longer able to compete." She returned to the ring in January and has now provided details about her ongoing condition during interviews to promote TNA Rebellion on April 11 in her hometown of Cleveland.

Speaking to The Generation of Wrestling podcast, Elegance explained that she is still dealing with health issues that do not necessarily involve a traditional injury. She noted that what initially forced her retirement has "trickled" into her current condition and is part of an ongoing issue for which she is still seeking a full diagnosis.

"I'm still going through it, which is crazy. You don't have to have an injury. What I'm going through is a health issue. A lot of people, especially in my situation, I don't look sick. I feel good today, but tomorrow might not. So, it's definitely a health issue," Elegance said.

She described her situation as mysterious, explaining that medical professionals have struggled to identify the root cause. "My issue is weird. They call it like a medical mystery. I've done so many things, I spent numerous days in the ICU that nobody really knows about because I haven't put that out there yet until I honestly can find out really what this is. We're on a good track now," she continued.

Elegance indicated that the various health problems she has experienced are now consolidating into what may be a single diagnosis. "In the beginning, what had happened that kind of forced me into retirement has trickled now into this, and it's kind of all adding up to being this one diagnosis. I'm just not going to say it yet until it's really out there," she explained.

Despite her condition, Elegance maintains that she has been cleared to wrestle, though the unpredictability of her symptoms presents challenges. "I'm good to wrestle, it's just sometimes it's difficult because my body kind of goes a little wonky," she said. She also noted that she will be undergoing more tests in the coming week as she continues to seek answers.

In a separate interview with BCP+, also promoting Rebellion, Elegance revealed that her health has been "shaky" for the last couple of weeks but emphasized her determination to compete. "The last couple weeks have been a little shaky with me and my health. With that being said, I am more than determined, more than motivated, and wrestling is what keeps me going," she stated.

Elegance reiterated her commitment to sharing her complete story once she receives a proper diagnosis. "It's unfortunate that I have to say it is health issues that I'm going through and once I have the proper diagnosis and everything, I am going to come out and share my story with everything that has been going on, because I am a very open and honest person," she said.

At TNA Rebellion on April 11, Elegance is scheduled to team with her Elegance Brand stablemates Heather By Elegance and M By Elegance in a six-person Hardcore country match against the TNA Knockouts dream team of Mickie James, Taryn Terrell, and ODB.
