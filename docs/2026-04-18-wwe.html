🔥 WWE WrestleMania 42 Main Card and Hype

WWE WRESTLEMANIA 42 MAIN CARD AND HYPE

WWE's biggest annual spectacle WrestleMania 42 is set to light up Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas on April 18 and April 19, featuring the company's top talent and highest-profile matches of the year. The two-night event will stream on the ESPN App with select opening matches airing on ESPN networks.

The main events are locked in place after months of creative changes and injury-related adjustments. Undisputed WWE Champion Cody Rhodes will defend his title against Randy Orton in the Night One main event, while World Heavyweight Champion CM Punk will face Royal Rumble winner Roman Reigns in the Night Two main event. According to Paul Heyman, WWE's original plans called for different matches at different stages. Initially, everything was driving toward Seth Rollins vs. Roman Reigns for the World title, but Rollins' torn rotator cuff injury forced a pivot. The secondary plan was then supposed to be Bron Breakker vs. CM Punk for the World title and Cody Rhodes vs. Roman Reigns for the Undisputed title. However, Triple H decided against running Rhodes vs. Reigns for the third time in four years, leading to the current card. Heyman expressed frustration about not being involved in either main event for the first time since 2019, but he remains part of WrestleMania 42 as he will accompany Brock Lesnar in his match against Oba Femi on Night Two.

The injury-plagued build to WrestleMania 42 has been historically challenging. Beyond Rollins' issues, planned matches like Roman Reigns vs. Seth Rollins, Cody Rhodes vs. CM Punk, Rollins vs. Bron Breakker, and GUNTHER vs. Rey Mysterio all fell through at various planning stages due to injuries. WWE has also canceled or postponed several other matches including a potential three-way featuring Giulia, Tiffany Stratton, and Jordynne Grace for the Women's United States Championship, and a highly anticipated program between IYO SKY and Asuka that had been building for years. These changes have led to criticism about the overall card quality and storytelling leading into the event.

The women's tag team championship match represents one of the biggest questions heading into WrestleMania. The Bella Twins are scheduled to challenge Lash Legend and Nia Jax for the WWE Women's Tag Team Championship in a fatal four-way that also includes Charlotte Flair and Alexa Bliss plus Bayley and Lyra Valkyria. However, Nikki Bella suffered an ankle injury on the March 27 episode of SmackDown during a tag match and has been wearing a walking boot throughout her recovery. WWE continued promoting the match with Nikki included, even as reports emerged that she had not been medically cleared.

The situation created confusion heading into WrestleMania weekend. Reports indicated that Paige, the former WWE Divas Champion who departed the company in 2018, had re-signed with WWE and would be positioned to replace Nikki as Brie's tag team partner if the injury prevented Nikki from competing. However, at the WWE Hall of Fame Red Carpet show, Nikki Bella claimed she was still set to wrestle despite wearing a walking boot. She stated, "I'm just getting that extra rest in so I can be ready for tomorrow. I would never leave Brie's side, ever. I'm still in it, I'm still there tomorrow, and we're gonna go win those Tag Titles."

During a SmackDown promo, Brie Bella also addressed the situation, emphasizing that despite Nikki's ankle injury, they were moving forward. "Even with Nikki fighting through an injury, we are showing up. Nikki's rehabbing and fighting like hell to get back in the ring. At WrestleMania, we are not here to remind people who we once are. It's to show people who we still are." This message suggested determination but left the door open for a last-minute change. Multiple reports indicated that WWE was deliberately keeping the situation fluid, potentially planning a surprise reveal either way. Whether Nikki competes, Paige makes her return, or some other scenario unfolds will be one of WrestleMania's biggest wildcards.

The Night One main event between Cody Rhodes and Randy Orton has been heavily influenced by Pat McAfee's surprise involvement. McAfee was revealed as the mystery caller who orchestrated Orton's attack on Rhodes, leaving the WWE Champion bloody on commentary. The addition of McAfee to the storyline generated significant fan backlash, with many questioning his involvement at wrestling's biggest stage.

In a promo on SmackDown, Cody Rhodes addressed the situation directly, calling McAfee's involvement a curveball that fundamentally changed the match's dynamic. Rhodes explained that he originally expected to face Orton in a student-vs.-teacher scenario where he could prove to his mentor that he had evolved into a different animal. Instead, Rhodes characterized the current version of Orton as fake and desperate to achieve his 15th world title reign. Rhodes added, "If you're a Randy Orton fan and you want him to get 15, I don't think you wanted him to get 15 standing next to Pat McAfee and being something that he's never been before."

Cody also took aim at McAfee's broader behavior, stating that the broadcaster had inadvertently united wrestling fans on one point: "For the first time in the centuries that the sport of pro wrestling has existed, everyone can agree on one thing: No one wants Pat fucking McAfee at WrestleMania."

McAfee has leaned fully into his heel role heading into the event. At WWE World, the convention center in Las Vegas, he trolled fans who were counting down to a promised surprise. When the countdown ended, McAfee appeared on screen mocking the audience. He stated, "Surprise, nerds! The surprise that you thought was maybe going to be your favorite wrestler of all time, maybe somebody from AEW was going to sign with WWE at WrestleMania and you marks were going to get zipper burn all over yourself. Instead, well, well, well, Pat McAfee is smarter than all of you dumb marks yet again."

Triple H has addressed the McAfee controversy while promoting WrestleMania. When asked about the negative fan reaction, the WWE Chief Content Officer stated that the discourse is actually beneficial for the company. He said, "It's always pressure but the thing is talk is good. People are talking and that is a good thing. Pat, love him or hate him, Pat makes people talk, and I think that's what's important here is people are talking. This is gonna be an amazing show. This will be incredible and it will deliver on all levels." Levesque also hinted that McAfee may face consequences for his outspoken behavior, suggesting there is something on the horizon for the storyline.

The Orton-Rhodes storyline has generated genuine emotion both on-screen and off. In a segment on the final SmackDown before WrestleMania, Cody and CM Punk shared a moment together. Cody questioned whether Punk would betray him like Orton allegedly did, asking, "If we shake hands and hug right now, are you going to kick me in the balls and turn on me too?" Both wrestlers discussed their shared history and partnership, with Cody becoming emotional while praising Punk. Punk responded by saying he was not here to cry but to celebrate with the fans. The segment highlighted the interpersonal dynamics within the company while building toward WrestleMania's main events.

The Night Two main event between Roman Reigns and CM Punk features high personal stakes. Reigns won the Royal Rumble and earned this title opportunity against Punk, the World Heavyweight Champion. Their rivalry has been built on their storied history and personal animosity, with both stars appearing on ESPN programming to promote the match.

Roman Reigns made an emphatic statement about the match's significance. When asked what would happen if he lost to Punk at WrestleMania, Reigns said, "If I lose to CM Punk at WrestleMania, I don't belong in WWE anymore. I think if I were to lose on Sunday, I'd say my work is done." He elaborated by attacking Punk's credibility, claiming the champion brags about missing 10 years of work and his wealth, while questioning what good money is without a legacy to pass down.

However, Reigns also clarified that he does not have immediate retirement plans. Speaking about his future, he stated, "I'm 40 years young. With everything we have now—sports science, nutrition, all the information—careers have been extended. You grind it out earlier in your career, and then once you get toward the back end, that equity kicks in. And then you get into a different ballpark financially. I'm not going to leave the park now, you know what I mean? If the ATM machine's not broken, you're not going to run away from it. You're going to keep collecting that money. So I still feel like, creatively, there's so much left for me to do, and there's still a great deal of earning for me to take advantage of."

It should be noted that reports indicate WWE has deliberately allowed conspiracy theories about Reigns' contract status to circulate, as the company is holding silence on his situation to drive engagement ahead of the match. PWInsider reported that Reigns' contract situation was worked out with time to spare, and any rumors about the deal expiring are inaccurate.

Oba Femi's match against Brock Lesnar on Night Two has emerged as potentially the most consequential bout on the card. While no championship is on the line, the match represents a generational changing of the guard between WWE's aging veteran and its brightest emerging prospect. Lesnar has reunited with Paul Heyman, his longtime advocate, ahead of the contest. The match carries immense significance for Femi's career trajectory. Unlike the Punk-Reigns and Rhodes-Orton main events, where losses can be reversed relatively easily through rematches, a loss for Femi at this stage of his career could damage his momentum for months or years.

Former WWE star Big E has expressed enthusiasm for the match, noting that it feels custom-made for him given his appreciation for powerful competitors. He stated, "This is like this was booked for me. This is like they made it for me. When I saw Oba Femi, when I met him for the first time, he had something. Then I watched him on NXT and I thought, 'Yeah. This guy can go.'" Big E praised Femi's well-rounded skill set, noting his combination of power, speed, and technique. He suggested that if Femi loses to Lesnar, there would still be a way for him to continue his momentum, but it would be difficult.

Paul Heyman has hinted at potentially expanding his Vision faction at WrestleMania. The current lineup includes Logan Paul, Austin Theory, and IShowSpeed in a six-man tag team match against LA Knight and The Usos. Heyman has significant pull in storylines, and various reports suggest he could recruit additional talent into the faction post-WrestleMania, potentially including GUNTHER, Brock Lesnar, CM Punk, or emerging stars like Royce Keys, Trick Williams, and Oba Femi.

Emerging talent has received significant promotional attention heading into WrestleMania. Trick Williams will challenge Sami Zayn for the United States Championship on Night Two with Lil Yachty in his corner. During interviews, Williams teased that Lil Yachty intends to remain involved with WWE beyond WrestleMania, suggesting the rapper genuinely loves the business and wants to appear in smaller markets rather than just serve as a part-timer for the biggest events.

Tiffany Stratton earned a future Women's United States Championship opportunity by defeating Jordynne Grace in a Number One Contenders match on SmackDown. The bout was originally supposed to be a non-title three-way involving champion Giulia, but scheduling changes shifted it to a contenders match instead. Stratton finished Grace with the Prettiest Moonsault Ever, setting up a future title program.

Women's Division storylines have been significantly impacted by changes and cancellations. Beyond the Nikki Bella injury situation, the IYO SKY vs. Asuka program that had been building for years appears to be off the WrestleMania card. Reports indicate that SKY was definitively beaten by Jade Cargill on SmackDown to build momentum for Cargill's Women's Championship match against Rhea Ripley, rather than being protected for her Asuka program. Additionally, SKY's social media deletion of related content suggested the match had been pulled.

Rhea Ripley challenges Jade Cargill for the Women's Championship on Night Two, with WWE President Nick Khan identifying Ripley as potentially the company's biggest star in coming years. Khan stated, "I think there's a shot for a couple people, I think. Keep in mind Rhea is in her late 20s, Rhea Ripley. She's amazing. She has a chance. She's already up there."

Khan also expressed enthusiasm for several other rising talents, particularly naming Trick Williams as someone who naturally understands media and possesses authenticity that matches his on-screen presentation. Khan stated, "I like the fact that he's an SEC athlete. I like the fact that he sort of inherently understands media. I like the fact, I always look for people whose inner voice matches their outer voice. I look for that in wrestlers. You know, if your inner voice matches your outer voice, you have a shot. Trick Williams is Trick Williams. So I like that."

AJ Lee will defend the Women's Intercontinental Championship against Becky Lynch on WrestleMania, continuing their bitter rivalry. AJ has submitted Lynch three times over the past year, winning the title at Elimination Chamber 2026 after her third submission victory.

The intercontinental Ladder Match has also been promoted as a major highlight of WrestleMania, though specific details about participants have not been fully clarified in available reporting.

Other marquee matches on the card include GUNTHER vs. Seth Rollins, with The Ring General challenging Rollins after stepping in to help Paul Heyman and attack The Visionary. Rollins' return from his rotator cuff injury will be on full display in this contest.

Ric Flair, WWE's legendary figure, has offered high praise for Roman Reigns' WrestleMania promotion efforts. Flair stated, "This Morning Before Going To A Signing, I Had The Privilege Of Watching @WWERomanReigns On First Take. It Was The Greatest Interview I Have Ever Seen Of Any Athlete In Any Sport And Any Generation. So Damn Professional. Proud To Know You Roman Reigns!" Flair also addressed John Cena breaking his world title record by winning his 17th championship at last year's WrestleMania, expressing happiness for Cena while acknowledging the success other wrestlers have found in Hollywood.

The Under performer category has seen significant roster movement. Royce Keys won the Andre the Giant Memorial Battle Royal on SmackDown before WrestleMania, marking his official WWE TV debut as a main roster performer.

Celebrity involvement has been heavy throughout the build. Beyond Pat McAfee and Lil Yachty, Jelly Roll has been involved in the Cody Rhodes-Randy Orton storyline. I'm probably the biggest name that you ever had a fight with because you're not your father. You're not The Rock."

🔥 WWE SmackDown April 17 Go-Home Show

WWE SmackDown April 17 Go-Home Show Delivers Emotional Moments and Major Competition Ahead of WrestleMania 42

The final episode of WWE SmackDown before WrestleMania 42 packed the show with emotional segments, championship implications, and significant character development as the company's biggest event loomed just 24 hours away. Held in Las Vegas, the go-home show set the tone for numerous WrestleMania matchups while delivering unexpected twists and genuine moments of vulnerability from its top stars.

Cody Rhodes and CM Punk Share Emotional Moment

The closing segment of SmackDown featured an emotionally charged exchange between Cody Rhodes and CM Punk that nearly brought the Undisputed WWE Champion to tears. Rhodes began the segment in his characteristic high-energy style, quickly pivoting to seriousness as he focused on his upcoming WrestleMania match against Randy Orton for the world title. He questioned how much Orton has changed since his 2009 run as a villain, dismissing distractions like Pat McAfee, The Rock, and Travis Scott.

When CM Punk arrived wearing a formal suit for the Hall of Fame ceremony that followed the broadcast, the tone shifted into a mix of jokes, respect, and underlying tension. Punk humorously asked if Rhodes was worried about being kicked in the groin, then offered genuine support, noting how far both men have come to reach the WrestleMania main event stage. Punk advised Rhodes to stay focused against the Viper and referenced his influence from legends like John Cena.

As Punk prepared to leave, Rhodes stopped him, leading to one of the most touching moments of the evening. Rhodes praised Punk's journey, highlighting how he carved his own path in wrestling by training under Harley Race and connecting with legends like Terry Funk. He compared Punk to greats like Shawn Michaels and Macho Man Randy Savage, suggesting Punk could become the next "Mr. WrestleMania." Rhodes then delivered the most emotional moment, saying that Dusty Rhodes, his late father, would be extremely proud of Punk's accomplishments. As Rhodes finished the thought, he nearly broke down in tears, visibly struggling with the emotion of remembering his father. The two men shared an embrace before Punk, sensing the emotional weight of the moment, lightened the mood by saying he would save his tears for after beating Roman Reigns. Both then celebrated their titles with the crowd.

Randy Orton Addresses Pat McAfee Situation

Randy Orton opened the show with a promo that addressed his ongoing alliance with Pat McAfee, though he took pains to clarify the nature of their relationship. He insisted he doesn't need McAfee but rather wants him by his side, describing the ESPN host as "the only straight shooter in this whole company." Orton blamed Rhodes for everything negative that has happened to him in recent years, claiming Rhodes had been protecting him instead of focusing on his own career. He stated his goal is to capture his 15th world title with an RKO at WrestleMania.

However, critics noted that Orton's heel turn felt late in the storyline and that the claim McAfee had done more for him than Rhodes throughout his career was a blatant falsehood that stood out awkwardly. Earlier in the show, Nick Aldis handed McAfee a mysterious contract that was never explained, with McAfee signing it while Orton cut his promo. The ambiguity of what McAfee signed left uncertainty about whether WWE plans additional angles with this storyline even after WrestleMania.

Royce Keys Wins Andre the Giant Memorial Battle Royal

Royce Keys made a significant statement by winning the 2026 Andre the Giant Memorial Battle Royal in his second official match on WWE television. The 41-year-old, who made his WWE debut at the Royal Rumble in January after spending over five years in AEW, eliminated Talla Tonga in the match's closing sequence to claim the historic trophy. Keys had wrestled nine dark matches before finally debuting on SmackDown the previous week with a three-minute victory over Berto.

The battle royal featured a stacked field of 25 competitors, including Matt Cardona, Johnny Gargano, Ilja Dragunov, Aleister Black, Shiloh Hill, Joe Hendry, and international stars like Psycho Clown from AAA. Kit Wilson was the first eliminated after tripping over a planking Gargano. Joe Hendry received a special entrance, with his nameplate notably missing the NXT logo, signaling a potential call-up to the main roster.

As eliminations came rapidly, the final four emerged with Otis, Aleister Black, Talla Tonga, and Royce Keys remaining. Black eliminated multiple competitors throughout the match, including Joe Hendry and Ilja Dragunov, briefly appearing as the favorite to win. Keys, however, quietly built momentum while staying under the radar. When Black threw out Otis, Keys faced Tonga in the final matchup. Tonga attempted a chokeslam, but Keys escaped, delivered a spinebuster, and confidently pulled his strap before sending Tonga over the top rope with a clothesline to secure the victory. Keys celebrated by posing with the Andre trophy outside the ring as fireworks and pyro lit up the arena.

Bryan Alvarez reported that WWE has "big long-term plans" for Keys following the victory. While the Andre the Giant Memorial Battle Royal has a mixed history of producing sustained main event pushes, Keys enters with strong momentum following his impressive performance.

Sami Zayn Faces Crowd Rejection Despite Championship Status

Sami Zayn encountered unexpected fan rejection during his United States Championship segment on SmackDown, with Las Vegas fans openly chanting "We Want Melo!" in reference to Carmelo Hayes. This reaction caught Zayn by surprise, as he questioned what he had done wrong to be booed by the crowd despite being the reigning champion.

The segment began with Trick Williams calling out Zayn for attacking Lil Yachty with a Helluva Kick the previous week, claiming the assault cost him Yachty's presence in his WrestleMania entrance. Williams introduced a gingerbread man mascot as a mocking representation of Zayn. When the actual Zayn arrived, he was met with boos and the persistent "We Want Melo!" chants. Zayn attempted to brush off the reaction, claiming he was fighting for fans who had always supported him, though his frustration was evident.

Despite being the champion heading into WrestleMania, Zayn admitted he hasn't been enjoying things lately. He praised Williams for his growth before calling him an "unproven rookie." Williams fired back, declaring himself "the plan" and stating Zayn was cracking under pressure. The two men then brawled in the ring, with Zayn sending Williams to the outside before hitting the gingerbread man mascot with a Helluva Kick. However, Williams capitalized on the distraction, returning to deliver a Trickshot and standing tall holding the United States Championship.

The crowd's preference for Carmelo Hayes has clear reasoning. Hayes held a strong 91-day title reign before losing the United States Championship to Zayn in a controversial finish involving Williams' interference. Many fans believed Hayes deserved his spot at WrestleMania, leading to their vocal support for the absent champion. Williams has further amplified his position by releasing a diss track titled "Gingerbread Man" alongside rapper Lil Yachty, taking direct shots at Zayn's credibility and his past struggles with the Bloodline. In response, Zayn tagged rapper Kendrick Lamar on social media, asking for help in a nod to Lamar's famous diss track battles.

Jacob Fatu Arrives in Police Car, Escalates McIntyre Rivalry

Jacob Fatu made a dramatic entrance on SmackDown, arriving in a police car before delivering a promo about moving past his criminal history and warning Drew McIntyre of what's to come at WrestleMania. The visual callback to McIntyre's previous attempts to embarrass Fatu by bringing up his arrest and imprisonment history turned the narrative on Fatu, who owned his past rather than being shamed by it.

Standing atop the police car with a microphone, Fatu declared that being handcuffed in front of his family did not embarrass him. He made clear he is not the same person McIntyre keeps referencing and that he is not afraid of anything. Fatu warned that their unsanctioned WrestleMania match, with no rules and no officials, would feature action that even a SWAT team couldn't stop. This segment followed McIntyre's brutal attack the previous week, where he beat Fatu down with chairs, steel steps, and a Future Shock DDT on the floor before handcuffing him to the ring post while continuing the assault.

McIntyre responded not in the arena but through a social media video, refusing to "waste the chaos" by responding live. He explained he wanted to stay comfortable and save the violence for WrestleMania. In the video, McIntyre made clear he doesn't respect Fatu and dismissed him in favor of attending the Hall of Fame ceremony to see peers and legends. He accused Fatu of stealing everything from him, including his WWE Championship and his main event WrestleMania spot, before delivering a harsh final warning.

McIntyre's most personal attack came when he said, "Most of all, I feel bad for your kids, because after I'm done with you and your career goes down the toilet, you're gonna end up back in that police car. You're gonna end up back in that jail cell." He also criticized Fatu's family, suggesting none of his relatives would make it to WWE while standing out in their unwillingness to wear latex protection. McIntyre concluded by noting the two giant men beating each other to death in the first hour of WrestleMania Night One, which airs on ESPN2, will draw massive viewership and set a high bar for the rest of the show.

Women's United States Championship Number One Contender Decided

Tiffany Stratton earned her shot at WWE Women's United States Champion Giulia by defeating Jordynne Grace in a number one contender's match. Stratton secured the victory with the Prettiest Moonsault Ever in a clean finish, defeating Grace in their first meeting ever. The match took place at the end of SmackDown's second hour with significant implications for the Women's United States Championship picture.

Giulia watched the bout from ringside alongside Kiana James. Stratton, a former WWE Women's Champion and NXT Women's Champion, will be looking for her first Women's United States Championship reign after her Women's title run ended in November at the hands of Jade Cargill. The date and stipulation for the Giulia versus Stratton title match has yet to be officially announced, though all signs point to next week's SmackDown. Giulia will be defending her second reign that began in January, with Stratton's victory being her first defense in over three months.

Tag Team Championship Defense and Other Notable Matches

Damian Priest and R-Truth successfully defended the WWE Tag Team Championships against Kofi Kingston and Grayson Waller. Kingston and Waller dominated early portions of the match before Priest cleaned house with aggressive offense including an Old School rope walk maneuver. R-Truth performed his signature Five Knuckle Shuffle after Truth stood in New Day's corner and started the "New Day rocks!" chant with the crowd. The champions hit double Five Knuckle Shuffles before delivering a Shatter Machine-like maneuver to Waller for the victory.

In a Las Vegas Street Fight between the Wyatt Sicks and the MFTs, the MFTs emerged victorious when Solo Sikoa pinned Uncle Howdy with a Samoan Spike. The match featured kendo sticks, chairs, ring steps, and wild action throughout. Erick Rowan had a rare high-flying moment, jumping onto Talla Tonga during the chaos. Following the match, tension within the winning MFT faction became apparent, with Tama Tonga and Solo Sikoa engaging in an intense stare-down. This moment builds on recent backstage segments showing Tama conversing with Shinsuke Nakamura, teasing a potential split within the stable.

Alexa Bliss and Charlotte Flair competed against Bayley and Lyra Valkyria in another SmackDown bout. The match was part of the show's loaded card leading into WrestleMania.

Unexpected Segments and Character Development

Maryse returned to WWE television for the first time since Evolution 2 to participate in a vow renewal ceremony with The Miz. The segment was meant to celebrate their marriage ahead of WrestleMania Week in Las Vegas. However, the moment descended into chaos when Danhausen interrupted, claiming multiple roles in the ceremony. When Kit Wilson misspoke and told Danhausen he could "curse the bride," Danhausen seized the opportunity, declaring Maryse cursed. The Miz immediately reacted, going after Danhausen, but in the melee, Maryse was knocked face-first into the wedding cake. This marked Danhausen's ninth successful curse since his WWE debut earlier in the year. The Miz and Danhausen's dynamic continues to develop as an interesting counterpart to other comedic team storylines on the roster.

Overall Assessment

The April 17 SmackDown successfully built momentum for WrestleMania 42 while delivering emotional character moments, unexpected crowd reactions, and significant developments across multiple divisions. The show balanced high-stakes competition with vulnerability, setting up numerous compelling WrestleMania encounters while creating new angles and questions that will extend beyond the showcase of the immortals weekend.

STEPHANIE MCMAHON BECOMES 2026 WWE HALL OF FAME HEADLINER WITH FAMILY INDUCTION

The 2026 WWE Hall of Fame ceremony kicked off with Stephanie McMahon's induction, making her the first inductee to be honored during the April 17 event at Dolby Live at Park MGM in Las Vegas. She was inducted by her mother Linda McMahon and her three daughters — Aurora Rose Levesque, Murphy Claire Levesque, and Vaughn Evelyn Levesque — alongside her husband Paul "Triple H" Levesque.

A video package highlighted McMahon's extensive career in WWE, showcasing her time as an on-screen personality, her role in pioneering women's wrestling, and her behind-the-scenes work as an executive. Charlotte Flair and Trish Stratus appeared in the video segment dedicated to her contributions to the Women's Revolution, stating that women's wrestling in WWE would not be the same without her.

Stephanie's three daughters spoke first, with each emphasizing how their mother made them feel "seen" and helped break barriers for women everywhere. They also noted they consider being compared to their mother the highest compliment. Linda McMahon then delivered her induction speech, praising her granddaughters and recalling stories from Stephanie's childhood, including a time when Shane McMahon bodyslammed his sister at home. Linda directly named Vince McMahon as she discussed Stephanie's transition into the creative side of WWE, noting that she left the company for a "somewhat different path" but was able to watch from the outside as Stephanie became Co-CEO and Chairwoman. Linda praised Stephanie for marrying Paul Levesque and producing three beautiful daughters.

When Stephanie made her entrance, she was visibly emotional. She immediately noted that "the only person who is missing is my grandmother." She shared that she had been told she might not be able to conceive naturally but through modern science and the grace of God, she has three beautiful daughters. She then expanded on her daughters' achievements in sports before pivoting to her mother.

Stephanie made a poignant statement during her speech, saying "Truthfully, my mom should be the one going into the Hall of Fame tonight. If it weren't for her, and my father, Vince McMahon, none of us would be here tonight. Especially not me." When she mentioned Vince McMahon directly, the crowd responded with loud cheers, and once she finished thanking him, chants of "Thank you, Vince!" erupted throughout the venue. This reaction was particularly notable given that Vince McMahon has been largely absent from WWE since his forced retirement and the legal issues stemming from former employee Janel Grant's lawsuit alleging sexual assault and sex trafficking. His likeness has also been removed from various parts of WWE programming in recent years.

Stephanie then thanked her brother Shane, stating "No matter what has happened or what will happen in this life, I will always love you all." She reflected on her upbringing in wrestling, recounting how she simply accepted that Pat Patterson was gay, had her first dinner date with Andre the Giant at age 14, and once received drinks from the Iron Sheik. She introduced a package for Connor's Cure, WWE's pediatric cancer charity, and thanked Jimmy Spagnolo, a pediatric cancer survivor.

The inductee also thanked her in-laws for welcoming her into their extended family, and she specifically thanked Nick Khan and his family, Ari Emanuel, and Mark Shapiro. Most importantly, she thanked her husband, saying "There was something irresistible about him" and noting that the fact he was "forbidden" because he was talent only added to the intrigue. She called him her soulmate and hero, and for the record, stated "you flirted with me first."

Stephanie revealed that a few years ago she felt what she had with WWE had been lost. She then cut a promo in character as the Billion Dollar Princess to accept her induction, calling herself a "Boss B***" and name-checking Sami Zayn as a B+ player. This reference harked back to the 2013-2014 B+ Player storyline that dominated WWE during Triple H and The Authority's treatment of Daniel Bryan, a narrative that Stephanie was heavily involved in promoting. Bryan ultimately overcame the angle and won the title at WrestleMania 30 after the "Yes!" Movement forced WWE to pivot his direction. Now that Sami Zayn has inherited the B+ Player moniker, only time will tell what he does with it. Sami currently walks into WrestleMania 42 as United States Champion, facing Trick Williams in a singles match with the title on the line.

DEMOLITION FINALLY INDUCTED AFTER DECADES WAITING

Demolition's long-awaited Hall of Fame induction came courtesy of fellow tag team specialists Haku, Arn Anderson, and Warlord. The legendary tag team consisting of Bill Eadie and Barry Darsow was finally honored for their contributions to professional wrestling after years of speculation about their worthiness.

Warlord opened the induction speeches by calling Demolition one of the greatest tag teams of all time. He highlighted their legendary 478-day reign as WWE Tag Team Champions — a record that was eventually broken by the New Day and later the Usos. Warlord's references to "WWF" were repeatedly censored during his speech. Haku recalled wrestling Demolition alongside Andre the Giant at WrestleMania VI, saying "It was an honor to be in the match with you guys" and thanking them for taking care of him during their encounters. Arn Anderson concluded the induction speeches, stating that Demolition "broke the standard" and "beat the crap out of everybody, and I mean everybody."

When Demolition took the stage, Barry Darsow spoke first, recalling his initial meeting with Vince McMahon about the Demolition characters. He expressed deep gratitude toward Eadie, saying "All I did was follow his lead in the matches, and they were phenomenal." Darsow also thanked Brian Adams, also known as Crush, who was part of the group during its evolution.

Bill Eadie remarked that he never thought this day would come to fruition. He thanked numerous wrestling luminaries including Andre the Giant and Antonio Inoki, as well as Vince McMahon and his father, Vince Sr. Both Darsow and Eadie thanked their family members, manager Mr. Fuji, their various opponents throughout their careers, and the fans who supported them. Darsow suggested they do a run-in at WrestleMania during the ceremony. Eadie closed out Demolition's acceptance speech by recalling that he had been asked how it would feel to be a WWE Hall of Famer. He quoted the film "Dumb and Dumber," saying "I like it, I like it a lot."

DENNIS RODMAN INDUCTED BY NWO STABLE MATES

Dennis Rodman was inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame as the ceremony's celebrity inductee, with fellow nWo members Kevin Nash and Sean Waltman performing the honors. Rodman came out to the Wolfpac theme, bringing his signature energy to the proceedings.

Nash highlighted Rodman's previous induction into the NBA Hall of Fame, playing up the "Bad Boy" legend of the basketball icon. He recalled the famous story of Rodman skipping NBA Playoffs practice to appear on WCW Nitro smoking cigars, winning the game, and then being fined considerably less than he made from the WCW appearance — a testament to his marketability and star power.

Rodman made his entrance to the original nWo theme. He mentioned that Stephanie McMahon had prepared a speech for him, but he chose to amble on about WWE being the greatest sports organization in the world, speaking extemporaneously rather than following a prepared script. He discussed how many wrestlers have been physically damaged by the business but claimed that WWE as an organization has never turned its back on anyone.

The former NBA star thanked Jimmy Hart and Eric Bischoff before becoming visibly emotional while discussing his neighbor. After composing himself, he thanked Nick Hogan, explaining that Nick had been going through hard times and his father, Hulk Hogan, always stood by him and never gave up on him. Rodman expressed pride in Nick's journey. He concluded his speech by doing an impression of "the Hulkster" while paying tribute to the statue honoring him at WWE World, offering one final tribute to the late WWE Hall of Famer.

AJ STYLES JOINS HALL OF FAME FOLLOWING RETIREMENT

AJ Styles was inducted into the 2026 WWE Hall of Fame class following his retirement at the 2026 Royal Rumble after a 28-year career. Styles had spoken about his Hall of Fame induction on The Nikki & Brie Show prior to the ceremony, expressing some anxiety about the event. He admitted "Anxious to get it over with, I think. When I'm nervous or whatever it is I may be feeling, I just wanna get it over with. I don't wanna have to deal with it."

When asked about who would be inducting him, Styles indicated it was a difficult decision, stating "It was (hard), but some of them couldn't induct me. That's how that is. I get it, it's fine. I found the right person." However, he did not reveal the identity of his inductor, though possibilities had been discussed among fans and wrestling analysts. The Undertaker had appeared on WWE Raw during Styles' retirement celebration to announce his Hall of Fame induction. Luke Gallows and Karl Anderson appeared during that retirement segment, suggesting they were in good standing with Styles and could potentially perform the induction. Other possibilities included Chris Park (formerly known as Abyss in TNA), Bobby Roode, and Xavier Woods, all of whom had histories with Styles dating back to his TNA days.

Prior to his induction, Styles had offered criticism of the WWE Hall of Fame ceremony itself. During his podcast "Phenomenally Retro," he expressed that the ceremony takes place too frequently and runs too long. He suggested that WWE could benefit from holding the Hall of Fame every other year rather than annually, stating "I really believe we could probably do the Hall of Fame every other year, or maybe put some years in between and make it more special. I think it's going to be really difficult to do this every year. Just a suggestion."

Styles also commented on the length of the speeches, noting that while WWE likely gives inductees time limits, the ceremony often runs long anyway. He acknowledged understanding the impulse, saying "I'm sure once I get up there, I'm thinking, 'This won't be long,' but then you'll talk about things that are important to you." He compared the situation to WrestleMania becoming so lengthy that being the main event became undesirable for performers.

POSTHUMOUS LEGACY INDUCTEES AND IMMORTAL MOMENT

The 2026 Hall of Fame class also honored wrestlers posthumously in the Legacy wing. Sid Eudy, known as Sycho Sid and Sid Vicious, was inducted after passing away in August 2024. Sid was a two-time WWF Champion and two-time WCW World Heavyweight Champion, making significant impacts in both major wrestling promotions throughout his career.

Bad News Brown, whose real name was Allen Coage, was also honored in the Legacy wing. Coage was a 1976 Olympic bronze medalist in judo who brought legitimate combat credentials to professional wrestling. He is remembered for his victory in the WrestleMania IV battle royal and remained a notable figure in WWE history from his era until his passing in 2007.

The Immortal Moment Award was presented to Hulk Hogan versus Andre the Giant from WrestleMania III, one of the most iconic matches in wrestling history. The match is highlighted by Hogan's legendary bodyslam in front of a reported 93,173 fans at the Pontiac Silverdome. This recognition made Hulk Hogan a three-time Hall of Famer while Andre the Giant became a two-time inductee, further cementing the legendary status of both performers and their historic encounter.

CEREMONY DETAILS AND LOGISTICS

The 2026 WWE Hall of Fame Induction Ceremony took place on April 17 at Dolby Live at Park MGM in Las Vegas, immediately following that evening's SmackDown broadcast. The ceremony aired at midnight Eastern (9 p.m. Pacific) via ESPN Unlimited in the United States, with international viewers able to watch on YouTube.

Michael Cole and The Miz served as hosts for the event. Cole remarked on his undefeated streak at WrestleMania, while Miz mentioned that this would be the 25th time Cole had called a WrestleMania, referring to him as a GOAT. The Undertaker narrated the opening video for the ceremony.

The 2026 Hall of Fame featured a red carpet special hosted by Peter Rosenberg and Jackie Redmond in the time between SmackDown and the main ceremony. Various WWE Superstars and personalities appeared for interviews, with many expressing excitement about specific inductees. Carmelo Hayes mentioned looking forward to Dennis Rodman's induction, while Kelani Jordan spoke about AJ Styles being a mentor to her. Je'Von Evans also discussed Styles' mentorship. The Miz and Maryse spoke with Big E about the event, while R-Truth discussed Little Jimmy and other topics.

John Cena appeared on the red carpet and spoke about there being much less stress being around WrestleMania when retired. He discussed AJ Styles extensively during his interview. CM Punk, appearing with AJ Lee, stated that the Hall of Fame was his favorite night of the year. Big E mentioned working on a segment with Stephanie McMahon during the red carpet portion. Becky Lynch and Seth Rollins, along with Big E, discussed the possibility of Lynch and Rollins being inducted on the same night someday.

Ticket sales for the 2026 Hall of Fame were notably affordable on a last-minute basis. With 3,054 total tickets available, 2,549 had been distributed even a few hours before SmackDown. According to reports, 505 tickets remained unsold during the early hours of April 17. The cheapest standard admission ticket was priced at $62.37, with last-minute tickets available for just $35 in the hours before the show — a significant discount to fill remaining seats.

The 2026 Hall of Fame ceremony took place as part of WrestleMania 42 weekend, with WrestleMania 42 itself scheduled for Saturday, April 18 and Sunday, April 19 at Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas. The Hall of Fame served as the traditional precursor to the largest wrestling event of the year, maintaining its status as a celebration of wrestling history and legacy before the current generation competed for the company's top championships.

🔥 Danhausen WWE vs AEW Career Reflections

Danhausen has become an overnight superstar in WWE after joining the company at Elimination Chamber in February 2026, a dramatic shift from his experience during his previous four-year tenure with AEW between 2022 and 2026. The "very nice, very evil" curse-giving character recently opened up about the stark differences in how he was utilized between the two promotions, revealing candid reflections on his lack of television time in his former company.

During an appearance on Cheap Heat with Peter Rosenberg, Danhausen corrected the host's assertion that he was seen frequently in AEW programming. The character revealed that despite being with the company for four years, he actually wrestled in only three or four televised AEW matches. Instead of being a regular presence on television, Danhausen spent much of his time working the independent scene, performing at conventions, and building his brand through online content. He explained that his perceived visibility came from self-produced vlogs and backstage videos that he shared with his audience, creating an illusion of constant presence even though he was rarely featured on actual AEW broadcasts.

"Maybe three or four (times I wrestled in AEW). You didn't see much of me in AEW. You definitely did not. You saw me online a lot. Perception is reality. Danhausen would do vlogs backstage on his own and do videos with his enemies and his friendhausens, and he'd put those out there so maybe it seemed like I was doing more than I was. I was not there. I was doing the independent scenes. About three a weekend or doing conventions and some sort like New York Comic Con and things like that," Danhausen explained.

The tone of Danhausen's remarks suggested frustration with his utilization in AEW, particularly given his four-year commitment to the organization. This sentiment contrasts sharply with his current status in WWE, where he has quickly become a staple of television programming in just a few months. The character's meteoric rise in WWE appears to have vindicated his abilities and drawing power, which remained largely untapped during his previous tenure.

Danhausen has been capitalizing on his newfound WWE platform by making rare media appearances to promote WrestleMania 42, scheduled for April 18 and 19 from Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas. His appearances have extended beyond wrestling programming and into mainstream media, including an unexpected encounter on ESPN's First Take. During the segment with Stephen A. Smith, tensions apparently escalated when the ESPN personality appeared less than thrilled with Danhausen's presence, prompting the WWE character to respond by placing a curse on Smith, staying true to his character's gimmick.

Beyond the ESPN appearance, Danhausen has been making headlines for various curse-related activities tied to his character. Earlier in the week, he engaged in a social media exchange regarding uncursing the New York Mets after being asked by former WWE head writer Brian Gewirtz, a longtime Mets fan. Gewirtz reportedly offered to get Danhausen's face on a WWE production truck in exchange for removing the curse on the team. Danhausen agreed to the deal and demanded proof that Gewirtz would follow through on his end of the bargain.

However, when the Mets continued to lose games after Danhausen claimed to have uncursed them, including a 12-4 loss to the Chicago Cubs, the character explained the situation to Fox News Digital. Danhausen attributed the continued failure of the curse removal to non-payment from Gewirtz, describing the situation as a "layaway" scenario. "I did un-curse the Mets. But it didn't work because, I believe it was Brian Gewirtz who did not pay Danhausen. He did not send me my money so it did not take full effect. Once I have the money, perhaps it will actually work because right now it's probably about a half of an un-cursing. It's like a layaway situation," Danhausen told Fox News Digital on Friday morning.

Danhausen's WWE debut featured an elaborate entrance coordinated by his wife, who served as one of the dancers in his entrance presentation. When discussing his debut on Cheap Heat, Danhausen emphasized that WWE provided him with the most theatrical and grand entrance possible, featuring him being delivered like the classic Dracula character alongside a group of performers known as the Ghoulhausens. His wife's involvement in choreographing the dancers' routine demonstrated her active participation in supporting his career transition to WWE.

Prior to WrestleMania 42, Danhausen's visibility has been significantly boosted by a Goodyear Blimp image featuring the character surfacing on social media, with the image depicting him cursing Las Vegas. Reports indicate that despite not being officially advertised for an in-ring match at WrestleMania 42, the character will be featured in a segment during the weekend festivities, with rumors suggesting a potential involvement with WrestleMania 42 host John Cena.

The trajectory of Danhausen's career demonstrates the importance of proper character utilization and television exposure in professional wrestling. His four years in AEW, while allowing him to develop his character and maintain a cult following through independent work and social media presence, resulted in minimal televised opportunities. His transition to WWE has proven that the same character, when given adequate television time and company support, can become a prominent part of programming and generate mainstream crossover appeal. The contrast between his AEW and WWE experiences underscores how critical it is for wrestling promotions to effectively deploy their roster to a television audience in order to maximize wrestler visibility and connection with fans.

Trish Stratus Career Reflections

Trish Stratus is making it clear that her WWE appearances are not driven by nostalgia or paydays, but by purpose and creative challenge. Speaking on Busted Open Radio, Stratus explained that she carefully evaluates every opportunity before agreeing to return. For her, the deciding factor is whether the role offers something new as a performer.

"From a personal perspective, I want to make sure I'm doing something different that's challenging me as a performer," Stratus stated. She pointed to her recent feud with Becky Lynch as an example of that mindset. Rather than returning in a familiar role, she actively pushed for a different direction.

"The first one going back with Becky… it was, yeah, go back babyface. I said if I can come back and turn heel in this and make it a little bit of a longer story, I'd love that to challenge myself… just to make something different interesting," Stratus explained. By choosing to work against type and develop a more complex narrative, she ensured her return would feel fresh rather than repetitive.

She also emphasized the importance of working with newer talent, using her pairing with Zoey Stark as a key example. "I also want to make sure that I'm touching another generation in a certain way… working with Zoey was a great way to get someone brand new in," Stratus said. This focus on mentorship and elevating rising stars reflects her commitment to contributing meaningfully to the current product.

Despite already cementing her legacy, Stratus is focused on making each return count. Her approach reflects a desire to contribute to the current product in a meaningful way rather than simply revisiting past success. That perspective highlights a broader shift in how legends are used in modern wrestling.

Rather than short nostalgia-driven appearances, there is increasing value in integrating veteran talent into ongoing stories that elevate newer stars. When executed well, it creates a bridge between eras, giving credibility to rising talent while allowing established names to evolve their characters. Stratus embracing that philosophy suggests her future appearances could continue to feel relevant instead of purely celebratory, potentially reshaping how WWE approaches its legendary performers in the years ahead.

Liv Morgan New WWE Persona

Liv Morgan is leaning fully into her evolving WWE character, and her new "Trouble" music video is a major part of that transformation. During her WrestleMania vlog, Morgan explained how the project unexpectedly grew from a simple request into a full creative venture. What started as a conversation about new entrance music quickly turned into something far outside her comfort zone.

"You know, the music video is not anything I'd ever would've thought for myself. We just wanted new music and they asked would I sing it. And I'm like, I'm not a singer, but for sure, I'll give it my best go," Morgan said. That initial step led to even bigger opportunities, including performing in a full music video despite her own doubts. "And that turned into, oh wow, we love the track, do you wanna do a music video? And I'm like, wow. Like I am not a dancer, but I'd love to take on that task."

Morgan made it clear that the experience itself mattered more than how it's received, embracing the challenge regardless of public reaction. "Whether people love it or hate it, I had such a blast doing it and such a blast doing something that was out of my comfort zone. But I am super proud of what we put out," she explained.

The "Trouble" video, released on April 13, 2026, reflects WWE's commitment to pushing Morgan's new identity. The visuals lean heavily into themes of chaos and rebellion, reinforcing the direction her character has taken in recent months. The video opens with Morgan setting a car on fire, immediately establishing a destructive tone. From there, it features scenes of her getting tattooed, performing choreographed dance sequences, and embracing an unpredictable, almost unhinged persona.

One of the most striking moments comes toward the end, when Morgan is shown being taken into police custody before posing for a mug shot, a clear callback to her real-life arrest photo that previously circulated online. This bold creative choice underscores the willingness to blur the lines between character and reality in service of her new identity.

The project signals WWE's broader approach to character development, extending storytelling beyond matches into music and visual media. For Morgan, it represents a shift from traditional presentation to a more immersive identity built around attitude and unpredictability. With WrestleMania 42 approaching, Morgan's evolution is happening on multiple fronts. She is not just entering the event as a competitor, but as a fully realized character with a distinct visual and thematic direction. If the presentation connects, it could elevate her beyond the division and position her as one of WWE's most creatively defined stars.

WWE House Shows Expansion

WWE President Nick Khan has addressed the company's current stance on live events, making it clear that there are no immediate plans to significantly expand the house show schedule. Khan made these comments while appearing on The Bill Simmons Podcast ahead of WrestleMania 42.

Khan's position directly responds to recent public support from Undisputed WWE Champion Cody Rhodes, who has been vocal about bringing back a more consistent house show schedule. Rhodes has even suggested a trial run of one loop per month as a potential solution. His argument centers on helping talent stay sharp in the ring while also strengthening locker room chemistry through increased time on the road together.

However, Khan acknowledged the reasoning behind WWE's current lighter schedule approach, pointing to the long-term toll that heavy travel once took on performers. During his podcast appearance, Khan referenced the common narrative seen in wrestling documentaries, explaining the dangers of the old model: "Think about every wrestling documentary you've ever seen. Usually about wrestlers in the 80s started off with, 'I was on the road 300 days a year.' It's no way, and then you saw the results of that. Forget family life, but you saw what people would do to make sure that their bodies could perform at that level."

Currently, WWE's live event schedule remains selective and strategic. House shows are largely reserved for international tours and the company's traditional holiday loop in the United States. WWE is set to tour Europe this summer, with additional live events planned in South America later in the year.

Khan's comments suggest that while the idea of expanding live events has been discussed internally, WWE is prioritizing talent health and sustainability over a return to the demanding schedules of previous eras.

Rhea Ripley WWE Future Star

Rhea Ripley has spoken candidly about her struggles with an eating disorder and the toll that online negativity has taken on her mental health. Appearing on Pod Meets World, Ripley discussed how her challenges have fluctuated over time, noting that her environment and support system play a major role in how she manages those struggles.

"I find it goes through waves where it's really difficult, and then sometimes really easy. This past year has been really difficult because I've found that if my circle at home isn't at peace, that's when I really struggle," Ripley explained.

Ripley emphasized the importance of surrounding herself with positive influences, crediting her close relationships for helping her stay grounded. "That's why I try to surround myself with people I know are there for me and care about me, because that's my strong point. My husband is my strong point. My dogs are my strong point. My friend Tommy, who's at our house every day—we work out together—it's a positive environment."

She also addressed the challenges of dealing with constant criticism on social media, particularly the difficulty of separating her professional presence from personal attacks. "It's really hard going onto Twitter. I just need to delete the app. It's really hard going on and trying to make it a work environment, but because it is a work environment, I get all these things coming up on my 'For You' page as soon as I open the app. It's just negativity toward me. I can't even escape it. I can't even doom scroll like a normal person without seeing things about me—about my body, my booking, me as a human. They don't know who I am."

Ripley explained that sharing her experiences publicly has become important to her, especially in hopes of helping others who may be going through similar situations. "It does get really tiring, but that's also why I like to share what I'm going through. I tried to keep the eating disorder quiet for a while because I was still figuring it out, but I try to be as vocal as possible with my struggles. If it's going to help someone, then why not talk about it? I needed that when I was a kid. If I knew my idols were going through these struggles and still powering through—being amazing—and I look up to them, then I'd feel like I could get through it too."

She also spoke about the root of her struggles, linking them to stress and a loss of control in various aspects of her life. "With the eating situation, it definitely stems from stress and not being in control. That's my biggest thing—I like being in control. But when I can't control work, my schedule, or at the time my home life—with a now ex-friend who was terrorizing my life—and then going to Australia and not being able to control how crowds reacted to my peers, or the pressure they were under 24/7, and then getting thrown into all that media—it was just a lot all at once. It kind of broke me for a little bit."

Ripley also addressed speculation surrounding her physical changes, pushing back against rumors. "It wasn't seen by everyone yet, but now it is. And I saw so many things out there like, 'Oh, she had to lose weight because of back pain,' or 'she's off steroids.' We get tested. I'm not on anything."

Ripley's openness highlights the ongoing challenges faced by performers in the spotlight, as well as the importance of mental health awareness within and beyond the wrestling industry.
