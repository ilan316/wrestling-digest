🔥 TNA blocking MJF vs. Nic Nemeth match

TNA Wrestling's decision to block Nic Nemeth from facing MJF at an independent wrestling show has sparked considerable controversy within the professional wrestling community. The match, which had been approved and heavily promoted for weeks, was scheduled to headline Create-A-Pro Wrestling's Monumental Moment event on May 1 in Melville, New York, but was cancelled three weeks before the event when TNA President Carlos Silva pulled his talent from matches involving wrestlers signed to other promotions, particularly AEW.

Pat Buck, owner and founder of Create-A-Pro Wrestling and current AEW Vice President of Talent Development, issued a comprehensive statement addressing the situation directly to PWInsider. Buck made clear that he rarely speaks publicly on such matters but felt compelled to do so in this instance. "I don't usually speak on things like this, but this one's too ridiculous to ignore," Buck began, directly attributing the decision to Carlos Silva and TNA Wrestling. He emphasized that the company forced a change to the show's main event three weeks out after it had already been approved and promoted, questioning who such a decision actually benefits.

Buck went to great lengths to explain what Create-A-Pro represents within the wrestling landscape. He described it as a training school with a promotion focused on developing talent while running consistent live events, characterizing it as "a school, dojo, and a mom and pop business... just with two dads." He stressed that live events serve as necessary training tools and that wrestlers need these opportunities to keep their skills sharp and earn money. Buck pointed out that live events are disappearing even at the highest levels because they are not cost-effective, with money concentrated in television. He positioned Create-A-Pro as providing a direct service to wrestlers, helping them make money while maintaining their in-ring abilities.

Buck acknowledged that technically talent was not pulled from the event, but he made clear that altering a main event match at the last minute had the same practical impact. He emphasized that anyone who has actually promoted shows would understand the damage caused by such a change. He expressed frustration at what he perceived as a lack of professional courtesy, stating that when something changes that affects another promoter's show, the expectation is to make it right through various means, whether sending replacement talent, strengthening the card, or finding another way to return value. He criticized TNA for creating the problem and then walking away from it without attempting to remedy the situation.

Buck specifically called out the decision as "unnecessary and cringe," and stressed that promoters should meet people with grace rather than chaos. He positioned Create-A-Pro as not a competing media company but rather a training ground that built its own platform to develop talent without a TV deal, streaming service, or attachment to a larger company. He noted that between Create-A-Pro and WrestlePro, his organizations run approximately 25 events per year while reinvesting everything back into leases, rings, and equipment.

Buck then invoked Create-A-Pro's historical relationship with TNA, reminding the company that it had previously needed to rent equipment from his promotion to operate in the New York market. He pointed out that when TNA needed content and additional matches, talent was placed on Create-A-Pro and WrestlePro shows because there was not enough content to support TNA's needs at that time. He questioned whether anyone at TNA had been informed about this past positive working relationship. Buck noted that years ago, TNA used Create-A-Pro students for meaningful extra work, but suggested this dynamic has changed to "carrot dangling, ring crew, and helping with catering."

Buck highlighted Create-A-Pro's broader role in professional wrestling, stating that it "has been a pipeline and a support system to just about every major company in wrestling." He added that most people in the industry recognize this fact, with the notable exception of WWE's ID program, which he suggested acts as though Create-A-Pro does not exist. He expressed willingness to believe that Carlos Silva may not know who he is, but emphasized that anyone in an executive position should understand the broader wrestling community and the professional etiquette required when operating within it.

Buck used this moment to praise his own employer, Tony Khan at AEW, suggesting that Khan could provide Silva with a "quick crash course on how to protect your brand while still helping the overall scene." Buck stated he was "glad to work for someone who actually champions the entire sport, not tries to shrink it." He added that while he is on AEW's payroll, his criticism comes from experience rather than corporate influence.

In closing, Buck reiterated that Create-A-Pro will adjust and move forward regardless of this situation, as they have done before, but questioned whether TNA's approach actually serves the goal of growing the wrestling industry or even TNA's own company. He concluded with the phrase "Keep it up!" suggesting heavy sarcasm about TNA's direction.

This incident represents part of a broader pattern of disrupted bookings. Beyond the Nemeth versus MJF match, Leon Slater versus Ricochet was cancelled at WrestleCon's Mark Hitchcock Memorial SuperShow, and Moose was blocked from appearing at a Maple Leaf Pro Wrestling event. These cancellations indicate that TNA's policy affects multiple independent promotions and wrestlers across the industry.

AEW World Champion MJF has also been vocal about the situation, calling into Busted Open Radio on SiriusXM to further address the matter on April 9. MJF questioned the logic of TNA's decision, asking whether preventing young talent from wrestling against wrestlers from other promotions actually helps those young wrestlers grow or improve their craft. He emphasized that TNA barely pays these young talents any significant money, making the restrictions even more problematic for their financial wellbeing. MJF directly called the actions of Carlos Silva "disgusting," stating that he found it difficult to understand how anyone could support TNA or Silva at this point.

The controversy reflects growing tension between TNA and independent wrestlers regarding creative freedom and earning opportunities. MJF's criticism focused on the practical impact on young talent development, emphasizing that blocking these opportunities prevents young wrestlers from putting food on their plates while simultaneously limiting their ability to grow as performers. His comments resonated with Buck's broader concerns about the importance of independent wrestling events in developing and supporting professional wrestlers.

The situation highlights broader questions within professional wrestling about talent restrictions, independent promoter relations, and the ecosystem that supports wrestling development. Create-A-Pro's historical role as a training facility and breeding ground for professional wrestlers has positioned it as an important part of the wrestling landscape, making TNA's decision to restrict talent appearances particularly contentious in the eyes of industry observers and those directly affected.

🔥 EC3 return to TNA at TNA Rebellion

EC3 is heading back to TNA Wrestling. On April 10, 2026, TNA Wrestling posted a teaser video featuring a mysterious lockbox that contained unmistakable clues to the former champion's return. The video revealed a Cleveland Indians block 'C' hat, a foam finger, and wrestling trunks that belonged to EC3, with TNA announcing the reveal would happen at Rebellion, taking place Saturday, April 11 from the Wolstein Center in Cleveland, Ohio.

EC3 competed for TNA from 2013 to 2018 and won the World Heavyweight Championship twice during that tenure. He also held the Impact Grand Championship. After his initial run, he signed with WWE in January 2018 but had a lackluster stint that ended in 2020. He then moved on to start Control Your Narrative, competed for Ring of Honor, and more recently worked for the National Wrestling Alliance, where he won the NWA National Championship and NWA World Heavyweight Championship. EC3 announced his departure from the NWA in January 2026.

The last time EC3 competed in TNA was at Bound For Glory 2020 in a match against Moose, which was a one-off appearance. Full-time, he had wrapped up with the company in 2018 before his WWE signing. Currently, he runs Exodus Pro, an independent promotion based out of Cleveland, which is significant given that Rebellion is taking place in Cleveland.

In response to the teaser, EC3 posted a cryptic message on X/Twitter using biblical language. He wrote: "Threezus 2:37 – And the Ark was opened before the congregation. And The Top 1% was inside. And it had not changed. Had not diminished. Had it apologized for its existence. And the people saw. And Rebellion was no longer just a name. It was a warning." This message references "The Top 1%" persona he crafted during his original TNA tenure.

Recently, EC3 had clarified comments he made about being blackballed from wrestling, stating that the remark was sarcastic and that wrestling media had misinterpreted his tone and context. During his clarification, he seemingly teased a potential return to TNA or at least a return to the "Top 1%" persona. He stated: "Blackballed? Please. The only thing my balls are is sunned. No. I have been 'building.' Quietly. Developing talent. Crafting Exodus Pro as a prestige independent. Working on a 'creative conclusion' to The Top 1% that will blow 'wrestling brains loads.' Blackballed implies someone has power over me. Name one."

In a previous appearance where he was asked about a potential TNA return, EC3 indicated that he wasn't chasing deals but rather waiting for moments. He explained: "I don't chase three-letter brands, man. I chase moments. The top one percent returning. Let me quote John Cena: 'The time is now'—to bring the top one percent and the idea back. But is this a nostalgia run, or something worse? Time will tell." He also emphasized the importance of alignment and impact, suggesting that wherever he goes next, it will be better when he arrives and better when he leaves.

It was not initially specified what role EC3 will play at Rebellion or whether he is returning full-time to TNA. The promotion has kept the details under wraps, letting the teaser speak for itself. However, his cryptic response and previous comments suggest that his return will be about more than just a nostalgic appearance—it appears designed to be a significant statement heading into one of TNA's major shows of the year.

The timing of EC3's potential return is notable given that other former TNA star Taryn Terrell has also recently returned to the company and will make her in-ring return at Rebellion as well. This suggests TNA is focused on bringing back some of its former talent to boost the event.

🔥 Alberto Del Rio arrested for domestic violence in Mexico

Former WWE Champion Alberto Del Rio remains in legal custody following a serious domestic violence allegation in Mexico. The case stems from an incident on April 6, 2026, in San Luis Potosí, where authorities responded to a reported disturbance that has since escalated into formal legal proceedings.

According to reports, the incident took place in the Lomas del Tecnológico neighborhood in the state capital. Del Rio's wife called for help on the afternoon of Monday, April 6th, and was responded to by agents of the State Civil Guard. Upon arrival, authorities allegedly found Del Rio in the act of physically and verbally assaulting his partner. His partner allegedly showed visible injuries on her face and arms. He was detained at the scene and subsequently turned over to the Special Prosecutor's Office for Crimes Against Women, the Family, and Sexual Offenses.

According to his wife's statement, an argument began while the two were inside a residence in Lomas del Tecnológico, which escalated into multiple acts of physical and verbal abuse. Following his arrest, Del Rio was handed over to the Public Prosecutor's Office, which oversaw the investigation and prepared the case for presentation before a judicial authority.

A control judge has ordered that Alberto Del Rio remain in preventive detention after being formally charged with family violence by the State General Prosecutor's Office. The decision to keep the former world champion behind bars was made on April 9, as the case moves forward through the legal process. His initial hearing before a judge took place on Thursday behind closed doors, primarily to protect the integrity of his children. His wife, Mary Carmen Rodriguez Lucero, was present for the hearing.

The investigation remains ongoing, with the State General Prosecutor's Office formally charging him with the crime of family violence. The alleged victim is currently under medical and legal protection, according to multiple reports. Prosecutors are evaluating potential charges under Mexican law. One option includes a standard domestic violence charge, which carries a lower sentencing range. However, a more serious classification involving gender-based violence could result in significantly higher penalties if pursued. San Luis Potosí's penal code indicates the alleged crime, dependent on severity, can carry either one to seven or eight to twenty years custodial imprisonment.

Del Rio's defense team has requested to double the constitutional term to define his final legal status, indicating that further developments are expected in the coming days. It is important to note that Del Rio has not been convicted and is presumed innocent as the legal process continues.

This situation adds another chapter to Del Rio's complicated history. He was previously arrested and charged with sexual assault and aggravated assault in San Antonio, Texas, in 2020. His trial was ultimately delayed until December 2021 and he was acquitted after his accuser did not show up to testify. He was also suspended by AAA prior to his eventual departure for an altercation with fans in Tijuana.

Former WWE and NWA referee David Dwinell passes away

The pro wrestling community has lost another respected member. David Dwinell, a veteran referee who made a significant impact on the sport throughout his career, has passed away at the age of 76. The International Professional Wrestling Hall of Fame confirmed the news via a Facebook post announcing that Dwinell died on March 19 after battling various health issues.

Dwinell had a remarkable career spanning over 30 years in professional wrestling. He worked as a referee for major promotions including WWE and NWA, and also appeared in numerous independent promotions throughout the Northeast wrestling scene. His work as a referee earned him recognition and respect throughout the wrestling industry.

In December 2025, Dwinell publicly shared that he had been diagnosed with Paraneoplastic Neurological Syndrome, a rare neurological condition. His health continued to deteriorate, leading him to enter hospice care before his passing on March 19.

Beyond his wrestling career, Dwinell maintained an accomplished professional life outside the ring. He worked in tax administration for 23 years, running one of the largest tax offices in New York State. After retiring from wrestling in 2015, he remained deeply involved in his community, serving as Treasurer for the Greene County Democratic Committee. Later in life, he also explored his passion for music.

Dwinell's contributions to wrestling history extended beyond his work as a referee. He authored an autobiography titled "Ringman," which documented his experiences and observations throughout his time in the wrestling business. The book has been recognized as essential reading for anyone interested in professional wrestling history.

The International Professional Wrestling Hall of Fame released a statement honoring Dwinell's life and legacy. "We are sad to report the family of Dave Dwinell has announced that he passed away on March 19th. Dave was a longtime referee for pro wrestling in the Northeast US. Dave's autobiography 'Ringman' is a 'must read' for anyone interested in the history of pro wrestling. I'd like to believe that Dave is reunited in heaven with his family and friends like Freddie Blassie and the Iron Sheik. He will be missed by all of us at the International Professional Wrestling Hall of Fame, where Dave often was a volunteer tour guide. Our prayers go out to Dave's family during this difficult time. May Dave Dwinell rest peacefully."

Even after stepping away from in-ring work, Dwinell remained connected to the wrestling community. He served as a volunteer tour guide at the International Professional Wrestling Hall of Fame, staying engaged with the industry and sharing his extensive knowledge with visitors.

David Dwinell leaves behind a legacy defined by hard work, dedication to professional wrestling, and compelling storytelling. His contributions as a referee, author, and community member will be remembered by the wrestling world and those whose lives he touched.

TNA talent being pulled from indie shows

Austin Aries, a former three-time Impact/TNA World Heavyweight Champion, weighed in on the current controversy surrounding TNA's decision to pull talent from independent wrestling shows. Earlier in the week, TNA President Carlos Silva had withdrawn several wrestlers from upcoming indie dates that would have featured current AEW competitors. The decision sparked significant backlash from multiple wrestling personalities.

Aries revealed on social media that this behavior from TNA is not new, citing his own experience from early in his career between 2005 and 2007. He explained that TNA once attempted to pull him off a Ring of Honor show on the day of the event to fly him to Orlando early for their pay-per-view, and they contacted ROH before even informing Aries himself. At the time, Aries was working on a per-appearance deal, making the situation particularly problematic.

The immediate catalyst for the current situation was the cancellation of a match between AEW World Champion MJF and former TNA World Champion Nic Nemeth, which was scheduled for May 1st at Create A Pro Wrestling. This marks the second time this highly anticipated match has been canceled. MJF expressed his frustration with Silva's decision, criticizing the TNA President for not caring about fans or his roster's ability to earn money on independent dates. MJF stated that Silva is "actively a dumb mother****r" for paying talent poorly while simultaneously forcing them off shows where they could earn additional income. MJF vowed to attend the Long Island show regardless, questioning whether Nemeth would have the courage to show up.

Silva justified the controversial decision by citing "partner conflicts," particularly regarding TNA's multi-year partnership with WWE that began in January 2025. This partnership appears to be the primary reason for restricting talent from working with AEW competitors on independent promotions.

In his comprehensive statement, Aries provided additional context about his own suspension following his refusal to comply with TNA's demands in 2005. He explained that he refused TNA's request to abandon his Ring of Honor commitment, arguing it was bad business and that TNA lacked the contractual right to control his bookings, especially when they weren't paying him enough to warrant such control. Despite the logistical challenges, including a snowstorm and various delays, Aries worked the ROH show and still managed to arrive in Florida hours before TNA's pay-per-view. However, he was suspended the following day, not for being late as TNA claimed, but for refusing to obey the company's demands.

Aries noted that Dave Meltzer reported TNA's version of events without seeking his side of the story, which he identifies as the origin of his "difficult to work with" label. He connected this early experience to larger industry problems, stating that Jim Cornette would have called him a "malcontent." Rather than shy away from the label, Aries embraced it as a badge of honor, explaining that his dissatisfaction stemmed not from creative decisions or booking outcomes, but from the fundamental lack of integrity and honesty with which talent is treated in professional wrestling.

Two decades later, Aries observed that the same patterns persist. Companies continue engaging in political games, underpaid talent serve as pawns in larger business disputes, and media outlets shape narratives without complete information. He emphasized that both talent and fans suffer the consequences of these practices. While expressing gratitude for his 15 years under contract and on television, Aries stated that he now values his independence as a truly independent contractor, preferring to control his own bookings and avoid industry politics. He concluded by emphasizing that booking him and treating him with professionalism, honesty, and fairness will result in the same from him in return.

TNA is preparing for its annual springtime pay-per-view event, Rebellion, scheduled for tomorrow night in Cleveland, Ohio. Meanwhile, AEW will host its next pay-per-view event, Dynasty, on Sunday from Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada.
