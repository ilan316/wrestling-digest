🔥 TNA Impact on AMC TV

TNA Impact Aims for Growth on AMC TV Platform

TNA Impact debuted on AMC TV earlier this year, establishing a new weekly cable television destination for the Thursday night prime time program. During an interview, TNA Hall of Fame legend Mickie James addressed the company's potential for expansion now that it has secured a stronger cable home. James reflected on TNA's history across multiple network homes including Spike TV before expressing enthusiasm about the current opportunity.

"I've seen TNA through so many waves of different networks and all the things, Spike TV and that," James said. "But to be now on AMC, I think that's the one thing that I've said, is the amount of eyeballs that can actually watch. TNA has a wonderful product. They have a strong division. They have some amazing superstars. They have some amazing knockouts. The division is strong, and creatively, it's just such a great show."

James emphasized that the move to AMC represents a significant platform upgrade for reaching new audiences. "But now on AMC, you know, there's going to be so many more eyeballs on it, and I think just you've seen like with the collaboration with the network and having some stars from other shows, from other genres kind of cross over and come to a wrestling show and kind of be there and hang out, and yeah." The weekly Thursday night 9/8c timeslot on AMC provides consistent programming for the wrestling audience.

Major Returns and Title Changes at TNA Rebellion 2026

TNA Rebellion delivered several notable developments on Saturday night, setting the stage for upcoming episodes of Impact. EC3, also known as Ethan Carter III, made his long-awaited return to the company after last wrestling for TNA in 2020 at Bound For Glory. The star had pursued other opportunities with NWA before coming back to TNA. His return came with considerable fanfare, as Eric Young had spent time earlier in the show disrespecting Cleveland, Ohio.

EC3 quickly took control of the situation when he came out, immediately brawling with Young and hitting him with a big slam. After confirming his return, EC3 grabbed the microphone and declared that his next plan of business was to take on Young in a match. He challenged Young to face him on this Thursday's episode of Impact and shouted out his hometown of Cleveland before leaving. The two are now scheduled to face off on the April 16 episode of Impact. During his previous tenure with TNA, EC3 established himself as a major star, capturing the TNA World Heavyweight Championship twice and also winning the Impact Grand Championship.

In another significant return, KC Navarro made a shocking appearance during a match between A.J. Francis and Nic Nemeth. Navarro had last been seen in TNA in 2025 when he suffered a torn ACL while performing. After the injury, Francis kicked him out of First Cla$$ and replaced him with Rich Swann. During the Rebellion match, Navarro's music hit late in the action, and he came out to distract Francis at a critical moment. This opening allowed NFL legend Bernie Kosar, who was accompanying Nemeth, to blast Francis with a steel chair. Nemeth capitalized on the chaos by delivering the Danger Zone to secure the victory over Francis. The angle set up a singles match between KC Navarro and AJ Francis for the upcoming Impact episode.

Championship Glory Changes Hands

The TNA International Championship changed hands on Saturday night in a controversial finish. Mustafa Ali defeated Trey Miguel to capture the gold at Rebellion 2026. The match was a back-and-forth battle throughout, with both competitors trading near falls and keeping the outcome in doubt. Miguel showed resilience as champion while Ali continued to press the pace in pursuit of the title.

However, things broke down late in the match. Tasha Steelz created an opening by distracting the referee, which allowed The Great Hands, consisting of Jason Hotch and John Skyler, to get involved. The duo struck Miguel with cyborg helmets behind the official's back, shifting momentum decisively in Ali's favor. Ali wasted no time capitalizing, connecting with a 450 splash to seal the pinfall victory. With the win, Ali now adds to his resume of championships with the company. Prior to this, he also held the TNA X Division Championship and was named the TNA X Division Star of the Year in 2024.

The TNA World Tag Team Championship also changed hands at Rebellion as The System duo of Brian Myers and Bear Bronson defeated The Hardys to capture the gold. The match was a back-and-forth battle throughout, but in the end, The System got their hands raised. With the victory, The Hardys' 265-day title reign came to an end. For Bear Bronson, this marked his first-ever championship win in TNA. For Myers, this represented his fourth tag team title victory with the company.

🔥 AAA El Grande Americano Mask vs Mask Match

A bitter rivalry between two wrestlers operating under the El Grande Americano name has reached a critical juncture with a high-stakes mask vs. mask stipulation now officially on the table. The feud came to a head at AAA's event on April 11, 2026, when the second Americano, identified in some reports as the Ludwig Kaiser version of the character challenging Chad Gable's El Grande Americano, finally issued the challenge that many had seen coming.

The chaotic night was filled with multiple confrontations between the two rivals. Earlier in the evening, the Original Americano defeated Octagon Jr., capturing his mask and forcing him to submit. However, the victory was followed by an unprovoked assault when Americano confronted comedian Ojitos de Huevo and locked him in an ankle lock. When interviewer Andrea Bazarte attempted to intervene by slapping Americano to break the hold, she became the next target as Americano began stalking her through the arena. The second Americano rushed out to make the save, leading to the first of several pull-apart brawls between the two rivals throughout the evening.

The tensions continued to escalate as the night progressed. During the Intercontinental Championship match, the two Americanos were spotted fighting in the background. After Penta retained his title, the Americanos continued their brawl into the stands before eventually making their way back to the ring. It was at this point, after needing to be separated once again by arena personnel, that the second Americano grabbed a microphone and declared that their feud needed to end, officially issuing the mask vs. mask challenge.

While no official date was announced during the broadcast itself, reporting indicates that the match is planned for AAA's next major event, Noche de Los Grandes, scheduled for May 30 in Monterrey at Arena Monterrey. According to Dave Meltzer's reporting in The Wrestling Observer Newsletter from March 30, 2026, the show is set to air as a full live broadcast rather than being broken up into three one-hour blocks as AAA typically does with Fox broadcasts.

The timing presents an interesting contrast with WWE's schedule. The WWE will be on a European tour at that time, with a live event in Liverpool on May 28, followed by SmackDown in Barcelona, Spain on May 29. WWE's Clash event is scheduled for May 31 in Italy, with Raw from the same venue on June 1. Additional live events throughout Portugal, Spain, and France are planned for the following week.

With emotions boiling over and no resolution achieved through multiple altercations throughout the evening, the stage is now set for a definitive mask vs. mask showdown. The stipulation ensures that whoever loses will be unmasked, bringing an end to at least one version of the El Grande Americano character. The match has all the ingredients of a marquee bout for AAA's May 30 event, with months of buildup and legitimate animosity between the two competitors.

🔥 La Catalina CMLL AAA WWE WrestleMania

La Catalina has officially arrived in AAA with a bang. The former CMLL luchadora requested and was granted her release from CMLL last week, and she wasted no time making her presence felt in her new promotion. During the latest AAA on FOX episode on April 11, La Catalina crashed Lady Flammer's in-ring celebration marking her historic achievement as the longest-reigning Reina de Reinas Champion of all time.

The segment began with Flammer celebrating her unprecedented title reign alongside Los Toxicas. During her speech, Flammer mentioned that she had beaten everyone, including foreigners like WWE's Bayley who had come to AAA to face her. She declared that she had nothing left to prove. That declaration would be short-lived.

The lights cut out and a video played featuring a pink car driving across the screen with "La Catalina" displayed on the license plate. When La Catalina made her way to the ring, she initially played it cool, congratulating Flammer on her accomplishment. However, she quickly turned the tables, reminding Flammer that she had never faced her and certainly had never beaten her. Catalina declared that she was coming for the Reina de Reinas Championship.

The segment quickly turned chaotic as La Catalina engaged in a brawl with Flammer and the rest of Los Toxicas, including Lady Maravilla and La Hiedra. The melee ended with Catalina driving Flammer's head face-first into her celebratory cake. Catalina then held the Reina de Reinas Championship belt aloft while Flammer scrambled up the ramp to safety, establishing a clear collision course between the two competitors.

The momentum for La Catalina shows no signs of slowing. Following her dominant AAA debut, WWE announced that she will be part of WWE World festivities during WrestleMania 42 week, appearing alongside her new AAA allies. WrestleMania 42 is scheduled to take place on April 18 and April 19 from Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas, Nevada.

La Catalina's arrival in AAA marks a significant moment in her career trajectory. She spent three years with CMLL before her recent departure and has previous experience with WWE. She previously signed with WWE and competed on RAW under the ring name Katrina Cortez, teaming with Sin Cara against Zelina Vega and Andrade before being released in 2020. Prior to her WWE stint, she also spent time in WWE NXT, where she worked under her real name Catalina Garcia. Her crossover appeal and prior WWE experience make her an intriguing addition to AAA's roster as the company continues to strengthen its relationship with WWE.

Bill Goldberg Interview

Bill Goldberg didn't just build a wrestling career — he says he set out to change how people viewed his identity from the very beginning. During an appearance on the Duke Loves Rasslin podcast, the WWE Hall of Famer spoke about why he chose to wrestle under his real last name instead of creating a separate persona. Goldberg explained that he wanted to challenge stereotypes and reshape how people reacted when they heard his name.

He described his journey as more than just a rise to fame. According to Goldberg, it became a personal mission to represent his background in a positive way while showing fans something different from what they might have expected. Goldberg became one of the most recognizable names in wrestling history during his run in WCW, where his undefeated streak turned him into a household name. But beyond the wins and losses, he made it clear that representing himself authentically was always part of the bigger picture.

"Success is obviously a driving force, but my mission in life is to be that representative for my faith that is different than the uh the norm stereotype that had been portrayed for years before," Goldberg explained. "And there are different type of people in different races, different, you know, religions and everybody's represented by, you know, different types of people. But the fact is is that uh there are some people that have the last name Goldberg that uh you know are are are a little bit different than the stereotype and I wanted to make sure that you know I added a little strength to uh my past and my future and my son's future and uh if I can change the perception in a positive way uh one little bit then my job is done but my job isn't."

His decision to use his real last name helped turn "Goldberg" into something associated with strength, toughness, and determination rather than stereotypes he said existed before his rise to fame. Even years after stepping away from full-time competition, Goldberg still views his platform as an opportunity to make an impact beyond wrestling.

However, Goldberg made it equally clear during the podcast appearance that what matters most to him now has nothing to do with championships or fame. The WWE Hall of Famer opened up about balancing life as a public figure while raising his son and maintaining his family life. Goldberg reflected on how fans often see him as a larger-than-life personality, but behind the scenes, he's focused on being the kind of husband and father his family can rely on.

Goldberg explained that despite the expectations placed on him as a famous athlete, his responsibilities at home always come first. He made it clear that being seen as a hero inside the ring doesn't compare to the role he plays in his own household. "The being being a superhero is one thing, but being a good father and being a good husband, I think are the most important things in the world. Uh I'm lucky enough to try to be all of them at the same time. So, uh it's a it's a very lofty expectation, but it's one that I take with open arms."

Goldberg has long spoken about how his return to WWE years ago was largely motivated by wanting his son to see him perform live. That moment helped shape how fans viewed him in a new light — not just as a powerhouse in the ring, but as a father proud to share his legacy with his family. Now at this stage of his life, Goldberg seems more focused than ever on setting the right example at home, even as his wrestling career continues to be remembered by fans around the world.

Goldberg's comments show that even one of wrestling's most dominant figures views success differently today than he did during his peak years. For him, the legacy that matters most isn't tied to titles or streaks — it's tied to family and the impact he has on those closest to him.

IWC Commentary on Wrestling Age Dropoff

Welcome back to the column that puts the incessant whining and complaining in IWC. I'm a clueless malcontent named SkitZ who thrives on the calamities that kayfabe constantly throws at us. This just happens to be my fiftieth post, so I figured why not base a celebratory theme around it? Not exactly the most relevant piece as we approach WrestleMania week, but it seemed like a better alternative than spending 2,000 words bitching about Iyo Sky getting snubbed. As someone who's watched the product for nearly 30 years, I've seen hundreds of wrestlers come and go, and an alarming number of them that don't know when to walk away. They cling on for so long that it often leads to public scrutiny and sympathetic pleas from disenchanted fans such as myself. The more I thought about it, the more I noticed a common thread connecting all these guys together that had me obsessing over a specific number like Jim Carrey in 23. Is 50 the breaking point in pro wrestling when everything starts to fall apart? Do aging stars use it as a finish line of sorts? Could this pattern be purely coincidental? Let's dive into the data I've collected on names like Austin and see if my theory is any more justified than the screen time wasted on The Vision's biggest charity case.

HBK retired at 45 following a second run that was arguably better than his first. If any wrestler should be content with the body of work they left behind, it's The Showstopper. We could've easily forgiven him for coming back to face AJ Styles at WrestleMania, but watching a 53-year old bald Shawn Michaels stink up the joint with his buddies was like a bad buzz when you're already out of your comfort zone. Some fantasy scenarios should just remain a pipe dream, you know?

Triple H ultimately competed in his last match a few months shy of turning 50. Even if it weren't for the meddling pacemaker, Hunter knew he could no longer keep up with the younger guys. While still solid between the ropes for a guy who could rarely escape office duty, the falloff was evident in his final few WrestleMania matches.

Should Undertaker have retired at 49 when Brock ended the streak? WM31 felt like a shaky return to form for The Phenom, 32 was a serviceable stunt brawl with Shane, 33 a borderline disaster, 34 a glorified squash, 35 an unusual night off, and 36 a pre-recorded performance. The ideal scenario for Taker's farewell will always be subject to debate, but the sad truth is his mystique and momentum never fully recovered following WrestleMania 30.

Kane's storied career more or less ended when he was 51. The botchmania classic between DX and the Brothers of Destruction seemed to have that effect on everyone involved. The Big Red Machine had a historic run, but it's rather ironic how the quietest, most law-abiding dude in the locker room made a career shift into politics after wrestling.

Due to a gnarly painkiller addiction and sweet deal with TNA, Kurt Angle didn't return until 48 when the Hall of Fame came knocking. The wear and tear was obvious, which is why it's hard making too much of a fuss over Baron Corbin retiring The Olympic Gold Medalist at that stage. Poor Kurt was moving around like fucking Mae Young over the last year and a half of his active career.

Sting had his own reasons for waiting too long to sign with WWE. The writing was on the wall when he finally showed up at 55; we simply refused to read the warning signs. Sounds crazy given his age, but retirement felt prematurely rushed upon The Icon, who then joined AEW and had one helluva final run from 61-64. Darby Allin took most of the bumps of course, but Stinger carried his weight just the same. Dude turned into Terry Funk all of a sudden. I'm glad he got the sendoff that Vince refused to assume liability of following the Seth match.

Chris Jericho signed with AEW at 48, and he's still going strong with the company 7 years later. I spent an entire column singing the guy's praises roughly 13 months ago.

Road Dogg and Billy Gunn on Celebrities in Wrestling

Ex-WWE Writer Road Dogg And AEW's Billy Gunn Comment On Celebrities In Wrestling

The theme of celebrities in pro wrestling is not an uncommon one as hundreds have either appeared on shows or stepped into the ring themselves throughout the years. Still, the question as to whether their usage is beneficial to the wrestling business is up for debate.

During a virtual signing with Signed By Superstars, ex-WWE writer Brian "Road Dogg" James and AEW star Billy Gunn, together once known as the New Age Outlaws, weighed in on the topic. For Gunn, simple outings from celebrities are fine. When it extends beyond that, though, it dips into questionable territory.

"I'm okay with like an appearance kind of thing," Gunn said. Paul Wight Arnold Schwarzenegger Prank

Paul Wight recounts a humorous story about his long-running interactions with Arnold Schwarzenegger. During a recent appearance on INSIGHT with Chris Van Vliet, the former Big Show recalled that the two first bonded while filming Jingle All the Way, where they spoke about cigars and André the Giant. At the time, he believed they had formed a genuine connection.

However, that perception changed during a later encounter when Schwarzenegger reintroduced himself as if they had never met. A couple of years went by before they crossed paths again. When Wight approached him, Schwarzenegger responded with, "Arnold Schwarzenegger, nice to meet you." Wight was shocked, initially assuming Schwarzenegger simply didn't remember him due to his busy lifestyle and extensive commitments.

Wight initially brushed it off, but as the same interaction repeated itself multiple times, it began to frustrate him. As he became more famous and more recognizable, he started to understand that people don't typically forget him. Yet each time he encountered Schwarzenegger, he received the same introduction: "Arnold Schwarzenegger, nice to meet you." Wight began to feel increasingly bothered by the repeated snub, especially since he knew they had shared meaningful conversations before.

Wight recalled bumping into Schwarzenegger again during a backstage meeting at WWE RAW, where Joe Manganiello was also present. Schwarzenegger once again introduced himself the same way, making the situation even more awkward. This was now the fourth time the same interaction had occurred. Wight expressed his frustration at the fact that if Schwarzenegger didn't remember his name, he should at least remember meeting him. Manganiello even chimed in, asking, "Weren't you in Jingle All the Way?" The situation left Wight visibly annoyed as he excused himself to get to work.

Determined to finally be remembered, Wight said he mentally prepared himself for a confrontation the next time they crossed paths. That opportunity came at the Arnold Classic, where he was scheduled to appear. WWE called him and informed him that Schwarzenegger was receiving an award and that they wanted Wight to present it or participate in the ceremony. Wight attended with Mark Henry, who knew the entire history of these encounters. Mark Henry was aware of Wight's frustration and tried to calm him down before the event.

Wight was genuinely worked up about the situation. He told Mark Henry that if Schwarzenegger pulled the "Nice to meet you" line again, he was going to confront him about it. Wight emphasized that respect was important to him and that Schwarzenegger was going to remember him this time, even if the actor didn't particularly like him. Wight was determined to make a lasting impression. Mark Henry tried to de-escalate the tension, telling him it wasn't that big a deal, but Wight insisted that Schwarzenegger would indeed remember him.

However, the moment unfolded very differently than Wight had anticipated. While Wight was waiting off to the side to go out and handle the award presentation, Schwarzenegger began telling stories to the audience. He said, "My good friend Paul, we were in Jingle All the Way together, and so big and such a tremendous athlete." Schwarzenegger went into an elaborate speech where he praised Wight publicly, acknowledging their shared history and putting him over. When Wight went out to join him, Schwarzenegger looked at him and winked.

In that moment, Wight realized that Schwarzenegger had ribbed him for 12 years with an elaborate, long-running prank. He recognized the brilliance of the setup, as Schwarzenegger had exploited exactly what Wight knew was his greatest strength. Wight explained that he knew what he brought to the table wasn't necessarily being the most handsome, the most athletic, or the strongest, but rather that people would remember meeting him. That memorable presence was his ticket to success in the business. Schwarzenegger had deliberately taken that away from him for over a decade, targeting the one thing Wight was most confident about. Wight acknowledged that this demonstrated just how clever Schwarzenegger truly was.

Alex Pereira To Receive Forrest Griffin Community Award

Alex Pereira, former UFC middleweight and light heavyweight champion, has been named the 2026 recipient of the Forrest Griffin Community Award. The announcement was made at UFC 327, honoring his exceptional volunteer and charity work and the meaningful impact his efforts have on the community.

The Forrest Griffin Community Award is named in honor of UFC Hall of Famer Forrest Griffin and is presented annually as part of the UFC Honors President's Choice category. The award recipient is personally selected by UFC President and CEO Dana White himself. As the 2026 recipient, Pereira will receive a $25,000 donation to the charity of his choice, courtesy of the UFC Foundation.

Additionally, Pereira will partner with celebrity artist Ant Kai to design and auction a one-of-one pair of UFC-branded gloves as part of UFC's Gloves for Good Program, with funds raised benefiting his foundation, The Instituto Poatan. Pereira will be honored for this award during the 2026 UFC Hall of Fame Induction Ceremony, which takes place on Thursday, July 9 at T-Mobile Arena in Las Vegas as part of the 14th Annual UFC International Fight Week.

Dana White expressed his enthusiasm for Pereira's recognition, stating: "Alex is one of the biggest stars in the world and it's great to see him use his platform to give back to the community by helping youth in Brazil. By offering free after-school tutoring, computer literacy programs, and jiu-jitsu training to more than 500 local youth, he is directly helping the students create better lives for themselves. It's an honor to present him with this award."

Pereira founded The Instituto Poatan in 2024 after purchasing and renovating a building in Sao Paulo, Brazil. The institute's mission is to promote social and sports initiatives, using sports as a tool for transformation. The nonprofit's vision is to become a benchmark for encouraging, expanding, and spreading sports practices that drive social, educational, and community development.

The institute operates with core values centering on transparency, excellence, integrity and commitment. It offers free afterschool tutoring, English courses, computer literacy, nutrition, and jiu-jitsu training to underprivileged youth. Since its inception, more than 500 local youth have registered for the institute's programs, and the organization plans on continuing its expansion in 2026.

Beyond his work in Brazil, Pereira continues to give back to local communities around the world. He regularly visits children's hospitals, teaches jiu-jitsu, and speaks with underprivileged youth in conjunction with other athletes and in partnership with UFC's youth mentoring programs.

Pereira is a veteran of 16 fights in MMA with a professional record of 13-3 (10-2 UFC) since his professional MMA debut in 2015. Before transitioning to MMA, he competed in 40 professional kickboxing fights, registering a 33-7 record and capturing the GLORY middleweight and light heavyweight titles, establishing himself as a formidable competitor across multiple combat sports disciplines.
