🔥 Lady Frost AEW Departure and MLW Signing

Lady Frost's time on the open market proved to be remarkably brief. After confirming her AEW departure on April 1, 2026, the veteran wrestler inked a deal with Major League Wrestling just hours into her free agency period. In interviews with Sean Ross Sapp of Fightful, Frost explained the rapid succession of events that led to her new signing.

"I was only a free agent for a matter of hours, we can say, which feels pretty good," Frost told Fightful. She credited MLW's immediate outreach and their demonstrated knowledge of her career trajectory as pivotal factors in her decision. The promotion had reportedly been following her work for an extended period, dating back to when she attended an MLW taping approximately six years ago early in her career, before she had even achieved extra status on the show.

MLW officials wasted no time in reaching out once Frost's free agency was confirmed. According to reports, the promotion expressed immediate and strong interest in bringing her on board. The speed at which the deal came together caught attention within the industry, signaling that MLW had prioritized securing Frost without waiting to see what other offers might materialize.

Frost explained that her own promotional efforts helped generate broader interest in her availability. She noted that a promo package and vignette she created generated significant attention across the industry. "I posted, I think, which created a good amount of interest, a promo package and vignette, and they said, 'We love your work,'" Frost explained. "I said, 'That's the direction that I want to go. This is kind of where I see my career going.'"

What resonated most strongly with Frost was MLW's stated commitment to supporting her creative endeavors and artistic vision as a professional wrestler. "They wanted to be on board with letting me be creative and who I want to be as an artist and pro wrestler. So I loved the call and I thought it was a really great fit for right now. So that's where I landed," she said.

The decision to move quickly to MLW was somewhat informed by Frost's recent experience in AEW. Her tenure with the promotion had become progressively limited over recent months, with her final televised match occurring on the January 10 episode of AEW Collision against Mina Shirakawa. Prior to that final appearance, she had been absent from AEW and Ring of Honor programming since July of the previous year, indicating that her role within the company had substantially diminished.

During her time with AEW, Frost had competed against several notable performers including Toni Storm, Thekla, Kris Statlander, and others, but she never recorded a victory during her television run with the promotion. She revealed that some of her creative ideas never made it to air, including an "Ice Cold Killer" vignette she had filmed and pitched to AEW producers and agents on her own dime. The vignette was produced by Dani Venen and created exactly according to Frost's vision, but it ultimately did not receive approval for broadcast.

Frost explained the frustration of having creative direction that was never given an opportunity to be showcased on AEW programming. "That's actually something that I filmed last year. I was trying to present options to my former employer saying, 'Here's how I see myself. This is what I kind of want to get into.' I did that on my own dime, my own time. [Dani Venen] filmed it. She is absolutely incredible. I had so many ideas in my brain. I said, 'I want it to look like this, feel like this, sound like this,' and somehow she created exactly what I wanted to present," Frost said. "I went on and I passed that to producers, agents, everyone in AEW that I could think of. It didn't get used, it wasn't produced."

The lack of creative outlet for her vision made the timing of her free agency and MLW's immediate interest particularly meaningful. When she eventually released the vignette on her own following her AEW departure announcement, the response was substantial. "So when I knew I was not getting renewed, I dropped the promo on my own. I think everyone was like, 'Holy shit, what is this?' Because I wasn't really given the opportunity to express any of that. So it was great to hear the feedback and the response because that came right from my brain. I didn't have help. It wasn't someone pushing the machine. It was just, 'Here's what I want to offer,' and I think a lot of people really took to that," she explained.

Despite her departure from AEW, Frost maintained positive feelings toward the promotion and its leadership. In her initial free agency announcement on April 1, she wrote: "As of today, I am a free agent. I want to thank @TonyKhan for being an incredibly kind and passionate boss. I had some of the most incredible moments with @AEW and I am so grateful for the years that I spent there. For now, I'm on the hunt. Onto the next…"

Her subsequent tweet announcing the MLW signing read: "Not a free agent anymore. That didn't take long."

The specifics of Frost's MLW contract remain undisclosed, including its length and terms. However, sources indicated that MLW officials were very excited about finalizing the deal, and Frost is already scheduled to appear at several upcoming MLW tapings as she begins this new chapter of her career.

Frost's career has been notably diverse prior to her MLW signing. Beyond her time in AEW and Ring of Honor, she has competed in WWE, TNA Wrestling, CMLL, and the NWA. She spent time competing in Mexico for CMLL during her AEW tenure, even achieving success in tournaments like the Copa Bicentenario in 2022 and challenging for the promotion's World Women's Tag Team Championship. Her career spans over nine years and includes more than 250 matches across multiple promotions.

The quick resolution of Frost's free agency status stands in contrast to initial speculation about her next destination. Some observers had wondered whether she might return to the independent scene, explore opportunities in TNA Wrestling, or pursue other international opportunities. Instead, MLW's swift and decisive action in securing her services settled the matter almost immediately.

The speed of the MLW signing appears to reflect genuine enthusiasm from the promotion's leadership about what Frost can bring to their roster. Her experience across multiple major promotions, combined with her demonstrated creativity and willingness to develop her own character presentation, makes her a potentially significant addition to MLW's women's division. With tapings already scheduled and clear support for her creative direction, Frost appears positioned to have a more prominent role during this new phase of her career compared to her recent tenure in AEW.

🔥 AEW Wrestlers Comments and Updates

WRESTLERS SHARE PERSPECTIVES ON ACTIVISM, LEGACY, AND PERSONAL IDENTITY IN LATEST AEW UPDATES

Willow Nightingale recently sat down for an interview where she addressed the persistent issue of wrestlers being personally attacked on social media. The AEW star expressed frustration with how fans target performers for who they are as people rather than critiquing their in-ring work or character choices. She explained that when people attack her, it almost always stems from misogyny, racism, or fat phobia, which are personal assaults on her as a human being who wears "sparkly boots" to perform, rather than legitimate criticism of her wrestling character Willow.

Nightingale emphasized the distinction between character-based feedback and personal attacks. While she accepts criticism that her character might be considered annoying or cringey—traits she intentionally incorporates into her performance—she believes fans should refrain from attacking wrestlers as individuals. She advocated for a wrestling industry where people could be themselves without fear of harassment. According to Nightingale, professional wrestling is meant to be an escape where fans get caught up in stories and adventures, and the prevalence of personal attacks undermines that fundamental purpose of the entertainment medium.

MJF recently gave insight into his respect for Bryan Danielson while discussing their iconic Iron Man match from Revolution 2023. Speaking on Julian Edelman's Games With Names podcast, MJF called Danielson the greatest bell-to-bell professional wrestler of all time, placing him on wrestling's Mount Rushmore for in-ring technical ability. MJF elaborated that Danielson is a freak athlete who is tough as nails and truly put him through an intense experience during their hour-long match.

When asked for an NFL comparison, MJF did not hesitate to invoke Tom Brady, calling Danielson the GOAT level of wrestler comparable to the legendary quarterback. MJF suggested that Danielson, currently in a semi-retired status, may eventually return to competition because he is "a sicko" who cannot get enough of wrestling, much like Brady with football. MJF also revealed that he was genuinely angered by Danielson's assumption that he would not be able to hang with him for a full hour, noting that Danielson wanted the match thinking it would put him at a disadvantage. MJF's confidence proved justified as he went the distance with one of wrestling's technical masters.

Julia Hart recently spoke about the anti-ICE chants that have become prominent at AEW shows, particularly during the February 4 edition of Dynamite in Las Vegas when Brody King faced MJF. Hart praised King's activism efforts, which included wearing a shirt promoting awareness and donating substantial amounts of money to families affected by ICE operations. Hart, who grew up in Bloomington, Minnesota, stated that the issue hit close to home for her personally.

Hart explained that some of the incidents she learned about occurred right near places from her childhood, including a donut shop she frequently visited growing up. She emphasized that all the wrestling community can do is help those in need through donations and community support. Hart's connection to the issue stems from Minnesota's proximity to areas where ICE operations have had significant impact, making King's activism particularly meaningful to her and demonstrating how wrestlers can use their platforms for social good.

Chris Jericho recently discussed his philosophy of constant character reinvention, crediting legendary musician David Bowie as a major inspiration for his approach to maintaining relevance throughout his career. Jericho explained that Bowie never presented himself the same way twice, constantly evolving his imagery and sound while maintaining his core identity. At the heart of every Bowie creation was unmistakably Bowie, yet each era was distinctly recognizable and unique.

Jericho compared his career trajectory to this Bowie model, noting that fans could theoretically attend a Halloween party and spot ten different versions of Chris Jericho from various eras, each immediately recognizable. The AEW World Champion stated that his primary goal is to never become a nostalgia act, instead continuously changing and doing things that initially make people angry before they come to appreciate the new direction. This philosophy has served Jericho well, as he has accumulated 36 different championships across ECW, WCW, New Japan, WWE, AEW, and ROH throughout his storied career.

Eddie Kingston reflected on his extensive history in Ring of Honor, describing the promotion during its independent era as "AEW without the money." Kingston has worked through multiple eras of ROH, beginning twenty years ago when he made his first appearance as part of an invading CZW roster. He subsequently worked during the Gabe Sapolsky era, the HD Net era, the Destination America era, and the Sinclair era before eventually becoming ROH World Champion under Tony Khan's ownership of the promotion after Khan acquired it in 2022.

Kingston emphasized that Ring of Honor holds deep significance for him due to his lengthy tenure spanning different eras of the company. He credited the promotion for being the premier destination for independent wrestlers at one point in time, presenting a distinctive wrestling style that no other promotion was offering. Kingston expressed pride in representing that legacy and acknowledged Khan's role in building ROH into a place where independent wrestlers wanted to work, maintaining the spirit that made it special throughout its history.

The Don Callis Family expanded its membership when Rocky Romero announced that Volador Jr. had joined the group. While not an actual family relation, Romero and Volador have a history spanning back to 2003 when Romero won the CMLL World Super Lightweight Championship from Volador. Twenty years later, their rivalry was reignited when Romero defeated Volador for the World Historic Welterweight Championship, after which Volador gained revenge in a hair versus hair match and eventually turned heel to align with Romero in storyline.

Volador Jr. represents a significant addition to the Callis Family given his status as one of CMLL's top superstars, second only to Mistico in star power. The family's trio of Hechicero, El Clon, and Volador Jr. will challenge Mistico and JetSpeed for the AEW World Trios Championship at Arena Mexico on April 3, 2026. The Callis Family roster has grown substantially and now includes Kazuchika Okada, Kyle Fletcher, Konosuke Takeshita, Andrade El Idolo, Hechicero, Josh Alexander, Wardlow, Mark Davis, Jake Doyle, Lance Archer, Brian Cage, El Clon, Trent Beretta, Rocky Romero, and Volador Jr. Don Callis has indicated his hunger for expansion continues, having recently expressed interest in bringing in Tommaso Ciampa and Lio Rush.

🔥 Willie Mack AEW Booking Issues and Status

Willie Mack's frustration with his current situation in AEW boiled over on social media late Tuesday evening when he posted cryptic messages expressing his discontent with his booking status under Tony Khan. The veteran wrestler, best known for his time in Lucha Underground as The Mack, took to X to share his troubles, writing "When ya boy is about to become a homeless superhero because the booker doesn't think he's worth the money" with the sarcastic hashtag "WhereTheBestWrestle."

In a follow-up post, Mack elaborated on his experience over the past two years, stating: "It's like for 2 years straight I'd call every week and ask if you got anything for me? You signed right? You still getting paid right? I want to Wrestle." The comments quickly circulated through wrestling media, with several outlets initially interpreting the posts as an announcement that Mack had departed AEW. That interpretation, however, proved incorrect. When responding to reports about his status, Mack shut down speculation with a blunt reply: "Wrong. Still there."

Mack also pushed back against a fan who claimed he was working under only a per-appearance deal rather than being signed to a full contract. The wrestler indicated that assumption was also incorrect, though backstage reports paint a more nuanced picture of his actual contract situation. According to sources, Mack has indeed recently been shifted back to a tier-zero deal, meaning he is being paid on a per-appearance basis. This represents a significant change from his previous arrangement. When Mack first arrived in AEW, he was reportedly on a tier-zero agreement, essentially a per-appearance arrangement. He was later moved to a full-time contract over the past couple of years, which provided a salaried structure. That situation has now changed once again, with no confirmed reason given for the shift in contract status.

The lack of bookings has been particularly stark in 2026. As of the time of these reports, Mack has not competed in a match for either AEW or ROH in 2026. His last appearance in AEW was in March 2025, and his most recent ROH match took place in December 2025, where he and Aaron Solo lost to Wheeler Yuta and Daniel Garcia. His total output for 2025 was also limited, with only six matches between the two promotions that year. Throughout his time with AEW and Ring of Honor, Mack has wrestled only sporadically, with most of his appearances coming under the ROH banner rather than on AEW television.

Despite the frustrating situation with AEW and ROH, Mack has been able to maintain his career by securing independent bookings. The 39-year-old wrestler has kept busy primarily competing for Juggalo Championship Wrestling, where he is currently one half of the JCW Tag Team Champions alongside 2 Tuff Tony. However, there have been complications surrounding his independent appearances in the past. Fans may recall issues that arose when Mack was pulled from Game Changer Wrestling bookings in late 2024 during a period when AEW and GCW experienced a brief falling out. His current tier-zero deal allows him to remain active on the independent circuit while waiting for potential opportunities within AEW and ROH.

Mack's career spans several major promotions and has included championship reigns in multiple organizations. The wrestler has worked for AAA, MLW, NWA, and TNA throughout his career. He is perhaps best known for his run in Lucha Underground across all four seasons of that promotion. His work on the independent scene demonstrates that he remains an active and capable performer, making his lack of bookings in AEW and ROH all the more frustrating for someone still under contract with both companies.

The situation highlights a broader challenge facing All Elite Wrestling and its sister promotion Ring of Honor, as both companies' rosters have ballooned to the point that there is not enough television time to accommodate all of the talent. With limited programming slots available, many wrestlers find themselves in similar situations to Mack, signed to deals but rarely or never appearing on shows. For Mack specifically, the lack of opportunities over an extended period has created genuine financial hardship, motivating his public complaints about being undervalued by Tony Khan's booking decisions.

🔥 Mercedes Moné AEW Championship Losses

Mercedes Moné's Tumultuous Return: From Belt Collector to Humbled Competitor

Mercedes Moné has not been seen on AEW television since dropping the TBS Championship on the January 7 edition of AEW Dynamite. The loss of that title came just days after she suffered defeat at the hands of Willow Nightingale on December 31 at New Year's Smash, where Nightingale ended Moné's historic 584-day reign as TBS Champion, setting the record for the longest run of any TBS Champion in the title's history. Following these losses, Moné was embarrassed on the January 7 Dynamite episode when Willow's friends Kris Statlander and Harley Cameron tripped her up and she landed in a cake before they taunted "The CEO" for her humiliation.

Since then, Moné has been traveling all over the world on what she has called the "Moné Madness Tour," losing her impressive collection of championship gold in the process. What was once a dominant belt collector's run has transformed into a significant losing streak throughout March 2025 and into early 2026, with Moné making stops in Mexico, Poland, Italy, England, and Canada. During these international appearances, she has systematically lost championships that she had won throughout 2025.

The losses have been steady and decisive. In Poland on March 28 at a show named after her called "More Moné More Problems," Mercedes lost the Prime Time Wrestling Women's title to Diana Strong, ending a reign that started the previous July when she defeated Strong at another PTW show in Poland. That loss was particularly poignant as it marked another instance of facing an opponent she had previously defeated, only to come up short in the rematch.

Things took a more dramatic turn when Moné returned to North America for Winnipeg Pro Wrestling's Fight at the Museum event. On March 31 (some sources list it as April 1), at a sold-out show in Canada, she faced Jody Threat in a match for the WPW Women's Championship. Threat had previously lost the title to Moné back in October, but she earned her rematch by winning the Voyageur Cup tournament, defeating competitors including Allie Katch and Shayna Baszler along the way. When Threat defeated Moné to reclaim the championship, ending Moné's 164-day reign, it marked another in her mounting series of defeats.

What makes this latest loss particularly significant is how it affected Moné emotionally. After the match, she was not feeling as gracious as she had been during her European tour stops. Instead, "The CEO" crashed out and threw beer all over fans after losing her second-to-last title, visibly frustrated as she was losing her identity as the multi-decorated champion who had defined herself by her collection.

The impact of these losses cannot be overstated. Just over a month removed from holding more than a dozen active titles, Moné has been reduced to a single championship after her string of defeats on the independent scene. She now holds only the APAC Women's Championship based out of Malaysia as an active physical championship. Additionally, she holds the 2025 Owen Hart Tournament belt, though this is not a title defended outside of the tournament itself in the following year, and it is unclear whether she will continue to count it as part of her collection now that the audience has begun to embrace her as a character.

These losses represent a major shift for Moné. Her global championship tour, which once made headlines across multiple promotions as she stacked titles and bragged about her dominance, has instead become a tour of humility where she has systematically lost almost everything. The contrast between her arrogant departure from AEW and her current stripped-down position could not be starker.

As for her AEW future, Moné has remained coy but teasing. She has posted cryptic messages on social media hinting at a big day on April 1, with one post simply stating "4 days" followed by a money bag emoji, leading many fans to believe a return announcement is imminent. With Dynamite being held in Winnipeg on the night she lost her latest title, there is significant speculation that she could appear on television imminently, potentially on the April 1 or subsequent episode.

Multiple potential scenarios have been discussed for Moné's return to AEW programming. A rematch with Willow Nightingale for the TBS Championship is one possibility, as the women are now 1-1 in their head-to-head matchups in All Elite Wrestling. Another scenario would involve Moné seeking revenge on Kris Statlander and Harley Cameron for the disrespect they showed her, particularly during the cake incident. She has claimed that she was coming for more than just titles when she came back.

Perhaps most intriguingly, some have speculated that Moné could turn babyface upon her return. When she originally made her AEW debut, she was greeted with applause and cheers from the company's fanbase, though she quickly turned heel. However, throughout her time in All Elite Wrestling, she has received some positive reactions despite her villainous behavior. After losing her collection of gold and being humbled by her recent losses, Moné could potentially return more humbled, wanting to redeem herself to the fans. A turn to babyface that involves her helping those she kept down for the past two years would be interesting as she attempts to regain the trust of the fans and her fellow AEW stars.

There is also the matter of the AEW Women's World Championship, the one major prize that heel Moné could never capture. With Timeless Toni Storm currently sidelined and the villainous Thekla holding the championship, there are questions about whether Moné could finally claim the company's top women's title now that the landscape has shifted. Will she return as the same egomaniac who left the promotion three months ago, or will the humbling experience of losing nearly all her titles have changed her approach to professional wrestling?

The answers to these questions are likely to come very soon. With Moné teasing something big for early April and with her most recent title loss occurring at a sold-out Winnipeg show on the same night Dynamite is in that same city, her return to AEW programming appears imminent. When she does return, fans and fellow wrestlers alike will be watching closely to see how "The CEO" has evolved from her time away and whether she will be able to rebuild her championship collection or pursue a completely new direction in the company.

MVP and Hurt Syndicate AEW Updates

An encouraging update has surfaced regarding The Hurt Syndicate's return to AEW programming. MVP took to social media to provide fans with a promising status report, confirming that the group is officially back together and gearing up for an in-ring comeback in the near future.

"The coolest thing is that we are back together in AEW and we'll be back in action SOON," MVP wrote. "Hurting people!!!" That's a bold statement, and one that suggests their return may be right around the corner, especially with AEW Dynamite taking place in Winnipeg, Manitoba.

The Hurt Syndicate has been off television in recent months, largely due to the absence of Bobby Lashley. Back in December 2025 during an episode of AEW Collision, MVP revealed that Lashley was dealing with an injury, though specific details regarding the issue were never disclosed. During the recovery period, Lashley addressed potentially targeting the AEW World Championship upon his return.

Lashley's last match was at Full Gear in November 2025 in a Casino Gauntlet match for the AEW National Championship. Meanwhile, fellow Hurt Syndicate member Shelton Benjamin has remained active, competing in the Dynamite Diamond Ring 2025 Battle Royal and an AEW Continental Title Eliminator against Jon Moxley.

In the second half of 2025, the group was also in pursuit of the AEW Trios Championship but were defeated by The Opps in October. In January of last year, Lashley and Benjamin captured the AEW Tag Team Titles, which marked their first championship reign in the promotion. Benjamin, Lashley and MVP made their AEW debut in September 2024 as The Hurt Syndicate, a revamped version of their former WWE faction, The Hurt Business.

With MVP now signaling that the group is ready to roll again, it appears AEW fans won't have to wait much longer to see The Hurt Syndicate back in action.

Ruby Soho and Baron Corbin Coffee Business

Ruby Soho has shared details about her and Angelo Parker's new business venture, Victory Roll Board Game Cafe, which opened while she was away from professional wrestling. The cafe, located in Mishawaka, Indiana, has become a unique establishment that blends elements of the wrestling world with a family-friendly entertainment concept.

In an interview with Wheezy, Soho described the cafe's impressive offerings and vision. The venue boasts over 800 board games and features some of the best food available, with their chef being a Las Vegas-trained culinary professional who continually develops creative new menu items. One notable connection to the wrestling industry is that Tom Pestock, formerly known as Baron Corbin, serves as the cafe's coffee distributor, providing quality coffee for their customers.

The primary mission of Victory Roll Board Game Cafe extends beyond simply serving food and beverages. According to Soho, the establishment aims to bring families together regardless of age and provide them with an alternative to digital entertainment. The concept encourages visitors to disconnect from their phones and technology, engage with the world around them, and discover new board games they may not have encountered before. The cafe maintains a wrestling-themed aesthetic throughout, though the core purpose remains creating a welcoming space where families can enjoy themselves and immerse themselves in the hobby of board gaming.

Soho announced her pregnancy in April 2024, which led to her departure from professional wrestling competition. She remained focused on her business endeavors and family during this time. In July of the following year, she returned to ring training, indicating a potential comeback to professional wrestling while maintaining her involvement with the cafe.

Danhausen April Fools Prank WWE Stars

Danhausen just pulled off a perfectly on-brand April Fools stunt involving everyone he's "cursed" since arriving in WWE. Since debuting, Danhausen has made it his mission to spread his so-called curse across the roster and beyond, targeting names like Dominik Mysterio, The Miz, Kit Wilson, and even streamer IShowSpeed, turning it into one of his most entertaining running gags.

But on April 1, 2026, Danhausen flipped the script. Taking to Twitter for April Fools' Day, he fired off a series of posts claiming that several of his past "victims" were finally free from the curse, only to immediately reveal the joke. He kicked things off with IShowSpeed, setting the tone for what was about to follow: "Hello @ishowspeedsui you're uncursed april fools hahahahahaha"

From there, he moved on to The Miz, continuing the exact same gag as he worked through his list: "Hello The @mikethemiz you're uncursed april fools hahahahahaha" Next up was Kit Wilson, another one of his past "victims" getting the fake clean slate: "Hello @KitWilson_PD you're uncursed april fools hahahahahaha"

Danhausen then targeted Dominik Mysterio, one of the bigger names he's interacted with since arriving in WWE: "Hello @DomMysterio35 you're uncursed april fools hahahahahaha" Finally, he wrapped things up with Grayson Waller and even added Kofi Kingston into the mix as a possible bonus target: "Hello @GraysonWWE and or possibly a little bit of @TrueKofi you're uncursed april fools hahahahahaha"

Of course, none of it actually meant anything. Every single tweet ended with the same punchline, making it clear this was all part of Danhausen's April Fools routine. Still, by going one-by-one in order, he managed to revisit every name tied to his ongoing curse gimmick, keeping the bit alive while getting one more round of attention on the chaos he's been causing since joining WWE.

MJF vs Nic Nemeth AEW Conflict

Nic Nemeth Addresses Ongoing Issues With MJF

The war of words between MJF and Nic Nemeth continues to escalate, with the conflict becoming increasingly personal. MJF recently took aim at Nemeth in a lengthy social media post, accusing him of disrespect and issuing a challenge for a match. The bout has since been made official by Create-A-Pro Wrestling for an event scheduled in May in Melville, New York.

While promoting the upcoming clash, Nemeth spoke with Pep Shop Collectibles and addressed MJF's comments with candor. Nemeth acknowledged that while some of MJF's criticisms stung, his own response came from a place of respect. "We saw some beef online, it was Max being insecure and being mad at me and saying a bunch of mean things and some of them true. He made some good points. Some digs, some gut punches that hurt. All I did was say that I think the world of him and I want him to be better and I want him to be like Dylan Carrizo. I want him to be someone that leads his company and leads everybody into 10 years from now where they're talking about this might be one of the greatest of all time. So, I look forward to it," Nemeth stated.

Nemeth went further in his assessment, suggesting that MJF may be deliberately trying to create a situation where AEW President Tony Khan might intervene and prevent the match. "As long as his boss (Tony Khan) lets him fight me, I think he's counting on his boss stepping in and being like 'Oh, sorry' because he's afraid to back down now," Nemeth claimed. "I ain't afraid of sh*t, I don't lose to anybody. I think I'm the best damn wrestler going today and then some. That's me being humble."

The tone shifted when Nemeth issued a warning about his intentions for the May matchup. Rather than frame this as a standard competitive bout, he made clear his aggressive mindset. "I look forward to this match, I look forward to kicking the sh*t out of him, maybe breaking his leg and tapping him out if I have to," Nemeth warned.
