🔥 Konosuke Takeshita vs. Kazuchika Okada for AEW International Championship at Double Or Nothing

The highly anticipated AEW International Championship match between Okada and Takeshita has officially been locked in for Double or Nothing, marking the first official match announcement for the pay-per-view event. The rivalry between these two wrestlers has been simmering for months, with constant tension and family in-fighting within the Don Callis Family finally boiling over into a sanctioned title bout.

The groundwork for this match was laid prior to AEW Dynasty when Okada and Takeshita agreed to team together against The Young Bucks, with the stipulation that a successful partnership would earn Takeshita a future title shot against Okada. However, the tag team match at Dynasty did not go according to plan. During the bout, Takeshita shocked everyone by walking out on Okada mid-match, leaving the champion alone to face both members of the Young Bucks. The numbers disadvantage proved too much to overcome, and Okada was ultimately pinned, handing his team the loss. Adding insult to injury, when confronted about his decision afterwards, Takeshita mockingly replied "Oops," making it clear that his abandonment was intentional.

In the aftermath of Dynasty, Don Callis attempted to use Takeshita's betrayal as justification to negate their original agreement. During the April 16 episode of AEW Collision's backstage interview segment, Callis stated that Takeshita had broken their deal by failing to show the necessary teamwork and trust with Okada. Callis suggested he could use his influence to prevent the title match from happening at Double or Nothing, essentially voiding the promised title opportunity.

However, Okada had other plans. The AEW International Champion immediately pushed back against Callis's proposal, making it abundantly clear that he wanted the match to happen. Okada emphasized his status as a fighting champion, noting that he was willing and eager to face Takeshita. More importantly, he took a direct shot at his would-be challenger, boldly stating that he is a champion while Takeshita is not, and that he fully believes he will emerge victorious if given the opportunity to face him.

Following this segment, AEW President Tony Khan made it official. Okada versus Takeshita for the AEW International Championship is now confirmed as the first match announced for AEW Double or Nothing 2026. The event will take place on Sunday, May 24 at Louis Armstrong Stadium in Queens, New York.

This rematch carries significant history between the two competitors. The last time Okada and Takeshita faced off in singles competition was during the Continental Classic tournament semifinals in December at Worlds End, where Okada won the match using controversial tactics, including allegedly using a screwdriver to secure his victory. Despite that win, Okada went on to lose the Continental Championship finals to Jon Moxley.

By the time Double or Nothing takes place, Okada will have held the AEW International Championship for over 300 days with seven successful title defenses under his belt. Takeshita, by comparison, held the title previously for 148 days from late 2024 through early 2025, successfully defending it eight times before losing it to Kenny Omega at 2025's Revolution. This history makes the impending rematch even more compelling, as both men have proven themselves capable of holding and defending the prestigious title.

The storyline leading into this match is rich with complexity. The tension between Okada and Takeshita within the Don Callis Family has escalated dramatically over recent weeks, with their inability to work together coming to a head at Dynasty. Takeshita's deliberate betrayal of Okada during the tag match, combined with his sarcastic dismissal of the incident, has added a personal element to what was already a competitive rivalry. Okada's insistence on facing Takeshita despite Callis's attempts to prevent it shows the confidence the champion has in his own abilities and his desire to definitively prove his superiority over his stablemate.

With the first match officially announced, anticipation continues to build for Double or Nothing 2026. Additional matches are expected to be announced in the coming weeks as AEW continues to build out the card for one of the promotion's marquee events of the year. The Okada versus Takeshita championship bout figures to be a standout encounter, featuring two of AEW's most accomplished international stars squaring off in a heated rivalry that has been building for months.

🔥 Wardlow's injury recovery and AEW return timeline

Wardlow's journey back to the ring has been defined by unexpected setbacks and personal loss, a reality the AEW star recently addressed in depth during an interview with BCP+. The three-time TNT Champion confirmed that he is recovering from a torn pectoral muscle, an injury he suffered just weeks after making his return to AEW programming at the AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door pay-per-view in August 2024. However, that torn pec represents only the latest chapter in a challenging two-year stretch that has kept him away from active competition.

When asked to send a message to fans who have missed his presence, Wardlow offered an apology for his absence from social media and public view. "I miss wrestling so much I can't even put it into words. It's been over two years since I've wrestled. So it has been a rough two years. Basically everything I have loved and cared about was taken away from me almost all at once. From my friend to my dog to wrestling to the ability to work out. I mean, everything I loved was taken away from me," Wardlow stated. He continued, "I do wanna apologize to the fans for my absence, and for completely going ghost on social media. Because I just kind of hid under a rock for a while. So I apologize to the fans. I will never be absent again, and I am so excited to get back to wrestling in front of a live crowd and at this point, I don't care if they're cheering me, I don't care if they're booing me. I just want to feel that energy. Positive or negative. I just wanna feel that energy and get back to doing what I love, and we're very, very close to being ready."

The current torn pectoral muscle, while significant, represents something Wardlow wanted to clarify to fans and observers. He emphasized that this is actually the first injury in his entire career, including his time on the independent wrestling circuit, that has forced him to take time away from wrestling. "This torn pec is the first injury in my entire life, indies included, that I've ever taken time off for wrestling. Any time you saw me absent before the car accident, any time you saw me absent was never because of an injury. It was creative or there just wasn't anything going on at the time and I was sitting at home. Did I tweak my knee? Yes, and I continued wrestling. Did I tweak my hamstring? Yes. I continued wrestling," he explained.

The real turning point came in April 2024 when Wardlow was involved in a serious car accident that dramatically altered his trajectory. In a remarkable display of his typical work ethic, Wardlow didn't immediately seek medical attention at a hospital. Instead, he got into his car, drove to the airport, and flew across the country to fulfill his AEW commitments. The physical toll of this decision became apparent during the flight. "I had to stand in the bathroom most of the flight because it hurt so bad to sit down. When I got to work, I had our doctors take a look at me and my hip was actually popped out of place, and I flew there with my hip out of place which is why it hurt so bad to sit down, and they popped my hip right back into place right there, which I don't think I've ever made the noise I made when that happened. I have a pretty high pain tolerance. Boy, did that hurt," Wardlow recounted.

Despite being barely able to walk after the accident, Wardlow was prepared to continue working through the injury. However, his plans changed when the storyline he had been looking forward to was cancelled for what he described as the second or third time. This combination of circumstances finally led him to make the unprecedented decision to take time off. "So it was the car accident that really messed me up, and I was gonna continue to work, which maybe wouldn't have been smart but I was gonna continue to work, and then the storyline that was planned got thrown in the garbage for like the second or third time, and that's when I ultimately decided, okay, I'm gonna go home. The storyline that I was looking forward to for the second time got canned. I can barely walk at this point after the car accident. So I finally decided to take some time off, which was the first time ever in my career," he said.

During his recovery period following the car accident, an unexpected opportunity presented itself. Wardlow received the blessing from AEW and Tony Khan to participate in the American Gladiators reboot on Amazon Prime, where he competes under the name "Fang." The experience proved to be both rewarding and physically demanding. Wardlow participated in the show's combine process and was ultimately selected to compete on the actual series, which premiered on April 17th.

The Gladiators experience introduced Wardlow to Rick Boogs, the former WWE talent who also competed on the show. During the combine, the two athletes were paired together for physical challenges that included push-ups, pull-ups, rope climbs, tire flips, and wrestling. Wardlow had high praise for Boogs' abilities and strength. "I will say I've been on the mat with a lot of guys; wrestling, jiu-jitsu. I've never felt overpowered, even remotely. I'm pretty strong fella. First time in my life I've ever felt somebody match my strength, and I will say, Boogs is the real deal, and he is a collegiate Division I wrestler, which I never was. So he should be very skillful," Wardlow noted. The process was intense, and Wardlow admitted there was uncertainty about whether he and Boogs were being evaluated against each other or simply being put on display.

Remarkably, Wardlow filmed his appearances on American Gladiators while dealing with a fully torn left bicep, an injury that compounded his already challenging recovery situation. Despite this additional setback, he managed to complete his commitments to the show before his return to AEW programming.

That return at Forbidden Door in August 2024 initially seemed like the beginning of a new chapter. Wardlow aligned himself with the Don Callis Family and engaged in a brief feud with former AEW World Champion Swerve Strickland. However, just a couple of weeks into his return, the torn pectoral muscle he suffered ended his time on television and sent him back into recovery mode.

Despite the frustration of suffering a major injury so quickly after his long-awaited return, Wardlow maintains an optimistic outlook about his future. He affirmed that his recovery timeline is moving along as planned and that fans should expect his comeback in the near future. "We are most definitely going to come back the biggest, baddest version of Wardlow that anybody's ever seen," he promised. His dedication to overcoming adversity and his commitment to returning to professional wrestling at full strength suggest that AEW fans won't have to wait much longer to see the powerhouse competitor back in action.

AEW partnership with Tom's Bar for NFL draft lucha mask promotion

All Elite Wrestling and Tom's Watch Bar have announced a national partnership centered around the 2026 NFL Draft, marking another expansion effort for AEW's first-ever collaboration with the NFL. The partnership was officially announced on April 16, 2026, and will feature high-energy draft watch parties at all Tom's Watch Bar venues nationwide across the three-day draft event from April 23 to April 25, 2026.

As part of the promotion, fans can visit any Tom's Watch Bar location during the NFL Draft to enter for a chance to win an exclusive prize package containing items from Tom's Watch Bar, two tickets to an upcoming AEW Dynamite or AEW Collision live event, and an AEW NFL officially licensed lucha mask. Tom's Watch Bar is the only physical location where these limited-edition masks will be available to win in person. The masks are branded to represent local NFL teams based near Tom's Watch Bar venues, and all 32 team variants are available for purchase online at ShopAEW.com.

Brooks Schaden, Co-CEO and Co-Founder of Tom's Watch Bar, expressed enthusiasm about the partnership: "In partnership with AEW, Tom's Watch Bar is transforming the NFL Draft into a multi-night celebration that brings together some of the most passionate fans in sports. We're proud to offer immersive experiences that fans can't get anywhere else, reinforcing Tom's Watch Bar as the premier destination for sports viewing nationwide. We're thrilled to welcome fans into our venues across the country for an exclusive chance to win a limited-edition AEW NFL officially licensed lucha mask and AEW tickets while watching the 2026 Draft alongside fellow fans."

Tony Khan, CEO, GM and Head of Creative for AEW, added: "All Elite Wrestling and Tom's Watch Bar are both powered by passionate, engaged fan communities, and this year's NFL Draft is sure to be an unforgettable event. Tom's Watch Bar's premium sound system and stadium-scale screens will transform the NFL Draft into a high-energy, immersive community experience, which perfectly aligns with AEW's emphasis on surprise, spectacle and fan participation."

Tom's Watch Bar operates 18 locations across 14 states and Washington DC, defining itself through the "All the Sports, All the Time" model. The chain features cutting-edge audio-visual technology with more than 100 screens per location, a 360-degree viewing experience, state-of-the-art kitchen equipment designed to deliver food in under 10 minutes, and dual-service bar stations. The company was founded in 2014 and is rapidly expanding across major U.S. cities and entertainment hubs. Notably, while there is no Tom's Watch Bar location in Jacksonville, Florida, home to Tony Khan's Jaguars franchise, one location is situated in Orlando where the Jaguars will play during their new stadium's renovation period.

Chris Jericho files trademark inspired by AEW storyline

Chris Jericho has filed a new trademark inspired by his recent on-screen struggles in AEW, demonstrating his characteristic self-awareness and humor. On April 15, Jericho submitted a filing for the phrase "The Guy Who Gets His Ass Beat By The Demand Every Week That He Shows Up," which directly references his ongoing storyline battles against The Demand, the faction composed of Bishop Kaun and Toa Liona.

The trademark filing covers a wide range of entertainment-related uses, including wrestling performances, live and televised appearances, personal appearances, and online interviews connected to professional wrestling and sports entertainment. The filing also seeks usage in regards to providing wrestling news and information via a global computer network and entertainment services involving wrestling exhibits and performances.

Jericho's recent run on AEW programming has been marked by repeated losses against The Demand. Most recently, he suffered another defeat on the Dynamite Spring Break episode in Everett, Washington. His battles with Ricochet have been particularly significant, with Ricochet defeating Jericho at AEW Dynasty on April 12 in Vancouver before later getting the better of him on AEW Dynamite when Ricochet, Bishop Kaun, and Toa Liona left Jericho laying with a three-on-one attack. Ricochet had previously warned Jericho that he would become the guy who gets his ass beat by The Demand every week that he shows up, making the warning prophetic.

The continued losses have become a key part of Jericho's current storyline. However, his ability to laugh about the situation is evident in his decision to trademark the phrase. While the trademark shows Jericho's sense of humor remains intact, it does leave him in a position to continue suffering at the hands of The Demand unless he is able to secure some assistance.

Jericho's approach aligns with his long-standing character work throughout his career, as he has frequently leaned into catchphrases, nicknames, and evolving personas. Many of those have been trademarked over the years, with some making it to television while others remained behind the scenes.

This trademark filing comes after Jericho signed a new multi-year deal with AEW earlier in April, opting to stay with the company despite reported interest from WWE in bringing him back for a potential retirement run. He made his return to AEW on April 1 in Winnipeg and later competed at AEW Dynasty on April 12.

It remains to be seen whether Jericho will find assistance from his former allies Big Bill and Bryan Keith, whom he was previously aligned with as part of The Learning Tree. However, he has not interacted with the duo since his return to AEW. Bill and Keith have remained with AEW, though they have largely competed as a tag team in Ring of Honor under the name Paid in Full.

WWE interest in AEW star Persephone

WWE are reportedly interested in signing Persephone when her AEW and CMLL dual contract expires. According to the Wrestling Observer Newsletter, those in WWE are already talking about trying to get Persephone when her dual contract expires later in 2026.

Persephone has been competing under a unique arrangement that allows her to work for both AEW and CMLL, where she has built a strong reputation as one of the standout talents in the lucha libre scene. The timing of the report aligns with WWE's recent strategy of targeting talent across the global wrestling landscape as contracts come up. The company has been especially focused on strengthening its women's division, adding a mix of established names and emerging stars from both domestic and international promotions.

At this time, there is no indication whether AEW has made efforts to retain Persephone ahead of her contract expiration, nor is it clear how CMLL's involvement could impact any potential negotiations.

The 24-year-old was confirmed to be signed to her dual deal in January of this year alongside Mascara Dorada and Hechicero. While she had competed in ROH beforehand, Persephone's biggest exposure came at AEW Forbidden Door 2025, when she, along with Alex Windsor and Bozilla, competed in a four-way for Mercedes Moné's AEW TBS Championship. Even though she didn't defeat Moné for the TBS Championship, she did eventually dethrone "The CEO" for the CMLL Women's World Title in March, a title she has since defended in ROH. Persephone's most recent match on AEW TV was again for the TBS Championship, this time unsuccessfully challenging Willow Nightingale for the gold on the March 11 episode of Dynamite.

WWE's pursuit of Persephone would be somewhat reminiscent of their signing of former CMLL Women's World Champion Stephanie Vaquer in 2024. After well over a decade competing in promotions across the world, Vaquer, like Persephone, gained major exposure in the United States at AEW's Forbidden Door pay-per-view, where she took on Mercedes Moné for both the NJPW STRONG Women's Title and AEW TBS Championship. Vaquer lost the bout, but just one month later she signed with WWE after receiving a contract offer from both them and AEW.

"La Primera" has since become one of WWE's top female stars, winning the NXT Women's North American Championship, NXT Championship and WWE Women's World Title within her first year competing on TV. Vaquer is now set to walk into her first WrestleMania as Women's World Champion, defending her gold against 2026 Women's Royal Rumble winner Liv Morgan on Night One of "The Grandest Stage of Them All."

At just 24, Persephone may have a slower climb up the WWE ladder than Vaquer. However, given just how successful the Chilean star has been in a short amount of time, it is not difficult to see why stars may be tempted to follow a similar path. With AAA now a part of WWE, it seems likelier that Persephone, like CMLL alum La Catalina, may first arrive there rather than get a move straight to WWE NXT, Raw or SmackDown.

Toni Storm's mystery attacker in AEW

"Timeless" Toni Storm might be gone for the rest of the year after falling victim to a mystery attacker last month at AEW Dynamite. At the time, a comprehensive suspects list included big names like Ronda Rousey, Mercedes Mone, Dr. Britt Baker DMD, and AEW Women's World Champion Thekla.

Mina Shirakawa has taken on the role of detective to crack the case, and while she hasn't made much progress so far, she pointed the finger at a new potential suspect on the Spring BreakThru episode of Collision that aired on April 16. Could the culprit actually be former AEW Women's World Champion Hikaru Shida?

The theory gained traction following a match on the show, as Mina began questioning Hikaru Shida about her connection to Toni Storm's attack. Several factors have led Mina to suspect Shida. First, Hikaru has teased a heel turn multiple times since recently returning to AEW. Additionally, Mina has concerns about recent comments Shida made regarding Harley Cameron. Perhaps most damning is the physical evidence: a kendo stick was found at the scene of Storm's attack, and a kendo stick happens to be Shida's signature weapon of choice.

Is Mina Shirakawa onto something with this accusation, or is Hikaru Shida being framed? The mystery continues to unfold in AEW's women's division.

AEW and Adult Swim's Tales From The Top Rope animated series

Debut Episode Of AEW & Adult Swim's 'Tales From The Top Rope' Animated Series Featuring Darby Allin

AEW, in conjunction with Adult Swim, has released the debut episode of their new animated series "Tales from the Top Rope: AEW Origins." The first installment focuses on newly crowned AEW World Champion Darby Allin and features distinctive narration that sets the tone for the entire project.

The episode's narration opens with a dramatic assertion about professional wrestling: "There is more to pro wrestling than what happens inside of the ring. It's in the shadows where things get weird. Moments that break and bend wrestlers into something else, leaving behind scars that never fade. These are the tales from the top rope."

The narrative then delves into Allin's personal mythology, describing his lifelong relationship with danger and mortality. According to the narration, Darby Allin has spent his entire life cheating death, and death has taken notice. The narration emphasizes that while most people spend their lives running from death, death means nothing to a person with a dream.

The episode presents a metaphorical confrontation between Allin and death itself. Death offers Darby a bargain: cross the pit and live again, death-proof. If he falls, Darby's soul will belong to death forever. Darby accepts the challenge but realizes the trick too late. The narration describes how, where others would see certain doom, Darby sees salvation. Death tries to stop him, but Darby just keeps going. The episode concludes with the powerful statement that death built the pit, but Darby turned it into a weapon.

The series marks an ambitious creative partnership between AEW and Adult Swim, bringing animated storytelling to professional wrestling. The first episode is now available on the AEW On TV YouTube Channel.

AEW Grand Slam Mexico Special Event plans change

Backstage News On Change In Plans AEW Has For 2026 Grand Slam: Mexico Special Event

An update has surfaced regarding AEW's plans to bring Grand Slam back to Mexico. While AEW President Tony Khan previously confirmed in a late-2025 interview that the company intended to return to Arena Mexico for a second Grand Slam Mexico event in 2026, specifics on timing remained unclear at the time.

New details have now emerged regarding the scheduling of this anticipated return. According to Dave Meltzer in this week's issue of the Wrestling Observer Newsletter, AEW is still planning to run Grand Slam Mexico in 2026, but fans shouldn't expect it during the same window as last year's debut show. "There are plans for a Grand Slam Mexico show at Arena Mexico in 2026," Meltzer wrote. "But they will be later in the year than June."

That's a notable shift in AEW's approach to the event. The inaugural AEW Grand Slam Mexico event took place on June 18, 2025, from Arena Mexico in Mexico City, marking a significant international milestone for the promotion.

As of now, an exact date for the 2026 return has not been announced, but the event remains firmly on AEW's schedule. Updates regarding AEW Grand Slam Mexico will continue to surface as the company finalizes its plans for the return engagement.
