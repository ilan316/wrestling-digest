Chris Jericho AEW and Wrestling Longevity

# Chris Jericho Reflects on Wrestling Longevity: "I Don't Know If It's a Year, Two Years, Four Years"

At 55 years old and 35 years into his professional wrestling career, Chris Jericho remains one of the industry's most enduring performers. Yet despite his remarkable longevity, the wrestling legend has serious concerns about whether today's generation of wrestlers will be able to sustain similarly lengthy careers.

In a recent interview with GamesHub, Jericho expressed worry about the current state of professional wrestling and its potential impact on performer longevity. "What worries me about the guys working today is here I am at 55, 35 years in, with still some career left to go," he said. "I don't know if it's a year, two years, four years — I'm not sure. But I don't know how many of the guys working now will have the option to go 35 years. Hopefully all of them."

## The Dangers of Modern Wrestling Style

Jericho pinpointed the emphasis on high-impact, physical wrestling as a primary concern for career sustainability. "The longevity may be harder to sustain when there's so much emphasis on the physical at the expense of charisma and character," he explained. He pointed out that serious injuries, particularly neck surgery, can fundamentally alter a wrestler's career trajectory and options.

The veteran performer emphasized that serious injuries are not theoretical concerns in the industry. "But you can already see what serious injuries do — a bad neck surgery changes everything," Jericho stated.

## Learning to Work Smart

Jericho credited an early-career injury with teaching him a crucial lesson about risk management in the ring. "I remember the first time I ever got hurt. I thought I was invincible. I was in León, Mexico, did a dive over the top rope, and the guy who was supposed to catch me — a guy called Masaka — he just stepped aside. I landed on old-school bolted-down arena chairs. I messed up my arm and I thought, oh, I could actually get hurt doing this. And if a guy doesn't want to catch you, that's even worse."

That sobering experience fundamentally changed Jericho's approach to in-ring work. "After that I started getting smart about which moves were worth the risk. The guys who figure that out early are the ones who get the longevity," he said. This wisdom, gained through painful experience, represents the kind of strategic thinking Jericho believes is essential for modern wrestlers seeking extended careers.

## Reinvention as a Career Foundation

Beyond physical concerns, Jericho has maintained his relevance through constant creative evolution. He drew inspiration from music legend David Bowie, whose approach to reinvention became a template for Jericho's career philosophy. "It's hard to single one out because there have been so many reinventions over so many years. That idea comes directly from David Bowie, honestly. I'm a huge Bowie fan, and he was never the same guy twice," Jericho explained.

The comparison is apt: just as fans could recognize different eras of Bowie at a costume party, wrestling fans can immediately identify different iterations of Chris Jericho across his career. "The ones people still talk about most are Y2J, obviously. Then things like 'Never, Ever Again' from WCW. 'You just made the list' was only around for maybe six or eight months, but it still resonates," he noted.

Jericho stressed the importance of avoiding becoming a nostalgia act. "The main thing for me is I never want to be a nostalgia act. I want to keep changing, keep doing things that make people angry at first," he said. He acknowledged that bold changes—like abandoning his iconic countdown timer, cutting his hair, or switching from long tights to short tights—initially faced fan resistance. "When I stopped using the countdown, cut my hair, changed from long tights to short tights — it was like KISS taking off the makeup. People want the makeup. But if you leave the makeup on forever, you die. You have to try something new, commit to it, and most of the time it works."

## AEW Highlights and Underrated Moments

During his time in AEW, Jericho created what he considers one of the most special moments in wrestling history. His 2021 feud with MJF featured a remarkable acapella rendition of his band Fozzy's signature song "Judas," which had been banned as part of the storyline stipulations.

"It was a great moment," Jericho reflected. "We were doing the storyline with MJF, and he had all these stipulations — one of them was banning Judas from being played. The idea came up — I think it was collaborative, the whole storyline lasted exactly 366 days, a year and a day — that we should ban the song and see what the crowd does."

The moment could have easily been sabotaged through over-production. However, Tony Khan and Jericho decided to trust the organic nature of the moment. "There were a lot of theories in the production meeting about how to make it work. One producer was adamant we needed to put the lyrics on the screen with a bouncing ball. Someone else wanted to put lyrics under the chairs. Everyone had ideas. But Tony Khan and I talked about it, and he said, 'When you go to a concert, people know the words or they don't.' That's the organic nature of it. If you force it, it's not going to be as cool."

The gamble paid off brilliantly. "The first ten seconds were a little rough, but by the third line of the song everyone clicked and got on the same page," Jericho said. "That was one of the coolest moments I've ever been part of, and I think it's actually a fairly underrated moment in AEW history and in wrestling history. That's the only time I can ever remember that happening that way. It was the one time where Fozzy and wrestling collided perfectly."

## Underrated Rivalries

When discussing feuds deserving of greater recognition, Jericho highlighted his rivalry with The Rock, which he believes has been overshadowed by Rock's more famous programs with Stone Cold Steve Austin and Triple H. "A really great one that I think deserves more recognition is Jericho versus the Rock. We could go toe to toe in the ring, and Rock was a great wrestler," he said.

Jericho emphasized the psychological and verbal dimensions of their feud. "Rock liked to talk things through in promos first, which I can do too, but there were very few people who could genuinely stand up to him on the mic. To have a great dragon slayer, you need a great dragon, and I think we were very good from that aspect."

Their rivalry included significant accomplishments, with Jericho eventually defeating Rock for the Undisputed Championship after Rock had accused him of being unable to win the big one. "There was a whole run where he accused me of not being able to win the big one — and then I went ahead and beat him for the Undisputed Championship. We were even tag team champions together at one point, for about two weeks," Jericho noted.

He emphasized the quality of their interactions: "Every time we were in the ring together or did a promo or a backstage segment, it was excellent." However, Rock's extended absence from wrestling and his association with other rivalries has caused their feud to fade from collective memory. "I think Rock's been gone from wrestling for so long, and people associate him so strongly with Austin and Triple H, that the Jericho-Rock rivalry kind of gets forgotten. But go back and watch it — it's very top-level stuff," Jericho concluded.

## Looking Forward

Jericho's current status with AEW remains unclear, with speculation about potential returns to WWE or a final retirement run. What's certain is that the legendary performer still has unfinished business in professional wrestling. Whether that final chapter lasts one year or four, Jericho's legacy as one of wrestling's greatest technical workers and character creators is already secure—a legacy built on the principles of working smart, constant reinvention, and never settling for being a relic of the past.

AEW Spring BreakThru & Playoff Palooza Television Specials

# AEW Announces Spring BreakThru and Playoff Palooza Specials For April During WrestleMania Week

AEW is bringing back a pair of themed television specials this April, marking the continuation of what has become an annual tradition for the company. According to the latest HBO Max monthly schedule release, both Spring BreakThru and Playoff Palooza editions of AEW Dynamite and AEW Collision are set to return, though with some notable scheduling adjustments.

## Spring BreakThru Details

Spring BreakThru kicks things off with a special episode of AEW Dynamite scheduled for Wednesday, April 15 in Kent, Washington. The themed programming will then carry over to Collision, but the episode will air on Thursday, April 16—a notable shift from its usual Saturday slot.

The Thursday airing is believed to be due to NHL playoff coverage on TNT, which is expected to impact AEW's regular weekend scheduling. As a result, the April 16 episode of Collision will not air live. Instead, it will be taped immediately following Dynamite on April 15.

Both Spring BreakThru shows will serve as fallout from AEW's upcoming Dynasty pay-per-view, continuing storylines emerging from that event. The concept was originally launched last April in Boston with back-to-back live episodes, making this year's shows the follow-ups to that initial run.

## Playoff Palooza Return

Playoff Palooza is also set to return later in the month, again influenced by NHL scheduling conflicts. The special themed edition of Collision will air on Saturday, April 25 with a slightly adjusted start time of 8:30 PM Eastern. That episode will also be taped in advance, taking place in Portland, Oregon on Wednesday, April 23.

## Full April Schedule

The complete AEW television schedule for April includes:

- Wednesday, April 1 – AEW Dynamite, 8 p.m.
- Saturday, April 4 – AEW Collision, 8 p.m.
- Sunday, April 5 – AEW Dynasty PPV, 8 p.m.
- Wednesday, April 8 – AEW Dynamite, 8 p.m.
- Saturday, April 11 – AEW Collision, 8 p.m.
- Wednesday, April 15 – AEW Dynamite: Spring BreakThru, 8 p.m.
- Thursday, April 16 – AEW Collision: Spring BreakThru, 8 p.m.
- Wednesday, April 22 – AEW Dynamite, 8 p.m.
- Saturday, April 25 – AEW Collision: Playoff Palooza, 8:30 p.m.
- Wednesday, April 29 – AEW Dynamite, 8 p.m.

These scheduling adjustments demonstrate how external programming commitments continue to shape AEW's broadcast strategy during peak sports seasons.

Ronda Rousey AEW Debut and Future

# Ronda Rousey's AEW Future in Doubt Following Revolution Appearance

Ronda Rousey made her debut appearance with All Elite Wrestling at AEW Revolution 2026 on March 15, confronting Timeless Toni Storm following Storm's victory over Marina Shafir. However, the appearance has been confirmed as a one-time event rather than the beginning of an ongoing partnership with the promotion.

## Details of the Debut

During the Revolution pay-per-view, Rousey came out to face off with Storm after her match concluded. The moment allowed Rousey's MMA Four Horsewomen ally Marina Shafir to get a shot in on Storm following the contest. Since that appearance, Rousey has not returned to AEW programming, including AEW Dynamite or Collision.

## Limited Plans for Return

According to multiple reports, Rousey is "not expected back anytime soon" in AEW. The appearance was primarily intended to promote her upcoming MMA return for Most Valuable Promotions against Gina Carano, with their fight scheduled to stream live on Netflix on Saturday, May 16. Additionally, the appearance served to show support for her longtime friend Marina Shafir.

Sources indicated that the timing simply "made sense" as Revolution took place locally to Rousey and all elements aligned. There was reportedly no follow-up between Rousey and Storm planned before Storm was recently written off television.

## Potential Future Opponents

Despite the one-off nature of the appearance, multiple potential opponents exist should AEW decide to pursue a follow-up. Timeless Toni Storm, the four-time AEW Women's World Champion and widely regarded as the biggest female star in the company's history, seems like the natural first opponent given their Revolution confrontation. However, Storm was attacked on the March 18 episode of Dynamite and is expected to be out of action for an extended period.

Mina Shirakawa, Storm's Timeless Love Bombs tag team partner, emerged as an alternative option after replacing Storm in a No Holds Barred Match against Marina Shafir. Shirakawa picked up what was described as the biggest win of her AEW run. If Rousey proves to be the mystery assailant behind Storm's attack, it could set up a high-profile encounter between the two competitors.

## Company's Open Position

While AEW has not confirmed any future appearances by Rousey, the company's officials are reportedly open to the possibility of her returning. However, Rousey will not be available for any physical competition before her Carano fight on May 16.

## The Case for Follow-Up

Rousey's Revolution appearance has created a built-in storyline that AEW could revisit in the future. Whether the promotion should follow up ultimately depends on fan reaction, particularly given some of Rousey's previous comments about professional wrestling. Questions remain about whether she would be the right fit for AEW's roster, especially given the promotion's emphasis on the in-ring aspect of the business.

Despite potential fan resistance, tying her appearance into a follow-up and eventual payoff rather than leaving it as a standalone event would provide more narrative closure and investment. After her scheduled MMA fight in May concludes, the foundation for a potential return story is already in place.

Kyle Fletcher Praises AEW Locker Room

# Kyle Fletcher Praises AEW Locker Room, Credits Company Culture for Personal Growth

Kyle Fletcher has expressed overwhelming enthusiasm for the atmosphere within All Elite Wrestling, describing a passionate and motivated roster that he believes represents the best wrestling environment in the world.

Speaking recently with WhatCulture Wrestling, Fletcher highlighted the collective mindset of the AEW roster and what he believes sets the company apart from its competitors. "The vibe—it's hard to put your finger on—but I think it's the fact that everyone there wants to be there. Everyone is so passionate, not just about pro wrestling, but about All Elite Wrestling itself," he explained.

## A Roster Committed to Excellence

Fletcher emphasized the exceptional level of performance and commitment across the company's entire roster. "Everyone just wants to go out there and do their absolute best every single time. I think, individually, every single person on the roster is on their absolute A-game, and they want to see this place succeed," he stated. "They want to keep this momentum rolling, and the ratings, the pay-per-view buys, and all that stuff are kind of just reaffirming that AEW is the best place in the world to wrestle."

The positive environment has had a tangible effect on Fletcher's own performance and motivation. "It just makes me excited to go out there every single night. I could be doing anything—I could be wrestling anybody, cutting a promo, whatever—and I just want to go out there and do my absolute best for the fans, for the company, for all of it," he said.

Fletcher concluded by expressing gratitude for his position within the company. "I feel so grateful to be in this position, so grateful to be with such a great company. I just want AEW to be the biggest and best it can possibly be, and I want to do my part in that."

## Reflecting on Personal Growth

Beyond commenting on the locker room atmosphere, Fletcher has also reflected on his own trajectory within AEW. A recent backstage interaction with AEW World Champion MJF on the March 11th edition of AEW Dynamite served as a striking reminder of how far he has progressed since joining the company three years ago.

In the segment, Fletcher towered over MJF and refused to do the world champion's bidding, creating obvious tension as the Australian star continues to establish himself as potential main event-level talent. Speaking to Soundsphere Magazine about the encounter, Fletcher noted that the interaction "reminded him of just how far he's come" in his three years with the company.

"Because, when you're living it and when you're experiencing it, you see every single little incremental step. So it doesn't feel like this massive growth and change until you see something like that, and then you can retrospectively look and go, 'Oh my god, look how much has changed.' That kind of interaction was very, very telling and it reminded me of just how far I've come in those three years. It's always a cool feeling to realise how much growth you've gone through," Fletcher reflected.

Ricochet MS Comment Controversy

# Rob Van Dam Defends Ricochet Amid Backlash Over Multiple Sclerosis Comment

Rob Van Dam has weighed in on the controversy surrounding Ricochet's inflammatory tweet about a fan's multiple sclerosis diagnosis, suggesting that the social media backlash went too far.

The controversy erupted following AEW's Revolution event on Sunday, when the company posted a clip of Ricochet destroying a backstage area after losing the AEW National Championship in the Blackjack Battle Royal. A fan identified as Sandi T., whose social media bio mentions her battle with MS, commented on the clip: "His acting hasn't improved."

Ricochet responded to the criticism with a post stating: "I'm glad you got MS." The comment sparked swift and widespread outrage across social media, with many wrestling fans sharing personal stories about loved ones who have suffered from the disease.

## Apology and Continued Fallout

The backlash intensified when reports emerged that Ricochet's wife, Samantha Irvin, had received death threats and stepped away from social media for her safety. Ricochet initially stood by his tweet before issuing a formal apology a day later.

"I took out my hatred for the IWC on Sandi, and inadvertently others who are effected by MS," Ricochet wrote in his statement posted on X (formerly Twitter). "She didnt deserve it and for that, I sincerely apologize. Moving forward I'll do better."

According to reports, a close wrestling friend—later identified as Chris Hero—had reached out to advise Ricochet to reconsider the post. While many fans accepted his apology, others remained angry and called for his release from AEW.

## RVD's Defense

During an appearance on TMZ's Inside the Ring podcast, the WWE Hall of Famer offered a contrarian perspective on the situation. Van Dam acknowledged that while Ricochet's post was "a horrible thing to say," he believed the online reaction was disproportionate.

"You guys know I have a history with the internet mark community," Van Dam explained. "I think they went too far. I'm not saying I have his back, but big deal, is it that much worse than saying, 'F*** off and die?' Nobody would care if he said that."

Van Dam further suggested that Ricochet's intentions should be considered when evaluating the severity of his words. "It's freedom of speech. What a horrible thing to say. But come on, so what? Why take it that seriously? And who even knows if he was trying to get heat, being a heel," he said. "I doubt that he really, truly felt that way. I always think that you should judge by the intentions behind the words."

When the TMZ hosts praised his viewpoint, Van Dam added: "Yeah, the different perspective I'll go with, you know. Not always on the side of the wrestlers, but I do look at it differently than most people, and I expect to get a lot of hate for saying that, by the way."

## WWE Return Possibility

During the interview, Van Dam was also asked about the possibility of returning to WWE for a retirement tour. The 55-year-old veteran expressed openness to the idea, stating that he remains willing to consider various opportunities.

"I am open to consider anything, that's part of being open-minded," he said. "I try not to shut anything out without at least considering it. And when it comes to something like that, they got my number. They can talk to me."

However, Van Dam clarified that he is not interested in formalizing his exit from professional wrestling. "But as far as a retirement tour, unless I change my perspective — which always happens, you're always looking at everything from a different place in life if you keep going — but the way I feel now, I don't like the idea of labeling something 'retirement' or I don't really want to know if I'm having my last match when I am," he explained.

Thunder Rosa Reflects on Britt Baker Lights Out Match

# Thunder Rosa Questions Britt Baker's Motivation Behind Landmark Lights Out Match

Thunder Rosa has reflected on her historic Unsanctioned Lights Out match with Britt Baker from March 2021, suggesting that while she entered the bout with noble intentions, her opponent may have had different motivations.

The match between Rosa and Baker became one of the most talked-about encounters in AEW Women's Division history. Contested during the pandemic in front of only their peers, the hardcore bout featured extreme stipulations including ladders, chairs, and thumbtacks. Most notably, it marked the first time two women bled on television in an AEW match, earning both competitors Match of the Year honors for the year.

Speaking to Real 92.3 LA, Rosa reflected on the significance of the contest and her approach to competing in it. "I was a lone person there," Rosa explained, noting that she wasn't even signed to AEW at the time, having been working for NWA instead. "I remember Britt was telling me like, 'We're going to make sure that this goes great and we don't mess up or whatever.' She said something else, and I'm just thinking like, 'Girl, I know you're doing it for the clout.'"

Rosa contrasted her own perspective with what she perceived from Baker. "I'm doing it because people don't understand that this is not only going to change my life, but this is going to change a lot of morenitas and brown people's lives," Rosa said, emphasizing the broader representation and impact she hoped the match would achieve.

Rosa ultimately won the encounter, sending Baker through a table with a Fire Thunder Driver. The rivalry between the two competitors continued to develop over the following year, as they squared off in two AEW Women's Championship matches. Baker won the first title encounter, but Rosa secured victory in the second, which took place inside a steel cage.

Bear Bronson Exits AEW

# Bear Bronson Praises AEW For Flexibility During Departure, Credits Company For Indie Opportunity

Bear Bronson has nothing but gratitude for AEW despite his departure from the promotion in June 2025. The wrestler, who became a free agent that month before later signing with TNA, reflected positively on how the company handled his exit during a recent interview.

Bronson's time with AEW concluded following what he described as "controversial stuff" that led to the company deciding not to use him further. Rather than simply releasing him and forcing him to wait out his deal, AEW took an unexpectedly generous approach to the situation.

"The thing with AEW where everything had happened, the controversial stuff had happened and it came to a conclusion, like, 'It's just too soon. We're not going to use you.' They do have a policy where you have to clear all your Indies with them and I was like, 'Well, if my deal is coming up and I want to hit the ground running, I want to start now. I don't want to wait 'till it's done,'" Bronson explained. "They were so cool about it. They were like, 'Just go, just do your thing.' That was very, very cool of them to do that. They didn't have to do that, but they were very cool to do that."

The decision proved transformative for Bronson's career. With AEW's blessing to pursue independent bookings while his contract wound down, he was able to maintain momentum during the crucial transition period. "That's where in that calendar year of 2025 into 2026, I was able to lock in 120 and hopefully maybe even get more this year," he said, referencing the number of independent shows he secured.

Beyond AEW's professional courtesy, Bronson also took a moment to express personal gratitude to those who supported him during difficult times. He specifically thanked Sean Ross Sapp for reaching out with genuine concern rather than professional motivation. "You were one of the people that hit me up just to see if I was okay and to see if my wife was okay. That was just really cool. Like it was not a professional thing. It was purely personal. That meant a lot to me," Bronson shared.

Following his stint on the independent circuit, Bronson eventually signed with TNA Wrestling, where he continues his career today.
