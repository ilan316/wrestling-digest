🔥 AEW Forbidden Door event and updates

AEW's Forbidden Door event takes place this Sunday, June 27 from San Jose, California, and will air at 8pm EST, competing directly with WWE NXT's The Great American Bash at 7pm EST. The card features several compelling matchups headlined by the Owen Hart Foundation Tournament finals, where AEW wrestler Maya World will face Mercedes Mone in what represents a major milestone for the young competitor.

Maya World's journey to this main event has been remarkable and deeply emotional. She was called into the Owen Hart Tournament just two weeks after the tragic passing of her younger brother Jatwane. Despite the overwhelming grief, Maya has picked up significant victories over both Skye Blue and Athena to earn her spot in the final. In an interview, Maya reflected on the surreal nature of her accomplishment, stating that if someone had told her in January or February that she would be facing her idol at Forbidden Door as one of the main events from the Owen Tournament, she would not have believed them. She noted that last year she was in the audience at All In as an extra, never imagining she would be competing on such a stage just twelve months later.

Maya opened up about the emotional toll of competing during such a difficult time in her life. She explained that mentally it has been really tough, but she feels a responsibility to be strong for herself, her family, and her brother because she is doing it for him. She described feeling a lot of numbness, which paradoxically makes competing easier because it feels like she is on a mission with full steam ahead. Maya is using the momentum in her heart and brain to get through these matches. Facing Mercedes Mone holds special significance as Mone has previously been cited as Maya's inspiration in wrestling.

Maya World and her tag team partner Hyan were signed to AEW in January after taking an unsigned booking on AEW television in December. Maya spoke about her journey to AEW and her goals from the beginning of her indie career, including wanting to make her name known and eventually catch the attention of promoters like Tony Khan. She also mentioned being part of the next generation of women wrestlers alongside her friends Jada Stone and Maggie Lee from TNA who were signed just months before her.

Tony Khan has been candid about the significant challenges involved in putting Forbidden Door together, particularly regarding international logistics. He revealed that visa issues have severely impacted the event, with multiple wrestlers from around the world who were originally factored into AEW's plans currently unavailable to compete. Khan expressed that this has been a massive challenge for the event and acknowledged that there are a number of wrestlers from countries all over the world that he was looking forward to having return who are not currently available.

Specific wrestlers affected by visa issues include several Mexican stars. Khan named Beast Mortos, Komander, and Hechicero as wrestlers from Mexico who have not had their visas in recent weeks. He also mentioned that AEW is going through visa renewals with a number of other wrestlers in Mexico that he had not yet named publicly. Beyond Mexico, the visa challenges have extended to other international talent, including Gabe Kidd, who is also going through the renewal process.

Despite these significant setbacks, Khan praised the depth of the AEW roster and its ability to maintain strong programming. He noted that it speaks to the great depth of the AEW roster that AEW has been putting on tremendous television every Wednesday and Saturday, producing four hours of what he believes is very strong television. The company has managed to create an exciting pay-per-view card for Forbidden Door without many of those affected stars being available.

Khan did offer some encouraging news regarding absent talent. He specifically mentioned that Mark Davis, the National Champion who recently left and is a huge part of AEW, is expected back very soon. Khan stated that he would expect Mark to return possibly sooner than some of the other people he had named, but expressed hope that all of the affected wrestlers would get everything worked out soon.

Tony Khan has also indicated that AEW remains open to expanding Forbidden Door with more promotions in the future. He explained that there is a lot of great wrestling in the world, and with AEW, the company is trying to embrace that global talent. Khan noted that AEW works with a lot of promotions and partners with other wrestling companies all over the world, not only to scout wrestlers but also to develop wrestlers and wrestling companies.

Forbidden Door has already expanded beyond just AEW and NJPW partnerships. Khan pointed out that CMLL and Stardom have also become part of the event, giving AEW access to top talent from Mexico and Japan. CMLL is described as a tremendous partner for AEW, and Stardom brings some of the top women wrestlers in Japan to compete in AEW. Khan acknowledged that putting together an international event like Forbidden Door comes with numerous logistics, including work visas and dealing with other promoters who may have different ideas about how their stars should be used. Still, Khan expressed excitement about the weekend's event and indicated that more wrestlers and promotions could join the mix in future years.

A new match has been added to the Forbidden Door Buy-In pre-show. Skye Blue will face Maika in the final Survival of the Fittest Qualifier for the TBS Championship. Paul Heyman compares AEW to ECW, Tony Khan reacts

Paul Heyman recently compared All Elite Wrestling to the spirit of ECW while praising the company's impact on professional wrestling. During an appearance on the Insight podcast with Chris Van Vliet, Heyman discussed how AEW has positively changed the business landscape since its 2019 launch.

Heyman credited Khan with transforming the compensation packages for professional wrestlers. He explained that before AEW's arrival, WWE was essentially the only major wrestling company in the United States following WCW's closure in 2001. "Well, I certainly hope so. I mean it short changed the compensation packages for a lot of talent because there was a wolf across the river named Tony Khan that had a billion dollar checkbook that he could afford to pay a lot more money for talent than talent was making back in 2017, 2018, 2019," Heyman said.

Beyond financial improvements, Heyman praised AEW's wrestling style and presentation. He noted that while AEW's product differs from WWE's approach, the company clearly carries ECW's influence throughout. Heyman drew specific comparisons, likening Jon Moxley's character to The Sandman from ECW and describing AEW's fast-paced, physical matches as reminiscent of the famous Rob Van Dam versus Jerry Lynn rivalry from 1999. He also acknowledged that PWG played a role in bridging the gap between ECW's legacy and AEW's current style.

Rather than viewing these similarities negatively, Heyman embraced them as beneficial for the industry. "So it certainly changed the compensation for talent. And they're on a viable network. They have a style that's different than WWE's. They present an alternative, if not a competitive brand," he stated. Heyman emphasized that having another major wrestling company benefits both wrestlers and fans because competition drives everyone to improve.

Tony Khan responded to Heyman's comments with genuine appreciation. Speaking on 107.7 The Bone, the AEW President expressed gratitude for the praise, particularly because Heyman served as a major influence during Khan's childhood as a wrestling fan. "Well, I appreciated what he said. I thankfully grew up as a big wrestling fan and I appreciate that he had some kind things to say about AEW and when I was growing up he was a major influence on me and a lot of people in pro wrestling. So that was gratifying," Khan said.

Khan also responded positively to Heyman's "wolf" reference, taking it as a compliment rather than criticism. "I also appreciated that he called me a wolf. That's a nice thing to be referred to," Khan remarked with good humor.

Khan used the opportunity to reflect on AEW's accomplishments over its seven-year history. He highlighted an impressive milestone regarding Wednesday Night Dynamite's longevity on cable television. "Wednesday Night Dynamite is the longest running prime time pro wrestling show ever on TBS or TNT. And I think that's amazing because there's such a rich history of pro wrestling on TBS and TNT. They're great channels. The legacy of wrestling on TBS goes back to 1971. And on TNT goes back to 1995. And none of the shows on those channels have lasted in prime time longer than Wednesday Night Dynamite," Khan explained.

The AEW President emphasized that the company has accomplished far more than simply surviving in a competitive market. "So, very proud of our run, and AEW definitely came at the perfect time to launch a challenger pro wrestling promotion. And we've done a lot more than launch. We've continued to grow and develop, I think, and I believe that we've gotten better," Khan stated.

Khan expressed particular pride in AEW's recent output, describing the company's current product as among its strongest ever. "AEW continues to put on really strong shows. And after all these years, I thought that our most recent pay-per-view, Double or Nothing 2026 last month, is in my opinion the best AEW show ever," he said. Khan noted that many fans share this sentiment, and he credited recent programming with maintaining a high standard. "A lot of people feel that way, that in 2026 AEW's been putting out our best shows ever. I thought that Revolution and Dynasty were tremendous. The TV every week, Wednesday Night Dynamite and Saturday Night Collision is at such a high standard right now. So, very proud of what we're doing now, seven years into our run and still going very strong with AEW."

GCW discussions with AEW about MyAEW partnership

Game Changer Wrestling has been streaming its shows on Triller TV since 2018, but with Triller facing significant financial and operational difficulties, questions have emerged about the promotion's future home. GCW owner Brett Lauderdale acknowledged Triller's issues to reporters but emphasized that his promotion has been exempt from those problems and maintains a strong relationship with the platform.

House of Glory recently announced they were leaving Triller as well, with industry observers anticipating they would join MyAEW.

The growth of MyAEW's roster appears to be continuing. Tony Khan recently stated that numerous promoters have reached out to him about streaming their shows on the platform, with particular interest expected around WrestleMania weekend next year. GCW's ongoing discussions would position the major independent promotion as a potential significant addition to MyAEW's expanding catalog of wrestling content.

WWE had internal unhappiness this week as the entire episode of Friday Night SmackDown leaked online in different places before its scheduled broadcast. Even the post-show dark match got out there, as Seth Rollins and Oba Femi defeated The Vision before Rollins performed the Oba Strut, mimicking Femi's signature celebration.

The leak represents an ongoing challenge for WWE when taping major shows days in advance, particularly when significant surprises are involved. The question now is whether WWE will change how it handles taped international episodes going forward, because this one clearly did not stay under wraps. There is no word on how the leak occurred, but those within the company were said to be unhappy with the situation given the obvious impact on the show's surprises and presentation.

This incident highlights the difficulty WWE faces in maintaining surprise angles and story developments when content is filmed far in advance of broadcast. The London taping had considerable distance between filming and air date, creating multiple opportunities for the material to be captured and distributed online through various platforms before the official presentation.
