🔥 WrestleMania 42 Preview and Analysis

WRESTLEMANIA 42 PREVIEW AND ANALYSIS

WWE's biggest annual event arrives this weekend as WrestleMania takes over Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas for back-to-back nights on April 18 and 19. The card features several marquee matches with significant implications for the company's future direction, though the build has been marked by both compelling storytelling and considerable controversy surrounding certain creative decisions.

The centerpiece is the Undisputed WWE Championship main event between Cody Rhodes and Randy Orton on Saturday night. The match pits longtime rivals against each other with deep history, as Orton brought Rhodes into WWE and mentored him early in his career. Their relationship has been tested by Orton's recent turn toward a more sinister character, culminating in a brutal contract signing attack. However, the storyline has been complicated by the surprise revelation that Pat McAfee, the ESPN host and former WWE commentator, is Orton's mystery backer. McAfee's involvement reportedly came from TKO executive Ari Emanuel rather than WWE creative, and the decision has generated substantial criticism from fans, wrestlers, and media observers.

Rhodes has been particularly vocal about his displeasure with McAfee's role, calling him a "full-blown rat" and describing the image of McAfee holding the WWE Championship as "the most offensive image in wrestling history." Rhodes acknowledged during his media rounds that he has dealt with outside forces during three consecutive WrestleMania builds, following The Rock's involvement two years ago and Travis Scott's presence last year. He suggested that the constant external additions might eventually force him to change his character, potentially exploring a heel turn that he has long resisted. Meanwhile, Triple H acknowledged that WWE must constantly adapt their creative plans based on crowd reactions and unexpected circumstances, noting that unlike sports, there are no backup stars to rely on if someone like Rhodes or Orton becomes unavailable.

The Pat McAfee situation has drawn criticism from multiple quarters. CM Punk, who is headlining Sunday's main event defending the World Heavyweight Championship against Roman Reigns, delivered a sharp promo addressing McAfee's involvement, claiming that wrestling has traditionally embraced people who can draw eyeballs but that McAfee has forgotten he is a tourist in the business rather than a permanent member of it. Punk suggested that while McAfee is great at his ESPN job, he is not great at professional wrestling. Punk stated that his promo came from a feeling shared by many people in the company, including hair, makeup, production, and crew members who felt disrespected by McAfee's elevated role. Some analysts, including Bully Ray, wondered whether WWE had lost confidence in the Rhodes-Orton match, though the company maintaining the main event slot suggests continued faith in the program despite its controversial direction. Reports indicated that WWE was considering adjusting the storyline following fan backlash over McAfee's involvement.

Several WWE stars face critical moments at WrestleMania 42 beyond the main event. Liv Morgan's battle with Stephanie Vaquer for the WWE Women's World Championship carries significant weight, with Morgan's Royal Rumble victory being widely viewed as overdue recognition for her contributions to the women's division. A victory would validate her resurgence and solidify her third reign as Women's World Champion. Randy Orton's quest for his 15th world title represents a major career milestone that would freshen up the main event scene and potentially lead to new storytelling opportunities for Rhodes following his reign.

Oba Femi's match against Brock Lesnar provides an opportunity for the dominant young star to cement his main roster credentials. Femi vacated his NXT Championship in December 2025, made his main roster debut at the Royal Rumble, and has demonstrated early dominance. A decisive victory over Lesnar at WrestleMania 42 would accelerate his career and help establish him among wrestling's elite. Finn Balor's "Demon" persona faces off against Dominik Mysterio, with Balor's return to this presentation marking his first appearance in the character since WrestleMania 39. The victory would help revert Balor to the push he missed during his time in Judgment Day.

Seth Rollins attempts to dethrone Gunther and end his impressive title reign. The two have brief history from a 2023 World Heavyweight Championship semifinal, and their unexpected WrestleMania collision could prove compelling, particularly if Paul Heyman becomes involved as has been speculated. Gunther has accused Rollins of avoiding him throughout his career, suggesting that Rollins, despite his reputation as wrestling everyone, has been ducking the Ring General. Gunther positioned himself as composed and focused compared to Rollins' emotional volatility, setting the stage for their stylistic clash. Cody Rhodes identified their match as his most anticipated bout on the card, praising it as a classic wrestlers' wrestling match emphasizing old-school sports action.

Rhea Ripley's challenge against Jade Cargill for the WWE Women's Championship comes amid concerns regarding Cargill's in-ring work and limited title defenses since winning the championship in November 2025. Ripley has earned fan support through her singles work and positioning, with the addition of Cargill's new alliance with B-Fab and Michin creating numbers disadvantage despite Ripley having IYO SKY in her corner. A Ripley victory would restore her to championship status and establish her dominance in 2026 while shifting her back to singles spotlight on the SmackDown brand.

Outside the ring, several celebrity and special guest appearances have been confirmed or anticipated. John Cena returns as the event's host after retiring from in-ring competition last year, with Triple H praising Cena's enduring passion for wrestling despite pursuing his film career. Stone Cold Steve Austin is expected to make an appearance following his Hall of Fame induction at WrestleMania 41. Various celebrities including Jelly Roll, IShowSpeed, and Lil Yachty have been incorporated into storylines throughout the build, while names like Tyrese Haliburton, Metro Boomin, and Kevin Hart have been speculated as potential guest appearances.

Becky Lynch has been featured in the WrestleMania 42 promotion, with the former champion reflecting on a defining moment from her career when a broken nose and severe concussion from Nia Jax at Survivor Series 2018 unexpectedly changed her trajectory toward main event status. That injury forced her out of a scheduled match but opened the door for her famous Royal Rumble victory and WrestleMania 35 main event win, creating the iconic "Bloody Becky" image that catalyzed her ascent. She currently faces AJ Lee in another major singles competition.

CM Punk has opened up extensively about his WrestleMania journey, revealing that he believes WWE manipulated him during his first run by dangling the main event opportunity as incentive. He suggested that his obvious desire for the spot made him vulnerable to being controlled based on that ambition. Punk finally achieved the milestone last year by headlining WrestleMania 41 in a triple threat match against Roman Reigns and Seth Rollins, but now defends his World Heavyweight Championship against Reigns again on Sunday. He reflected that had he achieved the main event earlier in his career, he likely would not have returned to WWE, as he would have considered it a completed goal rather than ongoing unfinished business.

Ticket sales have been tracking slightly behind last year's WrestleMania, with WrestleMania Saturday showing 42,715 tickets distributed out of 45,767 configured seats as of four days before the event, compared to 60,151 distributed tickets last year. WrestleMania Sunday similarly showed 43,394 distributed compared to 61,389 the previous year. Pat McAfee announced a 25 percent discount sale for Saturday tickets in an effort to boost attendance, though the discount and online resale markets still featured standard admission pricing around $215.50.

The two-night event marks the first WrestleMania broadcast on ESPN, with the first night airing on ESPN2. The card is expected to be WrestleMania's best offering for the year, though the McAfee situation and its impact on the Rhodes-Orton main event storyline remain topics of considerable discussion among wrestling fans and industry observers heading into the show.

🔥 WWE NXT Revenge Event Coverage

WEEK ONE HIGHLIGHTS AND REVIEWS

The first night of WWE NXT Revenge has concluded, and while the show delivered solid in-ring action, reception was mixed regarding whether the event truly lived up to its "Revenge" billing. Wrestling Inc. staff breakdown of the evening highlighted both impressive performances and disappointing creative choices.

Among the praised aspects was the performance of Myles Borne, the NXT North American Champion, who demonstrated readiness to carry the midcard as the brand faces potential main roster call-ups following WrestleMania. Borne delivered a strong promo and outsmarted the faction Darkstate during his segment, displaying what one writer described as "John Cena swagger." With Tony D'Angelo settling into his NXT Championship role, Borne's elevated performance during a time of transition in NXT demonstrates that new benchmarks are being set for the brand.

A significant highlight was the debut of Kali Armstrong, who competed against Skylar Raye in a match that benefited both competitors. Despite Armstrong emerging victorious in under four minutes, both wrestlers were given substantial television time, allowing Raye to make a proper introduction to the NXT Universe while Armstrong showcased her abilities. The match lasted long enough to feel competitive rather than like a squash, making it an effective debut vehicle for Armstrong.

However, the event drew criticism for not delivering on its thematic promise. For a show called "Revenge," the card felt like any other episode of NXT, with too much focus on establishing new stories and debuts rather than delivering actual payback and retribution. While Jaida Parker did attempt revenge against Kelani Jordan, and Shiloh Hill constructed a casket that would play a role in next week's main event, the overall narrative didn't feel sufficiently revenge-focused. One month removed from Vengeance Day, the event lacked the visceral satisfaction of seeing grudges actually settled, making the name feel somewhat disconnected from the content.

CHAMPIONSHIP DEFENSES

Tony D'Angelo successfully defended his newly won NXT Championship against Ethan Page in a hard-fought battle. Page had earned the match after stealing a pin from D'Angelo the previous week in a six-man tag match against DarkState. Before the bout, Page made peace with his frenemy Ricky Saints, promising Saints the first title opportunity should Page win the championship.

During the match, Saints attempted to interfere but was intercepted under the ring by Shiloh Hill, who tied Saints' feet together and dragged him back up the ramp. D'Angelo initially used this distraction to land a back body drop on Page, but the match quickly turned into a back-and-forth battle with both competitors trading suplexes, spears, and near-fall attempts. Page showed impressive resilience, kicking out of the Forget About It and attempting his own finishing maneuvers.

The finish came when Page grabbed the championship belt and engaged in a tug of war with the referee, eventually hitting the Ego's Edge on D'Angelo, who landed on the title but kicked out. Page then set up for the Twisted Grin on the commentary desk, but D'Angelo sent him face-first into the ring barricade instead. D'Angelo concluded the match by spearing Page on the commentary desk and then hitting the Dead to Rights for the victory, retaining his championship in impressive fashion.

In the Women's Championship main event, Lola Vice successfully defended her title against Jacy Jayne. Vice had won the championship at Stand & Deliver in a triple threat also involving Kendal Grey. Jayne looked positioned to become a three-time Women's Champion on several occasions, particularly after Fatal Influence interference saw Fallon Henley drive Jayne into the ring post. However, Vice kicked out of the cover and eventually caught Jayne attempting a spinning backfist into a winning cover.

Rather than celebrate her title victory, Vice was surrounded by a mass staredown featuring multiple contenders. First, Kali Armstrong attacked all three members of Fatal Influence and climbed onto the apron. Kendal Grey appeared on the opposite side of the ring, Izzi Dame watched from the rafters, and Zaria made her entrance. Sol Ruca cut off Zaria's advance to close the show, setting up their scheduled Last Woman Standing match for Week 2.

NOTABLE MATCHES AND DEBUTS

Kelani Jordan opened the show with a hard-hitting bout against Jaida Parker, continuing their ongoing rivalry that has seen multiple confrontations over recent weeks. Jordan targeted Parker's leg throughout the match, eventually ramming it into an exposed turnbuckle. This injury work set up Jordan's signature moonsault finish for the victory.

The Speed Championship Tournament continued with EK Prosper defeating Dorian Van Dux in a fast-paced opening-round match. Both competitors displayed high-flying athleticism with multiple dives and near-fall attempts. As time wound down, Van Dux grew desperate and attempted a Shooting Star Press, but Prosper avoided it and hit an impressive moonsault with just seven seconds remaining in the three-minute time limit for the pinfall victory.

Prosper, formerly known as Eli Knight, previously competed in a triple threat that saw the now-injured Elio LeFleur win the Speed Championship in February. With LeFleur forced to vacate due to injury, Prosper advances to challenge Lexis King in the tournament finals scheduled for Week 2.

Kali Armstrong made her official NXT debut against Skylar Raye, showcasing her power-based offense. Though Armstrong dominated most of the match and won in quick fashion with her Kali Konnection finisher, Raye received meaningful offense and time to demonstrate her abilities. The Kali Konnection, a running shoulder tackle or pounce, generated mixed fan reaction, with some viewing it as too simple and basic while others praised how Raye's selling made the move look more impactful. Armstrong had used this finisher throughout her 140-day reign as WWE Evolve Women's Champion before losing the title to Kendal Grey.

JOE HENDRY AND KEANU CARVER CONFRONTATION

Joe Hendry attempted to hold a concert segment following his NXT Championship loss to Tony D'Angelo at Stand & Deliver. Hendry came to the ring questioning his future and whether he could reclaim the title. During his performance, he sang "The title was my life, it's a shame that I lost her... Can I get it back? Or will I go to the..." before being interrupted by Keanu Carver.

The pause in Hendry's lyrics sparked speculation among observers, as "lost her" rhymed with "roster," creating an apparent tease of a potential main roster call-up. This type of subtle hint is typical of WWE's storytelling approach, and the intentional stop suggested deliberate foreshadowing. Following previous call-ups like Trick Williams, rumors suggest Hendry could be called up soon, possibly alongside stars like Jacy Jayne and Sol Ruca.

Carver's interruption proved devastating. Hendry initially fought back and even broke a guitar over Carver's head, but Carver shrugged off the impact and took control of the physical altercation. As security attempted to separate the two, Carver drove Hendry through the barricade, leaving him visibly bloodied. This brutal attack sent a clear message and set up their scheduled singles match for Week 2.

WOMEN'S NORTH AMERICAN CHAMPIONSHIP STORYLINE

The Women's North American Championship feud between Tatum Paxley and Blake Monroe continues to escalate with a casket match stipulation set for Week 2. The rivalry began when Paxley successfully defended against Izzi Dame in a cage match on March 17. Following that victory, Monroe revealed herself through a trap door in the stage and dragged Paxley to the backstage area, taking possession of the championship for several weeks.

Paxley eventually reclaimed the title at Stand & Deliver in a tough match, but Monroe's pursuit continued. Last week, Monroe and Jackson Drake defeated Paxley and Shiloh Hill in a mixed tag match, with Monroe pinning the champion. The Vanity Project subsequently gifted Monroe a custom Women's North American Championship belt, further inflaming the rivalry.

On the April 14 episode, General Manager Robert Stone agreed to grant Monroe a title rematch, but Paxley stipulated she would choose the match type. Paxley, determined to end the rivalry permanently, selected a casket match and declared her intention to send Monroe and her fake title to hell. Shiloh Hill constructed a custom casket, which was displayed to confirm the match stipulation. Former WCW wrestler Crowbar commented positively on the dynamic between Paxley and Hill, noting they resemble him and the late Daffney, and expressing that he believes Daffney would have enjoyed watching their work together.

WEEK TWO CARD

NXT Week 2 of the Revenge special, airing April 21, features an ambitious lineup designed to deliver on the revenge theme with multiple high-stakes encounters.

The Women's North American Championship casket match between Paxley and Monroe headlines that night, representing the culmination of their months-long feud. Sol Ruca and Zaria will collide in a Last Woman Standing match, continuing their recent rivalry. Their conflict concludes with Ruca attacking Zaria on the stage during Week 1's closing moments.

For the men's titles, Myles Borne defends his North American Championship against Dion Lennox. The match was set after Lennox attacked Borne following Stand & Deliver when Borne defeated Johnny Gargano. The segment also highlighted tension within DarkState, as Shaquon Shugars accused Lennox of prioritizing himself over the faction's interests, creating internal conflict that may impact future storylines.

The Speed Championship finals will crown a new champion when EK Prosper faces Lexis King. Lexis defeated Starboy Hall in his first-round match to secure this spot, setting up what should be a fast-paced technical showcase given the Speed Championship's format.

Joe Hendry will seek revenge against Keanu Carver for the brutal assault and concert interruption from Week 1. The week's teased main roster call-up for Hendry could influence booking decisions for this encounter.

The card demonstrates WWE's commitment to escalating stakes and delivering on the revenge-focused theme that Week 1 was criticized for not adequately executing, with multiple feuds reaching conclusion points and new angles continuing to develop alongside established rivalries reaching boiling points.

Will Ospreay Trademarks United Empire

The United States Patent and Trademark Office continues to receive numerous filings from the world of pro wrestling. Will Ospreay has filed with the USPTO to trademark "United Empire," the name of the group he is part of from New Japan Pro Wrestling, which includes Callum Newman, HENARE and Francesco Akira, who joined Ospreay on AEW Dynamite last week, as well as Great-O-Khan.

The filing was made on April 10 and covers merchandise including shirts, sweatshirts, hooded sweatshirts, hats, bandanas, and socks. The trademark also covers entertainment services, namely wrestling exhibits and performances by a professional wrestler and entertainer, entertainment in the nature of wrestling contests, televised appearances, personal appearances, and live appearances by a professional wrestler and sports entertainer. Additionally, it includes providing online interviews featuring professional wrestlers and sports entertainers in the field of professional wrestling and sports entertainment for entertainment purposes.

Alberto El Patrón Reaches Settlement in Domestic Violence Case

New details have surfaced regarding the legal situation involving former WWE Champion Alberto El Patrón. According to multiple reports out of Mexico, El Patrón was formally charged with repeated domestic violence following his arrest on April 6. A judge reportedly found enough evidence in the case for it to move forward to trial, where he was facing a possible prison sentence ranging from one to seven years.

However, the situation has since taken a significant turn. El Patrón was ultimately released after reaching a financial settlement with his wife totaling 1.13 million pesos, which is approximately 65,000 USD. Under the terms of the agreement, he must attend rehabilitation therapy, pay damages, and have no contact with the victim moving forward. The conditions of the settlement are set to remain in place for six years, with a compliance hearing scheduled for October 29. The case will continue under judicial oversight until all requirements tied to the agreement are fully satisfied.

As of now, El Patrón has not returned to the ring since his arrest, and Mexican promotion The Crash has announced that he has been suspended indefinitely.

After spending a decade away from WWE following creative differences and his 2014 departure, CM Punk made his long-awaited return at Survivor Series 2023. His comeback marked one of professional wrestling's biggest stories, with the path back to the company being paved by an unexpected reconciliation with Triple H, the man who now oversees WWE's creative direction.

In recent appearances on All The Smoke: Unplugged, Punk opened up about what led to his decision to return. The turning point came when he decided to reach out to Triple H following the latter's cardiac event in 2021. Triple H had taken time away from WWE during the fall of 2021 after suffering a cardiac incident that ultimately led to his in-ring retirement. Shortly after, Vince McMahon stepped down from his position of power, and Triple H ended up taking over WWE's creative department in 2022.

What sparked Punk's change of heart, however, was deeply personal rather than professional. Punk explained that he had seen a clip of Hulk Hogan and the Ultimate Warrior having a conversation where they apologized and forgave each other. Tragically, the Ultimate Warrior died just a couple of days after that reconciliation was filmed. This moment profoundly shifted Punk's perspective on his long-standing issues with WWE and Triple H specifically.

"It just made me realize this s*** ain't worth it, and I don't care," Punk said, reflecting on the impact of witnessing that Hogan and Warrior moment. Recognizing that life is too short to hold onto grudges, Punk made the decision to bridge the gap with Triple H. According to Punk, they had real conversations about his potential return, moving beyond the superficial business dealings that had defined their previous relationship.

Punk expressed genuine admiration for how Triple H conducts himself outside the professional sphere. "My favourite thing is when Triple H isn't at Raw, and it's not because 'oh the boss is away' or anything like that. I know he's at a daddy/daughter dance, I know he's at his daughter's football game, and it makes me feel it's wholesome, because we've had the conversations, it's how we got to the point where I was asked 'hey what does it look like, can we get you to come back?'" This shift to seeing Triple H as a fully realized person, rather than just his adversary in the wrestling world, proved crucial to their reconciliation.

Shortly after these personal conversations, Punk received a call from Nick Khan, WWE's Chief Executive Officer, asking if he would consider returning to the company. Punk said he was initially cautious and wanted to understand what the landscape looked like now. He emphasized that WWE had changed dramatically since his departure, and that change was a prerequisite for his return. "What does that look like? Is the place different? It's so wildly different, and if it wasn't, I wouldn't be here," Punk stated.

The reunion between Punk and WWE was especially significant given his complete disconnection from wrestling during his decade away. Unlike many former wrestlers who maintain some connection to the industry, Punk had truly walked away from wrestling entirely. He admitted that once he left WWE in 2014, wrestling wasn't even on his mind. He didn't watch anything until the following year's WrestleMania, and he only tuned in to that event to watch his wife, AJ Lee, who continued to perform for WWE for about a year after his departure.

Even when major wrestling moments occurred, Punk remained unmoved. When Brock Lesnar faced The Undertaker at WrestleMania 30 to end the Undertaker's legendary 21-0 streak, Paul Heyman repeatedly called Punk trying to convince him to watch. Punk initially refused, saying he had no interest in watching Brock's match. It was only when Heyman persisted and finally revealed that Lesnar was facing The Undertaker that Punk understood the magnitude of the moment. Despite this, Punk still only watched WrestleMania 30 because his wife was competing, not because of any personal investment in wrestling itself.

During his decade away, Punk missed aspects of the wrestling life, particularly the locker room camaraderie and personal connections with wrestlers. However, he stressed that none of the matches themselves held any appeal for him. He had completely moved on from wrestling after dealing with injuries, creative frustrations, and the organizational issues that led to his departure. His return was therefore not driven by nostalgia or a lingering itch to wrestle, but rather by a fundamental shift in his perspective about holding grudges and by genuine changes in WWE's environment and leadership.

Now back in WWE, Punk has become involved in a high-stakes rivalry with Roman Reigns. According to Triple H, the tension between these two competitors is not merely a storyline construct but represents genuine, palpable animosity that extends far beyond their in-ring performances. Triple H discussed this at length ahead of WrestleMania 42, where Reigns challenges Punk for the WWE World Heavyweight Championship.

In his comments to media, Triple H broke down the rare nature of this particular feud. He explained that while WWE typically operates in the space between sport and entertainment, the Punk and Reigns rivalry crosses into both territories in a way that is exceptionally uncommon. Triple H stated, "This is one where you break down the sport and entertainment aspect of what we do. Very rarely does it crisscross into both. This one crisscrosses into both. Obviously, there's a tension when they're in the ring with each other. There is a palpable, you can cut it with a knife, feeling of intensity. That intensity does not stop backstage."

The tension that exists between Punk and Reigns creates genuine volatility within the backstage environment whenever they are in proximity to each other. Triple H emphasized that having these two in the same backstage area produces an exceptionally tense atmosphere where anything could explode into a real confrontation at any moment. "When they have to be in the vicinity of each other in backstage environments, it is on epic levels of tension. You know, where at any point in time, you will feel like this will go incredibly unprofessionally at any moment. You are just sitting there. You can smell the gas in the air and there are sparks everywhere, and you're trying to get it in the ring at WrestleMania before it blows up, before the fire ignites and it goes really, really badly," Triple H explained.

Triple H was emphatic about the authenticity of this feud, stating plainly that the animosity between Punk and Reigns represents something genuinely real in a way that most people cannot fully comprehend. He characterized the fundamental feeling between the two as one of intense mutual disrespect and negativity.

The stakes surrounding WrestleMania 42 are further elevated by the lack of backup contingencies for the main event competitors. Triple H drew a parallel to professional sports, noting that in football or basketball, teams have backup players who can step in if star athletes are injured or unavailable. However, WWE has no such luxury with its top competitors. "If you lose your quarterback, if you lose your first-string offensive lineman, if you lose your star center or star guard in the NBA, you have a second-string guy to back him up, and you hope that those second-string guys are pretty good. There is no backup Cody Rhodes. There is no backup Randy Orton, there's no backup Roman Reigns, there's no backup CM Punk," Triple H noted, referring to the four men involved in the two main events scheduled for WrestleMania 42.

The WrestleMania 42 event is scheduled to take place on April 18 and April 19 from Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas, Nevada. With Roman Reigns challenging CM Punk for the WWE World Heavyweight Championship, and the genuine tension between them creating an unpredictable and volatile situation, this match represents one of the most significant and authentic rivalries in contemporary WWE programming.

🔥 Gunther Career and Matches

Gunther has cemented his legacy as WWE's most dominant force by orchestrating the retirement of three iconic wrestlers, each representing a different era of professional wrestling. His journey as the "Career Killer" began when he faced Hall of Famer Goldberg in his WWE retirement match at Saturday Night's Main Event earlier in his career. Speaking on WWE Retrospective, Gunther reflected on that historic encounter and his mindset going into the match. He explained that when he watched Goldberg matches growing up, what always captivated him was the unpredictability and danger of the performances. "When I came up in wrestling and watched Goldberg matches, the most exciting part was always that something catastrophic could happen at any time. I think that was kind of the appeal too, it looked dangerous because it was," Gunther recalled.

Gunther revealed that Triple H approached him nearly a year before the match occurred, asking if he would be interested in wrestling Goldberg. His response was immediate and enthusiastic. "Yeah, I want to wrestle Bill Goldberg, and I want to have the best match he maybe ever had," he told WWE Retrospective. The encounter held a surreal quality for Gunther, as wrestling Goldberg had never been on his agenda, though he acknowledged the nostalgic element of having played as Goldberg in the N64 wrestling game with friends during his youth.

Gunther spoke highly of Goldberg's professionalism and the deliberate choices he made throughout their match. "He was doing so many things intentionally right that impressed me and I was like 'that's really cool'," Gunther noted. He also appreciated the setting of the match, with the return of Saturday Night's Main Event providing an old-school atmosphere that felt appropriate for such a historic encounter. The crowd response was overwhelmingly in Goldberg's favor, with the audience invested in seeing the legend secure victory. "At the end of the day it's all about big characters, that's the thing, everyone knows Goldberg. When we got to the place where it was time for him to hit his signature things, in that building, everyone was for Bill Goldberg, they wanted to see Goldberg win that night," Gunther explained.

Despite the crowd's preference for Goldberg, Gunther successfully defeated him and believes the match exceeded expectations for both men. "I think that match did a lot for me and my career, and I hope he can look back at it and be happy with the performance he put on that night, because I was very happy with that match and it definitely exceeded expectations I would say," Gunther commented. This victory marked the beginning of what would become Gunther's dominant run as a career-ending force in WWE.

Later that year, Gunther set his sights on John Cena in December 2025. Cena, who had returned to WWE and was facing his final match, competed against Gunther at Saturday Night's Main Event in Washington DC on December 13. Gunther made Cena submit to a Sleeper Hold, forcing the man known for his "never give up" philosophy to literally give up. This moment represented one of the most emotionally charged victories of Gunther's career.

Speaking about the Cena match on WWE Retrospective, Gunther noted that there was no extended storyline leading up to it due to a tournament that had taken place beforehand. However, he felt like he was wrestling someone still in his prime, even though Cena was himself critical about his physical condition. Gunther reflected on Cena's unique position in WWE history, noting that the crowd's relationship with Cena had evolved over the years. "It's funny in a wrestler's career too because I remember when Cena was just shoved down everybody's throat and everybody was like 'I don't want that guy' and then after a while you go away and once you come back, everybody wants you. That's just a natural thing in WWE, and I totally get why he had the career that he had and was always in the spot that he was."

What made the Cena match particularly special for Gunther was the authentic emotional reaction from the crowd. "That night it totally switched for me because people were legit pissed off, they were really pissed off. Not like 'we play along and boo you', they were frustrated that this is how it ended, this was the best outcome for me," Gunther explained. The significance of making Cena submit cannot be overstated, as it directly contradicted Cena's entire career philosophy. "You hear the reaction after the tap out, that's the biggest achievement too. The guy that said 'never give up', I can make him give up. That's maybe worth more worth than any title you can win. For the rest of my career I can talk about that and it's going to annoy people forever."

Gunther acknowledged that being part of WWE as an outsider had always appealed to him, given that he did not grow up as a WWE fan during the 1990s. "When I grew up in the 90s I was never a WWE guy, and now being here and fitting in, and being the guy on screen that dumps on it, it's a lot of fun to do," he said. The Cena match felt particularly heavy and tense compared to other major events, with the backstage atmosphere reflecting the emotional weight of the moment. Despite this intensity, Gunther maintained his characteristic emotional detachment, allowing him to fully absorb and enjoy the historic nature of the victory.

The third and most recent retirement came at WWE Royal Rumble 2026 when Gunther faced AJ Styles. This victory, however, held the deepest personal significance for Gunther. He explained that it was an open secret backstage that AJ Styles was planning to retire at some point, and Gunther made it his mission to be the one to end the legendary wrestler's career. Gunther forced Styles to pass out, continuing his dominant streak as the "Career Killer."

What made the AJ Styles retirement different from the previous two was the personal connection Gunther felt to Styles as a wrestler. "AJ is different because you had Goldberg, a WCW legend, and John Cena, the guy that stands for WWE. Then, you have AJ, who is somewhat more similar to me when it comes to his career because he had a great run here, but a lot of the stuff people love him for happened before WWE," Gunther explained. Styles represented a different era for Gunther, one defined by his development as a young wrestler learning his craft. "He is one of those guys where the generation that I came up with, we looked up to guys like AJ Styles. When he was in New Japan, we always watched his big matches there and they were fantastic."

Gunther emphasized that from a wrestling purist perspective, retiring AJ Styles meant more to him than retiring John Cena, despite the latter's cultural impact. "I always had so much respect for AJ's work. It almost felt, from the wrestler purist heart, it felt more important to me than the Cena match," Gunther stated. This sentiment reflects the deep respect between two generations of wrestlers who approached the craft from a similar philosophical standpoint.

The circumstances surrounding Styles' retirement also resonated with Gunther. Unlike Goldberg and Cena, whose final matches were widely anticipated and known to be their last, many fans expected AJ Styles to continue his career after facing Gunther. "A good chunk of them expected AJ to win and carry on with his career, but he went out on his own terms, that's something a lot of wrestlers can't manage, it's always best to go at a point where people are still going to miss you, not at a point where people are like 'ah it was time'," Gunther reflected. He expressed admiration for Styles' ability to retire gracefully while still maintaining the audience's affection and respect.

On screen, Gunther relished the opportunity to antagonize Styles in his final run. "But on screen being able to rub it in and try and ruin that last run he has was so much fun," he said. Despite the heel work involved in their narrative, Gunther's genuine respect for Styles as a performer remained evident. "So hat off to AJ, great career, fantastic wrestler, but I took it away, what can I do," he concluded with a mixture of respect and characteristic arrogance that defines his character.

Alternatively, Heyman's favor might be utilized to propel Gunther back toward a World Heavyweight Championship opportunity, particularly given Heyman's reported issues with both Roman Reigns and CM Punk depending on who holds the title heading into WrestleMania.

Additionally, there was a reported plan for Gunther to face Rey Mysterio at WrestleMania prior to an injury to Mysterio, with speculation that Rey's career could have been on the line in that match. This further illustrates the momentum Gunther has built as WWE's most dangerous threat to the careers of legendary wrestlers.

Gunther's rise as the "Career Killer" represents a significant chapter in WWE's current storytelling. His ability to craft compelling matches with legends while respecting their legacies has elevated both his standing and the historical importance of these retirement matches. Whether his next target will be Lesnar, Mysterio, or another major figure remains to be seen, but the pattern established with Goldberg, Cena, and Styles suggests that Gunther's next retirement match will be treated with similar gravitas and importance.

The difference between WWE and other entertainment mediums lies in the unpredictability of the human element. There is no waiting until the talent recovers to shoot the final scene in live professional wrestling. The show happens on the scheduled date regardless of circumstances, forcing WWE to be ready for anything and able to move on from any situation.
