🔥 Paul Wight AEW In-Ring Future

Paul Wight has given an in-depth update on his status and plans with AEW following his appearance on Insight with Chris Van Vliet. The legendary performer, previously known as The Big Show during his lengthy WWE tenure, signed with AEW in 2021 and has primarily served as a commentator while making limited in-ring appearances. His last televised match took place in November 2023.

Reflecting on his current health and future trajectory, Wight expressed optimism about his physical condition while maintaining a realistic perspective about what lies ahead. He acknowledged that his body has been through significant wear and tear, having undergone nearly yearly surgeries since 2018-2019, each requiring extensive rehabilitation and recovery. Despite these challenges, he stated that he is feeling very good and has made considerable progress in his physical transformation.

A central theme in Wight's remarks is his understanding that he has a limited window remaining for in-ring competition. He made clear that he harbors no delusions about returning to championship-level competition or becoming a main event draw. According to Wight, those days are definitively behind him, and he has made peace with that reality. He emphasized the importance of fairness to younger talent, noting that the newer generation does not need to see him taking up valuable television time and ring time on a regular basis.

Wight's approach to his remaining AEW contract centers on enjoyment and mentorship. He stated that there is nothing he can accomplish that would make his career any bigger or better, so his focus now is on enjoying the last years of his contract while having fun with younger talent and passing down the lessons he has learned throughout his multi-decade career. He expressed gratitude for his situation at AEW, noting that he has a good boss in Tony Khan who believes in him, supports him, and provides him the opportunity to remain involved in the business he loves.

To facilitate a potential in-ring return, Wight has been working intensively on his physical conditioning and weight management. He acknowledged that he does not need to be at his former peak weight of approximately 500 pounds. Instead, his target is to reach around 350 pounds, down from his current weight of 386 pounds. This represents a 36-pound decrease from his current state. Wight emphasized that conditioning and weight are the two things he can directly control at this stage of his career.

Wight provided candid insight into his physical limitations following multiple surgeries. With two artificial knees and two hip replacements, his proprioception and joint connectivity have fundamentally changed. He acknowledged that he will never physically be what he once was and that he must accept his current state as an athlete. However, he remains confident that there are certain things he can still do that will enhance the product while maintaining his character as a large, dangerous giant. The key for him is ensuring that whatever he does in the ring still looks credible and does not appear to be hurting the quality of programming.

A significant portion of Wight's discussion focused on retirement narratives and the concept of a planned retirement match. He expressed frustration with the current trend in wrestling where many performers seek to write their own story and engineer a carefully crafted sendoff. Wight stated bluntly that he is tired of hearing about everyone wanting to script their retirement narrative. He made clear that not everyone gets a story, and he may very well be in that category.

Wight emphasized that he may not receive a retirement match or a special farewell program, and he has genuinely come to terms with this possibility. Rather than chasing a storybook ending, his focus is on working hard and holding himself accountable to reach a physical level where he can simply compete for fun. He stressed that the only reason he wants to return to in-ring competition is because he enjoys it and wants to have fun doing it, not because he is seeking validation or a perfect conclusion to his career.

The former Big Show reflected on his lifestyle choices throughout his career, acknowledging that he did not treat his body well during his younger years. He engaged in poor eating habits and smoking, which took a toll on his long-term health. Now at 54 years old, Wight has already surpassed the lifespan of Andre the Giant, who died at 46. This reality has motivated him to take the work he is doing in the gym seriously, as he aims to live as long as possible and maintain the best quality of life he can achieve.

Beyond in-ring competition, Wight has contributed to AEW in various capacities, including commentary work and community outreach efforts. He has a clear vision of how he sees himself being utilized in the future. Rather than positioning himself as a trainer for younger talent, Wight recognizes that there are others, such as Billy Gunn, who are better suited for that role. Wight sees himself as someone who is more naturally inclined toward mentoring and coaching, particularly when it comes to working with larger wrestlers who may benefit from his decades of experience as a giant-sized performer.

Wight's realistic and grounded approach to the final chapter of his career stands in contrast to many contemporary wrestlers who pursue legacy-defining moments. He has made peace with the fact that his career may not end with a grand narrative or a specially orchestrated retirement bout. Instead, he is committed to simply enjoying the time he has remaining on his AEW contract, competing when he is physically ready to do so, and contributing his knowledge and experience to the next generation of wrestling talent. This perspective reflects both his maturity as a performer and his genuine love for the business, separate from accolades or narrative closure.

Brody King AEW Career

Brody King has quietly become one of AEW's most consistent and reliable performers over the past several years, and now he's reflecting on what he describes as an "amazing" run with the company. Speaking with Denise Salcedo, King looked back on his AEW career and highlighted how much he's accomplished since first arriving, from his time with House of Black to his current singles momentum.

"I mean, my time in AEW has been amazing … from House Of Black to where we're at now, it's just been a huge upward trajectory, and yeah, I mean having a main event title match with MJF in Australia was incredible. Countless pay-per-views. Everything has been amazing. Being AEW Tag Team Champions with Bandido has been amazing. I can't complain," King stated.

King's versatility has been a major factor in his continued presence on AEW programming. For the past four years, he has been one of AEW's most intriguing characters, with his intimidating persona and consistent in-ring work making him a reliable performer on the brand. Whether working in a faction, a tag team, or now as a singles competitor, he believes the transitions between roles have come naturally.

"I feel like I'm able to adapt with what I have and use certain pieces of what was given to me with the House Of Black and use that going forward with the Hounds Of Hell and then going into what we're doing with Brodido and now what I'm doing as a singles wrestler," King explained. According to King, that adaptability has allowed him to stay active without missing a step or needing time away to reset his character. "I feel like it's all very seamless and I didn't have to take any time off. I didn't have to think about what was next."

Despite his current focus on singles competition, King's efforts have continued to pay off as he's been enjoying a singles run this year. However, he made it clear that his tag team with Bandido, known as Brodido, is far from finished. King also credited House of Black as a key turning point in his AEW career, explaining that the group helped introduce him to a broader audience who may not have been familiar with his previous work in NJPW and ROH.

King's comments highlight the importance of adaptability in modern professional wrestling. In a landscape where talent frequently shifts between roles and storylines, the ability to evolve without losing momentum is what separates reliable roster members from breakout stars. His steady rise suggests that AEW views him as a long-term piece of the company's future.

MJF and Speedball Mike Bailey AEW Dynamite Match

Speedball Mike Bailey has shared his thoughts on his recent encounter with AEW World Champion MJF on Wednesday's edition of AEW Dynamite. Bailey came to the defense of Kenny Omega earlier in the show before facing off against MJF in the main event.

During his latest vlog, Bailey reflected on the match and his feelings toward the AEW World Champion. He began by acknowledging that he had three days to process the encounter, noting that the Wednesday show had now carried into Saturday. Bailey made it clear that he wanted to give MJF proper credit for his abilities as a performer.

"I want to give MJF his due. I want to say that he's a great athlete, a great pro wrestler, but I can't even do that," Bailey explained. He drew a comparison to his previous comments about Kyle and other members of the Don Callis family, noting that while those wrestlers are talented, Don Callis is the problematic element. With MJF, however, Bailey stated that the problem lies with MJF himself.

"He is a truly despicable human being. He would be a great pro wrestler, if he didn't do the things he did, but he does, so he isn't," Bailey said bluntly. Bailey also addressed MJF's recent comments about Kenny Omega, calling them "quite frankly unforgivable." He expressed his hope that Omega would defeat MJF at the upcoming Dynasty event.

Despite the loss in his match, Bailey expressed considerable pride in his performance and overall trajectory. "I'm very proud of what happened. I'm extremely proud of my performance," he stated. Bailey highlighted his continued improvement over his more than one year in AEW, noting his accomplishment of becoming a two-time Trios Champion and challenging for the World Championship once. He also mentioned that he has challenged for numerous other championships and believes he is steadily moving closer to achieving his ultimate goal.

MJF on Bryan Danielson AEW Return

MJF Believes Bryan Danielson Will Wrestle In AEW Again: 'He's A Sicko'

Bryan Danielson's injuries may have put a halt to his pro wrestling career, but his former opponent in AEW, MJF, thinks that his passion for the business will make him wrestle again.

MJF, who has begrudgingly praised Danielson in the past, once again spoke glowingly about him during his appearance on Games With Names. "Bryan Danielson is considered the greatest bell-to-bell professional wrestler of all time by a lot of people. He's on that Mount Rushmore of that specific thing — not the overall package, but that specific thing," MJF began. "He is one of the greatest technical, like, in-ring technicians of all time. He is a freak athlete. He's tough as nails and he really put me through it that night. I mean incredible. And again, 42, is the typical age for most top guys in my sport, honestly."

The AEW World Champion, uncharacteristically, went on to further praise his former rival, comparing his legend to that of Tom Brady. He also did not rule out a potential return to the ring for Danielson. "That's like a Tom Brady level. I would say he's Tom Brady. Like, he's definitely GOAT status. He's like soft retired right now, and I don't know if he'll ever come out of retirement. He's a sicko, so he probably will because he can never get enough, much like Brady," MJF added.

While he was full of praise for Danielson, MJF was not pleased that the former WWE star thought he couldn't go toe-to-toe with him in the ring for an hour, something he wanted to prove him wrong about in their clash at AEW Revolution. Danielson last wrestled on AEW television in 2024, with his final match coming against Jon Moxley. However, he also made a surprise return to the ring later that year in a dark match on AEW Collision. A few months after the match, Danielson clarified that his desire to return to the ring was a "delusion" due to the seriousness of his injuries.

Renee Paquette Favorite AEW Moment

Renee Paquette Names Jon Moxley Spot As Favorite Moment In AEW

Renee Paquette has revealed that her favorite moment in AEW to date is very much related to her work with Jon Moxley. Married since 2017, Paquette and Moxley are currently both signed to AEW, with the former being a backstage interviewer and her husband as the leader of the Death Riders.

During a recent episode of AEW Who Is?, Paquette revealed that her favorite moment in the company has been with Moxley as Continental Champion, providing her notes during his entrance for the first time. Also noting that she has seen him fall back in love with wrestling during their time together, Paquette shared her thoughts on what makes this opportunity so special.

"I sort of feel like my favorite moment is kind of new. Because I do those little hits during the Continental Championship matches, with Jon being the Continental Champion, I love getting to go out for his entrances. I like getting to do those anyways, just to give a little piece of information, I feel like I'm just doing more, and I like doing them. But the fact that I get to do them with Jon now is really cool," Paquette explained.

She continued by reflecting on watching Moxley's journey in the wrestling industry and how it has changed her own perspective. "I also just feel like in the last couple years, just being able to watch Jon and watch him fall back in love with wrestling, and understand wrestling from his point of view is really cool. Then for me to get to go out there and be there for his entrance and hear the crowd reacting to him, to even having just a little moment of him passing through the shot while I'm speaking to camera, giving a note on something about him going into the match or his opponent."

Paquette emphasized the significance of the first time they performed this task together. "I have multiples of those, but I do think the very first time we got to do one of those, I was like, whoa, it was really cool. I get to watch him all the time, but it's usually from a monitor, it's from a different perspective. To get to be right there at ringside as he's coming up over the barricade, or out where he's about to walk into the crowd, is really cool."

The couple's professional journey together spans several years. Paquette and Moxley first started dating when they were both signed to WWE, known at the time as Renee Young and Dean Ambrose. Getting married in April 2017, they would welcome their daughter into their lives on June 15, 2021, naming her Nora Murphy Good.

From being Renee Young in WWE to going by her real name in AEW, Paquette has brought her voice to every role she occupied, be it backstage interviewer, commentary partner or analyst. Even with the scripted nature of some backstage interactions, Paquette has always had a fine grasp of the emotion of the situation, allowing her voice to search for the answers that the fans may be having, regardless of who she happens to be interacting with.

Her time in WWE saw her take up the commentary role on NXT, with the then-Renee Young going on to be the first woman to call a whole episode of Raw back in August 2018, securing herself a spot on the main roster team. WWE Backstage would give Paquette another string to her bow alongside Booker T as they discussed the stories across the main brands, wrapping around a number of recurring segments that saw popularity among its viewership.

Paquette departed WWE in August 2020, heading to AEW in October 2022, with her time between roles seeing the creation of the Oral Sessions interview podcast. In AEW, Paquette serves as a backstage interviewer and pre-show panelist, bringing the same vocal heart to proceedings in the Tony Khan-led company.

Will Ospreay AEW and NJPW G1 Climax

Will Ospreay Hopes To Compete In NJPW G1 Climax Again

Top AEW star Will Ospreay recently spoke with Tokyo Sports about various topics, including his aspirations to arrange time away from his current promotion to participate in New Japan Pro Wrestling's prestigious G1 Climax tournament.

During the interview, Ospreay addressed the logistical challenges of pursuing this goal. He acknowledged that requesting approximately six weeks off from AEW would present a significant hurdle, given his commitment to the American promotion. However, he revealed that he has already discussed the possibility with his fiancee, indicating serious consideration of the matter.

Ospreay expressed his competitive drive regarding the tournament, stating he would like to compete in the G1 Climax again someday. He emphasized that he has never won the tournament, which fuels his desire to challenge himself and ultimately claim the title. The opportunity to test himself against the world's best junior and heavyweight competitors remains an important goal for the accomplished wrestler.

Beyond the G1 Climax, Ospreay also discussed his interest in pursuing NJPW's top prize. He outlined his desire to win the revived fourth-generation IWGP Heavyweight Championship. Ospreay highlighted the historical significance of the title by noting the legendary champions who have held it, including Hiroshi Tanahashi, Kazuchika Okada, AJ Styles, and Kenny Omega. Each of these wrestlers holds special significance for Ospreay, as he has admired them throughout his career, making the prospect of following in their footsteps particularly meaningful.
