Carmella Comments on AEW's Use of Code of Silence

Carmella has weighed in on AEW's use of a submission hold that bears striking similarity to her former signature finishing move, and she's made it clear she's not happy about it.

The incident occurred during the April 25 episode of AEW Collision, when B3CCA faced off against Kris Statlander. During the match, B3CCA applied a head vice submission hold against Statlander that closely resembled Carmella's Code of Silence, the submission move she regularly used during her time in WWE.

When clips of the match circulated on social media, fans quickly brought the footage to Carmella's attention by tagging her and making direct comparisons to her well-known finisher. The former WWE star didn't take long to respond, taking to Twitter to express her displeasure with what she witnessed.

Carmella's response made a critical distinction in her mind regarding the use of similar moves. "Hmmm.. there's a difference between homage and theft," she wrote, directly suggesting that B3CCA's use of the move crossed the line from being a simple tribute or homage into outright copying. The comment sparked immediate discussion among wrestling fans about originality and respect for signature maneuvers across different promotions.

But Carmella wasn't finished with her statement. She followed up with a second message that addressed her wrestling status and potentially hinted at future plans. "I didn't retire, I had two babies… might have to come back and show these bitches how it's really done," she wrote, suggesting she may not be completely done competing in professional wrestling.

The comments are particularly notable given that Carmella hasn't competed in a professional wrestling match since March 2023. Her mention of having two babies clarifies that she's been focusing on her personal life and motherhood rather than formal retirement from the sport.

B3CCA occasionally appears on AEW programming, while similar moves are frequently shared and adapted across different wrestling promotions. However, Carmella's public accusation has now intensified the broader conversation about intellectual property, originality, and the respect wrestlers should have for one another's signature maneuvers. The situation raises questions about whether certain moves should be considered exclusive to their originators or whether they fall into the category of standard professional wrestling vocabulary that any competitor can utilize.

WWE/AEW Talent Interaction

Released WWE Stars Potentially Heading to AEW

On April 24, WWE released 24 wrestlers in the latest round of talent cuts from the main roster and NXT. From established names to up-and-coming stars, there are a variety of talents that will now be available and could make the jump to All Elite Wrestling when their non-compete clause expires. These released WWE stars could add a great deal to the AEW roster and add further depth to the talent that appears on Ring of Honor as well.

Santos Escobar was one of the notable releases. Back in October 2025, his WWE contract expired, but the company decided to re-sign him just one day later with reports stating that he was in line for big plans. Escobar would make his next appearance in Lucha Libre AAA where he was being positioned in a feud with El Hijo de Dr Wagner Jr. While he had a prominent spot in AAA, Escobar got injured and was set to be sidelined before being released from WWE in this round of talent cuts. Escobar being available could see him join forces with his longtime friend RUSH in LFI on AEW TV when he is healthy and ready to step back in the squared circle.

Another injured star who was released by WWE is Zoey Stark. Suffering a serious knee injury in 2025, Stark had been sidelined for over eight months and looked to be getting ready for her return. However, Stark is now gone from WWE and will look for a new lease on her career outside of the company. Based on her experience and ability inside the ring, Stark feels like someone who can offer a lot to both the AEW and ROH women's divisions if she signs with Tony Khan's promotion.

Tyra Mae Steele represents a different type of prospect. Following her Olympic Gold Medal win in the 2021 Olympics, Steele made the transition to pro wrestling and signed with WWE. After training at the WWE Performance Center, Steele became one of the breakout stars and eventual Season One winner of LFG. This gave Steele an NXT contract, but she wound up becoming a mainstay on the WWE Evolve brand instead. After being released from WWE, Steele could continue her passion in wrestling by signing with AEW where she would be an ideal fit in Ring of Honor at this stage of her career.

Joe Gacy brings main roster experience and championship credentials to the free agent market. Before signing with WWE, Gacy was known as one of the most prominent stars on the independent scene. Signing with the company in 2020, Gacy first made a name for himself on WWE NXT before being called up to the main roster in 2024. Joining forces with Uncle Howdy in The Wyatt Sicks, Gacy formed a duo with Dexter Lumis that went on to win the WWE Tag Team Championship in 2025. Despite his accolades and success, Gacy is now gone from WWE and could be an excellent pickup for All Elite Wrestling where he has some supporters in the form of his independent peers like MJF.

Nikki Cross has long been one of the best character workers on the entire WWE roster. Whether it be as a member of SAnitY or as Nikki ASH, Cross has made anything given to her from the company work to her favor. In her role with The Wyatt Sicks, Cross has unfortunately not competed in the ring on WWE TV since September 2024. Cross is someone who could help to bolster the AEW roster and offer her years of experience to younger stars in the women's division right now.

Alba Fyre, also known as Kay Lee Ray, was also among those released. Many fans were surprised to hear Alba Fyre part of this latest round of WWE releases due to her consistent role alongside Chelsea Green on Friday Night SmackDown. Since signing with WWE, Fyre has accomplished a lot, including a record-breaking reign as NXT UK Women's Champion. With a reign as WWE Women's Tag Team Champion alongside Isla Dawn as well, it always felt like the company hadn't even scratched the surface of what Fyre could offer to the women's scene. Now free to become her true self again, Fyre will have a ton of advocates on the AEW roster to bring her in where she could excel against competitors in the women's division.

Cross-Promotional Friendships on Display

While WWE and AEW remain competitors, the lines between the promotions can blur when it comes to personal relationships among wrestlers. Former AEW women's champion Kris Statlander shared photos on Instagram from a poolside bikini photoshoot, captioned "dad making sure you don't dive." The post drew comments from several wrestlers, including AEW's Harley Cameron who wrote "DADDY CALL ME BACK" in response to the photos. WWE star Bayley also commented on the images with the message "Dove in da deep end help," demonstrating the friendly interactions that exist between talent across both major promotions despite their corporate rivalry.
