🔥 MJF Burberry Scarf and Bryan Danielson

AEW World Champion MJF recently sat down for an in-depth interview on the Games With Names podcast hosted by three-time NFL Super Bowl Champion Julian Edelman, covering multiple aspects of his career, character presentation, and creative process.

During the conversation, MJF was asked to provide an NFL comparison for Bryan Danielson, and he immediately drew parallels to Tom Brady. MJF stated that Danielson is GOAT status and compared his retirement to Brady's "soft retirement" approach. According to MJF, Danielson is currently in a soft-retired state, though MJF expressed uncertainty about how long that will last. He explained his reasoning by characterizing Danielson as "a sicko," suggesting that because of this personality trait, Danielson will likely return to in-ring competition because he simply cannot get enough of wrestling, much like Brady with football. MJF emphasized that if wrestling fans are asked to name one of the greatest wrestlers bell-to-bell of all time, they would answer "Dragon," referring to Danielson's wrestling persona. Danielson has not wrestled a full-length match since October 2024, though he has made occasional run-ins and gotten physical, including delivering a Busaiku Knee to Wheeler Yuta during the main event of AEW All In Texas. In late 2025, Danielson shared that he was struggling with travel for his AEW commentary duties due to neck pain.

The discussion also touched on MJF's trademark Burberry scarf, a consistent element of his presentation both on-screen and off-screen. When explaining his relationship with the luxury brand, MJF revealed a surprising business perspective. He stated that he does not want Burberry knowing he exists because he makes a significant amount of money wearing the scarf. According to MJF, the brand is either going to love or hate his association with the product, which is why he has deliberately stayed away from attempting any official relationship with Burberry. This approach allows him to maintain a profit from wearing the scarf while avoiding potential brand complications.

Beyond these topics, MJF also discussed Seth Rollins, describing him as one of the top guys in the industry and calling him an "incredible professional wrestler." He praised Rollins for being "the man" and noted that Rollins is also excelling as a great football analyst.

MJF further pulled back the curtain on his creative process for constructing promos, revealing a highly calculated and methodical approach. He explained that he isolates himself for hours at a time, deliberately coming up with harsh things to say about his opponents. Rather than relying on improvisation, MJF maintains a Notes app on his phone that serves as what he calls "a cornucopia of just ruthlessly unhinged, mean things to say." His insult collection is extensive and often wrestler-specific, with entire sections dedicated to individual competitors. For example, he shared one line from his notes: "You want a comeback? If you want my comeback, you'll have to go scrape it off your mother's teeth." MJF acknowledged that his material is highly targeted, with some insults tailored so specifically to individual wrestlers that the general audience might not fully appreciate the personal nature of the attack.

When discussing the aftermath of delivering these cutting promos, MJF noted that while things may appear fine publicly, he often receives feedback backstage that his words have crossed a line with the wrestlers he targeted. However, rather than toning down his approach, MJF indicated that he does not care about these complaints and continues to escalate, stating: "And then they go to the back and pretend everything's fine. But later on, my boss will walk up to me and be like, 'Hey, you kind of hurt that guy's feelings.' And I'm like, 'Hey, I kind of don't give a s***.' And then it gets worse. So… that's wrestling."

MJF emphasized that his promo work is not off-the-cuff trash talk but rather a calculated, personal assault designed to maximize impact every time he grabs a microphone. This deliberate approach to mic work is a significant factor in establishing him as one of wrestling's most effective talkers.

Looking ahead, MJF will be defending the AEW World Championship at the AEW Dynasty pay-per-view on April 12th against Kenny Omega.

🔥 AEW Don Callis Family Addition

The Don Callis Family continues its expansion with another major addition from AEW's promotional partner CMLL. In a video announcement released late Monday, AEW star Rocky Romero confirmed that Volador Jr., a legendary CMLL luchador, has officially joined the controversial faction. The announcement came at a crucial time, as Volador Jr. is set to compete immediately in a high-stakes championship match alongside his new stablemates.

Volador Jr. represents a significant acquisition for the Don Callis Family. The 45-year-old veteran has been among the top luchadors in CMLL since the early 2000s and boasts an extensive resume that includes notable feuds with Mistico, Ultimo Guerrero, and fellow Callis Family member Andrade El Idolo, who famously unmasked Volador at CMLL's 80th Aniversario in 2014. Beyond his CMLL work, Volador Jr. has made multiple appearances across major promotions including TNA, MLW, New Japan Pro Wrestling, and AEW. His NJPW experience includes several appearances in both the Best of the Super Juniors and Super Junior Tag League tournaments, establishing him as a wrestler of considerable international standing. He made his AEW debut in 2024 and has made five appearances for the promotion since the beginning of the AEW/CMLL partnership.

The timing of Volador Jr.'s recruitment could not be more significant. This Friday night at Arena Mexico, Volador Jr. will team with Callis Family members Hechicero and El Clon to challenge for the AEW World Trios Championships. Their opponents will be Mistico, "Speedball" Mike Bailey, and "The Jet" Kevin Knight, who became the new champions when they defeated Kazuchika Okada, Kyle Fletcher, and Mark Davis at AEW Revolution. This match marks the second title defense for the new champions and represents the first time in the short history of the AEW Trios Titles that the championships have been defended on a non-AEW event. The Arena Mexico main event represents a significant platform for Volador Jr.'s debut with the Don Callis Family.

The Don Callis Family's expansion comes as the faction has dealt with recent setbacks. Before Volador Jr.'s addition, the stable featured ten active members, not counting injured wrestlers such as Josh Alexander, Wardlow, Brian Cage, and Jake Doyle. The group was recently dealt another blow when Josh Alexander was announced to require knee surgery and extended time away. Reports also suggest that Kyle Fletcher may be dealing with an injury following his match on AEW Collision. Despite these challenges, the addition of Volador Jr. strengthens the faction's international presence and provides additional depth to their roster.

The Arena Mexico card on April 3 will feature additional AEW talent, including Thunder Rosa, who will team with Marcela and India Sioux in a six-woman tag match against Persephone, Princesa Sugehit, and Olympia. The event continues to showcase the deepening partnership between AEW and CMLL, highlighted further by Mistico's recent dual contract announcement with both AEW and CMLL following his victory at Revolution 2026. This cross-promotional collaboration reflects the growing importance of international lucha libre talent to AEW's roster development and storytelling.

Chris Jericho Match Quality and Longevity Concerns

Chris Jericho Says Match Quality Is Overemphasized, Worries About Wrestler Longevity

In today's pro wrestling landscape, many fans and wrestlers hold different opinions on the value of "work-rate" compared to storytelling and psychology. AEW star Chris Jericho is of the belief that many wrestlers have lost sight of what's really important, as revealed in an interview with GamesHub.

"Match quality has become so overemphasized," Jericho said. "Wrestling is about connecting with the crowd, capturing the imagination and the interest of the people watching."

Jericho then reflected on his WWE WrestleMania 18 match against Shawn Michaels, which he previously referred to as one of his favorite storylines. He pointed out that this match had everything he just listed, but noted that they got there because of a great storyline. According to Jericho, many of today's highest-rated matches don't get the same kind of engagement from the live audience.

"A five-star match should mean the crowd is going crazy in a sold-out stadium," Jericho stated. "You can also have a five-star match in a community center in front of 50 people, so the rating in isolation is irrelevant. It's character, its storyline, it's charisma. That's what wrestling is about — it was in the 1930s and it is now."

Jericho believes that a given star's success in pro wrestling should depend on their connection with the audience, with the AEW star citing The Ultimate Warrior as an example of someone who didn't have "great matches" but built a relationship with fans nonetheless.

At 55 years old, Jericho acknowledged his limited time left in the industry. However, looking at the current state of the industry, he isn't too sure if today's wrestlers will reach the same point of their careers. "You can already see what serious injuries do – a bad neck surgery changes everything," Jericho warned. "The longevity may be harder to sustain when there's so much emphasis on the physical at the expense of charisma and character."

Aubrey Edwards AEW Contract Update

Aubrey Edwards' AEW Future Revealed Following Contract Update

Aubrey Edwards' status with AEW has been clarified following recent developments regarding the company's podcast lineup. AEW has ended the AEW Unrestricted podcast, which was co-hosted by referee Aubrey Edwards and Will Washington. However, the termination of the show does not signal any concerns about Edwards' future with the promotion.

According to recent reports, Edwards remains "firmly" under contract with AEW. Sources within the company have confirmed that AEW had plans to sign Edwards to a new deal some time ago. While her current contract remains in effect, the company's intentions are clear: AEW aims to keep Edwards with the organization long-term.

The exact expiration date of Edwards' current contract has not been disclosed publicly at this time. However, as additional information becomes available regarding her contractual status and future with the company, updates will be provided.

Edwards has maintained a significant presence within AEW since joining the company in September 2019. Her role with the promotion extends beyond her work as a referee. Edwards has also served as a co-host of the AEW Unrestricted podcast alongside Will Washington. Additionally, she has worked behind the scenes in AEW's video game department, leveraging her professional experience as a producer in the video game industry.
