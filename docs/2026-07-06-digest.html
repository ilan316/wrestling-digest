Will Ospreay AEW Reflection

Will Ospreay Reflects on His Most Memorable AEW Moments

Following a successful run in NJPW, Will Ospreay officially became "All Elite" in February 2024, with his full-time AEW stint yielding several memorable moments so far. In Ospreay's eyes, two specifically stand above the rest.

Ospreay identified his critically acclaimed match against Brian Danielson at the 2024 AEW Dynasty event as one of his most significant moments in the company. The encounter took place before a lively crowd in St. Louis. "When I wrestled Brian Danielson at Dynasty, I couldn't even imagine that match was going to happen because he got medically disqualified. I genuinely thought I'd never wrestle him, so when he showed up to AEW, I was like, 'Aha, there's a chance,'" Ospreay told "KRON 4." "The Aerial Assassin" ultimately conquered Danielson, courtesy of a Hidden Blade and a pinfall, after 32 minutes of in-ring action.

The second standout moment for Ospreay came at AEW All In: London, where he wowed fans with an Assassin's Creed-themed entrance. "The other one. We had a collaboration deal with Assassin's Creed and I got to do an entrance that featured Ezio, who was the main character for the Assassin's Creed franchise for so long. He got to do a voiceover for me. I essentially got inducted into the brotherhood. That wouldn't happen without AEW. Assassin's Creed was a way to portray the character that I wanted to do in wrestling. So then to finally have them pay me money to do an entrance, this is a dream come true," Ospreay explained.

Four months after Dynasty, Ospreay arrived to All In: London with an elaborate "Assassin's Creed"-themed entrance filled with footage from the "Assassin's Creed: Shadows" video game and a voiceover from Roger Craig Smith, the man behind the Ezio Auditore character. Ospreay went on to defeat MJF at the event, and in the process, regain the AEW International Championship, formerly known as the AEW American Championship.

Beyond his recent AEW highlights, Ospreay has also reflected on his career-defining moment from a decade prior. It's been 10 years since Will Ospreay and Ricochet wrestled in the 2016 Best of Super Juniors tournament. The match was immediately met with resounding praise, becoming something of a sea change in modern professional wrestling. While many lauded the match, it had plenty of detractors as well.

"Watching it back from my perspective...There is things about the match, that I would've gone 'Maybe we could've [taken] a little longer there. We were both young," Ospreay said on a recent episode of "Talk Is Jericho." The former IWPW Heavyweight Champion understands critiques about the match. "We would just do things, and we had that energy, and that ability to like move on to the next thing, but now looking back on it, there are things I would liked changed. But like, I wouldn't change the match, because it became such a conversation topic."

Ospreay credits the match with exposing him to a much larger audience. "That was my first break," Ospreay said. "Until that point, I did one PWG. I did a lot of the British Indies...but I'd never really stretched my wings."

After that match, Ospreay and Ricochet essentially went their own ways, as Ricochet signed with WWE in 2018, while Ospreay stayed with NJPW until becoming a member of the AEW roster in 2023. Ricochet was released from WWE around that time, and now both men find themselves in All Elite Wrestling, where Ricochet is a former AEW National Champion, and Ospreay is the winner of the 2026 Men's Owen Hart Cup, earning a shot at the AEW World Championship in Wembley Stadium at All In.

Jake Roberts AEW Departure

Jake Roberts' time with AEW has come to an end, but the WWE Hall of Famer is departing on positive terms. Roberts confirmed his departure from Tony Khan's company after spending six years with the promotion since its 2020 launch. Despite his limited presence on television in recent times, Roberts expressed nothing but gratitude for his experience with the organization.

Speaking with Bill Apter, Roberts made clear that his employment with AEW has officially concluded. He was complimentary of Khan and the company's direction, calling the AEW owner "awesome" and describing him as "a breath of fresh air." Roberts' comments suggest an amicable separation between both parties, with no lingering resentment on his part.

Roberts became best known in AEW for his partnership with Lance Archer, providing veteran leadership and experience to the roster. The pairing gave the company another seasoned voice on programming during his more active years with the promotion. However, as Archer's television appearances decreased, Roberts similarly faded from regular on-screen action. Many observers speculated that Roberts may have been contributing behind the scenes during his less visible period.

The veteran had apparently renewed his AEW contract as recently as May 2024, according to reports at that time. That deal is no longer active as of his recent confirmation. While Roberts did not disclose the specific timing of his departure or the reasons behind the split, he made one point abundantly clear: there is no animosity between him and the promotion.

Roberts' six-year chapter with AEW now closes on what appears to be mutual understanding. His journey with the company represented another phase in the legendary career of one of professional wrestling's most iconic figures. What comes next for the Hall of Famer remains to be seen, but his positive words about his time in AEW suggest the door remains open for any future interaction between Roberts and Khan's organization.
