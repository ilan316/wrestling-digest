🔥 Chris Jericho AEW Return and Name Change

Chris Jericho returned to AEW television on April 1 in his hometown of Winnipeg after nearly a year away from the promotion. Upon his return, fans immediately noticed a subtle but significant change: he was being referred to simply as "Jericho" rather than using his full name "Chris Jericho." This change appeared across commentary, graphics, and ring announcements, confirming a deliberate creative decision for his new run.

In an interview with Ringer Wrestling, Jericho explained the inspiration and philosophy behind his stripped-down presentation. He revealed that the concept was directly inspired by legendary self-titled albums from iconic bands. "I like the idea of what I'm doing now with Jericho," he said. "And by not using that first name a lot, it created this whole intrigue online like, 'What's he doing? What's the idea? What's the reason?' Honestly, it's like a self-titled album. That's where I got the idea from."

Rather than creating another nickname or character gimmick, Jericho wanted to lean entirely on the legacy and recognition already attached to his name after decades in the wrestling business. "I could create a dozen other nicknames, but how many more do I need? Everyone knows who Jericho is. And when you hear that name, then you can remember whatever memories you have on your own," he explained. He drew specific comparisons to some of music's most recognizable albums: "So it really is like Metallica, 'The Black Album.' It doesn't need to be called anything. You know who Metallica is, you know what's going on. The Beatles, 'The White Album,' same thing. That's kind of what the idea is for the 'Jericho' era."

When asked about his favorite period of his career, Jericho stated that he always considers his current era to be his favorite. "Whenever I get asked that, I always say the current version, because if I didn't, then I'd be like, 'What am I doing?'" This philosophy reflects his approach to reinvention throughout his legendary career—rather than constantly looking backward, he focuses on what he is actively creating in the present moment.

The return itself came amid intense speculation about Jericho's future in professional wrestling. Prior to his AEW return, persistent rumors suggested that Jericho might be heading to WWE, particularly with the timing around WrestleMania 42 and his contract with AEW having expired. Jericho's return in Winnipeg answered the question of where he would sign, but it did not fully satisfy fan curiosity about whether he had entertained conversations with other promotions.

When directly asked whether he had spoken with WWE prior to his AEW return, Jericho notably stopped short of giving a definitive answer. "I never said the rumors became fact. I said that the rumors became fact to the fans," he stated. When pressed further on whether he had conversations with other places, Jericho replied: "I'll leave that for another day. I like the idea of the intrigue."

Jericho addressed the broader phenomenon of online speculation and how it shapes fan perception in the modern wrestling landscape. "The interesting thing about fans and having a career where you can watch anything online, is that fans in this day and age form their own opinions, and it becomes law because the opinions spread," he explained. "Whatever people believed or didn't believe, I wasn't ever a part of any of it."

He also commented on the tribal nature of contemporary wrestling fandom and how his decision, whichever way it went, was guaranteed to generate both positive and negative reactions. "I think the best part was there was so much speculation of what is he going to do, that either way, with the tribalism of wrestling nowadays, there would be people happy about it and not happy about it, and this is the worst thing ever and this is the best thing ever."

Rather than focusing on negativity surrounding creative decisions or career moves, Jericho emphasized his approach to dealing with criticism. "I've never really focused on that side of like, 'This is terrible and it's never going to work,' because to me, anything can work and you always want to do your best to make it work. And I focus on the people who are excited about things and the people who want to be entertained and want to be happy and want to have fun. That's kind of the crowd that I focus on."

Jericho's immediate post-return booking included a appearance at Double or Nothing, where he competed in a Stadium Stampede match. The match pitted Jericho alongside Bobby Lashley, Shelton Benjamin, The Young Bucks, Jack Perry, and Kenny Omega against a team comprised of Ricochet, The Gates of Agony, Mark Davis, Andrade El Idolo, Clark Connors, and David Finlay.

During his interview, Jericho also reflected on his underrated character work throughout his career. He specifically highlighted his time as "The Wizard" in AEW as an underappreciated era. "When you're going through underrated Jericho eras, I think the Wizard was pretty cool, when I started throwing fireballs, that was something nobody had seen for years in pro wrestling," he said. He noted an interesting detail: when performing in Las Vegas, he had to obtain an actual magician's license from the Nevada commission in order to legally throw fireballs during his matches. "I had to get a magician's license for real to throw a fireball because that's the rules the commission has, which I still renew every year and carry with me at all times. So yes, I am a licensed magician in Nevada."

Jericho has remained one of AEW's most recognizable and integral stars since the company's launch in 2019, having been crowned the inaugural AEW World Champion and playing a crucial role in establishing the promotion during its early years. With his re-signing following his April return, it appears that AEW will be where Jericho concludes his in-ring career when the time comes. While a full-time WWE return seems unlikely at this stage of his career, industry observers expect that Jericho will eventually return to WWE for a Hall of Fame induction once his in-ring career concludes.

AEW World Champion Darby Allin

Darby Allin's World Championship tenure has taken on a distinctly unique character within AEW's landscape. One wrestling columnist broke down what has been dubbed "The Darby Allin one month title reign plan," a formula involving consistent title defenses across Dynamite, Collision, and pay-per-view events on a weekly basis. This approach has fundamentally shifted how the world championship is presented, making it feel like the most important prize in the company is constantly on the line and in the forefront of everyone's minds.

The columnist noted that while Darby hasn't formally instituted an open challenge gimmick, the spirit of the concept is certainly present. Competitors from all levels of the card have stepped forward to challenge for the title, and every broadcast since Allin captured the championship has centered around the world title being a focal point of the programming. This differs from traditional world championship reigns that typically build toward one major challenger over an extended period.

Analysis of this rapid-title-defense strategy suggests it may be building toward a significant title change. The columnist initially believed the upcoming hair-on-a-pole stipulation match between Darby and MJF at Double or Nothing was necessitated by MJF's real-life movie commitments requiring a haircut, but extended observation of Allin's title reign has shifted that perspective. The more this championship run continues with its relentless pace of defenses, the more it appears designed as a speed-run version of a world title reign, potentially setting up MJF to capture the championship at the next major pay-per-view.

Beyond the main narrative of his title reign, Darby Allin recently demonstrated his commitment to wrestling's grassroots foundations by making a surprise appearance at One Fall Wrestling's television taping in McDonough, Georgia. Fresh off a successful championship defense against Sammy Guevara on Collision, Allin traveled to the independent wrestling show to interact with his protégé Kiran Grey and address the importance of independent wrestling promotions.

The segment began with Grey standing tall over multiple opponents and calling for fresh challengers. When Allin's music hit and the lights dimmed, the crowd erupted with excitement at the unexpected arrival of their AEW World Champion. The angle quickly became physical, with Allin dodging a chair shot attempt from Grey before delivering a series of punches and his signature finishing moves, including the Scorpion Death Drop and Coffin Drop, to leave his protégé laid out in the ring.

After the physical confrontation concluded, Allin addressed the gathered fans and spoke passionately about the significance of independent wrestling. He explained that he had just arrived by plane from the previous night's Collision taping but made it a priority to visit the promotion because independent wrestling is where the business truly originates. His message emphasized the importance of supporting independent wrestlers and grassroots promotions like 1FW, which is owned by AEW executive and performer QT Marshall.

Allin encouraged the audience to follow the careers of independent wrestlers they support, noting that success in this industry often begins on smaller stages and builds from there. This appearance underscored Allin's philosophy about wrestling's developmental pipeline and his personal investment in supporting the next generation of talent coming through independent circuits.

Looking forward, Allin faces another significant title defense challenge. He is scheduled to defend the AEW World Championship against Mike Bailey in the main event of a special three-hour combined episode of AEW Dynamite and AEW Collision airing this Wednesday. This defense continues the pattern of frequent title matches that has characterized his reign and maintains the emphasis on the world championship as the centerpiece of AEW programming.

Sting's Son Garrett Borden Debut

Garrett Borden, the eldest son of WWE Hall of Famer Sting, officially made his professional wrestling debut over the weekend and picked up a victory at the Final Reckoning show promoted by the Rhodes Wrestling Association in Leander, Texas. Borden defeated Brandon Barretta in singles competition, incorporating several nods to his father's legendary career during the match. At one point, he connected with Sting's trademark Stinger Splash in the corner before finishing Barretta with an Angle Slam transitioned into a neckbreaker for the win.

The debut marks another step in the growing wrestling involvement of Sting's family following the icon's retirement from active competition in 2024. Garrett previously appeared on AEW television during Sting's retirement storyline leading into AEW Revolution 2024, portraying the classic "Surfer Sting" version of his father. His younger brother, Steven Borden Jr., appeared alongside him in Wolfpac-inspired Sting gear during the same presentation, eventually getting revenge on the Young Bucks during Sting's retirement match at Revolution.

Over the last year, Steven has been the primary focus of wrestling observers tracking Sting's children. The former college football player made his in-ring debut in October 2025 after training with AEW stars such as Darby Allin and FTR, teaming with JD Drake against Darby Allin and Killer Kross. He has since worked multiple dark matches for AEW and ROH while also making appearances in NJPW and indie promotions such as DEFY and Maple Leaf Pro Wrestling. Garrett's foray into wrestling appears to have been inspired by his brother's success in the business.

There had been no prior hints that Garrett would be following his father or brother into the wrestling business or had begun training prior to this match. It's unclear whether Garrett has been training with Dustin Rhodes, who operates both the Rhodes Wrestling Association and Rhodes Wrestling Academy. However, Garrett's wife, Kaetlyn Borden, has direct ties to the Rhodes Wrestling Academy system. She previously wrestled under the name Jael before stepping away in 2025 following the birth of their son and now works within AEW's Community Outreach department, having competed for the Rhodes Wrestling Association, Ring of Honor, and other promotions.

Garrett's previous involvement in wrestling included being part of an angle involving Sting and Jeff Jarrett in TNA back in the mid-2000s and supporting his father during Sting's retirement tour in 2024, which led to him and Steven being attacked by the Young Bucks. The duo got their revenge during Sting's retirement match at Revolution, with the brothers confronting the Bucks at the start of the match in tribute gear.

The Rhodes Wrestling Association and Rhodes Wrestling Academy are operated by Dustin Rhodes, the older half-brother of Cody Rhodes and one of Sting's final tag team partners during his AEW run. The Final Reckoning card also featured Brock Anderson, son of wrestling legend Arn Anderson, alongside several Rhodes Wrestling Academy trainees and graduates.

Sting officially retired at AEW Revolution 2024 in Greensboro, North Carolina, teaming with Darby Allin to defeat The Young Bucks in the final match of his nearly four-decade wrestling career. With both of his sons now pursuing professional wrestling and his wife working in the AEW organization, Sting's legacy appears positioned to continue through the next generation of the Borden family.

AEW has continued to expand its lineup of branded Dynamite specials in recent years, with Beach Break remaining one of the more recognizable themed events on the company's calendar. AEW's Paul Wight Details Taking Care Of His Giant Frame

Paul Wight, the legendary giant formerly known as Big Show during his time in WWE, recently opened up about managing his health and massive frame in his mid-50s. The 54-year-old spoke on the "High Performance" podcast to discuss his life, career, and current approach to wellness.

Wight was diagnosed with acromegaly, a rare growth disorder typically caused by a benign tumor near the pituitary gland, and underwent surgery to address the condition at age 19. While the condition gave him the extraordinary size and strength that defined his legendary wrestling career, he explained that maintaining such a massive physique becomes increasingly challenging with age.

"The only thing now that's tough is time, bones," Wight said during the interview. "A lot of things that give you your gifts and your strength when you're younger, the amazing athleticism and power and all that stuff, in time, you can't carry that size and mass around, so you have to find a way to make yourself healthier."

Currently weighing around 385 pounds, Wight has been working to trim down to approximately 340-350 pounds, which he feels represents a healthier target weight for his frame. He explained that reducing his mass by another 30 to 40 pounds would significantly improve his mobility and quality of life.

"For me, at 385 now, 350, 340 looks really healthy for me now. Another 30, 40 pounds off, it's easier to move, walk more... I'd love to be able to go on a hike through the mountains. 'You, hike?' I'd like to know that I could if I wanted to," Wight shared.

Despite the challenges of his condition, Wight expressed gratitude that he avoided many of the serious complications associated with acromegaly. He noted that his joints remain in good condition despite undergoing two knee and hip replacements, and he has not experienced the sinus problems that often plague acromegaly patients.

While managing his health, Wight did acknowledge the unique difficulties that come with his exceptional size, joking about the practical challenges. Finding properly fitting gloves and watch bands, for example, presents ongoing obstacles due to his extraordinarily large frame.

🔥 Roman Reigns vs Jacob Fatu Feud

The feud between Roman Reigns and Jacob Fatu has become one of the biggest stories on WWE RAW since Reigns' return after WrestleMania 42. This tension led to a World Heavyweight Championship match at WWE Backlash 2026, where Reigns managed to retain his title despite a hard-fought contest. However, the loss only intensified Fatu's aggression, as he attacked Reigns with the Tongan Death Grip immediately following the bell and continued his assault in subsequent weeks.

WWE has presented Fatu in an increasingly threatening manner, marking a significant shift from how they typically book Reigns' opponents. One of the clearest indicators of Fatu's elevated status is the need for The Bloodline to unite against him. Historically, Reigns has been portrayed as an unstoppable force who could overcome challengers either alone or with minimal assistance from The Usos. With Fatu, however, the entire faction must work in concert to contain him. During their encounters, Fatu has repeatedly destroyed The Usos before targeting Reigns, using the Tongan Death Grip to choke out the champion. The Usos have proven unable to handle Fatu on their own, a departure from earlier angles where they could overpower certain opponents through numerical advantage.

Fatu represents something Reigns has never truly encountered before: an opponent who is completely unpredictable and seemingly uncontrollable. While Reigns has faced masterminds, underdogs, family members, and various other archetypes throughout his career, Fatu operates as a wild force driven purely by emotion. He shows no respect for WWE management or Bloodline traditions, attacking officials without fear of consequence and even using the sacred Tongan Death Grip on his own blood relative. Fatu has made it clear he doesn't care about winning the title; instead, he wants the life Reigns is enjoying and is willing to risk everything to achieve it. This unpredictability directly counters Reigns' greatest strength, which has always been his ability to control matches, manipulate opponents, and force acknowledgment through psychological warfare.

Reigns' own reaction to Fatu reveals how different this situation is from his previous family conflicts. In the past, when dealing with family members like Jey Uso and Solo Sikoa, Reigns attempted to control them through manipulation and the demand for loyalty. With Fatu, however, Reigns quickly abandoned that approach and instead viewed him as a dangerous problem that needed to be eliminated. Initially, Reigns believed he could force Fatu to acknowledge him with The Usos' help, but Fatu's unhinged nature made this impossible. On the May 18 episode of WWE RAW, after attacking Fatu, Reigns told Adam Pearce that the General Manager should fire Fatu, suggesting Reigns saw no path to control this particular rival.

The events on May 18 RAW escalated the rivalry to its breaking point. Fatu opened the show and demanded Reigns come to the ring, but was instead met by The Usos, who attempted to talk sense into him. Jimmy Uso warned Fatu that his actions could cost him everything, but Fatu refused to back down, repeatedly stating he would do anything to better his family's life, even if it meant refusing to acknowledge Roman Reigns. The conversation quickly turned physical when Jey Uso swung a chair at Fatu. Despite being outnumbered, Fatu continued fighting back against all three men. When Reigns finally entered the fray, the numbers game caught up with Fatu as he absorbed multiple superkicks, a spear, a 1D finisher, and Superman Punches before being left laying in the ring.

As Reigns and The Usos prepared to leave, Adam Pearce rushed to the ring and suggested Fatu should be fired due to his recent issues with management. Before Pearce could continue, however, Fatu grabbed a microphone and issued a challenge to Tribal Combat. Pearce immediately attempted to shut down the idea, but Reigns paused, looked toward The Usos, and accepted the challenge. This acceptance was particularly significant as it showed Reigns' desire to handle Fatu on his own terms rather than through management intervention. Michael Cole noted on commentary that the match would likely take place at WWE Clash in Italy, and it was subsequently made official for May 31 at the Inalpi Arena in Turin, Italy, with Reigns' World Heavyweight Championship on the line.

The stipulation itself carries historical weight. Tribal Combat is essentially a no disqualification match specifically for Bloodline members, with the added stipulation that the losing competitor must acknowledge the winner as their Tribal Chief. Only two Tribal Combat matches have taken place in WWE history: Reigns defeated Jey Uso at SummerSlam 2023, and Reigns defeated Solo Sikoa on the RAW Netflix premiere in early January 2025. Both victories have served to cement Reigns' position as the supreme leader of The Bloodline. However, many believe this upcoming match with Fatu could prove different due to the unprecedented nature of his threat level.

The feud has created a fascinating dynamic regarding fan allegiance. While Reigns remains one of WWE's biggest attractions and a massive superstar that fans pay to see perform live, the storyline itself has created legitimate sympathy for Fatu's position. Former WWE star Matt Morgan has publicly questioned why fans aren't rallying behind Fatu, noting that his story is more sympathetic. Fatu consistently speaks about turning his life around after his prison sentence, bettering himself, and wanting to provide for his children and create a better future for his family on his own terms. In contrast, Morgan points out that Reigns has reverted to the arrogant Tribal Chief character that fans once despised, still demanding acknowledgment and using his power and family connections to maintain control, despite refusing to help Fatu's family during his dominant universal championship run.

Morgan's commentary highlighted an interesting irony in the storyline. Despite Fatu's violent attacks and chaotic behavior, the underlying narrative makes Reigns and The Usos appear to be the real villains. Reigns is presented as arrogant and controlling, while The Usos serve as his enforcers, maintaining the status quo that benefits The Bloodline at the expense of hungry individuals like Fatu. Morgan tweeted that he cannot fathom how fans continue to cheer for Reigns when the actual storyline shows Jacob as the character pursuing personal growth and family stability, only to be suppressed by the arrogant Roman and his henchmen. However, WWE has structurally presented segments in a way that naturally pushes fans toward Reigns due to his legendary status and the way The Usos are still massively popular.

Fan reactions to the feud have remained split since its inception. Some fans continue supporting Reigns because of his star power and legendary status as a top performer. Others believe Jacob Fatu has every reason to be angry and view him as the legitimate underdog in the story. This gray area appears to be exactly what WWE intends, as it creates organic debate and discussion around the feud. The complexity of the storyline suggests that WWE is not simply positioning Fatu as a traditional challenger but rather as a legitimate threat whose motivations blur the lines between hero and villain.

Looking ahead to WWE Clash in Italy, the stakes are considerably higher than a standard championship match. The event will also feature Cody Rhodes defending the Undisputed WWE Championship against GUNTHER, and many observers believe a title change is more likely to occur in that bout than in the Reigns versus Fatu match. Most wrestling analysts suggest that the best booking decision would be for Reigns to retain his championship and continue building toward a significant title defense at SummerSlam in August. However, WWE will need to ensure that Fatu is protected in defeat, particularly given how he has been positioned as such a serious and dangerous threat throughout this feud.

The Reigns versus Fatu Tribal Combat match represents a culmination of months of storytelling that has fundamentally altered how WWE presents the concept of a dangerous rival. By portraying Fatu as someone who cannot be controlled, manipulated, or reasoned with, and by showing the complete Bloodline struggling to contain him, WWE has created a unique challenger archetype. Whether Fatu will finally dethrone Reigns or whether Reigns will overcome this unprecedented threat remains to be seen, but the match is positioned as potentially one of the most significant encounters in recent Bloodline history.
