🔥 Kairi Sane WWE Release

Kairi Sane WWE Release Sparks Outpouring of Support from Wrestlers and Fans

On April 24, 2026, WWE announced a wave of roster cuts affecting both main roster and NXT talent ahead of the Friday Night SmackDown episode. Among the 24 confirmed departures was Kairi Sane, a former multiple-time WWE Women's Tag Team Champion and former WWE NXT Women's Champion. The release came as a surprise to many, as Sane had been actively involved in an ongoing feud between Asuka and IYO SKY on Monday Night Raw for the past six months, with her even performing on the recent episode of Raw before her departure was announced.

Sane herself addressed her departure with a brief but heartfelt message posted to social media. She wrote: "I'm truly grateful to have people like you by my side. Thank you for always being with me. I'll set sail... in my own time. Until then... I love you." The message struck a grateful yet hopeful tone, suggesting she remains positive about her future despite the unexpected timing of her WWE exit.

The reaction from her colleagues has been immediate and deeply emotional. Asuka, who recently teamed with Sane as part of The Kabuki Warriors, responded directly to Sane's post with a message of solidarity and support. "No matter what, I'll always be in your corner, Kairi. I'll never leave you feeling alone," Asuka wrote, emphasizing the depth of their bond beyond their on-screen partnership.

However, perhaps the most striking response came from IYO SKY, Sane's former tag team partner in Damage CTRL and recent on-screen rival in the Asuka-SKY feud. SKY broke character to share an emotional and detailed tribute, expressing genuine heartbreak at the sudden nature of Sane's departure. She opened her statement by acknowledging that while athletes eventually face turning points in their careers, "I feel so heartbroken that it came at such an unexpected time." SKY's message, posted in both English and Japanese, went far deeper than a simple goodbye.

In her tribute, SKY praised Sane not merely as a performer but as a person, describing how much she meant to her colleagues throughout the locker room. "Kairi was truly loved by all her colleagues, not just as a performer but as a person. She was kinder than anyone, a little airheaded in the most endearing way, funny, charming, and my proud best friend," SKY wrote. She continued by reflecting on their shared experiences during their time together in WWE, describing Sane as "a comrade-in-arms who faced countless hardships in America, shed so many tears, and grew together with me."

SKY's message became particularly poignant when she addressed the personal impact of Sane's absence, writing: "It's so hard to accept that Kairi won't be in the passenger seat of the rental car anymore, but..." This line captured the intimate friendship between the two, referencing the everyday moments they shared during their travels together in the United States.

Despite the sadness, SKY remained optimistic about Sane's future. She expressed confidence that Sane's talent and experience would lead to something meaningful: "I'm absolutely certain that with her innate talent and the incredible knowledge and experience she's gained in WWE, Kairi's new journey will be something truly amazing. From the bottom of my heart, I wish for my beloved Kairi's success and happiness. I love you so much Kairi. We'll be best friends forever and always."

The release also drew public support from Bayley, another member of Damage CTRL, who wrote: "I'm having a hard time finding words for this one. I love you and it was an honor to tell stories with you." Additionally, Dakota Kai (now known as Charlie), who was previously released from WWE in May 2025 and had been Sane's partner in Damage CTRL, commented on Sane's farewell post: "Love you so much you're so special to so many people."

Beyond the locker room, fan reaction has been overwhelmingly supportive and vocal. The hashtag #WeWantKAIRI quickly surged to the number two trending topic on Twitter as of April 25, 2026, with thousands of posts calling for WWE to reconsider its decision and bring Sane back. The movement demonstrates the strong connection Sane built with audiences during her WWE tenure.

In a sign that even some WWE personalities sympathized with the fan sentiment, ring announcer Jackie Redmond posted the hashtag #WeWantKAIRI multiple times in a social media post, though the tweet was later deleted. This public stance from someone affiliated with WWE broadcasts showed that support for Sane extended beyond just fans.

In an effort to formalize the push for Sane's return, supporters launched a petition on Change.org calling for WWE to re-sign the former champion. The petition description praised Sane's accomplishments and her connection with fans, noting her accomplishments as a former NXT Women's Champion and multiple-time WWE Women's Tag Team Champion. "A former NXT Women's Champion and one-half of the WWE Women's Tag Team Champions, Sane's accomplishments speak for themselves, and her contributions have left an indelible mark on the industry," the petition read.

The petition specifically highlighted Sane's beloved "Pirate Princess" persona and its impact on audiences. It stated: "During her tenure, Kairi Sane inspired countless fans with her Pirate Princess persona, an embodiment of strength, resilience, and elegance. She carried herself with a grace and professionalism that set a standard both inside and outside the ring." The petition made a direct appeal to WWE leadership, arguing that "Her presence would invigorate storylines, increase viewer engagement, and inspire a new generation of wrestlers and fans alike."

The timing of Sane's release creates significant questions about the future direction of the Asuka versus IYO SKY feud. The two were previously slated to potentially face off at WrestleMania 42, though reports suggested the match may have been moved to WWE Backlash 2026 in Tampa, scheduled for two weeks after the release announcement. However, with Sane gone from the company, the entire context and motivation for the feud has been fundamentally altered, as Sane was arguably the central figure driving the storyline forward.

On the same episode of Raw that Sane performed, IYO SKY and Rhea Ripley officially went their separate ways as a team, with Ripley now appearing on SmackDown as part of a team with Charlotte Flair and Alexa Bliss. This means WWE will need to find new directions for both IYO SKY and Asuka, whether by continuing their feud without Sane or by pivoting to other potential programs on their respective brands.

Per WWE policy, Sane and all other main roster talent released on April 24 are subject to a 90-day non-compete clause before they are cleared to sign elsewhere. This means Sane cannot appear for another wrestling promotion until approximately late July 2026, though speculation about her future destination has already begun.

Industry observers have suggested several possible destinations for Sane once her non-compete period ends. AEW represents the most obvious option for many fans, though concerns have been raised about the promotion's already crowded women's roster. Japan represents another realistic possibility, with Marigold promotion being frequently mentioned as a natural fit, especially given its connection to IYO SKY, who has wrestled there in recent years. Marigold also features Sane's former partner Mayu Iwatani and is actively seeking a new top star following Utami Hayashishita's announced departure from the promotion. Alternatively, Sane could follow the freelance path chosen by fellow former WWE star Sareee, appearing across multiple Japanese promotions.

Regardless of where Sane's career leads next, the immediate impact of her departure is clear: the wrestling community, both professional and fan-based, has demonstrated the deep respect and affection in which she is held. The #WeWantKAIRI movement, the emotional tributes from colleagues like IYO SKY and Asuka, and the fan petitions all underscore that Sane's impact on WWE extended far beyond her in-ring work and championship accomplishments. Whether WWE will reconsider its decision remains to be seen, but the public response to her release has made clear that Kairi Sane's wrestling career and personal character have left a lasting impression on the sport.

🔥 Wyatt Sicks WWE Release

All five members of the Wyatt Sicks departed WWE on Friday, April 24, following a wave of 24 talent releases from the company. Bo Dallas (Uncle Howdy), Erick Rowan, Dexter Lumis, Joe Gacy, and Nikki Cross formed the faction in June 2024 to carry on the legacy of Bray Wyatt following his passing on August 24, 2023. The group's final televised match came on the SmackDown before WrestleMania 42 on April 17, when they lost to The MFTs in a street fight.

Dexter Lumis was among the first to publicly address his release with an emotional message posted to Instagram on Saturday. In his statement, Lumis emphasized the deep bond he shared with his faction members, writing: "Won't make this too sappy, but I love each and every member of this group. The task we took on together was truly monumental. I won't look back with any regrets." He also thanked everyone he worked with behind the scenes, noting that "The WWE locker room is filled with amazing talent."

The most poignant part of Lumis' message directly addressed the fans of the Wyatt Sicks and the spiritual presence that defined their run. He wrote: "To the fans of Wyatt Sicks, we truly felt Bray's presence each and every time we made our entrance. I would like to think most of you did too..." Lumis closed his farewell with a quote attributed to CM Punk: "If your dreams don't scare the shit out of you, you need bigger dreams," followed by "See you soon," hinting at his future in professional wrestling. All released talent members are subject to a 90-day non-compete clause before they can work elsewhere.

Joe Gacy also shared an extensive emotional message on Instagram reflecting on his journey and time with the Wyatt Sicks. Gacy opened by expressing how he had achieved a lifelong dream, recalling how he and his brother used to watch Monday Night Raw as children and fight each other on their beds. He acknowledged his years of work in WWE and expressed genuine appreciation for the opportunity, stating: "I worked for years and got the chance and it honestly was a pleasure."

In his message, Gacy extended specific thanks to key figures who helped shape his WWE career. He thanked Shawn Michaels for giving him his early opportunity in NXT and allowing him to develop as a performer, and Triple H for placing him in a position with the Wyatt Sicks that allowed him to achieve his dream and see parts of the world he otherwise wouldn't have experienced. He wrote: "Thank you for giving me my early opportunity in WWE NXT and allowing me to try something new and become a star. To Triple H for putting me in a position with the Wyatt Sicks to achieve a dream and see parts of the world I wouldn't have seen otherwise."

Gacy also expressed his gratitude toward each individual member of the Wyatt Sicks faction, making clear that the relationships formed transcended the professional wrestling storyline. To Bo Dallas, he wrote: "Thank you for trusting me and accepting me to be a part of this legacy. I am truly honored." Regarding Erick Rowan, Gacy shared: "Getting to know you was honestly the best. Laughing during travel and talking just about ideas and how things were with Windham in the early years. It was a blast." He referred to Nikki Cross as his "older little sister," thanking her for showing him the ropes when he was newly called up to the main roster, while addressing Dexter Lumis as "My partner, my brother. Thank you for being someone I can rely on in and out of the ring."

The most emotional aspect of Gacy's statement came when he addressed Bray Wyatt directly, acknowledging that the faction's primary purpose was to preserve his legacy. Gacy wrote: "Windham — I just want to thank you for letting me in. Allowing me to be a part of all of this has been a true honor. I really can't even believe I got to do this and be a part even a little in keeping your legacy alive." This message underscored the deep respect the group held for the late wrestler and their commitment to honoring his memory through their work.

The releases sparked tributes from other WWE performers who had connections to the Wyatt Sicks. Alexa Bliss, a former Bray Wyatt associate and two-time WWE Women's Tag Team Champion with Nikki Cross, posted a heartfelt message on Twitter: "Bliss cross forever thank you WWE Nikki Cross for all of the amazing times, fun car rides, laughs & for being Hendrix's backstage bestie love you Nikki – can't wait to see what's next." In response, Nikki Cross wrote: "I love you Lexi. Thank you for everything. I cherish our friendship, and I'm so proud of the memories we built and moments we shared. Seeing you and Hendrix together and getting to hang out with you guys has been such a wonderful gift. Keep Rollin Rollin Rollin. Bliss Cross Forever."

Bliss also sent a separate message thanking the entire Wyatt Sicks faction, writing simply: "Thank you Wyatt Sicks." The tributes suggest a genuine camaraderie had formed among the talent involved, with multiple wrestlers expressing deep personal connections that extended beyond their on-screen personas.

Analysis from wrestling observers suggested that WWE may have missed an opportunity by releasing the entire faction. When the Wyatt Sicks first debuted in June 2024, there was significant fan anticipation that the group could interact with performers who had history with Bray Wyatt, particularly Alexa Bliss, who had been featured prominently in his storylines during his time as The Fiend in 2020 and 2021. Bliss has continued to honor Bray's legacy upon her return to WWE in early 2025, regularly using a DDT variation of his Sister Abigail finishing move, often on the same shows where Bo Dallas was using the same move with the Wyatt Sicks.

Despite the initial promise surrounding the Wyatt Sicks' debut, critics noted that WWE's creative direction for the group failed to capitalize on their potential. The faction found itself engaged in endless feuds with The Final Testament and, more notably, The MFTs, which many observers felt diluted the group's mystique and purpose. The indefinite feuds with mid-card opponents led to the group losing much of the promise and intrigue that surrounded their initial arrival in WWE. The absence of Bray Wyatt's one-of-a-kind creativity, presence, and charisma made it difficult for the group to connect with audiences in the way he had during his storied career, and creative direction appeared to compound those challenges.

The releases have had a notable impact on SmackDown's tag team division, which had been the subject of fan criticism in recent months. The division, which had showcased incredible talent at SummerSlam 2025, has been significantly depleted by recent departures. Beyond the Wyatt Sicks members, the division also lost Tommaso Ciampa, Andrade, Alex Shelley, and Chris Sabin. With these departures, SmackDown's tag team division now consists primarily of The MFTs, Fraxiom, Los Garza, Damian Priest with R-Truth, and various hastily assembled duos such as The Miz and Kit Wilson. The once-strong division has noticeably suffered, with the tag titles falling into the background as the creative focus has shifted elsewhere on the brand.

With all five members of the Wyatt Sicks now released and subject to their 90-day non-compete clauses, fans and industry observers are left to wonder what could have been for the faction had WWE allowed their storyline to develop more organically or positioned them more prominently within the company's creative vision.

Reigns secured victory using two Spears to claim his new title. According to internal reports, WWE had planned for Reigns to leave the event as champion well in advance due to long-term storyline plans involving a potential feud with GUNTHER. An alternate finish had been discussed in which Punk would have retained the title at WrestleMania but dropped it at Backlash, though WWE ultimately decided to proceed with Reigns winning at the event to protect Punk's position while advancing their championship storyline.

Speaking with Sports Illustrated, Rhodes explained his reasoning for wanting time limits reinstated across all wrestling promotions. He highlighted how time limits can organically create stakes in matches that otherwise lack storyline history or prior heat between competitors.

"Say you have a cold match, and I mean 'cold match' by there's no previous heat or anything of that nature," Rhodes said. "Say you just had Dom Mysterio vs. anybody, name 'em. New guy. Ricky Saints, 'NXT.' You have a match, and they have no previous equity, no storyline. There's a natural stakes when you first hear, 'five minutes gone, ten remaining.' There's a natural stakes. There's a clock. Jim Ross taught me that and he was really accurate about it. You can give a match stakes that didn't really have, because crowds are the most important engagement we have for this."

As a student of traditional wrestling methods influenced by his late father Dusty Rhodes, the Undisputed WWE Champion further noted the psychological impact of time limits on live audiences. He referenced his experience in New Japan Pro Wrestling, where he observed how crowds physically react when time remaining announcements are made during matches. According to Rhodes, fans rise from their seats in quiet anticipation when they realize a match is entering its critical final minutes.

"The 'American Nightmare' acknowledged that while he appreciates the dramatic effect of time limits, he recognizes they are unlikely to make a comeback in WWE's current programming philosophy. Nevertheless, Rhodes remains a vocal advocate for the practice as a valuable tool for building match drama and crowd engagement.
