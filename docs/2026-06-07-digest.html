🔥 AEW Collision - Owen Hart Foundation Tournament

Hazuki emerged as an unexpected victor in the Owen Hart Foundation Women's Tournament quarterfinal main event of AEW Collision on June 6, 2026, defeating Persephone in a hard-fought matchup that saw the Stardom wrestler advance to the semifinals where she will face Mercedes Moné.

The tournament had been building with clear storyline trajectories. After Mercedes Moné replaced Willow Nightingale as the wildcard entrant, her bracket seemed poised for revenge angles. Moné defeated Alex Windsor in the quarterfinals to exact revenge, as Windsor had won the RevPro Women's title from Moné during the Ultimo Moné era. It appeared logical that Persephone would advance so Mercedes could gain revenge over her as well, since Persephone had previously won the CMLL Women's World title from Moné.

However, the match did not follow the expected narrative. Hazuki used her speed and savvy to escape a powerbomb attempt, countering with a Codebreaker. Hazuki then demonstrated her lucha libre abilities by catching Persephone in a Magistral Cradle for the pinfall victory. The finish was particularly well-constructed from a storytelling perspective, as Persephone's character was protected in defeat due to her vision still being bothered from Julia Hart's black mist attack from weeks prior. This detail provided explanation for the loss while keeping Persephone strong for potential future matches.

The post-match segment elevated the angle further when Mercedes Moné refused to let Hazuki's victory close the show quietly. As Hazuki made her way up the ramp following the match and respectfully exchanged acknowledgment with Persephone, Mercedes emerged and attacked Persephone from behind, locking in the Bank Statement submission hold. This aggressive move sent a clear statement about Moné's presence and intentions in the tournament.

Hazuki immediately returned to the ring to make the save for her fallen opponent. However, Mercedes quickly shifted her attention and attacked Hazuki as well, laying her out with punches before exiting the ring. The two officials and security rushed in to break up the escalating situation. Despite the initial assault, Hazuki recovered and pursued Mercedes to the outside, ultimately hitting a suicide dive that left her standing tall as the show came to a close. This finish gave Hazuki momentum heading into the semifinal while establishing Mercedes as an aggressive, unpredictable force in the tournament who was willing to attack anyone, even her next scheduled opponent.

The segment worked on multiple levels for the storyline. It provided Mercedes with television time and allowed her to reestablish her aggressive character following her return after spending the end of 2025 away from action. She had last wrestled when dropping the TBS Championship to Willow Nightingale. Her attacks on both Persephone and Hazuki demonstrated that Moné was not concerned with traditional wrestling etiquette and was willing to take shortcuts and ambush tactics.

The other side of the Owen Hart Foundation Women's bracket was also advancing. Athena had already secured her spot in the semifinals by defeating Mina Shirakawa at Double or Nothing. Athena's semifinal opponent would be determined by a match between Sareee and Skye Blue scheduled for AEW Dynamite Summer Blockbuster.

Shane Taylor Trademark Filing

Shane Taylor, the leader of Shane Taylor Promotions, has filed to trademark his ring name and faction logo with the United States Patent and Trademark Office. The filings were submitted on June 5 under Shane Taylor Promotions LLC. This protective measure covers both merchandise and entertainment services related to professional wrestling.

The trademark applications include comprehensive descriptions of services spanning the entertainment industry. The filings cover providing wrestling news and information via global computer networks, entertainment services involving wrestling exhibits and performances by professional wrestlers and entertainers, entertainment in the nature of wrestling contests, and televised appearances by professional wrestlers and sports entertainers. Daniel Bryan's meteoric rise to the top of WWE remains one of the most memorable fan-driven success stories in modern wrestling history. According to discussions about this era, fan support for Bryan reached a level that WWE could no longer ignore. Throughout that period, Bryan became the centerpiece of the wildly popular "Yes Movement," with audiences routinely hijacking shows by chanting his name and demanding a bigger role for him on WWE television.

Multiple observers have noted that McMahon's initial hesitation to push Bryan stemmed from his belief that Bryan lacked the larger-than-life personality that McMahon associated with main event superstars. The comparison was frequently made to how McMahon had booked Bret Hart in earlier days - a great performer in the ring, but someone McMahon didn't believe possessed the kind of personality necessary to be the next Hulk Hogan.

Despite any reported reservations from McMahon, Bryan ultimately became one of WWE's most beloved performers of his generation. Meanwhile, the Total Divas situation remains a cautionary tale about how entertainment commitments can compromise the integrity of a professional wrestling division when booking decisions are made based on factors other than in-ring talent and championship legitimacy.

These stories collectively demonstrate how Vince McMahon's personal biases and business decisions during critical periods in WWE history often conflicted with what talented performers and passionate fans believed was best for professional wrestling.
