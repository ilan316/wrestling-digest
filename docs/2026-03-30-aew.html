🔥 AEW Anti-ICE Chants with Julia Hart and Brody King

During the February 4, 2026 edition of AEW Dynamite in Las Vegas, fans loudly chanted "F**k ICE" (United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement) as Brody King's match with MJF began. The chants caught MJF off guard and generated significant mainstream news coverage for AEW. This moment represented an escalation of anti-ICE activism that King had initiated earlier by wearing an "Abolish ICE" t-shirt during an episode of Dynamite, which he had also worn at Arena Mexico nearly a year prior in June.

The chants have since become more of a staple at AEW shows, even taking place outside of matches involving King himself. As these demonstrations have grown more frequent, more AEW talents have begun discussing them publicly. The responses have been mixed within the wrestling community. Anthony Ogogo, an AEW and ROH star, has expressed disapproval of the chants, feeling that wrestling should serve as an escape from real-world politics. However, other wrestlers have been more supportive of King's activism and the fan response it generates.

Julia Hart, King's former House of Black and Hounds of Hell stablemate, has been one of the most vocal supporters. During an interview with Inside the Ropes, Hart explained that her support stems from a deeply personal connection to the situations King is highlighting. Hart was born and raised in Cambridge, Minnesota, less than an hour away from Minneapolis, where significant ICE-related incidents occurred earlier in 2026. She specifically referenced Renee Good and Alex Pretti, who were killed by ICE agents during the government agency's occupation of Minneapolis, which prompted nationwide protests.

Hart revealed the emotional weight this carries for her by noting that some of the incidents that occurred were literally at locations she frequented growing up. "Like, some of the stuff that was happening was right out of a donut shop that I used to go to while growing up," Hart said. "You know, it's kind of like a weird feeling and all we can do is help anyone in need and donating money and being supportive as a community."

Despite this personal impact, Hart was careful to praise King's approach to the activism. She commended him for using a simple method—wearing a t-shirt—to raise awareness while making concrete contributions to help those affected. "Brody did a great job of just wearing a t-shirt that's all. He donated so much money to the families that needed it," Hart stated. The former AEW TBS Champion concluded by expressing her full support, saying "I think it's great what Brody has done."

When King himself addressed the chants in an interview with Denise Salcedo, he framed the issue in moral rather than political terms. He emphasized that he does not view it as political activism but rather as taking a stand on what is fundamentally right and wrong. King explained his motivation by referencing his younger self: "You know, when I was younger, I always wanted somebody that felt like me on TV or wherever to express the way that they felt, so I can have someone to champion for me. And if I can be that for other people that are being marginalized, then I want to be that for them."

King acknowledged that everyone attempts to characterize his activism as political, but he rejected that framing. "Everyone tries to bring up the political thing. In my opinion, it's just what's right and what's wrong. And there's a lot of wrong happening in the world, and if I can speak out against it then I will," he stated. He further noted that given the current state of affairs, people are naturally frustrated, and he believes a wrestling show provides a positive outlet for expressing that frustration.

Reflecting on the timeline of his activism, King noted that while he wore the Abolish ICE shirt at Arena Mexico in June of the previous year, the viral "F**k ICE" chants didn't occur until this year, right before AEW's Australia tour. He suggested that as conditions in the world have become more heated, people's frustration has intensified, leading to the spontaneous outbreak of organized chanting.

King admitted that the moment did catch him by surprise when it happened during his match. However, rather than being negatively impacted, he found it energizing. "It took me off guard, but it also like — I felt like I could run through a wall at that moment," King said with a laugh. He also noted that MJF clearly sensed the energy shift in the arena, as evidenced by the way MJF looked at the camera, seemingly recognizing that King was now operating at peak intensity heading into their match.

🔥 Vince Russo AEW Partnership Pitch

Vince Russo Pitches AEW-JCW Collaboration to Tony Khan

After AEW President Tony Khan publicly praised Vince Russo's work with JCW Lunacy on the Coach and Bro Show podcast, Russo seized the opportunity to make a bold pitch directly to Khan. The creative head and investor for Juggalo Championship Wrestling took to Twitter on March 30, 2026, to respond to Khan's compliments and propose a partnership that could shake up the wrestling landscape.

Khan had initially accepted an invitation from Jonathan Coachman to appear on the Coach and Bro Show podcast, where he expressed that he is a fan of both Coachman and Russo. Khan specifically praised Russo for doing a great job with JCW Lunacy. This endorsement from the AEW President did not go unnoticed by Russo, who quickly leveraged the moment into a full-fledged partnership proposal.

In his response, Russo acknowledged Khan's kind words while making it clear he wanted more than just a pat on the back. "Tony Khan—I got it CONFIRMED on a Good Word that you REALLY DO enjoy JCW Lunacy!!! While that's cool and much, much appreciated—give me a follow back," Russo wrote. From there, he escalated his message significantly, transitioning from gratitude to a direct creative pitch.

Russo then made his main proposal, directly addressing what he sees as an opportunity for both parties: "Let's talk some creative. Let's do something that will blow the WWE INTO OBLIVION!!! You've heard about me over the years, but you've never had the opportunity to get to know me. LET's GIVE THEM SOMETHING TO TALK ABOUT!!!"

The pitch highlights Russo's belief that a collaboration between himself, Khan, and AEW could create something significant enough to challenge WWE's dominance. Russo emphasized that while Khan is familiar with his reputation in the wrestling industry, they have never actually worked together on a creative project. He positioned this as an untapped opportunity that could result in attention-grabbing content for the wrestling world.

This move represents a strategic play by Russo to capitalize on Khan's public recognition of his work. Rather than simply accepting the praise, Russo used it as a springboard to propose a deeper professional relationship. The proposal also reflects Russo's confidence in his creative abilities and his belief that teaming up with Khan and AEW's resources could produce compelling content.

Notably, Russo also recently revealed ideas he had pitched for JCW that were rejected. During an appearance on the Two Man Power Trip podcast, he discussed a planned angle involving Glacier and a wrestler named Mecha Wolf. The concept involved introducing Glacier in full riot gear with "ICE" written on the back, seemingly targeting Mecha Wolf before revealing the surprise that "ICE" stood for "Glacier" as a play on words. Russo acknowledged that the angle never came to fruition because it would have generated immediate backlash from audiences who would have objected to the premise within the first week of execution, preventing the storyline from reaching the planned reveal.

Whether Tony Khan will respond positively to Russo's collaboration pitch remains to be seen, but the public nature of the proposal ensures it will generate discussion within the wrestling community about the possibilities of such a partnership and what it could mean for both AEW and the broader wrestling landscape.

Anthony Ogogo AEW Signing Story

Anthony Ogogo Explains How He Was Signed To AEW

In 2021, former Olympic boxer Anthony Ogogo burst onto the scene in AEW alongside QT Marshall to have a brief feud with Cody Rhodes. During an interview, Ogogo revealed the path that led to his signing with the promotion, crediting Diamond Dallas Page with making the initial introduction.

Page, who serves as godfather to his daughter, introduced Ogogo to Rhodes, which set him on the path to AEW. When the two first connected, Ogogo shared his personal story with Rhodes, detailing his athletic background and the significant obstacles he had overcome throughout his life.

"I told Cody the story, the Olympics, my eye injury, my mother not being well at the start of the Games," Ogogo recalled. His mother suffered a brain aneurysm just weeks before his Olympic match in 2012. Years later, he would face another devastating setback when he suffered a severe eye injury during his fight against Craig Cunningham on October 22, 2016, an injury that rendered him legally blind.

Rhodes was impressed by Ogogo's background and personal resilience. "Cody was like 'Damn, that's a good story, you're an athlete, you look good, you have pedigree, and one of the best boxers on the planet," Ogogo said, recalling Rhodes's reaction to his story.

Rhodes then took the next step by speaking to Tony Khan about Ogogo. The two had previously met before the first Double Or Nothing at a Fulham vs. Newcastle football match, where they spent the entire game talking. "He said listen I have got this for you and then offered me a contract. It was a wonderful day," Ogogo recalled of Khan's decision to bring him into AEW.

Ogogo expressed his gratitude for Khan's role in making this opportunity possible, noting that Khan has been pivotal to the wrestling industry as a whole. Through this chain of connections and introductions, Ogogo was able to pursue his dream of becoming a professional wrestler.

Beyond the initial signing, Ogogo has experienced Khan's compassion firsthand. In 2022, Ogogo's grandmother passed away. While discussing the funeral proceedings with Khan, the two shared a touching personal moment. "He teared up for me that I lost my grandmother. He said, 'Go take the time you want and just be there for your family,'" Ogogo recalled, emphasizing Khan's empathy and support during a difficult time in his life.

Chris Jericho AEW Impact Discussion

Chris Jericho's impact on AEW has been undeniable, as one of the biggest names to first sign with Tony Khan in 2019 and the promotion's inaugural AEW World Champion. During an interview, Jericho commented on the financial impact he's had on AEW, and compared his journey from WWE to the promotion with that of ice hockey legend Bobby Hull jumping from the NHL to an upstart league called the WHA.

Jericho pointed out that Hull received a million-dollar contract when he made the jump, and as a result, every player in the NHL received a raise. Notably, Jericho's father was a hockey player, and according to the veteran, Hull's transition to the WHA bumped his father's pay up. "The moment I left WWE, Vince was thinking, 'It's happening again — raise everybody's pay.' There had been a ceiling for years," Jericho explained.

Jericho further claimed that nobody was paid more than a million dollars on guaranteed contracts in WWE before his departure, and added that his move to AEW was such a major needle-mover in the industry that it changed the pay structure across wrestling. "Guys started earning above it for the first time," he said. "That changed everything in the best possible way."

Jericho also noted that the competition between WWE and AEW similarly boosted WWE's product, just as their rivalry with WCW did. He criticized the fact that wrestling became a monopoly again after WCW's acquisition. "When I first had a meeting with Tony Khan about this new company, I thought there was no way it would work, because I'd heard it so many times before," he admitted.

The veteran then shared his belief that a successful wrestling company needs three important things to start: plenty of money, new talent, and a strong TV deal. According to Jericho, Khan had all of that. "That's all you need to start a company. And then once we started, I knew I had about three months to build as many guys as I could while the spotlight was on me, since I was the only nationally recognized star besides Jim Ross," he added.

Jericho explained that he closely began to work with the likes of Cody Rhodes, Kenny Omega, Adam Page, Darby Allin, Scorpio Sky, and Jungle Boy, to pass on the torch in different ways. "Then I put together the Inner Circle with Sammy, Ortiz, Santana, and Jake," he recounted. "Suddenly it's not all on my back — it's on six backs, then ten, then twelve. That's how you build a company."

AEW Okada International Championship

Kazuchika Okada proved that sometimes winning isn't about wrestling by the book. At AEW Collision on March 28, 2026, the AEW International Champion successfully defended his title against Kevin Knight in the main event, but his path to victory required bending the rules.

Knight brought the fight to Okada from the opening moments, building momentum with a springboard clothesline from the outside. He repeated the move inside the ring, but the champion managed to kick out and stay in the match. The drama continued to escalate when Knight executed a coast to coast dropkick that left the crowd on the edge of their seats.

The turning point came when Knight climbed to the top rope to attempt his UFO splash finisher. Okada wisely rolled out of harm's way, or at least attempted to. Knight calculated the distance and took flight anyway, but Okada had positioned himself strategically with his knees up, planting Knight on the mat and turning the momentum firmly in his favor.

With Knight stunned, it appeared the Rainmaker's finish was imminent. However, Knight displayed impressive resilience by countering Okada's signature move into a series of roll-ups that kept the champion on his heels. Okada regained control by applying a waistlock, but Knight tried to escape by rolling underneath. Okada's experience and ring awareness proved decisive in this moment.

As Knight attempted his escape, Okada stacked him down on top and went for the pin. In a blatant display of rule-bending, the champion hooked the ropes with his arm to gain additional leverage, giving himself the advantage he needed. The referee counted to three, and Okada secured the victory.

Okada's reign as AEW International Champion now extends to 260 consecutive days. He is steadily closing in on the previous record held by Orange Cassidy, who held the title for 326 days. The question now facing the wrestling community is who will ultimately prove to be the chosen one capable of dethroning Kazuchika Okada and ending his dominant run with the championship.
