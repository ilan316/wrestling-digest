WRESTLING NEWS ROUNDUP: WWE WRESTLEMANIA 42 AFTERMATH

The 42nd annual WrestleMania took place over two nights in Las Vegas and delivered major moments including surprise returns, shocking retirements, title changes, and memorable matches. However, the event also left several wrestlers dealing with legitimate injuries and set the stage for significant storyline developments in the coming weeks.

CHAMPIONSHIP CHANGES AND INJURIES

At WrestleMania 42 Night One, Cody Rhodes defeated Randy Orton to retain the Undisputed WWE Championship, but the victory came at a cost. After the match, Orton attacked Rhodes with a title belt shot to the face and delivered a Punt Kick to the head. Following the event, Rhodes was placed under concussion protocol and suffered an orbital bone injury, raising questions about his ability to defend the championship at the next premium live event.

Orton's heel turn has opened multiple storyline possibilities. Some potential opponents to replace Rhodes at WWE Backlash 2026 include Jelly Roll, who has been involved in the storyline and was attacked by Orton alongside Pat McAfee; Aleister Black, who was originally pitched to manipulate Orton before McAfee was inserted into the story; Matt Cardona, who was injured by Orton and could seek revenge; Drew McIntyre, who could use a title shot after losing to Jacob Fatu; Sami Zayn, who is transitioning to a heel character; and Trick Williams, who won the United States Championship at Mania.

Brock Lesnar's match against Oba Femi concluded with Lesnar's apparent retirement from professional wrestling. SETH ROLLINS' BLACK EYE AND BRON BREAKKER

Seth Rollins has been sporting a significant black eye since WrestleMania 42, which he confirmed came from Bron Breakker during an unexpected interference spot. Rollins did get some payback by striking Breakker with a chair backstage, busting him open, though Rollins acknowledged this may have only intensified Breakker's focus.

🔥 Paige's WWE Return and New Role

Paige made her long-awaited return to WWE at WrestleMania 42 Night One after being absent from the company for nearly four years. Teaming with Brie Bella, she defeated Charlotte Flair and Alexa Bliss, Bayley and Lyra Valkyria, and The Irresistible Forces to win the WWE Women's Tag Team Championships. This marked her first in-ring competition since December 2017, when a neck injury forced her into retirement before she could participate in major women's division milestones like the inaugural Royal Rumble match for women.

The path to Paige's return was contingent on two specific requirements from WWE. In a meeting with WWE President Nick Khan, he laid out the conditions clearly: her neck had to be medically cleared and she had to pass a drug test. Paige described Khan as easygoing and approachable during their encounter. She underwent an extensive 10-hour medical evaluation in Birmingham that ultimately cleared her for in-ring competition. The evaluation was particularly emotional for Paige, as she learned her neck was in good condition, a stark contrast to when she was forced to retire eight years prior due to neck issues.

An unexpected figure played a crucial role in facilitating Paige's return. Rapper Jelly Roll, during an appearance on a podcast alongside Dwayne Johnson's agent Brad Slater, questioned why WWE hadn't brought Paige back. He directly called WWE President Nick Khan about the matter, and within 30 minutes, Khan agreed to meet with Paige. A week later, Khan flew to WWE's Los Angeles offices for the meeting that would ultimately set her comeback in motion.

Paige has been remarkably candid about her personal journey during her absence from WWE. In 2017, after private videos of her leaked online, she reached one of the darkest points of her life. She admitted that she considered suicide during this period, fearing how her father would react to the scandal. Her father's compassionate response and unexpected suggestion that she could turn the situation around like Kim Kardashian had helped save her life. She credited him with giving her the perspective needed to move forward during that lowest moment.

Paige also revealed that during her initial return to WWE in 2018, after sitting out the first Women's Royal Rumble match backstage, Shane McMahon's introduction of her as SmackDown General Manager gave her a new sense of purpose and excitement about working for the company, even if she could not compete in-ring at that time.

Looking toward her current run, Paige has set specific goals for herself. She expressed her desire to finally participate in the Royal Rumble, Elimination Chamber, and other stipulation matches that were not available to women during her first active tenure. She reflected on the bittersweet experience of watching the first Women's Royal Rumble from backstage in 2018, unable to compete and unsure if she would ever get the chance. Now that her neck is cleared and she has returned to in-ring action, she views these opportunities as a priority for the next couple of years.

Paige has also been transparent about her mindset going into this run. She stated that she likely will not wrestle after her current WWE stint, which makes her determined to savor every opportunity and embrace the entire journey. She wants to be happy and have fun during this period, viewing it as a chance to finally experience the evolution of the women's division that she helped pioneer but missed due to her injury and time away.

She is also eight years sober, a significant milestone that contrasts sharply with her earlier WWE career when she failed multiple drug tests and was clearly struggling. Stephanie McMahon acknowledged in retrospect that officials had been deeply concerned about Paige's trajectory during her first run. When Paige initially returned in 2017 as a general manager, Stephanie expressed genuine relief that she was still alive.

During recent interviews on "What's Your Story?" with Stephanie McMahon, Paige also discussed emerging talent and recent WWE signings. She and McMahon praised Danhausen, who was revealed as WWE's latest addition at the 2026 Elimination Chamber PLE. Paige commented that Danhausen is a great worker with exceptional charisma that only he can pull off, and she was pleased that fans quickly embraced him. McMahon agreed that Danhausen's skills are evident when he gets opportunities to perform in the ring. Danhausen responded to their praise on social media, thanking them and offering to curse anyone they needed cursed.

Speculation has also emerged about Paige potentially forming a new faction within WWE. Given her alignment with Brie Bella and the close friendships she has maintained with various performers from her first run, several names have been suggested as potential members, including Nikki Bella, Zelina, Tatum Paxley, Nia Jax and Lash Legend of The Irresistible Forces, Nattie, and even AJ Lee, despite their historical on-screen rivalry that helped launch the Women's Revolution.

🔥 Billy Gunn's Wrestling Future

Billy Gunn's Wrestling Future: The Veteran's Perspective on Longevity, Legacy, and the Changing Landscape

At 62 years old, Billy Gunn remains an active competitor in professional wrestling, still performing in matches as recently as April 2026 while signed to WWE legends contract, AEW, and ROH. During interviews with Chris Van Vliet, Gunn addressed questions about his wrestling future, revealing that while he has no concrete retirement plans, he also doesn't aspire to work until age 70.

When asked if he plans to wrestle until 70, Gunn neither completely accepted nor rejected the idea. Instead, he emphasized that he'll continue wrestling as long as he's having fun. "So I want to do it while I still can, and while I'm still having fun," Gunn said. "I mean, you can ask my wife, she hates watching me, because I'm just such a goof. I'm such a goof because I just don't have any stress anymore. There's no stress for me to go out there and have everybody go 'oh watch, it's going to be an unbelievable wrestling match'. The argument could be made, depending on what someone looks for in their wrestling, that the NWA had a much better in-ring product than the WWF in its heyday, but the business plan of the WWF was far superior. At that phase of the industry, sizzle won over substance.

As mentioned, the debate more or less has always been, and probably always will be a part of the discussion of pro wrestling. The under card of WCW in the late-90s was stellar, but Steve Austin vs. Vince or The Rock in the main event slots ultimately tilted the tide of the wrestling war of that era. Even today, when the WWE is touting record-setting revenue, there are some fans that insist the match that got seven stars in front of a much smaller crowd was better. There's not necessarily a right or wrong answer, it merely depends on perspective.

However, there could definitely be a point where the commercialism, especially of corporate pro wrestling jumps the shark, souring even the most dedicated fans in the process. TKO and the suites that run the global, publicly-traded conglomerate might be tip-toeing on the fine line where they attempt to squeeze every dime out of the product to the point that it actually costs them a portion of the audience in the process.
