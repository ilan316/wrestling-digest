🔥 Darby Allin wins AEW World Championship from MJF at Spring BreakThru

Darby Allin reached the pinnacle of All Elite Wrestling on Wednesday night, capturing the AEW World Championship with a dominant victory over MJF in the main event of Dynamite Spring BreakThru. The title change came in Allin's home state of Washington, marking a full-circle moment for the performer who began his professional wrestling training roughly 20 minutes from the arena in Everett. The win represents the culmination of years of growth within AEW and marks Allin's first reign as AEW World Champion, though he had previously held the World Tag Team Championship alongside Sting and captured the TNT Championship twice.

The journey to this championship moment was fraught with drama. At the start of Dynamite, Renee Paquette informed MJF that he would be defending his title that evening, freshly off his successful defense against Kenny Omega at Dynasty just days earlier. MJF's reaction was immediate and hostile. He complained that he wasn't ready, claimed his back was injured from Sunday, and threatened legal action against everyone involved, including AEW President Tony Khan. He insisted that Allin wasn't ready for the World Championship and attempted to evade the match entirely.

Darby Allin responded with an emotional promo that connected deeply with fans. The challenger spoke about his journey and his willingness to either succeed or fail as himself, leaving many viewers moved by his words. However, MJF dismissed the sentiment entirely. The situation escalated when Bryan Danielson stepped in during commentary with a message directly from Tony Khan. Danielson announced that the match would happen in the main event, and if MJF refused to defend the title, he would be stripped of it immediately. This ultimatum left MJF with no choice but to accept the challenge.

Before stepping into the ring, Allin received a crucial visit from Sting, his longtime AEW tag team partner and mentor. The legendary Icon, appearing in face paint he had retired from years prior, delivered final words of motivation to his protege. Sting told Allin, "This isn't showtime, this is your time." The appearance of the retired legend gave Allin an emotional boost as he prepared for the most significant match of his career.

When the match finally began, it became immediately clear that Allin would not be denied his moment. MJF attempted his signature cheating tactics early, hitting Allin with a low blow while the referee Aubrey Edwards was distracted. However, Allin answered right back, giving MJF a low blow of his own when Edwards looked away. From that point forward, it was all Allin. The challenger took control and remained relentless. He delivered a Scorpion Death Drop, then followed with four consecutive Coffin Drops, one from each corner of the ring. To finish the match, Allin delivered a side headlock takeover that echoed their previous encounters, securing the pinfall and winning the championship in under five minutes.

The arena erupted in celebration following Allin's victory. Members of the locker room came out to support their colleague, including Kevin Knight, Mike Bailey, the Young Bucks, Brody King, Mascara Dorada, Mistico, the Outrunners, the Conglomeration, and Evil Uno. The emotional crescendo came when Sting made his entrance to embrace Allin, celebrating with his former tag team partner as the show concluded with the new champion surrounded by support from his peers.

Despite the emotional resonance of Allin's victory and the powerful imagery of his celebration, the wrestling community's immediate reaction was far more divided than AEW might have hoped. Online criticism erupted almost immediately, with fans expressing frustration not just with the result but particularly with the match's brevity and execution. Many felt the quick finish undermined everything that should have made this moment special.

Critics argued that the squash format damaged multiple elements simultaneously. Fans pointed out that MJF had just completed a prestigious title run that included eight successful defenses. His recent victories over Adam Page in a brutal, bloody encounter at Revolution and over Kenny Omega in a grueling main event at Dynasty had positioned him as a credible, dominant champion. One fan tweeted that MJF's unreal title run had helped AEW regain momentum, making it inexplicable that they would end it with a four-minute loss to Darby Allin.

The devaluation extended to the championship itself. Multiple observers noted that the title now appeared vulnerable and weak if it could change hands in such a short timeframe. Others felt the quick victory made Allin look like a flukey champion who got lucky rather than earned his position through a compelling match that proved his worthiness. The narrative of the moment seemed undermined by the manner in which it was executed.

The booking decision also prompted broader questions about AEW's creative philosophy. Several critics lamented that Tony Khan's promotion had become too focused on shocking moments and dramatic swerves rather than building long-term storytelling. One commenter noted that this would never happen in WWE, stating that neither Roman Reigns nor Cody Rhodes would ever be squashed in a title defense on television immediately after defending successfully on a pay-per-view event. The concern was that AEW was prioritizing short-term surprise moments over protecting the credibility of its talent and titles.

Some wrestling observers questioned whether disrupting the apparent long-term plans was worth the immediate impact. Reports suggested that many had expected MJF to hold the championship until AEW's All In event at Wembley Stadium in London, where he would lose to Will Ospreay, continuing a narrative thread from their New Japan Pro Wrestling history. By changing the title on Dynamite in this fashion, AEW had potentially altered those plans without clear justification.

Additionally, the quick finish raised strategic questions about what comes next. If Allin's reign leads to another unexpected title change in the near future, it could establish a pattern of instability at the top. Meanwhile, if MJF doesn't reclaim the belt before All In in August, the entire landscape of main event plans would need reconsideration, potentially leaving Will Ospreay without a clear direction for his own championship aspirations.

MJF himself did not take the loss quietly. Shortly after the broadcast concluded, the former champion took to social media with a heated reaction directed at Tony Khan. He posted, "WHAT THE ACTUAL FUCK?!?!? @TonyKhan THIS WILL NOT STAND!!!!" The passionate response suggested that MJF's character would likely pursue rematches or retribution, which could serve as a storyline bridge if MJF is positioned to reclaim the title down the line.

The contrast between in-arena and online reactions proved particularly striking. The crowd in attendance, including Allin's home state advantage in Everett, celebrated the triumph genuinely and emotionally. The locker room support appeared authentic and heartfelt. Yet as the news spread across wrestling media and social media platforms, the negativity intensified, with critics far outnumbering those who defended the decision.

Some writers noted that while the title change itself wasn't necessarily wrong, the execution was problematic. Allin's ascension to the world championship is defensible. He has been one of AEW's most consistent and recognizable stars since the company's inception in 2019. He embodies the spirit of AEW with his unique style and willingness to take risks, both in and out of the ring. His journey, which included nearly summiting Mount Everest just a year prior, had demonstrated his capacity to overcome extreme odds. In many ways, Allin represents what an AEW World Champion should be.

The question surrounding Allin was never whether he deserved a world championship reign. Rather, it was whether this particular moment, in this particular manner, was the right time and way to execute it. Could the creative team have built more anticipation? Could a longer, more competitive match have elevated both participants rather than diminished them? Could the story have been told more effectively across a longer timeline rather than compressed into a five-minute segment?

What made the situation particularly frustrating for critics was that the lead-in to the match had been well-executed. Allin's promo was emotionally effective, and many viewers reported being moved by his words. Sting's appearance was a perfect piece of visual storytelling. The stakes felt real when Bryan Danielson delivered Tony Khan's ultimatum. The setup was excellent. But the payoff, in the eyes of the majority of observers, simply didn't match the presentation.

Allin earned his title shot by defeating Andrade El Idolo at Dynasty the previous Sunday, making it a legitimate earned opportunity. He had been pursuing MJF since immediately after a coffin match victory over Gabe Kidd roughly a month prior, making it a storyline that felt organic and built. The pieces seemed to be in place for something special. The execution, however, fell short of expectations for a significant portion of the wrestling audience.

Whether Allin's reign will prove successful in the long term remains to be seen. He certainly has the tools to be an effective world champion. His ability to connect with audiences, his commitment to his character, and his willingness to take physical risks in and out of the ring give him credibility as a top star. However, the manner in which he captured the title has created an immediate narrative problem that he will need to overcome through strong championship defenses and continued character development.

For now, Darby Allin stands atop AEW as its world champion, having achieved what many thought would come eventually but questioned whether it should happen in this way. The celebration continued as Dynamite went off the air, but the wrestling world's reaction serves as a reminder that how a moment is executed is often just as important as the moment itself.

🔥 AEW Spring BreakThru Dynamite and Collision matches announced

AEW's Spring BreakThru event continued building storylines and announcing new matches across multiple episodes of television. Will Ospreay competed in a 17-minute match against Hechicero, the CMLL Heavyweight Champion and Don Callis Family member, on the Spring BreakThru edition of Dynamite on April 15. Despite carrying injuries from his loss to Jon Moxley at AEW Dynasty, Ospreay was cleared to compete and used his speed and quickness to his advantage throughout the match. Marina Shafir, a member of the Death Riders, watched from the crowd as Ospreay was targeted by Hechicero on his taped-up arm and shoulder. Ospreay ultimately hit the Hidden Blade to secure victory, but immediately after the match, he was attacked from behind by Mark Davis, another member of the Don Callis Family and Ospreay's former tag team partner in United Empire. The attack set up an official one-on-one match between Ospreay and Davis for April 22 Dynamite in Portland, Oregon, with the storyline being that Davis brutalized his former friend on behalf of the Don Callis Family.

Kevin Knight made his first TNT Championship defense on the April 15 Dynamite, facing Claudio Castagnoli, who was the only Death Riders member not to compete in the Casino Gauntlet Match at Dynasty that crowned Knight as the new champion. Knight had won the title by pinning former champion Daniel Garcia in that gauntlet, which also featured Anthony Bowens, Bandido, El Clon, Mike Bailey, PAC, Rush, Tommaso Ciampa, and Wheeler Yuta. The title had been vacated by Kyle Fletcher due to injury. In their match, Castagnoli looked dangerous as he went for his Giant Swing and attempted the Neutralizer off the top rope, but Knight escaped and connected with his signature UFO Splash to retain the championship. After the match, Knight gave props to Castagnoli for being one of his toughest opponents and cryptically stated he would be keeping an eye on the main event because "Jet2Belts sounds too fly," referring to the possibility of holding multiple championships.

Chris Jericho returned to action on April 15 Dynamite following his loss to Ricochet at Dynasty. Jericho spoke to Renee Paquette about his performance and said he was happy with his match despite the loss, but he didn't attribute it to ring rust. Instead, he pointed out that Ricochet had help from The Demand members Toa Liona and Bishop Kaun from Graterphobia. Ricochet came to the ring and disrespected Jericho, suggesting he should walk away while he still could and that Jericho's career would be better if he quit now. Jericho fired back by repeatedly mocking Ricochet's appearance, calling him bald and challenging him to fight one-on-one without his allies. Ricochet eventually entered the ring, and Jericho landed a hip toss and clothesline, but The Demand quickly rushed in to make it a three-on-one attack. Ricochet and his crew left Jericho laying in the ring after hitting him with another Ricosault. The segment added significant heat to their rivalry and suggests that Jericho may need to assemble a group to counter The Demand, potentially involving his former allies like Big Bill and Bryan Keith, who Jericho had been mentoring before he took time away from AEW.

The special Thursday edition of AEW Collision on April 16 featured multiple championship matches and notable contests. AEW Women's World Champion Thekla put her title on the line against Alex Windsor after Windsor interrupted Thekla's attempt to gloat about defeating Jamie Hayter at Dynasty. During their verbal exchange, Windsor revealed that she is the NJPW STRONG Women's Champion and criticized Thekla for getting fired from STARDOM in Japan. Thekla responded by saying Japan couldn't handle her and that no one in AEW could either. The match was made official when Windsor challenged Thekla to a title match, though the Sisters of Sin came out to attack Windsor before the match was announced. However, Windsor got the better of Skye Blue and Julia Hart and tried to pursue Thekla into the crowd, but the champion backed away. AEW President Tony Khan then made the match official. This would be the first singles match between the two wrestlers, as they had previously faced off in tag team and multi-person matches, including at the Casino Gauntlet match at AEW All In Texas in July 2025.

Jon Moxley defended his AEW Continental Championship against Nick Wayne in a title eliminator match on April 16 Collision. A victory for Wayne would earn him a future title shot. Wayne was a Washington native making his first AEW match since July 2025. The match gave Wayne an opportunity to prove himself against one of AEW's top champions.

The Young Bucks faced The Rascalz on April 16 Collision after the Bucks defeated Konosuke Takeshita and Kazuchika Okada at Dynasty. The Rascalz team consisted of Myron Reed and Zachary Wentz. Additionally, former AEW Women's World Champions Kris Statlander and Hikaru Shida teamed up once again on the card after picking up their first win as a team the previous week.

The Conglomeration, the newly crowned AEW Trios Champions, faced The Lethal Twist in a trios championship eliminator match on April 16 Collision. The Conglomeration, featuring Orange Cassidy, Kyle O'Reilly, and Roderick Strong, won the titles at Dynasty by defeating The Dogs, which consisted of David Finlay, Gabe Kidd, and Clark Connors. Their opponents, The Lethal Twist, featured Blake Christian, Jay Lethal, and Lee Johnson.

Other matches announced for April 16 Collision included PAC facing Lio Rush in singles action and a trios bout featuring Brody King, Mistico, and Mascara Dorada. Additionally, Adam Copeland made an appearance to address the fans, though it was unclear whether the segment would be live or pre-taped. This came days after Copeland lost to FTR at the Dynasty event.

The AEW Continental Championship eliminator match between Jon Moxley and Nick Wayne carried implications for title opportunities, as Wayne's victory would grant him a future shot at Moxley's championship. The card represented significant action for the Spring BreakThru event, establishing multiple feuds and title storylines heading into future programming.

🔥 Sting returns to AEW Dynamite Spring BreakThru

Sting made an unexpected return on the April 15, 2026 episode of AEW Dynamite Spring BreakThru, appearing backstage just moments before a major championship main event. The WWE Hall of Famer's presence wasn't advertised, and while the segment was brief, it added a sharp emotional edge to an already high-stakes night, proving once again that when The Icon shows up, it's never just another segment.

The moment unfolded backstage as Darby Allin was being interviewed ahead of his AEW World Championship match against MJF. Before Allin could fully address the camera, Sting stepped into frame, immediately shifting the tone. Rather than delivering a long speech, Sting kept things simple and direct. He approached Darby Allin backstage and told him that he already knows what to do. He then told Darby, "It's not showtime, it's your time," before repeating that this moment belongs to Allin. The line landed like a signature move, quick, impactful, and impossible to no-sell. Allin, visibly focused, acknowledged the message before heading toward the main event.

Sting's presence served as both a reminder and a passing of the torch moment, reinforcing their long-standing connection within AEW. The appearance also marked Sting's first notable on-screen involvement in some time. Since retiring from in-ring competition in 2024, he has remained under an AEW contract but largely stayed out of the spotlight. Reports noted Sting visited AEW and ROH tapings in Texas, but nothing pointed directly to this return.

In a business built on moments, this was a classic Sting reminder. Sometimes you don't need a bat or a brawl to make an impact, just the right words at the right time. Whether it changes the outcome remains to be seen, but the message was clear: the spotlight may have once been Sting's, but now it's Allin's to claim. Sting's return came at a critical moment, just before Darby Allin prepared to compete in the main event. With the stakes at their highest, Sting stepping back into the picture added another emotional layer to the night. Even though the appearance was brief, it carried major weight given Sting's history with Darby and the role he played in helping shape his rise in AEW.

Allin went on to win the title in minutes and celebrated with his mentor as the show went off air. After the conclusion of the show, the moment felt like a clear passing-of-the-torch style message, especially considering Sting's long-standing connection with Darby throughout his AEW career.

Beyond the main event focus, the evening also featured multiple appearances by members of Sting's family and other wrestlers. Steven Borden Jr., Sting's son, competed in a dark match before his father appeared on the broadcast. The action got underway early inside the Angel of the Winds Arena in Everett, Washington on Wednesday night. AEW held a pair of dark matches prior to the start of their special annual Spring BreakThru installment of AEW Dynamite.

The first of two dark matches saw Steven Borden Jr., the son of WWE Hall of Famer Sting, picking up a victory over Lobo Del Desiento, a masked wrestler with long blond hair. Borden has been following in his father's footsteps by wrestling for AEW, albeit exclusively in dark matches, since the end of last year. He has yet to suffer a defeat during his time working both singles and tag matches. Steven Borden Jr. got another win and continued to build momentum as he gains more in-ring experience.

As of yet, Borden has only appeared on AEW TV donning the Wolfpac makeup during his father's retirement match alongside Allin against the Young Bucks at Revolution 2024. Reports from observers noted that Steven Borden Jr. getting another win made sure the night started with some momentum and attention before the main show even began.

In the second of two pre-show dark matches, Hook beat Evil Uno via submission. Prior to the bout, Hook cut a heel promo, grabbing the microphone and trashing the local crowd. Hook made noise before the match even started by calling Everett a "sh*t town." Once the match began, Hook backed it up in the ring and forced Evil Uno to tap out for the submission victory. Hook's heel-style moment with his promo degrading Everett, Washington certainly made an impact alongside the other dark match performances before things switched over to the live episode of Spring BreakThru.

🔥 AEW Willow Nightingale retains TBS Championship

Kamille returned to AEW at Dynasty after a lengthy absence spanning a year and a half. She attacked TBS Champion Willow Nightingale backstage before the event, then won a quick squash match on Zero Hour. After her victory, Kamille attacked the champion once again in the ring. Willow Nightingale, willing to face the former NWA World Champion and current American Gladiators star, challenged Kamille for the TBS Championship at AEW Dynamite Spring BreakThru in Vancouver on April 15, and the match was made official.

Leading into the match, Nightingale was selling injuries from Kamille's attacks at Dynasty as well as the toll from two title defenses in the previous two weeks. During a promo to promote the match in Everett, Washington, the champion emphasized how much she had been through. The heavily taped right shoulder that Kamille had targeted became the focal point of the championship bout.

The match began with Kamille taking control in the early going, using her strength as an advantage while looking to capture the TBS Championship. Nightingale started off aggressively, bouncing Kamille's face off the turnbuckles multiple times in attempts to get her challenger off her feet. However, as the match progressed, Kamille's strategy became clear as she consistently targeted Nightingale's injured shoulder throughout the contest.

The Brickhouse continued her assault on the champion's arm, eventually locking Nightingale into an armbar submission to further injure the shoulder. Nightingale managed to counter the submission, getting Kamille's shoulders to the mat in the process. Despite her injury, the champion sent Kamille face-first into the turnbuckle once again and managed to get her down in the corner, connecting with a cannonball. However, Nightingale could not get Kamille up for her Babe with the Power Bomb finisher.

Kamille nearly secured the championship with a near fall off a face buster into a leg drop combination. As Kamille looked to put the finishing touches on the match and claim the title, Willow Nightingale made her comeback. The Babe with the Power Bomb caught Kamille in a backslide pin and picked up the victory, retaining her TBS Championship. With this loss, Kamille's win streak from her return was snapped at one, and Nightingale proved why she remains the TBS Champion despite entering the match with significant injury concerns.

🔥 AEW X NJPW Forbidden Door 2026 announced

AEW has officially locked in the details for one of professional wrestling's biggest crossover events of the year. The Mercury News first reported that AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door will take place on June 28 from the SAP Center in San Jose, California. AEW confirmed the announcement on April 15, revealing that the fifth annual Forbidden Door event will feature the brightest stars from AEW, New Japan Pro Wrestling, CMLL, and STARDOM.

The return to San Jose marks a significant shift in scheduling for the annual interpromotional showcase. The event had previously moved to August in 2025, but fans speculated it would return to its traditional June slot, when the first three Forbidden Door events were held. That assumption proved correct with the announcement. Premium ticket sales will begin on April 21 for registered AEW Insiders, while the official ticket sale opens to the general public on April 27 at 10 AM PT.

The promotional poster for the event features logos of all four involved promotions, with several dual AEW/CMLL contracted wrestlers prominently displayed, including Mistico, CMLL World Heavyweight Champion Hechicero, and CMLL World Women's Champion Persephone. While no Stardom talent was shown on the initial poster, the inclusion of the company's logo signals a strengthened commitment to featuring wrestlers from all four promotions.

However, the June 28 date presents an interesting wrinkle for the wrestling landscape. Forbidden Door will air simultaneously with TNA Slammiversary, which is also scheduled for Sunday, June 28 in Boston, Massachusetts. This marks a rare same-night clash between two major wrestling promotions. The scheduling conflict adds another layer to recent tensions between AEW and TNA. The two companies found themselves at odds just two weeks prior when Warner Brothers Discovery moved AEW Collision to Thursday nights, putting it directly against TNA Impact. TNA President Carlos Silva was reportedly angered by this action, and it is believed to have contributed to a ban on TNA talent working with AEW talent, a decision that affected several upcoming matches.

AEW CEO, GM and Head of Creative Tony Khan expressed enthusiasm about the event's significance. "AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door has become one of professional wrestling's biggest events of the year thanks to our incredible partnerships with New Japan Pro Wrestling, CMLL and STARDOM, and we look forward to fans in attendance and those watching around the world being a part of history," Khan stated.

New Japan Pro Wrestling President Hiroshi Tanahashi echoed the excitement while emphasizing a competitive spirit heading into this year's show. "This year sees the fifth AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door and whenever New Japan Pro Wrestling and All Elite Wrestling collide, we see great chemistry between the wrestlers and many memorable moments," Tanahashi said. "This year, NJPW will be playing for keeps to win more than ever. Our roster is fired up to show just how exciting New Japan Pro Wrestling is, and fans can expect a sensational event."

One wrestling observer noted that while the 2024 and 2025 iterations of Forbidden Door were very good, possibly even great, they did not capture the same feel as the 2022 and 2023 events. The 2024 show was headlined by an all-AEW event, while much of the 2025 card featured AEW versus AEW matches, which could be seen on any standard AEW pay-per-view. The move back to June and the strengthened involvement of CMLL and Stardom should help rectify this issue and give the event more of a true cross-promotional extravaganza feel. The expectation is that AEW, New Japan, CMLL, and Stardom will work to capture the spirit of the earlier Forbidden Door events and move away from the template of recent years.

Additional information regarding AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door will be announced in the near future.

Tony Khan on AEW creative direction in 2026

Tony Khan, speaking ahead of AEW's Dynamite Spring BreakThru show at Angel of the Wind Arena in Everett, Washington, framed the event not just as another television taping but as part of what he sees as significant momentum carryover from what he repeatedly described as a banner 2025 for the promotion. He pushed the show as the one-year anniversary of Dynamite becoming, in his words, "the longest running prime time pro wrestling show in the history of TBS and TNT," and said the Everett card would feature multiple championship matches topped by MJF defending the World title against Darby Allin in a rematch of one of the company's foundational rivalries.

Khan was especially strong in putting over the idea of Allin vs. MJF as an "original AEW" matchup with added local significance because Allin is the hometown favorite. He described Darby as being on a "phenomenal winning streak" and said the match was "gonna be a lot of fun," presenting it as a big match title defense rather than a routine TV main event. On the undercard, Khan also heavily emphasized Kevin Knight vs. Claudio Castagnoli for the TNT title, noting that Knight had just won the championship at Dynasty and that Claudio had been "loaded up, ready to fight." Khan also leaned into Castagnoli's resume, calling him one of AEW's top stars and a former CMLL World champion and AEW Trios champion, while stressing the kind of unpredictability AEW has tried to establish around title reigns. He also plugged Willow Nightingale's return, tying it into Kamille's comeback angle and noting that Kamille had been away filming American Gladiators. Khan referred to Willow as "the face of TBS" and essentially pitched that match segment as another major hook for the Everett show.

The more interesting part of the interview came when Khan discussed philosophy and his approach to creative direction. He went back to the fact that he was fantasy booking long before AEW existed, saying he started writing a show called Saturday Night Dynamite in 1995 when he was 12 years old, posting it on message boards, on dial-up Internet, and sending it to friends. He clearly still sees a straight line from that kid booking imaginary shows to the actual AEW schedule that includes Dynamite and Collision.

Khan made the familiar distinction that writing wrestling as a fan and running a company are completely different things, comparing it to the difference between running a fantasy football team and running an actual football team. His point was that ideas and instincts matter, but working with real people, managing personalities and evaluating how somebody fits into the locker room is where the real job begins. That flowed into his comments on talent evaluation where he said "AEW is where the best wrestle," but also made clear that ring work alone is not the only criterion.
