🔥 Mercedes Mone APAC Championship Vacation

Mercedes Mone, once known as "13 Belts Mone" for holding multiple championships simultaneously, announced the vacation of her last remaining active title through her Mone Mag newsletter. The announcement came while she was in London and marked the end of her 140-day reign as APAC Women's Champion, a title she won by defeating Nor Phoenix Diana.

In her statement, Mone explained the difficult decision behind the vacation. She wrote that while she and her team made genuine efforts to make the arrangement work, APAC Wrestling ultimately told them they did not have the budget to fly her out to Malaysia and were not willing to work with other promotions to find alternative solutions. Mone emphasized that this situation did not align with her vision and values as a performer. She reflected on the broader challenges of the wrestling business, stating that not every partnership works out as hoped, but that she has learned to stand firm in her worth, her time, and what she brings to the table. In her characteristic style, she concluded by saying "Trust me… I'm always moving forward," framing the decision as part of her continued growth and evolution.

However, Mone's version of events prompted an immediate and detailed response from APAC Wrestling founder Ayez Shaukat. Waking up to a flood of messages and questions about the situation, Shaukat took to social media to provide his account of what actually transpired. He began by expressing his respect for Mone as a global star and acknowledged that her schedule and travel limitations are real concerns. He stated that he did not blame her for the situation and appreciated the opportunity to work together.

Shaukat's core contention was that the public narrative being pushed did not fully reflect what actually happened. He insisted that APAC Wrestling did attempt to find solutions and actively explored working with other promotions precisely because that was what was requested of them. He personally reached out to multiple organizations, followed up, and tried to make various scenarios work. Shaukat specifically emphasized a crucial point: he did not ask other promotions to fly his wrestler out for APAC. He claimed to have text and email receipts to back up this assertion and stated he was prepared to stand on that truth if necessary, though he preferred to handle business professionally and had chosen not to publicly post private communications lightly.

Shaukat detailed his efforts to find solutions on multiple fronts. He explained that he was willing to go beyond APAC's financial limits by offering to take on extra work and secure sponsors to bring Mone to Malaysia. When that ultimately proved unworkable, which he said he understood, they pivoted to exploring solutions abroad instead. He mentioned trying to make something work with Arek in Poland and Hal in France, both of whom he praised as respectful and professional individuals who genuinely tried to find real solutions. However, when looking at the full picture, the arrangements simply did not make business sense financially for APAC Wrestling.

Regarding Winnipeg Pro Wrestling specifically, Shaukat stated that their interaction was minimal. He reached out, was initially ignored, and only received a single response declining based on budget and their story direction. Shaukat said he respected that decision and that this was the extent of their communication, not the more extensive back-and-forth that the public narrative suggested.

Shaukat also provided context about APAC Wrestling's financial realities. The promotion operates without major funding or sponsorships and often runs its shows at a loss, making the costs of international travel and logistics extremely difficult to manage. These constraints ultimately prevented them from bringing Mone to Malaysia, which in turn affected their plans for Nor Phoenix Diana, who was left without the in-ring closure that had been originally intended for her in the title program.

While Shaukat did not want to tear anyone down or create additional drama, he felt compelled to protect his name and reputation when the public narrative did not reflect the actual communications and efforts that took place. He addressed Mone directly, offering a sincere apology for any public discomfort the situation caused her. He reiterated that he still has respect for her and acknowledged that she was likely speaking from what had been communicated to her.

The situation reflects the complex realities of independent wrestling promotions trying to work with high-profile talent. Mone has been on hiatus from All Elite Wrestling television since losing the TBS Championship to Willow Nightingale at the end of 2025. During this time away from AEW programming, she has been losing her independent championships one by one. However, she recently appeared on an Italian TV station and teased that her return to AEW was coming very soon, indicating she would be back in action with the major promotion soon.

The APAC Championship vacation represents a symbolic shift for the wrestler who had built her brand partly on accumulating multiple titles across different promotions. Now holding zero physical championships, Mone's focus appears to be on her upcoming return to AEW and her continued European tour activities. The dispute, while handled with relative professionalism by both parties, illustrates the ongoing challenges independent promotions face in securing international talent and the importance of clear communication when complex logistics are involved in professional wrestling agreements.

🔥 Chris Jericho AEW Return

Pro wrestling legend Chris Jericho made a surprise return to AEW during the April 1 episode of Dynamite, which took place in his hometown of Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada. His appearance was kept under wraps before the broadcast, following months of speculation about a possible move to WWE. The return came as a shock to fans and marked Jericho's first appearance in All Elite Wrestling after a year-long hiatus.

Jericho's return was notably dramatic in its execution. He interrupted what was scheduled to be an MJF vs. Kenny Omega AEW World Title Dynasty contract signing, immediately putting him back into a prominent position within the company's main event picture. The timing of his arrival was incredibly tight, with his return being communicated to Warner Bros. Discovery only shortly before Dynamite went live. Jericho arrived just minutes before the show started, demonstrating the level of secrecy surrounding his comeback.

According to reporting from Dave Meltzer in the Wrestling Observer Newsletter, Jericho is expected to stay with AEW for the long term. More significantly, his potential return to WWE is currently considered "dead." WWE's primary interest in Jericho was reportedly for a retirement tour and farewell appearance rather than an active, ongoing role. At one point, WWE had reportedly planned for 2026 to revolve around retirements, including those of AJ Styles and Brock Lesnar, with Jericho considered as part of that plan. There were brief on-screen teases involving Adam Pearce and CM Punk that hinted at a potential Jericho return to WWE, but no follow-up occurred.

Although Jericho was linked to both companies as recently as the week before his AEW return, a WWE source informed the Wrestling Observer Newsletter that Jericho would not be joining WWE anytime soon, and possibly never. This effectively ends the speculation that had dominated wrestling news coverage for months leading up to his return.

Regarding his contract situation, Jericho's original contract had expired at the end of 2025, but AEW President Tony Khan extended it to account for the time Jericho had missed for various reasons. This extension ensures his long-term presence with the promotion.

As of now, there has been no official announcement regarding Jericho's next storyline direction. Details about his role following his return remain unclear, with no confirmed matches or segments beyond his initial appearance. However, several potential first opponents have been speculated within the wrestling community.

Kenny Omega represents one intriguing possibility given that Jericho interrupted the MJF vs. Kenny Omega segment upon his return. This could be a red herring or could allude to what will be his first rivalry back in All Elite Wrestling. The history between Jericho and Omega is significant, dating back to AEW Double Or Nothing 2019, when Jericho defeated Omega to earn the right to become the first AEW World Champion. The match was actually a rematch from NJPW Wrestle Kingdom 12, where Omega had previously defeated Jericho. Seven years later, Jericho could rekindle this rivalry with both men having the chance to prove who is the better man once and for all.

Jack Perry is another potential opponent worth considering. Perry was one of Jericho's first rivals during the early days of AEW. Back in December 2019, Jericho and Perry faced off in a 10-minute time limit match that saw Perry, then known as Jungle Boy, take the then-AEW World Champion to the limit. Now all these years later, Perry has grown significantly, becoming a former TNT Champion, AEW World Tag Team Champion, and the current National Champion. Jericho's return could see him wanting to pursue gold and potentially put the National Title on the map by taking the championship from Perry.

The prospect of Jericho feuding with one of the many talented names on the AEW roster remains interesting to anticipate, whether such a feud plays out on Dynamite, Collision, or at a pay-per-view event. While online discussions led up to his return, they did not significantly impact ticket sales for the Dynamite episode.

MJF Indie Appearance

AEW World Champion MJF made a shocking surprise appearance at WrestlePro's 10th anniversary show on Saturday, April 4 in Rahway, New Jersey. The unannounced arrival came immediately following the main event of the promotion's milestone celebration event. MJF surfaced as WrestlePro owner and founder Pat Buck was addressing the crowd and thanking fans for their support throughout the company's decade of operation.

The moment quickly turned chaotic when MJF interrupted Buck's remarks and struck him with a low blow, creating an unexpected angle that electrified those in attendance. The attack represented a shocking turn of events during what had been a celebratory evening for the independent promotion. WrestlePro documented the incident on social media, stating: "After the conclusion of our main event, owner and founder Pat Buck was thanking the fans when AEW World Champion MJF interrupted and struck Pat below the belt."

Capitalizing on the chaos, WrestlePro immediately announced a match between the two men. The promotion declared: "So now, on Sunday May 31st it is Pat Buck vs AEW World Champion MJF!!!" The bout is scheduled to take place when WrestlePro returns to Rahway, New Jersey on May 31, setting the stage for a rare indie appearance from the reigning AEW World Champion.

The appearance is particularly notable given MJF's status as AEW's top champion and his ongoing professional relationship with Buck, who serves as AEW's Vice President of Talent Development. MJF trained under Buck and Brian Myers at the Create-A-Pro Academy, making the indie booking significant from both a storyline and professional standpoint.

MJF's indie schedule includes additional bookings beyond the WrestlePro match. He is scheduled to compete at Create-A-Pro Wrestling's event on May 1, where he will wrestle Nic Nemeth. Additionally, MJF must fulfill his AEW championship obligations when he defends the AEW World Championship against Kenny Omega on April 12 in Vancouver at Dynasty.

Thunder Rosa Dual Contract CMLL

Thunder Rosa has officially signed a dual contract with CMLL and All Elite Wrestling, marking a significant development in her career. The announcement was made at a CMLL event on Friday, April 3rd at Arena Mexico, where Rosa appeared in action and the promotion made the official declaration on social media.

On the night of the announcement, Thunder Rosa competed in trios action with Marcela and India Sioux against Persephone, Princesa Sugehit, and Olympia. Rosa earned the victory for her team in the deciding third fall by submitting Olympia with the Koji Clutch. Following the match, Rosa called out Salvador Lutteroth, CMLL's general manager, for an answer regarding her status with the promotion. Lutteroth responded by stating that it would be an honor to welcome Thunder to CMLL, and the organization subsequently announced the dual contract agreement officially.

This development was widely anticipated, as Rosa had recently expressed her desire to hold a dual contract with both AEW and CMLL. According to reports, the deal for Rosa to be under a dual contract was nearly finalized, with sources expecting her to work with CMLL for the rest of the year while maintaining her AEW obligations.

Prior to the official announcement, Thunder spoke with The Takedown on SI about the possibility of signing with CMLL. She described it as a big deal and an honor to be recognized for inclusion in such a prestigious organization. Rosa views this opportunity as opening a new chapter in her career and her life, stating that she feels more confident and committed to giving her best effort both inside and outside the ring.

Thunder is particularly excited about the learning experience that working in CMLL will provide. She acknowledged the different style of work required, noting that while she is accustomed to working for television in AEW, lucha libre presents a distinct challenge. Working live with some of the best women wrestlers in the world without a television format, she explained, will be a valuable learning opportunity. She emphasized her philosophy of approaching everything as a learning experience and expressed confidence in her ability to adapt and thrive in this new environment.

With this new contract, Thunder Rosa joins an exclusive group of wrestlers who maintain dual agreements between AEW and CMLL. Claudio Castagnoli is identified as the only other member of the AEW roster with dual CMLL contracts. From the CMLL side, wrestlers including Mistico, Hechicero, Mascara Dorada, and Persephone also maintain dual contracts with AEW.

Rosa's current AEW contract is expected to expire sometime in 2027, though the exact date remains unknown. Her last AEW competition occurred on the March 4th episode of AEW Dynamite, where she faced Thekla for the AEW Women's World Championship. Thekla won that match and retained her title. With the signing of her dual contract, Rosa will now balance her commitments between both organizations while continuing her professional wrestling career at an elevated level of competition and opportunity.

Thekla Contract Extension

AEW already locked down Women's World Champion Thekla with a new multi-year contract, but a new update reveals just how serious the company was about keeping her and how close things could have gotten if timing were different.

According to reports, Thekla used professional representation to negotiate her new deal with AEW, and that move paid off in a big way. The updated details indicate the agreement came with a substantial raise, showing AEW was willing to invest heavily to keep one of the top names in its women's division locked in long-term.

Those representing Thekla believed there would have been real interest from WWE if she had reached free agency. However, AEW moved so quickly to finalize the new contract that those potential talks never even had the chance to begin. The timing appears to be the key part of the story. AEW did not wait until her original deal was close to expiring. Instead, they worked to secure her future early, preventing any bidding war or outside interest from becoming a factor.

That aggressive approach makes more sense when looking at Thekla's recent run. Since arriving in AEW after leaving STARDOM in 2025, she has steadily climbed the ranks and eventually captured the AEW Women's World Championship in a strap match victory over Kris Statlander earlier this year. With the championship around her waist and strong momentum behind her, AEW clearly saw enough value to not only extend her deal but to do so with a raise and before any rival company could make a move.

If WWE had shown interest, things could have looked very different. Instead, AEW secured its champion early and avoided the kind of contract drama that often surrounds major free agents in wrestling. AEW making this move early sends a clear message that they view Thekla as a major piece of the company's future, not just another champion passing through the division.

MJF Claims AEW is Only Company on Fire

MJF isn't interested in wrestling fans branching out to explore different promotions. The AEW World Champion made it clear on Twitter that he believes there's only one place viewers should be watching, and that's AEW.

The champion jumped into a discussion after a fan suggested wrestling fans should stop limiting themselves and start exploring different promotions around the world, especially given how strong 2026 has been across the entire industry. The fan framed the message as a bigger-picture perspective, encouraging people to look beyond just one or two companies for their wrestling entertainment.

"There is an abundance of GREAT wrestling out there. If you're bored, explore. 2026 has been an incredible year with mind-blowing stuff on multiple continents. Why limit yourself to one or two companies if you're jaded or tired with what they offer? Be curious, hungry," the fan wrote.

MJF rejected the notion immediately and decisively. Rather than engaging with the nuanced point about exploring the wider wrestling landscape, the champion pushed back strongly and made his position crystal clear.

"No Andy. We aren't gonna play this game. There's only one company to watch that's truly on fire right now. It's AEW on TBS/TNT/HBOMAX," MJF responded.

The timing of his response carries particular significance in the current wrestling landscape. WWE is currently dealing with substantial backlash following the April 3 episode of SmackDown. The show featured the reveal of Pat McAfee as Randy Orton's ally in his ongoing feud with Cody Rhodes, a decision that didn't resonate well with a significant portion of the audience. Many fans and critics felt the move undermined the carefully built history between Orton and Rhodes by inserting a celebrity figure into a rivalry based on long-term storytelling.

The episode's viewership reflected the negative reception. The show ended up with a 0.25 rating on Cagematch, marking the lowest rating in SmackDown history on that particular platform. Analysis and criticism centered on the decision to bring a celebrity into a feud that had been constructed around deep personal and historical connections between the competitors.

Meanwhile, AEW has been moving in the opposite direction. The company has been trending upward in both quality perception and viewership metrics. AEW Dynamite's April 1 episode drew 730,000 viewers on TBS with a 0.15 rating in the key demographic. Recent booking decisions have been generating increasingly stronger reactions from audiences, particularly following the successful AEW Revolution event.

MJF's comment appears to function on multiple levels simultaneously. While it certainly works as character work consistent with his persona as an arrogant champion promoting his own company, it also reads as a pointed jab in the midst of an ongoing industry debate about which promotion is delivering compelling content in 2026. With fan frustration toward WWE rising noticeably and AEW gaining traction both critically and commercially, the timing and substance of his statement carry real weight beyond typical promotional banter.

As WrestleMania 42 approaches and opinions grow increasingly vocal within the wrestling community, comments like MJF's are only adding more fuel to the broader conversation about which company is truly delivering quality wrestling content right now.

AEW Stars to Sign with WWE NXT

AEW Stars To Sign With WWE NXT Soon

Over the past two years, WWE NXT has added quite a few stars who made their name in All Elite Wrestling. Whether it be Ethan Page, Ricky Saints or Blake Monroe, there could be even more former AEW stars who become prominently featured acts on the NXT brand. Looking at the AEW roster, the future could see former champions and popular names making their way over to WWE NXT.

The following are six AEW stars who could potentially sign with WWE NXT soon.

ANNA JAY

Anna Jay was last seen on AEW television in November 2025 when she was part of the AEW Women's World Tag Team Championship tournament. Since her absence from the company's programming, there has been some news in regards to Jay's future with All Elite Wrestling. Recent reports have indicated that Anna Jay's contract is set to expire sometime in 2026 and that she had some interest from WWE. With the company being interested, Jay would be a fitting acquisition for WWE and have a role to play in the NXT women's division moving forward.

KAMILLE

Another AEW star who has been absent from the company's TV over the past year is Kamille. Signing with All Elite Wrestling in 2024, the former NWA Women's World Champion came in with some lofty expectations. However, it is safe to say that Kamille has not quite lived up to that and has seen her kept off of AEW television since November 2024. Kamille leaving AEW almost seems inevitable at this point and could see her making the move to WWE NXT where her size and strength might be better utilized as well as highlighted.

WARDLOW

Making his AEW debut in 2019, Wardlow was seen as a must watch prospect and aligned himself with Maxwell Jacob Friedman. After several years of being by MJF's side, Wardlow finally broke free, defeated his former boss and went on to become a three-time TNT Champion. However, Wardlow was never able to reach his full potential due to start-and-stop pushes and a series of injuries. Despite currently being sidelined with an injury, Wardlow feels like someone who will eventually sign with WWE and could become a major singles talent on the NXT brand.
