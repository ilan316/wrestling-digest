🔥 Hangman Adam Page AEW Return

"Hangman" Adam Page is back in AEW. The former AEW World Champion made his return to television during the opening segment of the July 11 episode of AEW Collision, marking his first appearance in four months. The return was especially significant as it took place in his home state of Virginia. Page had been out of action since his Texas Death Match against MJF for the AEW World Championship at Revolution in March.

During his return promo, Page reaffirmed the vow he made following that loss, making it clear that he has no intention of challenging for the company's top prize again. "I wanted to let you know that I intend to be the man of my word," Page said. "I will never challenge for the AEW Men's World Championship again." The stipulation tied to his match with MJF at Revolution means this is a commitment Page cannot break without losing credibility.

Page began his promo by discussing his time away from wrestling, talking about spending his time gardening. He spoke about returning to plant one more seed, even though he had no idea whether it would grow. This metaphor about planting seeds appeared to foreshadow his new direction in the company. The conversation quickly shifted to his failed title challenge against MJF in March.

Page addressed the existential question his loss has created for him. For seven years, his entire AEW career has been built around chasing, winning, and holding the World Championship. Now that door is permanently closed. "For seven years, for seven years, I have defined myself with the one goal that I held above all others, the one goal that I held above all others: to chase, to win and to hold the World Championship. But now I am faced with the question, Who is Hangman without the World Championship? And the honest truth is that I don't know, but we are damn sure going to find out, aren't we?" This question represents a fundamental shift in Page's character and motivation.

While ruling himself out of the AEW World Championship picture, Page made it clear that his career is far from over. He noted that he still has plenty left to accomplish in AEW and mentioned the possibility of pursuing other championships, including the TNT Championship, National Championship, Continental Championship, and International Championship. Page also praised Kenny Omega for winning the AEW World Championship and spoke about his longtime friend wanting to finish the race.

Page specifically mentioned current TNT Champion Kevin Knight among those he would pursue, calling him a "current dipshit" and making it clear that friendship will not stop him from coming after another belt. This suggests immediate storyline potential between the two former partners. Kenny Omega recently agreed to a similar stipulation against MJF but succeeded in defeating MJF for the World title on Dynamite, accomplishing what Page was unable to do.

Page's return gives AEW a different version of Hangman. He is no longer the anxious cowboy trying to prove he belongs at the top. Now he has to rebuild his identity without the one prize that controlled nearly every major chapter of his career. The AEW World Championship may be off limits, but Page still has plenty of gold to chase and several champions now have a major problem standing in their way.

🔥 Kenny Omega AEW World Championship Win and Celebration

Kenny Omega Captures Second AEW World Championship in Major Title Victory

Kenny Omega has reclaimed the AEW World Championship, defeating MJF in the main event of the July 8 Beach Break edition of AEW Dynamite. This marks Omega's second reign with the promotion's world title, with his previous championship run occurring in 2021. The victory comes with significant stakes, as the match featured a stipulation where a loss would have resulted in Omega being permanently banned from ever challenging for the AEW World Championship again for the rest of his career.

Following his championship victory, Omega's next major challenge has already been secured. He will defend his newly won AEW World Championship against Will Ospreay in the main event of AEW All In, which takes place at Wembley Stadium in London in August. This high-profile matchup represents one of the company's biggest international events on the calendar.

Celebration Segment Announced for July 15 Dynamite

To capitalize on Omega's title victory momentum, AEW has announced a Kenny Omega AEW World Championship Celebration segment for the July 15 episode of AEW Dynamite taking place in Boston, Massachusetts at the MGM Music Hall. The announcement was made during the July 11 episode of AEW Collision. This celebration will air live on TBS and HBO Max, giving fans the opportunity to see the newly crowned champion acknowledge his historic victory.

The July 15 Dynamite card has been bolstered with additional matches beyond the Omega celebration. A significant trios match has been added featuring Willow Nightingale, Maya World & Hyan competing against Mercedes Moné and Divine Dominion, consisting of Megan Bayne and Lena Kross. This matchup is particularly noteworthy as it will take place in Moné's hometown of Boston and serves as a precursor to Willow's potential involvement in matches at both AEW All In and AEW Redemption. The card also features Andrade El Idolo facing Jake Doyle in a match where a victory for Andrade would earn him a shot at the AEW National Championship currently held by Mark Davis of The Don Callis Family.

Hangman Page Returns With New Merchandise

Following Hangman Page's first appearance in months during the July 11 episode of AEW Collision, merchandise company ShopAEW has already begun promoting a new "Hangman Rides Again" collection. The quick promotional response suggests that Page's return has been positioned as a significant moment for the company, with merchandise capitalizing on fan enthusiasm for the veteran wrestler's comeback.

Backstage Details on Match Relocation

According to reporting in the July 13 edition of the Wrestling Observer Newsletter, the Kenny Omega versus MJF championship match was originally scheduled to headline AEW Redemption on July 26 in Montreal. The event name and location were specifically chosen to provide thematic significance to Omega's story, as the match represented his final opportunity to win the championship in his home country of Canada following two high-profile losses he had previously suffered.

However, plans changed and the title match was moved from the pay-per-view card to the weekly television program Beach Break. The shift was believed to have been pushed by MJF, though no specific explanation was provided for the reasoning behind the decision. Tony Khan ultimately approved the change despite its significance as a major alteration to the company's original planning. Observers note that Khan must feel confident about having compelling pay-per-view worthy content planned for the now-vacant main event slot at AEW Redemption on July 26.

AEW Redemption Scheduled for Montreal

AEW Redemption is confirmed to take place on July 26 at the Bell Centre in Montreal, Quebec. The show's Buy In portion will feature a celebration of the 80-year history of the Rougeau Wrestling Dynasty, honoring one of the most prolific wrestling families in the history of Montreal. The main card currently has one officially confirmed match, with AEW Women's World Champion Thekla defending her title against Willow Nightingale, though additional matches are expected to be announced as the event date approaches.
