🔥 Mick Foley Leaves WWE, Joins AEW

Mick Foley, the WWE Hall of Famer and hardcore legend, has officially joined All Elite Wrestling after more than 30 years of association with WWE. His departure marks a significant moment in professional wrestling, driven by political and personal conviction rather than traditional career considerations.

Foley's decision to leave WWE stemmed directly from the company's relationship with President Donald Trump. The final straw came when Trump made what Foley described as heartless and cruel comments about the death of filmmaker Rob Reiner, apparently finding joy in how the man died and connecting it to Reiner's dislike of Trump. Foley explained his reasoning using the metaphor of a ball-peen hammer on a windshield, suggesting that while individual incidents are like individual taps, the cumulative effect creates the crack. For him, Trump's comments about Rob Reiner represented that final tap that broke the windshield.

Beyond that specific incident, Foley felt uncomfortable with WWE's broader association with Trump. He noted that when five different WWE personalities posed in the Oval Office after receiving Stone Cold Stunners, it represented an uncomfortable coziness between the company and the president. Despite holding only a Legends deal rather than an active employment contract, Foley felt complicit in his silence. He reached out to WWE's head of talent relations, whom he considers a friend and still sends handwritten Santa letters to annually, and informed the company of his decision. His Legends contract ran out at the end of 2025.

Foley acknowledged that his decision came with significant financial sacrifice. He gave up two lucrative positions during WrestleMania week with WWE, positions that paid extremely well for minimal work. By stepping away, he realized he would need to work 24 hours on his own to make what he could have earned in six hours with WWE. However, he stated he was okay with that tradeoff. Foley emphasized that he made the move he felt was right for him personally, that he still loves WWE as a company, and that he will not disparage them. Rather, it simply did not feel like a good fit, and it did not allow him to look at himself in the mirror before bed.

When asked about the possibility of returning to WWE in the future, Foley left the door slightly open but with a clear condition. He said he did not close the door forever, but "as long as" Trump is in office, he will remain away from WWE. He expressed his belief that more people are beginning to realize that Trump engages in cruelty for its own sake and for self-enrichment. While emphasizing he did not want to discuss politics extensively, Foley felt compelled to speak about why he left WWE and encouraged others not to wait too long to do what they feel is right. He expressed hope, perhaps naively, that even if the president's ratings dipped into the teens, it might force him to make changes, and that perhaps appeals could be made to better angels of his nature if they exist.

Foley officially joined AEW as a member of the broadcast team, making his debut at Double or Nothing on May 24, 2026, appearing on the pre-show. His first televised moment in AEW involved taking a low blow from Maxwell Jacob Friedman, the three-time AEW World Champion and fellow Long Island native. MJF had pitched a six-week television angle to Foley before the latter had called off plans for a retirement match he had wanted to have when he turned 60.

In his new role, Foley described himself as the "Swiss Army Knife" of AEW, indicating his willingness to work wherever needed. He mentioned several potential responsibilities including hosting shows alongside Renee, working on publicity by being sent out early to drum up interest in events, and taking on managerial roles for select talent. His primary mission appears to be helping wrestlers in the middle of the card get closer to the top of the card, essentially serving as a mentor and advocate for emerging talent.

When comparing AEW and WWE, Foley identified one area where he believes AEW does a significantly better job: the midcard. In his view, AEW treats its midcard with much more pride and importance than WWE does. He criticized WWE's historical approach of building tag teams for years, having them win the championship multiple times, and then having two unestablished big stars face them and decisively defeat them, sending the message that the tag team was never important to begin with. Foley noted that he does not see that type of destructive booking in AEW, and it was one of the things that drew him to the company.

Foley also praised Tony Khan's management style at AEW. He described Khan as "joyous" in his position, filled with genuine happiness and enthusiasm. Khan's first instinct is to ask people if they are okay when talent comes through, and he actively jumps up from his seat to greet and check on performers and staff. Foley contrasted this with Vince McMahon's approach, noting that while McMahon had his own valid style, he did not frequently offer praise and did not make the effort to get up from his seat to meet with talent personally. As someone who has always responded better to pats on the back than kicks in the butt, Foley admitted he would have greatly enjoyed Khan's approach during his active wrestling career. While acknowledging there is no single formula for success in any field, from wrestling to producing pay-per-views, Foley found Khan's style more personally appealing.

The question of whether Foley might actually wrestle again has intrigued the wrestling community. Foley has not definitively ruled it out. He recently lost 100 pounds, which has significantly improved his quality of life. He noted that he used to have to stop several times when walking up moderate hills due to winded-ness, but now he hardly notices he is walking uphill. This physical improvement gives him renewed confidence about his capabilities.

However, Foley is realistic about the limitations any return would face. If he were to have a match, it would absolutely have to avoid any trauma to the head, given the years of accumulated head injuries from his hardcore career. He made a self-deprecating joke that while there is much of him to avoid traumatizing below the neck, his head is off-limits. He also mentioned that he had harbored a long-term goal of dropping an elbow off a TV truck onto a parked car but reconsidered after serious reflection on how his hip replacement and knee replacement had improved his life. He doubted there were any statistics on former knee replacement recipients safely dropping elbows off TV trucks and felt the 50-50 risk-reward proposition was not worth potentially undoing the good health benefits he has gained.

Despite these concerns, Foley did not completely rule out wrestling again. He stated that he believes he has one last good match in him, but all the stars would have to align. He would need to come up with enough creative moves that lower risk while still delivering an entertaining match for fans. He also emphasized that he would need to feel genuinely up to it physically and mentally. He suggested that perhaps a couple of years down the road, wrestling might be a possibility if circumstances work out. He concluded by noting that stranger things have happened in professional wrestling.

🔥 Tanea Brooks Rebel ALS Ambassador

Tanea Brooks, better known to wrestling fans as Rebel, has announced a significant new chapter in her life following her recent ALS diagnosis. The wrestling veteran revealed that she has joined I AM ALS, a patient-led advocacy organization, as an official ambassador. Through this role, she aims to raise awareness and push for meaningful change in ALS research and treatment.

Rebel publicly disclosed her ALS diagnosis in April after a lengthy and frustrating medical journey. Doctors at the Mayo Clinic identified amyotrophic lateral sclerosis while she was preparing for an unrelated lung surgery. She has since shared that the disease has progressively affected her ability to walk and speak. Despite the terminal nature of the diagnosis, Brooks made it clear that she intends to keep fighting, just in a different arena.

In her announcement, Brooks stated: "I am Tanea Brooks. I am Rebel. Now, I AM ALS. ALS may be a terminal diagnosis, but I'm showing up to fight as an Ambassador for I AM ALS. This patient-led movement is stepping into the ring every day to drive real progress and passing laws like the ACT for ALS for research and treatments. I'm proud to join I AM ALS and I'm asking you to step into the ring with us. Please take action."

According to wrestling records, a total of 287 title changes have taken place on June 3rd across promotions around the world. Here are some of the most important championship moments tied to this date throughout wrestling history.

The earliest documented title changes on June 3rd occurred in the 1960s and 1970s. Billy Red Lyons and The Destroyer captured the All Asia Tag Team Titles in 1965. Six years later, in 1971, Crazy Luke Graham and Tarzan Tyler claimed the WWWF World Tag Team Titles, establishing themselves as major tag team competitors during that era.

The year 1983 saw significant activity on June 3rd within championship circles. The Mexican National Middleweight Title was declared vacant during this time, while the NWA World Middleweight Title was won by Lizmark, marking an important moment in his career.

WCW Main Event 1991 took place on June 3rd, proving to be a pivotal show for multiple championship reigns. Badstreet, Jimmy Garvin, and Michael Hayes won the WCW World 6-Man Tag Team Titles at the event. Steve Austin also captured the WCW World Television Title at the same show, years before he reinvented himself as Stone Cold and became one of the biggest stars the business has ever produced.

The 2000s brought additional significant championship moments on this date. In 2002, Bradshaw added the WWE Hardcore Title to his résumé. Shane Hagadorn won the ROH Top of the Class Trophy in 2006. Bobby Lashley captured the ECW World Title in 2007, continuing his rise as a major player in professional wrestling.

All Japan Pro Wrestling saw two of its championships vacated on June 3rd, 2011. Both the AJPW World Junior Heavyweight Title and the AJPW World Tag Team Titles were declared vacant on the same date, representing significant changes for the promotion.

More recent title changes on June 3rd have continued across the wrestling world. In 2018, Jeff Jarrett won the AAA Mega Title in Mexico, adding another championship to his extensive collection. In 2019, Samoa Joe claimed the WWE United States Title, strengthening his position on the WWE roster. Most recently, in 2025, Daiki Inaba and Manabu Soya teamed to win the GHC Heavyweight Tag Team Titles in Pro Wrestling NOAH, continuing the tradition of significant title changes on this date.

Volador Jr. Reveals He's Received Offers To Leave CMLL: "It Just Doesn't Catch My Attention"

During an interview with TVC Deportes, the veteran luchador revealed that while there have been overtures made to him to depart CMLL, he has no intention of leaving the Mexican wrestling promotion where he has spent his entire career since 2000.

"I'm not going to lie to you: there have been offers, but it just doesn't catch my attention," Volador Jr. stated. "The truth is that I have always been faithful to the CMLL, I have always belonged here and I was born here, I want to be here and I like the wrestling style of CMLL, so I think that in another company it would not be the same even if they offered me double or triple."

The luchador emphasized his emotional and professional connection to the promotion, explaining that monetary incentives alone would not sway his decision to leave. Volador Jr. expressed genuine satisfaction with his current situation at CMLL, praising the company's commitment to developing young talent through its training infrastructure.

"I'm really very happy to be in this great company, where the wrestlers are honed in and there are wrestling schools," he continued. "There are wrestling cauldrons within CMLL and they are doing a lot little by little, specially with the Torneo de Escuelas, as the rest have been growing and not all companies have that possibility of having the type of schools as we have."

Volador Jr.'s comments reflect his deep roots in CMLL and his appreciation for the organization's emphasis on training and developing the next generation of wrestlers. His statement comes at a time when various wrestlers have explored opportunities outside of traditional promotions, making his renewed commitment to CMLL noteworthy.

Bret Hart Business Discussion

Gary Vaynerchuk appeared on the latest installment of the INSIGHT with Chris Van Vliet podcast for an in-depth interview covering professional wrestling. During the discussion, Vaynerchuk spoke about various topics related to his passion for the industry and his observations about wrestling's business side.

When asked about his favorite wrestler growing up, Vaynerchuk revealed his deep connection to "Macho Man" Randy Savage. He explained that Savage was huge for him because of how he was introduced to the WWF, with all the managers wanting him before he announced Elizabeth as his manager. Vaynerchuk noted that he was reaching an age where he appreciated Elizabeth's appearance, but it was Savage's charisma and the way the company built him up that truly captivated him. Growing up as a four-sport fan supporting the Rangers, Knicks, Yankees, and Jets, Vaynerchuk said that Macho Man winning the tournament for the heavyweight title was the biggest thing of his childhood, even surpassing the Rangers' 1994 Cup victory. When Savage beat Tito Santana for the title, Vaynerchuk felt proud discussing it at school. He remained loyal to Savage even when the wrestler turned into a villain, citing his incredible charisma.

Vaynerchuk praised the entire era of wrestling that included introductions lasting weeks before wrestlers even entered the ring. He mentioned other notable figures like Mr. Perfect, Razor Ramon, Billy Jack Haynes, and Bad News Brown, emphasizing that by the time wrestlers like Macho Man had their first match, the audience was already completely invested.

Regarding his video titled "Why Bret Hart Is a Better Businessman Than You," Vaynerchuk explained his reasoning centered on the concept of excellence of execution. He stated that execution is everything, noting that many creative people have ideas but fail to actually implement them. Vaynerchuk acknowledged that Bret Hart's current public perception is somewhat mixed, but he emphasized being a huge fan himself. He noted that his sister was also a huge fan of Hart, thinking he was extremely attractive during his wrestling days. While everyone knows about Hart's wrestling abilities regardless of their current opinion of him, Vaynerchuk argued that this reflects broader business principles.

Vaynerchuk explained that regardless of profession, whether wrestling or otherwise, many people possess similar skills like knowledge and charisma. What separates the successful from the unsuccessful is discipline and execution. He cited Hart's consistent schedule of Tuesday, Thursday, and Friday wrestling, along with proper team structure, as examples of execution. Vaynerchuk created the article to integrate wrestling into discussions about business principles, wanting to emphasize that people underestimate how crucial execution is to success. He stressed that all the best ideas in the world never get executed because the person who conceived them lacks the ability to follow through.

When asked about his wrestling Mount Rushmore, Vaynerchuk selected Ric Flair, Hulk Hogan, Andre the Giant, and The Undertaker. He acknowledged that while The Rock, Stone Cold Steve Austin, Bret Hart, and Shawn Michaels are all incredible, he believes people underestimate Andre the Giant's impact on wrestling. Vaynerchuk noted that Andre carried wrestling through the 1970s when the industry was still regional, and whenever Andre traveled, his presence elevated the entire business. He expressed frustration that Andre is often omitted from Mount Rushmore discussions, believing he should be included in every legitimate ranking. Vaynerchuk also emphasized that Hogan changed everything for professional wrestling.

When discussing underrated wrestlers, Vaynerchuk championed Macho Man Randy Savage, arguing that his match against Ricky "The Dragon" Steamboat at WrestleMania III ranks among the top two, three, or four matches of all time, suggesting that Savage's wrestling abilities are frequently overlooked in historical discussions of the industry.

Former WWE co-president explains why company continued relationship with Saudi Arabia after Jamal Khashoggi murder

Former WWE co-president George Barrios has opened up about the promotion's controversial decision to maintain its relationship with Saudi Arabia following the murder of journalist Jamal Khashoggi. In his recently released book "Sometimes Wrong but Never in Doubt: How a Cuban Kid from Queens Transformed WWE," Barrios provided detailed insights into WWE's negotiations with the Saudi General Sports Authority and the difficult choice the company faced.

WWE announced its ten-year partnership with Saudi Arabia's General Sports Authority in January 2018, with the agreement launching through the Greatest Royal Rumble event. According to Barrios, the negotiation process had been lengthy and contentious. "Our negotiations dragged on because the Saudis are notorious hagglers. By 2018, despite having made great progress, we were struggling to get the deal over the finish line. Complicating matters was the disappearance of Saudi journalist and dissident Jamal Khashoggi," Barrios wrote in his book.

On October 2, 2018, Khashoggi, a Washington Post contributing columnist, entered the Saudi Arabian consulate in Turkey and was never seen again. The CIA subsequently concluded that he had been murdered at the direction of Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman. While seventeen Saudis faced sanctions, bin Salman himself was not sanctioned. Five individuals received death sentences, three were imprisoned, and three were acquitted in connection with the case.

The Khashoggi murder created significant uncertainty around WWE's Saudi Arabia deal. "As outraged as I was by what happened, I still defy anyone to argue they understand who was truly at fault. All I know is that on our end, Khashoggi's disappearance called into question our deal with the Saudis," Barrios wrote. Despite the controversy, WWE proceeded with its Crown Jewel event on November 2, 2018, in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, even as other companies pulled out of the country and major backlash ensued.

The decision to move forward was made after significant internal debate among WWE's leadership. "Vince, Michelle, and I spent a great deal of time debating the pros and cons of moving forward. Vince in particular was deeply uncomfortable with the uncertainty surrounding the events. In my opinion, we couldn't walk out on the deal. It was too worthwhile for WWE. But Vince kept shaking his head. 'This thing with Khashoggi. Doing the deal could be the biggest risk we've ever taken with the company.' 'Vince, listen to me,' I said. 'I understand how serious this is. But no one really knows what's going on,'" Barrios detailed in his book.

The decision had immediate financial consequences. WWE's stock price fell from $93 to below $70 following Khashoggi's murder, and the numbers never fully recovered until the company signed a new deal with TKO in 2023.

Barrios had joined WWE in 2008 and became co-president alongside Michelle Wilson. Both executives were let go in January 2020. Despite the initial controversy and uncertainty, WWE has since expanded its partnership with Saudi Arabia significantly. The company now holds two annual events in the country and has plans to host WrestleMania in Saudi Arabia in 2027.

Hulk Hogan’s No Holds Barred Went From Box Office Bomb to Cult Classic

When No Holds Barred was released on June 2, 1989, the WWF hoped it would turn Hulk Hogan into a major movie star. However, the movie received poor reviews and performed modestly at the box office. But almost four decades later, No Holds Barred has found a second life as a cult classic among wrestling fans and lovers of so-bad-it's-good movies.

The film starred Hulk Hogan as Rip Thomas, the WWF Heavyweight Champion, who faces off against the evil TV executive Mr. Brell and his powerful enforcer, Zeus, played by Tommy Lister Jr. For WWF, No Holds Barred was a part of Vince McMahon's plan to expand the company into the entertainment business. The movie was produced through Shane Pictures, a company named after Vince's son Shane McMahon. It followed a similar idea to WWF's music label, Stephanie Music, named after his daughter Stephanie.

The movie struggled with both critics and audiences. According to Box Office Mojo, it earned around 16 million dollars worldwide on an 8 million dollar budget. However, its heavy marketing costs made it difficult to generate a significant profit. It holds a critic rating of 10 percent and an audience rating of 35 percent on Rotten Tomatoes and a 4.4 out of 10 rating on IMDb.

In 1997, McMahon even joked about the movie on an episode of RAW, saying Hogan had promised to repay his salary if the film lost money, adding that he was still waiting for the check. Instead of letting the story end in theaters, WWF brought Zeus into its TV storylines as a real wrestler. Tommy Lister appeared as the same character from the movie, claiming Hogan only beat him because the film was scripted. The feud stretched from SummerSlam to Survivor Series before leading to one of the most memorable PPV concepts in wrestling history.

Ironically, the movie became much more popular after its theatrical run ended. Fans embraced its over-the-top action, wild performances, and memorable one-liners. Scenes like Rip punching through a limousine roof and the famous "What's that smell? Dookie!" line became a cult classic among wrestling fans. Today, No Holds Barred is remembered less as a successful movie and more as a fun piece of wrestling history.

Its colorful 1980s style, larger-than-life characters, and outrageous storytelling have cemented its status as a favorite. It also showed how wrestling and Hollywood could work together, paving the way for future crossover stars like The Rock. Moreover, it proved that even a box office disappointment can leave a lasting legacy. No Holds Barred is available to stream on YouTube and Tubi.
