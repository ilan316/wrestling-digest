🔥 WrestleMania 42 Card Announcements

John Cena Returns as Host of WrestleMania 42

John Cena officially confirmed on March 30 that he will serve as the host of WWE WrestleMania 42 in Las Vegas, making this his first WWE appearance since retiring from in-ring competition at the end of 2025. The announcement came via a video posted to social media where Cena, currently filming a Netflix movie called "One Attempt Remaining," explained that Hollywood's proximity to Las Vegas made the opportunity convenient. He stated, "Rumor around this town is WrestleMania needs a host. Well, I'm already on that road, so I will see you at WrestleMania."

Cena's last match took place on December 13, 2025, at WWE Saturday Night's Main Event in Washington D.C., where he tapped out to GUNTHER in a controversial finish that left fans conflicted. The loss marked the end of his year-long farewell tour, during which he won his 17th WWE Championship at WrestleMania 41, surpassing Ric Flair's record. Since retiring, Cena has signed a long-term deal with WWE as an ambassador and has been exploring ways to remain involved with the company in a behind-the-scenes capacity.

WWE is heavily promoting Cena's hosting role as a major selling point for WrestleMania 42. According to reports, the company specifically selected Cena to help drive ticket demand and boost interest in the event, particularly for the packed Allegiant Stadium venue. WWE plans to feature Cena prominently across Raw, SmackDown, and all major programming leading up to the show, with repeated mentions, video packages, and appearances tied to his return. The timing of this announcement represents WWE's broader strategy to strengthen WrestleMania's overall appeal during the final weeks before the event.

While Cena has been adamant about his retirement from active wrestling, speculation continues about whether he might get physical at WrestleMania 42. Some observers have theorized various scenarios, including Cena potentially serving as a special guest referee for the Cody Rhodes vs. Randy Orton Undisputed WWE Championship Match, getting involved in a segment with GUNTHER, or even interfering during select matches. However, very few expect Cena to break his retirement commitment and compete in a traditional match, though his hosting duties could leave room for physical involvement if the storylines warrant it.

Meanwhile, WWE continues building the WrestleMania 42 card with eight matches already announced. The company is considering several potential additions to existing matches. For the women's division, there is speculation that Kairi Sane could be inserted into the Asuka vs. IYO SKY matchup to create a three-way dance featuring the three former Damage CTRL members. The friction between these three has been building throughout 2025, with Asuka taking a domineering role and showing jealousy toward IYO's individual success, leading to betrayal and increasingly abusive interactions.

On the men's side, Penta has emerged as a potential addition to what is shaping up to be a major intercontinental championship storyline. After Penta defeated Dominik Mysterio on the March 2 episode of Monday Night Raw to capture the WWE Intercontinental Championship, tensions within The Judgment Day have escalated. Finn Balor chose not to assist Mysterio during the match and prevented JD McDonagh from getting involved, leading to The Judgment Day attacking Balor after years of internal dissension. A grudge match between Balor and Mysterio appears to be heading to WrestleMania 42, though WWE could elevate the intercontinental title's profile by making this a triple threat match with Penta retaining his championship.

The WWE Women's Tag Team Championship picture is also expected to expand at WrestleMania 42. The Irresistible Forces currently hold the titles and have been facing challenges from The Bella Twins and the combination of Charlotte Flair and Alexa Bliss on Friday Night SmackDown. Simultaneously on Raw, Bayley and Lyra Valkyria earned a title shot that they will pursue on the March 30 episode from Madison Square Garden against Nia Jax and Lash Legend. The Judgment Day's Roxanne Perez and Raquel Rodriguez could potentially interject themselves into this equation, setting up a multi-team match for the titles at the Showcase of the Immortals.

WrestleMania 42 takes place April 18 and 19 at Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas. The confirmed matches include Roman Reigns vs. Cody Rhodes and a WWE Women's Championship bout between Jade Cargill and Rhea Ripley, among others. With Cena's hosting duties officially locked in and the card continuing to develop, WWE is positioning WrestleMania 42 as a can't-miss event that combines established storylines with the excitement of seeing one of wrestling's biggest legends return in a new capacity.

🔥 Trick Williams WrestleMania 42 Match Details

When discussing his entrance on Mornings with Mero on HOT 97, Williams hinted at bringing celebrities to the show. He referenced his entrance music's connection to the song "Family Ties" by Kendrick Lamar and Baby Keem, saying "We've got that family ties thing going on. Williams emphasized his reputation for impressive entrances, suggesting that a live remixed performance of his entrance theme by the featured artists could be part of the experience.

Beyond the potential Kendrick Lamar and Baby Keem involvement, Williams has already extended invitations to other celebrities. He has publicly invited rapper Lil Yachty to the April 3 SmackDown episode in St. Louis, Missouri, and during his HOT 97 appearance, Williams cryptically stated "We got something cooking. The stars is coming out. That's all I'm gonna say" when discussing his WrestleMania plans.

Williams also addressed the cultural significance of his music during his media appearance. The original songwriter of "Whoop That Trick," Memphis rapper Al Kapone, reached out to Williams after discovering that the crowd had adopted the chant as part of Williams' entrance. According to Williams, "He found me on Instagram. He loved it. He sent me a whole bunch of merch, boxing gloves, 'Whoop That Trick' this and everything. This demonstrates the organic connection between Williams' character and hip-hop culture, which WWE appears eager to capitalize on for his WrestleMania presentation.

Regarding his opponent, Williams will face Zayn in what represents a significant step in his main roster career. Zayn won the title by defeating Carmelo Hayes during the same March 27 SmackDown episode where Williams' interference played a pivotal role in the title change.

Williams also spoke extensively about Carmelo Hayes, his former NXT tag team partner and rival. Despite the competitive nature of their in-ring relationship, Williams expressed genuine respect for Hayes and acknowledged their long history together. When asked about Hayes not having a confirmed spot at WrestleMania following the title loss, Williams said "Me and Melo, we go way back, of course. Williams also took time to celebrate the current wave of young Black talent emerging in WWE. Alongside himself and Hayes, he mentioned recently called-up stars Oba Femi and Je'Von Evans as part of an exciting new generation. According to Williams, company leadership tells him "Go out there and do your thing" and "Nobody can be Trick Williams. We can't write for you, you go out there and you be Trick Williams." This approach has clearly resonated with Williams, who has become one of the most dynamic performers on the SmackDown roster in recent weeks.

The stage is set for Williams to make a major impression at WrestleMania 42 with what promises to be one of the most talked-about entrances and matches of the event.

🔥 Giulia Injury After SmackDown Match

Giulia Experiences Significant Post-Match Soreness Following SmackDown Victory

Women's United States Champion Giulia faced off against Tiffany Stratton in a non-title match on the March 27 edition of WWE SmackDown. Giulia picked up the victory when she got her knees up to block Stratton's Prettiest Moonsault Ever before rolling her into a cradle for the pin. A slight distraction from Kiana James played a role in helping Giulia secure the win.

However, the physical toll from the match was evident in the days following the broadcast. According to a now-deleted Instagram post from her trainer Matthew Ennis, Giulia arrived at her training session "very banged up" from the match. Ennis detailed the extent of her discomfort across multiple areas of her body.

"Giulia came into her session today very banged up from her match last Friday on SmackDown," Ennis said. "Normally, this would be our full lower body day, but today was not a good day for that because she was in so much pain. Low back pain, knee pain, hip pain, shoulders, you name it."

The level of pain Giulia was experiencing forced Ennis to completely adjust their planned training regimen. Rather than pushing through with the scheduled lower body workout, they pivoted on the fly to prioritize recovery and mobility work.

"So instead of sticking to a rigid plan, we adapted today on the fly, and the goal is just simply to have her walk out of the gym feeling better," Ennis explained. "Thankfully, through a full body activation, mobility, stretching circuit, we were able to achieve that goal. And now we'll be more prepared to push harder tomorrow, and that's a big thing for pro wrestlers. Depending on how your body feels, you may need to adjust the workout for the day."

The adjustment appears to have been effective, as Ennis indicated that Giulia left the session feeling better and they could return to more intense training the following day. Ennis did not mention any specific injury that would impact Giulia's in-ring schedule going forward.

One Particularly Brutal Spot During the Match

During the match, Stratton executed a particularly impactful sequence that may have contributed to Giulia's post-match soreness. Stratton dropped Giulia off the top turnbuckle with a Gordbuster and followed up with a stiff-looking swanton bomb, landing flat on Giulia's midsection and sternum. The move looked rough, and Giulia felt the effects acutely.

After the match, Giulia shared a clip of the swanton bomb on her Instagram Stories and made a candid admission about the impact. "No, seriously, it's not even funny... I actually peed a lot," she wrote, commenting on how hard the move had hit her.

Road to WrestleMania

Whether Giulia's feud with Stratton will continue into WrestleMania 42 remains uncertain. During a recent interview, Stratton addressed rumors of a potential championship match between the two at the Showcase of the Immortals, stating that she had not been officially informed of any WrestleMania booking. "Yeah, I have heard those rumors, too, and I feel like they're just rumors because I haven't really been told anything that I'm on WrestleMania," Stratton said. "But we'll have to wait and see. I hope there's a championship involved."

Despite the significant soreness and full-body pain Giulia experienced following the SmackDown encounter, there are currently no indications that she will miss any scheduled ring time. The recovery work undertaken with her trainer appears to have positioned her well for her upcoming obligations.

🔥 Cody Rhodes WrestleMania Bus Tour Pricing

Cody Rhodes is aiming to deliver a memorable experience for fans willing to invest nearly $10,000 during WrestleMania 42 weekend. Rhodes explained that this isn't a brand-new concept, revealing that a version of the tour happened quietly the previous year and gave fans rare access. "We did this tour last year very quietly. And it's just a sweet On Location deal for the people that want a little bit of that extra moment. What I did last year was very fun, they got to see my WrestleMania gear in advance and we got to talk WrestleMania," Rhodes said.

He then directly addressed the pricing discussion. "I've got a good plan for them this year, because like you said, that's an absurd amount of money. And, gosh, thanks for — the fact that people are out there paying that, that makes me feel incredible. So I want them to definitely feel like they got a unique and cool experience and something intimate for us."

Rhodes emphasized the value of personal connection with fans. "Because when you get those moments with your fans where you can sit down across from them and actually get to know them — two of the guys that were on the bus tour last year are actually coming on my podcast. I wanted to bring a fan onto the podcast to see what that experience was like. We've stayed in touch. So I look forward to a whole new motley crew on the bus this year. And thank you for doing that."

The American Nightmare's comments highlighted that the experience has led to lasting connections beyond WrestleMania week, demonstrating it is more than just a one-time meet-and-greet. The bus tour offers limited spots, restricted to just 12 guests, who ride along on Cody's personal "Liberty 1" tour bus as he drives to one or two iconic Las Vegas landmarks, with a professional guide providing live commentary.

This reframes the conversation around premium WWE fan experiences. While the $9,950 price tag remains one of WWE's most expensive fan offerings, Rhodes is clearly focused on delivering something personal and meaningful for those who commit to it. Jinder Mahal Wrestling Finance Series

Former WWE star Jinder Mahal, whose real name is Raj Dhesi, has announced the launch of a new educational series dedicated to financial literacy for professional wrestlers. Through a post on his Instagram account, Mahal explained that while the series specifically targets wrestlers, the financial principles discussed apply to anyone seeking to improve their financial situation.

Mahal emphasized that financial planning is an area frequently overlooked within the wrestling industry, affecting both contracted wrestlers signed to major promotions like WWE, AEW, and TNA, as well as independent wrestlers working outside those organizations. He stressed that as independent contractors, wrestlers bear the responsibility of establishing and funding their own retirement accounts, a burden that many in the industry may not fully understand or properly address.

For wrestlers based in the United States, Mahal encouraged taking time to understand available retirement options. He also acknowledged that international wrestlers should research retirement savings options specific to their respective countries.

The former WWE star highlighted two primary retirement account options worth understanding. The first is a Roth IRA, which is funded with after-tax dollars. The key advantage of a Roth IRA is that it allows for tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals once you reach retirement age. However, there are annual contribution limits, and income limits apply, meaning higher earners may find themselves unable to contribute directly to this account type.

The second option Mahal discussed is a Solo 401(k), which allows contributions to be made with pre-tax income. This reduces taxable income in the current year while the funds grow tax-deferred until withdrawal in retirement. Solo 401(k)s offer significantly higher contribution limits compared to Roth IRAs, making them suitable for those pursuing more aggressive retirement savings strategies.

Mahal noted that wrestlers do not need to choose between these two account types exclusively. Instead, both a Roth IRA and a Solo 401(k) can work together as part of a comprehensive retirement strategy. He concluded his guidance by recommending that wrestlers speak with an investment professional to develop a personalized financial plan suited to their individual circumstances and goals.
