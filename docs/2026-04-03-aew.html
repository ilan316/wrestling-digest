🔥 Chris Jericho AEW Return

Chris Jericho Returns to AEW, Rejecting WWE Retirement Tour Offer

The professional wrestling world erupted when Chris Jericho made his shocking return to AEW on Wednesday's episode of Dynamite, held in his hometown of Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada. The former AEW World Champion's comeback marked his first appearance in nearly a year and immediately sparked widespread speculation about the circumstances surrounding his decision and what the future holds for the veteran performer.

Jericho's return was kept under extraordinary secrecy by AEW management. The promotion did not bring Jericho to the venue until just two minutes before the show went live, driving him directly to the gorilla position moments before his scheduled entrance. During the opening segment, Jericho made a brief but impactful appearance, delivering only four words: "Winnipeg! AEW! I'm home!" Despite the brevity of his on-air time, Jericho remained active throughout the evening, filming extra content for future use, participating in a photoshoot, and making a point to introduce himself to AEW talents he had not previously met.

According to multiple reports, there is a general belief within the AEW locker room and among industry insiders that Jericho reached an agreement on a new deal with the promotion prior to his return. Reports indicate that Jericho's previous contract had technically expired at the end of 2025, but Tony Khan extended the agreement to account for time Jericho had missed while fulfilling obligations with his band Fozzy and various acting projects. While backstage speculation suggests a new contract was negotiated, the exact details remain unconfirmed, with some sources noting the possibility that Jericho could still be operating under his previous agreement with extended terms.

The backstory behind Jericho's choice to return to AEW rather than move to WWE reveals significant insight into his thinking. In recent months, there had been considerable speculation and rumors suggesting Jericho might jump to WWE. Dave Meltzer reported receiving a text message a few days before the Dynamite episode indicating Jericho might appear at WrestleMania. However, plans changed, and Jericho instead returned to AEW. Meltzer noted that WWE had once believed Jericho would return to the company, but those plans evidently fell through.

According to Meltzer's reports, WWE's interest in Jericho was specifically limited in scope. WWE only wanted to bring Jericho in for a retirement tour and farewell run. At one point, industry sources had indicated that 2026 would be built around retirements for AJ Styles, Brock Lesnar, and presumably Jericho in WWE. However, despite teases from WWE officials and even CM Punk, Meltzer repeatedly cautioned that stories circulating about WWE and Jericho should not be believed. The belief within the industry is that Jericho was not interested in that type of arrangement.

Meltzer's analysis suggests that Jericho's decision was driven by his desire for creative fulfillment and input into his booking. At this stage of his career, Jericho's financial situation is reportedly quite secure due to the substantial earnings he accumulated during his previous AEW tenure. Given his age and financial stability, Jericho appears to have prioritized creative satisfaction over other considerations. AEW is expected to provide him with significantly more creative input and control over his booking than he would receive in WWE. While WWE likely offers a larger audience, the creative freedom and autonomy available in AEW appear to have been the determining factor in his decision.

With Jericho's return now official, the industry consensus is that any possibility of him heading to WWE is "now dead." Meltzer stated that the belief within the industry is Jericho is back in AEW for the long term. However, many questions remain unanswered regarding his specific plans and direction. According to Meltzer, details about what Jericho will actually be doing and how the crowd will react to his new direction will become clearer once AEW moves beyond their Canadian television dates.

Jericho was scheduled to address the AEW fans directly on the April 8 episode of Dynamite emanating from Edmonton, Alberta, Canada, providing an opportunity for him to explain his return and outline his plans going forward. Kenny Omega was also scheduled to address the fans at that same event. The April 8 Dynamite card was reported to include several other matches leading toward the Dynasty pay-per-view event, including a TBS Championship defense by Willow Nightingale against Queen Aminata and a trios match featuring Darby Allin, Luchasaurus, and Jack Perry facing off against Andrade El Idolo, Konosuke Takeshita, and Mark Davis of the Don Callis Family.

One notable detail about Jericho's presentation was the manner in which he was referred to on AEW programming. During an announcement on AEW Collision, he was referred to simply as "Jericho" rather than by one of his standard monikers, adding another layer of intrigue to his return and suggesting that AEW may be repositioning him in some way.

While some observers expressed skepticism about Jericho's return, noting that he had been removed from consistent quality matches and promos during his final years before his hiatus, others pointed out that his addition to AEW's roster need not be cause for concern. Jericho's presence brings legacy value and name recognition that could prove valuable as AEW navigates negotiations for its next television deal in an uncertain media landscape. Rather than being positioned as a main event draw, observers suggested Jericho would likely occupy a midcard to upper midcard role on AEW's extensive weekly programming, leaving ample time for the promotion to continue developing other talent and storylines.

Warner Bros. Discovery, AEW's parent company, was reportedly made aware of Jericho's return before it occurred, indicating that the decision had been approved at the corporate level. This suggests that bringing Jericho back was not merely a creative decision by Tony Khan but had broader strategic implications for the promotion.

The Hurt Syndicate AEW Return

The Hurt Syndicate made a major comeback to All Elite Wrestling during the April 2 episode of AEW Collision with a backstage segment that signaled the group's imminent return to action. MVP instructed fans not to adjust their televisions, declaring "your eyes do not deceive you. We. Are. Here." and announcing that Bobby Lashley had been cleared to compete. The faction leader emphasized that it was time to add new talent to their ranks, stating "It's time to add somebody new to the ranks because we are back in business. And business is about to pick up."

The announcement came shortly after MVP posted on social media on March 31, expressing excitement about the reunion. "The coolest thing is that we are back together in AEW and we'll be back in action SOON! Hurting people!!" he wrote, confirming the group's commitment to returning to competitive action.

Lashley's clearance marks a significant development after several months of absence from AEW programming. He last competed in the Casino Gauntlet Match for the National Championship at Full Gear. A December report indicated that Lashley was dealing with an injury, though the exact nature and timing of the injury remained unclear. In a March interview, Lashley clarified that he was not injured, though he provided no further explanation at that time. Now cleared and ready to return, his reinstatement strengthens The Hurt Syndicate considerably.

Shelton Benjamin has not been as absent as Lashley, having last appeared on the January 7 episode of Dynamite where he competed in a Continental Title Eliminator Match against Jon Moxley. Prior to that appearance, he defeated Dante Martin on Collision just days earlier. Before their recent hiatus, Lashley and Benjamin held the AEW Tag Team Championships for 214 days until losing the titles to BRODIDO at Forbidden Door 2025, making their return even more significant to the tag team division.

The AEW tag division currently features a strong roster of teams including veteran squads like Cope and Christian and the Young Bucks, newer teams like JetSpeed and The Rascalz, and the reigning AEW Tag Team Champions FTR, who are currently in their third reign. The Hurt Syndicate's return adds another major force to an already competitive division.

Looking ahead, the faction has made it clear they are actively seeking to recruit a new member to their stable. This expansion indicates The Hurt Syndicate's intentions to establish themselves as a dominant force in AEW and broaden their impact beyond just the tag team picture. The addition of fresh talent to the group could reshape the landscape of both the tag division and other areas of the promotion.

Colten Gunn AEW Clearance

After more than eight months out of action, Colten Gunn has been cleared to return to the ring. Gunn suffered a knee injury on Collision in July 2025 and has been sidelined since. His AEW return could be on the horizon with his father Billy revealing that Colten is now medically cleared to compete again.

Billy Gunn confirmed the clearance during a virtual signing appearance with Signed by Superstars on April 2. When asked about Colten's status, Billy stated, "He just got cleared yesterday, so he's good," and later added, "He's great. He just got cleared yesterday. So he's good. Thank you for asking."

The injury occurred in a four-way tag match where The Gunns faced Gates of Agony, Big Bill & Bryan Keith, and Lio Rush & Action Andretti. Colten landed awkwardly after throwing a lifting punch and immediately grabbed at his knee, forcing him out of competition. This injury derailed plans for The Gunns to participate in a Tag Team title eliminator tournament to determine who would face The Hurt Syndicate at Forbidden Door 2025.

Colten and Austin Gunn are former AEW Tag Team Champions, having held the titles for 56 days in 2023. The belts are currently held by FTR. The Gunns are members of The Bang Bang Gang in AEW alongside Jay White, Juice Robinson, and Ace Austin. With Colten's clearance, the faction could soon operate closer to full strength, though Jay White, the leader of the group, has also been sidelined due to hand and shoulder injuries with his most recent match taking place in March 2025.

With medical clearance now confirmed, attention turns to when Colten Gunn will actually step back into an AEW ring and whether The Bang Bang Gang will soon be able to operate at full capacity once again.

Ricky Saints AEW Absence

Ricky Saints is opening up about his time sitting on the sidelines in AEW, and he made it clear that being kept off television took a toll on him mentally. While still under contract with the promotion, Saints went missing from AEW television after a March 2024 loss on Collision. During this extended absence, he continued to wrestle for independent promotions when he could secure clearance.

While speaking on Getting Over: Wrestling Podcast, Saints talked about the stretch when Tony Khan wasn't using him regularly on AEW television. He said there wasn't much communication from his side about why he wasn't wrestling, which made the situation even tougher to deal with. Saints explained that staying active in the ring has always been part of who he is, so suddenly not wrestling created a strange mental challenge.

"Yeah. No, no, you know, no real discussion or or anything like that from my side of it in terms of uh you know, what was discussed and all that, but there was some restlessness just because I'm so used to wrestling all the time. And I think that's the type of person I am. I'm used to creating. I'm used to using my body for these things. And to not have that was a very odd uh place to be at mentally," Saints explained.

He also said that trying to stay active outside of AEW wasn't always easy because getting independent bookings approved could be complicated at the time. Even with those challenges, he made it clear he doesn't hold bitterness toward the situation and is grateful for how things turned out. "And so you're having to figure out these other things, especially when it goes to like the independence. It was not as easy to get some of these independent dates cleared. And um for there's no that's not a knock on the company itself. It's just conflict of interest for AEW and whatever independent that I wanted to do. Um, but at the end of the day, it all worked out. Truthfully, it all worked out. And I'm thankful for both sides. I'm thankful for AEW. I'm thankful for the Independence as well," Saints said.

Saints' last indie appearance dates back to his DEFY World title win at DEFY Hundredth - 8 Year Anniversary on February 7th, 2025, following which he made his WWE NXT in-ring debut on the February 25th episode of NXT. Shortly after joining the WWE NXT roster, Saints became a one-time NXT and one-time NXT North American Champion. His comments pull back the curtain on how difficult it can be when a wrestler is under contract but not being used regularly. Even though he stayed professional about it, he admitted the lack of ring time created a mental struggle that stuck with him.

Kenny Omega AEW Ticket Prices

Kenny Omega has weighed in on one of the most pressing issues facing the wrestling industry today: ticket pricing. In a recent interview, the AEW star made a direct comparison between his company's approach and that of WWE, focusing on accessibility for fans of all financial backgrounds.

Speaking with CBC News Manitoba, Omega praised AEW's philosophy regarding fan experience and value. He explained that the company intentionally positions itself as an alternative in the wrestling landscape, neither fully indie nor WWE-sized, but distinctly AEW. According to Omega, this positioning allows the company to focus on delivering quality entertainment at reasonable prices.

"What we try to be is the most fun live show that you can watch available to you," Omega stated. He emphasized that for family-friendly programming, AEW maintains genuinely affordable ticket pricing without compromising on content quality. "We never skimp on the content, you'll always get a ton of wrestling for your money value," he explained.

The AEW star was particularly direct when addressing the value proposition. "You are getting a whole heck of a lot of AEW and you're not having to mortgage your first house or sell off your first child. That's not even a knock on any of the other promotions or anything," Omega said. He stressed that fans interested in experiencing AEW, whether they are longtime supporters or casual attendees looking for family entertainment, can do so at accessible price points.

Omega's comments arrive at a significant moment in wrestling's landscape. Ticket pricing has become an increasingly discussed topic among fans, with some reports suggesting that WWE struggled to move tickets for WrestleMania this year. This trend appears to have caught Omega's attention and motivated his remarks.

The AEW performer expressed genuine pride in the company's pricing strategy and accessibility. "I'm just really glad that if there's any interest whatsoever, whether it be because you're a diehard AEW fan or you just want to go out and check out a night of wrestling or something fun with the family or friends, I'm glad that there is price point options available for all walks of life. That's one thing I'm very proud of with AEW," he concluded.

Hikaru Shida AEW Return

Hikaru Shida Makes AEW Return To Answer Collision Open Challenge

For the first time in 16 months, All Elite Wrestling fans saw Hikaru Shida in action. The Japanese star returned on the special Thursday edition of AEW Collision to answer an open challenge laid out by TBS Champion Willow Nightingale. According to Nightingale, she wanted to face the very best amongst the roster, which opened the door for Shida, a former three-time AEW Women's Champion, to resurface.

In the closing moments of their TBS Championship match, Shida dodged Nightingale's top rope moonsault, then blasted her with a running knee for a near fall. Eager to finish Nightingale off, Shida followed with another running knee. Again, though, Nightingale kicked out, and ultimately, surprised Shida with a backslide pin for the win.

Evidently frustrated by the result, Shida raised her cane as if she were about to strike Nightingale with it from behind afterward. Instead, she dropped it, with Nightingale then offering a thumbs up for Shida's efforts.

Prior to the April 2 edition of Collision, Shida's last in-ring appearance for AEW came in late November 2024, when she took on Kris Statlander in a singles match on AEW Dynamite. Following that, Shida experienced visa issues that were eventually resolved by October 2025. The same month, she also reportedly signed a contract extension with AEW.

In between her AEW outings, Shida continued to compete in Japan for promotions such as WAVE, ZERO1, and OZ Academy. Most recently, she took part in OZ Academy's Sonoko Kato Retirement show in November 2025 through an eight-woman tag bout. Whether she will serve as a regular fixture for AEW now has yet to be seen.

Scott D'Amore Tony Khan Praise

Working with AEW and Tony Khan has been an enjoyable experience for Maple Leaf Pro Wrestling boss Scott D'Amore. At the end of March, D'Amore and Khan worked together as ROH and MLP co-promoted their Global Wars Canada event. D'Amore spoke with Late Night Grin about the experience and praised Khan for how he's made a positive impact on the wrestling industry. He described Khan as being a fun and happy guy, which balances well with D'Amore's more analytical approach.

"I think it's been really enjoyable. One of the things is — Tony Khan has a love and a passion for professional wrestling that is near-unmatched," D'Amore said. "He is a superfan, and he's very proud of that fact. And the enjoyment he gets out of being around wrestling, creating wrestling, and really making a difference in the business is huge for him."

"I mean, he's a good guy. You know, people complained about this and that and anything else. And that's cool. But when you look at it, here's somebody who has changed wrestling for the better. People make more money in wrestling because of what he's done and what AEW has done. More people are getting opportunities. And people genuinely get treated extremely well. You've got someone who's taken the pro sports approach to how you treat your athletes and applied that to professional wrestling — and that's an amazing step forward in our industry."

It was announced during Global Wars weekend that MLP has agreed to a television deal with TSN in Canada. D'Amore told Late Night Grin that, when launching the promotion, his goal was to create something that would make a difference in pro wrestling and shine a spotlight on Canadian talent. Getting this TV deal is a big step toward that.

"For people that don't know, in Canada, TSN — that's The Sports Network. That's what TSN stands for. It's basically our ESPN," D'Amore said. "It was the very first all-sports network in Canada. It's legendary here. It's the equivalent for American fans of being on ESPN or UK fans back in the day, the equivalent of being on Sky Sports. So It's huge."

D'Amore noted that, like NJPW caters toward the Japanese market, MLP is geared toward Canadian fans but welcomes viewers from all around the world. The promotion is holding an event in Las Vegas during WrestleMania week as part of Palms Slam Fest.

Kenny Omega opened up about his health journey and mindset regarding his place in AEW during an interview with CBC News Manitoba. Ahead of his AEW World Championship match against MJF at AEW Dynasty on April 12, the wrestling star discussed competing for the title in his home country of Canada and his recovery from diverticulitis, a condition that nearly ended his career.

When asked about his willingness to accept a different role within the company, Omega expressed genuine contentment with that possibility. "Yeah, I think that's great. I think a big part of it, too, which I think helped me a lot in this journey, was that I was at peace with being able to take a huge step back if I needed to," Omega said. "If I needed to have a complete non-physical role within the company, and just be an office guy in the back, that's never seen but is still contributing. I was happy to just be involved with this project, that is AEW, with this company, with its wonderful fans, its athletes."

Omega explained that this mental approach actually enhanced his recovery efforts. The wrestler noted that accepting his potential circumstances helped him focus on rehabilitation rather than panic. "I think having that sense of calmness and being able to accept the current situation actually helped me put forward more of an effort in the pursuit of getting back what I lost," Omega continued. "Because I feel like if I was in a panic mode, I would've run around like a chicken with its head cut off, not knowing whether to go left, whether to go right, should I try something new, or should I keep really doubling down on what I'm currently doing?"

The wrestler also emphasized the importance of medical advancements in his recovery process. Omega credited current-day medical science for providing him with numerous treatment options that were not available earlier in his career. "I think just being honest with yourself, in this case, or honestly, with myself in this case, and just being very open-minded to not only the fact that things may work if I try something new, but if they don't work, to be able to accept that. 'Okay, I've tried everything that I could. I can't say that I didn't try. Let's see what's next,'" Omega explained.

Omega highlighted how modern supplementation and medical innovations specifically helped address issues like inflammation and energy levels. "But being able to have this smorgasbord of options for me to have available, especially with current-day medical science, supplementation, and all that, there are all these things I never had access to years and years ago, which are absolutely for sure helping me in today's world with things like inflammation, energy. I probably would've been in a world of hurt without that sort of help," he stated.

The wrestler also expressed gratitude for the support system surrounding him at AEW. "So I've had a lot of wonderful people all around me, around the team of people that I work with in AEW, that have always been there to help, and to always pursue whatever the next best medical or supplementation scientific advancement might be out there to help my condition," Omega concluded. His positive mindset and access to modern medical resources ultimately enabled him to return to in-ring competition for his championship opportunity.
