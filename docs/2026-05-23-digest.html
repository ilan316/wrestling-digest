🔥 AEW Broadcast and Paramount Deal

AEW heads into a critical period for its future as the wrestling industry watches the fallout from Paramount Skydance's acquisition of Warner Bros. Discovery. While the deal remains pending and is expected to close by the end of September, AEW leadership has been actively addressing concerns about the company's status under new ownership.

During an appearance on Bloomberg TV's The Close while promoting Sunday's Double or Nothing pay-per-view in Queens, New York, Tony Khan expressed optimism about the coming changes. When asked about the shift in corporate ownership given Paramount's existing business relationships with WWE owners TKO, Khan remained positive. "I am very excited about Paramount acquiring WB and Paramount and WB coming together. This is going to be great for AEW," Khan said. When directly asked if he believes Paramount will stick by AEW post-merger, Khan answered emphatically, "100%."

Khan emphasized the strategic benefits of the merger during the interview. "With the power of HBO Max and Paramount Plus together, it's more buyers for our PPVs and more people watching the AEW shows. I think it will make TBS and TNT stronger cable channels with Paramount and David Ellison coming in," he explained. Khan reiterated his past statements about his strong relationship with Paramount head David Ellison, a connection the two share through their NFL ties.

The current AEW domestic rights deal with Warner Bros. Discovery runs through 2027, with WBD holding a one-year extension option. There is currently no wrestling programming on Paramount Plus or its current networks, though that could change depending on how the new corporate structure handles content distribution.

MJF, the former AEW World Champion who faces Darby Allin in a title versus hair match at Double or Nothing, has also voiced confidence about AEW's future. Speaking on The Ariel Helwani Show, MJF acknowledged that while he doesn't know all the specific details about where shows and pay-per-views might air or how content might be bundled, he remains assured. "What I do know is that Warner Bros. Discovery loves us," MJF stated. "I also know that Tony Khan... is friendly with the Ellison family. Guys, I think we're going to be okay. To be honest, no matter what happens, we're going to land on our feet."

MJF drew a comparison to the corporate structure being created. "Right now, look at Cablevision. The heavy hitters are Fox News and live sports, and we're live sports. So, we're going to land on our feet no matter what happens, but, I have a feeling you're going to be seeing us on TBS and Turner Broadcasting for a very, very, very, very long time," he said, expressing long-term confidence in AEW's placement on cable channels.

🔥 Mance Warner AEW Indie Booking Dispute

AAW Wrestling created confusion on Friday evening when they announced that Mance Warner would not be appearing at their Homecoming event scheduled for May 22 in Merrionette Park, Illinois. The promotion stated that the change happened "per AEW's request" and that they were immediately restructuring the show. Warner had been originally scheduled to face Richard Holliday in one of the featured matches on the card.

The announcement seemed unusual to many observers, as AEW is generally known for permitting its wrestlers to work independent shows when schedules allow. Recent examples include talents like Ricochet and MJF appearing for indie promotions and crossover events, with the only typical restrictions being injuries, scheduling conflicts, or television commitments. This announcement coincided with AEW's Double or Nothing weekend taking place in Queens, New York, which led some fans to assume Warner had been pulled because of obligations connected to the pay-per-view.

Tony Khan responded swiftly and directly on social media, completely refuting AAW's claim. Khan stated, "That's the first that I've heard of this, and it's the first time anyone in AEW's office has heard of it. He is welcome to do your show." This public response from the AEW President immediately created confusion about what had actually transpired with Warner's booking situation and cast doubt on AAW's initial explanation.

Mance Warner then issued his own clarification on social media the following day, providing transparency about what actually occurred. He wrote, "Want to clarify this situation. AEW did not ask me to pull out of this show. My rate was never agreed upon. I sent $400 to the promoter to cover the flight costs. I am sorry to any fans who were looking forward to seeing me at AAW. With transparency, I made a decision to withdraw from the show to avoid any percent chance of travel issues or any fuck ups ruining a big opportunity. I hope y'all can understand."

According to Warner's account, the real issue stemmed from a contract disagreement rather than any AEW restriction. He explained that his appearance fee had never been finalized with the promotion, and despite his good faith effort in providing $400 to cover flight expenses, he decided to withdraw himself to prevent potential complications. Warner's statement directly contradicted AAW's initial narrative that blamed AEW for the cancellation.

The situation raised questions about why AAW publicly blamed AEW in the first place when the actual issue appeared to be an internal booking dispute between Warner and the promotion. Khan's public response effectively called out AAW for making a false claim about AEW's involvement, while Warner's clarification shifted focus to the contract negotiation failure that actually led to his withdrawal.

Warner's current status involves continued appearances with Ring of Honor, including tapings that took place on May 18. He has not been featured prominently on AEW television in recent months, though he remains connected to Tony Khan's wrestling landscape through ROH appearances. Earlier in 2026, Warner and his wife Steph De Lander made headlines when they abruptly departed TNA. De Lander stated that the exit occurred after TNA's medical team refused to clear her for in-ring competition despite her claim that she had fully recovered from neck fusion surgery performed the previous year.

Since leaving TNA, both Warner and De Lander have remained active on the independent wrestling circuit and have made appearances for Game Changer Wrestling. The controversy surrounding the AAW booking appears to have been resolved through the clarifications provided by both Khan and Warner, though it highlighted the potential for miscommunication or misinformation when independent promotions make public statements about wrestler availability without clear confirmation from all parties involved.

🔥 Adam Copeland AEW Contract Status

Adam Copeland is very zen about the rest of his AEW career, and his recent comments shed light on exactly why. Back when he went by Edge in the WWF and WWE, Copeland already said goodbye twice. The first was in 2011, when everyone thought he'd never wrestle again because of his neck and spine condition. The second was 12 years later, after medical technology advanced and his body healed enough to allow him to return in 2020, when he got to celebrate 25 years in the business by wrestling in his hometown against his friend Sheamus before leaving WWE for AEW.

This history explains why Copeland doesn't seem hung up on his next farewell, or really thinking about it all, whether that goodbye is just to AEW or to pro wrestling as a whole. Speaking with Clutch Points ahead of AEW Double or Nothing 2026, Copeland revealed the current status of his wrestling future and how added injury and filming time has impacted the length of his AEW contract.

When asked about his contract status, Copeland explained that time missed due to injuries and outside projects continues to extend his current AEW deal. "I broke my leg, so there's injury time added, and I had to go film Beekeeper 2 and Percy Jackson, so that's added," Copeland said. "Anytime I have to take a break, that gets added on the back end. I'm not even sure when it's up." His casual attitude about not knowing his own contract expiration date perfectly encapsulates his zen approach to his wrestling future.

While discussing the possibility of doing a retirement tour similar to what fans are currently seeing from John Cena, with so many of his contemporaries like AJ Styles and rivals like John Cena already retiring, Copeland admitted he already feels like he had that farewell moment back in 2011 when he was forced into retirement due to cervical spinal stenosis. "I already had my sendoff," Copeland stated. "This time, it'll be my choice, and that makes it a different experience entirely. I don't care if I get this big, tearful sendoff. I have no preconceived idea of what it needs to be."

The Rated R Superstar also reflected on how the reduced schedule affects his body compared to wrestling consistently throughout the year. "That's the interesting part," Copeland explained. "We've always talked about within the industry the best you feel is when you can wrestle the most, because it's almost like you stay in this purgatory of pain and never really come out of it. You're always kind of in that zone, and gosh, if you take two weeks off and take a bump, the first time it's like, 'Oh, man.' And if you're doing that live on national television or on pay-per-view, that can be a disconcerting thing."

At 52 years old, Copeland has previously gone on record that his existing deal would be his last as an active wrestler, with his plan being to retire from the ring at age 53. Since debuting with All Elite Wrestling at WrestleDream in October 2023, Copeland has enjoyed a successful spell in AEW, winning the TNT Championship twice, challenging for the AEW World Title on several occasions, and now pursuing the AEW World Tag Team Championships with his lifelong best friend Christian Cage.

Copeland and Cage are set to challenge FTR for the AEW World Tag Team Titles at AEW Double or Nothing in an I Quit Match where, if they lose, they will be forced to retire as a tag team. This high-stakes match adds another layer to Copeland's already complicated timeline, though knowing his zen approach, he's likely not sweating the details.
