**Airica Demia's Signing**

On Friday, March 28, Airica Demia was presented with her WWE ID contract at Palmetto Championship Wrestling in Camden, South Carolina. The 21-year-old independent wrestler is a second-generation wrestler who made her in-ring debut in 2023. Like Grey, Demia recently participated in a WWE tryout at the Performance Center in Orlando.

WWE ID confirmed the signing on social media, announcing: "BREAKING…. @Airica_Demia was just given a WWE ID contract at @PCWofSC in Camden, South Carolina! Congrats to Airica Demia on being ID'd! These two signings represent WWE's continued commitment to building its next generation of talent through the WWE ID program. Brock Lesnar Personal Reflections

---

In the world of MMA, Brock Lesnar is known as a former UFC Heavyweight Champion. In pro wrestling, he's established himself as a multi-time world champion, with title reigns spanning across both New Japan Pro-Wrestling and WWE. At the end of the day, though, none of that athletic success matters to "The Beast" as there's something even more important to him.

"For me, none of my championships or none of my accolades, they all mean nothing, boys," Lesnar told "Spittin' Chiclets." "It means nothing to me. My kids are my legacy. I mean, in today's dark ass world that we're living in, if you can come out and have good kids, I take a lot of pride in that. When parents come to me and say, 'Man, your boys are really respectful,' that pumps my tires, you know?"

Lesnar is a father to four children, including Mya, who found great success on the Colorado State University track and field team as a shot putter. In her collegiate career, Mya solidified herself as the first CSU thrower to become indoor and outdoor national champion. The Mountain West Conference has also honored her as the Women's Outdoor Track and Field Athlete of the Year. Mya has a twin brother named Luke. Lesnar's other sons, Turk and Duke, are rising ice hockey players.

Lesnar himself is currently on the road to WWE WrestleMania 42, where he will collide with former NXT Champion Oba Femi. This year's WrestleMania will emanate from Las Vegas' Allegiant Stadium on April 18 and 19.

First breaking into WWE when he started in Ohio Valley Wrestling back in 2000 following his successful amateur career at the University of Minnesota, Lesnar debuted on the main roster in 2002, when the likes of The Undertaker, Big Show, Kurt Angle, Eddie Guerrero, and others were dominating the roster. It was certainly a different time than today, and Lesnar reflected on how the industry has transformed during an appearance on "Spittin' Chiclets."

"I was a shooter, an amateur guy. I had a lot of credentials to my name," Lesnar explained. "Of course, I got tested along the way by the old-timers. You have to pay your dues. Back then, you paid your dues and you had to earn your respect. It's different now, obviously. Things have gotten a lot softer. There isn't no more kangaroo courts. So, it's different now. I go there, I'm the old guy, now."

The match against main roster newcomer and former NXT Champion Oba Femi came about after Lesnar and career-long manager Paul Heyman issued an open challenge for "The Showcase of the Immortals" on an episode of WWE Raw. In the following weeks, Femi, whom Lesnar eliminated from the Royal Rumble after numerous teases of a showdown, answered the challenge. "The Ruler" hit Lesnar with a big Fall From Grace powerbomb, placed a foot on his chest, and pointed to the WrestleMania sign, with WWE confirming the match was official later that night.

WWE continues to expand its intellectual property portfolio with a new trademark filing for "Club WWE." The company filed the trademark application on Friday, March 27, 2026, according to reports from wrestling news outlets.

The filing indicates WWE is exploring a structured membership program designed to offer exclusive benefits and access to its audience.

According to the official description submitted to the United States Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO), the "Club WWE" trademark covers a broad range of services under Classification 035. These services include membership club services in the nature of providing discounts and special offers to members in the field of retail sales, special event planning, marketing, and subscriptions. Additionally, the filing encompasses administration of a discount program for enabling participants to obtain discounts on goods and services through use of a membership.

The trademark application also includes advertising services and the provision of consumer product and services information via the Internet and mobile applications. Furthermore, "Club WWE" would provide membership club services offering online information to members in the fields of branding, business development, business marketing, and marketing.

## WrestleMania Build Faces Mounting Creative Concerns as Card Remains Fluid

Concerns about WrestleMania's build have taken center stage in recent wrestling media discussions, with prominent voices questioning whether WWE has established enough compelling storylines heading into the company's biggest show of the year.

During a prominent podcast appearance, Vince Russo and Jonathan Coachman offered pointed criticism of WWE's creative direction. Coachman highlighted that matches appear to be getting added to the card without strong angles behind them, warning that without real buildup, the event risks feeling hollow. "They're going to throw matches out there to fill that number that have no storylines or no juice whatsoever," Coachman stated.

Russo doubled down on these concerns, arguing that weekly television leading into WrestleMania has relied on the same repeated segments rather than layered storytelling. He claimed the formula has become predictable, depending mostly on confrontations rather than developing long-term narratives. "That's filler. Seven matches — Triple H is saying seven matches are filler. If we don't have those matches by this point in time, then that is filler," Russo explained. "Coach, listen… I'm watching highlights of both of these shows. There are two things that they're doing — either a brawl or a face-to-face promo. That's it. That's the road to WrestleMania. There is not one single story. It's either a brawl or we're going to have a face-to-face promo. That is it. And now you're going to have seven matches that are going to be nothing but filler."

The criticism centers on the belief that WrestleMania storylines should already feel established at this stage of the build. Instead, observers argue that the lack of defined narratives could lead to matches that feel thrown together rather than built through compelling storytelling.

Despite these concerns, there remains time for WWE to strengthen its biggest angles before the event arrives in Las Vegas. With three weeks remaining until WrestleMania 42, the card could still undergo significant changes that reshape the overall presentation.

Several potential alterations could drastically shake up the big event. In the Undisputed WWE Championship match between Cody Rhodes and Randy Orton, Orton's recent heel turn has included mysterious backstage phone calls, suggesting a major surprise could still materialize. Speculation surrounds the potential addition of a special guest referee, with industry observers suggesting a major celebrity appearance—potentially The Rock in his "Final Boss" persona—could add intrigue to the match or other high-profile bouts like CM Punk versus Roman Reigns.

The United States Championship picture could also expand. After Sami Zayn recaptured the title on SmackDown, setting up a WrestleMania 42 match against Trick Williams, Carmelo Hayes could demand a rematch given that he lost his title due to interference from Williams. This could lead to a three-way match scenario before the event.

The Women's Tag Team Championship match may require roster adjustments following an apparent injury to Nikki Bella during recent television. With continued conflict between The Bella Twins, Charlotte Flair & Alexa Bliss, Lyra Valkyria & Bayley, and current champions Nia Jax & Lash Legend, multiple scenarios could unfold—from a multi-team match to a surprise partner substitution that could reshape fan expectations.

While critics express frustration about the overall narrative direction, WWE still has the opportunity to establish stronger storyline foundations in the coming weeks. However, comments reflecting growing concern among industry observers suggest that the road to WrestleMania should feel driven by stories rather than repetition.

Candice Michelle WWE Title Wins Reflection

---

Candice Michelle's journey through professional wrestling has been marked by two distinctly different championship moments, each offering unique perspectives on her career and the opportunities that have come her way.

Michelle's first taste of championship gold came in 2007 when she captured the WWE Women's Championship by defeating Melina at the Vengeance Night of Champions pay-per-view. The moment was particularly memorable because of how last-minute the decision was made. "It was like hours before the match [I was told about the title win]," Michelle recalled on "Insight With Chris Van Vliet." "We're literally down in the ring. I think it was Fit Finlay and he's like 'You're going over tonight.' It's so casual and I'm like 'What do you mean?' He's like 'You're taking the title tonight.' I'm like 'Okay.' That match got cut. I think it was three minutes. It got cut so short."

Despite the abbreviated nature of the match, Michelle's performance was decisive. Her title match with Melina spanned about four minutes, during which Michelle survived aggressive offense from the defending champion. Eventually, she ran the ropes to gain enough momentum to deliver a powerful spinning heel kick and keep Melina down for a three count. Afterward, Michelle took a moment to reflect on what was to come. "What I learned is when I won the championship, I remember telling myself, take a moment because I know it's going to go fast," she said. "But what I learned is that moment lasts for a second. And right when you walk through the curtains, now you have a whole locker room chasing you."

Trish Stratus Greatest Matches Retrospective

---

WINC Watchlist: Trish Stratus' Greatest Matches

To this day, Trish Stratus is widely regarded as one of the most influential women's stars in professional wrestling history, as she played a major role in the WWE women's division being taken more seriously. Throughout the mid-2000s, Stratus proved that she could be just as athletic and skilled as the men on the roster, and eventually earned the opportunity to be featured in major storylines and marquee matches, instead of allowing management to continue to book her in lesser-regarded feuds. Eventually, Stratus' hard work paid off, with her efforts during the Ruthless Aggression Era paving the way for the current generation of women's wrestlers, who now main event weekly shows and Premium Live Events on a regular basis.

Seven WWE Women's Championships later, Stratus would be inducted into the WWE Hall Of Fame in 2013, and would return to the company five years later for a short stint before her most recent run from 2023-2025. As we continue to honor Women's History Month, the following are our picks for standout matches of Stratus' career, all of which impressed in the ring but had an impact on the industry as a whole.

**Trish Stratus vs Lita - WWE Raw 2004**

The first-ever women's match to main event "WWE Raw" couldn't have featured two better competitors than Stratus and Lita, who were determined to deliver in the spot they were given on the night of December 6, 2004. Coming into the match, Stratus had just eclipsed 170 days as WWE Women's Champion as one of the company's top heels, and in just seven minutes, she was able to give Lita the most memorable win of her career.

Knowing they only had limited time to work with, both Stratus and Lita focused on executing fast-paced action while not shying away from putting together a hard-hitting matchup. Due to the hot start, the audience in attendance were invested from the opening bell, and they only continued to get louder with every near fall and counter, with many wanting to see Lita dethrone Stratus. That said, the unforgettable moment of Lita being crowned the new WWE Women's Champion doesn't happen without Stratus' excellent character work as a heel, having purposely shown her frustration in being unable to outsmart her opponent.

Whether it's the incredible atmosphere, Stratus' iconic pink striped attire, or Lita's final Moonsault before winning the title, the first women's main event on "Raw" continues to be remembered for being a true representation of what women could accomplish when given an opportunity to shine when the lights are the brightest.

**Trish Stratus vs Mickie James - WrestleMania 22**

Although Stratus versus Mickie James at WrestleMania 22 isn't best known for its in-ring quality, it's easily become one of the most memorable women's matches at "The Grandest Stage Of Them All," but also an important championship bout in the history of women's wrestling.

WrestleMania 22 was the conclusion to a terrific storyline that unfolded between both women, with James bursting onto the scene as the newest member of the women's division, but being an obsessed Stratus superfan. Eventually, the two would become friends, but James' obsession with Stratus went too far, especially when she started to be affectionate towards her, leading Trish to put an end to their friendship. Shortly thereafter, James' character took a dark turn, having become Stratus' stalker and psychologically tormenting her for weeks until WrestleMania, where she ultimately defeated her idol for the WWE Women's Championship.

To this day, nobody can forget the crazed look on James' face at the top of the entrance ramp after winning the gold, along with Jim Ross iconically yelling "The nutjob has won the title" at the end of the match. Most importantly, James was surprisingly cheered by the Chicago crowd despite being the heel in the match, which is an indicator of how well the story was told between both competitors and the strength of their combined work.

WWE PLE Streaming Marketing Lawsuit

---

WWE is facing a class-action lawsuit from two consumers who allege the company engaged in deceptive marketing practices regarding its streaming arrangement with ESPN for premium live events.

According to Brandon Thurston of Wrestlenomics, the plaintiffs claim that WWE advertised ESPN would provide access to WWE premium live events without any additional fees. The lawsuit was initially filed against WWE alone, with ESPN conspicuously absent from the defendant list.

The plaintiffs' decision to exclude ESPN from the lawsuit appears strategic. ESPN's subscriber agreement contains an arbitration clause, which typically requires disputes to be resolved through private arbitration rather than in court. By not naming ESPN as a defendant, the plaintiffs likely sought to avoid triggering that arbitration requirement and keep the case in the public court system.

ESPN has since made a move to counter this strategy by filing a motion to intervene in the ongoing class-action lawsuit. The streaming giant is seeking to compel arbitration, effectively attempting to shift the dispute into the private arbitration process outlined in their subscriber terms. ESPN's intervention suggests the company views itself as a necessary party to the litigation and wants to enforce its contractual arbitration clause.

The memorandum of law supporting ESPN's motion to intervene provides additional details about the legal arguments being made by the streaming platform.

ESPN became WWE's primary streaming partner for premium live events last year, with the arrangement beginning with WrestlePalooza 2025 in September. These events are made available to U.S. subscribers who maintain an Unlimited tier subscription through the ESPN App. The partnership represents a significant shift in how WWE distributes its premium content, moving away from the legacy model that previously existed.

The lawsuit raises important questions about disclosure and consumer protection in digital streaming services, particularly regarding whether companies adequately communicate subscription tier requirements and associated costs when marketing content access.

Match Spotlight: Ultimate Warrior Vs. Randy Savage, WWE WrestleMania VII

The year is 1991. Divas like Madonna, Whitney Houston, and Mariah Carey are trading the number one spot on the Billboard Hot 100 back-and-forth. "The Silence of the Lambs" is dominating the box office when it comes to film, and in the World Wrestling Federation, WrestleMania has had to be moved from a 100,000 seat coliseum to an arena which held just over 16,000 people.

WWE (formerly known as WWF) WrestleMania 7 was a show that was supposed to be the biggest event the company had ever put on, even surpassing the gargantuan WrestleMania 3. However, low ticket sales that were disguised as the company fearing for its own safety due to Sgt. Slaughter's Iraqi sympathizer gimmick, led to WrestleMania 7 moving from the Los Angeles Memorial Coliseum to the Los Angeles Memorial Sports Arena across the street. This was arguably for the best as the venue was packed to the rafters in anticipation of two very big matches: the main event between Hulk Hogan and Sgt. Slaughter for the WWE Championship, and the Career Ending Match between the "Macho King" Randy Savage and The Ultimate Warrior.

Savage had cost Warrior his WWE Championship at the Royal Rumble, something Savage was hoping would catapult him back into the title scene after chasing the top prize in WWE since WrestleMania 5. That wouldn't end up happening, and in the hopes of putting an end to any and all nonsense surrounding the two men, Warrior challenged Savage to a Career Ending Match at WrestleMania 7. The loser would have to walk away from wrestling altogether, while the winner would likely move on to challenge either Hogan or Slaughter in the coming months.

With WrestleMania 7 celebrating its 35th anniversary, there is no better time to go back and watch one of the greatest matches from the early days of WrestleMania. The Career Ending Match between Randy Savage and The Ultimate Warrior remains one of the most significant encounters from that era.

**The Ultimate Warrior's Greatest Match**

Everyone knows that The Ultimate Warrior wasn't the best wrestler between the ropes. He could get the people hyped up, hit a couple of power moves, and leg it out of the arena before he or the crowd could have the time to catch their breath. When matches went longer than just a few minutes, that's when he was exposed to be, well, not that good. However, there were a few occasions when Warrior rose up to the occasion, and during his entire career, he never reached the lofty heights of his performance at WrestleMania 7.

What helped from the start was the fact that for the first time in his WWE career, Warrior walked to the ring rather than running, meaning that he only got blown up about halfway through rather than the first few minutes. Realistically, all of the heavy lifting in this match is done by the "Macho King." This is an all-time classic performance from Randy Savage who manages to squeeze more out of the Warrior in 20 minutes than anyone else in wrestling. He positions himself as the dastardly heel who has to resort to underhanded tactics in order to gain the advantage. ## Bully Ray Recalls Dropping Vince McMahon On WWE Raw, Getting 'Receipt' On SmackDown

Vince McMahon was no stranger to getting into the ring with his biggest stars, especially during WWE's Attitude Era, when The Dudley Boyz were putting grown men through tables on a regular basis. Naturally, McMahon ended up clashing with the men from Dudleyville, and while looking back at the greatest disasters of his career, Bully Ray recalled accidentally dropping the boss on his head.

The match took place on an episode of WWE Raw that aired on June 12, 2000, and saw the Dudleys take on Vince and Shane McMahon. Bully Ray claims Vince didn't arrive until 30 minutes before it was meant to air live, so they had no window to practice a spot that would see Bully attempt to hit Vince with the notorious Superbomb.

"Vince McMahon is a very heavy, dense man who – no matter what he's ever done in the wrestling ring – he doesn't know the art of making himself light," the veteran said during an episode of "Busted Open: The Master's Class." Bully explained that there was a lot that went into setting people up for the top rope powerbomb, and with the lack of practice, he was concerned about the spot.

"Vince, at that time, would've been the absolute heaviest person I have ever had on my shoulders, in my hands, and complete unbalanced weight, not knowing how to make himself light!" he added. "I'm telling Vince like: 'Vince, we gotta do something!' 'No, we'll be fine, pal! You'll do it – don't worry, once we get out there!'"

Bully convinced Vince to at least let Bully lift him up in the hallway in front of Vince's office, where he realized that McMahon was heavier than he thought. "I knew something was gonna go wrong," he said.

### The Superbomb Attempt Ends in Disaster

Not content to just go ahead with the spot with Vince, Bully then checked in with Road Dogg and X-Pac, who also had a role in the match to come. The D-Generation X members, then part of The McMahon-Helmsley Regime, were supposed to come out and save Vince by interfering in the Superbomb attempt, running in to hit D-Von and breaking up the spot. Vince, however, would already be up in the Superbomb position when this occurred, and Bully implored Road Dogg not to hit D-Von too hard, or he would fall into Vince and disrupt the balancing act.

"The only time I'd been nervous doing this spot before," Bully said. "And if you go back and you watch it, Vince is up there and he's up there perfectly, Road Dogg runs in, nails D-Von in the back just enough for D-Von to push forward.

"And just that little bit of a push – you can't beat gravity, inertia, momentum – pushes Vince's weight towards me, and we go toppling backwards, ass over tea kettle, from the top rope to the concrete; dropped Vince McMahon right on his head."

### The Dudley Boyz Learn Not to Mess with the Boss

Bully remembers Vince playing off the incident as though nothing had happened, but it turns out there are indeed consequences when you drop the boss.

"Disaster! Vince crashes and burns, he tries to tell us that he's totally fine, there's not gonna be any heat from it – all is good," Bully said, before ominously recalling how both he and D-Von were put in a dumpster the following SmackDown.

"They put us in a dumpster and dropped us 15 feet off the stage," he said. "And D-Von did not tell me – my wife never told me after 20 years of marriage that she was claustrophobic. D-Von had an anxiety attack when he went into that dumpster."

Bully recalled having to hold D-Von's hand while they were in the dumpster and trying to make him imagine it was a rollercoaster ride. "When that dumpster hits, you see the cover of the dumpster pop off, and D-Von come flying out, gasping for air!" he added. "The disaster was dropping Vince. The disaster was putting D-Von in a dumpster when he was claustrophobic, and that was our receipt."
