🔥 Chris Jericho AEW Return and Dynasty

Chris Jericho made his shocking return to AEW television last week, and this week on the April 8 edition of Dynamite in Edmonton, Alberta, he made his comeback official by signing a new contract with the company. Jericho addressed the crowd, reflecting on his journey and the major gamble he took in helping launch AEW alongside Tony Khan. He emphasized that his passion remains rooted in building the company and that his position on the card is irrelevant so long as AEW continues to grow.

Before Jericho could officially sign his deal, he was interrupted by Ricochet and The Demand. Ricochet immediately drew heat by taking shots at Canada, claiming he "hates it," before shifting his focus to Jericho. Despite saying they came in peace, Ricochet urged Jericho not to sign the contract and questioned whether the returning legend was still in "ring shape," suggesting there's a major difference between being in "singing shape" and ring shape. Ricochet acknowledged Jericho's legacy as one of the greatest AEW World Champions but declared that AEW now belongs to him. He touted rising attendance and ratings while reminding everyone that he is the inaugural AEW National Champion. Ricochet bluntly stated that nobody wants Jericho back and told him to leave the ring, return to his cruise ship, and stay away before he gets hurt.

Jericho fired back by brushing off Ricochet's comments with a jab about his appearance before proceeding with business and signing his contract. With the deal made official, Jericho revealed a key clause that immediately changed the landscape heading into AEW Dynasty. The clause grants him the power to choose any available opponent for a match at the pay-per-view this Sunday. After teasing several potential names, Jericho turned his attention back to Ricochet and delivered a familiar callback that longtime fans instantly recognized. He revealed "The List" is back, officially naming Ricochet as his opponent for AEW Dynasty by adding him to the list and setting up a clash between the returning legend and the former AEW National Champion.

According to reports, Jericho's contract was extended due to his time off, and he is "there for the foreseeable future." AEW President and CEO Tony Khan shared that Jericho was a free agent who "chose" AEW over other options. Contrary to popular belief, wrestlers can communicate with other companies while under contract, and as contracts near their end, it becomes impractical to pursue legal action over such matters.

Numerous reports had suggested that Jericho could move to WWE, but he ultimately chose to re-sign with AEW. WWE Hall of Famer JBL expressed his support for Jericho's decision during an appearance on "Something To Wrestle," arguing that when wrestlers have taken care of their finances like Jericho has, they have the luxury of choosing work that is enjoyable. JBL stated, "You know, when you're younger, you do stuff for money reasons. And there's nothing wrong with that. You know, sometimes you do stuff and you end up in a gimmick. You end up in a character. You end up in a place that you probably wouldn't choose to be in. When you get older and you've taken care of your money like Chris Jericho has, you have the options, and the options are awesome and Chris now is just gonna do stuff that's very enjoyable to him and I greatly admire that."

JBL praised what Jericho will accomplish in AEW, noting that the veteran always has a new look and is constantly bringing fresh ideas. There had been talk that Jericho may have had a retirement tour if he were to join WWE, potentially facing GUNTHER, who has already retired two legends in John Cena and AJ Styles. However, JBL argued against the idea of a retirement tour, stating that Jericho is still in excellent shape and can continue to wrestle without needing to end his career. JBL added that there are other wrestlers in WWE aside from GUNTHER who could have been opponents for Jericho, including names like Brock Lesnar, Seth Rollins, Roman Reigns, and CM Punk.

Jericho's return marks his first match in AEW since last year's Dynasty, where he lost the ROH World Championship in a Mask vs. Title match against Bandido. His first match back will take place this Sunday at AEW Dynasty in Vancouver against Ricochet. The show will also feature another newly announced match, as Kazuchika Okada and Konosuke Takeshita will square off against The Young Bucks in tag-team action.

🔥 AEW Kyle Fletcher Injury and TNT Championship Tournament

Kyle Fletcher's reign as AEW TNT Champion has come to an unexpected end due to injury. On April 8, Tony Khan announced that Fletcher suffered a meniscus injury during a tag team match on the March 28 episode of AEW Collision, where he was seen visibly favoring his lower body and hobbling. Khan confirmed that Fletcher will be out of action for months, though he is expected to return to the ring before the end of the year. With Fletcher unable to compete, the TNT Championship has been officially vacated.

To crown a new champion, Tony Khan announced that a Casino Gauntlet Match will take place at AEW Dynasty 2026, the company's pay-per-view event being held in Vancouver on April 12. This match format offers an exciting opportunity for multiple AEW stars to compete for the vacant title, with the winner being crowned the new TNT Champion.

On the April 8 episode of AEW Dynamite, Tommaso Ciampa faced Mascara Dorada in a qualifying match to determine who would earn the coveted number one entry spot in the Casino Gauntlet. Ciampa, the former TNT Champion who lost the title to Fletcher on February 11, defeated Dorada in a competitive match. With this victory, Ciampa has secured the first and best chance to capture the TNT Title once again. While no additional entrants have been officially announced for the Gauntlet, many names in such matches are typically kept secret until the event itself.

Several AEW stars are being considered as potential winners of the vacant TNT Championship. Ciampa has an excellent chance as the number one entrant and a former champion looking to regain gold. Bobby Lashley, who recently returned to AEW with The Hurt Syndicate alongside MVP and Shelton Benjamin on the April 2 episode of AEW Collision, could be looking to make an immediate impact by winning his first singles title in the company. Shelton Benjamin, another member of The Hurt Syndicate who has been with AEW since 2024, has impressed fans with his athleticism and strength. He could capture his first singles title in nearly two decades by winning the Casino Gauntlet.

Sammy Guevara has made his interest in the title known by posting a picture of himself with the TNT Championship on social media following Fletcher's injury announcement. Guevara is one of the most prolific TNT Champions in history, holding the title three times and currently serving as a three-time and reigning ROH World Tag Team Champion. A fourth TNT Championship would give him a record-breaking achievement in that particular title's history.

David Finlay, who officially signed with AEW in March after completing an 11-year run with New Japan Pro Wrestling, represents another strong candidate. Recognized as one of the best in-ring performers in NJPW in recent years, the Casino Gauntlet could serve as his first opportunity to win championship gold in AEW. Similarly, Gabe Kidd, who has been a presence on AEW television since 2025 and serves as the frontman of The Dogs in the company, could use his IWGP Global Heavyweight Championship experience to win the TNT Title.

The news of Fletcher's injury came as AEW also made several other announcements regarding Dynasty. Don Callis addressed the situation during the April 8 Dynamite, comparing his wrestling Family to a sports team. He referenced frequently bickering Family members Kazuchika Okada and Konosuke Takeshita as his Michael Jordan and Scottie Pippen, drawing parallels to the legendary 1990s Chicago Bulls dynasty. Callis initially announced that Okada and Takeshita would face The Young Bucks at Dynasty, though neither was happy about the booking. Takeshita demanded a singles match with Okada for his International Championship, and Callis agreed to book that match at Double or Nothing in New York in May, provided they could work together against The Bucks at Dynasty. The tension between them was evident when Okada declared himself the Jordan of their duo, prompting Takeshita to need to be restrained.

The Dynasty 2026 card now features several high-profile matches including AEW World Championship MJF defending against Kenny Omega, the AEW Women's World Championship match between Thekla and Jamie Hayter, FTR defending the AEW World Tag Team Championship against Adam Copeland and Christian Cage, Jon Moxley defending the AEW Continental Championship against Will Ospreay, and Darby Allin facing Andrade El Idolo. Chris Jericho will also compete in his first match in over a year against Ricochet. The TNT Championship Casino Gauntlet, featuring Ciampa in the number one spot, stands as one of the marquee matches of the event.

AEW Dynamite April 8 Episode Coverage

Chris Benoit's son, David Benoit, was spotted backstage at this week's episode of AEW Dynamite, which took place at Rogers Place in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada. David Benoit took several pictures with top AEW stars and shared one on Instagram with the caption, "Thankfully, I had the benefit of flash photography for a 5 second pose - only the cool kids will get it." The photo included himself posing with Cope, Christian Cage, and Chris Jericho. He also clicked a picture with AEW's Ricochet during the event.

David Benoit is the son of Chris Benoit and his first wife, Martina, and has a sister named Megan. While he had wrestling aspirations, his father's double murder and suicide incident in 2007 hindered his chances of signing with any major promotion. He's been to other Canadian shows before, and Chris Jericho has maintained a close relationship with him over the years. Jericho has mentored David and supported him after his father's 2007 incident.

In related news, WWE Hall of Famer Mark Henry recently shared on the Busted Open Podcast that he had a difficult time in the early 2000s dealing with injuries and self-doubt, which almost led him to retirement. Henry dislocated his shoulder right before WrestleMania, three weeks out, and felt mentally drained by the injury. He would pass a mirror and start crying, feeling like he let the boys down and let the business down. At one point, he told Chris Benoit that he didn't think his heart was in it anymore. Benoit responded by saying, "I will come to your house and fight you every day if you don't get your a*s back in here." Henry's simple response was, "Yeah, I don't think I want to fight Chris every day." This threat from Benoit ultimately kept Henry in the wrestling business.

The April 8 episode of AEW Dynamite featured several storylines and matches that drew mixed reactions from critics. The women's division continued to showcase its strength with a fun TBS Championship Match between champion Willow Nightingale and Queen Aminata. Nightingale retained her title in what was described as an incredibly fun and engaging match. The two wrestlers kept good pacing throughout and didn't hold back on strikes against one another. Queen Aminata displayed nice little reactions throughout the match when Nightingale would kick out of pin attempts, making viewers feel increasingly frustrated alongside Aminata. Hikaru Shida watching the action from the crowd added importance to the match and made clear that AEW wasn't done with the storyline between Nightingale and Shida, setting up potential future championship programs.

However, not everything on the show worked. Darby Allin's continued focus on winning the AEW Championship drew criticism for becoming repetitive and tiresome. Allin's slam poetry segment about wanting to be champion was described as poorly executed, with the wrestler running out of ways to say that he wants to be champion. While some understood that AEW was trying to tell a long-term Sting-style story with Allin taking the place of his mentor and acting as an avenging angel for AEW, the weekly repetition was making him look like a petulant child with main character syndrome rather than strengthening his position. Critics noted that Sting was smart enough to let people miss him, disappearing for weeks at a time so that his returns felt like big moments, whereas Allin appears every week talking about how badly he wants to be champion.

Chris Jericho's return with "The List" was celebrated as a welcome sight for those fatigued by his recent gimmicks. One reviewer had convinced themselves that nothing Jericho could do would resurrect feelings for the Jericho of old, given that previous characters like "The Learning Tree," "The Wizard," and "The Painmaker" had been poorly received. However, seeing Jericho return with "The List" provided an almost deceptively welcome feeling and a modicum of excitement that had been absent during his other recent iterations.

The show also featured a chaotic main event tag match and drew criticism for what was described as the longest Kenny Omega promo ever, as well as confusion about the current storyline direction involving Okada and Takeshita. This week's episode served as table-setting for Dynasty, the upcoming AEW pay-per-view event, with Dynamite and Collision still to air before the big show.

AEW Dynasty and Double or Nothing Matches

AEW has announced several major matches across their upcoming pay-per-view events and weekly programming. The April 11 episode of AEW Collision will feature a stacked lineup including The Young Bucks facing El Clon and Hechicero, an AEW World Trios Championship match between Mistico and JetSpeed defending against The Dogs, and an AEW International Championship bout with Kazuchika Okada defending his title against Myron Reed. Additionally, RUSH will compete against Anthony Bowens with the winner earning the number two spot in the Casino Gauntlet Match for the vacant TNT Championship at Dynasty.

Tommaso Ciampa has secured the number one spot in the Casino Gauntlet Match for the vacant TNT Championship at AEW Dynasty after defeating Mascara Dorada on the April 8 episode of AEW Dynamite. This victory brings Ciampa one step closer to his stated goal of reclaiming "Sylvie."

The focus of Dynasty, however, centers on internal Don Callis Family drama. During the April 8 Dynamite broadcast, Don Callis announced that Okada and Konosuke Takeshita will team together to face The Young Bucks at Dynasty. This pairing came about due to circumstances beyond their control. Kyle Fletcher, the now-former TNT Champion, was originally scheduled to team with Okada as part of Callis' "ProtoKada" concept, but Fletcher has been forced out of action. Rather than search outside his family for a replacement, Callis made the decision to pair Okada with Takeshita, two Don Callis Family members who have been rivals for months.

The announcement did not sit well with Takeshita, who immediately voiced his displeasure. Rather than accept the pairing quietly, Takeshita demanded the opportunity to challenge Okada for the AEW International Championship. Callis, however, responded diplomatically to the situation. He agreed to Takeshita's demand but with conditions designed to keep the family intact. Callis stated that as long as Takeshita and Okada work together at Dynasty against The Young Bucks, Takeshita will be granted his championship match against Okada at Double or Nothing on May 24. According to Callis, this arrangement will allow both men to "get it out of their systems" while ensuring that no one leaves the Don Callis Family fold.

This booking represents a clever piece of wrestling psychology, as it allows for both a forced alliance at Dynasty and a high-profile singles match at Double or Nothing. The Young Bucks previously ran out to assist Jack Perry, Bandido, and Darby Allin following their match against The Don Callis Family on Dynamite, setting up their tag team championship opportunity against Okada and Takeshita at the pay-per-view.

In addition to the Dynasty announcements, AEW has booked Okada versus Konosuke Takeshita for Double or Nothing on May 24, where Takeshita will challenge for the AEW International Championship. The two Don Callis Family members previously teamed together at WrestleDream to unsuccessfully challenge Brodido for the AEW World Tag Team Championships, making their potential conflict all the more significant within the faction's dynamic.

Tony Khan AEW and WWE Commentary

Tony Khan recently appeared on The Coach & Bro Show to discuss various aspects of his leadership of AEW and his perspective on the wrestling industry. Among the topics covered were his personal interactions with figures outside AEW and his evolving philosophy on how to position the company in the competitive landscape.

Khan opened up about a meeting he had with Shane McMahon a couple of years prior. According to Khan, Shane reached out to him and suggested they meet in person. Khan was planning to be in Dallas for a series of shows around Arlington, Texas, and he invited Shane to meet him there. The two had what Khan described as a pleasant conversation that lasted several hours at the airport.

What struck Khan as unusual was that a photo from their meeting eventually became public. Khan expressed surprise that the image surfaced and noted that he did not release it himself. He speculated that someone at the airport, recognizing Shane as a prominent figure in wrestling and sports, may have taken the photo without their knowledge. Khan pointed out that he meets with people from various media backgrounds regularly and that this was the only instance where such a photo emerged. In the image, Khan noted that he appeared very surprised while Shane seemed more composed about being photographed.

When discussing why the meeting did not lead to any on-screen collaboration or larger working relationship, Khan explained that it was simply a pleasant conversation between two people in the industry. He found it interesting that word of their discussion got out, but he did not believe Shane was interested in taking on an on-screen role in AEW. Khan suggested that Shane had different career interests and experiences that were distinct from his own, and that Shane did not approach the meeting with the intention of becoming a character on AEW programming.

Khan also addressed his changed approach to commentary and competitive positioning regarding WWE. He explained that at AEW's inception, there was a collective rebel spirit that motivated how he spoke about the industry and competitors. However, as AEW has matured and become established with a recognizable quality product and substantial fanbase, Khan's strategy has evolved.

Khan reflected on the difference between his current approach and how he might have operated six or seven years ago. He acknowledged that success in the wrestling business can be achieved through different methods and that what works for one person or company at one point in time might not be the best approach for another. He referenced Vince Russo, Vince McMahon, and D-Generation X as examples of people who found success through their own unique approaches, suggesting that he might succeed by taking a different path.

When addressing a suggestion from Russo about taking more aggressive action against WWE, Khan stated that he wants to listen to different perspectives and ideas, even if he does not incorporate every suggestion. Khan emphasized that he has intentionally refined his media strategy in recent years by attempting to take the high road whenever possible. He believes this approach has resonated positively with people and that being nice to others has served him well.

Khan acknowledged that different people may perceive similar statements differently depending on who is delivering them. He noted that his voice and approach in the industry may come across differently than how others in wrestling might communicate the same message. Rather than attempting to win people over through aggressive tactics, Khan stated that being nice to people has been an effective strategy for him, and he has been committed to this approach increasingly in recent years.

Willow Nightingale AEW TBS Championship

Willow Nightingale looked to continue building momentum on the April 8, 2026 episode of AEW Dynamite by defending her AEW TBS Championship against Queen Aminata. The challenger was never going to make it easy for the "Babe With The Power" as she was returning to action after missing five months of action with a neck injury. However, she was no match for the champion who made another successful defense of her crown.

Aminata suffered an early scare after being thrown into the guardrail on the outside where she was seen clutching her neck, which was injured at the end of 2025 by The Sisters of Sin. However, she regained her composure and took control by keeping the champion grounded before giving Nightingale some Chocolate Kisses as the match went to the commercial break. During the break, the challenger maintained her control by securing some submissions. Notably, Hikaru Shida, who had recently returned to AEW by answering Nightingale's TBS Championship open challenge on AEW Collision, watched on from the crowd.

Coming out of the break, Nightingale was able to take back control and targeted the neck of Aminata, landing a Death Valley Driver in the ring and a Cannonball on the outside. Nightingale missed a Moonsault when the action returned to the ring, and the two women engaged in a striking exchange, with Aminata even countering Nightingale's Pounce with a big elbow strike. The champion and challenger got some near falls, but Nightingale finally landed a big Pounce which almost sent Aminata out of the ring, and quickly followed up with the Babe With The Powerbomb for the victory.

Both women shook hands after the bell, and Nightingale went to the barricade to stare down Shida who was still in the crowd at the end of the bout.

Darby Allin AEW Future Plans

Darby Allin recently spoke with Jason Hall of iHeartMedia for an in-depth interview covering all things pro wrestling and AEW. During the discussion, the pro wrestling star offered high praise for Andrade El Idolo and spoke about how he needs to focus on his own career more as opposed to focusing on helping bring new talent up to his level.

When addressing his desire to become the face of AEW rather than serving as a mentor to younger talent, Allin was candid about his priorities. "I feel like I'm still on my own road. I'm not where I need to be right now to be a mentor in my eyes. I don't know, I truly — the whole thought of I'll never be the face of this company is eating me alive and I really need to be concerned with myself. I can't always keep reaching out and trying to pull people up. A lot of times man, the only way to truly get this done is if they do it themselves. Maybe you can guide them, but at the end of the day, they're going to have to do it themselves. I can't be on their ass every week. So, yeah. You either have that dog in you or you don't. You cannot create a work ethic, you either have that inside of you or you don't. I don't have that time to waste telling people to have that work ethic. I need to be on my own road and they'll be on their own road."

Regarding his perspective on new talent joining AEW and his feelings about Andrade El Idolo's current form, Allin demonstrated confidence in both himself and the company. "I feel like with Andrade leaving and coming back, he truly understood the importance of AEW. He truly understood that. With AEW, it's all up to you. How high you want to go, how low you want to go, it's all up to you. If you just go out there and you kick ass in that ring and you're willing to give everything for this, the favor will be returned. If you could just check your ego at the door, this can give you everything. I feel like Andrade is the best version he's ever been right now, minus being with Don Callis. It's incredible, man. I accept anyone that comes into AEW, I'm not concerned with losing my spot like 'Oh man, if this guy comes in, then I'm going to lose my spot so let's just guard everyone from coming in.' No, I welcome everybody as long as they're going to treat this place with the respect it deserves and doesn't bring an ego into this. That's all I ask. Andrade, he's kicking ass so I want to be in the ring with someone that makes me better because he is one of the best wrestlers in the world right now."

Allin will get his opportunity to test himself against Andrade El Idolo at AEW Dynasty 2026, which takes place this Sunday, April 12, live from Rogers Arena in Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada.

The wrestling community and AEW have shown support for King during this challenging period, with both his tag team partner and company leadership making accommodations for him to grieve and be present with his loved ones.
