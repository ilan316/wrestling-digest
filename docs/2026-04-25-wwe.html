🔥 WWE Post-WrestleMania 42 Releases

MASSIVE WWE POST-WRESTLEMANIA 42 ROSTER CUTS IMPACT MAJOR STARS AND STABLE

WWE has executed a significant wave of roster cuts following WrestleMania 42, beginning on April 24, 2026, with approximately 25 talents released across the main roster, NXT, and developmental system. The departures represent one of the larger post-WrestleMania cutdowns in recent years, affecting both established stars and developmental prospects.

Among the most notable departures are Aleister Black and Zelina Vega, a real-life married couple who had recently been paired together on WWE SmackDown. Vega's release came as a particular source of frustration for the performer, as she found out about her departure through a call that displayed "TKO" rather than "Stamford" on her phone screen, indicating the call came from WWE's parent company TKO Group Holdings rather than WWE's Connecticut headquarters. Speaking on a Twitch livestream, Vega expressed her disappointment about not being allowed to announce her own departure before wrestling journalist Sean Ross Sapp broke the news publicly. She stated that she was shocked by the call and would have appreciated the courtesy of announcing the news herself first.

Vega's second WWE run, which began in July 2021, had been largely successful. She won the inaugural Queen of the Ring tournament, competed as part of multiple factions including Legado Del Fantasma and the L.W.O., and captured the WWE Women's United States Championship in April 2025, holding the title for 63 days during a resurgent period. However, her character had become stagnant by the time of her release, particularly after turning heel alongside Aleister Black.

The entire Wyatt Sicks faction has been completely dissolved through these releases. The group, which was created as a tribute to the late Bray Wyatt and debuted in June 2024, consisted of Bo Dallas (Uncle Howdy), Nikki Cross, Joe Gacy, Erick Rowan, and Dexter Lumis. All five members have been released. The faction had initially gained significant traction due to its emotional connection to Bray Wyatt's passing but gradually lost direction as storylines progressed. Nikki Cross, a former RAW Women's Champion and three-time Women's Tag Team Champion, announced her departure via social media with a message thanking WWE, William Regal, Robbie Brookside, and the wrestling and production teams for their support.

The tag team division took a significant hit with the departure of the Motor City Machine Guns, comprising Chris Sabin and Alex Shelley. Despite their brief WWE tenure, lasting from late 2024 to early 2026, the team made an immediate impact by winning the WWE Tag Team Championship just one week after debuting. Their release marks a notable departure for a team that showed considerable promise during their short run.

Other main roster casualties include Kairi Sane, Santos Escobar, Apollo Crews, Zoey Stark, and Alba Fyre. Stark's release was particularly noteworthy given that she had only recently been cleared to return from a severe knee injury suffered during a Money in the Bank qualifying match on Raw in May 2025. Having spent nearly a year sidelined recovering from surgery, her release came just as she was expected to resume in-ring competition. Stark released a statement thanking WWE for their support during her recovery and expressing gratitude to trainers Terry Taylor and Coach Bloom.

Apollo Crews, who had been with WWE since 2014, was among the departures. Santos Escobar, who had recently re-signed with WWE after a brief departure, was released while recovering from a torn bicep injury. Alba Fyre, a former NXT UK Women's Champion and multiple-time tag team champion, had not been featured prominently on television recently, making her release less surprising. Fyre posted a cryptic message on social media suggesting that Kay Lee Ray would have defeated Alba Fyre, hinting at frustration with her character direction.

The developmental and NXT departures represent a substantial portion of the cuts. Andre Chase, the leader of Chase University and a two-time NXT Tag Team Champion, announced his release through an emotional video statement. Chase acknowledged that when he first signed with WWE in 2021, he was told he would never appear on television and was intended only to mentor younger talent. However, he credited fan support for changing his trajectory, allowing him to become an on-screen performer and champion. Despite the release of his chapter with WWE, Chase stated his professional wrestling career would continue, with bookings available starting in June.

Other NXT and developmental departures include Dante Chen, who became the first Singaporean-born WWE Superstar and thanked the company for that opportunity. Tyriek Igwe, part of the High Ryze tag team with fellow releasee Tyson Dupont, was also let go. Dupont was already dealing with hip surgery complications from an injury sustained in summer 2025 when his release came through. Tyra Mae Steele, an Olympic gold medalist from the 2020 Summer Olympics who won the women's division of WWE Legends & Future Greats, was among the developmental cuts, along with Sirena Linton, Malik Blade, Trill London, Luca Crusifino, and Chris Island.

Malik Blade's departure came after a significant injury setback. He suffered a torn ACL in October 2024 during his last televised match on NXT Level Up against Tavion Heights, sidelining him for nearly a year. His last match took place at a February 21 NXT live event, but he had not returned to television before his release.

WWE has not officially confirmed all the releases, and reports suggest there may be some distinction between outright releases and contract expirations that will not be renewed. Main roster talent released receives 90-day non-compete clauses, while NXT talent receives 30-day non-compete terms. Some departures may instead be contract expiration situations where WWE simply chose not to renew deals. Reports indicate that more releases may still be forthcoming.

Multiple talents were conspicuously absent from the SmackDown After WrestleMania taping that occurred on the same day as the releases, suggesting the departures were communicated to talent shortly before the announcement. Several of the released performers had been in Las Vegas just days prior participating in WrestleMania 42 festivities and meet-and-greet events, making the sudden nature of the cuts particularly jarring.

The timing of these releases follows the traditional post-WrestleMania pattern, occurring before WWE's European tour. However, the scope and impact of this year's cuts, particularly the dissolution of the Wyatt Sicks stable and the departure of several established performers, represents one of the more significant roster adjustments in recent memory. Reactions from the released talent have varied, with some expressing gratitude for their time in the company while others expressed frustration about the manner in which they learned of their departures.

🔥 SmackDown 4/24 General Coverage

WWE SMACKDOWN APRIL 24 GENERAL COVERAGE

The April 24 episode of WWE SmackDown took place at Dickies Arena in Fort Worth, Texas, serving as the primary fallout show from WrestleMania 42. The show featured championship celebrations, title changes, storyline developments, and notable appearances that set the direction for the blue brand moving forward.

TIFFANY STRATTON WINS WOMEN'S UNITED STATES CHAMPIONSHIP

Tiffany Stratton captured the WWE Women's United States Championship by defeating defending champion Giulia in the evening's primary match. The title had been pulled from the WrestleMania card despite being advertised, making this SmackDown defense the first opportunity for Stratton to pursue the gold after earning her number one contender status by defeating Jordynne Grace on the April 17 episode.

The match began with both competitors immediately going into high gear, with Giulia controlling key portions of the bout. Stratton attempted her signature Prettiest Moonsault Ever early on, but Giulia countered into a submission hold. The match then shifted dramatically when Kiana James, who accompanied Giulia to ringside, inadvertently collided with her ally during a sequence, providing an opening for Stratton to mount her comeback.

Giulia attempted to finish the match with her Northern Lights Bomb, but Stratton escaped and connected with an Alabama Slam instead. After positioning herself on the top rope, Stratton executed the Prettiest Moonsault Ever to secure the pinfall and the championship. Following the match, Stratton cut a promo noting that she had felt like a runner-up for much of the year after failing to win the Women's Elimination Chamber in February, but she no longer felt that way with the title now in her possession.

The victory marked Stratton's first reign as Women's United States Champion and ended Giulia's second run at either 112 or 113 days depending on the counting method. Stratton's momentum was interrupted when newly crowned Women's Tag Team Champions Paige and Brie Bella arrived at ringside for their own celebration.

PAIGE RETURNS FOR WOMEN'S TAG TEAM CHAMPIONSHIP CELEBRATION

Paige made her return to WWE at WrestleMania 42 and immediately captured gold alongside Brie Bella when they defeated The Irresistible Forces, Bayley and Lyra Valkyria, for the Women's Tag Team Championship. On SmackDown, the new champions came to the ring to celebrate their victory.

Brie Bella spoke about how fortunate she was to have someone she could call upon to help her at WrestleMania. Paige then addressed the crowd, noting that she had already lost her voice from the celebration but was thrilled to be home. She emphasized that SmackDown was her house and viewed WrestleMania as the perfect opportunity to return to the company.

The celebration was interrupted by former champions Charlotte Flair and Alexa Bliss, who made it clear they wanted their titles back. Despite their loss at WrestleMania, Flair and Bliss were not willing to accept the changing of the guard, and they quickly proposed a title match that Paige and Bella accepted.

TRICK WILLIAMS CELEBRATES US TITLE WIN, SAMI ZAYN ATTACKS IN DISGUISE

Trick Williams came to the ring to celebrate his United States Championship victory over Sami Zayn from WrestleMania 42. Lil Yachty, who has been appearing in Williams' corner, was at his side for the celebration, and the two were joined by a Gingerbread Man mascot for what WWE had advertised as a festive segment.

During the celebration, Lil Yachty addressed his role in WWE, making clear that his involvement was far more than a one-night celebrity cameo. He emphasized that he watches WWE religiously every Monday and Friday, and his presence was rooted in fandom rather than simply being a famous guest. Yachty compared his relationship with Williams to historical duos like Don King and Mike Tyson or Phil Jackson and Michael Jordan, emphasizing his commitment to being in Williams' corner.

The Gingerbread Man mascot participated in the celebration as pyro went off, but the segment took a sharp turn when the mascot struck Williams with a giant candy cane. The mascot then removed his head to reveal Sami Zayn underneath. Zayn delivered a Gingerbread Helluva Kick to both Williams and Yachty before dancing like a Fortnite emote and exiting the segment, making clear that despite losing at WrestleMania, his rivalry with the new champion is far from over.

Following the attack, there was speculation that Lil Yachty might have second thoughts about his association with Williams. However, earlier comments from Yachty suggested he plans to remain involved with the new champion. Williams subsequently announced that he would not be accepting open challenges for his United States Championship, indicating that any challengers would need to earn their opportunities rather than receiving automatic shots.

LIL YACHTY CLARIFIES WWE ROLE

Lil Yachty addressed the ongoing questions about his involvement in WWE and whether his participation would extend beyond a WrestleMania appearance. Standing alongside Williams, Yachty pointed out that while the celebrity road to WrestleMania may be over, he was still around and not going anywhere. His involvement, he suggested, was rooted in genuine fandom rather than a quick celebrity pop for the crowd.

Reports indicate that Yachty has begun in-ring training, suggesting that his partnership with Williams could evolve beyond his current role as a hype man and corner presence. In the immediate aftermath of the Sami Zayn attack, Yachty took physical bumps including the Helluva Kick, signaling that he is willing to be involved in physical altercations and potentially have a more hands-on role in Williams' title program.

CODY RHODES ADDRESSES TKO INFLUENCE

Cody Rhodes made an appearance on SmackDown still bearing visible effects from his WrestleMania 42 match against Randy Orton, including a black eye from the Viper's punt kick. Despite the physical toll, Rhodes remained unbowed and used his platform to send a message that extended beyond his immediate rivals.

Rhodes acknowledged that Orton had given him his best shot, but he was still standing with the Undisputed WWE Championship around his waist. However, the most significant portion of his promo involved addressing what he called "outside forces" who keep interfering with his WrestleMania plans. While not naming them directly, Rhodes was widely understood to be referencing TKO, WWE's parent company, and executives like CEO Ari Emanuel.

Rhodes referenced a pattern of interference spanning multiple years. He noted that Pat McAfee's presence at WrestleMania 42 was reportedly tied to decisions from TKO leadership, and he pointed to previous years when similar situations had occurred, including the involvement of Dwayne Johnson with Travis Scott at WrestleMania 41 and various complications around WrestleMania 40.

Declaring himself "0-3" against these outside forces, Rhodes delivered a firm message: "Send your best or count your money and mind your damn business." The statement was viewed as a carefully crafted but direct criticism of corporate interference in his storyline and booking, positioning the conflict as both a worked angle and a subtle acknowledgment of real-world tensions between creative and corporate leadership.

Rhodes also took time to address Sami Zayn, leaning into the "golden boy" label by embracing it and noting that success is not a matter of luck but rather legacy, family, and the support of fans, all wrapped in gold. He extended an open challenge across Raw, SmackDown, and NXT, emphasizing that while he is easy to find, beating him is another matter entirely.

DAMIAN PRIEST AND R-TRUTH RETAIN TAG TITLES

Damian Priest and R-Truth successfully defended the WWE Tag Team Championship against J.C. Mateo and Tama Tonga of The Muscle for the Truth (MFT) faction. The match was a rematch between the teams, as Priest and Truth had originally won the titles from MFT on March 20.

The early portion of the match saw MFT gain some momentum, with Tonga getting the better of R-Truth before colliding with the canvas during a missed elbow drop. When Priest entered the ring, he initially struggled to gain significant advantage due to interference from Mateo, who pulled him out of the ring to allow Tonga to continue his offense.

However, Priest eventually connected with a devastating clothesline on Mateo, allowing R-Truth to enter and hit his Five Knuckle Shuffle. When MFT attempted to isolate R-Truth and finish him with a swinging neckbreaker and Northern Lights Bomb combination, Priest broke up the pin attempt, showing the resilience and teamwork that has kept the champions on top.

The fresh Priest and R-Truth, who seemed to have an unlimited supply of energy, executed a Rise Up on Mateo to secure the pinfall and retain their titles. The victory extended their championship reign to 36 days and counting. As of the time of these reports, no new challengers had come forward to challenge the team for their titles.

FAN PROTEST OVER WYATT SICKS RELEASES

The SmackDown episode came immediately after WWE announced a significant round of post-WrestleMania releases that included the entire Wyatt Sicks stable. Released wrestlers included Bo Dallas (who played Uncle Howdy), Nikki Cross, Joe Gacy, Dexter Lumis, and Erick Rowan. Additionally released were Zoey Stark, Alba Fyre, Andre Chase, Kairi Sane, Aleister Black, and other talent.

The Wyatt Sicks faction had been a major storyline component of WWE television, building on the legacy of Bray Wyatt and his previous incarnations. The faction had developed a dedicated fanbase over its run.

Fans at Dickies Arena responded to these releases by using their smartphones to create a "firefly" effect with flashlights during the women's match between Rhea Ripley and Jacy Jayne of Fatal Influence. The fireflies, which had become an iconic symbol associated with Bray Wyatt throughout his WWE career, lit up various sections of the arena as a silent protest against the releases.

WWE's production team attempted to minimize the visibility of the protest by adjusting camera angles to avoid capturing the fireflies. When the production team was unable to completely avoid the imagery, they cut to a commercial break with the match in picture-in-picture format. By the time the show returned from the break, many of the fans had extinguished their lights, though the message had clearly been sent.

The firefly protest represented fan frustration with WWE's decisions regarding the Wyatt Sicks, a faction that had developed significant emotional investment and storytelling depth over its run on television.

RHEA RIPLEY FACES JACY JAYNE

Rhea Ripley appeared on SmackDown following her Women's Championship victory over Jade Cargill at WrestleMania 42. However, her presence on the show became notable not just for a championship celebration but for an in-ring encounter.

Ripley faced Jacy Jayne in a singles match, with Jayne bringing Fatal Influence to SmackDown after the group had established themselves as major forces on NXT. Fatal Influence had been highly successful in NXT, with Jayne being a two-time NXT Women's Champion and Fallon Henley holding several championships on the black and gold brand.

The match between Ripley and Jayne took place during the episode, with the firefly protest occurring during their bout as fans expressed their dissatisfaction with the recent releases.

DANHAUSEN DEFEATS THE MIZ WITH CURSE AND PYRO

Danhausen competed against The Miz in a match on SmackDown, continuing his push in WWE after being signed away from AEW. Danhausen has been generating significant momentum since his WWE arrival, performing well in matches and gaining fan support.

The match saw The Miz and his associate Kit Wilson gaining the upper hand as Danhausen was beaten down by the heel team. However, Danhausen employed his signature curse gimmick at a crucial moment. As The Miz and Kit Wilson taunted the crowd, Danhausen sat up like The Undertaker and performed his "you are cursed" gesture while getting the fans to chant along.

At precisely that moment, pyro went off that scattered both The Miz and Kit Wilson. The timing of the pyro coinciding with Danhausen's curse gesture created the appearance that his curse had caused the explosion, regardless of whether it was coincidence or planned timing. Capitalizing on the confusion and the heels' distraction, Danhausen connected with a small package for the pinfall victory.

After securing his win, Danhausen used a smoke bomb to make his exit, running off in his go-cart while fans cheered him on. The victory continued to build Danhausen's credibility in WWE and demonstrated that his unique character work is resonating with audiences.

OVERALL SHOW DIRECTION

The SmackDown episode from Fort Worth showcased WWE's focus on post-WrestleMania developments across multiple divisions and storylines. The show featured championship celebrations from new and returning champions, continuation of existing feuds with new twists, the emergence of new talent from NXT, and subtle acknowledgments of behind-the-scenes corporate tensions through Cody Rhodes' promo.

The coincidence of airing immediately after significant WWE releases created an unusual dynamic where fan sentiment was visibly expressed in real time, though WWE's production team worked to manage the visibility of the protest. The show continued WWE's recent trend of incorporating celebrity and mainstream personalities into storylines with Lil Yachty's continued presence alongside Trick Williams, while also advancing traditional wrestling narratives and championship programs.

🔥 NXT Call-Ups to SmackDown 4/24

WWE's annual post-WrestleMania roster shake-up continued on the April 24 edition of SmackDown, which aired live from Dickies Arena in Fort Worth, Texas. The show featured the debuts of multiple NXT call-ups as the company looked to inject fresh talent into its main roster following the week's significant roster changes.

The biggest in-ring debut of the evening came from Fatal Influence, the NXT faction consisting of Jacy Jayne, Fallon Henley, and Lainey Reid. The trio made their presence known during a women's tag team match featuring Paige and Brie Bella against Charlotte Flair and Alexa Bliss. Fatal Influence's interference led to the match ending in chaos, with Jacy attacking Paige and causing the bout to be thrown out. Later in the program, Jacy Jayne would challenge newly crowned Women's Champion Rhea Ripley in an impromptu singles match. Jacy and Rhea delivered a fantastic match that saw Fatal Influence once again get involved, with Henley and Nyx attacking Ripley to end the contest and allow Fatal Influence to stand tall. Jacy Jayne is a two-time NXT Women's Champion, with her second reign lasting 150 days before her call-up to the main roster.

Another major announcement came during the broadcast when it was confirmed that Ricky Saints, a former NXT Champion and NXT North American Champion, will officially make his main roster debut on next week's SmackDown. While no details were provided regarding what is in store for the Revolution and Saints going forward, the announcement itself generated excitement about his arrival to the blue brand.

In addition to those making their debuts on the April 24 episode, Blake Monroe, the former Mariah May from AEW, received a major promotional push with a hype video that aired during the broadcast. The video package, presented under her new persona "The Glamour," featured her in-ring highlights and fashion-style visuals while an announcer declared that she is beauty and grace with the caveat that she will probably punch you in the face. Monroe's own voiceover concluded the promo with her catchphrase: nothing burns brighter and darker than Blake Monroe. The video concluded with the announcement that she is "coming soon" to SmackDown, though no specific debut date was provided.

Monroe's call-up comes at a time when WWE is actively refreshing its roster with NXT talent. Earlier in the week, the Raw after WrestleMania saw the debuts of names like Ethan Page and Sol Ruca, with Joe Hendry also expected to join the main roster soon. Trick Williams, another recent NXT graduate, recently won the US Championship at WrestleMania 42.

Blake Monroe joined WWE NXT in June 2025 after her departure from AEW, where she had competed as Mariah May and held the AEW Women's World Championship. She won the Owen Hart Cup in 2024 and defeated Toni Storm for the AEW Women's World Championship at All In in London that same year. She later lost the title back to Storm in a match dubbed the Hollywood Ending, which marked her final AEW appearance.

In NXT, Monroe's debut came at the Great American Bash when she teamed with Jordynne Grace to defeat Fatal Influence. She quickly turned heel the next night by attacking Grace, kickstarting her first official feud in WWE. Monroe won the NXT Women's North American Championship at Halloween Havoc in October 2025, pinning Zaria who was defending the gold on behalf of Sol Ruca. Her title reign lasted 52 days before she lost the championship to Thea Hail in a match that generated discussion due to a botched finish. Monroe was also part of the Vanity Project group and had several rivalries that showcased her skills both as a wrestler and character performer.

Monroe's last major NXT appearance came at Stand & Deliver in April 2026, where she challenged Tatum Paxley for the Women's North American Championship and came up short. Her final NXT match took place during NXT: Revenge when she faced Paxley in a casket match, which she lost, seemingly sealing her fate for a main roster call-up that had been rumored for several months.

The SmackDown episode demonstrated WWE's commitment to keeping its programming fresh with multiple call-ups following WrestleMania. Fatal Influence's immediate impact in their debut showed that the company was positioning them as significant threats, while the announcements of both Ricky Saints and Blake Monroe's forthcoming arrivals suggest WWE has multiple interesting storylines planned for the blue brand in the coming weeks.

🔥 Ricky Saints Main Roster Debut

Absolute Ricky Saints is absolutely ready for his main roster debut. On an episode of SmackDown full of NXT call-ups and teases, Saints' incoming blue brand debut was announced via video package, with the former NXT Champion's debut set for next week in Tulsa, Oklahoma on May 1. The move comes as he is set to have one final match on NXT this coming Tuesday when he takes on Shiloh Hill.

Just before the main event, Saints' highlight reel graced the WWE Universe. In his promo package, Saints ran through his WWE highlights so far, including his run with the NXT North American Championship and with the NXT Championship. Glamorous smoulder shots were paired alongside action-packed clips showcasing his finisher, an Inverted Powerbomb/Inverted Front Powerslam affectionately named Roshambo. Saints shouted in the promo: "I am the man, because I am Absolute Ricky freakin' Saints!"

Saints isn't the only NXT talent expected to make their main roster debut that night, as former NXT women's North American Champion Blake Monroe is also slated for her breakout onto the main roster. This timing is particularly interesting as May 1 will be something of an AEW reunion on WWE soil. Before coming to WWE, Saints and Monroe wrestled in AEW, where they wrestled under the names Ricky Starks and Mariah May respectively. Saints made his first WWE appearance in February 2025, whereas Monroe stepped onto the WWE scene that following June.

The Revolution is coming to SmackDown. The former NXT Champion's call-up caps off a week of many NXT promotions. Ethan Page and Sol Ruca made their debut on Monday's Raw, with Joe Hendry set to make his official debut on Raw next week holding a concert. The previous Friday's show also saw the debut of all three members of Fatal Attraction (Jacy Jayne, Fallon Henley, and Lainey Reid). A Blake Monroe vignette also aired, making it clear she is heading to the blue brand.

WWE Main Event Landscape Analysis

The "Next Big Thing": Analyzing WWE's Main Event Landscape for Late 2026

Under Triple H's leadership, the transition from NXT to the main roster has become a calculated strategy designed to build "TV-ready" global brands for the Netflix era. As Shawn Michaels has noted, the primary goal is to create stars capable of headlining from day one. This ensures that athletes like Oba Femi or Je'Von Evans arrive as established marquee attractions for a pre-conditioned global audience, eliminating the traditional learning curve of main roster debuts.

The End of the Tribal Chief Era: Oba Femi

One name stands above all others when discussing pure physical dominance in the current landscape. Oba Femi is not just a powerhouse; he is a tactical anomaly in the ring. His transition from NXT to the main roster in early 2026 following a historic victory over Brock Lesnar proved that he can carry the weight of a brand. His "IT-factor" stems from a combination of elite Nigerian collegiate shot-put athletics and a calm, menacing charisma that feels authentic rather than manufactured.

To understand why he is the betting favorite for a potential world title run after his King of the Ring campaign, specific performance markers stand out. First, he possesses unmatched presence, commanding a room without saying a word and filling the vacuum left by the legends of the past decade. Second, his technical evolution is remarkable; despite his massive frame, his timing and safety in the ring are remarkably advanced, making him a reliable main-event worker. Third, his global marketability is undeniable; his background and unique presentation make him a perfect ambassador for WWE's expanding international footprint.

Tiffy Time is Forever: The Women's Evolution 2.0

By late 2026, the women's division has evolved beyond the "Four Horsewomen" era. The focus has shifted to stars who blend world-class athleticism with cinematic character work. Tiffany Stratton has transitioned from a promising prospect to a pillar of the industry. Her "Center of the Universe" persona has become the definitive heel archetype for the Gen-Z audience.

The landscape is also shaped by international dominance. Giulia and Stephanie Vaquer brought a "hard-hitting" style that forced the entire roster to adapt or be left behind. Sol Ruca, after returning from injury, has redefined what fans expect from high-flyers in the women's division through her innovative offense.

The Charisma of the Netflix Era

As WWE moves fully into its partnership with major streaming platforms, the requirements for a top star are fundamentally changing. High-definition cameras and global audiences demand personalities that feel larger than life. The modern fan expects a seamless experience whether they are watching a main event on their television or checking information on their mobile devices between matches. This digital-first environment favors stars like Trick Williams, who understands how to go viral through social media clips and cinematic entrance presentations.

The Pillars of 2026: Key Candidates

While many athletes show promise, only a few possess the resilience to stay at the top. Several athletes are currently the front-runners for the "Next Big Thing" status. Bron Breakker, currently on the road to recovery after a heroic performance leading into WrestleMania 42, remains the bridge between the Steiner family intensity and modern explosiveness. Trick Williams represents the "vibe" of the current generation, possessing a connection with the live audience that is almost impossible to manufacture. Je'Von Evans, at 22 years old, is already a mainstay on Raw, offering a high-flying style that brings a different dynamic to the heavyweight-heavy main event scene. Tony D'Angelo, having just secured the NXT Championship at Stand & Deliver in St. Louis in April 2026, has proven that character-driven personas are vital for the streaming era.

The NIL Gamble: Risk vs. Reward

The recruitment process has shifted toward NIL athletes, bringing in top-tier talent from various sports backgrounds into the wrestling ecosystem. This strategy represents both significant opportunity and considerable risk for the organization's long-term main event viability.

Jordynne Grace NXT Backstage Incident

Jordynne Grace has opened up about running into backstage issues in WWE following her hard-hitting NXT showdown with Giulia last year, revealing that one particular move led to her match getting flagged behind the scenes. Speaking with Adrian Hernandez of UNLIKELY, Grace reflected on her No. 1 contender bout against Giulia for the NXT Championship in May 2025, a match that was widely praised by fans but reportedly didn't land the same way internally with WWE officials.

According to Grace, the concern stemmed from her use of the Michinoku Driver, which is a move that is restricted in WWE due to the potential risk of neck and spine injuries, with only a modified version allowed in the company's style. However, Grace admitted she wasn't aware of the specific WWE version restriction at the time she used it.

"I'll give you a little behind-the-scenes information, we got in trouble for that match," she said. "They just said it was too much. I do the Michinoku Driver. At the time, I did not know that they don't do the Michinoku Driver from Japan here."

She continued, explaining that WWE instead uses a safer variation of the move where the opponent is seated differently on impact. "They do the one where you sit the person out all the way. I didn't know that was a thing until after this match."

Despite the backstage reaction, Grace made it clear she remains a fan of both the match and her opponent, Giulia, while also expressing interest in running it back under different circumstances down the line. "It was not taken very well but I loved the match. I love wrestling Giulia, she's awesome. I cannot wait to wrestle her again," she said. "Those matches are really the best moments for me because that's when the wrestlers really get to feel something, right? They feel like it's real. It is real but it's like real in that moment."
