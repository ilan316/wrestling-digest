AEW Redemption Adds Two Gimmick Matches Following Injuries and Ongoing Feuds

AEW's new Redemption pay-per-view event on Sunday, July 26, 2026, at the Bell Centre in Montreal, Quebec, Canada, has seen its card significantly altered with the addition of two new gimmick matches announced by AEW head honcho Tony Khan.

After Dynamite ended on Wednesday night, July 22, Tony Khan went live on social media to share updates regarding the company's weekend plans. Khan had originally factored lucha libre superstar Mistico into the Redemption card, but complications with Mistico's dual contract situation have forced a change. Mistico, who also holds a contract with CMLL and has never been defeated in AEW, sustained injuries last Friday night at Arena Mexico. Due to these injuries, Mistico was not cleared by medical personnel to work for AEW this week or to compete at Redemption. Khan expressed hope that Mistico will be available when AEW returns to Mexico City for Grand Slam in early August.

Khan had envisioned Mistico as a perfect opponent for the winner of Redemption's International Championship match between defending champion Kyle Fletcher and Ring of Honor World Champion Bandido. With Mistico unavailable, Khan pivoted and announced a six-way ladder match to determine the next challenger for the International title. The winner of this match will earn a future shot at whoever holds the belt following the Fletcher-Bandido encounter.

Four competitors were selected by random draw for the ladder match: Jack Perry, The Beast Mortos, Komander, and Speedball Mike Bailey. Two additional spots will be filled by winners of qualifying matches scheduled for Saturday's AEW Collision. Nick Wayne will face AR Fox in one qualifier, while Lio Rush will take on El Clon in the other. Both Clon and Mortos are returning to AEW action for the first time in considerable time due to previous visa issues that have affected their availability.

The second new gimmick match announced was a double chain tag team bout stemming from the heated rivalry between the Bang Bang Gang and The Dogs. During Dynamite, Jay White defeated Clark Connors in what marked White's first singles match in over a year. Following the match, David Finlay of The Dogs attacked White with his signature shillelagh and the faction quickly retreated before the rest of the Bang Bang Gang could respond. In a backstage promo, Juice Robinson of the Bang Bang Gang issued the challenge, stating that since The Dogs want to attack from behind, the Bang Bang Gang would chain them up to fight face-to-face.

The double chain match will see Jay White and Juice Robinson of the Bang Bang Gang face off against Clark Connors and David Finlay of The Dogs. This bout marks an escalation of their ongoing feud, which traces back to White's surprise return at Forbidden Door where he targeted The Dogs and initiated the rivalry. The Dogs' last tag team outing saw them unsuccessfully challenge Adam Copeland and Christian Cage for the AEW World Tag Team Championship at Forbidden Door. Meanwhile, the Bang Bang Gang, including Robinson, Ace Austin, and the Gunns, previously defeated The Dogs when they teamed with the Young Bucks against The Dogs and the Death Riders on Collision in May.

The AEW Redemption card now includes several championship matches and additional high-profile bouts. Kenny Omega will defend the AEW World Championship against Kevin Knight, with MJF reportedly serving as a creative driving force behind this match. The Don Callis Family member Knight will put his TNT Championship on the line against Darby Allin, having recently turned on Allin to join the Family. Kyle Fletcher will defend his International Championship against Bandido, though tension within the Don Callis Family surfaced when Fletcher announced that ringside bans would be in effect for his match. Tensions escalated when Kazuchika Okada hit Fletcher with the Rainmaker, allowing Bandido to secure a pin during their tag team interaction.

Additional title defenses scheduled for Redemption include Thekla defending the AEW Women's World Championship against Willow Nightingale, Hikaru Shida defending the TBS Championship against Maya World, and Mark Davis defending the AEW National Championship against Andrade El Idolo. The AEW World Tag Team Championships will be on the line as Adam Copeland and Christian Cage face the Death Riders' Claudio Castagnoli and PAC. The Young Bucks will face Jon Moxley and Will Ospreay in a non-title bout, and Chris Jericho will face Tommaso Ciampa in a no holds barred match.

The AEW Redemption card represents the company's first event under the Redemption banner in Montreal ahead of the major AEW All In London 2026 event scheduled for August 30 at Wembley Stadium. The company has utilized the show to address immediate storyline developments and create fresh opportunities for wrestlers to earn title shots, particularly following the unexpected unavailability of established plans that had to be quickly revised.

🔥 AEW Dynamite July 22 episode coverage

The July 22 episode of AEW Dynamite delivered strong viewership numbers and a mixed critical reception as the promotion built toward its Redemption pay-per-view event. The show pulled in 786,000 viewers, demonstrating the audience's continued investment following the previous week's Beach Break special that averaged 773,000 viewers. Additionally, Dynamite posted a 0.15 rating in the key 18-49 demographic, which tied for the highest number the program has recorded in that category this year. Tony Khan acknowledged the momentum, noting that every show is important given the multiple championship fights featured across recent episodes and the high stakes involved.

The evening kicked off with Kevin Knight successfully defending his AEW TNT Championship against Darby Allin, marking Knight's record tenth title defense. The match took place in the same building as their first encounter in November during the Continental Classic, where Knight had accidentally sent Darby to the hospital and forced him out of the tournament. This time, with a clear face-heel dynamic and Knight positioned as the villain who had previously injured Darby, the booking added layers to their rivalry. The match itself delivered on the in-ring action, with Darby launching himself out of a cannon early on, taking Knight into the crowd and hitting an elbow through the timekeeper's table within minutes. The match progressed with Darby being crotched on the guardrail and slingshot into steel steps while bleeding, all before the first commercial break.

The bout felt like a fireworks display from start to finish, moving at a rapid pace that felt snappier and quicker than their previous May encounter over the AEW World Championship, though not quite reaching those heights. However, the finish came via a referee bump that seemed somewhat unnecessary, as the non-clean conclusion kept Darby strong following his recent world title reign while still allowing Knight, who was headlining Redemption, to retain his championship. This booking decision drew mixed reactions, as while it made logical sense to protect both competitors, it slightly detracted from an otherwise excellent match. The encounter felt like a combination of their two previous matches put on fast forward, and had it not been for the dirty finish, it could have staked a claim as the best of their trilogy.

The women's division received significant attention with two championship bouts. The AEW Women's World Tag Team Championship match featured Megan Bayne and Lena Kross defending their titles against the Sisters of Sin consisting of Julia Hart and Skye Blue. The contest was contested under No Disqualification rules, making weapons fair game for all competitors. The match took a chaotic turn when Julia Hart grabbed a screwdriver with the intention of using it against the champions, but in the fast-paced action, she accidentally struck her own tag team partner Skye Blue instead, leaving Blue bleeding from her forehead. Despite this setback and showing considerable resilience, the Sisters of Sin were unable to overcome the champions. After Sisters of Sin put Megan Bayne through a table and came close to capturing the championships with a series of tandem attacks, momentum shifted when Bayne chokeslammed Skye Blue off the apron and through another table. Hart battled on alone, even locking Bayne in an octopus hold, but Divine Dominion ultimately retained the titles following a double spinebuster to Hart for the pinfall. The accidental screwdriver strike proved to be a defining moment in the match, as the challengers never fully recovered from the mistake.

The build to the women's world championship match at Redemption was highlighted by a heated confrontation between champion Thekla and challenger Willow Nightingale. The segment was moderated by hardcore icon Mick Foley, who brought star power and legitimacy to the title program. The discussion began with verbal exchanges before quickly escalating into physicality. Thekla questioned Nightingale's upbeat personality, accusing her of hiding behind a fake smile, while Nightingale fired back by suggesting Thekla's confidence masked personal insecurities. Nightingale declared her willingness to embrace the same sick and twisted mindset Foley became famous for if it meant winning the championship, showing her determination to do whatever necessary to capture the gold.

The confrontation reached its boiling point when Thekla slapped Nightingale across the side of her head with her signature leather opera glove, drawing blood. Thekla escalated matters further by using a microphone to strike her challenger after briefly using Foley as a human shield. However, Nightingale recovered from these cheap shots and answered with a thunderous Pounce that sent the champion flying out of the ring. Later in the evening, Nightingale posted on social media showing fans the effects of the attack, revealing blood around her ear in a short video paired with a collage featuring herself alongside Foley. Her caption read "Talk about being on that Mick Foley sheee," reinforcing the message she had delivered earlier in the night that she was prepared to absorb punishment in pursuit of the AEW Women's World Championship. The intensity and physicality of their confrontation suggested that their title match at Redemption would be anything but a standard contest.

While the show succeeded in generating strong viewership and delivering quality in-ring action, critical reception was mixed regarding certain storytelling elements. The tag team championship scene drew particular criticism, with the backstage assault between Cope and Christian versus PAC and Claudio Castagnoli described as unconvincing and failing to elevate the program.
