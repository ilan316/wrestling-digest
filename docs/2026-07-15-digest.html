🔥 AEW Queen of the Ring Lawsuit

AEW has filed a lawsuit against Ring Productions LLC, the production company behind the 2025 wrestling biopic Queen of the Ring, alleging the company failed to pay more than $105,000 owed under a promotional sponsorship agreement. The lawsuit claims that Ring Productions and AEW entered into a sponsorship deal in January 2025 valued at approximately $350,000, under which the wrestling promotion agreed to extensively promote the film across multiple platforms.

As part of the agreement, AEW implemented a comprehensive promotional campaign that included sponsored matches on AEW television programming, front-row appearances and ringside positioning for members of the film's cast at AEW events, broadcast of the movie's trailer during AEW shows and live arena events, and social media promotion from AEW talent and wrestlers. Notable cast members, including actress Damaris Lewis who played Babs Wingo, made appearances at AEW events, including a memorable moment during the Revolution 2025 pay-per-view main event. AEW wrestlers such as Mercedes Moné, Harley Cameron, and Christopher Daniels attended the film's premiere and promoted the movie across their social media accounts.

According to the lawsuit filing, Ring Productions paid $250,000 of the agreed-upon sponsorship fee by May 23, 2025. However, AEW claims that Ring Productions missed a payment deadline on February 28 and still owes $100,000 from the sponsorship agreement. The lawsuit also seeks more than $5,000 in travel costs related to talent appearances and promotional activities. This brings the total amount AEW claims it is owed to more than $105,000.

The sponsorship agreement reportedly includes a 1.5 percent monthly interest rate on late payments, which AEW is also seeking as part of the lawsuit. According to the filing, AEW made multiple attempts to resolve the matter outside of court before pursuing legal action. The promotion contacted Ring Productions by email four times between September and December 2025 but received no response. AEW then hired the law firm Gunster Yoakley & Stewart to handle the matter, which sent a formal demand letter to Ring Productions on March 24, 2026, giving the company seven days to pay the outstanding balance.

According to the lawsuit, Ring Productions Chief Operating Officer Paul Leighton acknowledged that the company owed $105,262.51, but AEW says no payment was made following this acknowledgment. The lawsuit was filed on June 26, 2026, in the Fourth Judicial Circuit in Duval County, Florida. Notably, the sponsorship agreement reportedly includes an arbitration clause stating that disputes should be handled through JAMS, an arbitration service. However, AEW chose to file the lawsuit in court rather than pursue arbitration.

As of the time of reporting, Ring Productions has not filed a response to the lawsuit and has not yet been officially served with the legal documents. Once served, the company will have 20 days to respond to the allegations according to standard legal procedure.

Queen of the Ring, released in March 2025, is a biographical drama based on the life of pioneering women's wrestling legend Mildred Burke, portrayed by Emily Bett Rickards. The film chronicles Burke's rise as one of wrestling's first major female stars and her struggle for recognition in a male-dominated industry. The cast includes AEW talent Toni Storm, Kamille, and Britt Baker, along with WWE Superstar Naomi, wrestling legend Mickie James, and other wrestling personalities. Jim Cornette appeared in the film, and Jim Ross served as an executive producer.

Despite the promotional efforts and star-studded cast, Queen of the Ring had a limited theatrical run and performed modestly at the box office, grossing only $657,718 during its theatrical release.

🔥 Keith Lee AEW Status Uncertainty

Keith Lee's wrestling future remains deeply uncertain as retirement speculation grows within the industry. Lee, 41, has not wrestled since defeating Brian Cage at AEW Collision: Holiday Bash in December 2023, when he was pulled from Worlds End that same month. He later revealed he had been dealing with an injury that lingered since 2022.

Although Lee remained under contract with AEW throughout his absence, he has not made an in-ring return despite telling fans on multiple occasions that his health had been good for some time. In summer 2025, he stated that his health had been excellent, and in September 2025, he tweeted that he would "certainly" be making an in-ring return, though he admitted he did not know when or how that comeback would happen.

The speculation about Lee's status intensified following news that he and his wife, Mia Yim, had divorced. Mia Yim posted that while they are no longer a couple, they remain friends and still have love for each other. Lee himself has not posted on social media since September 2025.

During Wrestling Observer Live, Bryan Alvarez discussed Lee's status with F4WOnline.com, noting that while he does not have firsthand knowledge of the situation, the belief among many within wrestling is that Lee's career may be over. Alvarez stressed that he could not confirm the situation with 100 percent certainty but emphasized what he was hearing from people within the industry.

"There's been a lot of talk about Keith Lee over the last couple of days. I'm not saying this 100 percent, okay? I'm not saying this 100 percent because I don't know. But from talking to people, the belief within the business is that Keith Lee is done," Alvarez stated. "And we're not talking like, you know, when he's done with AEW, if that ever happens, he'll go elsewhere. It is believed that he is likely just retired. So, we won't get the return to AEW if that's the case. Obviously, we won't get that Swerve match."

The Swerve match referenced by Alvarez would have reunited Lee with Swerve Strickland, his former AEW Tag Team Champion partner, in what many fans had anticipated would be a major returning angle.

Earlier reports had suggested that there was some activity surrounding a potential return. In June, it was reported that AEW had discussed a potential Lee return "a number of months ago" and had been working on tentative creative plans. However, the report indicated that Lee had not been backstage at AEW shows and it was unclear whether he had even been made aware of the return discussions. Nothing ultimately came from those conversations.

Lee's extended absence contrasts sharply with his previous career achievements. He is a former AEW Tag Team Champion with Swerve Strickland and previously held the NXT Championship and PWG World Championship during his pre-AEW career, establishing himself as one of professional wrestling's most impressive physical specimens and promising talents.

At this point, Keith Lee has not publicly called his career to an end, leaving open the possibility that he could prove the industry speculation wrong. However, if the people Alvarez spoke to are correct, the comeback that fans have been waiting years to see may never happen. Until Lee himself makes a definitive statement about his future, his status remains officially unresolved, though the prevailing sentiment within wrestling circles suggests his in-ring career has already concluded.

🔥 Buddy Matthews AEW Return

Buddy Matthews is officially cleared to wrestle again after more than a year of injuries, surgery, and frustrating setbacks. The AEW star confirmed the news himself on Instagram, sharing a cryptic message that made it clear his long road to recovery has finally come to an end.

"The Devil signed the clearance. Hell stamped its approval. No more barriers. Only timing," Matthews posted on Tuesday, seemingly implying he has been cleared by the company and is awaiting the right time to return to in-ring competition.

Matthews was originally injured at AEW Grand Slam Australia in his native country in February 2025. The incident occurred when he jumped from the top rope into the ring during his entrance and landed awkwardly on his ankle. Despite the injury, he proceeded to compete in his scheduled Continental Championship match against Kazuchika Okada, initially believing the injury would not sideline him for an extended period. However, his recovery proved far more complicated than expected, ultimately leading to ankle surgery and a lengthy rehabilitation process.

Throughout his recovery, Matthews faced additional complications that prolonged his absence from the ring. In December 2025, he revealed that lingering nerve damage and numbness had slowed his rehabilitation significantly, making it uncertain when he would be able to return. More recently, however, he showed himself training again and hinted that a comeback was getting close, writing that he had "worked through the setbacks" and that his comeback would "speak for itself."

A few months ago, Matthews' wife, WWE Superstar Rhea Ripley, revealed that he had resumed in-ring training as part of his recovery. The couple were also in attendance at a recent Power Slap event, where former WWE and AEW star Jake Hager was knocked out by former TNA standout Mahabali Shera.

Although Matthews made a surprise appearance for indie promotion World Series Wrestling in his home country of Australia in May, he admitted at the time that he still hadn't received full medical clearance. That has now changed. With Matthews officially cleared, he is expected to return to action during World Series Wrestling's October and November tour across Australia and New Zealand, where he'll be wrestling on a five-show tour.

While AEW has yet to officially announce Matthews' return, his latest comments suggest that his long-awaited in-ring comeback could be on the horizon. The company is holding its next pay-per-view, titled AEW Redemption, on July 26, though the only bout confirmed for the show so far is an AEW Women's World Championship match pitting Thekla against a newly-returned Willow Nightingale. When Matthews will be returning to AEW programming specifically remains to be seen.

Before his injury, Matthews was teaming alongside Brody King as the Hounds of Hell, following the departure of their former House of Black leader, Malakai Black, who left AEW to return to WWE. During Matthews' absence, King has since started teaming with Bandido, and the pair even had a run with the AEW World Tag Team Championships. King has also competed in singles action, including a feud with Swerve Strickland in the first half of 2026.

After 17 months of rehab, setbacks, and waiting, Buddy Matthews has finally cleared the last hurdle in his recovery journey. Now it's just a matter of when and where he wrestles next.

Chris Jericho AEW Career

Chris Jericho wants his career remembered the way people remember legendary rock and roll bands that endure through multiple eras and evolution. The veteran wrestler, now 55 years old, isn't rushing toward retirement and maintains that he still performs at an elite level despite becoming a more polarizing figure in recent years.

In an interview with Fox News Digital, Jericho discussed how he hopes fans will view his legacy once he eventually steps away from in-ring competition. He used The Rolling Stones as his primary comparison point, noting that the iconic band has been around for 60 years and has experienced many different eras, some of which fans loved and others they didn't enjoy as much.

"I think it's like a great rock and roll band, you know?" Jericho said. "I mean, the Rolling Stones just put out a new album, and it's amazing, but the Stones have been 60 years and some of the eras you like, some of the eras you hate, some of the eras you wish they would have gone away, and some of the eras you wish they would have done more, and you're still glad that they're here. I think when I'm done, people will be sad that I'm not around anymore."

Jericho made headlines earlier when there were rumors he could return to WWE around the start of 2026, but he ultimately decided to remain with AEW, where he's currently involved in a feud with Tommaso Ciampa. It's worth noting that Jericho was the first-ever AEW World Champion when the company launched in 2019, making him a foundational figure in the promotion's history.

The wrestling veteran has faced considerable criticism on social media from fans who believe he has overstayed his welcome in the business. However, Jericho doesn't seem bothered by these complaints and addressed them directly when speaking with Fox News Digital. He argued that critics don't realize how good they have it while his career is still ongoing.

National gas prices have climbed to an average of $3.83 per gallon as of mid-July 2026, up significantly from $2.73 earlier in the year due to escalations in the Middle East. Higher fuel costs drive up transportation expenses and increase the cost of goods transported by truck, affecting everything from concessions to hotel accommodations. Many economists describe the current economic situation as a "K-Shaped" economy, where the gap between wealthy and working-class consumers has widened considerably. Consumers have not fully recovered from the pricing shocks of COVID-19 or the inflation surge of 2021-2023, making discretionary spending on expensive events increasingly difficult for average wrestling fans.
