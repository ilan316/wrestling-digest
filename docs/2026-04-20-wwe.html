🔥 WrestleMania 42 Night Two - Roman Reigns defeats CM Punk for World Heavyweight Championship

ROMAN REIGNS CAPTURES WORLD TITLE, SIGNALS END OF PART-TIME ERA

Roman Reigns closed out WrestleMania 42 Night Two in dominant fashion, dethroning CM Punk to become the new WWE World Heavyweight Champion in a brutally physical main event inside Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas on April 19, 2026. After nearly 35 minutes of all-out warfare, Reigns secured victory by delivering back-to-back spears to the exhausted Punk, pinning him for the three-count to claim his seventh WWE world title overall and his first world championship reign since his legendary 1,316-day Undisputed WWE Championship run ended at the hands of Cody Rhodes at WrestleMania 40.

The match between Reigns and Punk delivered the kind of high-stakes battle fans anticipated, with months of heated personal promos and physical confrontations building to this moment. The contest quickly turned into a brawl, with both competitors taking the action outside the ring repeatedly. Reigns sent Punk crashing through the Spanish announce table via a devastating powerbomb while Punk responded with a top-rope elbow drop that sent Reigns through the English announce table. The physicality escalated when Reigns busted Punk open by striking him with the steel ring steps while Punk was trapped in a tree of woe position on the outside apron. Punk later returned the favor, getting his own blood on Reigns during their brutal exchange.

Throughout the match, both men teased and hit signature moves from their opponent's arsenals, creating moments of near-falls that kept the crowd on edge. Punk landed multiple GTS attempts, even attempting a Superman Punch that Reigns countered. At one point, Punk grabbed the ula fala from the crowd and mockingly prepared to hit Reigns' signature spear, only to run directly into Reigns' grip as he began choking out the challenger. The momentum shifted repeatedly as both men dug deep into their reserves, with submission exchanges and near-falls punctuating the main event.

Late in the match, with both competitors clearly exhausted and bloodied, Punk nearly pulled off an upset by landing a low blow followed by another GTS attempt. Reigns miraculously kicked out, keeping his championship dreams alive. The final sequence saw both men collapse in the ring after failed offensive attempts. Reigns was the first to recover and summon enough strength to hit a massive spear on Punk. Not satisfied, he returned to the ropes and connected with a second spear moments later, finally putting Punk away for the pinfall victory. The finish was met with a massive roar from the crowd as Reigns celebrated with his family at ringside.

Hall of Famer Mark Henry was among those deeply impressed by the main event, calling it the best match he had seen since The Undertaker versus Shawn Michaels at WrestleMania 26. In a video shared on social media, Henry stated: "I just want to let y'all know that this is the best match that I've seen since Taker and Shawn Michaels' last match. I was excited, you didn't know where it was going, either way, who was gonna win, who was gonna lose. It was like watching Rakim freestyle against KRS-One, Kendrick Lamar or something like that. It was just really elite, above and beyond wrestling. Everything that they did worked, even when things didn't work and they both went down, that was a win for us as fans."

Following his title victory, Reigns appeared on the WrestleMania 42 post-show where he reflected on his match against Punk. When asked if he gained respect for Punk after their encounter, Reigns offered a measured response that acknowledged Punk's professionalism despite their personal differences. "The fact that I'm the Tribal Chief and I still hear people out here chanting for CM Punk, I have to respect that. Do I respect the man and his personal life? I don't know. But when it comes to when he steps into the arena, when the ring's set up, when the pyro is gonna go off, I got a lot of respect for the man that steps into the arena and performs in front of all these people because he puts his heart and he puts his soul, he puts his physicality on the line," Reigns said.

He continued, "Do we see eye-to-eye? Hell no. Are we cut from the same fabric? Hell no. But at the same time, we're a part of the same fraternity, just like you and I, just like me and Big E. There's very few of us. So, when you can go out there and you can main event, and you can put it on the line, and you can actually hold your own. CM Punk didn't just run his mouth. He backed it up. He just ran into a better man today."

On the match itself, Reigns reflected on the subjective nature of professional wrestling and how he and Punk found common ground despite their vastly different approaches. "It feels good. It feels as it should. That's what's so pure about our business is we know right away, there's nothing like this. That's the beauty of how subjective this is, you have two extreme examples of professional wrestlers and sports entertainers in me and CM Punk, and somehow we can go out there and find a middle. That's how I love this business," Reigns explained.

Meanwhile, CM Punk's reaction to the loss told its own story. A fan captured footage of Punk walking backstage inside Allegiant Stadium immediately after losing the title, showing him moving slowly and deliberately, clearly feeling the toll of the brutal encounter. When a fan reached out to acknowledge him, Punk stopped briefly, and his emotion came through in a raw, unfiltered moment. "I'm sorry, it fucking stings," Punk said before giving the fan a quick fist bump and continuing toward the back. His body language made it clear that losing the World Heavyweight Championship opportunity on the grandest stage of them all was not something he would brush off easily.

REIGNS ENDS PART-TIME STATUS, ANNOUNCES FULL-TIME PRESENCE FOR SUMMER 2026

In a major announcement that signals a significant shift in how WWE will utilize its newest world champion, Roman Reigns addressed long-standing criticism about his part-time schedule during the WrestleMania 42 post-show. When "part-timer" chants erupted from the crowd, Reigns responded directly and decisively. "I'm gonna take you all to Monday Night Raw tomorrow night. All this part-time crap? We're gonna fix that. Y'all better make some room, because you're gonna see my ass all Summer!" Reigns declared, making it clear that fans should expect to see him on WWE television far more frequently moving forward.

This marks a dramatic departure from his arrangement over the past several years, where Reigns has operated on a selective schedule, picking the dates he works to maintain his health and spend time at home. With the World Heavyweight Championship now around his waist, Reigns is committing to a full-time presence throughout the summer months of 2026, beginning with an appearance on the April 20 episode of Monday Night Raw at the T-Mobile Arena in Las Vegas.

The announcement addressed a significant narrative point that had been pushed heavily during the build to this match, with CM Punk criticizing Reigns for being absent while holding championship gold. Punk had declared that "the marquee says wrestling, not cinema," seemingly referencing Reigns' Hollywood endeavors and limited in-ring schedule. With this new commitment, Reigns appears to be directly answering those criticisms by committing to a more active schedule at least through the summer.

OBA FEMI EMERGES AS NEXT CHALLENGER; REIGNS ISSUES MEASURED RESPONSE

Earlier in the WrestleMania 42 Night Two card, Nigerian rising star Oba Femi made perhaps the biggest statement of his fledgling WWE career by defeating Brock Lesnar in under five minutes in the opening match of the main show. The victory was so emphatic and shocking that it immediately put Femi on the map as a serious threat in the WWE hierarchy. The win came just months after Femi made his main roster debut as part of the 2026 Royal Rumble match in January, defeating notable competitors like Kit Wilson, The Miz, Johnny Gargano, and Rusev en route to his historic victory over The Beast.

I think in the coming weeks is the establishment of a new main event player. And I think at the end of the year, the OTC can get some!" Femi boldly proclaimed, showing no hesitation in aiming straight at Reigns.

This dynamic sets up a potentially significant program down the line, particularly if Femi can maintain his current trajectory and impress during the summer months.

🔥 Oba Femi defeats Brock Lesnar at WrestleMania 42, Lesnar retires

Brock Lesnar's era in WWE seemingly came to an end on Sunday, April 19, 2026, when he lost to Oba Femi in the opening match of WrestleMania 42 Night Two at Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas, Nevada. The match lasted less than five minutes, with Femi scoring a decisive victory over the legendary former WWE and UFC Champion.

The match itself was competitive in its early moments, with both men establishing they could trade blows evenly. Femi notably stood his ground against consecutive Lesnar clotheslines before sending The Beast down with one of his own. However, Lesnar's experience soon showed as he took control, throwing Femi into the steps and planting him with an F5. The momentum seemed to be firmly on Lesnar's side as fans braced for another dominant Lesnar victory.

But Femi's resilience proved to be the deciding factor. After being hit with the F5, Femi powered back to his feet in a moment that drew a massive pop from the crowd. The young powerhouse then delivered a pop-up chokeslam followed by his signature Fall From Grace, pinning Lesnar cleanly for the three-count. No shenanigans or interference tainted the victory. Oba Femi had straight up defeated Brock Lesnar at WrestleMania.

What followed the match told the real story. As Femi celebrated and made his way up the ramp, Lesnar remained laid out in the ring for several minutes. When he eventually rose, an emotional Lesnar began removing his gear, a traditional wrestling signal long associated with retirement. He slowly unlaced his boots and removed his gloves as "Thank you Brock" chants echoed through Allegiant Stadium. Paul Heyman then entered the ring, and the two longtime collaborators embraced as both men appeared visibly emotional. Lesnar then kissed the mat before walking out of the ring with Heyman, all but officially announcing his retirement from in-ring competition.

While WWE has not released an official statement confirming Lesnar's retirement, the symbolic gestures and the reactions from wrestling's biggest names make it clear that fans may have just witnessed The Beast Incarnate's final match. Lesnar flashed the X, the industry sign sometimes associated with injury, before departing. Commentators acknowledged the retirement implications during the broadcast.

The reaction to Lesnar's apparent retirement was immediate and widespread across social media. WWE Hall of Famer Kurt Angle, who shares extensive history with Lesnar, posted a heartfelt tribute on social media shortly after the match concluded. Angle wrote, "Love you my brother. There will only be one like you. Enjoy retirement Brock," along with a photo of the two men from their WrestleMania 19 encounter, where Lesnar defeated Angle for the WWE Championship. The message served as a respectful acknowledgment of the end of an era.

Kofi Kingston also weighed in with a humorous response referencing his own infamous quick loss to Lesnar years ago. Kingston posted, "Avenged...at long last," suggesting that Femi's victory felt like justice for past victims of Lesnar's dominance. Femi himself had previously joked about Lesnar's dominance over certain wrestlers, noting that almost no one had escaped Lesnar's path except for Cody Rhodes, jokingly referring to him as "Raheem" and suggesting he seemed "immune" to Lesnar's dominance.

The backstage details surrounding the match added another layer of significance to the moment. Paul Heyman, Lesnar's longtime on-screen advocate, was listed as a producer for both the Lesnar versus Femi match and the WrestleMania 42 main event between CM Punk and Roman Reigns. This meant Heyman's involvement went beyond his on-screen role, placing him directly in the construction and planning of what may have been his most important client's final match.

For Oba Femi, the victory represents a massive milestone in his young WWE career. Femi was brought into WWE as part of the company's NIL program for college athletes and quickly made an impact.
