Steven Borden Jr. NJPW Debut

# Sting's Son Steven Borden Jr. Makes Successful NJPW Debut at Academy Showcase

Steven Borden Jr., the son of WWE Hall of Famer Sting, marked another significant milestone in his budding professional wrestling career by making his New Japan Pro-Wrestling debut this past weekend. The second-generation wrestler competed at the NJPW Academy 3rd Anniversary Showcase, held at the LA Dojo on March 21.

In his NJPW debut match, Borden teamed with Allan Breeze in tag-team action against Hitt and Vin Parker. The match showcased both the young wrestler's potential and his respect for his legendary father's legacy. Borden paid tribute to Sting by incorporating some of his father's signature-style offense into his performance, adding an extra layer of intrigue to his outing.

Borden ultimately secured the victory for his team, catching Parker off a springboard jump and dropping him with a Full Nelson Slam before pinning him to get the win. According to reports of the match, Breeze and Parker kicked off the bout with multiple attempts to wear down each other before Borden and Hitt were tagged in simultaneously. Despite Hitt's imposing physique, Borden managed to gain the upper hand. He took several brutal slams during the bout but sustained momentum throughout, eventually pinning Parker to secure the victory.

This NJPW appearance represents another step forward in Borden's young career as he continues to gain experience across multiple wrestling platforms. He originally made his professional wrestling debut in October 2025 at the 52W Hardway show in New York City. Since then, Borden has competed in a series of dark matches for All Elite Wrestling, where AEW CEO Tony Khan has described him as someone the company is developing for the future.

Beyond his AEW work, Borden has also appeared at a pair of DEFY Wrestling shows, competing against Kiran Grey on both occasions, as well as at ROH tapings. Earlier this year, NJPW's Fred Rosser announced that he was training Borden, further demonstrating the interest major wrestling organizations have in the second-generation talent.

As of now, it remains unclear whether Borden will make future appearances under the NJPW banner, whether at the academy, the NJPW STRONG brand, or at shows in Japan. However, with multiple organizations showing interest in his development and his strong debut performance, the young wrestler appears poised for continued growth in the professional wrestling industry.

Indi Hartwell TNA Wrestling

# TNA Wrestling and WWE's Evolving Partnership: Stars Share Their Experiences

The partnership between TNA Wrestling and WWE has created a unique environment where talent from both promotions are crossing over and working together. Recent interviews with several wrestlers who have made the transition reveal insights into how this collaboration is reshaping the wrestling landscape.

## Indi Hartwell's First Year in TNA

Indi Hartwell has been reflecting on her inaugural year in TNA Wrestling, describing her journey to the promotion as unexpected but meaningful. The former WWE NXT star credits Gail Kim, a legendary TNA figure, with reaching out and encouraging her to make the move.

"I had Gail Kim reach out to me. She's no longer with the company, but she's done so much for TNA, and she's someone that I grew up watching on TNA," Hartwell explained. "She reached out to me and asked if it's something that I'd want to do, and I was so glad she did because I was such a huge fan of TNA growing up. I don't know if I ever saw myself going there, but I guess everything happens for a reason."

Since her debut almost a year ago, Hartwell has found herself in a unique position within TNA's roster. She shares the locker room with familiar faces from her NXT days while also working with fresh talent she had never encountered before. "It's funny because I feel like I'm sharing a locker room now with girls that I used to wrestle with in NXT," she noted.

During her time in TNA, Hartwell has developed respect for several rising stars, particularly highlighting Dani Luna as a talented, hard-hitting competitor deserving of more attention. She has also reconnected with wrestlers like Tessa Blanchard, whom she had worked with years before joining WWE.

## The Digital Scanning Experience

While Hartwell was building her TNA career, she also reflected on her time in WWE and the unique processes involved in that promotion. She described the behind-the-scenes procedures for creating WWE action figures and video game representations, comparing the two scanning methods.

The action figure scanning process was relatively quick, taking approximately five minutes, while the video game scanning involved a more elaborate setup in a trailer with multiple cameras positioned in a circle around the performer. "For the video game, you're in a trailer. You're sitting in a chair, and then the girls had no makeup on at first. And then there's just a circle of cameras around you, and there's an iPad with Drake on it – like Drake the rapper – and he's making faces, and you've got to copy the faces that he's doing," Hartwell explained.

The process continues with makeup application followed by additional photography before the digital data is compiled for the game. Hartwell expressed satisfaction with her 2K model, stating, "My 2K model looks so good. I was so happy with it."

## Understanding the Stylistic Differences

NXT star Kelani Jordan has also provided perspective on the differences between WWE and TNA Wrestling. Jordan, who won the TNA Knockouts World Championship at Victory Road in September 2025 and held it for 53 days before losing it to Le Ying Lee, emphasized the training and wrestling style variations between the promotions.

"One is a different locker room. I'd never wrestled any of those girls at TNA, and most of them were brought up in a different system," Jordan explained. "Different locker room, I would say, training. We're all training in the Performance Center. They're wrestling in other promotions, and there are different styles, so different upbringings."

She elaborated on how the Performance Center training creates consistency in NXT's development system, whereas TNA wrestlers have experience across various promotions, resulting in more diverse wrestling styles. "When you go to a different promotion, it's different styles of wrestling," Jordan noted.

## The Growing Partnership

The cross-promotional talent exchange became official in January 2025 when WWE and TNA announced a multi-year partnership, building on years of informal cooperation. This arrangement has allowed wrestlers like Jordynne Grace and Joe Hendry to transition from TNA to WWE, while NXT stars including Channing "Stacks" Lorenzo and Trick Williams have held championship gold in TNA.

The partnership continues to evolve, with multiple title defenses taking place across both promotions and further opportunities for wrestlers to establish themselves on a larger stage. As the partnership deepens, both Hartwell and Jordan's experiences suggest that these interactions are providing wrestlers with valuable experience and exposure to different wrestling philosophies and audiences.

# Gallus Signs With MLW, Rebrands as GBOT

The legendary stable formerly known as Gallus has officially signed with Major League Wrestling. Joe Coffey, Mark Coffey, and Wolfgang have joined the promotion following their WWE release in May 2025, and will operate under the new moniker GBOT—Glasgow Boys on Top.

The trio made their MLW debut at Battle Riot VIII in Kissimmee, Florida, immediately making an impact on the promotion's roster. The move marks a significant acquisition for MLW as the group looks to establish themselves in a new chapter of their careers.

## A Fresh Start With New Branding

Speaking about the decision to join MLW, Joe Coffey explained the appeal of the promotion and their recent debut. "Well, you know, we were part of MLW's Battle Riot in Kissimmee, Florida, and it seemed like a good fit," he said. "We wanted to go and show what we could do. And it just seemed like a really good environment to be in. Especially like getting a taste of the Battle Riot. MLW has a deep and talented roster, and a lot of people who I really want to go mix it up with, so hopefully the MLW fans just get a slight taste of what I, Mark, and Wolfy are about."

The rebranding to GBOT carries significant meaning for the stable. According to Joe, the name represents far more than just their geographical roots. "We are the GBOT. It stands for a lot of things, but it's Glasgow Boys on Top. We're from Glasgow, and that's the mentality we have," he explained. "I've done my time, just playing my part and being a good soldier."

Joe's comments suggest an ambitious approach to his MLW tenure, indicating that the group intends to pursue championship gold. He referenced Killer Kross and his accomplishments, noting: "You know, Killer Kross, I'm not really interested in him. I'm interested in what he's got around his waist, to be honest. He continued to emphasize the group's intentions to make their mark on the promotion. "I'm here to really establish myself and who I am, because before my time for the past couple of years that I had in other companies, I was a world-traveling superstar. I was wrestling people from Hall of Famers to world champions. I'm not here just to make up any numbers. I'm here to show what Joe Coffey is all about and what I can do, and I'll be leaving my mark as well."

Steph De Lander Explains Where Her ‘Persia Pirotta’ Ring Name Came From

# Steph De Lander Reveals the Inspiring Origins Behind Her 'Persia Pirotta' Ring Name

Steph De Lander has opened up about the story behind her WWE ring name, tracing its origins back to an unexpected source from her time working outside the wrestling industry.

During her stint with WWE's NXT brand, De Lander competed under the moniker "Persia Pirotta." In a recent interview with The Stunner, she explained how she came to adopt that particular name and the personal touches woven into it.

"I did come up with that name," De Lander revealed. "I had, funnily enough, when I was working as a hairdresser in Australia, one of my coworkers had a pet snake called Persia. I love snakes. She told me, and I was like, that's a cool name."

The wrestler noted that she wasn't even competing professionally at the time she heard the name, but she recognized its potential. "I was like, I'm just going to put that in the back pocket for something," she explained.

When De Lander eventually began her wrestling career and brought the name "Persia" to WWE, she encountered pushback from management. "I wanted to just be Persia, and they were like, Vince wants everyone to have two names. I was like, 'Can I just be Persia?' They were like, no, and I was like, damn it," she said.

Forced to come up with a surname to pair with her first name, De Lander sought something that would flow naturally. She turned to her family for inspiration. "So I wanted something that rolled off the tongue. My grandpa's surname is Parotta. So it's a little ode to my grandpa," De Lander explained, explaining how "Pirotta" became her ring surname.

De Lander gave this interview following a significant achievement in her wrestling career—winning the ACW Women's title, marking another milestone in her post-WWE journey.

Former WWE Star Recalls Losing Push After Planned Steve Austin Feud

# Former WWE Star Marc Mero Recalls How Sable's Rise Cost Him Dream Program with Stone Cold Steve Austin

During professional wrestling's most iconic era, landing a feud with Stone Cold Steve Austin was considered the ultimate opportunity for any mid-card competitor. For Marc Mero, that chance was within reach during the late 1990s, but an unexpected twist in his storyline with his real-life wife, Sable, derailed the entire program.

In a recent interview with Chris Van Vliet, Mero opened up about how his ongoing narrative with Sable ultimately cost him a planned program with Austin in 1998. At the time, Mero had transitioned into a heel character as "Marvelous" Marc Mero, with his story centered on jealousy over Sable's rapidly growing popularity among fans. While Austin dominated the main event scene, working with established stars like Shawn Michaels, Mick Foley, and The Undertaker, Mero was engaged in what he described as a very entertaining mid-card storyline with his wife.

## The Push That Never Materialized

According to Mero, WWE had genuine plans to elevate his position on the card. "They really wanted to push me and give me some momentum. So they have me enter this Intercontinental tournament where I had to beat Owen Hart, Stone Cold Steve Austin, Ron Simmons. I mean, these are some top guys that you're they're putting you over to win the Intercontinental title," Mero explained. "So I was thinking, 'Now they're really going to start giving me that push I always dreamed about.'"

The storyline between Mero and Sable was resonating with audiences. Mero strategically made himself look weaker on television to allow Sable to become the standout performer. While his character frequently belittled her, segments consistently showed Sable outsmarting or embarrassing him in ways that entertained fans and built her credibility.

## The Moment That Changed Everything

The momentum shifted during an episode of Raw is War when Sable delivered her signature "Sable Bomb" to Mero, a turning point that Mero says played a crucial role in eliminating his planned program with Austin.

"I was ready to go into a program with Steve Austin too, which that's top of the top of the food chain, right there," Mero recalled. "When she Sable bombed me, now she's a good athlete, but I basically Sable bombed myself. But it took me out of the program with Steve because Steve thought, 'If a girl's going to do that to him, I'm not going to let him get in the ring with me.'"

Austin's perception that Mero had been physically dominated—even if unintentionally—on television was enough to nix the entire feud before it could begin.

## A Missed Opportunity Without Regret

While the cancellation of a Marc Mero-Steve Austin program might seem like a catastrophic missed opportunity for any performer, Mero has maintained a pragmatic outlook about the situation. He acknowledged the reality of the wrestling business and understood Austin's perspective.

Additionally, personal circumstances softened the blow. Since Mero and Sable were married at the time, her professional success directly benefited them both financially. Combined with the strong contract Mero had secured upon joining WWE in 1996, he remained in a solid financial position regardless of his placement on the card. Mero's lack of bitterness about the situation reflects a mature understanding of how professional wrestling operates and the interconnected nature of storylines and booking decisions.

Wrestling betting odds shift more rapidly than odds in any traditional sport, driven by factors completely foreign to conventional athletics. In this environment, timing becomes crucial for anyone attempting to profit from wrestling wagers.

## Surprise Returns: The Ultimate Market Disruptor

Among wrestling's most unpredictable moments are surprise returns—when previously absent stars suddenly reappear. These moments typically involve closely guarded secrets known only to select members of the production and writing teams.

Surprise returns represent the biggest market disruptions in wrestling betting. Some bookmakers offer special markets specifically for these appearances, though they prove extremely difficult to price accurately. Only well-informed bettors with genuine insider knowledge can consistently win on these wagers, or bettors must rely entirely on luck.

## Live Betting and Cash-Out Chaos Explained

Live betting amplifies every unpredictable element that makes wrestling wagering unique. Odds fluctuate throughout matches, while storylines contain numerous false finishes—moments when matches appear to be concluding, only to feature wrestling's signature last-second kick-outs that extend competition.

This structure creates dramatic swings that can transform seemingly safe wagers into risky positions in seconds. Cash-out features, designed to provide flexibility in sports betting, translate poorly to wrestling's theatrical structure. Because matches are deliberately constructed to create dramatic momentum shifts, odds can swing wildly in moments.

Bettors who react too quickly to cash-out options risk significant losses. The feature's traditional benefit—allowing bettors to secure partial returns before uncertain conclusions—becomes a liability when conclusions are designed to be uncertain as entertainment rather than sporting competition.

## Low Limits, High Risk: How Bookmakers Manage Exposure

Bookmakers approach wrestling betting cautiously, fully aware that insider information creates potential unfair advantages. Even minor leaks from reliable sources can translate into substantial advantages for those with advance knowledge of creative decisions.

To address this concern, operators impose low betting limits on wrestling events and closely monitor odds movements. By restricting wagering amounts, bookmakers allow participation in the market while simultaneously reducing exposure to potential insider exploitation and fraud.

This approach means fewer opportunities exist for bettors to place large wagers on wrestling events compared to traditional sports betting markets.

## The Information Asymmetry Problem

Information asymmetry plays a fundamental role in wrestling betting. Traditional sports bettors can analyze extensive data about team performance, matchup histories, and athlete statistics. Wrestling bettors possess no equivalent resources.

Instead, a select group of individuals with insider connections to production teams possesses genuine information advantages. This concentration of knowledge among a privileged few creates an inherent unfairness that distinguishes wrestling betting from virtually all other wagering opportunities.
