🔥 MJF and Darby Allin AEW Championship Run

Following his recapture of the AEW World Championship at Double or Nothing 2026, MJF has publicly pushed back against reporting that claimed he had been supportive of Darby Allin's title reign. The newly crowned three-time AEW World Champion dismissed the claim in character on social media just days after defeating Allin for the belt, creating yet another chapter in his long-running rivalry with the former champion. The timing of his response raised eyebrows given what had been originally reported by multiple sources within AEW.

Darby Allin held the AEW World Championship for 39 days after defeating MJF on Dynamite on April 15, ultimately losing the title back to Friedman at Double or Nothing on May 24. During his brief but memorable reign, Allin successfully defended the championship eight times. All seven of those defenses, as well as his loss to MJF, received four-star ratings or higher from Dave Meltzer of the Wrestling Observer, with his matches against Tomasso Ciampa and Konosuke Takeshita earning perfect five-star ratings.

Reports from multiple sources noted that Allin's title reign had initially faced some skepticism within AEW before it began. However, sentiment shifted considerably once the championship run played out. Several people who were initially doubtful reportedly changed their stance after witnessing how committed Allin was to making the championship run feel important and memorable. There was widespread appreciation for the extra effort Allin put into elevating the title and the intensity he brought to the role, with his in-ring performances receiving strong internal praise.

One particularly notable report indicated that MJF had been supportive of Allin's world title reign for quite some time. Sources believed Friedman had backed the decision and praised Allin's efforts as champion. The report highlighted how many within AEW applauded the former titleholder for going the extra mile during his reign.

However, this report did not sit well with MJF's public persona. Responding on X shortly after Double or Nothing, Friedman directly rejected the claim and called for it to be corrected through Community Notes. He wrote: "This article is false." He then doubled down on the denial with a characteristically sharp follow-up: "I hate Darby Allin. If he died I would laugh. Plz fix this. Thanks." He tagged both the Community Notes and Community Notes Violations accounts to ensure the proper authorities were alerted.

The response appeared to be a classic case of MJF protecting his on-screen image. The two stars have spent years as rivals in AEW, and Friedman has built much of his reputation around being one of wrestling's most unapologetic villains. In that sense, admitting support for Allin's success would be a difficult pill to swallow, even for The Devil. MJF's public denial maintained consistency with his character regardless of what may have occurred behind the scenes.

Meanwhile, MJF's focus now shifts back to the ring. During this week's Dynamite, he rejected a challenge from Mark Briscoe but agreed to defend the AEW World Championship against RUSH. The match is scheduled for next Wednesday's Dynamite and will mark the first title defense of MJF's third reign as champion.

"On Raw, they said that Paul Heyman had put 'The Vision' and not individual members of the team in all contracts related to the title," Meltzer wrote. "So Theory and Bron Breakker are the only members of the team right now not injured, although I expect them to add members, besides Maxxine Dupri, and there has been talk of that even before this injury, are going to be the tag team champions for now."

The current version of The Vision consists of Bron Breakker, Bronson Reed, Logan Paul, Austin Theory, and Paul Heyman as the group's manager and spokesperson. Injuries have created some uncertainty around the faction's immediate future.

Logan Paul recently revealed that he suffered a torn tricep at WWE Saturday Night's Main Event on May 23, and WWE is reportedly estimating a recovery timeline of around six months. Paul, however, has publicly stated that he believes he can return sooner than expected.

Meanwhile, Bronson Reed has remained sidelined since earlier this year, although fans recently noticed the powerhouse appearing noticeably larger in a recent "Break the internet" post shared on X, fueling speculation that his WWE return could be getting closer. Additional updates regarding WWE adding new members to The Vision will be provided as more information surfaces.
