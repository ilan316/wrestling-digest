TNA Talent Working With Other Promotions

TNA Wrestling appears to be taking a harder line on its talent competing outside the company, marking a significant policy shift just days before one of wrestling's biggest weekends. According to reports, Moose was originally approved to appear at the Maple Leaf Pro Wrestling Multiverse show on April 18, but that booking has now been cancelled. Despite initial clearance from TNA, the former champion was pulled from the card before the promotion could even make an official announcement.

The timing of this reversal is raising eyebrows throughout the wrestling community. The belief among insiders is that Moose's involvement would have meant working with talent from another promotion, most likely AEW, and that cross-promotional aspect appears to have triggered TNA's decision to halt the appearance. This isn't being viewed as an isolated incident but rather as evidence of a broader philosophical shift happening behind the scenes at TNA.

According to available reports, TNA Wrestling has been reassessing how its talent is presented in matches outside their own programming. There is growing concern within the company about wrestlers appearing in streamed or recorded matches on rival platforms, particularly if those appearances result in losses. The worry is that such outcomes could negatively impact how TNA talent is perceived by their own audience and potentially affect their standing within the company.

The ramifications of this policy change are already becoming apparent across multiple events scheduled during WrestleMania weekend. The previously announced match between Ricochet and TNA's Leon Slater at WrestleCon's Mark Hitchcock Memorial Supershow is now under scrutiny. While that match had reportedly received approval from all parties involved, its current status has become unclear following TNA's stricter stance on cross-promotional appearances.

What triggered this sudden shift in TNA's approach remains a matter of speculation. Some observers believe the change could be related to pressure from broadcast partners now carrying TNA programming, who may have expressed concerns about their talent appearing on competing platforms. Others have speculated that outside influence, potentially from WWE, could have played a role in prompting TNA to tighten control over its talent's outside bookings.

What is certain is that TNA Wrestling is now exercising tighter control over where and with whom its wrestlers compete. This decision arrives at a particularly consequential time, coming during WrestleMania weekend when cross-promotional opportunities and independent wrestling shows are at their peak. The company appears to be prioritizing how their roster is presented and protecting their talent from potential negative exposure on rival platforms, even if it means canceling previously approved bookings.

The long-term impact of this policy shift remains to be seen. Talent working for TNA may find their opportunities for outside work and cross-promotional matches significantly limited moving forward. Whether this represents a temporary measure specific to WrestleMania weekend or a permanent change to TNA's operational philosophy has not been clarified by the company. For now, wrestlers and promoters are left to navigate this new landscape of restrictions as one of wrestling's biggest weeks unfolds.

Lady Frost and Other Wrestler Promos

Lady Frost recently opened up about her first television promo in a candid interview. The experience was far from smooth, as she was thrust into an unfamiliar situation with minimal preparation or wrestling background.

At the time of the promo, Frost had not yet been formally trained as a wrestler. She was not part of the independent wrestling scene, did not own proper wrestling gear, and was wearing Nikes instead of wrestling shoes. A producer approached her with a piece of paper containing a simple script and asked her to read it. When asked if she felt comfortable with the material, she agreed, and the producer inquired whether she had memorized it.

After Frost confirmed she had memorized the four-line promo and volunteered to recite it aloud, the producer's reaction was telling. He said "Oh. Oh, shit. Okay" before walking away. Unbeknownst to Frost, this prompted behind-the-scenes discussions that would dramatically change what was supposed to be a simple task.

What made the situation particularly confusing was that Frost had been told the promo would not air live. However, that apparently changed after her initial take. She was asked to cut the promo again, and again, and again until she had re-recorded it approximately 10 times. As someone completely new to the industry and unaware of wrestling production standards, Frost found the entire process bewildering and had no idea what was happening or why she was being asked to repeat the segment so many times.

She vividly remembers being in gorilla, the production area near the entrance, when someone asked if she wanted to cut the promo again. Frost confidently said no, believing she was finished with the task. However, her response was misinterpreted by the production staff. When she said she was good, they understood it as her being ready for another take. Realizing the miscommunication, Frost quickly understood that "no, I'm good" in their context meant she needed to cut it again.

During the same interview, Frost also discussed how different wrestling companies maintain varying levels of production values, highlighting how her early experience in the industry exposed her to different operational standards.

CMLL Global Expansion Plans

CMLL owner Salvador Lutteroth III has outlined his company's strategic vision for global expansion, positioning Mexican lucha libre on the international wrestling stage. During an exclusive interview, Lutteroth spoke about CMLL's landmark first-ever full international show, which will take place in Las Vegas on April 16, 2026.

Lutteroth explained that the show, branded as Slamfest, represents a significant milestone for the promotion. "It's the first time CMLL goes abroad with a complete show," he stated. "An opportunity for us to show a complete program of CMLL talent in the States. We're asking our wrestlers to do what they usually do in Mexico City, Guadalajara, Puebla, and the rest of the country."

The executive emphasized that this event serves as a platform for CMLL's wrestlers to demonstrate their abilities to an American audience while maintaining the authentic Mexican lucha libre experience that defines the promotion. Rather than creating a specialized show for international audiences, CMLL intends to present the same style and caliber of wrestling that fans experience at home.

Beyond this single event, Lutteroth addressed CMLL's broader strategy of forming alliances with major wrestling promotions and establishing a sustained international presence. "What we all aim is a presence of CMLL, or Empresa Mexicana de Lucha Libre, at the international level," Lutteroth explained. "We formed alliances with New Japan Pro Wrestling, with AEW and the branches that belong to AEW, RevPro, MLW, MLP, and we still want to be part of a more global image of Mexican lucha libre."

The Slamfest lineup showcases this commitment to international collaboration while featuring CMLL's top talent. The card includes a main event pitting CMLL Light Heavyweight Champion Mistico against CMLL Heavyweight Champion Hechicero. The CMLL World Women's Championship will be defended by Persephone against a yet-to-be-announced challenger.

Additional matches feature international talent alongside CMLL competitors. Claudio Castagnoli faces Atlantis Jr., while Blue Panther takes on Ultimo Guerrero. The undercard includes Mascara Dorada and Neon facing Capitan Suicida and Flip Gordon, and Chamuel with Tengu squaring off against Kemalito and Periquito Sacaryas.

These alliances and the Slamfest event represent CMLL's calculated approach to building a worldwide presence while maintaining the promotion's Mexican heritage and wrestling traditions.

AJPW Champion Carnival Changes

All Japan Pro Wrestling has made a significant late change to its Champion Carnival tournament lineup. The 2026 Champion Carnival is set to begin in Tokyo at Korakuen Hall on Sunday, April 12. On Wednesday, the promotion announced that Cyrus will no longer be participating in the tournament due to circumstances surrounding his travel to Japan.

The promotion released a detailed statement explaining the situation and the steps that had been taken prior to discovering the flight cancellation.

AJPW's statement reads in translation: "Regarding Cyrus, who had been scheduled to appear at 'Champion Carnival 2026' starting April 12 (Sunday) at Korakuen Hall in Tokyo, we regret to inform you that it has been decided he will be absent from the entire series."

The statement continues: "After reaching a contractual agreement, we announced his participation and proceeded with preparations for his arrival in Japan, including visa arrangements. However, we were subsequently unable to make contact with him. Today, when we asked his agent to confirm his flight with the airline, it was discovered that the flight he was scheduled to board had been canceled by Cyrus himself. As of now, we have received no report from him regarding the cancellation of the flight, and as a result, we have determined that it will be difficult for him to participate in 'Champion Carnival 2026.'"

AJPW has stated that a replacement for Cyrus will be announced at a press conference on Thursday. The B Block of the tournament currently lists Xyon's opponent as TBA, which is where the replacement wrestler will slot into the bracket.

The Champion Carnival 2026 is divided into two blocks. The A Block features Rei Saito, Kento Miyahara, Go Shiozaki, Yuma Anzai, Kuma Arashi, Kengo Mashimo, and Talos Oddyssey. The B Block includes Hideki Suzuki, Ren Ayabe, Jun Saito, Ryuki Honda, Daisuke Sekimoto, Madoka Kikuta, and Xyon, with one spot remaining for the replacement competitor.

This marks an unexpected turn for Cyrus, who had been working as a regular talent for AJPW. According to social media updates from the promotion, Cyrus had been actively participating in AJPW since at least late December of the previous year and had recently returned to the United States in early February before the Champion Carnival announcement was made.

Former NFL Star Le’Veon Bell Tells Full Story About Fight With Logan Paul Being Set Up And Falling Through, Blasts WWE Star For ‘Ducking Him’

Former NFL star Le'Veon Bell appeared on Da Get Got Pod with Marshawn Lynch and Mike Robinson this week and detailed the full circumstances behind his planned boxing match with Logan Paul that ultimately never materialized. According to Bell, the fight was set up with specific rules: 10-ounce gloves, no headgear, six rounds with three-minute intervals, and "doghouse rules" where the match would continue until someone quit or couldn't continue.

Bell explained that after he sent Paul his counter-proposal for the fight details, Paul agreed and said he would set up the contract. However, after 24 hours passed without receiving the contract, Bell issued an ultimatum on Twitter, giving Paul 48 hours to deliver it. That Sunday, something unexpected happened that Bell believes was orchestrated.

"He had somebody call while he's doing a live for Pokemon," Bell recounted. "Think about if I had somebody call me right now while I'm doing this. 'No matter what, tell me I can't do this. Tell me I can't go to WWE and pull up to wrestling. Tell me I can't do that.' That's all he did, tell whoever Chris was, told him to say no. Faked a little phone call and act like the WWE said he couldn't fight."

Bell continued to explain that the person who called, identified as Chris, claimed he didn't want Paul to get hurt or risk slipping and falling because of the injury risk. This excuse struck Bell as particularly hollow given Paul's other activities outside WWE.

"Last time I checked, wasn't Logan Paul out there playing flag football with Tom Brady?" Bell pointed out. "The WWE clears flag football, but they don't clear you to basically spar in your gym. It's your gym. You can't work out in your gym, basically, is what you're trying to say? WWE told you can't work out in your gym, but you can play flag football?"

Bell emphasized the contradiction in Paul's reasoning, noting that Paul's own tweet about the situation seemed to reveal the real issue. According to Bell, Paul tweeted that he would have to stop his WWE obligations and train extensively to fight someone of Bell's caliber, which Bell interpreted as Paul essentially admitting he wanted to fight someone who couldn't actually fight back.

More Details On Ex-WWE/AAA Star Alberto Del Rio's Arrest For Domestic Violence

Former WWE and AAA wrestler Alberto Del Rio, whose real name is Alberto Rodriguez, has been arrested for domestic violence in Mexico. The Attorney General's Office of the State of San Luis Potosí confirmed on Tuesday that an investigation has been initiated into Rodriguez following his arrest on Monday.

According to reports from Mexico, Rodriguez was taken into custody after an altercation with his wife. She contacted emergency services during the incident and sustained visible injuries to her face and arms. State Civil Guard officers arrived at the scene while the assault was occurring and discovered the victim bearing signs of violence, leading to the immediate arrest.

Following his arrest, Rodriguez was placed in the custody of the Attorney General's Office and specifically transferred to the Specialized Prosecutor's Office for Attention to Women, Family and Sexual Crimes. This specialized unit handles cases involving crimes against women and families within the state.

An official statement from the Attorney General's Office provided details of the allegations. "According to the victim's complaint, upon meeting Alberto [Rodriguez] inside a home in the Lomas del Tecnológico [neighborhood], an argument ensued that led the accused to commit various physical and verbal assaults against his partner," the announcement stated.

The office further explained the procedural steps that would follow. "After the arrest carried out by [State Civil Guard] agents, the accused was placed at the disposal of agents of the Public Ministry, who will be in charge of duly compiling the investigation file and presenting him before the judicial authority at the initial hearing, where he will have to answer for the acts he is accused of."
