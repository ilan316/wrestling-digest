Leila Grey ACL Recovery Update

Leila Grey provided an encouraging update on her ACL injury recovery during a virtual signing for East Coast Autograph Auctions, sharing details about her rehabilitation progress and the support she has received from fellow wrestler Mercedes Mone.

The AEW and ROH women's wrestling star suffered the ACL injury during her TBS Championship match against Mercedes Mone on December 6, 2025, during an episode of AEW Collision at the Greater Columbus Convention Center in Columbus, Ohio. She subsequently underwent successful ACL surgery in March 2026.

During her appearance at the virtual signing, Grey expressed her gratitude for the recovery process while highlighting her determination to return to competition. "I'm actually healing up pretty well so, keeping up with my therapy and yeah, we're gonna come back strong. It's a long process, but I'm trying to stay positive. Doing what I gotta do to come back strong, make a big comeback because I miss all you guys so much. I miss going to work, I miss wrestling, I miss being in the ring," she stated.

Grey emphasized that while the recovery timeline will be extended, she is making exceptional progress in her rehabilitation efforts. "It's gonna be a long recovery. That is for sure, but I am definitely doing good on my physical therapy, and I'm healing up, and I'm going even a little faster than the healing timeline by my doctor, so it's going good, but it's definitely gonna be a while," she continued.

One of the most notable aspects of Grey's recovery has been the unwavering support from Mone, the opponent against whom she suffered the injury. When asked about Mone's involvement in her recovery, Grey was effusive in her praise. "Of course, of course she did. Yes, and she was with me the whole time afterward. She did not leave my side. Mercedes has been great," Grey said.

Currently, there is no confirmed timeline for Grey's return to in-ring competition. Prior to her injury, she was a member of the SkyFlight faction within AEW/ROH.

AEW Trademark Filings

All Elite Wrestling has filed with the United States Patent and Trademark Office to trademark four new names, continuing the wrestling industry's steady stream of intellectual property registrations. On Friday, April 17, 2026, AEW submitted its trademark application for four distinct monikers: "Timeless Love Bombs," "The Conglomeration," "Divine Dominion," and "Triangle of Madness."

According to the official USPTO filing documentation, the trademarks cover two primary categories of goods and services. Under International Class 025, the filings include shirts and t-shirts, indicating merchandise plans for these names. The broader entertainment category, classified under IC 041, encompasses a comprehensive range of professional wrestling-related services and performances.

The IC 041 classification specifically details conducting entertainment exhibitions in the nature of professional wrestling exhibits and performances by professional wrestlers, with particular emphasis on performances by tag teams during professional wrestling events. The filing also covers entertainment services in the nature of live professional wrestling performances, televised appearances by tag team professional wrestlers, and live appearances by tag team professional wrestlers.

The nature of these trademark filings, particularly the focus on tag team performers across multiple categories, suggests AEW may be developing new team concepts or group identities for their roster. The variety in the proposed names hints at diverse character concepts ranging from comedic to ominous themes that could appeal to different segments of AEW's audience.

Wrestling Headlines

Becky Lynch Offers Support to MJF

Becky Lynch is offering some unexpected backup to MJF following his shocking AEW World Title loss. The fallout from the April 15 episode of AEW Dynamite continues to stir conversation after MJF dropped the AEW World Championship to Darby Allin in an extremely short match. The quick defeat raised eyebrows immediately, and MJF himself hasn't stopped voicing his frustration since.

Taking to social media in the days that followed, Maxwell Jacob Friedman went on a passionate rant, demanding what he described as justice. He didn't hold back either, calling out several key figures in AEW as responsible for what went down. MJF specifically named Tony Khan, Bryan Danielson, Aubrey Edwards, Renee Paquette, and Darby Allin as guilty parties in the situation.

That's when WWE Superstar Becky Lynch chimed in. Despite working for a rival company, Lynch has been openly complimentary of MJF in the past, and she once again showed support in response to his latest claims. Replying directly to his post, Lynch wrote, "Let me know, I'll put you in contact with the BEST lawyers!" A bold offer that certainly got fans talking.

MJF remains convinced that he was cheated out of his championship reign. He has pointed to the circumstances surrounding his title defense against Kenny Omega, noting that he appeared in the arena following the Dynasty event without even realizing he was scheduled to compete in a World Title match. Something clearly isn't sitting right with "The Salt of the Earth."
