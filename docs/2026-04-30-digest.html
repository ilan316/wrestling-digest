🔥 TNA-AEW Match Cancellations and Carlos Silva Controversy

WRESTLING CONTROVERSY: TNA PULLS TALENT FROM AEW MATCHES, SPARKS MAJOR INDUSTRY DISPUTE

The professional wrestling world has been rocked by a significant business dispute after TNA President Carlos Silva made the controversial decision to cancel multiple scheduled matches between TNA and AEW talent during WrestleMania week. The cancellations affected several high-profile bouts, including a match between AEW World Champion MJF and TNA's Nic Nemeth at Create-A-Pro on May 1, and a contest between AEW's Ricochet and TNA X-Division Champion Leon Slater at WrestleCon on April 16, 2026. The decision also impacted TNA's Moose, who was pulled from a Maple Leaf Pro Wrestling card.

Silva announced the decision to pull the talent at the last moment, with matches already advertised to fans. The sudden cancellations sparked immediate backlash from multiple quarters of the wrestling community, including criticism from independent wrestling promoters and AEW-affiliated talent. MJF was particularly vocal in his criticism, taking to social media to blast the decision and its impact on wrestlers who would have been compensated for those appearances.

The tension escalated when Silva made his first public comments about the situation during an appearance on Busted Open Radio on April 29. He acknowledged that the decision was difficult and that he understood the criticism. Silva stated, "It was super tough. And look, all these business decisions are tough. And I don't like being called a little bitch on social any more than anyone does. You do take the goods with the bads and you gotta make some hard decisions. My job sometimes is to make hard decisions."

Silva framed the decision as a necessary protective measure for TNA, explaining that the company faces competitive pressures from rival organizations. "And we've had some other folks come at us and they've moved into our nights. They've made some decisions. They've tried to block us in arenas and venues and that stuff's real and it's out there," Silva said. He referenced the Under Armour slogan "You gotta protect this house," indicating that safeguarding TNA's interests took priority over honoring the previously announced matches.

The TNA president revealed that multiple companies have engaged in tactics designed to hurt TNA's business operations. While he did not name specific organizations, his comments appeared to reference scheduling conflicts and venue access issues. According to industry reporting, Silva was apparently upset about AEW Collision being scheduled head-to-head with TNA Impact Wrestling when it was bumped off Saturday by TNT's NCAA Basketball Tournament coverage. This scenario aligns with Silva's reference to competitors "moving into our nights."

Despite taking full responsibility for the decision, Silva emphasized that he was not acting alone. "I've got a great team and we talk about these things together, but sometimes I'm the guy that has to make that decision and then you move on and you sort of go to work the next day and you work hard again and get stuff done," he explained.

MJF did not accept Silva's explanation quietly. Just hours before AEW Dynamite aired on April 29, the AEW star took to social media and directly accused Silva of dishonesty. "I think it's cool that you can just lie. My turn. I think Kevin Knight has a shot at beating me at 8pm sharp and I also think Darby Allin is a GREAT champion," MJF wrote in a sarcastic post that questioned the legitimacy of Silva's stated reasoning.

MJF had previously criticized the decision, suggesting that the cancellations hurt wrestlers financially by blocking their ability to work independent dates. His public response indicated that he did not believe Silva's explanation about protecting TNA due to competitive pressure from other companies. The AEW star's reaction added significant fuel to what had already become a contentious situation.

However, not all TNA talent or industry observers sided with MJF's criticism. Matt Hardy, a veteran who has worked across WWE, TNA, and AEW, offered a more nuanced take on the situation. Speaking to Ariel Helwani, Hardy suggested that the matches should not have been booked in the first place due to TNA's partnership with WWE.

"In my honest opinion, I think if we are partners with WWE, and WWE's laid out how we're working together with TNA, and AEW is its own entity, we probably shouldn't do AEW-TNA bookings," Hardy stated. He added that while the matches may have been designed to benefit all parties involved, long-term business alignment should have taken priority. "These things happen, and I think in these finishes, they had things that were going to work for both parties when it was all said and done. It probably should have never been set is what I would say from the beginning," Hardy said.

Hardy also revealed details about how Silva attempted to mitigate the fallout from his decision. "The thing that I can tell you is the guys who he pulled out of those matches, he compensated them and paid them their full fee. I know he talked to Mike at Highspots and compensated him for the plane ticket and everything else. He did everything he could to try and make it right," Hardy explained. This information contradicted any notion that Silva made a callous decision without regard for the financial impact on the affected wrestlers.

AJ Francis, another TNA talent, similarly defended Silva's decision on social media. When questioned about whether Silva was wrong to pull his talent, Francis responded critically to those complaining about the cancellations. "No, because nobody had a problem w/ AEW not allowing Powerhouse Hobbs to lose to me at 4th Rope even though the next night in the SAME BUILDING I was in a SOLD OUT MAIN EVENT vs Nic Nemeth for the TNA WORLD TITLE. Cut that victim bullshit out. Every company protects interests," Francis wrote.

Francis's comments highlighted a broader point about how professional wrestling promotions typically operate. His reference to AEW preventing Powerhouse Hobbs from losing to him at an independent event, despite Francis main eventing against Nic Nemeth the following night at the same venue, suggested that competitive protection is standard practice across the industry. Francis argued that critics applying a double standard by accepting AEW's protective decisions while criticizing TNA for doing the same.

Silva's explanation about competitive pressure from other companies was echoed in his references to specific tactics. Beyond the scheduling conflicts already mentioned, Silva indicated that some companies have attempted to limit TNA's access to venues or arenas. While Silva did not elaborate extensively on these venue-blocking claims, they appeared to reference broader competitive tensions in the independent wrestling landscape during the WrestleMania season.

The situation has raised questions about the viability of crossover matches between promotions in the modern wrestling landscape, particularly when one of the companies involved has partnership obligations with a third major promotion. The WWE-TNA partnership appears to have played a significant role in Silva's thinking, as Hardy outlined the implications of that business relationship.

Hardy also discussed the WWE-TNA partnership more broadly during his interview with Helwani. He noted that crossover appearances between TNA and WWE may increase following WrestleMania season. Hardy expressed enthusiasm about TNA's expanded partnership with WWE, which now includes inclusion in WWE 2K26 video game and increased merchandising opportunities. He characterized WWE and TNA as having different roles in his career, describing WWE as "our childhood home" and TNA as "our adult home."

The dispute between MJF and Silva has not yet shown signs of cooling. MJF's public accusation that Silva was lying, combined with Silva's firm defense of his decision-making, suggests that tension between AEW and TNA leadership could persist. The situation highlights the complex business relationships and competitive dynamics that exist between major wrestling promotions in 2026.

Looking forward, industry observers will be watching to see whether this remains an isolated business dispute or develops into a more prolonged conflict affecting future crossover opportunities. Silva's apparent new policy against TNA-AEW bookings, justified by the company's WWE partnership and competitive pressures, could reshape independent wrestling opportunities for talent working in multiple promotions.

The controversy also raises broader questions about the power dynamics between wrestling promotions and their contracted talent. While Silva compensated the affected wrestlers, the cancellation of already-advertised matches represented a significant disruption to independent wrestling cards and the wrestlers who had been promoted for those appearances.

🔥 MJF AEW Title Matches and Pursuit

Kevin Knight Retains TNT Championship Against MJF On April 29, 2026 AEW Dynamite

On April 29, 2026, Kevin Knight successfully defended his AEW TNT Championship against Maxwell Jacob Friedman in a match that opened the show. The contest came after the two exchanged heated words on the April 22 episode of Dynamite, with MJF seeking an "easy dub" and looking to add the TNT title to his resume while primarily focused on his pursuit of the AEW World Championship.

The match itself saw MJF control much of the early proceedings, employing a strategic approach centered on grounding Knight. MJF showed his technical prowess by getting into the champion's head, but his strategy quickly shifted to the outside when Knight began his comeback. MJF targeted Knight's knee early in the match, sending him violently into the ring steps and hitting a power bomb across the ring barricade. Throughout the bout, whenever Knight met him with aerial offense on the outside, MJF continued to roll out of the ring at every opportunity, forcing Knight to chase. On the fourth such instance, Knight surprised MJF with a springboard lariat.

The action between the ropes featured several high-impact sequences and near-finish moments. MJF attempted his Heatseeker and nearly won with a cutter, while Knight delivered a frog splash from the top rope. MJF's continued offense on Knight's injured knee included an Alabama Slam, further wearing down the champion. Despite the damage, Knight showcased his resilience and refused to stay down.

The finish came when MJF retrieved the TNT Championship from ringside, attempting to execute his signature tactic. With referee Bryce Remsburg distracted by taking the title away, MJF pulled out his Dynamite Diamond Ring from his trunks. However, Knight was prepared for this move. The TNT Champion struck MJF with a low blow, countering MJF at his own game, and then rolled him up in a small package for the pinfall victory.
