🔥 Darby Allin AEW World Championship Reign

Darby Allin captured his first AEW World Championship on the Spring BreakThru episode of AEW Dynamite after defeating MJF. The victory came just over two minutes into the match after Darby hit four consecutive Coffin Drops on the tired champion. Allin earned his title opportunity by defeating Andrade El Idolo at Dynasty 2026, but rather than waiting for a pay-per-view main event, he capitalized on MJF's exhaustion following MJF's nearly 40-minute match against Kenny Omega the same evening.

Immediately after winning the championship, Darby made a declaration that would set the tone for his reign. He announced he would be a fighting champion and committed to defending his title every week for as long as his body would allow it. This promise reflected Darby's history as champion throughout his AEW career. His previous runs with the TNT Championship and his World Tag Team Championship with Sting all saw him be exceptionally active. His first TNT reign lasted six months before he was worn down and forced to pass out by Miro. His second TNT Championship reign lasted one month in early 2023 but featured four title defenses in that brief period. His tag team reign with Sting also lasted approximately one month before they vacated the titles after Revolution 2024 when Sting retired.

Darby's commitment to defending weekly was tested immediately on the April 22 episode of AEW Dynamite when he faced Tommaso Ciampa in the main event. Ciampa had challenged for the title after defeating Dezmond Xavier on the Spring BreakThru show, setting up the first defense just one week after Allin's championship victory. The match between Allin and Ciampa was brutal from the opening bell, with both men bringing an intense physicality to the ring.

From the start, Ciampa attacked hard and used rough moves around ringside, even exposing parts of the ring to increase the damage he could inflict. Darby, true to his nature, took enormous risks throughout the match. He launched himself off the ring post and crashed through the timekeeper's table with a suicide splash. Both men ended up bleeding during the contest, with Ciampa's face covered in crimson as the match escalated in intensity. Darby attempted to recreate the successful Coffin Drop that had won him the title the previous week, but Ciampa avoided it and Darby crashed to the outside. Darby followed up by hitting a Coffin Drop on the outside, sending both men through the timekeeper's table.

Ciampa came out of the wreckage with a crimson mask, which Darby immediately capitalized on with a Tope Suicida. When the action returned to the ring, Ciampa attempted to expose the top turnbuckle. After taking a Scorpion Death Drop, Ciampa managed to expose the buckle but then took a huge Psycho Driller off the second rope to the floor during a commercial break. The challenger injured his tailbone hitting that signature move but continued fighting.

The match saw several near-fall sequences as momentum shifted back and forth. Ciampa hit multiple Knee Tremblers on Darby, who refused to stay down despite the damage being inflicted. Ciampa attempted to use the exposed turnbuckle to his advantage, but Darby improvised and countered with a Stomp. Darby fired back and hit the Code Red for a near fall. He was then caught going for a Coffin Drop and found himself in Ciampa's guard, where Ciampa locked in a Rear Naked Choke. After escaping, Darby hit his head on the exposed turnbuckle and was struck with another knee strike for another near fall.

A third near fall came after a Project Ciampa and another knee strike from Ciampa. The challenger attempted to lock in the Scorpion Death Lock, but Darby countered and locked in one of his own. Exhausted after the grueling match, Ciampa could not reach the ropes and eventually tapped out, giving Darby a successful first title defense. After the match, Ciampa showed respect to the champion by shaking his hand and handing him the championship belt.

The celebration did not last long. Brody King emerged immediately after the match. King had defeated Lio Rush earlier in the evening and had been watching the main event closely. King initially praised Allin for his performance and then reminded him of their history. The two had wrestled multiple times across different promotions over the years, with their first encounter being a ten-minute time-limit draw at Evolve 99. They had also faced each other in AAW, PWG, and SFP.

King revealed that he had already spoken to AEW President Tony Khan and secured a title match for the following week. Their overall record stood at five matches with King holding a 2-2 advantage in AEW singles competition, though their head-to-head history showed King with a 5-2-1 overall record. King had previously challenged MJF for the world title at Grand Slam Australia in February but came up short. After that loss, King promised he was not done pursuing the championship.

Darby did not say much in response to King's challenge. He simply shook King's hand, accepting the challenge without hesitation. This set up a title defense for the April 29 episode of AEW Dynamite in Fairfax, Virginia, just one week after his victory over Ciampa. The quick turnaround exemplified Darby's commitment to his promise of defending the title every week.

The April 29 episode also featured another significant title match. TNT Champion Kevin Knight was set to defend against MJF. Knight and MJF had traded words on the April 22 show when MJF called out Darby for a title rematch. Knight came out to share his thoughts about the former champion. MJF remarked that the TNT title was for those with a ceiling, which Knight took immediate issue with. MJF said he realized how proud Knight was of the title and wanted to take it from him. Knight was prepared to fight on the spot, but MJF told him he would "dance with the devil next week, kid," officially making the title challenge. MJF had previously faced Knight in March when MJF still held the world championship, so they had recent history with each other.

Darby's active championship reign already demonstrated the pattern that had defined his previous title runs. His aggressive fighting style and willingness to take risks for the fans meant he would likely suffer significant wear and tear. Analysis of his championship prospects suggested that while his reign might be exciting and memorable, it probably would not last an extremely long period due to the physical toll his wrestling style takes on his body. However, his commitment to being a fighting champion meant the fans would see quality wrestling and title defenses frequently.

Multiple potential challengers were already being discussed as possible future opponents for Darby's world title. MJF was considered the most likely next champion, particularly because his quick loss on Dynamite set up a natural rematch scenario. Wrestling insiders suggested that MJF and Darby would likely meet again at Double or Nothing 2026, which would be taking place in New York City in MJF's home state. This would provide symmetry with Darby winning the title in Washington state, his home state, setting up MJF's comeback bid in his own backyard.

Other potential challengers discussed included Swerve Strickland, who had returned from injury and expressed focus on winning the world title, Kenny Omega, who was said to have renewed confidence after his conversation with Will Ospreay, and several rising stars like Kevin Knight, who had impressed with his own recent championship reign. Will Ospreay spoke in an interview about the possibility of challenging Darby for the world title at AEW All In 2026, noting that he would love to win the most coveted prize in professional wrestling in front of a British crowd at Wembley Stadium. Andrade El Idolo, who had earned Darby's opportunity by nearly winning the Dynasty tournament, was also expected to pursue another title shot after his initial loss to Allin.

Darby's approach as a fighting champion meant that fans could expect to see frequent title matches and defenses. His history showed that when he held championships, he was willing to take on all comers and defend regularly. The combination of his high-risk wrestling style and his commitment to frequent defenses suggested that while his reign might produce excellent television and memorable matches, it would likely not extend for an exceptionally long period. The wrestling community was prepared for an active, exciting reign from the new AEW World Champion.

🔥 MJF AEW World Championship Loss and Future Plans

MJF SHIFTS FOCUS TO TNT TITLE AFTER WORLD CHAMPIONSHIP LOSS

Maxwell Jacob Friedman didn't waste time addressing his AEW World Championship loss on the April 22 episode of AEW Dynamite, quickly shifting focus toward a new opportunity. Just one week removed from dropping the title to Darby Allin at Spring BreakThru, MJF opened the show in a fiery mood, making it clear he wasn't ready to exit the title picture quietly.

Friedman was still fuming over what he called the "Seattle Screwjob," accusing Darby Allin of winning under questionable circumstances. He referenced his prior match with Kenny Omega and claimed he entered the title defense less than 100 percent, insisting the result didn't reflect his true standing in the company. Rather than dwell solely on the past, MJF demanded a rematch with the champion, wanting the title returned to him immediately.

Instead of Darby Allin responding to the challenge, AEW TNT Champion Kevin Knight interrupted the segment. Knight challenged Friedman's complaints, reminding him that he had previously come close to pinning MJF in a previous encounter and positioning himself as a fighting champion. Knight even referenced his own controversial finish with MJF, claiming that if he hadn't been screwed himself, he would be a double champion.

The exchange quickly escalated into an unexpected proposition. MJF mocked Knight's credentials while sarcastically praising his title, noting he'd enjoy taking it just as much as he enjoyed taking shots, at least verbal ones. When Friedman questioned what he would get out of wrestling Kevin, he admitted the TNT Championship suddenly had his attention. He proposed a match with the title on the line, turning the segment into an unexpected pivot from world title frustration to mid-card gold ambition.

Knight accepted the challenge on the spot and didn't want to wait, insisting the match happen immediately. MJF appeared to agree, and a referee was brought to the ring to make the match official. However, in classic MJF fashion, Friedman backed off at the last moment, telling Knight he would "dance with the devil next week" instead, rolling out of the ring before the bell could ring. This move proved once again that he's as strategic as he is outspoken, playing psychological games with his opponent.

Following the segment, AEW President Tony Khan made the TNT Championship match official for the April 29 episode of Dynamite. The stakes are significant for both competitors. If Knight wins, it will mark his second successful defense of the title after defending it against Claudio Castagnoli on April 15. If MJF wins, he will become only the third man in AEW history after Kenny Omega and Jon Moxley to win three separate singles titles in AEW, having already held the world title and the AEW International Championship in the past.

Despite losing the world championship, sources within AEW indicate that MJF remains central to the company's creative plans and is not disappearing from the spotlight anytime soon. Reports from multiple wrestling news outlets confirm that there has been significant internal praise for MJF's recent media appearances, with many in the company reportedly pleased with how he has represented the brand in recent weeks. That momentum is expected to carry over onto television in the coming weeks.

According to backstage reports, MJF is confirmed for AEW's Double or Nothing pay-per-view next month, further confirming that he remains a key figure in the promotion's immediate plans. More significantly, MJF is already factored into AEW creative storylines well into the summer, signaling that his presence will continue to be a focal point on programming in the months ahead. His involvement in major storylines stretches well into the summer months, indicating that losing the championship didn't signal the end of MJF's momentum.

The former world champion's next move may not be a straight line back to the world title, but it's clear he's not stepping out of the spotlight anytime soon. Instead, he's switching belts and pursuing the TNT Championship while remaining positioned as one of AEW's top stars. With MJF still locked into major creative plans, attention now turns to how AEW positions him next and whether his road back to the AEW World Title begins sooner rather than later.

🔥 Adam Copeland FTR Double or Nothing Challenge

During a backstage promo on AEW Dynamite, Adam Copeland directly addressed FTR and made it clear that he and Christian Cage want another shot at the AEW World Tag Team Championships. Copeland explained that he heard FTR believed they had already beaten everyone in the division and didn't see a reason to give Copeland and Cage another match. FTR, with advice from Stokely Hathaway, even suggested that Tony Khan should look at other promotions for new challengers.

Copeland wasn't discouraged by this rejection. Instead, he came up with a bold idea to challenge FTR directly. He proposed a New York Street Fight for the AEW World Tag Team Championships at AEW Double or Nothing. This type of match means no rules, no disqualifications, and a lot of chaos. Copeland explained that he had been working his mind to figure out a deal that could appeal to them and settled on this stipulation to prove that he and Cage are truly the greatest tag team of all time.

In case FTR were wondering what was in it for them, Copeland laid out the major stakes: if they win and Copeland and Cage lose, then Copeland and Cage will retire as a tag team. This means the potential match at Double or Nothing would instantly become one of the highest-stakes tag team bouts possible, especially considering the long history between the teams.

Copeland also employed psychological tactics in his promo, hinting that if FTR refuses the match, it would mean they are scared or think Copeland and Cage are the better team. This mind game put additional pressure on the champions to accept the challenge.

The history between these teams makes this challenge particularly significant. Copeland and Cage first wrestled FTR at All Out in Toronto last September in a match that Copeland and Cage won. However, Beth Copeland was then taken out afterward by a spike piledriver. Copeland and Cage then took time off and didn't return until March's Revolution to revive the feud. They wrestled again at Dynasty with FTR picking up the victory and successful title defense. With the record now standing at 1-1 between the teams, this would be the deciding match that could determine the true greatest tag team.

As of the reporting, the match is unofficial but expected to be added to the Double or Nothing lineup. FTR has yet to publicly respond to the challenge, but the pressure is now firmly on them to decide whether they'll accept the terms. With Double or Nothing approaching on Sunday, May 24 in Queens, New York, this could quickly become one of the most talked-about matches on the card if it's made official.

🔥 Samoa Joe AEW Dynamite Return

Samoa Joe is officially back in action for All Elite Wrestling. Tony Khan confirmed that Joe has been medically cleared and will return to competition on April 22's episode of AEW Dynamite, marking his first match since being sidelined earlier this year with a non-contact injury.

Joe's last in-ring appearance came on the January 21 edition of Dynamite, where he scored a victory over Mike Bailey. It was revealed on the January 28 episode of Dynamite that he had suffered a non-contact injury, placing him on concussion protocol and preventing him from being cleared to compete at the time. The exact circumstances of the injury were not disclosed, though it occurred during or after his match with Bailey.

Khan shared the news during his livestream on X, expressing enthusiasm about Joe's return. He stated, "I got some great news about medical clearance. I found out today that Samoa Joe is cleared to return to the ring. Samoa Joe has been out of action with injury, and the former two-time world champion, the leader of The Opps is back. Samoa Joe will be in action on Wednesday Night Dynamite tonight. I am so excited about that. We've got so much great action in the show tonight and it's gonna be great to get Samoa Joe, the Samoan submission machine back in AEW tonight."

Two months ago, Joe had told fans that he would return "sooner than later," and the announcement of his clearance marks the fulfillment of that promise. His return adds significant momentum to AEW's programming as the company builds toward Double or Nothing.

At the time of Khan's announcement, Joe's opponent for the evening had not yet been confirmed. However, the show featured a notably stacked lineup that included other significant matches. Brody King was scheduled to face Lio Rush in singles competition, while AEW World Champion Darby Allin was set to defend his title against Tommaso Ciampa in the night's main event. Will Ospreay faced Mark Davis, and Hikaru Shida competed against Mina Shirakawa.

Joe's return also has implications for his stable, The Opps. During his absence, fellow member Hook stepped up as a leader and has been attempting to recruit new members, with Anthony Bowens set to learn about joining the group on Saturday's Collision show. Now that Joe is back as the group's main figure, it will be interesting to see whether Hook continues in a leadership capacity or steps back, as Joe reasserts his position within the faction.

The announcement came as a last-minute addition to the show, enhancing what was already a loaded card and giving fans another reason to tune in to the broadcast from the Veterans Memorial Coliseum in Portland, Oregon. Joe's return immediately added intrigue to the show, especially considering his history as one of AEW's most dangerous competitors and former world champion.

CHRIS JERICHO TEAMS WITH HURT SYNDICATE FOR AEW COLLISION TRIOS MATCH

In a surprising turn of events that would have seemed impossible just years prior, Chris Jericho and MVP will be aligned on the same team. The two men have a storied history of conflict, including a reported fight in 2022 when both were working for different companies and an earlier incident during the 2020 Jericho Cruise when MVP reportedly knocked Jericho out. The trios match was established on Wednesday's AEW Dynamite after Ricochet continued his assault on Jericho's credibility, specifically mocking the veteran wrestler for burning all his bridges and having no friends. Ricochet pointed out that no one wants to be associated with someone who gets his ass kicked by The Demand. Rather than being discouraged, Jericho responded by noting that he doesn't need to find partners who like him, just two individuals who don't like Ricochet.

Later in the same episode, backstage interviewer Renee Paquette caught up with Jericho, who revealed he had already secured partners. MVP emerged from Jericho's locker room alongside Shelton Benjamin and Bobby Lashley, forming The Hurt Syndicate's alliance with the legendary Jericho. When the two competitors locked eyes, MVP broke the tension by saying, "It's never personal. It's just business."

The Hurt Syndicate has a notable history with The Gates of Agony, having feuded with them in the past. Benjamin, Lashley and MVP had been absent from AEW Collision programming for an extended period, with Lashley missing more time than the others, before returning last week to secure a dominant squash victory. Their return and subsequent alignment with Jericho signals a significant development in the current storyline.

The Demand, consisting of Ricochet and Gates of Agony members Toa Liona and Bishop Kaun, will provide formidable opposition. This feud has been building momentum over recent weeks, with Ricochet defeating Jericho at AEW Dynasty 2026, followed by The Demand attacking the legendary wrestler in a three-on-one assault on the April 15 episode of Dynamite.

AEW Dynamite April 22 2026 Review

AEW Dynamite - 4/22/2026: 3 Things We Hated And 3 We Loved

The first "AEW Dynamite" of the Darby Allin Era has come and gone. Allin took center stage for the show, not only being the subject of MJF's ire, but also defending his title against Tommaso Ciampa, and being challenged by his friend, Brody King. Yes, it was a Darby-tastic episode of "Dynamite," which you can read about on the results page.

As always, it is now time for the wrestling staff to discuss their feelings. It's time to love and time to hate.

There was plenty to love, like the crackerjack main event, or The Death Riders kidnapping Will Ospreay, and there was plenty to hate, like Copeland's sweaty stipulation or the fact that MJF's issues with Darby Allin have neutered every other challenger.

Enough of pontificating, it's time to get into the best and the worst from Darby Allin's "AEW Dynamite."

HATED: MJF Stealing The Spotlight

It's only been a week, but so far Darby Allin's reign as AEW Champion has been all about MJF. Allin had a killer match with Tommaso Ciampa in the main event, and he's set to have another likely-killer match with Brody King, but until MJF gets his rematch, I'm not too worried about Darby's chances.

It has a certain deflating effect on these challenges that people are making, while MJF raises a stink.

Considering how much of Darby's title reign feels like making up for time lost on Everest, it also feels like AEW isn't quite ready to commit, instead making sure MJF is in the wings, ready to take the title off the little guy the second he falters.

I hope I'm wrong. I hope Brody King dethrones Darby and forces MJF and Darby to work out their issues without the title. It would at least be the most narratively interesting choice, as we are currently on a deathmarch to MJF's likely rematch in NYC at Double or Nothing.

Again, I hope I'm wrong.

LOVED: The Death Riders Abduct Will Ospreay

This week's "AEW Dynamite" saw Will Ospreay taking on the Don Callis Family's Mark Davis in what was always going to be a very good match between wrestlers who understand each other very well.

And during that match, Ospreay suffered a neck injury and couldn't continue the match, a neat little way to give Davis the win in a logical way that doesn't undermine any championship momentum being built with Ospreay. Ospreay is struggling in his return, his very injured neck and back against the wall at the hands of a "Dunkzilla" that sees the end of his career as a statement point. And that provides the groundwork for Ospreay to return and take out Davis at a later date.

What was really interesting about the segment, however, came afterwards. Davis was looking to ensure Ospreay doesn't get up again, but had drawn the audience of the Death Riders and Jon Moxley. Ospreay challenged Moxley for the Continental title at Dynasty and fell short, but Moxley wasn't here to see any further damage done to his challenger.

He stood, stared, and watched Davis retreat from the Riders, like the leader of a pride come to acquiesce to what another lion had killed. From there, Ospreay was at their mercy. And mercy was exactly what he got. Well, I think it was mercy. On Moxley's order, the Riders took Ospreay up, Claudio Castagnoli putting him on his shoulders, and left the venue without ceremony.

I have no idea if this is supposed to indicate Ospreay will be working with the Death Riders going forward, though this is the time of year that Anarchy in the Arena or a Stadium Stampede occurs with two groups against one another; Mark Davis and the Don Callis Family against Will Ospreay and the Death Riders does sound like a worthy proposal.

But for the time being, at least it was something that hooked me in for the next show, a reason to want to tune in and find out what on earth was actually happening. Moxley and Ospreay had one of the better matches on an exceptional Dynasty card, so anything they've got cooking up is likely to deliver quality storytelling going forward.

"I'm hoping I qualify anyway," Ospreay said. "But like, I've had four matches, I think, so far, and like, I've barely escaped with the wins in quite a lot of them. So I don't know if I'll be in contention. Hopefully, the fact that I was a finalist last year is enough to put me in there. But I need to start racking up some big wins, because I know the toll it's taken on my body, and I'm not meeting the mark. I'm not getting the big wins I need to get. So hopefully this match with Davis will change things this Wednesday."

Qualifying and then winning the Owen Hart Cup tournament would put Ospreay in line for an AEW World Championship match at AEW All In later this summer, a show that happens to be held in London's Wembley Stadium, not far from where Ospreay grew up. Ospreay is no stranger to wrestling at Wembley, having previously defeated Chris Jericho and MJF at AEW All In in 2023 and 2024 in the legendary arena. Winning the AEW World Championship in front of his home fans, however, is something even Ospreay recognizes would be a step above the rest.

"That moment would be...I couldn't put it into words, because years ago, when they first did that Wembley show, it didn't seem like it was real, and it didn't seem like it was possible to, especially, fill it at the time," Ospreay said. "And I wasn't even, like, a part of the company back then. So the fact that...I could never imagine what Wembley Stadium would look like. And now I've been in there, and I've felt it. Winning a championship in that venue, to win the most coveted prize in all of pro wrestling, I honestly think it would be one of those moments where, like, I finally did something cool in my life, that I can actually look back on and be proud of that."
