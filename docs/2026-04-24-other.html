🔥 TNA Impact Lineups and Matches

TNA Impact's April 30 episode is shaping up to be a stacked card following announcements made during the April 23 broadcast from the Upstate Medical Arena at The Oncenter War Memorial in Syracuse, New York. Mustafa Ali will defend his TNA International Championship against Adam Brooks in an open challenge match. Brooks, representing Australia, answered Ali's call during the closing moments of the April 23 episode, setting up what promises to be a competitive title bout.

One of the most unique matches on the card is the Walk with Elijah Guitar Strap Match between Elijah and Frankie Kazarian. The stipulation was born from an intense confrontation on the April 23 episode, when a man disguised as Elijah emerged in the ring with a guitar and microphone, taking verbal shots at Syracuse before attempting to perform a song called "Kaz Is My Daddy." The disguised man was revealed to be Kazarian himself, who was then attacked by the real Elijah with a guitar strap. During this attack, Elijah announced their upcoming match. The Walk with Elijah Guitar Strap Match rules are unique: the action begins backstage with both competitors linked via a guitar strap with no disqualifications and no countouts. Victory is achieved when one competitor touches all four turnbuckles in the ring in succession while still connected to their opponent. This match provides Elijah an opportunity to even the score after losing to Kazarian at TNA Rebellion. Before their encounter, Elijah performed a song dissing Kazarian and invited fans to join him at a local restaurant afterward.

🔥 Leon Slater WrestleCon Incident

The WrestleCon Mark Hitchcock Memorial Supershow was originally set to be headlined by Leon Slater versus Ricochet on April 16, 2026, in Las Vegas during WWE WrestleMania week. However, TNA President Carlos Silva pulled Slater from the bout just days before the scheduled date, citing "partner conflicts." The decision affected not only Slater but multiple other TNA wrestlers who were booked to face AEW talent at recent events pulled by the TNA President.

WrestleCon promoter Michael Bochicchio initially expressed frustration over the last-minute change, stating that Slater was not scheduled to lose the match and that there had been no prior communication from TNA regarding the decision. The sudden cancellation created significant disruption to the card and drew considerable backlash toward Silva for the handling of the situation.

However, the dispute between the two parties has since been resolved through direct communication. Silva reached out to Bochicchio personally about a week after pulling Slater to explain his reasoning for the decision. During this conversation, Silva asked what he could do to make amends for the late change. Bochicchio requested that Silva reimburse WrestleCon for Leon Slater's flight and hotel expenses since the wrestler was no longer performing at the event.

Silva followed through on this request. On Thursday, April 24, 2026, Bochicchio publicly announced that he had been reimbursed for all of Slater's travel and lodging costs as promised. In his statement, Bochicchio acknowledged Silva's efforts to make things right, saying: "He took a beating the last few weeks, so I want to give Carlos Silva his props today. A week ago, he called me personally to explain his reasoning for pulling the match. He then asked if there was anything he could do to make up for it. I simply asked that he reimburse me for Leon's flights/hotel since he was no longer wrestling for us. Today, I was reimbursed for the flight/hotel as promised. Thank you Carlos."

With Slater no longer on the card, WrestleCon proceeded with a new main event. The Demand faced off against Mike Bailey, Kevin Knight, and Michael Oku in the headlining bout of the Supershow. The resolution of this situation demonstrates how professional communication and willingness to take financial responsibility can resolve booking conflicts between wrestling promotions.

Andre the Giant Historical Marker

Andre the Giant is being honored by a small North Carolina town with a new roadside historical marker recognizing his unique connection to the area. Ellerbe, North Carolina, a quiet community with fewer than 1,000 residents, officially unveiled the marker on Thursday, April 23, at the intersection of NC Highway 73 and Old NC Highway 220.

The marker reads: "Andre The Giant. 1946-1993. Actor and professional wrestler. Was born Andre Roussimoff. Known for role in The Princess Bride in 1987. Lived nearby." According to North Carolina's Department of Natural & Cultural Resources, Andre's time in the community left a lasting impression on residents.

During his time in Ellerbe, Andre became involved in local civic efforts beyond simply enjoying a slower pace of life. He notably participated in efforts opposing a proposed radioactive waste site in Richmond County, where his presence reportedly carried significant influence. Residents came to know Andre as a "gentle, thoughtful man" who valued privacy and friendships during his time living in the area.

Andre also received a major posthumous honor last week when his iconic match against Hulk Hogan at WrestleMania III was inducted into the Hall of Fame as the 2026 Immortal Moment Award winner. Andre's daughter Robin and Hogan's son Nick were both on hand to accept the recognition on his behalf.

Liv Morgan Admits She Lost Faith After Lengthy 2025 WWE Injury Absence

Liv Morgan is enjoying the peak of her career as the current Women's World Champion. However, her path to success has not been without significant setbacks during her decade-long career in WWE. One of the most challenging periods came in 2025 when a serious injury derailed her momentum at what felt like the worst possible time.

Morgan suffered a right shoulder injury during a match against Kairi Sane on RAW in June 2025. The injury required surgery and a grueling recovery process that lasted approximately five months. During this time, she was forced to vacate her tag-team title and sit out of action while she rehabilitated.

Speaking on the What Do You Wanna Talk About podcast with Cody Rhodes, Morgan opened up about the emotional toll that the lengthy absence took on her. She revealed that the injury hit her particularly hard because it challenged one of her core beliefs about life and adversity.

Morgan explained her mindset going into the injury: "Honestly, the injury was really hard for me. Like I took it really hard. I was very sad for a very long time. I'm like a very much like 'everything happens for a reason' type person. Even if something happens that's terrible. I'm so, like, optimistically delusional that I'm like, 'No, this happened for my greater good, and maybe I don't see it now, but I know this happened for my greater good.' My injury, I was like, 'No, this happened just to f*ck me.'"

The uncertainty surrounding her return made the situation even more difficult. Morgan felt like her career had come to a complete halt at the worst possible moment. Missing major events like SummerSlam in her home state and WWE Evolution compounded her frustration and sense of loss.

Morgan reflected on her fears during the recovery period: "I didn't know what I was gonna come back to as far as work. Like I was gonna be out for six months. Like that is so unknown. I could come back and everything that I know could be different. And so I just was sad, and I just really didn't know, like, what to believe in if I didn't believe that I was gonna be okay at the end of the day."

The turning point in Morgan's recovery came unexpectedly during a conversation with her teammate Raquel Rodriguez. Morgan was venting her frustrations during a FaceTime call when Rodriguez offered her a perspective that proved life-changing, though simple in its delivery.

Morgan recounted the moment: "I was just on FaceTime, just like being upset as I was always being. And I was just telling her like, 'I just don't believe in anything. I don't have any faith right now on what this looks like on the other side.' But she just was like, 'Sister, I feel like when you feel like you have no faith is when you're supposed to have the most.' And it was just so simple. But I just was like, 'Huh!'"

Rodriguez's words did not immediately solve all of Morgan's emotional struggles, but they marked the beginning of a mental shift. The message resonated deeply with her and helped her gradually change her perspective on her circumstances.

Morgan described how this conversation began her road back: "I feel like that was when I was kind of able to stop feeling sorry for myself. And just pick myself up a little bit. But that was like the start of me feeling like, 'Okay, I'm coming back like mentally, emotionally, and physically.' And so Raquel definitely, definitely helped me out of some rainy days."

Morgan's comeback began at Survivor Series in November 2025, where she made a significant impact by attacking John Cena during his match against Dominik Mysterio. This moment signaled her return to prominence and set the stage for what would follow.

The momentum continued to build in 2026. Morgan won the Royal Rumble, establishing herself as a major force heading into the flagship WrestleMania event. At WrestleMania 42, she captured the Women's World Championship by defeating Stephanie Vaquer, cementing her status as one of WWE's top female competitors.

Cody Rhodes Reveals Vince McMahon Once Blasted John Cena’s Music on WWE Jet

A fun behind-the-scenes story from Cody Rhodes has given a funny look at what goes on behind the scenes on WWE's corporate jet, and it includes Vince McMahon blasting John Cena's music at full volume during a flight. Cena's album, You Can't See Me, came out on May 10, 2005. It wasn't just a fun side project for Cena. He self-financed it, released it, and even performed it in front of live crowds, just like a proper hip-hop artist. And the album actually did really well. It reached No. 15 on the Billboard 200 and later went platinum, selling over 1.3 million copies. It also included Cena's famous entrance theme, The Time Is Now, along with songs like Bad, Bad Man and Right Now. The album became a big part of Cena's character and helped boost his rise in WWE.

Interestingly, Vince wasn't always sure about Cena's rap-style character in the beginning. Early on, Cena even had more generic music that didn't match his personality. But once he started making his own music and fully committed to the "word-life", McMahon changed his opinion. By the time the album was released, McMahon apparently became one of Cena's biggest fans.

Speaking on his podcast, What Do You Wanna Talk About, along with Liv Morgan, Cody Rhodes shared a story he heard from his father, Dusty Rhodes. It goes back to 2005, when Cena released his album. At that time, Cena was already getting popular with his "Doctor of Thuganomics" character, but no one expected his music to become such a big deal backstage, especially with Vince McMahon.

According to Rhodes, McMahon played Cena's album on the company jet at full volume. "When the album first came out, when John did that album, most people know he self-financed it and did everything himself. When that album first came out, Vince was such a fan of it that he played it on the corporate jet at max. My dad had just come back to work here and he couldn't tell if it was a rib or it was like, 'Oh, this is what these jet rides are like?' It's just so loud. Like the whole flight, 'What? John was on the flight too?'" Rhodes said.

This story also shows the kind of relationship McMahon had with Cena. Cena has often said he saw him as a mentor and someone who helped shape his career.
