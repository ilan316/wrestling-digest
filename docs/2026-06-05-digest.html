🔥 MJF Injury and Beyond Wrestling Replacement

AEW STAR ANDRADE EL IDOLO STEPS IN FOR INJURED MJF AT BEYOND WRESTLING EVENT

A significant change has been made to Beyond Wrestling's upcoming "Break The Walls Down" event after an injury update sidelined one of the promotion's originally advertised headliners. MJF had been scheduled to face Bobby Orlando on the June 5 card in Cranston, Rhode Island. However, following events on AEW Dynamite, those plans became subject to change. This created a last-minute opening that Beyond Wrestling quickly moved to fill.

MJF reportedly suffered a hyperextended knee during his AEW World Championship match against Rush on the June 3 episode of Dynamite. As a result, the reigning AEW World Champion did not receive medical clearance to compete or travel. According to reports from Justin Barrasso, Andrade El Idolo will replace MJF against Bobby Orlando at the event. The change comes as Andrade makes his Beyond Wrestling debut. The AEW and NJPW star, who currently holds the IWGP Global Heavyweight Championship, will now step into a match that suddenly carries a very different flavor. While fans won't witness the originally advertised showdown, they'll still see a major international star make his first appearance for the company.

The injury is believed to be legitimate, though there is optimism among those close to the situation that it may not result in a lengthy absence. The timing of the injury is particularly noteworthy given AEW's upcoming schedule, as the company is rapidly approaching its annual AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door pay-per-view later in the month. Several potential challengers have already begun positioning themselves for opportunities at the AEW World Championship, with Andrade having publicly expressed interest in challenging MJF.

Beyond Wrestling founder Drew Cordeiro detailed the effort required to save the card. "I've spent the last 20 hours calling everyone I know, and I can't thank the wrestlers at AEW enough for going above and beyond and checking virtually everyone's availability to make this happen on such short notice," Cordeiro said. He also noted that Andrade, Lio Rush, and Wheeler YUTA were added to the event within hours. Moreover, he revealed that talent and management from several promotions helped explore options wholeheartedly, noting that people in WWE were even exploring options as well.

Cordeiro expressed pride in how the wrestling community rallied around the independent promotion. "My heart swells with pride that so many people within the wrestling industry care about Beyond Wrestling and our fans. To me, independent wrestling doesn't mean avoiding all of the biggest companies—it means pulling out all the stops to navigate the different dynamics to give this Cranston crowd the best possible show," he stated.

TNA also made adjustments to help the promotion, allowing Bear Bronson to change his travel plans for their tapings in Colorado this weekend so he could appear at Beyond's event. The show will also feature AEW's Megan Bayne against indie standout Gabby Forza. An announcement regarding Beyond's biggest show of the year, "Americanrana," will be made during the show.

MJF personally informed Drew Cordeiro that he fully intends to fulfill a future commitment to Beyond Wrestling once he is healthy enough to do so. The timing made this cancellation particularly noteworthy because MJF had recently stated that his booking against Bobby Orlando would likely be his final independent wrestling match for what he described as "an extraordinarily long time," as he said he needed to focus on AEW.

With Andrade now officially stepping into the spot, Beyond Wrestling's "Break The Walls Down" event remains intact despite losing one of its biggest advertised attractions. The event will stream live on IWTV from Rhodes On The Pawtuxet on June 5. As of now, there has been no indication that MJF's status for upcoming AEW events is in jeopardy, but fans will undoubtedly be monitoring his condition closely in the days ahead.

🔥 The Butcher Departs AEW

The Butcher and The Blade have officially left All Elite Wrestling following the expiration of their contracts. According to multiple reports, AEW executives made the decision not to extend the duo's agreements, making both men free agents immediately available to work with any promotion. The pair had been with AEW since November 27, 2019, when they debuted on Dynamite as part of the company's original wave of signings.

During their seven-year tenure with AEW, The Butcher and The Blade became one of the promotion's recognizable early tag teams, known for their gritty and hard-hitting style. Over the years, they were featured in various storylines across multiple AEW programming including Dynamite, Rampage, and Collision. The team worked alongside several notable acts throughout their run, including The Bunny (Allie), MJF, Kip Sabian, and the Matt Hardy Family Office. While they were never positioned as consistent championship contenders, they provided depth to AEW's tag team roster throughout multiple eras of the promotion.

Their in-ring activity had slowed considerably in recent years, particularly due to health issues. The Blade was sidelined with a spine injury that kept him off AEW television since January 2024. He underwent spinal fusion surgery in September 2024 before returning to the ring on December 13, 2025. During his absence, The Butcher continued working singles matches in AEW, with his last match for the company coming in February 2025 when he lost to Gabe Kidd on Collision. The Butcher had not wrestled for AEW since that February outing, and there were several instances throughout 2025 where he expressed his desire to get back in action under the AEW and Ring of Honor banner.

Their most recent teaming together took place outside of AEW at Limitless Wrestling's Bissell Brothers Bash in April, where they defeated former WWE ID talent Bryce Donovan and Vinny Scalice. Prior to that, their last AEW tag team match was a six-man tag team bout on March 1st, 2024, where they teamed with Kip Sabian against Mark Briscoe and The Hardys.

With their contracts now expired and no extensions forthcoming from AEW, both men have immediately become available to the wider wrestling landscape. The Blade has already worked matches for GCW and various promotions in Canada since his return from injury. The Butcher recently competed in mystery wrestling events, challenging for the PROGRESS Atlas Championship against former AEW star Bear Bronson and other competitors.

The Butcher has already capitalized on his free agency, with his first post-AEW match officially announced. Andy Williams will face "The Warmaster" Josh Barnett at PRODUCE Volume 3 AZUCAR on August 3rd at 8 PM at PioneerWorks in Brooklyn, New York. The event will also feature Rocky Romero competing against Mistico.

At 48 and 46 years old respectively, The Butcher and The Blade are not young enough to be considered developmental prospects for larger companies like WWE, though there has been speculation about WWE's potential interest in bolstering its tag team division. Regardless of their next destination, both men now have a clean slate and can pursue opportunities with any promotion as they explore the next chapters of their wrestling careers. Their departure marks another significant shift in AEW's roster as the company continues to evolve from its foundational years.

🔥 Athena ROH Women's Title Defense

ROH Women's World Champion Athena will put her record-long title streak on the line against Stardom's Syuri at ROH Global Wars: Cincinnati. The match was announced on June 5, 2026, as part of the newly-announced ROH versus CMLL versus Stardom Global Wars show that will air on June 18 on HonorClub. The event will be filmed during AEW's Summer Blockbuster shows in Cincinnati.

This matchup represents a rematch between two competitors who have crossed paths before. The two women previously shared the ring during last July's women's Casino Gauntlet match at All In: Texas, where Syuri made her AEW debut. Most notably, Syuri defeated Athena on February 27 at NJPW The New Beginning USA while successfully defending her IWPW Women's Championship, giving Syuri a decisive victory over the ROH champion.

Athena has been pursuing this rematch to handle what she considers unfinished business. The ROH Women's World Champion has been chasing this match for a while and is using Global Wars as an opportunity to demand a rematch to get her win back against the Stardom wrestler. Athena called out Syuri directly, stating she plans to rip Syuri limb from limb and is looking to right a wrong and even the score.

Athena's ROH title reign has been nothing short of dominant. The Fallen Goddess has held the ROH Women's World Title for over 1,270 days after winning it at ROH Final Battle 2022. During her tenure, she has made 36 successful title defenses, making this one of the most impressive runs in women's wrestling history.

Syuri still holds the IWPW Women's Championship, but only the ROH title will be on the line at Global Wars. This rematch will test whether Athena can finally defeat Syuri or if the Stardom wrestler will prove once again that she has Athena's number.

The Global Wars event is scheduled as part of the run-up to AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door, which is set for Sunday, June 28 in San Jose, California. Athena also has her own responsibilities to attend to, as she is awaiting her opponent in the semifinals of the AEW women's Owen Hart Foundation tournament, which will likely take place while she is in Cincinnati as well.
