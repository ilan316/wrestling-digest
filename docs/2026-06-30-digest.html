🔥 Mercedes Mone Teases Match Against Bayley at AEW

Mercedes Mone is causing a stir in the wrestling community after winning the 2026 Owen Hart Foundation Women's Tournament at AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door. Fresh off her victory over Maya World in a highly praised match, Mone took to her Instagram Stories on June 29 to share a fan-made graphic teasing a potential showdown with WWE star Bayley at AEW WrestleDream 2026. The graphic billed Bayley under her former indie wrestling name Davina Rose while also depicting Mone as the AEW Women's World Champion, a title she does not currently hold but will be challenging for at AEW All In on August 30 at Wembley Stadium in London.

Mone and Bayley share one of the most celebrated rivalries and partnerships in modern women's wrestling. During their time together in WWE, when Mone performed as Sasha Banks, the two had one of the most influential women's matches ever at NXT TakOver Brooklyn in 2015. They feuded over championships and also achieved success as a tag team called The Boss N' Hug Connection, becoming the inaugural WWE Women's Tag Team Champions in 2019 and regaining the titles in May 2020. The two have not had a singles match against each other since 2020, though they remain close friends and have maintained a strong connection since Mone departed WWE for AEW.

Since Mone joined AEW, Bayley has spoken openly about wanting to reunite on-screen with her one day. However, Bayley has never indicated an interest in leaving WWE and has expressed a desire to eventually transition into a backstage role with the company. At the SAP Center in San Jose, California, where Forbidden Door took place, Mone also paid tribute to Bayley and her late hero Eddie Guerrero on her Instagram Stories, suggesting that her former rival was definitely on her mind while in Bayley's hometown.

Significant obstacles currently stand in the way of such a match becoming reality. Bayley remains under contract with WWE with no indication she is looking to leave the company. According to the latest reports, her current WWE contract is expected to run through the end of 2026 or potentially early 2027. During an appearance on Insight with Chris Van Vliet in April 2025, Bayley stated she had approximately a year and a half remaining on her deal at that time but did not give a specific end date. A multi-year extension was reportedly signed in late 2023, and there is currently no concrete information regarding contract negotiations or her future plans beyond WWE.

As things stand, Bayley remains a fixture on WWE television. Most recently, she was teaming with Lyra Valkyria, though that partnership came to an abrupt end one week prior to Forbidden Door when Valkyria turned heel on Raw following their failure to capture the Women's World Tag Team Championships from Brie Bella and Paige. She is set to feud with Valkyria going forward. Bayley has positioned herself as a locker room leader in the women's division and has spoken about possibly working behind the scenes at WWE whenever her time in the ring comes to an end.

WrestleDream's 2026 date and location have not yet been announced, though the event has traditionally been held in October. Whether Bayley would have any interest in or contractual ability to join AEW remains unknown. The tease has certainly fueled fan speculation about what the future holds for both wrestlers, though whether Mone is simply working the audience, testing the waters, or engaging in genuine wishful thinking remains unclear.

Mone's focus for the immediate future remains on her championship challenge at All In. Having won the Owen Hart Cup for the second straight year, she earned a guaranteed shot at the AEW Women's World Championship. She will be challenging Thekla for the title at All In 2026 on August 30 at Wembley Stadium in London. Her victory at Forbidden Door demonstrated her continued dominance, with Hall of Famer Mick Foley stating before the event that he had never met anyone as driven to be the best as Mercedes. Foley also noted that in AEW, there is one way to prove you are the best, and that is to win the AEW World Championship.

🔥 AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door 2026 Coverage

AEW and New Japan Pro Wrestling presented their Forbidden Door event on Sunday, June 28, 2026, a co-promoted show that aired on the same day as TNA's Slammiversary. The event marked another chapter in the annual tradition of inter-promotional dream matches, though this year's card reflected challenges facing NJPW in terms of star power due to recent signings by AEW and organizational changes following a Cyber Fight acquisition.

The broadcast ran over five hours with nine matches, extending past midnight on the East Coast and nearly 1 AM in some regions. While the event delivered quality in-ring action that fans expected, critics noted the excessive length made it an impractical purchase for a Sunday night pay-per-view priced at $50. The extended runtime meant viewers were going to bed well past midnight on a work week, which arguably limits appeal beyond the most dedicated wrestling audience. The rating of broadcast to in-ring time was notably strong, with nearly four hours of actual wrestling across the card, but the cumulative effect of multiple lengthy matches created pacing issues.

Opening with a three-way tag team match that featured spectacular high spots and dives, the show kicked off with the Young Bucks defeating Titan and another team after hitting the Meltzer Driver. While this type of opening match serves to energize the crowd with fireworks, critics noted that the repetition of high-flying tag team spotfests throughout the show yields diminishing returns.

Kenny Omega faced Zack Sabre Jr., one of the few key New Japan performers that AEW has not signed, in a match that carried genuine anticipation. The bout exceeded 25 minutes and served as only the second match of the evening. It featured technical exchanges, striking sequences, and dramatic back-and-forth momentum swings typical of New Japan main events. Omega ultimately won with the One-Winged Angel, but the extended length meant nearly four hours of programming remained, leading some to suggest the match could have been tightened to 15 minutes without losing effectiveness.

The Jon Moxley versus Bandido match appeared to be included primarily to ensure both wrestlers were featured on the pay-per-view, suggesting it was potentially shoehorned into the lineup rather than serving a narrative purpose. This further contributed to the sense of an overstuffed card.

The marquee match of the evening was the introduction of Death's Door, a 12-man tag team match contested inside a rounded steel cage. Mark Briscoe led The Conglomeration alongside Darby Allin and Konosuke Takeshita to victory over Team MJF, which featured AEW World Champion MJF and the Don Callis Family. The match was described as AEW's most gruesome of the night, featuring explosive bags, thumb tacks, multiple tables, skateboards, and Allin executing a Coffin Drop from the top of the cage structure. Dave Meltzer stated on Wrestling Observer Radio that the match will likely become a staple of future Forbidden Door events, suggesting the specialized cage match could be an annual tradition going forward.

Following his victory at Forbidden Door, Briscoe was announced to receive an AEW World Title shot against MJF on Wednesday's AEW Dynamite. This marks the third encounter between the two wrestlers in ten months. MJF defeated Briscoe in their first match, while Briscoe won their second encounter in a Tables N' Tacks match at AEW All Out the previous year. Additionally, Andrade appears positioned for a title opportunity after betraying MJF during Death's Door.

Other notable results included Shota Umino retaining the IWGP Global Heavyweight Title against Pac. Cope and Cage defeated The Dogs of War in the AEW World Tag Team Championship match, with Jay White making his long-awaited return during the bout. AEW President Tony Khan revealed that White had just been cleared medically that week after an extended absence. Khan noted that the friction between the Bang Bang Gang and The Dogs had been building even during White's time away, with The Bang Bang Gang consistently alluding to seeking payback. Khan believed Forbidden Door was the perfect venue for White's return given his history with New Japan Pro Wrestling.

Mercedes Mone won the Owen Hart Cup Finals, defeating Maya World. Mone wore black and pink gear reminiscent of Hart Family colors as a tribute. WWE's Natalya acknowledged the gesture, stating she was "truly honored" by Mone's nod to the Hart legacy. With this victory, Mone earned a World Title opportunity against Thekla for the AEW Women's World Title on August 30 at Wembley Stadium in London during AEW All In.

During the post-show press conference, Tony Khan addressed WWE's decision to book multiple shows against AEW programming. Khan responded positively to the competitive scheduling, comparing it favorably to historical rivalries and stating it was "double the compliment Jimmy Crockett got." He expressed confidence in AEW's upcoming AEW All In event at Wembley Stadium and described it as what he believes will be "the biggest night in AEW wrestling." Khan also gifted NJPW President Hiroshi Tanahashi a custom guitar backstage as a gesture of respect for his legendary career.

The weekend itself was packed with wrestling content, as Forbidden Door was part of a 36-hour wrestling marathon that included WWE Night of Champions, AEW Collision, AAA Lucha Libre, TNA Slammiversary, and NXT Great American Bash. Across these six events, 118 wrestlers competed in 44 matches totaling roughly 18 broadcast hours. Of those, approximately 10 hours and 6 minutes consisted of actual in-ring wrestling time. Forbidden Door delivered the best broadcast-to-wrestling ratio with nearly the entire show dedicated to matches, including two bouts that exceeded 30 minutes in duration.

The event's structure reflected ongoing creative decisions at AEW regarding match placement and card management. While the Forbidden Door concept of inter-promotional dream matches remains compelling, the challenge of producing an annual event with limited fresh matchups between available talent has become apparent. The addition of Death's Door as a potential recurring match type suggests AEW and NJPW are exploring new creative directions to sustain the event's appeal in future years.

🔥 Mick Foley Potential AEW Match with Darby Allin

Mick Foley has left the door open to potentially wrestling one final match in AEW, though he's being cautious about the possibility. The WWE Hall of Famer recently signed with AEW in a broadcaster role and is expected to continue hosting events for the promotion going forward. During a conversation with Ariel Helwani on The Ariel Helwani Show earlier this month, Foley addressed the possibility of one more match. He emphasized that while he wouldn't rule it out entirely, several conditions would need to be met.

"I had this great 30-minute conversation with Ariel Helwani and he asked me about the possibility of one more match," Foley explained. "You never know. I did say if the stars were aligned, but we have to see how the stars align. I'd have to be in really good shape and feel like I could have a match that wouldn't disappoint people."

Foley reiterated these sentiments during a subsequent interview with Going Ringside, conducted at River City Wrestling Con in Florida on June 5-6. He gave the impression that some fans may have gotten ahead of themselves with his initial comments about a potential return. The 61-year-old remains realistic about the physical demands of wrestling, especially given his current condition and his medical history.

When asked about potential opponents if he were to step back into the ring, Foley immediately brought up AEW's Darby Allin. The WWE legend has previously spoken highly of Allin's fearless wrestling style. "I pointed out to Darby that I am on record as saying he would be a dream opponent," Foley said. "So you never know what the future may bring."

However, Foley was candid about the work he would need to do before such a match could happen. Despite having lost a considerable amount of weight and undergone hip and knee replacements that have greatly improved his mobility, the veteran wrestler acknowledged his current limitations. "I'm down a lot of weight, but my cardio is suspect at best, and I'd still have to lose another 30 pounds just to hit poor condition. So we'll see if there's some changes in the next year."

The physical considerations are significant. Foley would need to structure any potential match carefully to avoid jeopardizing the improvements he's made through his weight loss and surgeries. Additionally, he would need to limit head trauma as much as possible given his long history of concussions throughout his wrestling career. His last televised match appearance was a cameo in the 2012 WWE Royal Rumble, and his last one-on-one match came over a year before that when he faced Ric Flair in a Last Man Standing Match in TNA Wrestling.

While his AEW role remains primarily as a broadcaster and pre-show host, Foley has indicated during his recent interviews that he could see himself contributing in a variety of other capacities with the company. He mentioned the possibility of serving as an on-screen manager for some of the AEW talent and believes he can be useful in promoting the AEW product through media appearances and other promotional work.

Wrestling Title Changes Historical Coverage

June 29th and 30th represent particularly busy periods in professional wrestling history when it comes to championship title changes. According to wrestling databases, June 29th has seen 334 major title changes throughout wrestling history, while June 30th has recorded 299 significant championship transitions. These back-to-back dates have produced some of the most memorable moments in wrestling history across multiple promotions and time periods.

The earliest documented title change on June 29th dates back to 1973, when Kim Sung Ho won the NWA World Light Heavyweight Title. Two years later in 1975, Blue Demon captured the NWA World Welterweight Title on this date. The modern era began in 1998 when Stone Cold Steve Austin regained the WWF Championship on RAW, just one day after losing it to Kane in a First Blood match at the King of the Ring pay-per-view event. The following year in 1999, Jeff Hardy and Matt Hardy won the WWF Tag Team Titles on June 29th.

June 30th's historical record begins even earlier, with Buddy Rogers winning the NWA World Heavyweight Title in 1961. Throughout the 1980s and 1990s, the date saw various international title changes, including Great Samu winning the Canadian International Heavyweight Title in 1986, Solar #2 claiming the NWA World Welterweight Title in 1988, Head Hunter A and Head Hunter B winning the CMLL World Tag Team Titles in 1995, and Masanobu Fuchi capturing the AJPW World Junior Heavyweight Title in 1996. Heavy Metal won the AAA Campeón de Campeones Title on June 30th, 2001.

The year 2002 stands out as particularly notable for both June 29th and June 30th, as the WWE Hardcore Championship's 24/7 rule produced multiple title changes on consecutive days. On June 29th, four wrestlers held the Hardcore Title: Steven Richards, Shawn Stasiak, Bradshaw, and Spike Dudley each became champion on this date. On June 30th, the same championship changed hands four times again, with Steven Richards, Raven, Bradshaw, and Crash Holly each winning the title under the same 24/7 rule stipulation.

The period from 2008 to 2013 brought several major title changes to these dates. CM Punk's Money in the Bank cash-in on June 30th, 2008, resulted in him winning the WWE World Heavyweight Championship and keeping the world title on the RAW brand after defeating Edge. June 29th, 2008 was equally active with four championships changing hands: Kofi Kingston won the WWE Intercontinental Title, Mark Henry captured the ECW Title, Flash and Stuka Jr. won the CMLL Arena Coliseo Tag Team Titles, and Cody Rhodes and Ted DiBiase Jr. won the WWE World Tag Team Titles. In 2013, La Máscara, Rush, and Titán won the Mexican National Trios Titles on June 30th, while Chris Sabin won the TNA X-Division Title on June 29th (which was also recorded as vacated that year).

The 2014 period saw continued championship activity on both dates. On June 29th, John Cena won the WWE World Heavyweight Title, Suwama captured the AJPW Unified Triple Crown Heavyweight Title, and Yujiro Takahashi won the NEVER Openweight Title. June 30th, 2014 saw the WWE Intercontinental Title vacated and AJ Lee winning the WWE Divas Title on the same date.

Recent years have continued this trend of significant title changes. In 2018, Jay Lethal won the ROH World Title on June 30th while Shuji Ishikawa and Suwama won the AJPW World Tag Team Titles on the same date. On June 29th, 2021, Felino won the Mexican National Light Heavyweight Title and Isaiah Scott captured the NXT North American Title. Kawato San won the CMLL World Super Lightweight Title on June 30th, 2019.

The most recent championship changes came in 2024 and 2025. On June 30th, 2024, Mercedes Moné won the NJPW Strong Women's Title and Tetsuya Naito won the IWGP World Heavyweight Title. The latest entries include Finn Bálor and JD McDonagh winning the WWE World Tag Team Titles on June 30th, 2025, HAYATA winning the GHC Hardcore Title on the same date, and Zack Sabre Jr. winning the IWGP World Heavyweight Title on June 29th, 2025. These consecutive dates continue to serve as historically significant markers for professional wrestling championship changes across all major promotions worldwide.
