🔥 AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door 12-Man Steel Cage Match

Mark Briscoe secured a historic opportunity for AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door 2026 during the Summer Blockbuster episode of AEW Dynamite on June 10. Following his victory over PAC, Briscoe called out AEW World Champion MJF, who has been consistently denying him a title opportunity despite multiple successful performances. MJF appeared through the crowd and again rejected Briscoe's challenge, insisting that he is "the final word" in AEW and that the champion has the responsibility to protect the title from wrestlers like Briscoe who he deemed unworthy of a world title picture opportunity.

The situation escalated when MJF revealed that "money talks" and orchestrated an attack on Briscoe. Jay Lethal, Blake Christian, and Lee Johnson rushed the ring to assault the Sussex County Chicken to MJF's amusement. However, The Conglomeration quickly came to Briscoe's aid, with team members like Tomohiro Ishii bulldozing through opponents while Orange Cassidy arrived in his trademark laid-back fashion to restore order. With the numbers game now in his favor, Briscoe delivered stunning news.

Briscoe revealed that he had spoken directly with AEW President Tony Khan, and Khan had approved a groundbreaking match for the Forbidden Door card. The main event will feature a 12-man steel cage tag team match with Briscoe leading one team against MJF's team. The stakes for this encounter are massive: if Briscoe's team secures victory inside the cage, he will earn a guaranteed AEW World Championship opportunity against MJF. This marks the second consecutive year that a multi-man steel cage match will main event the annual crossover event, as the 2025 Forbidden Door concluded with a Lights Out Steel Cage Match featuring multiple top talent from both promotions.

While Briscoe will naturally have his fellow Conglomeration members filling most of his roster, the composition of MJF's team remains a point of intrigue. The champion quickly moved to address this problem by turning to Don Callis for assistance. MJF approached Callis with a business proposition: he would provide financial compensation in exchange for recruiting five team members to face Briscoe's squad. According to MJF, money talks loud, and he was prepared to back up that statement financially.

The recruitment process began immediately when Kevin Knight stepped forward and expressed interest in joining Team MJF. However, Knight did not simply accept the offer outright. Instead, he negotiated a condition for his participation, demanding that MJF guarantee him a future AEW World Championship opportunity in exchange for his services in the steel cage match. MJF agreed to Knight's demands without hesitation, committing to a title shot for the younger competitor. This agreement upset Andrade El Idolo, who made his entrance and stared down MJF while questioning Don Callis about the status of his own promised world title opportunity. For a man who claims to control everything in AEW, the walls may finally be closing in, literally inside a steel cage.

Forbidden Door 2026 is scheduled for Sunday, June 28 at the SAP Center in San Jose, California. The card will be the first edition of the annual crossover officially co-promoted by AEW, CMLL, and Stardom, marking the fifth installment of the prestigious event. Beyond the 12-man steel cage main event, the lineup includes the Owen Hart Foundation Men's Tournament finals featuring Will Ospreay versus Swerve Strickland, the Owen Hart Foundation Women's Tournament finals, and a singles match between Kenny Omega and Zack Sabre Jr.

🔥 Kenny Omega Returns to Action for AEW Forbidden Door

Kenny Omega's long-awaited return to action is set for this Saturday's AEW Collision Summer Blockbuster episode, where the former AEW World Champion will face Bad Dude Tito in his first match since Stadium Stampede at May's Double or Nothing. The announcement comes as part of a larger card that has been built around Omega's comeback and his upcoming high-profile confrontation at Forbidden Door later this month.

The primary focus, however, remains on the dream match that has been officially announced for AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door on June 28 in San Jose, California. Kenny Omega will go one-on-one with Zack Sabre Jr., a bout that many fans have wanted to see revisited for years. The match was announced during Wednesday night's AEW Dynamite Summer Blockbuster special through a video package featuring Sabre Jr. issuing the challenge to Omega. Excalibur revealed on commentary that Omega had been aware the challenge was coming and had already accepted, making the highly anticipated showdown official.

Sabre Jr. issued his challenge in a compelling vignette during AEW Dynamite, stating that the two haven't squared off in the United States and while he knows he's the best technical wrestler in the world, he questioned whether Omega was still the best. Sabre Jr. referenced the R.E.M. song "What's The Frequency, Kenneth?" in his promo, adding an intriguing layer to the challenge.

This marks the first meeting between Omega and Sabre Jr. since their acclaimed clash in 2018 during the G1 Climax tournament, where Omega picked up the victory. Their only other singles bout came in 2013 for a UK indie show. With Forbidden Door emanating from San Jose, California, this will be the third continent the two have wrestled one another on, and notably their first time facing off on American soil. The history between these two international technical wrestlers has fans anticipating what could be one of the most compelling matches on the Forbidden Door card.

For Sabre Jr., this represents a return to AEW for the first time since retaining the IWGP World Heavyweight Championship over Nigel McGuinness at last year's Forbidden Door in August. Kenny Omega currently holds a 2-0 record against Sabre Jr. in their singles encounters.

THE CALIFORNIA VS. FLORIDA DIVIDE

The arbitration process has also raised questions over venue selection. AEW contends that Nemeth's preference for California effectively gives him an advantage, noting the higher number of available arbitrators in that state compared to Florida. CONFIDENTIAL MATERIALS AND SEALING REQUESTS
