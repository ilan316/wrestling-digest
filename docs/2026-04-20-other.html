🔥 Former WCW star Van Hammer passes away

Former WCW Star Van Hammer (Mark Hildreth) Passes Away At 66

Mark Hildreth, known to wrestling fans as Van Hammer, has passed away at the age of 66. The news was confirmed on Sunday by his longtime friend and former WCW colleague Marc Mero via social media. According to Mero, the cause of death has not yet been determined and remains pending an autopsy.

"It is with a heavy heart that I share the passing of our dear friend, Mark Hildreth, known to so many as Van Hammer. At this time, we are still awaiting answers regarding the cause, pending an autopsy," Mero wrote in his statement.

Mero shared a heartfelt tribute, reflecting on their friendship that began before either man entered the wrestling business. The two first met while Mero was vacationing in Venice, Florida, working out at a local gym. Not long after that meeting, Mero received a call from Dusty Rhodes to come to his office. Hildreth immediately stepped up to help his friend achieve his dream.

"Mark didn't hesitate. He drove me eight hours so I could sign my very first contract with World Championship Wrestling. That's the kind of person he was, loyal, selfless, and always there when it mattered most," Mero recalled.

The two trained together under Boris Malenko in Tampa and later lived together in Atlanta as they pursued their wrestling careers. They shared a passion for the business and worked together to build their lives around wrestling.

"We went through wrestling school together, chased the same dream, and before long, he earned his own contract. We even lived together in Atlanta, training, grinding, and building a life around the passion we both shared. Those were unforgettable moments," Mero wrote.

During their time together in WCW, Hildreth and Mero were also chosen to help promote the company internationally. They were part of WCW's United Kingdom tour, appearing alongside music icon Gladys Knight for promotional events, doing countless personal appearances and television spots together.

"We were also chosen to help promote WCW for the United Kingdom Tour, doing countless personal appearances, television spots, and even promotional work alongside Gladys Knight. Those were unforgettable moments… times I'll never forget," Mero shared.

Mero concluded his tribute with a message of resilience and remembrance. "Mark was a fighter in every sense of the word. Life threw its share of challenges his way, but he had a resilience about him. He always found a way to rise, to push forward, to keep going. I have so many incredible memories with him that I'll carry forever. My heart goes out to his family, his friends, and the fans who supported him throughout his journey. Rest easy, my friend. You will always be remembered."

Hildreth began his career after serving in the United States Navy, training under Boris Malenko and Dan Spivey. He made his WCW debut in 1991 at Clash of the Champions XVI, quickly gaining attention with his "Heavy Metal" Van Hammer persona, which drew on his rock star presentation.

He built an impressive undefeated streak early in his run before it was ended by Cactus Jack at Clash of the Champions XVII. Throughout his years in WCW, Hildreth would challenge for the United States Heavyweight Championship, but would never win gold in his career. Hildreth remained with WCW through February 1995.

After a hiatus from wrestling, he returned to WCW in November 1997 as part of Raven's Flock. After winning a "loser leaves the Flock" match against Perry Saturn in May 1998, he was kicked out of the stable anyway and went on to briefly adopt a hippie gimmick. He remained with WCW until his departure in July 2000.

Following his time in WCW, Hildreth competed internationally, including a stint with Big Japan Pro Wrestling in 2001, and also worked in Maryland Championship Wrestling. He continued working select independent events, with his final match taking place in MCW in April 2009, where he defeated Champ Champagne.

After stepping away from wrestling, Hildreth transitioned into a career in home exterior remodeling while living in Florida. Our thoughts are with Mark Hildreth's family, friends, and fans during this difficult time.

NJPW vs DDT event announced for June 2026

NJPW vs. DDT Event Announced For June 8, 2026

Toru Yano, representing NJPW, and Super Sasadango Machine, representing DDT, announced a future inter-promotional event at a joint press conference held on April 20. The companies will present an NJPW (Team Yano) vs. DDT (Team Sasadango) event on June 8, 2026, featuring multiple matches billed as a five vs. five gauntlet format.

Officials later clarified the nature of the event's structure. Five vs. five does not necessarily mean five members on each team, nor that the card will consist solely of singles matches. Tag team competition may be included as part of the card. There will be at least five matches on the event. The announcement was made "in the interests of sportsmanship" with the event to be simulcast on both Wrestle Universe and NJPW World PPV platforms.

This announcement comes one year after the two promotions produced NJPW vs. DDT Toru Yano vs. Super Sasadango Machine on June 9, 2025. The main event of that card featured Toru Yano against Super Sasadango Machine, which ended in a two-hour double countout.

Speaking at the press conference, Sasadango reflected on their previous encounter. "Our last match was something that transcended the result. I have huge respect for Toru Yano and for NJPW. This will be an event all about sportsmanship and that's the spirit I hope to bring. I'm only thinking about winning and proving a point on behalf of DDT with that win," he said.

Yano offered his perspective on the upcoming event and his thoughts on last year's match. "Last year was about DDT and NJPW's history and relationship into one focal point, but there's a lot more to be seen, and a lot more to Toru Yano than just one side as well. There's more to show, and as Sasadango has said this is about sportsmanship, I absolutely embody that spirit. I wasn't entirely happy with last time ending on a double countout. I think there should be a decisive result. As for members, you might think it's weird I'm a Hontai guy but I am, and with Hontai teammates I intend to win this," Yano stated.

The event will be available to stream on both NJPW World and Wrestle Universe, allowing international fans to watch the inter-promotional clash between the two Japanese wrestling companies.

Buff Bagwell potential in-ring return

Buff Bagwell is eyeing an unlikely return to the ring following a life-changing surgery. After undergoing a right leg amputation this past July, Bagwell has remained active on the wrestling scene with appearances for Memphis Wrestling and TNA. Most recently, he showed up on the March 26 episode of TNA iMPACT, sharing a segment with Frankie Kazarian and Elijah.

Now, the WCW veteran is setting his sights on stepping back inside the squared circle. Speaking on Busted Open Radio, Bagwell opened up about how his mindset began to shift during physical therapy, leading him to believe an in-ring comeback might actually be possible.

"I was getting down on the floor and getting up pretty good in the session, and I started thinking, if I can get down and get up, I can wrestle again," Bagwell said. "So, Tommy called me and I went to the TNA taping. We put it out there. Next week, I'll be getting in the ring for the first time for me."

It's a bold claim considering the circumstances, but Bagwell sounds confident. Despite the amputation, he believes he can adapt and still deliver in the ring, even if it's just one more memorable performance.

"Within a few minutes of physical therapy, two things I learn real quick, I needed two elbow pads, and I need one kneepad," Bagwell continued. "I really think I can do this. On flat ground, I think I can pull this off and at least have one good match for somebody, and I hope it's TNA."

One match. One moment. That might be all Buff Bagwell needs to prove that his wrestling career isn't completely over.

‘The Splash Was Unbelievable’ – WWE’s Drew McIntyre Reacts To IShowSpeed WrestleMania 42 Match, Shares Advice Following Appearance

Drew McIntyre has reacted positively to IShowSpeed's in-ring performance at WrestleMania 42, sharing constructive advice for the famous streamer following his appearance on the April 18 event.

IShowSpeed made his WWE in-ring return at WrestleMania 42, teaming with Logan Paul and Austin Theory of The Vision in a losing effort against The Usos and LA Knight. Following the match, Logan Paul attacked Speed, prompting Jimmy, Jey, and LA Knight to make the save and encourage the streamer to hit Logan with a splash through the announcer's table.

Drew McIntyre shared a video to his Instagram Story discussing the WrestleMania match and segment with IShowSpeed. McIntyre was particularly impressed with the splash spot, saying: "When I was watching the end, the splash was unbelievable. I thought he broke his ribs, you bounced off him."

Beyond praising the physical execution, McIntyre offered deeper wisdom about succeeding in professional wrestling. He emphasized that while learning moves is important, authenticity matters far more: "The hardest thing to do – Logan has done the same thing, you and Logan are so similar – the hardest thing you can learn is just to be yourself on screen. You just know who you are. Everybody can learn the moves, maybe not be as good at executing them, (but) I think that's easy for you. The personality, knowing who you are, the between the moves stuff, that's the important stuff."

McIntyre highlighted that both IShowSpeed and Logan Paul possess an innate quality that resonates with audiences: "Yourself and Logan, you just got it instantly. The fans know who you all are, the fact that they can connect with you is because you know who the fuck you are. Plus you can do the moves."

McIntyre jokingly captioned the video by saying: "I left out the part where I gave him social media advice."

It is worth noting that McIntyre himself came up short at WrestleMania 42, losing to Jacob Fatu in their Unsanctioned Match.

The six-man tag-team match marked IShowSpeed's second in-ring outing in WWE, following his impromptu addition to the 2025 Royal Rumble match. The era of one-time celebrity appearances in WWE appears to have ended, with figures like IShowSpeed, Jelly Roll, Bad Bunny, and Logan Paul wrestling for the company multiple times in recent years. Logan Paul has since become a full-time WWE wrestler.

McIntyre's comments in the video suggest he believes IShowSpeed will return to the ring in the future. With Speed demonstrating clear athletic ability and maintaining an entertaining online persona, it makes sense that he could revisit WWE periodically. The streamer appears to fit well within the professional wrestling landscape and has already earned a favorable reputation among established wrestlers like McIntyre.

Former WWE Trainer Reacts To Photo Of Shawn Michaels & Kendal Grey: “Terrible Look And Perversely Inappropriate …”

Former WWE trainer Sarah Stock has publicly criticized a social media interaction between WWE NXT talent Kendal Grey and Shawn Michaels, raising concerns about workplace professionalism and boundaries.

Grey recently took to Twitter/X to share a photo of herself hugging Michaels, playfully captioning the image. "My boss is cooler than yours," she wrote. The post quickly made the rounds online, but not all reactions were positive.

Stock responded publicly to the image, taking issue with the optics of the interaction and questioning its professionalism in a corporate environment. In her initial response, Stock stated: "Terrible look and perversely inappropriate physical contact for a corporate environment. If you're a woman and just starting out, don't be naive and don't be afraid to jam the heel of your hand into your boss's sternum to keep him at arm's length."

Stock did not hold back in expressing her concerns. She continued by saying: "This kind of touching isn't 'cool' unless you're a mark or a rat, and no woman should be subjected to full-body contact by a male superior. Get off her, stay off her, and stay off of every woman you work with. Ever heard of a handshake?"

Following the initial backlash and discussion her comments generated, Stock later clarified that her criticism was not directed at just one side of the situation, emphasizing the concern applied to both parties involved. The exchange highlights ongoing discussions within the wrestling community about appropriate workplace conduct and power dynamics between supervisors and employees, especially regarding how such interactions might be perceived by women entering the business.

Tony Khan Identifies The 'MVP' Among AEW's Recent Arrivals

Tony Khan Identifies The 'MVP' Among AEW's Recent Arrivals

Like a kid in a luxurious candy store, AEW President and CEO Tony Khan enjoys the plethora of talent he gets to choose from within his promotion. With some of his most recent signees within the past year up until now, he describes one wrestler as the "Most Valuable Player" among all of his recent arrivals. That distinction belongs to someone all about the toxicity.

In an interview on "The Masked Man Show," Khan made his assessment clear: "Well, I think when you look at the champions on the women's side, I would say absolutely Thekla would be this new crop. You'd have to say, the new MVP of the new arrivals as world champion. She's been fantastic."

Khan went on to explain Thekla's trajectory since arriving at AEW. "Now when she arrived, she had a goal of winning the world title, but at the very first thing she did was go after Jamie Hayter. And Jamie Hayter's been a huge part of AEW, one of our most popular stars, a former world champion. And Jamie Hayter versus Thekla, I think, it's a really compelling, exciting rivalry."

"The Toxic Spider" retained her AEW Women's World Championship against Hayter this past Sunday at Dynasty. Four days later, she defended and retained the title again against Hayter's Brawling Bird mate, Alex Windsor at AEW Collision: Spring BreakThru. Thekla won the women's world championship against Kris Statlander in the company's first-ever women's strap match on the February 11 edition of AEW Dynamite. The gutsy and not so stealthy champion has held five title defenses thus far, including matches against Mina Shirakawa and another former women's world champion in Thunder Rosa.

Former Nexus Member Michael Tarver Looks Back On WWE Debut

The first season of WWE NXT introduced fans to talent like Bryan Danielson and Wade Barrett, who would both go on to have lengthy runs in WWE and capture gold. After winning the show, Barrett later rallied his fellow contestants to form the Nexus. One of the group's members, Michael Tarver, recently looked back at the Raw debut of the Nexus back in 2010.

"It was a crazy night because everything was done in secret," Tarver said during an interview on Rewind Recap Relive. According to Tarver, the faction members were brought in backstage and instructed by John Cena and Vince McMahon to "destroy" Cena in the ring, while being given their signature Nexus armbands. "If it didn't look real, we were fired. So, we said 'Okay,' so we just destroyed everything," Tarver explained (h/t Wrestling Inc.).

Tarver was known for the mask he wore around his mouth, and explained the significance of the attire piece. "I remember I put the mask on for the first time – or the second time, 'cause I did it on an episode of NXT – and I was standing by the guardrail ... and a lady standing next to me noticed me, looked at me, and screamed!" he recalled. "I remember I laughed, I winked at her and then jumped the guard rail," Tarver continued (h/t Wrestling Inc.).

Tarver went on to describe the Nexus debut as surreal, and expressed that while he enjoyed the debut a lot, it all went by so fast that in hindsight it seems like a dream. "I still remember every detail vividly; it was just an amazing night," he recalled. Tarver then claimed that the debut segment paid off, and McMahon was very happy with their performance.
