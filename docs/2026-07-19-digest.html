🔥 AEW Redemption PPV Matches

The Young Bucks successfully challenged Jon Moxley and Will Ospreay to a tag team match at AEW Redemption during the July 18 episode of AEW Collision, and both teams quickly accepted. This blockbuster match announcement came as part of a larger card reveal for the inaugural Redemption pay-per-view, scheduled for July 26 at the Bell Centre in Montreal, Quebec.

The Young Bucks' motivation for issuing the challenge runs deeper than simply testing themselves as the top team in AEW. The Jackson brothers are concerned about protecting Kenny Omega and believe Ospreay cannot be trusted now that he is a member of Jon Moxley's Death Riders faction. With Kenny Omega set to defend the AEW World Championship against Ospreay at the upcoming All In event, the Bucks want to prove that the Death Riders have not changed and that Ospreay's recent friendliness with Omega may be part of a larger trap. Matt and Nick Jackson stated during their challenge that "Mox & the Death Riders haven't changed."

Jon Moxley and Will Ospreay wasted no time accepting the challenge. Moxley expressed his characteristic disdain for all things elite, making the bout personal for him. Despite the young tag team pairing having limited history together as a unit, the star power is considerable, with Moxley and Ospreay forming a true all-star team for the Death Riders. The bout should serve as a strong payoff for their recent tag team matches on television.

Ospreay's actions on the same episode of Collision further illustrated his evolution as a member of the Death Riders. During a warm-up match against his former training partner Adam Brooks, Ospreay initially wrestled as a clear babyface while Brooks took on a heel role. When Brooks spit at Ospreay as he charged for the Hidden Blade, Ospreay responded with cold aggression, delivering a relentless barrage of elbow strikes to Brooks's head until the former ally was forced to tap out. This finish highlighted the Death Riders mentality that appears to be influencing Ospreay's approach, raising questions about whether this aggressive attitude will ultimately consume his soul.

The Young Bucks also made sure to remind current AEW World Tag Team Champions Adam Copeland and Christian Cage that the titles rightfully belong to them, establishing further tension within the tag team division. The tag team division in AEW is positioned for significant expansion, with speculation increasing that the New Day and the Motor City Machine Guns are soon to be All Elite. Additionally, a dream match between the Young Bucks and Adam Copeland and Christian Cage appears to be simmering for the All In event.

The AEW Redemption card has expanded significantly with multiple title defenses and stipulations announced. The updated card includes Kenny Omega defending the AEW World Championship against Kevin Knight, Kyle Fletcher defending the AEW International Championship against Bandido, Mark Davis defending the AEW National Championship against Andrade El Idolo, Christian Cage and Adam Copeland defending the AEW World Tag Team Championship against Pac and Claudio Castagnoli of the Death Riders, Thekla defending the AEW Women's World Championship against Willow Nightingale, and Hikaru Shida defending the AEW TBS Championship against Maya World.

Chris Jericho will face Tommaso Ciampa in a No Holds Barred match, with Jericho adopting his "Painmaker" persona for the bout. The Shida versus World match was set up after Maya World saved Queen Aminata following a brutal attack by Shida on Collision. The Hikaru Shida TBS Championship defense announcement came after this save, establishing the challenger for the title.

According to reports, some plans for the show were kept "close to the vest" until the announcements were made on Dynamite. The TNT Champion receiving a World Championship match around this time of year has been planned for quite some time. Kevin Knight is being considered for an advanced push within the company, which includes not only the World Title shot at Redemption but also potential feuds with other world-level talent. Tony Khan Comments on WWE and AEW

Tony Khan, the AEW President and Jacksonville Jaguars Chief Football Strategy Officer, recently participated in Jeremy Lambert's 24-hour In The Weeds charity stream, where he discussed several wrestling and sports-related topics.

Regarding Bryan Danielson's absence from AEW programming, Khan expressed his admiration for the veteran wrestler while explaining the reasoning behind limiting his appearances. "I think Bryan's incredible at everything he does. Bryan's beat up and I've been giving him time to rest and recover. I would love to have Bryan out there all the time, but he's been home and with his family and he's doing very well," Khan stated. He emphasized that the toll of the wrestling business has heavily impacted Danielson and that taking him off the road allows him to recover properly. Khan noted that even sitting in the commentary chair and moving his head around the monitor is difficult given Danielson's injury history. "People don't realize the toll that this business and this sport has taken on this man. He's done so much for all of us, so, it means the world to have him in AEW and be part of this family but also, that means now he's done everything he can in the ring for AEW at this point and I don't need to drag him out and beat him up every week even more when he's hurting," Khan explained.

On the topic of WWE's customary practice of presenting championship belts to winning sports teams, Khan addressed whether the Jacksonville Jaguars would accept such an honor if they won the Super Bowl. When asked directly, Khan cryptically responded, "They don't have a Jaguars — there's only 31 of those. There's no Jaguars one. You can draw your own conclusions." The implication suggests that WWE has not licensed the Jaguars brand for championship belt customization. It should be noted that the Jacksonville Jaguars have never won a Super Bowl and are one of four NFL franchises that have never even appeared in the championship game, though the team has reached the AFC Championship Game three times in 1996, 1999, and 2017, losing each time.

Khan also discussed Blake Christian's entrance music, revealing that he has only secured one percent of the rights to Jack Harlow's "Lovin' On Me" song for Christian's entrance in ROH and AEW as part of The Swirl gimmick. He expressed hope that Harlow could come in to help secure the remaining 99 percent of the song rights.

Additionally, Khan revealed the inspiration behind AEW Rampage's name, sharing that it came from his personal history with a wrestling video game. "I grew up playing Rampage Wrestling. Hence the name Rampage," Khan explained, connecting his childhood gaming experience directly to the naming of the AEW television program.

MJF Future WWE Potential

D-Von Dudley has weighed in on MJF's potential as a major professional wrestling star. "I think MJF is an excellent wrestler. I think he's one of the best on the mic. Hands down, I think he's got what it takes to be that major superstar," D-Von stated.

However, D-Von's assessment comes with a significant caveat regarding MJF's current situation. While discussing the wrestler's potential to reach Hulk Hogan-level stardom, D-Von questioned whether AEW is presenting MJF in a manner that maximizes his abilities. "I think if Tony really knew how to push him and do the right thing with him, he could be the next Hulk Hogan. What I mean by the next Hulk Hogan is that same fire Hogan had in the '80s, where he became a megastar," he explained.

Eric Bischoff Calls WWE Debut The Most Shocking Moment Of His Career

Eric Bischoff considers his surprise WWE debut the most shocking moment of his wrestling career. Speaking on 83 Weeks, Bischoff reflected on appearing alongside Vince McMahon on WWE television in July 2002. "It's number one, and there's no number two. Working with Steve Austin was the most fun I've ever had. Being the fake minister, probably as a performer, was the most exciting performance I've ever been able to deliver. Bischoff said the agreement came together approximately 10 days before his television debut. "Vince called me. We had a short conversation. I don't think the conversation lasted more than five minutes. I knew halfway through the conversation that he was going to offer me a job, and I'd already made up my mind what I was going to do if he did. He gave me his idea, and I said, 'Great. I'm in. Let's do it.'"

To preserve the surprise, Bischoff made his own travel arrangements to prevent his name from appearing in WWE's system. "I flew myself into New York. I didn't go through WWE travel because I didn't want my name showing up on anybody's computer. It would have ruined the surprise. I booked my own flight. I booked my own hotel, and I sat in my room. I didn't even leave my room. I got there about 9:00 the night before and sat in my room until the limo came to get me."

Bischoff then received an extensive script only hours before the show. "There was a knock on my door, and somebody slipped a big envelope underneath the door. It was basically my script for the night. I get the script, and it's like 15 pages. I'm probably exaggerating, but it was a lot. I already knew the expectation was for me to deliver the script word for word. It wasn't like I could take a look at the script, figure out what worked and put it into my own words. That wasn't going to fly. I had to memorize that script. I didn't have time to be nervous or excited because I had to really memorize it, and I didn't have a lot of time."

Despite the initial shock and excitement of his debut, Bischoff's time in WWE proved challenging. He says he struggled to develop a productive working relationship with Vince McMahon during his brief four-month stint. Bischoff said their personalities and working styles were never going to produce the necessary chemistry. "Vince was unique, and we can criticize him. I certainly didn't enjoy working for him. I couldn't make it work. The chemistry was just never going to be there. Other people have made it work and have done extremely well."

One of Bischoff's greatest difficulties was figuring out how McMahon thought and what he expected. "One of the hardest parts of that four-month experiment for me was trying to get a read on Vince McMahon. It was not a pleasant experience in any way, shape or form."

Despite their difficulties, Bischoff credited McMahon with having a strong eye for wrestling talent. "As unique as Vince was and as difficult as he was in many respects, he still had a good eye. He wasn't right all the time. Nobody's right all the time. If he saw something that he liked in somebody, he would push it and push it and push it until it worked or until, after giving it sufficient time, it didn't work."

Bischoff said McMahon was also capable of making extremely fast decisions about whether a wrestler possessed the qualities he wanted. "He would also make very quick judgments. WWE Interim Women's Champion at SummerSlam

With WWE Women's Champion Rhea Ripley sidelined with a knee injury, WWE announced Friday that a ladder match to crown an interim champion will take place at SummerSlam. The decision follows a practice long used in MMA, which took its lead from boxing. In the 1990s, the World Boxing Association introduced the concept to keep championships active when a champion couldn't defend because of injury or illness. When the champion returned, the interim titleholder would become the mandatory challenger. Immediately, that created a built-in marquee match to determine the true champion.

Historically in pro wrestling, injured champions were often stripped of their titles, and a new champion was crowned. That tradition was eventually challenged by AEW, which popularized the concept of interim champions, leading to a fiery storyline in 2022. After World Champion CM Punk was injured, Jon Moxley defeated Hiroshi Tanahashi at that year's Forbidden Door to win the Interim AEW World Championship. Moxley, though, took exception to the label and declared himself the true champion, setting up a compelling feud when Punk returned.

However, by choosing interim titles rather than stripping the champion and starting anew, pro wrestling loses a powerful device: sympathy. In 2019, Tommaso Ciampa was one of wrestling's most hated villains. But after a neck injury forced him to relinquish the NXT Championship, WWE chronicled his surgery and recovery, earning him fans' sympathy. By the time he returned, fans had rallied behind his quest to win back the title.

Whoever leaves SummerSlam as interim Women's Champion, WWE faces a creative challenge. Instead of telling the story of a popular champion fighting to regain what was taken from her, the company must make the eventual champion versus interim champion showdown feel like the biggest match possible upon Ripley's return. At the very least, it'll be intriguing to see how WWE responds to that challenge.
