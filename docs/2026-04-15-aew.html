🔥 AEW Dynasty Event and Coverage

All Elite Wrestling returned to Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada, on Sunday, April 12th, for the third consecutive year of its flagship Dynasty event. Held at Rogers Arena, the show featured an impressive card with multiple championship matches and dream matchups that delivered compelling storylines and emotional moments.

Kyle O'Reilly made his much-awaited return following a significant neck injury, competing in the Trios Championship match. Teaming alongside Roderick Strong and Orange Cassidy, O'Reilly faced Clark Connors, Gabe Kidd, and David Finlay of The Dogs of War. O'Reilly secured the victory for his team by trapping Clark Connors in an ankle lock, forcing a tap-out and capturing the AEW World Trios Championship. This marked O'Reilly's first singles championship win in AEW during his tenure with the company.

The championship victory carried significant emotional weight for O'Reilly. In backstage footage released by AEW following his win, O'Reilly opened up about the personal circumstances surrounding his return. He reflected on his history with Rogers Arena, explaining that his father had brought him to a show there years earlier, which sparked his dream of wrestling professionally. However, O'Reilly's return came during an incredibly difficult time in his life, as his father had passed away approximately two weeks before the Dynasty event. O'Reilly expressed the emotional toll of returning to the ring and winning a championship under such circumstances.

"My dad brought me here to a show, and since that moment, I knew this is something that I had to do. So now I'm here. I'm in Vancouver at Rogers Arena, and I'm about to make my return, and it's been an emotional roller coaster the last month or so. And my father passed away about 2 weeks ago. So, the weight of that on my shoulders, the weight of coming back to wrestle, I'm feeling it all, man. Nothing in life is guaranteed," O'Reilly stated before his match.

After winning the championship, O'Reilly expressed that he wished his father could have witnessed the moment. He thanked AEW President Tony Khan for the opportunity and reflected on what his partnership with Strong and Cassidy had taught him about perseverance and friendship. O'Reilly explained that while he is sometimes willing to give up on himself, having his friends depend on him provided motivation to refuse defeat and achieve their collective dream.

Roderick Strong, one of O'Reilly's teammates, offered emotional support to his partner following their victory. Strong praised O'Reilly as the strongest and most thoughtful person he had ever met, noting that O'Reilly's parents were proud and had been watching him compete that evening. With The Dogs of War now suffering injuries, particularly Gabe Kidd, there are currently no announced plans for O'Reilly's first title defense.

Prior to Dynasty, O'Reilly had discussed his wrestling future in an interview on This is VANCOLOUR. The 39-year-old veteran, who will turn 40 next year, expressed a desire to continue wrestling into his 50s. O'Reilly emphasized that modern wrestlers approach their profession with the mindset of elite athletes, incorporating dedicated strength and conditioning coaching and rehabilitation programs that extend careers beyond what was possible in previous eras. He acknowledged the physical and mental toll of professional wrestling, including the challenges of constant travel and time away from family. As a Type 1 diabetic, O'Reilly noted that managing his condition while wrestling adds significant stress to his body. Despite these challenges, O'Reilly remains inspired by older generations of wrestlers who continue to perform at high levels.

"It's hard to say. I think the most important things is that wrestlers, we look at ourselves as professional athletes now. It wasn't always like that in a bygone era, and so, we put a lot more into our recovery, into our rehab. I work with a strength and conditioning coach now who does all my programming and all my rehab," O'Reilly explained.

O'Reilly specifically praised Adam Copeland, Christian Cage, and Chris Jericho for their continued success wrestling at their advanced ages. He noted that he has been in the professional wrestling business since 2005, marking 21 years in the industry. While expressing his desire to wrestle into his 50s, O'Reilly acknowledged that his involvement with professional wrestling could extend beyond in-ring competition, including possibilities such as producing, coaching, or opening a wrestling school.

The main event of Dynasty 2026 saw MJF put his AEW World Championship on the line against Kenny Omega in a dream match pairing one of the best young guns in professional wrestling with one of the greatest wrestlers of the current generation. The match exceeded expectations, stretching beyond 40 minutes and delivering an exceptional wrestling performance. MJF successfully retained his championship in the contest.

The opening match of the broadcast featured The Young Bucks facing The Don Callis Family's team of Konosuke Takeshita and Kazuchika Okada. The rivalry between these teams had been building on AEW Dynamite, particularly following The Young Bucks' previous loss to FTR for the AEW World Tag Team Championships. The nearly 20-minute match displayed excellent technical wrestling from all four competitors.

The Young Bucks secured victory by executing their signature Meltzer Driver finisher on Okada, pinning him to win the match. However, the most significant development came at the match's conclusion. As Takeshita appeared positioned to prevent the finisher by grabbing the foot of one of the Bucks, he instead released his grip and backed away to the entranceway. Takeshita's decision to allow his partner Okada to be defeated suggests an internal conflict within The Don Callis Family. The implication is that either Okada or Takeshita will break away from the group, potentially setting up a future program between the two Japanese wrestlers.

The TNT Championship was contested in a 10-man Casino Gauntlet match due to the title being vacated. The previous champion, Kyle Fletcher, was forced to relinquish the belt following a leg injury sustained during his tag team match with Mark Davis against The Rascalz. Medical concerns indicated Fletcher may have suffered a meniscus injury that could sideline him for an extended period.

The Casino Gauntlet match saw ten competitors participate, including Tommaso Ciampa, RUSH, Bandido, Speedball Mike Bailey, El Clon, PAC, Anthony Bowens, Kevin Knight, and members of the Death Riders stable. The 20-minute match provided an opportunity for multiple wrestlers to stake their claim to the vacant championship. Kevin Knight emerged as the unexpected winner, pinning Daniel Garcia of the Death Riders to claim the vacant TNT Championship. This marked Knight's first singles championship victory in AEW, though he had previously held the Trios Championship alongside Speedball Mike Bailey and Hangman Adam Page. Knight's speed and technical proficiency in the ring suggest he could have competitive matches against any opponent on the AEW roster.

Additional championship matches on the Dynasty card included Jon Moxley defending his AEW Continental Championship against Will Ospreay, FTR putting their AEW World Tag Team Championships on the line against Cope and Cage, and Darby Allin challenging Andrade with a shot at the AEW World Championship on the line. Ricochet also competed against Chris Jericho, and The Young Bucks battled The Don Callis Family in the opening match discussed above.

The Bryan and Vinny Show featured coverage of AEW Dynasty with special guest referee Aubrey Edwards returning to discuss the event. The program included reviews of Dynasty, wrestling reports, Q&A segments, and a trivia contest among the hosts and guests.

🔥 Ricochet AEW Career

Ricochet's AEW Journey Marks a Complete Career Transformation

Ricochet's run in All Elite Wrestling has fundamentally redefined his career trajectory and established him as one of professional wrestling's most dynamic performers today. Since his debut at AEW All In Wembley in 2024, Ricochet has transcended the stigma of being merely a highlight-reel athlete, evolving into one of the most complete performers in the business. His earlier stops in NJPW and WWE showcased his exceptional athleticism, but the AEW platform has allowed him to showcase confidence, presence, and purpose that was previously untapped.

The transformation became most apparent through his intense rivalry with Swerve Strickland. That specific program pushed Ricochet to another level, bringing out dimensions of his character that many believed had always existed beneath the surface. On the January 1, 2025, episode of Fight for the Fallen, Ricochet viciously attacked and bloodied Strickland with a pair of scissors, cementing his transition to a heel character. To further cement this turn, on the February 5 episode of Dynamite, Ricochet defeated Strickland and proceeded to steal the Embassy robe that had been bestowed upon Strickland by Prince Nana in honor of Embassy member Jimmy Rave. These feuds provided Ricochet with crucial opportunities to develop his microphone skills and character work.

For years, Ricochet carried the stigma that he could not cut a promo effectively. Whether it was brought up directly in feuds or from critics online, that label was consistently attached to his name. In AEW, however, that narrative has completely changed. He has found a voice that genuinely connects with audiences. His delivery is sharper, more confident, and far more natural than ever before. The contrast with his time in WWE is striking, where his character often felt restricted and carefully managed, as if there were constant limitations placed upon his creative expression. In AEW, Ricochet has the freedom to define himself without those constraints.

As a heel character, the crowd reactions speak volumes about the quality of his work. He is no longer boxed into one dimension, and his character is no longer defined by executing high-flying offense. The evolution is happening in real time, and the results are undeniable. His emergence as one of the top heels in professional wrestling represents validation of his ability to embrace the role while adding multiple layers to his character while maintaining the dynamic style that made him special in the first place.

The crowds clearly recognize what Ricochet has become. Whether it's hundreds of rolls of toilet paper being thrown into the ring, chants of "BALD!", or the deafening "shut the f*ck up!" from the audience, it is obvious that his work is resonating with fans in a significant way. He is not just respected but has become a focal point of AEW programming.

Ricochet's rise reached another significant level when he became the inaugural AEW National Champion. The moment served as both recognition and validation that he is not just thriving but is essential to the company's future. This championship confirmed that Tony Khan and his peers regard Ricochet as a top-tier talent worthy of championship honor. Most importantly, this run feels like a genuine revival. The move to AEW has not only elevated his career but appears to have restored his passion for professional wrestling. There is clear energy in everything he does, a sense that he is fully invested in his craft again. Ricochet is no longer defined by what he was in previous promotions. In AEW, he has become what many believed he could be all along.

WrestleCon Match Cancellation Sparks Speculation

Leon Slater was set to face Ricochet at the WrestleCon Mark Hitchcock Memorial Supershow during WrestleMania weekend, representing what would have been a dream encounter between two rising stars. Both wrestlers are experiencing remarkable runs, with Ricochet being a former AEW National Champion and Slater being the current TNA X-Division Champion. However, days before the match was scheduled to take place, TNA decided to pull out and cancel all of their TNA talents versus AEW wrestler matches.

WrestleCon promoter Michael Bochicchio has since clarified the situation, pushing back against widespread fan speculation about the match's cancellation. When speaking with wrestling journalists, Bochicchio explained that both promotions had already approved the finish of the match, contrary to what many fans believed online. He emphasized that the match cancellation had nothing to do with Slater being booked to lose.

"We went directly to Leon's boss. We went directly to Ricochet's boss. We told them what the finish of the match would be to protect the individual. A lot of people think, 'They didn't want Leon to lose.' Guys, I'm going to be honest, Leon wasn't losing. Come up with a different theory on why they pulled him because that had nothing to do with it. The match and finish were cleared," Bochicchio stated.

The way Bochicchio learned about the cancellation caught him off guard. Rather than receiving direct communication from TNA leadership, he first saw news of the cancellation online before getting a message approximately two hours before their pay-per-view aired. The communication was brief and came from TNA's leadership asking to have a conversation, but that conversation never materialized.

"The first communication I got from TNA was about two hours before their PPV on Saturday. Mr. Silva sent me a text saying he would like to communicate. That was after we changed and replaced the match. I still haven't spoken to him. I said, 'Sure, call me anytime.' I haven't heard from him," Bochicchio explained.

Regarding the actual reason for the cancellation, there has been substantial online speculation that WWE played a role in TNA's decision to withdraw from the match. However, Bochicchio dismissed this theory. Instead, he offered his own perspective on what may have prompted the sudden change of plans. He believes the situation may have stemmed from business tensions between TNA and AEW related to AEW programming decisions.

"I believe Carlos got upset AEW ran on a Thursday, hurt their ratings, and everything changed. That's my opinion. Maybe WWE has more involvement than we ever thought, but Carlos can say to WWE, 'Guys, I gave my word on this match. I would like to be able to follow through with my word.'" Bochicchio stated.

With Slater versus Ricochet removed from the card, WrestleCon quickly announced a new main event featuring The Demand taking on Mike Bailey, Kevin Knight, and Michael Oku. Despite the quick replacement, many fans remain puzzled about what truly caused the original dream match to fall apart at such a late stage in the promotional cycle.

🔥 Tony Khan AEW Creative Philosophy

AEW President Tony Khan is making a major change to how he approaches creative direction for the company. During an interview with KING 5 News ahead of the April 15 episode of AEW Dynamite in Everett, Washington, Khan outlined his new creative philosophy and reflected on lessons learned since AEW's launch in 2019.

Khan made clear that AEW has experienced uneven stretches creatively, and he's identified the root cause: too many voices influencing decisions behind the scenes. Rather than continuing to juggle multiple creative inputs and collaborative approaches, Khan is centralizing creative authority under his own vision. "From now on… I will only trust my instincts. I will only execute the ideas that I believe in," Khan stated during the interview.

This represents a significant acknowledgment from Khan about past challenges at AEW. The company has faced ongoing speculation and discussion about creative direction and consistency throughout its history. By taking full ownership of the product rather than spreading decisions across various contributors, Khan is positioning himself as the final creative voice at the company.

Khan's reflection on his approach includes personal context about his lifelong passion for wrestling and television production. He revealed that his interest in creating wrestling programming began decades earlier, when he started writing a show called Dynamite on message boards using dial-up internet when he was just 12 years old in 1995, which he called "Saturday Night Dynamite." Over three decades later, that childhood passion has evolved into running one of the world's largest mainstream wrestling promotions.

The shift toward Khan's instinct-driven approach comes after months of discussion about talent input and creative influence within AEW. Khan has previously made clear he would never intentionally put on a bad pay-per-view, but this new statement shows he's focusing on the broader creative structure that shapes the entire product. With three championship matches scheduled for Spring Break Dynamite, Khan credits the company's current creative momentum to learning from mistakes and applying those lessons moving forward.

🔥 Swerve Strickland AEW Absence

Swerve Strickland's recent absence from AEW television has raised some eyebrows, and now there's clarity on what's been going on behind the scenes. The former AEW World Champion has been off AEW programming for several weeks, with his last appearance coming in late March on an episode of AEW Dynamite where he competed against Kenny Omega but came up short in the bout. The loss to Omega carried major implications, as it snapped a three-match winning streak for Strickland and gave Omega a title shot against reigning champion MJF at Dynasty, which Omega ultimately lost.

Strickland's absence was particularly noticeable at the AEW Dynasty pay-per-view on April 12 in Vancouver, where he was not scheduled to appear. Instead of competing or appearing on the card, Strickland was booked for a signing appearance on the day of the event, further adding to questions about his status with the company. Since returning from knee surgery last December, Strickland had been fairly active in AEW programming until the Omega loss interrupted his momentum.

Now, more details have surfaced regarding his time away from in-ring competition. According to multiple reports, Strickland recently spent time filming promotional material for Warner Bros. Discovery. However, there's still no word on exactly what the project entails or when it may be released. The Warner Bros. Discovery connection is particularly notable given AEW's relationship with the media company, as the promotion's flagship programming airs on TBS and TNT.

For now, Strickland's in-ring return timeline remains unclear. However, there is speculation that he could return to AEW television in the near future. With Dynamite emanating from Everett, Washington, it's very possible Strickland returns for that episode, as he was born in Tacoma and was a big part of the DEFY Wrestling promotion for years alongside Darby Allin, who will challenge MJF for the title.

Over the past two years, Strickland has established himself as one of AEW's top stars, including a run as AEW World Champion in 2024 before dropping the title to MJF. Updates regarding his status and return will be provided as more information becomes available.

Willow Nightingale Comments

Willow Nightingale is giving credit where she feels it's due when it comes to breaking stereotypes in wrestling. The reigning AEW TBS Champion recently appeared on Marking Out with MVP & Dwayne Swayze alongside her Babes of Wrath tag team partner Harley Cameron, where the duo reflected on representation and character work in the industry.

She continued by emphasizing how their presentation helped shift fan perception and open doors for others to follow a similar path. "Black joy and being carefree and whimsical," she continued. "I think they helped people accept that and be like, 'Yeah! That is cool. Right on' and can kind of make way for me to also be like, that is what I have been dishing out."

Willow Nightingale is scheduled to put her AEW TBS Championship on the line against Kamille at AEW Dynamite: Spring BreakThru, which kicks off this Wednesday night.

AEW Redemption Trademark

AEW has filed a trademark with the United States Patent and Trademark Office for the term "AEW Redemption." The filing was made with the following purposes listed: Entertainment in the nature of wrestling contests; Entertainment services in the nature of live professional wrestling performances; Entertainment services in the nature of production of professional wrestling programs and events; and Entertainment services, namely, production and distribution of ongoing television programs in the field of professional wrestling.

There has been no official announcement as of this writing as to what AEW Redemption will be, but it appears to be an event name of some form. The trademark could represent a new pay-per-view, potentially a title for a special episode of Dynamite and/or Collision, or even a new weekly show altogether. AEW has yet to provide clarity on which direction the company will take with this new branding.

AEW has a number of special titled editions of Dynamite and Collision throughout the year, such as Spring Breakthru and Winter Is Coming. However, there is a noticeable concern with that approach that wrestling observers have flagged. If there are too many special episodes, they are not special anymore as they just become the norm, and the importance of holding these uniquely titled shows wears off and loses its effect on audiences and viewership.

As noted, there is no confirmation yet as to exactly what form an AEW Redemption show will take, but even if this does not add to the list of special Dynamites and Collisions, it remains a point worth keeping in mind going forward for the promotion. AEW will be hosting the Spring Breakthru editions of Dynamite and Collision coming off the Dynasty pay-per-view event, with MJF set to defend his World Championship against Darby Allin. Additional updates on AEW Redemption will be provided as more information emerges.
