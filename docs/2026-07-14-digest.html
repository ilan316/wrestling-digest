🔥 Will Ospreay AEW All In and Title Plans

Will Ospreay's journey from New Japan Pro Wrestling to All Elite Wrestling represents both a professional evolution and a personal transition. The English wrestler spent nearly a decade establishing himself as a major star in New Japan, but his departure in 2024 was motivated by more than just the opportunity to join AEW. During an appearance on Marking out with MVP & Dwayne Swayze, Ospreay revealed the behind-the-scenes frustrations that contributed to his decision.

"I kind of knew that 2023 was gonna be my last year," Ospreay explained. "There was some things that were happening behind the scenes. I was just over the office. I loved the boys and I loved the fans." Beyond the office politics, Ospreay's personal life played a significant role in shaping his career decision. "I got a new girlfriend," he said. "I became a dad to her kid. So it was just one of these things where I wanted to be present and I wanted to be there more so I knew I needed to go to my next stage of my career."

What made AEW an ideal destination for Ospreay was the promotion's unique opportunity to maintain his connections with Japan while competing in the Western wrestling landscape. "I didn't wanna cut my ties with Japan," he said. "So that's why AEW was amazing, right?" This hybrid arrangement proved valuable, as Ospreay has continued to work in New Japan since joining AEW. He initially said goodbye to the promotion at New Beginning in Osaka in February 2024, when United Empire lost a steel cage match to Bullet Club War Dogs. However, he returned this year, wrestling a trio of trios matches alongside United Empire stablemates HENARE and Great-O-Khan. The group captured the NEVER Openweight Six Man Tag Team Championships from Hirooki Goto, Oleg Boltin, and YOSHI-HASHI at Wrestling Dontaku 2026 in Fukuoka, Japan, in May.

Now, Ospreay stands on the precipice of perhaps the most meaningful moment of his career. With an AEW World Championship opportunity against Kenny Omega scheduled for August 30 at Wembley Stadium during AEW All In: London 2026, the stakes have never been higher. The main event match represents a chance for Ospreay to etch his name into wrestling history as the first Englishman to win a world championship on home soil.

Reflecting on his journey to this moment, Ospreay recalled the foundational support from his parents. "So I tell people the story of when I was a 14-year-old kid, when my mum and dad bought me a wrestling ring to put in my back garden. Mainly because I was a little troublemaker, at that age where I grew up, it was a natural thing to find yourself in trouble." His parents recognized that channeling his energy into his wrestling passion was the best path forward. "My mum and dad thought that the best way to keep me off the streets and keep me home was to keep my passion burning alive, and put a wrestling ring in our back garden. I always remember in school, every boy wanted to be a football player. But I just wanted to be a professional wrestler, and people laughed at me."

The significance of competing at Wembley Stadium carries deep emotional weight for Ospreay. "Now, being the age that I'm at now, where I've wrestled in front of 40,000 people in the Tokyo Dome, nearly 80,000 people in Wembley Stadium, I've got to dip my toes into so many amazing moments in wrestling. Now I get to return to Wembley Stadium. I'm fighting for a piece of history that no one else in the world can say that they've done. Being the first Englishman to win a World Championship on home soil is such a huge thing that I could never even dream of."

Ospreay expressed the difficulty in articulating what this opportunity means to him and his place in the sport. "It's a feeling that I struggle to put into words, of how special it feels to be in this position and fighting for that prize. I am grateful, man. I'm so grateful. To be able to influence a new generation coming through and to give hope to anyone who was ever told that they're not going to be able to fulfil their dreams because of where they're from." He also hopes his story serves as inspiration for others pursuing seemingly impossible dreams. "I wasn't an academic kid; I just had a belief in my stomach that pro wrestling was going to pay off one day. And next month, I'll be in Wembley Stadium fighting for the most important prize in pro wrestling. I hope I can just walk away as the first Englishman to ever do it."

Regarding the pageantry surrounding his entrance, Ospreay has plans for future Assassin's Creed-inspired presentations but has decided against overloading the Wembley event with excessive spectacle. "Maybe not next month at Wembley, just because we had two real stellar entrances on both All In shows in 2023 and 2024, I don't want to oversaturate it. If we were to do it again, some strings will need to be pulled, and I do have something in my back pocket related to Black Flag, but I'm saving that for further down the line." This measured approach shows Ospreay's understanding of storytelling and long-term planning.

However, not everyone within AEW circles is necessarily rooting for an Ospreay victory. Jim Ross, speaking on his Grilling JR podcast, expressed strong praise for Kenny Omega's in-ring abilities and championship win. "He believes in his own abilities, and I don't know anybody in the wrestling business, on any side of the fence, that is a better in-ring performer than Kenny Omega, I really don't. There's some good ones, no doubt, but I don't know if there's anyone in the business today that performs at a higher level consistently than Kenny Omega."

Ross extended his perspective to the upcoming All In match, revealing his preference for how the championship dynamics should unfold. "Well, I hope it's not a passing of the torch with Ospreay beating Omega. It's a little early for that. For me, I'd like to see Kenny get a fair, long, productive run. But, I don't know what Tony Khan's plans are." Ross's comments acknowledge the widespread expectation among fans that Ospreay will defeat Omega at the Wembley event, yet he voiced his personal hope that the champion receives an extended reign rather than a quick loss to the challenger.

The tension between fan expectations, internal company perspectives, and Ospreay's historical opportunity creates intrigue heading into the match. Whether AEW's booking will align with the narrative of Ospreay making history at home or whether Omega's championship run will be protected remains to be seen. The match itself promises to be technically excellent given both competitors' abilities, regardless of the outcome.

🔥 AEW Full Gear 2026 Announcement

AEW has officially announced the location and date for its Full Gear 2026 pay-per-view, one of the promotion's flagship annual events. The show will take place on Saturday, November 14, at the Mortgage Matchup Center in downtown Phoenix, Arizona, a 17,000-seat arena that is home to the NBA's Phoenix Suns and WNBA's Phoenix Mercury. This marks AEW's return to Arizona after hosting Double or Nothing 2025 at Desert Diamond Arena in nearby Glendale earlier in 2025.

The announcement came on Monday, July 13, continuing AEW's recent pattern of confirming its major 2026 pay-per-view schedule. Just the previous Monday, the company had announced that All Out would return to Chicago in September at the NOW Arena in Hoffman Estates, Illinois. This Full Gear announcement represents another significant piece of the company's fall calendar.

AEW President Tony Khan expressed enthusiasm about returning to Arizona for Full Gear, particularly given the success of the previous year's event in the state. Khan told Arizona Central that Phoenix is an ideal destination for one of the company's biggest annual events. "Phoenix is a fantastic sports town. For the big matchups, it's a town that creates that big-fight feel, and Full Gear is one of those events. There will be a lot of excitement around this show," Khan said.

Khan also credited the momentum built by Double or Nothing 2025, which received widespread critical acclaim. "It was voted the best wrestling show of the year, 2025. Double or Nothing 2025 was voted the best wrestling show of the year worldwide by Sports Illustrated, and a number of the top publications picked Double or Nothing in Arizona as the best wrestling event of the year last year, and AEW is even better in 2026. We're having an even bigger and better year in 2026. So it's perfect that we're coming back," Khan stated.

Double or Nothing 2025 featured several notable matches, including "Hangman" Adam Page defeating Will Ospreay to win the 2025 Men's Owen Hart Cup tournament, along with Mercedes Mone defeating Jamie Hayter to win the Women's Owen Hart Cup tournament. The success of that event has apparently reinvigorated AEW's interest in returning to the Arizona market for another major offering.

Phoenix is not a regular pay-per-view destination for AEW, though the company has a history of running television tapings in the city. AEW has conducted television tapings in Phoenix on four occasions, including as recently as January 2026 when the company ran special episodes of Dynamite and Collision called Maximum Carnage, featuring MJF defending the AEW Men's World Championship against Bandido. Additionally, AEW held an episode of Collision in nearby Tucson on March 7. More recent AEW shows in Phoenix have taken place at the smaller Arizona Financial Arena, which holds 5,000 fans. The move to Mortgage Matchup Center for Full Gear represents a significant step up in venue size for the promotion's shows in the Phoenix area.

Full Gear has been an annual fixture on the AEW calendar since the promotion's inaugural year of 2019. For the past three years, the event has taken place in Newark, New Jersey, making the move to Phoenix a notable change in venue strategy. The shift to downtown Phoenix from Double or Nothing's Glendale location shows AEW's commitment to utilizing different Arizona venues and locations.

Ticket information for Full Gear 2026 is now available for fans interested in attending. AEW will offer early access to Full Gear tickets beginning Friday, July 20, with a VIP pre-sale starting July 21. The regular pre-sale runs from July 23 through July 26. General public tickets will go on sale on Monday, July 27, at 12 p.m. Eastern time and 10 a.m. Mountain time through AEWTIX.com.

With this announcement, AEW now has four major pay-per-views confirmed for 2026. Redemption takes place on Sunday, July 26, at Bell Centre in Montreal, Quebec, Canada. All In is scheduled for Sunday, August 30, at Wembley Stadium in London, England. All Out will be held on Saturday, September 26, at NOW Arena in Hoffman Estates, Illinois. Full Gear rounds out the confirmed schedule on Saturday, November 14, in Phoenix. The company has yet to reveal the locations for other potential 2026 pay-per-views such as WrestleDream and Worlds End.

Following Omega's championship victory, multiple competitors have positioned themselves as potential next challengers for the top prize in All Elite Wrestling. Swerve might have what it takes to dethrone Omega and finally achieve the power he desires by becoming a two-time AEW World Champion.

Kyle Fletcher also represents a viable option, though the details regarding his specific positioning were not fully included in available information.

The televised nature of Omega's title victory sparked broader discussion within the wrestling community. The match drew enormous viewership, representing one of the largest audiences for AEW programming in recent years. Observers noted that the title change occurred within a remarkably short window of major televised World Title changes across wrestling's landscape. Depending on how one defines major promotions, the last comparable scenario occurred in January of this year when Mike Santana defeated Frankie Kazarian to become the TNA World Champion on January 15, followed by Drew McIntyre defeating Cody Rhodes for the Undisputed WWE Championship six days earlier. If TNA is not considered a major promotion, the comparison must stretch back to January of 1999, when Mick Foley defeated The Rock for the WWF Championship on Raw followed by the infamous Fingerpoke of Doom on Nitro where Hulk Hogan defeated Kevin Nash for the WCW World Title.

Discussion emerged regarding the decision to place World Title matches on television rather than saving them exclusively for pay-per-view events. The traditional thinking suggests that major matches should be reserved for premium shows to encourage buys, but the modern wrestling landscape operates differently. Television rights deals now represent enormous corporate investments, and when major entities invest significant resources into programming, their needs and desires become paramount. This means more big matches like World Title bouts may appear on television if networks and companies deem it beneficial. These matches do not occur on a weekly basis, and pay-per-views continue to feature plenty of premium worthy matches, so the company's overall quality remains strong.

Notably, both the WWE and AEW World Title changes on television featured substantial controversy. The matches themselves and the finishes generated discussion and debate among fans and analysts, raising questions about whether both promotions made the correct call by featuring these matches on television and allowing the titles to change hands.

🔥 CM Punk WWE Championship Victory

CM PUNK RETURNS TO CAPTURE UNDISPUTED WWE CHAMPIONSHIP IN EMOTIONAL HOMECOMING

CM Punk made his triumphant return to WWE television for the first time since WrestleMania 42, defeating Sami Zayn to win the Undisputed WWE Championship on Raw in his hometown of Chicago. The victory marked a remarkable coincidence, occurring exactly 15 years after Punk's legendary Money in the Bank 2011 main event win against John Cena at the same venue. In behind-the-scenes footage captured by WWE, Punk reflected on the emotional significance of the moment and his mindset heading into the match.

Despite being 47 years old, Punk revealed that he still experiences nerves before major matches, but views that as a positive indicator. He stated: "I have lived these once-in-a-lifetime moments multiple times, and it's not lost on me how special that is. To me, like I live for these moments. You can't be the best you can be unless you experience those situations, the high-pressure stuff. I want the puck on my stick, game seven, overtime. I want the ball in my hand. I want to be the one that's counted on to run plays. I am not scared of failing very publicly, and in the biggest of ways. Those are moments where, man, fortune favors the bold, and I am fortunate."

Punk emphasized his deep love for professional wrestling as a business, acknowledging that he has been accused at various points in his career of taking it either too seriously or not seriously enough. He expressed gratitude for his lengthy career, which has allowed him to look back on the many significant moments he has created while still having the opportunity to make new ones. Immediately after his victory, Punk noted that he felt alive and that these moments are what he lives for.

The challenger initially replacing Cody Rhodes came after Rhodes was taken out by Gunther earlier in the show and deemed unable to compete. Zayn was visibly upset following his loss, having held the championship for only nine days. The former champion confronted a camera immediately after the match, expressing his frustration about how Punk could return after months away and immediately reclaim the title after Zayn had spent 24 years working his way up to achieve the championship.

In response to criticism that his return and immediate title win contradicted his previous comments about returning veterans taking opportunities from younger stars, Punk addressed the irony directly during his appearance on Stephanie McMahon's podcast. When McMahon noted that some talent on social media had pointed out this perceived hypocrisy, Punk acknowledged the comparison but stressed that there was nothing controversial about the outcome. He stated: "I won. Fair and square, clean, fair and square." He added that anyone reviewing the match would see that he defeated Zayn cleanly in the middle of the ring. Rather than distancing himself from the criticism, Punk laughed about the situation and acknowledged there was some truth to the claims, while maintaining that his championship victory was earned fairly.

During his podcast appearance, Punk humorously reiterated his clean victory, saying: "Oh, I won the title. I beat that 41-year-old youngster. Fair and square in the middle of the ring." He expressed amazement at how the moment unfolded exactly as planned, noting the remarkable timing of repeating his 2011 victory 15 years later. "I'm flabbergasted at that. Just how we drew it up, just how we planned it. History doesn't repeat itself, but it sure does rhyme."

PUNK MOTIVATED BY PEERS' RETIREMENTS AND FOCUSED ON LEGACY

Punk discussed his motivation for continuing to compete at the highest level, particularly in light of recent retirements from his contemporaries. John Cena and AJ Styles have both recently retired from in-ring competition, and their departures have only strengthened Punk's resolve to remain active. He explained: "We watched Cena retire. We watched AJ Styles retire. It's almost, I feel like, everybody's now looking at me, like, 'Oh, when are you going to retire?' But, guys like me and Sami, who came up the same way. We started out in the indies. Were told 'No,' said, 'Watch this.' Were told, 'You'll never make it here.' Made it here... Then told, 'You're never going to be champion.' Became champion. That says a lot, not just for me, but for him."

Punk expressed deep respect for Zayn, drawing parallels between their journeys and noting the kinship they share as wrestlers who came up through the independent wrestling ranks. He suggested that wrestlers like himself and Zayn understand the struggle in a way that resonates deeply. "Every situation's different, and history doesn't repeat itself, but it sure does rhyme. It's a testament to a guy like Sami Zayn."

The champion stated that watching his contemporaries retire has kept him motivated rather than making him consider stepping away. He emphasized that he feels he is operating at a high level and has never been in better shape or condition. "I feel like I'm operating on that high level. I feel like I've never been better. Why would I step away?" He took time away after losing the World Heavyweight Championship to Roman Reigns at WrestleMania, during which he spent quality time with his wife. He credited his ability to return and immediately connect with fans to the currency he has built with the audience over his career.

Punk also took the opportunity to reflect on his mindset and the value he places on wrestling. "This is a special business to me. And sometimes I've been accused of taking it too seriously, and sometimes I've been accused of not taking it seriously enough. But my career has been so long that we can now look back on all these moments. And I get to cherish that much more, the ability to still be here at the highest level in front of the best fans in the world to create more moments."

PUNK CRITIQUES SOCIAL MEDIA RELIANCE AND EMPHASIZES LIVE AUDIENCE FEEDBACK

One of Punk's primary concerns regarding the current state of WWE involves the heavy reliance wrestlers place on social media feedback rather than trusting the immediate reaction of live audiences. During his podcast appearance, he voiced his controversial opinion about what needs to change in the company. He stated: "I would like more feedback given to people coming through the curtain instead of just, 'Yep, it's fine.' Some people chase the hug. And they come back and they're just like, 'how was it?' They want to be told, you know?"

Punk then emphasized the critical distinction between social media reaction and live crowd response: "But I always say, 'well, get off your fucking phone.' You're looking at Twitter to see how your fucking match was. Did you listen to the people? Because that's your instant reaction. That's your boss when you're out there. I don't give a shit what somebody on Twitter says about your match."

The WWE Champion explained that there is often a significant disconnect between the social media response and the live audience reaction. Wrestlers can receive strong positive reactions from the crowd in the arena, only to check their phones and discover negative commentary online. Punk stressed that the live reaction should be the primary measure of success for any performer.

Regarding his own relationship with social media, Punk revealed that he abandoned Twitter years ago and does not have a TikTok account. While acknowledging that he spends perhaps too much time on his phone, he primarily uses Instagram to look at pictures of dogs and food. This personal distance from social media commentary has allowed him to avoid the trap of second-guessing performances based on online opinions.

When asked whether the feedback problem stems more from talent or those behind the scenes, Punk identified both as having responsibility. He explained that producers and agents sometimes avoid giving constructive criticism because they do not want to hurt people's feelings, but there needs to be a balance. "I think it can be both in some situations. I think sometimes some people don't want to hurt people's feelings. So I think they need to figure out a way to communicate things in a positive way."

Punk emphasized the importance of earnest desire to improve. He drew on his own experience as a young wrestler, recalling how he worked tirelessly to develop his craft. "I was a 15-year-old kid working at a comic shop that made a ball of tape and hung it from the ceiling and sat there and punched it 10,000 times a day." He wants producers and agents to focus on helping talent improve through constructive, positive feedback and demonstration rather than simply pointing out flaws. "I want the producers or agents to really focus on helping and giving constructive, positive feedback and showing things instead of just saying things."

Punk also stressed the importance of talent being sincere in their desire to improve and maintain an open mind when receiving feedback. While acknowledging that fewer live events make this process more challenging, he believes that wrestlers who genuinely want to improve will find ways to do so.

AI-GENERATED FAKE QUOTES AND DEATH THREATS

During his podcast appearance, Punk was shown several social media posts that initially appeared to be from fellow WWE wrestlers reacting to his title win. Upon examination, he quickly determined that the posts were AI-generated fabrications rather than authentic tweets. Posts allegedly from Seth Rollins and Becky Lynch criticized his championship victory and referenced his past commentary on returning veterans dominating the main event picture.

Punk expressed strong frustration with the phenomenon of fake quotes being created and spread online. He stated: "This is why I don't fuck with social media. These are like A.I.-generated. These are fake quotes. Seth did not tweet this. Becky did not tweet this. This is a double-edged sword about most people in our business but especially me. I do not say this lightly: These are mentally ill people who, I mean, I couldn't imagine making up fake tweets that somebody else said."

The champion acknowledged the broader societal issue extending beyond wrestling, noting that people instantly believe fabricated content without verification. "I think the problem, and it's not just in our business, I think it's a societal thing, is people will instantly see that, and then they believe it. But I don't know how to change people's minds about that, which is why I lean into not caring about changing anybody's mind about that."

Punk recognized a paradox inherent in his position within the business of professional wrestling. Because he is in the business of manipulating people's emotions and generating reactions, misinformation and fake quotes actually benefit him in certain ways. However, he also acknowledged the darker reality that such hostility online can translate into real-world danger. "I'm in the business of toying with people's emotions so if somebody's going to read that and they're going to believe it, that helps me in a way, you know? But there's also Phil who walks through parking lots with his wife who has his head on a swivel because those people are out there."

During the podcast, McMahon and Punk both acknowledged that they have been contacted by the FBI regarding death threats made against them. I think they're pretty slack on that too, by the way. I don't think they're catching all of them. But there's been a few phone calls, absolutely."

PUNK'S HISTORIC PIPE BOMB PROMO AND VINCE MCMAHON

Punk reflected extensively on his historic 2011 "Pipe Bomb" promo on Raw, which was later ranked by WWE as the greatest moment in Raw history. The segment occurred during a period when Punk was not with the company, which he found remarkable. "When I was out of the business, I was obviously persona non grata, right? And you guys put the pipe bomb at No. 1. And I remember being like 'I am flabbergasted at that.' To be so good, to make that moment what it was, to have him sign off on that and to make that the number one Raw moment is, to me, it speaks volumes."

During his discussion with McMahon, Punk revealed previously undisclosed details about how the promo was created and approved. When he initially wanted to reference Paul Heyman and Brock Lesnar in the promo, Vince McMahon rejected that element. "If I said, 'Hey, I want to go out there and talk about Paul and Brock,' he'd be like, 'No, don't mention them.' Okay, so I didn't. So I wrote up this bullshit and I gave it to him."

McMahon's only significant feedback on the original script was to ask Punk to incorporate criticism of Stephanie McMahon herself. Punk recalled McMahon's instruction: "He read it, and his only feedback was, 'All right, it's good. Good. Just make fun of Stephanie.' I was like, 'Excuse me?' He's like, 'Yeah, add her in there.'" This detail became a source of ongoing debate among wrestling fans online, with some arguing the promo was fully approved and others claiming it went off-script. Punk clarified the nuanced reality: "It was both. It was approved and it wasn't. It was approved to a degree."

While taking pride in the promo's legacy, Punk expressed hope that future talent, particularly from NXT, will create moments that surpass the Pipe Bomb in significance. "Well, somebody will top it. I hope somebody does. I hope there's somebody in NXT." When asked whether he worries about his accomplishments being overshadowed, Punk dismissed the concern. "They can try, but again, that's just noise, you know? I trust my instincts."

Punk credited his ability to navigate the business with techniques he developed before signing with WWE and during his year in developmental. "I was in the business, I don't even know how long before I got signed. And then I was in developmental for a year and so I had tricks up my sleeve that a lot of people coming in didn't, that a lot of people from developmental at the time didn't. So I was able to navigate things a little bit better."

PUNK'S DRAMATIC PHYSICAL TRANSFORMATION

Six weeks before WrestleMania 42, Punk made the decision to dramatically improve his physical conditioning for the event. Title Changes History

July 13th has established itself as one of the most active dates in professional wrestling history for championship transitions. According to wrestling records, approximately 314 major and notable title changes have occurred on this specific date throughout wrestling's modern era. This high volume of championship activity is partly attributable to several prominent wrestling events that have been scheduled on July 13th over the years, including WCW Bash at the Beach 1997, TNA Victory Road 2008, NOAH Destination 2024, WWE Evolution 2025, and NJPW Presents CMLL Fantastica Mania 2024.

The history of July 13th title changes stretches back to the early days of professional wrestling. The earliest documented entry dates to 1952, when Tarzán López captured the NWA World Middleweight Title. Throughout the 1980s, the date saw continued championship activity with Masa Saito and Mr. Fuji winning the WWF World Tag Team Titles in 1982, Califa claiming the Mexican National Lightweight Title in 1985, and Riki Choshu and Takayuki Iizuka winning the IWGP Tag Team Titles in 1989.

The 1990s brought additional significant title changes on July 13th. Junji Hirata and Shin'ya Hashimoto captured the IWGP Tag Team Titles in 1995, followed by Shane Douglas winning the ECW World Television Title in 1996. The late 1990s proved particularly active, with 1998 witnessing both Chris Jericho's WCW Cruiserweight Title victory and Kane and Mankind's WWF Tag Team Titles win. Shinjiro Otani and Tatsuhito Takaiwa rounded out the decade by winning the IWGP Junior Heavyweight Tag Team Titles in 1999.

The year 2002 presented an unusual situation on July 13th with three WWE Hardcore Title changes occurring on the same date. The Hardcore Title operated under a 24/7 rule during this period, allowing for multiple championship switches throughout a single day. Shawn Stasiak, Bradshaw, and Justin Credible each held the title at different points during July 13th, 2002.

From 2007 through 2018, July 13th continued its pattern of hosting significant championship changes. Dr. Wagner Jr. and Último Guerrero won the CMLL World Tag Team Titles in 2007, while Kotaro Suzuki and Yoshinobu Kanemaru captured the GHC Junior Heavyweight Tag Team Titles in 2008. Madison Rayne made history by winning the TNA Knockouts Title in 2010, and Lashley claimed the TNA X-Division Title in 2016. Later, Damián el Terrible and Rush won the CMLL World Tag Team Titles in 2018.

The years 2020 and 2022 saw title vacancies occur on July 13th. The NEVER Openweight 6-Man Tag Team Titles were vacated in 2020, and the GHC Heavyweight Tag Team Titles were vacated in 2022, continuing the date's significance in championship history.

Recent years have demonstrated that July 13th remains an important date for title changes. Four championships changed hands on July 13th, 2024, with Willow Nightingale winning the CMLL World Women's Title, Ulka Sasaki capturing the GHC National Title, Naruki Doi claiming the AJPW World Junior Heavyweight Title, and AMAKUSA winning the GHC Junior Heavyweight Title. Most recently, on July 13th, 2025, Naomi won the WWE Women's World Title, adding to the growing legacy of championship changes on this historically significant date in professional wrestling.

CM Punk Reveals Vince McMahon’s Immediate Reaction To Iconic Pipe Bomb Promo

CM Punk has reflected on the unforgettable night of his legendary 2011 "Pipe Bomb" promo, revealing that Vince McMahon's backstage reaction immediately confirmed the segment had become something special. Appearing on What's Your Story? with Steph McMahon, the newly crowned Undisputed WWE Champion recalled walking through the curtain after delivering the career-defining promo and hearing just three words from the then-WWE Chairman.

"When I came back it was 'I smell money,' is what he said," Punk said of McMahon. "At the time, that was the stamp of approval, that was it. That was everything right there."

The iconic promo aired on the June 27, 2011 edition of WWE Raw. Seated cross-legged at the top of the entrance ramp, Punk delivered a scathing, unscripted-feeling monologue that criticized WWE and its leadership, referenced promotions including Ring of Honor and NJPW, and blurred the lines between fiction and reality by discussing his expiring WWE contract. The segment became one of the most influential promos in modern wrestling history and led directly into Punk's victory over John Cena for the WWE Championship at Money in the Bank in his hometown of Chicago.

During the same interview, Punk also reflected on the "Summer of Punk," describing that period as the moment he truly reached another level in his career. As transcribed by Fightful, he said the run remains one of his favorite memories and called the Pipe Bomb promo "just magic."

"I feel like I busted my a*** in my career to get to that moment where I was finally recognized," Punk said. "I can either go home and squander this or I can ride this lightning bolt, and I rode the lightning bolt and it really made me feel like I had finally leveled up."

Punk has since returned to WWE television and captured the Undisputed WWE Championship by defeating Sami Zayn earlier this month. He is now set to make his first title defense against Cody Rhodes at SummerSlam.

Nothing has been officially announced beyond this year's television tapings, but if this report is accurate, WWE's biggest show in Mexico may only be a matter of time.

Real Reason Netflix Doesn’t Have WWE RAW’s Full Library Yet

WWE fans have been asking the same question ever since RAW landed on Netflix: Where are all the old episodes?

Although WWE's landmark deal with Netflix began in January 2025, the streaming service still doesn't have the complete Monday Night RAW archive available. During a recent Q&A, WrestleVotes was asked why so much of the library is still missing, and the answer appears to be surprisingly simple. According to JoeyVotes, the rollout is being done gradually because WWE's agreement with Netflix stretches over several years.

"The reason I heard is this Monday Night Raw contract goes for years, so they will continue to add episodes at their pace and leisure. That's just what it is, as far as I know," JoeyVotes explained.

TC believes there's another reason behind the slow rollout. In his view, WWE has little reason to dump decades of RAW episodes onto Netflix all at once. Instead, he thinks the company is strategically holding content back to maximize the value of its massive tape library over time.

"I think they're gonna leverage it to try to get more money from Netflix. So it's like, 'Hey, we know you guys want it, and if you want to continue to pay the subscription every month, we're gonna give a little bit here and there,'" TC stated.

That's not all as TC argued that the strategy ultimately comes down to protecting one of WWE's most valuable assets. "So it all comes down to money, and WWE does a good job of protecting their library and IP to get the most amount of money from the consumer—or business partners, in this case, Netflix—as they can. That's what their strategy is: extract money as best they can."

For now, WWE and Netflix haven't publicly explained exactly how often older RAW episodes will be added, but if this information is accurate, fans shouldn't expect the entire archive to appear overnight. Instead, it sounds like the library will continue to grow little by little over the life of the deal.

Stephanie Vaquer WWE Return Plans

Stephanie Vaquer has been absent from WWE television since losing the Women's World Championship to Royal Rumble winner Liv Morgan at WrestleMania 42. Her injury-related absence has left a notable void on the programming, with the company clearly adjusting its plans around her unavailability. Her eventual return presents numerous compelling storyline possibilities for the women's division.

One primary direction involves a rematch with Liv Morgan for the Women's World Championship. Since capturing the title at WrestleMania, Morgan has not defended it on television, with her first defense scheduled against IYO SKY at SummerSlam. The gap in Morgan's title program suggests plans may have shifted due to Stephanie's injury, making a rematch between the two a logical progression upon her return. There is hope that any rematch would be more substantial than their initial encounter, which lasted only five minutes.

Another intriguing possibility involves moving to SmackDown for a dream match with Rhea Ripley. The clash between Stephanie and Ripley has been teased multiple times since Stephanie's main roster call-up but did not materialize at this year's WrestleMania. Rhea currently reigns as the WWE Women's Champion despite an ongoing injury and has already defeated the top names on the SmackDown brand. A draft could facilitate Stephanie's move to the blue brand, setting up an extended build toward this highly anticipated encounter.

A more unexpected direction could see Stephanie joining the AAA roster to form an alliance with La Catalina. Both women are Chilean wrestlers who came to WWE from CMLL. La Catalina, the first Chilean to sign with WWE, recently transitioned to AAA and is currently pursuing the Reina de Reinas Championship from Lady Flammer and Las Toxicas. La Catalina has already enlisted help from Lola Vice and Bayley for this quest, and bringing in Stephanie would inject fresh energy into AAA's women's division.

Stephanie could also pursue a rivalry with Becky Lynch. During Stephanie's Women's World Championship reign, she primarily defended against Raquel Rodriguez and Nikki Bella before losing to Liv Morgan. Meanwhile, Becky Lynch held the Women's Intercontinental Championship during this period, and the two never crossed paths. With Becky now free of the Intercontinental title and awaiting her next direction, a non-title feud between these two talented performers would significantly benefit the Women's division on Raw.

A particularly dramatic return scenario would involve Stephanie turning heel by betraying IYO SKY. The two wrestlers have history on the main roster, with IYO being Stephanie's first opponent on Raw and Stephanie defeating her to win the vacant Women's World Championship at WrestlePalooza. A shocking return where Stephanie helps IYO against the Judgment Day only to betray her and assist Liv Morgan in retaining her title would create the kind of viral moment WWE actively pursues. Subsequently, a series of matches between the two would reinvigorate Stephanie's career as a heel while providing compelling television throughout the remainder of the year.

Finally, Stephanie could form a tag team with Giulia. When both wrestlers joined WWE in 2024, they were immediately connected and debuted on NXT television within weeks of each other. Giulia captured the NXT Women's Championship while Stephanie won the Women's North American Championship, eventually leading to them facing off in a title match. This established connection provides fertile ground for a tag team partnership or ongoing storyline collaboration.

Penta Confirms The Lucha Bros Will Eventually Reunite, Shares His Thoughts On WWE’s Version Of AAA Thus Far

Penta is dominating the scene in WWE right now, as the active WWE Intercontinental Champion on the Raw brand on Netflix every Monday night. Heading into tonight's show in Dallas, TX., which features a Gauntlet to determine who will challenge for his title at WWE SummerSlam, reigning WWE Intercontinental Champion Penta spoke with The Dallas Morning News for an interview to promote the event. During the discussion, the masked WWE Superstar explained why Dallas holds a special place in his heart, his thoughts on WWE's version of AAA thus far and how he and Rey Fenix will reunite as The Lucha Bros at some point.

On why Dallas holds a special place in his heart, Penta expressed deep gratitude for the city's role in launching his career. He stated that Dallas is very special for him and for his career because many years ago, he started one part of his career there due to the abundance of Mexican companies of lucha operating in the city. Penta specifically gave a saludo to his amigo Oscar Martinez Entertainment, noting that Martinez was one of the promoters who brought him to Dallas early in his journey. He emphasized his love for the energy of Dallas fans, describing them as amazing people.

Regarding his thoughts about WWE's version of AAA, Penta expressed optimism about the partnership. He believes it's a good deal between AAA and WWE because now everyone in Mexico and all luchadores in Mexico have a big opportunity to come to WWE. Penta explained that the partnership makes it more easy for wrestlers to transition to WWE because it serves as a bridge between both companies. He reflected on his own journey, noting that it took him approximately 20 years of hard work every single day to come to WWE. However, he acknowledged that times have changed and it is now more easy for others due to the partnership. Most importantly, Penta observed that everyone coming through the partnership is perfect and different than wrestlers from years ago, describing it as a good change for the business.

On his brother Rey Fenix's recent AAA Cruiserweight Championship win and the future of The Lucha Bros, Penta expressed certainty about their eventual reunion. He stated definitively that he definitely expects The Lucha Bros to come together again, explaining that he knows in his heart that at some point they will reunite. However, Penta acknowledged uncertainty about the timing, saying he does not know exactly when this will happen. For now, he expressed his intention to enjoy this season in his life and career pursuing singles competition. He noted that his brother is also enjoying his current path as an AAA Cruiserweight Champion while Penta holds his WWE Intercontinental Championship. Both brothers are currently building their careers in different ways, with separate championship pursuits taking priority at this moment. Despite this temporary separation, Penta made a promise that one time at some point, The Lucha Bros will be back together, demonstrating his commitment to eventually reuniting with Rey Fenix.
