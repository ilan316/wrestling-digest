🔥 AEW Forbidden Door Matches and Teams Announced

AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door is shaping up to be a major event with several championship matches and high-stakes encounters announced during Wednesday's episode of Dynamite on June 17. The show delivered significant developments for the June 28 card taking place at the SAP Center in San Jose, California.

The centerpiece of Forbidden Door remains the 12-man steel cage match between Team MJF and Team Mark Briscoe. MJF's team consists of AEW World Champion Maxwell Jacob Friedman, TBS Champion Kevin Knight, Kyle Fletcher, Jake Doyle, Kazuchika Okada, and Andrade El Idolo. Team Briscoe features Mark Briscoe, Orange Cassidy, Roderick Strong, Kyle O'Reilly, Konosuke Takeshita, and Darby Allin. If Team Briscoe emerges victorious from the cage, Mark Briscoe will earn a title shot against MJF.

The match carries significant intrigue due to the various tensions within and between the teams. On Team MJF, Kevin Knight appears to have been temporarily placated by the champion's flattery, though Knight previously demanded a title shot of his own. Andrade El Idolo remained openly skeptical about teaming with MJF, questioning whether he would truly participate despite Don Callis's attempts to persuade him. Team Briscoe features its own layers of conflict, with Konosuke Takeshita specifically selected to confront Kyle Fletcher and Kazuchika Okada due to past grievances, while Darby Allin seeks revenge against MJF for taking the AEW World Championship from him at Double or Nothing and against Kevin Knight for turning on him afterward.

Prior to the cage match announcement, both teams faced off in a six-on-six tag match that opened the door for future storytelling. Team MJF secured victory when Roderick Strong submitted to MJF's armbar, with Strong's tap-out coming after an interference spot where Don Callis distracted referee Bryce Remsburg following Mark Briscoe's Jay Driller attempt. The post-match beatdown continued when Callis struck Briscoe with the Dynamite Diamond Ring, potentially leaving multiple members of Team Briscoe banged up heading into Forbidden Door.

Additionally, Bandido captured a significant victory for the newly reunited Brodido when he and Brody King defeated the Death Riders. Bandido pinned Daniel Garcia during the tag team match, and afterward, the ROH World Champion challenged Jon Moxley for the AEW Continental Championship at Forbidden Door. Moxley hesitated momentarily before accepting the challenge. The Continental Title match will take place under Continental Rules, which means neither Brody King nor any members of the Death Riders will be permitted at ringside.

The AEW World Tag Team Champions Christian Cage and Adam Copeland made a significant statement by challenging The Dogs, Clark Connors and David Finlay, to a title defense at Forbidden Door. A confrontation broke out when The Dogs attempted to celebrate their recent title win and perform their Five-Second Pose on Dynamite. Cage and Copeland attacked the challengers, then cut promos referencing how The Dogs have been mimicking their own legendary tag team work from the 2000s. Copeland drew comparisons between The Dogs and young versions of Edge and Christian, stating that like they once faced veterans such as the New Age Outlaws and the APA, Cage and Copeland would now teach The Dogs their lessons. The Dogs attacked the champions during a promo segment and have been mocking and attacking them for weeks, with the challengers potentially incurring penalties if disqualified from a subsequent match carried into the cage match.

The women's Owen Hart Cup Tournament final will see Mercedes Mone face the winner of the Athena versus Maya World match, which is scheduled to take place during an earlier AEW Collision episode. The Owen Hart Cup tournaments advanced as Mercedes Mone defeated Hazuki to secure her spot in the women's final. Mone will await the winner of the Athena versus Maya World match that is scheduled for Collision. These developments were announced alongside the championship matches, creating a card that blends AEW talent with international stars from NJPW, CMLL, and Stardom for what is being positioned as a major cross-promotional event.

🔥 Mercedes Moné Advances to Owen Hart Foundation Tournament Finals

Mercedes Moné Returns To Owen Cup Finals At AEW Forbidden Door

The first finalist in the women's Owen Hart Foundation Tournament was determined on the June 17 episode of AEW Dynamite, and after a lengthy back-and-forth contest, Mercedes Moné punched her ticket to a second consecutive Owen Hart Cup final, defeating STARDOM's Hazuki.

As soon as Moné's music hit to reveal that she was the "Wild Card" entrant in the 2026 Owen Hart Foundation Women's Tournament, The CEO became the favorite to win the Owen Cup for a second straight year. Stardom's Hazuki was an unexpected semi-final opponent for Moné, but after upsetting CMLL Women's Champion Persephone on Collision in round one, that was who stood across the ring from Mercedes during the match. Moné had defeated Alex Windsor in her opening match after entering as a surprise entrant, subbing in for the injured Willow Nightingale.

Moné kicked off proceedings with her usual confidence, but Hazuki held her own to the point where she kept Moné out of the ring as the match entered the commercial break. Perhaps Moné overlooked the Spark Joshi World Champion. Hazuki made it through that early wave to go on the offensive herself, even drawing blood from the former AEW TBS Champion. The Stardom star was in the driver's seat at times, generating significant momentum that had the match looking like it could slip away from Mercedes.

Coming out of the break, Moné continued to go for the win by countering a Tilterwhirl Head Scissors into a Lungblower but only got a two count. She would take the action back to the outside by slamming Hazuki's head into the apron before once again trying to get the win. However, Hazuki fired herself up and got back into the match with a trio of Tope Suicidas, which got the crowd firmly behind the Stardom star. She followed up with a Shotgun Drop Kick from the top which earned a near fall, but she kept on Moné by locking in a Crossface, which Moné was able to escape from.

As Hazuki got Moné up to continue the action, Moné mustered enough strength to hit two of the Three Amigos, before countering Hazuki's counter by trapping her in the ropes and hitting a Meteora on the entrance ramp as the match entered another commercial break. During the break, Moné hit another Meteora when the action returned to the ring, as well as a pair of knees in the corner as Hazuki struggled to distance herself from her dominant opponent. Back from the break and Moné missed with the running knees as Hazuki bought herself some time.

Moné looked to go for a third Meteora but Hazuki hit a Lungblower of her own in the corner and followed it up with a Senton from the top. Moné kicked out but got caught in another Crossface, and despite rolling into a Backslide, Hazuki kicked out and kept wrist control. A strike exchange resulted in a double down, and when both women got back to their feet, Hazuki hit a DDT and a Brainbuster but Moné rolled out of the ring to prevent a pinfall. Moné nearly hit the Mone Maker but Hazuki rolled her up, leading to a series of two counts from both women, but when Hazuki went for one more roll-up, Moné caught her with a Backstabber, held on, and locked in the Statement Maker for the submission victory.

Moné turned the closing moments of the bout into a slugfest after Hazuki nearly took the match. She pushed Moné to her limit, but eventually she had to tap out to the Statement Maker submission hold.

That second Owen Cup, and the All In World title shot that comes with it, isn't a fait accompli for Moné, however. At Forbidden Door she'll either have her second ever one-on-one match with ROH Women's Champion Athena, or face the tournament's cinderella, Maya World. That match between Athena and World is scheduled for Saturday on Collision, which tapes after Dynamite in Houston. The finals are set for Forbidden Door on June 28 with the tourney winner challenging for the World title at All In on August 30.

On the men's side of the tournament, Will Ospreay and Swerve Strickland will face each other in the finals at Forbidden Door with the winner earning their own title shot.

FTR's Decision to Leave WWE Was Rooted in Broken Randy Orton Partnership

Cash Wheeler of FTR recently revealed that their brief alliance with Randy Orton during his feud with WWE Champion Kofi Kingston was the moment that changed the trajectory of his and Dax Harwood's careers. Before joining AEW, Wheeler and Harwood competed in WWE as The Revival, finding success in both NXT and on the main roster where they won several championships and earned their reputation as one of the best traditional tag teams in wrestling. In 2019, they teamed up with Orton while he challenged Kingston for the championship. Kingston had the support of Big E and Xavier Woods from The New Day, while Orton joined forces with The Revival for what seemed like a promising partnership. However, the partnership didn't last long after Kingston retained his title against Orton.

Wheeler expressed that the Orton pairing was the one he missed the most because he believed the group had tremendous potential together. He spoke about how Orton was a big proponent of pushing for the group to continue and was fully behind the idea. The partnership became a turning point when WWE's draft separated the group, moving Orton to RAW while The Revival stayed on SmackDown. This separation became the final straw for Wheeler and Harwood, as they realized the company didn't have major plans for them moving forward. Wheeler explained that they expected to continue working with Orton after the Kingston feud ended, but when WWE instead separated them during the draft, they took it as a sign that their ceiling in the company was limited.

Wheeler stated that he and Harwood believed that if WWE was willing to kill the Orton partnership, then the company didn't really have any expectations or plans for their future. The draft split made them realize that they needed to seek their release. Wheeler has never regretted that decision, even for a second, and the move to AEW has proven to be the right choice for both members of FTR, as they have accomplished nearly everything a tag team could hope for in their new home.

Christian Cage Weaponizes Words Against WWE Veterans and Rivals

Christian Cage lived up to his reputation as AEW's most ruthless talker on the June 17 episode of AEW Dynamite, delivering a cutting verbal shot at respected WWE veteran Fit Finlay while setting the stage for a major title match at AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door. The segment began with tensions running high between the AEW World Tag Team Champions and their upcoming challengers, The Dogs. Earlier in the show, Christian Cage and Adam Copeland had blindsided David Finlay and Clark Connors, continuing a rivalry that had been brewing since The Dogs interrupted the champions' signature five-second pose and briefly made it their own.

After the confrontation spilled into the ring, Christian grabbed a microphone and reminded fans why few performers can weaponize words quite like "The Patriarch." He teased that fans were likely expecting one of his trademark family-related insults before taking an unexpected detour. Christian claimed he had never seen a tag team named after their own mothers, a remark clearly aimed at The Dogs before he turned his attention to David Finlay's famous wrestling lineage. He brought WWE veteran Fit Finlay into the conversation with a harsh line, suggesting that if David Finlay was meant to be a gift to wrestling from his father, then Fit Finlay should have done the world a favor and let him run down the inside of his leg. The insult drew a strong reaction and immediately reinforced Christian's long-running persona as a master of uncomfortable punchlines, delivering what was less a cheap shot and more a verbal spear aimed directly at Finlay's family legacy.

Following Christian's comments, Adam Copeland shifted the focus back to business, acknowledging The Dogs' recent success before officially challenging them to a match at AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door on June 28. With the challenge accepted, the AEW World Tag Team Championships will be on the line at Forbidden Door, with Christian and Copeland hopeful of sending The Dogs home as housebroken little pups rather than champions.

Speedball Mike Bailey Seeks Revenge Against Don Callis Family Defectors

It has been a difficult few weeks for Speedball Mike Bailey after his partner Kevin Knight betrayed both Bailey and Darby Allin before joining the Don Callis Family. Knight then defeated Bailey in a singles match, motivating Bailey to seek revenge. At AEW Collision, Bailey spent time on his vlog trying to give Myron Reed advice on how to defeat Knight ahead of their TNT Title clash. Bailey warned Reed to watch out for dropkicks and to be cautious of Don Callis and his new friends, describing them as minions who help Knight win his matches.

However, Bailey's advice proved insufficient as Knight ultimately retained the title with a dropkick. Bailey was also proven wrong about Knight needing help, as he defeated Reed with little assistance, leading Bailey to conclude once again that Knight was too good to be associated with a group like the Callis Family. Bailey then turned his attention to Jake Doyle, another former friend and tag team partner who has also gone over to the Callis Family. Bailey expressed feeling deeply betrayed by Doyle, describing him as someone he had grown to love and a former DPW Tag Team Champion with whom he had fought some wars. Bailey announced his plan to take out the Don Callis Family entirely.

However, Bailey's plans were thwarted when Doyle defeated him in the semi-main event of Collision, giving Bailey his second straight loss. The mounting losses and betrayals have set up a potential extended feud between Bailey and the Don Callis Family as Bailey seeks to reclaim respect and redemption after losing multiple former allies to the faction.

🔥 General Wrestling History and Retrospectives

The passing of Pete Doherty marks the loss of one of professional wrestling's true throwback characters. Known to old-school wrestling fans as "The Duke of Dorchester," Doherty died at age 85, with the news shared by the Cauliflower Alley Club after being informed by his friend Danny Davis.

Born in Dorchester, Massachusetts in 1943, Doherty was a regular fixture in the WWF during the 1970s and 1980s, working throughout the Northeastern United States including Massachusetts, Maine, and New York. He dated back to the WWWF days in the early 1970s and remained active until his final match in 1992 at the Cape Cod Melody Tent, where he lost to Konnan.

Though Doherty was not typically the guy getting a big title run, he was instantly recognizable to fans who appreciated his wild personality, old-school grit, and the kind of presence that made him stand out even when he was there to make someone else look good. He shared the ring with giants of the business including Andre the Giant, Dusty Rhodes, Mil Mascaras, Tiger Mask, and Bret Hart. During his career, he competed against many of the era's top stars such as the Junkyard Dog, Bruno Sammartino, and even challenged Bob Backlund for the WWF Championship at a house show in Rhode Island in May 1983.

Doherty appeared on the first WWF Saturday Night's Main Event and competed in the 1991 King of the Ring tournament, where he lost to Bret Hart in the first round. He competed for several titles including both the WWF and WWWF Tag Team titles and the Intercontinental Championship. Dutch Mantell Agrees With AJ Styles That Wins And Losses Don’t Matter In Wrestling

Dutch Mantell recently weighed in on AJ Styles' comments about wins and losses in professional wrestling, saying he agrees that a wrestler's value isn't determined solely by their record. Discussing Styles' philosophy on Storytime with Dutch Mantell, the veteran manager and booker praised the former WWE Champion's mindset and explained why helping the overall product is more important than protecting individual statistics.

AJ Styles said his focus has shifted away from personal accolades and toward making the show better as a whole. "I don't care if I win or lose anymore. At this stage of my career, it's about making the show better. It's about helping the younger guys and doing whatever is needed to make the story work." Styles continued by emphasizing that wrestling is ultimately about entertaining the audience rather than padding a personal win-loss record. "If somebody gets over because of something we did together, that's a win for everybody. It's not always about having your hand raised. It's about people caring about what they're watching."

Mantell said he completely understood that perspective and believes many fans put too much emphasis on who wins and loses. "I've said it for years: this isn't boxing or MMA. It's the entertainment business. If you can make money, tell a story, and get people invested, that's what matters. Sometimes losing is exactly what you're supposed to do." He added that experienced veterans should recognize when it's time to elevate the next generation. "When you've had your run and you've made your money, part of your job is helping the guys coming behind you. That's how the business keeps moving forward. If nobody helps build new stars, eventually you don't have any stars left."

Mantell concluded by noting that a performer's legacy isn't defined by their record alone. "People don't remember every win and every loss. They remember moments. They remember characters. They remember stories. That's what lasts."
