🔥 John Cena WrestleMania 42 Host

John Cena Sets His Sights On WrestleMania 42 Host Role And New Generation Of Stars

The legendary 17-time world champion John Cena has officially announced that he will be hosting the two-night WrestleMania 42 Premium Live Event scheduled for April 18 and 19 at Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas, Nevada. The event will air live in the U.S. on ESPN Unlimited and internationally on Netflix.

According to multiple reports, Cena's role as host was not part of the original plan when he retired from in-ring competition in December 2025 with a loss to GUNTHER at Saturday Night's Main Event. The hosting opportunity came together much later, with WWE approaching Cena about the position in February 2026. He agreed to take on the role after learning about it at that time. WWE made the decision to bring in Cena as a strategic move to boost ticket sales for the event, which had fallen short of expectations compared to the previous year's WrestleMania.

Following his retirement from the ring, Cena signed a five-year WWE Ambassador deal in December. In a late March interview at MEGACon Orlando, Cena indicated that he was actively working to define his new role within WWE. He stated, "I am working diligently to try to figure out my next path as a contributing, useful member of WWE. I think I might crack the code, and I think we have something. And if the stars align and everybody signs off, I will be a whole lot more integrated in the system. And I'm very excited about the road ahead, and I'm just crossing my fingers that it works."

Beyond his hosting duties, Cena's WrestleMania weekend will be packed with additional appearances. He will join The Undertaker for a live recording of the "Six Feet Under" podcast at WWE World on Saturday, April 18, the same day as night one of WrestleMania. The podcast recording was announced through Fanatics, with the message stating, "Two icons. One stage. LIVE. John Cena joins The Undertaker for a live recording of Six Feet Under at WWE World. No script. No filters. Just conversation you won't hear anywhere else." WWE World will host several fan-focused events during the WrestleMania weekend, including meet and greets and live shows featuring numerous WWE stars.

Since his retirement, Cena has expressed particular enthusiasm for supporting the next generation of WWE talent. He recently took to social media to highlight his excitement about witnessing the WrestleMania debuts of five rising stars: Lash Legend, Je'Von Evans, Trick Williams, Oba Femi, and Stephanie Vaquer. In his tweet, Cena wrote, "Since retirement I've met so many wonderful people who've shared with me that they saw my first WrestleMania match and my last. I'm so excited to be able to say 'I was there!' for the WM debuts of this incredible group!"

The card for WrestleMania 42 features several of these newcomers in prominent positions. Stephanie Vaquer enters as the Women's World Champion and will face Liv Morgan of Judgment Day. Lash Legend, teaming with Nia Jax as The Irresistible Forces, will defend the Women's Tag Team Championship in a Fatal Four-Way match that includes Charlotte Flair and Alexa Bliss, Bayley and Lyra Valkyria, and The Bella Twins. Je'Von Evans will compete in a ladder match for Penta's Intercontinental Championship against Dragon Lee, JD McDonagh, and Rusev. Trick Williams will challenge Sami Zayn for the United States Championship. Oba Femi, who will be making his WrestleMania debut without a championship on the line, is booked against former 10-time WWE Champion Brock Lesnar in what is shaping up to be one of the top matches on the show.

Cena's decision to spotlight these emerging talents aligns with comments from former wrestler Baron Corbin, who has advocated for WWE to feature newer stars going into WrestleMania. The current card appears to be striking a balance between established superstars and the up-and-coming roster, with twelve matches announced as of the latest reports.

The confirmed match card for WrestleMania 42 includes World Heavyweight Champion CM Punk defending against Roman Reigns, Undisputed WWE Champion Cody Rhodes defending against Randy Orton, Women's Champion Jade Cargill defending against Rhea Ripley, an unsanctioned match between Drew McIntyre and Jacob Fatu, Women's Intercontinental Champion AJ Lee defending against Becky Lynch, Seth Rollins facing Gunther, and Finn Balor squaring off against Dominik Mysterio.

Throughout his farewell tour, Cena expressed openness to accepting non-wrestling-related opportunities within WWE and emphasized his commitment to not physically competing on-screen again, citing his promise to permanently retire from in-ring competition. His multi-faceted involvement with WrestleMania 42, from hosting to podcast appearances to championing the debuts of rising talent, demonstrates how WWE has leveraged the veteran's transition from active competitor to ambassador and mentor to the next generation of wrestlers.

🔥 Janel Grant WWE Lawsuit

TIMELINE AND ALLEGATIONS IN JANEL GRANT'S WWE LAWSUIT

The legal battle involving former WWE employee Janel Grant, WWE, and Vince McMahon has developed significantly since it was filed in January 2024, with multiple court filings laying out detailed allegations spanning several years and involving multiple individuals within the company.

Grant claims her relationship with Vince McMahon began in 2019 while she worked in WWE's legal department. Her legal team contends it was never truly consensual and quickly became coercive, with Grant describing feeling physically and mentally drained early in the relationship and telling friends she was barely functioning during this period.

By early 2020, Grant alleges that people inside WWE were already discussing the relationship. She claims McMahon told her that executives knew about it. Around this time, she was moved out of her role in the legal department and later brought back, something her attorneys argue was tied to the rumors circulating within the company. According to the filings, McMahon also allegedly began sharing explicit photos of Grant without her consent and started pressuring her for sexual encounters involving other men.

During the COVID-19 lockdown period, Grant claims McMahon had near-constant access to her, which she argues escalated the situation further. By late 2020, she alleges that former WWE executive John Laurinaitis was brought into the situation, with McMahon allegedly arranging multiple encounters involving Laurinaitis. Around this same time, her role within WWE began to shift.

In early 2021, Grant claims she was moved out of the legal department and into Talent Relations, a move her legal team argues gave McMahon easier access to her and more control over her day-to-day work environment. Her attorneys also contend that WWE leadership, including Nick Khan and Brad Blum, were made aware of the situation after McMahon disclosed it to them.

One of the most significant allegations involves professional wrestler Brock Lesnar. Grant claims McMahon brought Lesnar into the situation as part of both personal and business dynamics. She alleges she was directed to create explicit content for Lesnar and that McMahon used her as part of negotiations tied to Lesnar's WWE return. Grant further claims that Lesnar contacted her directly, requested explicit material, and pushed for in-person meetings including travel arrangements. She states that one planned encounter did not occur because Lesnar became too intoxicated.

THE LAURINAITIS CONNECTION AND RECENT ALLEGATIONS

According to Grant's most recent court filings from April 1, she alleged that in early March 2021, she was forced to participate in a sexual encounter with both McMahon and Laurinaitis at McMahon's condo that was so violent she blacked out for the majority of the encounter.

Laurinaitis was originally named as a co-defendant in Grant's lawsuit, but the claims against him were voluntarily dismissed in May 2025 after he agreed to cooperate with Grant's legal team and provide evidence supporting her case. Despite his removal from the lawsuit, Grant's latest court filings continued to reference alleged incidents involving him.

LAURINAITIS' ATTORNEY RESPONDS

Following the April 1 filing, Edward M. Brennan, the attorney representing Laurinaitis, issued a statement to Post Wrestling. Brennan dismissed the allegations as lacking weight and questioned whether they could survive legal scrutiny. He stated that his client had no need to comment on what he characterized as a "salacious affidavit," adding that anyone can make allegations but whether those allegations can withstand cross-examination is an entirely different matter.

Brennan further emphasized his client's legal standing and the results of prior investigations. He noted that after more than two years of litigation, Laurinaitis remains the only party exonerated by an independent investigation commissioned by the WWE Board of Directors to review the matters at hand. Brennan also highlighted that Laurinaitis was the only person dismissed with prejudice from the lawsuit, arguing these facts speak louder than the allegations being made.

THE NDA AND JANUARY 2022

The situation escalated significantly in January 2022, when Grant claims McMahon pressured her to resign and sign a non-disclosure agreement, warning of serious consequences if the situation became public. She describes feeling trapped, sleep-deprived, and under intense pressure during this period. Grant claims McMahon made it clear his lawyer needed to handle everything before the situation could be disclosed to WWE's board.

According to the filings, McMahon initially offered Grant 1 million dollars, which was later increased to 3 million dollars. Grant signed the NDA on January 28, 2022. Her legal team argues she did so under extreme duress, citing threats of public exposure, damage to her reputation, and broader consequences if she refused to sign. McMahon's legal team counters that Grant negotiated the payout herself, arguing this demonstrates she was not forced.

ONGOING PAYMENTS AND BREACH ALLEGATIONS

Grant claims the situation did not end after she signed the NDA. She alleges McMahon continued to contact her and that interactions with Lesnar also continued. She asserts she was still being directed to engage in activities and report back on communications. Her legal team also claims additional incidents took place after the agreement was signed, which they argue makes the NDA unenforceable.

The NDA called for 3 million dollars in payments to be distributed over time. Grant received the first 1 million dollars, but McMahon stopped the remaining payments after claiming Grant violated the NDA by sharing information with a third party. Grant denies breaking the agreement and claims it was McMahon who breached it by refusing to pay the remaining balance.

CURRENT LEGAL STATUS AND ARBITRATION QUESTION

Grant filed her lawsuit in January 2024 rather than pursuing the arbitration process that the NDA specified. The biggest outstanding question in the case centers on whether the NDA forces everything into private arbitration or if the case can proceed in open court. Grant's legal team filed a memorandum on April 1 opposing WWE and McMahon's efforts to move the proceedings to private arbitration, arguing the case should remain in the public court system.

McMahon resigned from TKO/WWE following the filing of the lawsuit in January 2024. The case continues to develop as both sides argue over jurisdiction and procedural matters, with the decision regarding arbitration versus public court potentially shaping how much of the case becomes public record.

🔥 Road Dogg WWE Departure

Road Dogg Brian James Explains WWE Departure, Shuts Down AEW Speculation

In early March 2026, it was reported that Brian "Road Dogg" James had exited his position as co-lead writer for Friday Night SmackDown at WWE. Mike Johnson of PWInsider.com noted that James had expressed his unhappiness and tendered his resignation. For the first time since his departure, James addressed the decision publicly during a virtual signing appearance on April 2, 2026.

When asked why he left WWE, James did not cite backstage drama or creative frustration. Instead, he pointed to a very personal reason centered around family priorities and work-life balance. "It was moving too fast. It was just moving too fast for me. We talked about my brothers and their grandkids and I didn't get to spend a whole lot of time with mine so, I'm gonna do more of that now," James explained during the signing.

This answer shifted the narrative surrounding his departure completely. Rather than suggesting he was searching for his next big professional opportunity, James made it clear that he was focused on slowing down and stepping away from the constant grind of the wrestling business. The demanding pace of his creative role at WWE ultimately conflicted with the time he wanted to dedicate to his own family.

During the same virtual signing appearance, James was asked about WWE's Unreal series on Netflix, which featured him participating in a creative writing meeting. When questioned about whether such content exposes too much about the inner workings of the wrestling business, James acknowledged that this is a concern longtime veterans often share. "As an old timer, I think we always feel that way (that it's exposing the business too much). But look, if there's an idea behind it, in my opinion, it's an on-ramp or an exit ramp into the actual episodic television show. And I don't know if there's a metric that can tell you whether that works or not," he said.

The timing of James's departure fueled speculation about his next move, particularly after he was photographed with longtime partner Billy Gunn, who is involved with All Elite Wrestling as both a talent and a producer. This reunion sparked questions about whether James might join AEW in a creative or producing capacity. However, James quickly and definitively shut down any such speculation.

When asked about the possibility of producing for AEW and how much of the company's production work makes it to television, James stated plainly, "I don't know the answer to that. I'm sorry." Billy Gunn, who was participating in the signing alongside James, echoed the sentiment, adding, "I don't know the answer to that either."

The conversation then shifted when James was asked whether he could potentially know the answer to questions about AEW's production process in the future. Both men gave the same response: "No." This clear and repeated denial effectively ended speculation about James potentially joining AEW's creative team or production department.

Rather than rushing into another high-profile position, Road Dogg's comments make clear that he is prioritizing a significant shift away from the intensity of the wrestling business. For now, his focus is on family time and reducing the pace of his professional life, not on finding his next creative role in the industry.
