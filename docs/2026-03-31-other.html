Francis Ngannou PFL Commentary

Francis Ngannou addressed his departure from PFL and his future involvement with the promotion's Africa brand during an interview with Ariel Helwani. The former UFC Heavyweight Champion parted ways with PFL in January, though he emphasized this was the organization's decision and not something he had initiated.

Ngannou explained that he had actually been contracted for one more fight with PFL and had been hoping to complete it before the split. He noted that while the separation from the main promotion happened, he remains interested in staying connected to PFL Africa, which he helped establish as an equity-partner when he first signed in 2023.

"Obviously I would like to be involved. My goal with PFL Africa was to bring the sport to the continent, launch that and give that opportunity to people in the continent. It was something separate from my promotional agreement. So I think if there is a way to keep that on, I would still be a part of it. Because for me, it's a personal project," Ngannou said.

The heavyweight fighter will make his return to MMA on May 16 on an MVP Netflix card headlined by Ronda Rousey and Gina Carano. This will mark his second fight since joining PFL, with his first being a victory over Renan Ferreira for the PFL Super Fights Heavyweight Championship in February 2024.

However, Ngannou expressed disappointment about the opponent he ultimately landed. He had been in negotiations to fight GLORY Kickboxing's Rico Verhoeven, a fellow crossover fighter transitioning to MMA. Verhoeven, who is the departed Heavyweight Champion in kickboxing, was originally being discussed as an opponent for Ngannou's return.

Those plans fell through when Verhoeven reached a verbal agreement with Turki Alashikh regarding a boxing fight. The kickboxer has now committed to facing Oleksandr Usyk in Giza, Egypt, on May 23 instead.

"No disrespect to Philipe but I was very excited for the Rico fight, and then when I heard that he had signed to fight Usyk I was a little disappointed, to be honest," Ngannou told Helwani.

Instead of Verhoeven, Ngannou will face Philipe Lins, an 18-5 fighter who won the 2018 PFL Heavyweight Championship Tournament and previously competed in the UFC's light heavyweight division. While acknowledging Verhoeven's commitment to his boxing agreement with Alashikh, which had been in place since December, Ngannou noted that he was unaware of the financial details that may have influenced Verhoeven's decision.

"I don't know about the amount. I don't know about numbers. But since December he had a verbal agreement to fight in boxing... he was always clear about it," Ngannou said.

Ngannou was also asked about the perception that Verhoeven's upcoming boxing match against Usyk was being sanctioned as a World title fight by the WBC, while his own 2023 crossover fight against Tyson Fury was not granted the same status. Ngannou explained that at the time of his Fury fight, there was less faith in cross-promotion fighters competing for legitimate titles. He noted that despite this, he was ranked after the Fury bout and the contest itself was contested for a commemorative belt.

GWF Sebastian Mahfood Kerry Von Erich Memories

On March 28, 2026, the Wrestling Epicenter welcomed Dr. Sebastian Mahfood as a guest on their interview show hosted by James Walsh. Mahfood's appearance marked a remarkable reunion with professional wrestling, coming over 30 years after he last participated in the industry. For fans who watched the Global Wrestling Federation during its run in the early 1990s, Sebastian was a recognizable heel character who worked alongside the legendary Gary Hart. The GWF had been a significant presence in wrestling culture, airing five days a week on ESPN at 4 p.m., showcasing quality professional wrestling action from the famous Sportatorium in Dallas, Texas.

One of the most memorable moments from Sebastian's wrestling career involved a spectacular spot with wrestler John Tatum. Tatum delivered a superkick that sent Sebastian flying off the ring apron into the front row, his glasses separating from his face during the flight. The spot generated significant crowd reaction and impressed the wrestlers involved in the match. This particular moment became so popular that it was featured in the cold open and used repeatedly in the training school's commercials throughout the remainder of the GWF's existence. The promotion continued operating until September 1994.

Sebastian's path into professional wrestling began with his childhood fascination during the 1980s. He explained that wrestling held a special appeal for his generation, particularly influenced by his older brother's enthusiasm for the sport. His brother maintained exclusive control of the family's remote control once one became available with their new television set, and the two watched wrestling every Saturday morning. During these formative years, Sebastian witnessed the careers of Hulk Hogan, the Von Erichs, and numerous other wrestling personalities. He would later have the opportunity to work with many of these same individuals at the Sportatorium where he had watched them perform as a young fan.

Sebastian's entry into the wrestling business came through an unlikely connection. Gray Pierson, an established attorney, became friends with Sebastian while the young man was a college student. Pierson was a law partner with Don Ray, the father of Sebastian's college roommate Scott Ray. Sebastian frequently visited the law office, where Pierson recognized his potential as a helpful associate. Around 1990 or 1991, Pierson assisted the Von Erich family with promotional work aimed at increasing the Sportatorium's profitability. This assistance led to Pierson becoming the official promoter for the venue. During this transition, Pierson faced the challenge of organizing weekly bulletins for the promotion.

Recognizing Sebastian's background as an English major, Pierson asked him to take on the responsibility of creating the weekly program. Sebastian accepted the offer and produced "The Grappler Volume 1," the first edition of what would become the regular publication for the GWF. This writing role served as Sebastian's entry point into the professional wrestling industry. What began as a simple administrative task evolved into an opportunity that would eventually lead to his in-ring work as the character Sebastian, performing as a heel under Gary Hart's guidance.

Throughout his interview with the Wrestling Epicenter, Sebastian presented himself as a humble individual who valued his experiences in professional wrestling despite the relatively brief nature of his career. He expressed gratitude for the opportunity to encounter wrestling legends and work in such a unique environment. The conversation touched on various topics related to his wrestling days and life experiences, with the full discussion available in both audio and video formats. The interview demonstrated that while Sebastian has moved on to a successful career as a medical doctor, his memories of his time in professional wrestling remain vivid and meaningful.

Leon Slater AJ Styles Praise Story

Leon Slater Shares Story Behind AJ Styles Praise

TNA X-Division Champion Leon Slater spoke with The Sun in the UK about various topics, including receiving an endorsement from WWE legend "The Phenomenal" AJ Styles at Slammiversary.

Slater recalled his first major interaction with Styles at the TNA event. "So the first major interaction I had with him was at Slammiversary where he returned to TNA and came out and sung my praises in the middle of the ring, which I will never forget. That's the moment I'll always remember. He also pulled me aside for a chat after we came back from the ring, and he spoke to me on everything like match feedback, how to handle yourself in wrestling, how to handle yourself as a man, be a family man, how to operate and how it's made him successful. He just gave me pure wisdom. So, getting to share that moment with him in my home at TNA was incredible."

The two would cross paths again on a much larger stage. Describing his experience facing Styles at WWE Saturday Night's Main Event, Slater expressed disbelief at being part of such a historic event. "And then getting to share another moment with him in his home for the last 10 years, in WWE, and especially on the night that it was – it's still hard for me to believe I was there. I'll see clips from that night of like John being stood in the ring and putting his armbands and his and his and his sneakers and I was like, 'Bro, I was stood right there!' It almost feels like a fever dream, but it was real. That whole day was just magical."

Slater praised the quality of talent involved in his Saturday Night's Main Event match. "For the match, I couldn't have asked to be around better people. AJ, who's one of my absolute idols. And then Dragon, another super hungry, young guy that's been smashing it and I used to watch his work when he used to wrestle all over the world. And then obviously, having Je'Von, one of my best friends as my partner was another dream come true."

The backstage experience proved equally memorable for the young performer. "I remember very distinctly as we walked back from that match, it was a good feeling. I remember trying to take it all in. And then I come in Gorilla, and it was like a who's who of of legends was stood there and I was like what is going on, like I can't believe my life right now. Triple H was in the chair there, Bruce Pritchard was sat next to him, Michael Hayes is there. The Undertaker stood at the back of the room, Shawn Michaels is there. I walk out of Gorilla and Kurt Angle's there, RVD's there; like everyone that I've ever watched growing up just seemed to be there, and it truly was a magical day, man. It was wicked."

Slater highlighted a particular moment with Triple H that stood out. "After the Saturday Night's Main Event match, I had a really cool little chat with Triple H where he pulled me to a side for a couple of seconds, and spoke to me and Gorilla, and especially on a night like that where there's so much going on and I am far from the priority, you know what I mean, I thought it was very cool of him to do that when he doesn't need to. And I've had a bunch of experiences like that, man, like I can't sing enough praises for how I got treated during my couple of months with WWE at the tail end of last year."

Another highlight came during the UK tour when Slater faced Carmelo Hayes on Smackdown. "I'll be honest, that's probably the most comfortable I ever felt in a WWE ring because I felt like I was with my people, you know what I'm saying, like I remember. There was one point where Carmelo was punching me in the face or something, like, I was getting battered, but I remember hearing 'he's one of our own, he's one of our own.' And then I was like, 'OK, right, we're really at home. We've got the chants going and everything!' Me and Melo hit it off, man, I feel like our chemistry and ring was on a different level and I'm glad we got to deliver that night in Wembley."

Back in TNA, Slater reflected on working with legendary talents. "It's been a dream come true, man. When I walk into the TNA locker room, I've been here for a couple of years now, like you said, I've been rubbing shoulders, I've been working with these guys, they're my peers now. So when I walk in the locker room, I see Matt, I see Jeff, I see Nick, I don't see Matt Hardy, Jeff Hardy and Nick Nemeth. Even though I should and there's these points in time where I do have to pinch myself."

Dragon Gate Wrestler Departure

Dragon Gate wrestler Dragon Dia has announced his departure from the promotion, effective at the start of April. The junior heavyweight posted a statement on Tuesday explaining that after extensive discussions with Dragon Gate, both parties have decided to part ways as of April 1.

In his statement, Dia expressed gratitude to everyone in Dragon Gate for the lessons he learned during his tenure with the company. He also thanked the fans for their continued support throughout his time there. The wrestler indicated that he will be taking some time to recharge before making his next move, promising to return as a better, more mature performer. He asked fans to continue supporting him moving forward.

Following his initial announcement, Dia clarified that his departure is not due to injury or health issues. He stressed that despite his exit from Dragon Gate, he has not yet signed with any other promotion. This leaves his future destination open, though several possibilities appear likely given his recent activity.

Dia's participation in New Japan Pro Wrestling's Best of the Super Junior and Super Junior Tag League tournaments in both 2024 and 2025 suggests NJPW could be a potential landing spot for the wrestler. With New Japan currently in the process of announcing entrants for this year's Best of the Super Juniors tournament, Dia would represent a logical addition to the field.

The 27-year-old made his professional wrestling debut for Dragon Gate in 2018 as a protege of Dragon Kid. During his seven-year tenure with the promotion, Dia established himself as a accomplished junior heavyweight competitor. He has won Dragon Gate's Open the Brave Gate Championship, the promotion's junior heavyweight title, on two separate occasions. Beyond his singles success, Dia has also held tag team and trios championships during his time with the company.

Dillon Danis Post-Logan Paul Loss

Dillon Danis continues to grapple with the emotional toll of his 2023 defeat to Logan Paul, revealing that the loss deeply affected his mental state. Danis fought Paul in a boxing ring in October 2023, losing via disqualification in the sixth round after performing a takedown and illegal choke move that brought an abrupt end to the bout. The fight had been marked by considerable personal animosity during its buildup.

Prior to the match, Danis had repeatedly posted pictures of Paul's wife, Nina Agdal, on social media as part of his promotional trash talk strategy. This behavior escalated the situation significantly, eventually resulting in Agdal suing Danis and obtaining a temporary restraining order against him as part of ongoing litigation.

When asked about his current legal situation, Danis explained the status of the case. He noted that he recently completed a deposition and anticipates another one followed by trial. The process has been lengthy and expensive, with Danis suggesting that Paul's financial resources have allowed them to prolong the litigation and increase his legal bills substantially.

Regarding the fight itself, Danis could not hide his anguish about the loss. He stated that he thinks about the match every day and that it deeply affects him. He revealed to Wade Plemons that the defeat made him feel suicidal, emphasizing his hatred of losing and his particular distaste for losing to Paul specifically. Danis acknowledged that better preparation could have made a difference since it was his first boxing match, though he stated he makes no excuses for the outcome.

When questioned about returning to boxing in the future, Danis confirmed his intention to remain focused on MMA fighting going forward. However, this path presents its own challenges, as he noted that no one currently wants to fight him.

Bret Hart Refused To Take WWE Hall of Famer’s ‘Dangerous’ Finishing Move

Bret Hart once refused to take a move from a WWE Hall of Famer because he felt it was dangerous. During his recent interview, WWE Hall of Famer Bret Hart looked back at his match against the late Scott Hall/Razor Ramon at Royal Rumble 1993. He revealed that he refused to take Hall's finisher, the Razor's Edge, because he felt it was too dangerous and he didn't want to get dropped on his head.

Bret Hart noted that he shot down Scott Hall's pitch and laid out the match himself, which he claims the latter never got over. However, he called it the best match Hall ever had.

"You know, the funny thing about that match was that Scott Hall hated it. I had never worked with Scott, and I didn't know him very well," Bret Hart said. "I only knew he's been in the business a short time and never made it to WWF or NWA, or anywhere else. He was an AWA wrestler, you know, worked part-time, I think, or worked in Japan, maybe. I didn't know what his career was like before, but he was not really well-known or established."

Hart went on to explain his reasoning for rejecting the move. He noted that Hall's finish involved picking up an opponent and dropping them on their head, which Hart considered far too risky regardless of who was performing it.

"His finish, what we call the Razor's Edge, he'd pick you up and drop you on your head. I go, 'Nobody is doing that to me ever.' I don't care, and I would stand by that today. He goes, 'Oh, don't worry, I set you down,' and I'm like, 'No, no, not on my fu*king head, you don't. No way. There's no fuc*ing way. You're 6'6 or something. You're gonna fall backwards with me, and the first thing that hits the ground is my head, in my upper back.' I said, 'It doesn't make any sense. It's too dangerous, too stupid a move for me to do,' and I refused to do it," Hart explained.

Hart emphasized that his stance was not personal to Hall but rather a principle he has maintained throughout his entire career.

"When I first met him, I'm like, 'Nobody drops me on my head like that.' I don't know him well enough to let him do that. I actually would never let anybody do that move to me, just for the same reason that I said that I would never take a bump where I could fall and land on the back of my head and maybe break my neck," Bret Hart said.

He further elaborated on the safety concerns inherent in the move, describing the loss of control that comes with being thrown backwards in that manner.

"If something goes wrong, there's no way I can even stop, and I'm falling out of control backwards with no control over how I land or where I land," Hart said. "It's a really dangerous move to do. I would never let anyone do that to me anywhere in any country, and in any company."

Despite the initial tension over the move, Hart indicated that his relationship with Hall improved over time. He noted that by the time they worked together again at King of the Ring, the two had become more comfortable with each other and had developed a stronger friendship based on their growing familiarity with each other's wrestling style.

Aaron Rourke Vs Cappuccino Jones WWE Evolve Championship Match Set For WrestleMania 42 Week

Aaron Rourke will defend his recently won WWE Evolve Championship during WrestleMania 42 week in Las Vegas. The championship defense has been officially confirmed for April 16, when Rourke will face former WWE ID Champion Cappuccino Jones at The ID Showcase event presented by FSWVegas.

Rourke captured the WWE Evolve Championship on the March 18 episode of WWE Evolve, defeating Jackson Drake in a match that had been taped back in February. His title reign will be put to the test against Jones, who has been seeking championship opportunities following his own loss of the WWE ID Championship to Starboy Charlie in March.

This championship match represents a historic moment for the WWE Evolve brand. WWE Evolve was established in March of last year, with the men's and women's championships being officially revealed in May. Despite existing during last year's WrestleMania week, the men's WWE Evolve Championship did not receive representation at WrestleMania 41. This year's match will be the first time the men's championship has been defended during WrestleMania week, marking a significant milestone for the developing brand.

The decision to place this match during WrestleMania week in Las Vegas serves a strategic purpose for WWE officials. With fans from across the world descending on the city for the two-night Premium Live Event and numerous wrestling events taking place throughout the week, there is a prime opportunity to showcase ID talent to a massive international audience. Many of these fans are likely to be watching main roster stars compete at WrestleMania, giving WWE ID performers significant visibility and potential exposure.

D-Von Dudley provides details on nearly dying from a stroke in 2020

D-Von Dudley, the legendary WWE Hall of Famer known for his time with the Dudley Boyz tag team, opened up about a life-threatening health crisis that nearly claimed his life in 2020. Speaking in detail on the VladTV YouTube channel, Dudley recounted the harrowing events that led to his stroke and the miraculous circumstances that may have saved his life.

The incident occurred while Dudley was in Hollywood, California, where he had been invited by WWE to help produce a fight scene for an episode of a Punky Brewster remake. Alongside fellow WWE personalities Charlotte Flair and Alexa Bliss, who had requested to appear on the show, Dudley traveled to California at the request of John Laurinaitis. The production involved extensive COVID-19 testing protocols, with Dudley recalling that the cast and crew underwent testing multiple times throughout the day, including upon arrival, before returning to set, and at various other points. The frequent nasal swab testing was something Dudley found particularly unpleasant, joking that it was "nearly killing me."

After finishing work on set, Dudley said goodbye to Charlotte Flair and spent time in the hotel lobby chatting with Alexa Bliss while waiting for her then-boyfriend (now husband) Ryan to arrive. Once Ryan showed up, the three exchanged pleasantries before Dudley headed to his room. That evening, Dudley ordered food through DoorDash at his wife's suggestion, a service he admitted he was somewhat oblivious to at the time, surprised that his wife in Florida could arrange delivery for him in California.

The night took a strange turn when Dudley was getting ready for bed. After using the bathroom, he felt an unexplained compulsion to unlock the door to his room. This was unusual behavior for him, as he typically kept the door latched and locked to ensure privacy and prevent accidental intrusions. Despite initially dismissing the feeling, the compulsion persisted, bothering him repeatedly. Dudley eventually gave in to the inexplicable urge and unlocked the door before going to bed, describing the experience as something that "clear as day" was telling him to do so.

In the middle of the night, Dudley attempted to get out of bed to take his first step onto the floor but immediately collapsed face-first, busting his mouth open. More alarming than the fall itself was the sudden realization that he could not feel his legs. Panicked and confused, Dudley found himself unable to stand or walk. Fortunately, this was the first time he had requested a suite from WWE, which worked in his favor as the larger room gave him more space to maneuver despite his incapacity.

What ensued was a desperate struggle for help. Dudley had to army crawl across the floor to the bathroom, where he somehow managed to lift himself onto the toilet. With his cell phone in the other room and unable to move quickly, Dudley heard both his cell phone and the hotel room phone ringing. The hotel operator informed him that his wife was calling, seeking to know why he was not answering. In what Dudley himself acknowledged was a jerky response, he told the operator to inform his wife that he was in the bathroom and would call her back once he was finished.

His wife continued calling back persistently, and the hotel operator called again. This time, Dudley responded more harshly, telling the operator emphatically not to call his room again and that he would contact his wife when he was ready. After hanging up, Dudley attempted to army crawl back to his bed but struggled immensely with the movement. His cell phone continued ringing as his wife tried repeatedly to reach him. Finally managing to get himself onto the bed, Dudley reached for the phone, but it fell out of reach on the other side. As he rolled over and retrieved it, he jokingly thought to himself that no writer could have scripted the unfortunate sequence of events that had unfolded. When he finally answered the phone, his wife was on the other end, and Dudley, exhausted and disoriented, responded irritably, asking what she wanted since he was sleeping.

Dudley's account underscores the critical role his wife's persistence played in potentially saving his life. Her repeated calls to the hotel, despite his initial rudeness in response, demonstrated her concern and intuition that something was seriously wrong. The stroke Dudley suffered that night could have proven fatal, particularly had he remained alone and unable to call for help. The unlocking of the door, an action driven by what Dudley described as an inexplicable inner voice or instinct, also proved crucial, as it may have allowed first responders easier access to his room once emergency services were called.
