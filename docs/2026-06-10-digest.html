🔥 AEW Stars Return and Injury Updates

RETURNS AND INJURIES HEADLINE AEW IN RECENT WEEKS

AEW has experienced significant roster movement in recent weeks, with several wrestlers dealing with injury setbacks while others have made triumphant comebacks. The promotions's depth has been tested as stars navigate recovery timelines and medical clearances.

Brian Cage's return to AEW came together in a whirlwind fashion. The Machine made his surprise comeback on May 13 during an episode of AEW Dynamite, answering Kevin Knight's TNT Championship open challenge after nearly a year's absence due to injury. While the match aired smoothly on television, Cage revealed the hectic nature of how his return was arranged. He received the call to return late Tuesday night, just hours before his scheduled appearance. Despite already having a full schedule and not being home, Cage managed to prepare himself quickly. He trained legs, knowing he would be traveling all day Wednesday, and finished his preparations around three in the morning. After just five hours of sleep, he woke at 5:40 a.m. to head to the airport, not arriving at the arena until around seven that evening. Upon arrival, Cage went directly to medical for state athletic commission clearance and received treatment before changing and heading straight to the Gorilla Position without even seeing the locker room. While Cage lost the match to Knight, he made his presence felt as a member of the Don Callis Family. His latest AEW appearance came on May 30 during an episode of Collision, where he teamed with Jake Doyle to defeat Jimmy Wild and Tommy Mars.

MJF's recent injury has created some concern, though the AEW World Champion remains optimistic about his status. During the June 3 episode of AEW Dynamite, MJF defended his title against RUSH in a no count out match and won, but the victory came at a cost. The injury occurred when RUSH charged into the corner and hit MJF with a running dropkick. As the move connected, RUSH fell on MJF's knee, causing it to hyperextend as MJF pulled his leg inward while trying to brace himself. The hyperextension was significant enough that MJF was pulled from a scheduled Beyond Wrestling appearance two days later against Bobby Orlando, with Andrade El Idolo replacing him. However, MJF took to Instagram to provide an encouraging update on his recovery. Filming himself in a cold plunge tub, the champion confirmed the hyperextension and noted that by Tuesday, just days after the Wednesday injury, his inflammation had drastically gone down. He acknowledged significant pain and swelling remained but credited the cold plunge therapy with accelerating his recovery. MJF stated that without the treatment, he believed it would have taken much longer to feel like himself. The champion emphasized that avoiding time off is imperative for both his career and AEW as a company, mentioning the importance to ratings, ticket sales, and pay-per-view buys. While still dealing with discomfort, all signs point to MJF staying on track for his creative plans at AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door on June 28.

Skye Blue's recovery journey has been both physically and mentally challenging, but the AEW star has found solid footing in her comeback. Blue suffered a career-threatening broken ankle on July 20, 2024, breaking her fibula in an injury that required major surgery and nine months of recovery. The prospect of surgery terrified her initially, as she had never experienced an injury severe enough to require an operation. She initially hoped for alternative options like casting, but doctors insisted on surgical intervention. During her recovery process, Blue's partner Kyle Fletcher suggested she reach out to Adam Copeland, who had dealt with a similar injury. Rather than providing false reassurance, Copeland gave Blue an honest breakdown of what to expect. He explained everything from the pain immediately upon waking from surgery to the strange sensation of having metal placed inside her body. Blue recalled that Copeland warned her about the mental aspects of recovery as well, explaining that she might lose interest in wrestling while watching others continue their careers. He noted that the transition from being constantly active to complete rest would be jarring. Beyond the physical and mental warnings, Copeland suggested that Blue start reading, believing it would help her through the difficult times. Blue was initially skeptical, as she had not read books since school, simply skimming them for homework. However, as doom scrolling became overwhelming during her recovery, she decided to give reading a try after Kyle got her a Kindle. She discovered that she genuinely enjoyed it, finishing books quickly and finding it to be a welcome escape from her phone. Now Blue loves reading and credited Copeland not only for preparing her for surgery but for guiding her through the emotional challenges of a long injury layoff. Despite her serious injury, Blue remains determined to continue wrestling. When asked about her future during a recent interview, she expressed her desire to keep performing until her body no longer allows it, stating that she wants to do what she loves and make other people happy.

Sareee is preparing for her highly anticipated AEW debut this week, but the former WWE NXT star faces some lingering health concerns. The Japanese standout is scheduled to face Skye Blue in the first round of the Women's Owen Hart Foundation Tournament during AEW Dynamite Summer Blockbuster. However, Sareee has admitted that her neck has not completely recovered from her previous injury. In a recent interview, she revealed that her longtime tag team partner Takumi Iroha will accompany her to the United States as a precaution. AEW has approved the arrangement, with Iroha prepared to step in as a replacement if Sareee is unable to compete. Sareee and Iroha are tag team partners in a unit called Spark Rush and plan to get advice from wrestling legend Masahiro Chono during this period. Chono, who conducted the interview, advised caution to Sareee, recommending that she not rush her return. He stressed the importance of protecting her long-term career rather than risking further injury by returning before she is fully ready, noting that she has more than ten years left in her career. Despite the concerns surrounding her condition, Sareee remains scheduled for her debut match this week in the Owen Hart Cup tournament.

The Butcher, The Blade & Maclin Update

What's Next For The Butcher, The Blade, & Maclin?

As we know, competition is the key to the health of any industry, as it gives options for both the fans and the talent themselves. Within the past few years in particular, we've seen the ebb and flow of that within the professional wrestling business with the shifting dynamics of the industry.

Of course, the free agent market will always be a topic of discussion when there are those previously mentioned options for talent, and as each organization evolves, it can alter the path that talent take when they are testing the waters of free agency. It's nothing new and actually part of doing business as a pro wrestler since it becomes imperative to maximize your value as a talent to make as much money as possible during an in-ring career. Keep in mind, there are no pensions or retirement plans for those within sports entertainment. It can be a difficult task because when a talent thinks they are being underutilized, it's a risk to willingly ask for a contract release since it abandons the security of the weekly paycheck that a national contract brings with it. Obviously, it goes without saying that there are no guarantees of a better spot or better pay elsewhere.

On the other hand, if a wrestler becomes too complacent for too long and content to take the money just to hang out in catering if the company doesn't have anything for them, they could end up under the radar so long that it neutralizes their stock as a commodity. It's definitely a balancing act as far as when to go and where, assuming it will be the right overall career move.

Former Impact champion Steve Maclin requested his release from the organization, and Impact confirmed that the release was granted. The rumor mill suggests that Maclin was frustrated with his direction, as well as the structure of the company. That in itself doesn't necessarily mean that those who follow Impact should push the panic button, as it's not uncommon for a talent to be dissatisfied with the management of a promotion if the office doesn't have plans for them.

The puzzling aspect is that Maclin spent a few months working with Mike Santana before this, so it appeared at least on the surface that he was in the mix as one of the spotlighted wrestlers for Impact. Still, it's possible that Maclin thought he hit a ceiling in the promotion, and considering that he was a former world champion, maybe he had. The group debuted on AMC at the start of the year after the working agreement with WWE brought them more publicity than they had in a decade. Impact might be in danger of being typecast as a minor league feeder system for WWE rather than a destination of its own for talent. If Maclin saw the writing on the wall, that might've solidified the ceiling as far as how much progress he could make under the Impact banner.

At 39, Maclin has had a decent career, albeit under the radar through no fault of his own. Maclin originally inked a WWE developmental deal in 2014 and was under contract for almost seven years before he was cut in 2021. The fact that he was trained and developed through the WWE system, especially for that length of time, proves that he brought something to the table as a performer. Unfortunately, when he was brought to the main roster in early 2020, it was right after the COVID-19 pandemic shuttered the entire world, and the WWE television product that had to be filmed in an empty building was problematic. He was initially brought to Smackdown as a part of The Forgotten Sons stable, but a tweet from Ryker in the midst of civil unrest in the country at the time saw the faction dropped from television. In many ways, Maclin was a victim of circumstances during his WWE tenure.

🔥 WWE Wrestler Commentary and Reflections

WRESTLER COMMENTARY AND REFLECTIONS ACROSS WWE LANDSCAPE

Rob Van Dam recently reflected on his iconic 2006 ECW One Night Stand WWE Championship match against John Cena during a WWE Vault retrospective video. While praising Cena's awareness and physical strength, Van Dam offered candid thoughts about working with the Cenation leader. Van Dam described Cena's submission maneuver as particularly intense, noting that when Cena locked in his headlock with the opponent's feet pinned down on the stomach, there was virtually no room to escape or even scream due to the pressure. Van Dam joked about Cena being stiff when applying the hold, saying "I don't know if a lot of people will say this, but he's a little stiff when he put... I don't mind saying that." Van Dam specifically explained how difficult it was to endure the move, comparing it to having his body folded in half with no air left. Despite the physical intensity, Van Dam won the match with a Five-Star Frog Splash in front of the famously hostile ECW crowd at the Hammerstein Ballroom nearly 20 years ago, and the bout remains one of the defining moments of both men's careers.

Rey Mysterio admitted during an appearance on Going Ringside that his infamous WCW lawn dart spot involving Kevin Nash was actually his own idea. The moment happened on the July 29, 1996 episode of WCW Monday Nitro during the early days of the New World Order storyline, when Nash threw Mysterio headfirst into the side of a production trailer backstage. Mysterio explained that as a young wrestler at the time, he was focused on making every segment as memorable as possible without thinking much about the risks involved. "I actually offered to do that," Mysterio said. "When you're young, you don't think about repercussions, which is normal in our business. You just want to make things look good, and you're willing to give whatever it takes to make that segment, 30 seconds or five seconds, stand out." Mysterio never expected the spot would still be talked about 25 or 30 years later. The moment worked so well because WCW wanted the nWo to feel like a real threat rather than just another group of wrestling villains, and their attacks felt chaotic and dangerous.

Former WWE star Kaitlyn recalled her 2013 Payback match with AJ Lee, which she considers her favorite match ever despite losing her Divas Championship. The match meant so much to both competitors because of how far they had come together and all the historical accomplishments they had achieved together, including the first-ever women's contract signing. What stands out in Kaitlyn's memory is that she and AJ went over their allotted time on the pay-per-view, which is an unwritten rule never to do. With the crowd fully behind them, the referee kept telling them to go home, but AJ refused and said they should keep going. As a result, they cut into then-WWE Champion John Cena's main event time. When they got back into Gorilla, Cena was waiting there with his match still coming up. Kaitlyn recalled being terrified in that moment, but Cena was cool about it and said "Never do that again, but great match." The bout remains one of the most highly regarded matches of the Divas Championship era and launched AJ Lee's record-setting run at the top of the division.

Rob Van Dam revealed that the late Sabu carried a nail in his boot all the time for protection, not just as a one-time stunt for television. While discussing a spot from an important ECW World Television Championship match on April 4, 1998 that featured Van Dam, Bam Bam Bigelow, and Sabu, Van Dam explained that the nail wasn't a gimmick or part of a wrestling character or storyline. "Sabu carried a nail in his boot all the time for protection," Van Dam said. According to Van Dam, Sabu viewed it as a form of self-defense in case something didn't go his way and he needed to defend himself in the ring. Van Dam stressed that this was the same nail Sabu routinely kept with him. "That's what he just pulled out and stabbed him in the eye with. But he always had that, like even in a, he always, it wasn't a gimmick, it was something ever since we started, he always had the nail." The revelation aligns with Sabu's reputation as one of wrestling's most unpredictable performers, who blurred the line between preparation and punishment during his hardcore wrestling career.

Taz addressed long-running questions about his WWE outfit change, clarifying that the shirt-and-pants look was his own idea and not something forced upon him. As part of the campaign, Bella participated in a powerful photoshoot designed to illustrate the severe constraints of marine park captivity. One of the most striking images from the shoot showed Bella sitting inside a bathtub, serving as a visual comparison to demonstrate how confined these massive animals are in their concrete tanks. "When you look at these huge orcas that are in these tanks, it's like a human being living in a bathtub for their whole lives. These mammals can swim for 150 miles a day. How do you do that in here? "If you ask me about going to SeaWorld, I'm just going to tell you no. We are miseducating our children when we take them to places like that.
