🔥 Seth Rollins AAW Return

Seth Rollins made a surprise return to AAW Wrestling at the Berwyn Eagles Club after more than 15 years away, appearing at the "Crush & Destroy" event on Friday. The appearance was particularly meaningful as the show marked one of the final wrestling events at the historic venue, which is saying goodbye to professional wrestling after hosting wrestling shows for many years.

Rollins' early career was built in this venue. Between 2005 and 2010, he competed there under the name Tyler Black, accumulating 36 matches at the location. He became one of the promotion's top stars during this period, winning the AAW Heavyweight Championship twice and the Tag Team titles twice. His last match at the venue before this return was in 2010 at "Defining Moment," where he teamed with Jimmy Jacobs.

The decision to end wrestling programming at the venue came after an altercation between wrestlers and a fan at a show in March that included a fake stabbing incident. The Berwyn Police Department confirmed that no actual stabbing occurred and the case has been closed. However, the building announced it would not be booking any future wrestling events, though the venue still maintains its license.

When Rollins opened the show at "Crush & Destroy," he came out to his WWE theme music, but the crowd quickly acknowledged his past, chanting "Tyler Black" and bringing back memories from his early days. The night proved to be highly emotional for Rollins, who told the crowd he couldn't miss such a special moment and that the venue represented his "home."

In his address to the fans, Rollins spoke passionately about his love for wrestling and how places like the Berwyn Eagles Club helped build his career. He emphasized that his dream was not just about becoming a global star and wrestling at WrestleMania, but also about wrestling in rings filled with passionate fans. He specifically thanked AAW promoter Danny Daniels, calling him an important mentor in his early career.

Rollins also used the platform to promote his Black & Brave Wrestling Academy, which is located a few hours from the venue. He noted that some of his students were part of the show, framing the moment as passing the torch to the next generation of wrestlers. Looking back on his journey, he thanked the fans for supporting him everywhere and for allowing him to live out his childhood dream.

The last wrestling show at the Berwyn Eagles Club will be an indie lucha event on Sunday, April 26, marking the end of an era for an important venue in independent wrestling history.

Lucha Underground Return Teaser

Lucha Underground may be making a comeback. The official social media account for the promotion unexpectedly became active on April 24, 2026, posting a teaser video that immediately caught the attention of fans across the wrestling community. The clip features a lucha mask with the word "?Más?" flashing on the screen before the video ends. The post itself was simple but effective, captioned only with "Soon..."

The original Lucha Underground ran for four seasons on the El Rey Network from October 29, 2014 to November 7, 2018, building a cult following thanks to its unique presentation, cinematic storytelling, and blend of lucha libre with episodic drama. The promotion had amassed budget concerns by the release of the fourth season and never returned for another. Its stars, ranging from the likes of Ricochet, Rey Mysterio, JC Mateo, Johnny Mundo, Fenix, and Penta, have since moved on to other promotions, and the brand had long been considered a relic of the past.

That status changed with this latest teaser. The official X account had not posted anything since 2019, but the El Rey Network has been re-uploading content from Lucha Underground's previous seasons. According to reports, some comments had called for the library to be made public, and the account responded to say they were working on getting the library on YouTube.

The timing of this teaser is significant given recent developments. A trademark for "Lucha Underground" was filed by "Lucha Underground LLC" in January 2025 and went live earlier this year. The filing indicates no named holder of the filing, but an address is listed. Property records link the address to members of the Fogelman family. John Fogelman left William Morris Endeavor in 2011 to found FactoryMade Ventures, the entity which would later collaborate with Robert Rodriguez to found El Rey Network. Also linked to the address is Max Fogelman, a former Associate Producer with the El Rey Network having previously worked as Media Manager for Lucha Underground.

Adding more fuel to the fire, the official website is also up and running. LuchaUnderground.com currently displays "© 2026 LUCHA UNDERGROUND, LLC. ALL RIGHTS RESERVED." in its footer, suggesting that something active is happening behind the scenes.

Speculation has arisen over WWE's acquisition of AAA and whether this could be a WWE-revived project due to the association between AAA talent and Lucha Underground when it ran. However, AAA only loaned talent, wrestling expertise, and trademarks to Lucha Underground and had no ownership stake in the promotion.

With the trademark filing, active website, and now the social media teaser, it appears clear that something is in development. What exactly that project will entail remains to be seen, but fans and industry observers will be watching closely for further announcements.

KC Navarro TNA Return

KC Navarro appeared on Busted Open After Dark for an interview where he discussed his early return to in-ring competition and the support system that helped him recover from a significant injury.

Navarro explained the timeline of his recovery, noting that he was originally expected to be sidelined for a full year. Instead, he received medical clearance three to four months earlier than anticipated. "It was a long road, eight months. I was supposed to be out for a year, but I got cleared about three to four months early. I'm very blessed for that, it was a lot of hard work," Navarro said during the interview.

The TNA Wrestling star described the most challenging portion of his rehabilitation. According to Navarro, the difficulty intensified around the three-month mark of his recovery when he began walking again. He acknowledged that while he started to feel more like himself during this period, he recognized he still had significant ground to cover before reaching full physical recovery. "You start to feel normal but I'm also very far away from naturally coming back," he explained.

A significant part of Navarro's recovery journey involved meeting Elayna Black, a former NXT competitor. Black had previously dealt with a similar injury during her time in WWE's developmental brand. Navarro credited Black for her invaluable support and guidance throughout his rehabilitation process. "Around that time is when I met Elayna. She had a similar injury when she was in NXT, so to have somebody like that have my back and be able to counsel me through all this and have my back and just be there for me every step of the way, it's been a blessing man," Navarro stated.

Navarro expressed his gratitude for both Black's personal support and the reception he received from fans upon his return. He also highlighted his successful comeback match, noting that he defeated AJ Francis on his first Thursday back in competition. "I'm so grateful for her and I'm so grateful to be back. The crowd was there for me, everybody was happy I was back. I beat AJ Francis that Thursday, I'm on fire right now," Navarro concluded.

NOAH Naomichi Marufuji Seth Rollins Credit

Naomichi Marufuji of Pro Wrestling NOAH has publicly thanked Seth Rollins for crediting him with originating the Stomp finishing move. Rollins has been using the Stomp as his signature finishing move in WWE for over a decade, and it has become one of the most recognizable and popular finishing moves in the company.

Rollins previously discussed the origins of the move, explaining that he first used it against Tyson Kidd at a live event before his main roster call-up in 2012. However, Rollins has acknowledged in interviews that the move was made famous by Japanese wrestling legend Naomichi Marufuji, and he credited Marufuji for inspiring the maneuver.

Following Rollins' public acknowledgment of Marufuji's influence on the move, the NOAH star responded via social media. Marufuji expressed his gratitude, writing "Thank You Seth" followed by an emoji, and then pitched the idea of a future match between the two wrestlers, saying "Someday... we should have a match, right?"

WWE has maintained a working relationship with Pro Wrestling NOAH for several years. This partnership has included NXT talent traveling to the promotion to compete in the N1 Tournament, while established WWE stars like Shinsuke Nakamura and AJ Styles have made appearances for NOAH to face top competitors including the Great Muta and Marufuji respectively.

The timing of this interaction is particularly relevant given WWE's recent shift in policy. Under the current WWE regime, the company has significantly opened its borders to outside promotions in recent years. During WrestleMania 42 weekend alone, numerous WWE names competed at Josh Barnett's Bloodsport event, demonstrating the company's willingness to allow talent to work outside the organization.

WWE has been actively sending talent such as Tavion Heights and Josh Briggs to Pro Wrestling NOAH for developmental purposes. These overseas opportunities allow wrestlers to gain additional match experience, face different opponents, and perform in front of different crowds, all of which contribute to their overall development as performers.

Omos previously made appearances in Pro Wrestling NOAH last year, where he won the GHC Tag Team Championship before returning to WWE. He now competes on the AAA show as a member of El Ojo alongside Hijo del Vikingo and Dorian Roldan.

There are currently several WWE-contracted stars who receive limited television time that could potentially benefit from working in Pro Wrestling NOAH. A stint in Japan would allow underutilized performers to stay active and gain valuable experience while maintaining their WWE contracts.

Rollins was most recently in action at WrestleMania 42, where he faced GUNTHER on night one. His match resulted in a loss following interference from a returning Bron Breakker, continuing storyline developments in his WWE tenure.

Brooke Hogan Claims Hulk Hogan Documentary Missed ‘98%’ Of Reality

Brooke Hogan has voiced strong concerns about the portrayal of her father, Hulk Hogan, in the recent Netflix documentary. She suggested the project may have left out more than it revealed. Speaking on The Bubba the Love Sponge Show, Hogan reflected on the documentary's approach. She acknowledged its presentation while questioning its accuracy and depth.

Hogan stated that while the documentary captured the surface-level milestones of her father's wrestling career, it lacked critical real-life context. "It captured every single moment of his career… it was definitely a showcase piece," she said, before adding, "in my opinion it was missing about 98% of critical real life factual information." This stark assessment highlights her belief that the documentary took a narrow approach to telling her father's story.

She went further, explaining that the version presented on screen didn't align with her lived experience. "I feel like I was living in an alternate universe… I didn't realize this is how it went down," Hogan said, noting that certain elements were "glossed over," including what she described as key personal storylines. Her comments suggest a significant disconnect between what was shown and what actually happened behind the scenes.

Hogan also pointed to how familiar controversies were framed. "They acted like everything… was news to everybody," she said. "We knew about steroids and we knew about drugs… but there were many key things that tie in." This observation indicates that the documentary may have presented well-known issues as if they were revelations while missing important connective tissue that would provide fuller context.

The documentary has already drawn attention for its handling of Hogan's personal and professional history. Notably, Brooke Hogan herself did not appear in the film beyond archival audio, despite her central role in the Hogan family narrative during previous public projects. This absence raises questions about who was consulted and whose perspectives were prioritized in the production.

Hogan's remarks suggest that while the documentary may have pinned the highlights, it may not have fully covered the full match card of real-life events, leaving gaps that those closest to the story still question. Her perspective highlights the difference between a produced narrative and personal experience, two versions of the same story that don't always align. As the conversation around the documentary continues, her voice adds an important counterpoint from someone who lived through the events being documented.
