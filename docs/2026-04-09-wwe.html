🔥 Royce Keys WWE SmackDown Debut

Royce Keys' WWE Future Finally Revealed With SmackDown Debut Announcement

After weeks of uncertainty following his Royal Rumble appearance, WWE has officially announced that Royce Keys will make his SmackDown television debut on Friday, April 10, 2026. The announcement came through an official WWE statement on social media, finally providing direction to Keys' situation since his Royal Rumble debut where he disappeared from television.

Keys, formerly known as Powerhouse Hobbs during his six-year run in AEW, made his WWE debut in the men's Royal Rumble match at the 2026 event. Since then, he has been working dark matches but had not appeared on television, aside from clips shown from his interview with Stephanie McMahon on her "What's Your Story?" podcast. That interview discussed his life story and WWE goals, which include winning championship gold and eventually working for the company behind the scenes.

WWE posted a video along with a promotional message on Twitter/X that read: "I refuse to be defined by where I'm from, but instead by where I'm going. From East Palo Alto to the world stage, Royce Keys' journey has shaped him into the man he is today. Keys writes another chapter in his story when he makes his official SmackDown debut TOMORROW!!" The announcement emphasizes Keys' background and progression, making it clear this debut has been building behind the scenes.

In the promotional video, Keys provided personal insight into his upbringing and journey to professional wrestling. He said: "I'm Royce Keys. This is my city. A place where choice is a luxury. I was born in it. Gang violence, drugs, everything you can think of, ran through East Palo Alto. For me, fortunately I had two grandparents that weren't having that. My grandma made sure, the stuff outside, you weren't bringing in the house. The main thing was love in that house. The Royal Rumbles we had here, we had to throw each other over this fence. We didn't have a lot, but we had each other. My grandma allowed us to dream big. She showed us a world beyond where we were from. Wrestling brought me so much happiness. I knew I wanted to do this. Not a lot of people can make it out of here. I just wanna do my part. This is all I ever wanted to do, this is all I know, this is what brings me peace, this is what motivates me. This is it. I refuse to be defined by where I'm from – but instead, by where I'm going."

The timing of the debut is particularly notable given that the show is being held in San Jose, California, near where Keys grew up in East Palo Alto. This represents a homecoming of sorts for the former AEW star. The event falls on the second-to-last episode of SmackDown before WrestleMania 42, which is just over one week away.

Since his Royal Rumble debut, Keys has been quite active behind the scenes. According to records, he has competed in and won nine dark matches across Raw and SmackDown tapings since February 13. During these dark matches, Keys has picked up victories over both Chris Sabin and Alex Shelley of the Motor City Machine Guns, Rey Fenix, Berto, Rayo Americano, and most recently, Brutus Creed. At the time of the announcements, it had not yet been confirmed who Keys will face in his televised SmackDown debut.

During his Royal Rumble match, Keys eliminated Damian Priest, and it appeared that WWE could be setting up a program between them, though it remains unknown if the company will still pursue that direction.

The timing of Keys' WWE debut is somewhat surprising given that WrestleMania 42 is now just over one week away. The longer the wait went on, it seemed likelier that WWE would reintroduce Keys after WrestleMania, with the Raw and SmackDown after the event typically serving as a time for big debuts and returns. While WWE booking Keys to debut now is a curious decision, it seems it will probably be too late for the former AEW TNT Champion to factor into WrestleMania 42 plans in any meaningful way.

What appears more likely is Keys participating in the Andre the Giant Battle Royal, which typically happens on the SmackDown before WrestleMania. While the line-up for that match is not yet known, Keys seems like one of the front-runners as the company will want to give him a big win to kickstart his WWE career.

The Friday, April 10 episode of SmackDown in San Jose will feature several other significant storylines and appearances. Cody Rhodes will respond to Pat McAfee and Randy Orton aligning ahead of WrestleMania, after Orton and McAfee unleashed a shocking attack on the Undisputed WWE Champion the previous week. Drew McIntyre has vowed to expose the "real" Jacob Fatu ahead of their Unsanctioned Match at WrestleMania. Additionally, Sami Zayn will react to Trick Williams after the U.S. Champion was attacked by his scheduled WrestleMania title challenger last week.

Keys signed with WWE shortly after wrapping up his six-year AEW run in January. His signing represented a significant move in professional wrestling, bringing another major AEW talent to the WWE roster. The company appears to be taking a measured approach to his introduction, building him through dark matches before finally presenting him on television.

🔥 Liv Morgan Royal Rumble 2026 Win and Character

Liv Morgan has opened up about her 2026 WWE Women's Royal Rumble win on January 31, marking a significant milestone in her career. Year after year since the Women's Royal Rumble was first introduced in 2018, Morgan entered the match but left empty handed. That changed this year when, unlike 2023 and 2024 which saw her finish as the runner-up overall, Morgan managed to eliminate Tiffany Stratton to win her first Royal Rumble.

Speaking with Chris Van Vliet on Insight, Morgan opened up about what her big win meant to her personally after coming close on so many occasions. She noted that she has been in every single Royal Rumble since they started doing these in 2018, adding that herself and Natalya are the only women that can say that at this point. Morgan has had every position in that match, coming out first and last, experiencing the fastest elimination time and the shortest elimination time.

To finally achieve the ultimate goal, Morgan stated: "To finally be the bride and not the bridesmaid this year, and not only that, but to solidify myself a WrestleMania spot where I was able to pick a champion of my choosing. None of that is lost on me. Even 11 years in, none of that is lost on me. So I don't know, I kind of felt like how I felt when I won the Money in the Bank match. Just kind of like, overwhelming awe and gratitude and it just being so surreal that all I can do was just look out into the audience, just because I wanted to really remember that feeling and try to, like, I said, just be really present in the moment. So it was a dream come true."

However, Morgan's WWE WrestleMania 42 status has come up in the air. There are fears among fans that the Judgment Day star could be forced to wait even longer for her big moment. On Monday's episode of WWE Raw in April, Stephanie Vaquer jumped Morgan from behind during a backstage segment, leading to Liv and Judgment Day stablemate Roxanne Perez to clash heads. Morgan and Perez both sported large bumps on their heads as a result of the incident, and it was reported that the pair were put into concussion protocol. As of the time of reporting, Morgan vs. Vaquer was listed as a "wait and see," with unclear plans regarding what WWE would do in the event that the match cannot go ahead.

During her appearance on the Chris Van Vliet podcast, Morgan also discussed her on-screen chemistry with Dominik Mysterio at length. Morgan and Mysterio have officially been together as an on-screen couple since mid-2024, when Mysterio betrayed his then-partner Rhea Ripley to side with Morgan. The pair have been together since then, a move that has led to both of them becoming bigger stars and more popular among WWE fans despite technically being heels the entire time.

Discussing their pairing, Morgan stated: "I think, ultimately, we just understand the assignment. I feel like the fans love to hate us, or they hate to love us. It's such an intriguing, complex, messy, wholesome love story that we haven't seen for a while, and I feel like the audience really loves that. I think, honestly, we're just iconic. I know I say that word a lot, but we're just iconic together. I think we are the greatest couple in the history of WWE. I'm not just saying that. I mean that wholeheartedly. We're better than Becky and Seth, we're better than AJ and CM Punk. We're better than Macho Man and Miss Elizabeth. I stand on that."

Morgan continued by explaining how their partnership has influenced WWE's approach to couples: "I feel like we've resurged couples in WWE. What couples were couples before Dominik and I? Now you have tons of people coupling up, trying to do what we do, but they just can't." When discussing their on-screen kiss that aired on WWE programming, Morgan reflected on the moment with enthusiasm: "Yes, I did, but also watching it back, hit a different level cause I was so in it. But also I could hear, but also it was an open stadium so the noise leaves as well. So you kinda don't realize how much of a ruckus it is until you kinda get to watch it back. So, I watched it back and was just like 'holy sh*t, holy sh*t it was so cool'. It was so funny and I even saw Stephanie McMahon's kids just shocked, just like 'what the hell?'. It was super funny just seeing everyone's reactions and their shock or their hatred for it."

In other interview segments, Morgan addressed her surprising return at WWE Survivor Series 2025 in San Diego. After suffering a shoulder injury in June 2025, Morgan made her surprise return five months later. She arrived during Dominik Mysterio and John Cena's Intercontinental Title Match. Morgan at first seemed to be turning her back on her on-screen boyfriend Mysterio after she slapped him and jumped into the unknowing arms of Cena. However, in a moment that was captured by WWE with a close up shot of Morgan's face, her happy expression quickly turned sinister as she stared coldly into the distance before jumping out of Cena's arms and kicked the 17-time world champion in the groin.

During her appearance on Insight, Morgan revealed she isn't sure how she pulled the expression on her face in that moment, but feels it was iconic. Morgan said: "I don't even know how I did that. My eyes, I was like, Whoa, dilated. I must have tapped into something right there. I don't know. But yeah, it was so iconic, and I couldn't have written a better return, and I mean that so wholeheartedly. I couldn't, first of all, I would not even have come up with that myself that was so much more grand and special to me than anything I could have thought of myself. So I was just so happy and honored."

Reflecting on her shoulder injury, which took place during the opening stages of a match with Kairi Sane on June 16 WWE Raw, Morgan recalled: "Yeah, I just took a weird fall, tried to brace myself, which is stupid. As soon as it happened, I knew, just because you can't feel anything. But first of all, it's like such an intense pain, then it just goes away, and then you can't feel anything because your shoulder is just not connected. So yeah, I knew. I rolled out the ring. I tried to pop it back in. Medical came and tried to pop it back in, and it was kind of just like, I can't continue, I can't do anything else. So I had to throw in the towel, which I hated. I think I tried to be really optimistic until my MRI news, I was like, I think I'm okay, but I had done the same thing two years prior. So I knew it wasn't okay, but I didn't really want to speak it into existence."

Additionally, Morgan revealed some rejected ring names she pitched while in NXT, as well as another name she nearly ended up with. Generally, the process for new WWE signings in the NXT system is they're instructed to come up with a list of names and management will then pick one from the list to go with. However, with Morgan, she wasn't having much success with any of the suggestions she was making and was ultimately given the choice between two names: Liv Morgan or Liv Gallow.

Speaking on Insight with Chris Van Vliet, she spoke about the process and how she didn't want a real human name. Morgan stated: "I walked into work one day and they were like, 'Okay, you're either Liv Morgan or Liv Gallow, pick.' I was like, 'Oh.' I had known we had Luke Gallows at some point in time there. He wasn't employed in that moment in time when I was there I don't think, but I had known of him and his history. So I was like, 'I'll be Liv Morgan,' and that was really it. I never really liked Morgan. Now I like it. But I remember, first time signing an autograph, I was like, 'I'm just gonna sign Liv,' and that's all I've done, that's just stayed with me my whole career. It was only because I didn't like Morgan."

Morgan explained her preferred options: "I wanted a name that was obviously not a real human name. I wanted to be Illy Milly. That was my number one, I wanted to be Illy Milly really, really bad. Or I remember I pitched the name Penny Wap, because I loved Fetty Wap. And they just were like, 'No, bitch.' All my names pretty much were denied. I sent in like two and a half lists of names, and they just were like, 'No, no, no, no, no.' And so I finally walked in and they were like, 'These are your two options, pick one.' And I was just like, 'Okay.' But I love it now. Liv is very punny. There's lots of room to play with the name Liv."

While there's definitely a place for gimmick names in WWE, especially now when there are so few of them, they can be somewhat limiting, especially when they're mainly driven by comedy. Morgan has gone on to become a multi-time champion including a reign as Women's World Champion, as well as other accolades like winning the Royal Rumble. While Liv Morgan is just a regular name and isn't particularly inspiring in and of itself, there's no doubt she can be taken more seriously at a high level with that name than she would have been if something like Illy Milly had gone through.

Both women were immediately checked on by WWE medical and placed into concussion protocol following the incident.

The initial outlook created serious concern among fans and WWE officials alike. Bryan Alvarez reported on Wrestling Observer Live that if WrestleMania 42 had been that weekend, the match between Liv and Stephanie Vaquer probably would not have happened. Alvarez emphasized just how serious the situation looked in the moment while stressing the unpredictability of concussion recovery timelines. He stated that it was a "wait and see" situation with roughly two weeks until the event.

However, the mood within WWE shifted dramatically in the days following the incident. According to reports from WrestleVotes Radio, there emerged a strong sense of optimism within WWE that both women would be cleared in time for the show. This represented a major change from the uncertainty that initially surrounded their status. The report made clear there was no internal heat on Stephanie Vaquer for her role in the accident, with the collision being viewed internally as an unfortunate accident rather than the result of negligence or recklessness.

Liv Morgan was shown in attendance at a San Antonio Spurs basketball game on Wednesday, April 9, appearing with Raquel Rodriguez. Photos shared on social media by both Morgan and Perez revealed gruesome swelling on their foreheads as a result of the head collision, providing visual evidence of the severity of the impact.

Liv Morgan remained scheduled to challenge Stephanie Vaquer for the WWE Women's World Championship at WrestleMania 42, which took place on April 18 and April 19 from Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas, Nevada. Roxanne Perez's status was equally important given her involvement in the situation and overall positioning heading into the show.

With concussion protocol being notoriously unpredictable, nothing was guaranteed to be certain. However, WWE's reported belief that there remained enough time for both Morgan and Perez to be medically cleared kept one of the company's key stories intact heading into their flagship annual event.

The incident also highlighted the broader context of Liv Morgan's recent injury history. Morgan had suffered a serious shoulder injury in June 2025 during a match against Kairi Sane when she took an awkward fall and attempted to brace herself. The injury resulted in a dislocated shoulder that kept her out of action for several months. Morgan later revealed on Insight With Chris Van Vliet that she knew immediately the injury was serious because of intense pain followed by complete numbness, indicating her shoulder was dislocated. While initially trying to remain optimistic, she had dislocated the same shoulder two years prior, which gave her a sense of what was actually wrong despite not wanting to speak it into existence.

Upon being medically cleared from the shoulder injury, Morgan returned to WWE programming at Survivor Series where she helped Dominik Mysterio regain the WWE Intercontinental Championship through a surprising segment where she initially appeared to turn on him by smacking him across the face before revealing it was a ruse and attacking John Cena instead. Morgan later described this return as iconic and more special than anything she could have conceived herself.

WWE submitted an application to the Nevada Governor's Office of Economic Development stating that the total budget for producing both nights of WrestleMania 42, along with RAW and SmackDown at T-Mobile Arena, amounts to $35,577,015, which is higher than last year's $33,693,464.

The tax credit is slightly more than the $4,240,456 approved for the promotion last year, even though last year's budget was lower. The Nevada Governor's Office of Economic Development focuses on bringing in film and entertainment business to the state. The Director of Communications Carli Smith confirmed that they accepted WWE's application and explained the audit process involved.

"Their actual tax credit amount will be determined by an independent audit based on their actual spend," Smith said, "but it cannot be more than the $4,314,821." This means the approved figure represents the maximum available to WWE, with the final amount to be determined after the weekend events conclude.

The GOED allowed WWE to redact the stadium fee and talent budget from the public record of their application. The agency also accepted the promotion's request to seal a required script, withholding it under an exception as proprietary information. However, WWE did not include the Hall of Fame ceremony and Tony Hinchcliffe's special WrestleMania edition of his podcast, both of which will take place at the Dolby Live theater, in the documents.
