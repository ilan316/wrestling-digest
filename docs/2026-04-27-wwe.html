🔥 WWE April 24 Roster Cuts

WWE CONDUCTS MAJOR ROSTER PURGE ON APRIL 24, 2026

WWE's post-WrestleMania 42 roster cuts sent shockwaves through the wrestling community on April 24, 2026, when the company released 25 Superstars and developmental talents. The departures, announced around 5:00 PM ET on Friday, represented one of the largest waves of releases in recent memory and sparked immediate discussion about creative decisions, business strategy, and the human cost of WWE's talent management practices.

PRIMARY REASONS FOR THE CUTS

WWE sources indicated that the majority of releases were made to create space for incoming talent, a strategic roster reshuffling common in the wrestling industry. Additionally, the company was in anticipation of a possible reduction of WWE SmackDown to a two-hour format in the near future, which would require fewer wrestlers on the active roster. While TKO Group Holdings, WWE's parent company, became a focal point of speculation regarding responsibility for the cuts, WWE sources clarified that Paul "Triple H" Levesque, WWE President Nick Khan, and WWE Talent Relations were ultimately the primary decision-makers behind the departures.

The release process itself revealed the tension surrounding the cuts. Talent throughout the week had heavily speculated about potential departures as Friday approached, with several wrestlers beginning to learn around or before 5:00 PM ET that they had either been released or that their contracts would not be extended. By approximately 5:20 PM ET, major wrestling news outlets began confirming releases after reaching out to WWE earlier in the day without receiving a response. An internal email was also sent to select departments informing them of the situation.

THE KAIRI SANE CONTROVERSY

Among the most shocking and controversial releases was Kairi Sane, who had been actively featured on WWE programming. Before leaving WWE, Sane was in the middle of a significant storyline with Asuka and IYO Sky, in which she was being bullied at the hands of The Empress of Tomorrow, with Sky stepping up for her. This angle had not reached any meaningful payoff or conclusion when Sane's release was announced.

There was heavy internal frustration regarding Kairi Sane being cut before any real payoff to her storyline with Asuka and IYO Sky was made. Sources indicated significant shock surrounding the decision, as Sane had been actively involved in this angle alongside two of WWE's most prominent female competitors. She is widely expected to have strong interest from multiple promotions moving forward.

Sane's departure sparked an emotional response from her stablemate IYO Sky, who took to social media to express her feelings. Sky acknowledged that Sane had overcome numerous hardships while in America and was one of the most beloved members of the locker room. She described Sane as "kinder than anyone, a little air-headed in the most endearing way, funny, charming, and my proud best friend," adding that they had faced countless hardships together and grown as individuals. Despite the unexpected nature of the cut, Sky expressed confidence that Sane would find success elsewhere, stating that with her innate talent and the incredible knowledge and experience gained in WWE, Kairi's new journey would be something truly amazing.

The fan reaction was equally passionate, with the hashtag WeWantKairi trending worldwide on social media platforms, even cracking the top five in the sports category on X. Fans launched a petition on Change.org aimed at WWE leadership to reconsider the decision, emphasizing that her return could add depth to ongoing storylines while helping attract additional viewers. Asuka publicly showed support for Sane following her departure, posting "No matter what, I'll always be in your corner, Kairi. I'll never leave you feeling alone."

WWE backstage interviewer Jackie Redmond openly supported Sane by joining the WeWantKairi movement in a post and agreed with IYO Sky's statement that Sane is one of the nicest people anyone could meet. However, Redmond subsequently deleted both posts.

THE WYATT SICKS RELEASES

The entire Wyatt Sicks faction was released as part of the cuts, including Nikki Cross, Bo Dallas (Uncle Howdy), Erick Rowan, Dexter Lumis, and Joe Gacy. This was particularly shocking given the group's high-profile debut and significant creative investment in their storyline. The faction had made notable appearances at major events including WrestleMania and international shows, and members felt a spiritual connection to the late Bray Wyatt during their performances.

Nikki Cross, who portrayed the live-action Abby the Witch character inspired by Bray Wyatt's Firefly Funhouse persona, released an extended statement reflecting on her time with the group. She called her role "the honour of a lifetime" and expressed deep gratitude for the experience, writing about the pride she felt when people referred to the group as "the Wyatts" backstage. She thanked the Windham and Huber families and noted that every night they performed, it felt like Bray and Brodie Lee were there with their hands on the group's shoulders. Cross described the emotional weight of the journey and her love for her stablemates, acknowledging that while she was struggling to find the right words, she would treasure the experience always.

Dexter Lumis also posted a heartfelt message to his fellow Wyatt Sicks members, stating that he loved each and every member of the group and would not look back with any regrets. He referenced the connection the group felt to Bray Wyatt's presence during their entrances and closed with a CM Punk quote: "If your dreams don't scare the sh*t out of you, you need bigger dreams."

Mika Rotunda, the sister of Bo Dallas and late Bray Wyatt, reacted to the releases by posting a lengthy quote from Bray Wyatt about wrestling's nature, concluding with "You will never meet a sister more proud than me. Ever ever ever ever ever. Ever."

OTHER NOTABLE RELEASES

Aleister Black was released despite being nearly a year into his return to WWE, shocking many observers. Zelina Vega, who had held the Women's United States Championship just last year, was also among the departed. Vega revealed on Twitch that the call to be cut came from TKO, not WWE, and because the call came from the parent company, she knew it was dire before answering. She expressed frustration that outlets reported her release before she could announce it herself, but thanked all fans who have stood by her since 2020. Vega noted that she had plans for her character that she felt would have been well-received had they been given the opportunity to materialize.

Chris Sabin and Alex Shelley of the Motor City Machine Guns were released, prompting an emotional tribute from Nathan Frazer. Frazer described the MCMG as one of his biggest inspirations as a tag team and praised them as brothers who made him feel at home during his main roster call-up. He wrote extensively about the personal connection formed between the groups and how the MCMG adopted him and his tag team on the road, describing the experience as among the best times of his entire life.

Apollo Crews, who had been with WWE for 11 years, was released as part of the cuts. Crews posted a statement thanking WWE for the opportunity and reflecting on his lengthy tenure. He expressed gratitude for the messages and texts he received and noted that throughout his career, he was fortunate to suffer only one serious injury. He concluded by saying "All things come to an end. This is my time," emphasizing his acceptance of the situation while looking forward to what's next.

CARLEE BRIGHT AND NXT CUTS

Carlee Bright, an NXT talent who did not appear on the initial list of releases, later announced her departure via Instagram on Sunday. She revealed that her WWE journey had officially come to an end just two days before her appearance on WWE LFG Season 3. Bright signed with WWE in November 2022 and made her in-ring debut at a live event in September 2023, with her television debut on NXT Level Up in January 2024. She had been wrestling regularly on WWE EVOLVE prior to her release.

The inclusion of Tyra Mae Steele, the Season 1 winner of WWE LFG, among those released sparked internal questions within NXT about the show's long-term purpose. People in NXT began questioning what the purpose of WWE LFG is if the winner is just going to be cut. Despite this backlash and uncertainty, WWE LFG was set to continue with its season premiere as part of the revived "Superstar Sunday" block of original programming on A&E.

CONTRACT DETAILS AND NOTICE PERIODS

It's important to note that many wrestlers were not technically released outright but rather had their contracts not extended. For those under contract during the termination period, main roster talent remain under contract for a 90-day right of termination notice, while NXT talent are subject to a 30-day notice. In cases of contract expirations, deals simply conclude at their agreed-upon end dates. This distinction matters legally and financially for the affected wrestlers.

The company had previously made controversial decisions to re-sign talent only to not utilize them on the roster. Santos Escobar, for example, was re-signed last year but was never used again on the main roster and remains sidelined with an injury expected to keep him out for several months. This practice, which runs contrary to previous WWE policy, has occurred more frequently in recent years, with talents such as Karl Anderson, BJ Ray, and Ridge Holland being released or not retained while awaiting medical clearance.

INTERNAL AND EXTERNAL REACTIONS

The reaction to these cuts extended throughout WWE's roster. Rhea Ripley, the newly crowned WWE Women's Champion, took to social media to express her feelings, writing "Today's been a very sad day... Absolutely guttered for my friends and comrades. Worst time of the year. Go prove them all wrong." Ripley emphasized support for those departing while acknowledging the emotional toll such cuts take.

Trick Williams admitted his frustration with the releases during an Instagram Live session, expressing that he hates all releases in general when asked about Apollo Crews' departure. He stated "All the brothers and sisters leaving," indicating the personal nature of these departures for the active roster.

Chelsea Green and Piper Niven both commented on their stablemate Alba Fyre being released. Piper Niven posted a tribute referring to Fyre as a "true pioneer and soon to be remembered as one of the greatest to ever do it." Green focused on Fyre's potential, writing "I know you saw bits & pieces, but trust me, there's so much more to her. She's a fearless wrestler... I'm sad to not have her on my side, but this is just the beginning for my girl."

THE BUSINESS RATIONALE

The overarching business strategy appears designed to refresh WWE's roster with new talent while maintaining programming quality. Several NXT cuts were made specifically because new Performance Center signings were expected to be announced soon. The timing after WrestleMania 42, traditionally a period when WWE conducts major business evaluations, reflects the company's annual evaluation and restructuring cycle.

However, the execution raised questions about storytelling priorities and creative planning. The release of wrestlers actively involved in ongoing angles like Kairi Sane created narrative problems that will require creative solutions or complete pivots from original plans. This pattern of prioritizing roster restructuring over creative continuity represents an ongoing tension in WWE's business model, where financial and roster management considerations frequently override narrative considerations that might serve the long-term interests of its programming.

🔥 WrestleMania 42 Coverage and Fallout

WRESTLEMANIA 42 COVERAGE AND FALLOUT

WrestleMania 42 concluded successfully at Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas on April 18-19, delivering major storyline developments and surprising business metrics heading into the next chapter of WWE programming.

LIV MORGAN RECLAIMS WOMEN'S WORLD CHAMPIONSHIP

Liv Morgan successfully defeated Stephanie Vaquer at WrestleMania 42 to reclaim the Women's World Championship in a match that drew significant fan attention despite its shorter-than-expected duration. The bout featured major moments including interference from Roxanne Perez and Morgan sealing the victory after sending Vaquer into the steel steps before delivering her finisher. Morgan even performed her own entrance music live during the segment, making it a significant WrestleMania moment despite the time constraints.

Initial reports had suggested that both Morgan and Vaquer were dealing with injuries heading into the event, leading to speculation that WWE shortened the match to protect both competitors. However, new information revealed that scheduling concerns, not health issues, drove the decision. According to reports from Fightful Select, the match was shortened due to strict production timing on the show rather than medical precautions. Morgan wrestled a longer 12-minute match shortly after WrestleMania, which contradicted the injury narrative. The reality appears much simpler: everyone involved was working within fixed time limits established by WWE production. Multiple women's matches ran shorter than expected at the event for the same reason, frustrating some observers who felt the talent deserved more time on wrestling's biggest stage.

PAIGE RETURNS TO ACTION, WINS WOMEN'S TAG TEAM CHAMPIONSHIP

In a major surprise return, Paige competed at WrestleMania 42 and won the Women's Tag Team Championship. Following her victory, Paige sat down with Stephanie McMahon for an interview series called "What's Your Story?" and opened up extensively about her tumultuous past in WWE and her personal struggles during her first run with the company.

Paige became champion at just 21 years old but faced significant challenges in the years that followed. In 2016, she was suspended twice for Wellness Policy violations after testing positive for an illegal substance. She underwent neck surgery that same year and became involved in an unhealthy relationship with Alberto Del Rio. Adding to these difficulties, private explicit videos of her from when she was 19 years old were leaked online.

During her interview, Paige reflected on that dark period: "You know, I got into a bit of trouble when I was younger. You know, like, started drinking and partying with the wrong people. Then I ended up having a certain relationship that wasn't the healthiest. I kind of relied on the drugs when it comes to that too. Then on top of that, these tapes get released from when I'm 19 years old."

Paige acknowledged that she found the situation humiliating and devastating, though she noted that humor became a defense mechanism for dealing with the trauma. McMahon validated this approach, explaining it was a survival tactic. Paige recalled that WWE consistently offered support and asked her to open up about her situation, but she refused their help at the time.

"I was like, 'I'm fine, I'm fine.' And you guys even flew me out to Connecticut and sat down with me and was just like, 'You have to talk to us. You have to let us help you.' I'm like, 'I'm fine.' You did this whole medical checkup on me. Because I got really skinny. I had mats in my hair, and I remember I had to put my arms on the wall so they could see like the bones in my back and stuff just to see. They were like, 'We want to help you but we can't unless you want us to.' And I'm just like, 'I'm fine,'" she explained.

Close friends Amy Lee and Renee Paquette became concerned enough to develop a running joke about the severity of her situation, with one of them asking: "Which one of us is gonna have to rescue her from the trunk of a car?" McMahon described this as "ribbing on the square," a wrestling term for wrapping legitimate concerns in jokingly framed language. Paquette visited Paige after a 2017 altercation with El Patron and expressed her deep worry about the situation.

Paige isolated herself completely during this period, believing that everyone around her hated her and that she was alone. The leaked videos proved to be the breaking point. She left home and ran as far as she could, deciding that she would call her father with the intention that if he could not accept what had happened, there would be no way forward.

"I'm sick. I'm like, 'Oh my gosh. People can see me,' and made mistakes in these videos and stuff I would never do. It's my private life. And I was young, I was like 19 years old. I was like, 'If my dad doesn't accept me for this, I'm gonna kill myself.' That was it, you know, I was like, 'I can't be here anymore,'" she said. Paige credited her father with saving her life during this critical moment, as his acceptance and support proved to be the turning point that allowed her to begin healing and eventually find her way back to professional wrestling.

MERCHANDISE SALES REVEAL TOP PERFORMERS

Merchandise sales inside Allegiant Stadium during WrestleMania 42 weekend provided interesting insights into which performers resonated most strongly with fans. The figures only reflected Superstar-specific items and did not include general WWE, WrestleMania, or replica title merchandise.

CM Punk led all performers in stadium merchandise sales, continuing a trend that has followed him throughout his entire WWE return run since late 2023. Punk headlined Night 2 of WrestleMania for the first time in his career, facing World Heavyweight Champion Roman Reigns for the title, which gave him major visibility during the biggest weekend of the year.

Cody Rhodes finished in second place on the merchandise sales list. Rhodes main evented his fourth consecutive WrestleMania and has consistently been one of WWE's top merchandise sellers for several years, making his placement near the top far from surprising.

The biggest surprise came at number three, where Danhausen landed among WWE's top merchandise movers. His placement might have seemed unlikely just a couple of months earlier, but his popularity quickly manifested in tangible sales numbers during WrestleMania weekend. WWE officials were reportedly caught off guard by how strong Danhausen's sales turned out to be, with multiple restocks reportedly necessary to keep up with demand. His strong performance even led to WWE adding him to the Fanatics Fest following WrestleMania.

WRESTLERS ADDRESS WRESTLEMANIA EXPERIENCE

Several wrestlers provided candid interviews about their WrestleMania experiences in the days following the event.

Carmelo Hayes opened up about losing the WWE United States Championship to Sami Zayn just three weeks before WrestleMania 42. Hayes had an impressive 98-day championship run that saw him successfully defend the title eight times and frequently deliver match-of-the-night performances against high-caliber opponents including Ilja Dragunov, Shinsuke Nakamura, Rey Fenix, and external talent. However, WWE booked him to drop the title before WrestleMania, and a subsequent storyline knee injury forced him to sit out of the event, marking the second consecutive year he missed the Showcase of the Immortals.

Speaking with Michael Fairman, Hayes acknowledged his disappointment: "I mean, it sucked. It definitely sucked and it was heartbreaking and it felt like I ran the race. I was so close to just throwing my arms up and crossing the finish line and then I got kind of sidestepped a little bit. Yeah it sucks."

However, Hayes maintained a professional perspective on the decision: "Listen I'm a pro. At the end of the day I'm a pro. I show up to work, what they need from me I do it and I go above and beyond no matter what every time. It's one of those things, you got to charge it to the game. It wasn't a personal thing right? It wasn't a me thing. It was whatever it needed to be, whatever that was and whatnot. It's not something that I'm going to sit here and go, 'Oh, woe is me.' I don't move like that. I had a day or two where I was like, 'What the hell?' And then after that I was like, 'Back to work man.' Just a chip on my shoulder and watch what's going to happen."

Hayes reflected on his championship run as evidence of his potential when given the opportunity: "When you think about my journey on the main roster, where I had to kind of start low. I start high and then just all over the place and kind of get in where I fit in. And I finally found my footing, and really all it came down to was just them giving me the ball. That was it. All I needed was the ball and I just needed them to say, 'Alright here, run.' And that was what I did. I ran and nobody could catch me and they had to take the ball away from me. But that's all I needed and that's all I'll ever need is just the ball and the opportunity."

Tiffany Stratton did not compete at WrestleMania 42, which proved to be a significant disappointment given her status as the defending Women's Champion from the previous year. There had been rumors that a WWE Women's United States Title three-way match involving Stratton, Giulia, and Jordynne Grace was in development for the Las Vegas event. Instead, Stratton moved forward and defeated Giulia for the newly contested Women's United States Championship on the SmackDown episode following WrestleMania 42, which aired on April 24.

During an interview with Joey Karni on "Unseen," Stratton addressed her WrestleMania absence and discussed her approach to setbacks: "I feel like my mindset has always been controlling what I can control which is making the most out of all my segments, making the most out of my matches, making sure I'm delivering on everything and having really good matches with everybody. Unfortunately being on WrestleMania wasn't on the cards for me this year. However, that just kind of leaves me with a chip on my shoulder for next year and I'm going to do everything in my power to never let that happen ever again."

Stratton viewed the situation through a longer-term lens, believing that everything happens for a reason: "Honestly, I'm only 26 years old. I have so much time left in this business to have all of those accolades like you said at such a young age. I feel just so blessed. I feel like I got into this business at the perfect time. I was the perfect age and the cards have always just kind of fell into place for me. I've always worked hard, don't get me wrong. But I've just had very good timing with everything and I fully believe everything happens for a reason. Me not being on this year's Mania is setting me up for something bigger. Whether that's SummerSlam in Minnesota this year and fighting for the WWE Women's Championship, or main eventing next year's WrestleMania. I fully believe everything happens for a reason."

JADE CARGILL ABSENCE EXPLAINED

Jade Cargill's non-appearance on the SmackDown episode that aired on April 24 following WrestleMania 42 initially sparked speculation about her status after losing the WWE Women's Championship to Rhea Ripley at the event. However, the situation was straightforward: Cargill had requested time off from WWE well in advance to attend a personal family commitment in Cincinnati, Ohio. She was in the city to support her husband, former Major League Baseball player Brandon Phillips, as he was inducted into the Cincinnati Reds Hall of Fame. Following Cargill's title loss, Ripley immediately kicked off a new direction on SmackDown, becoming entangled in a developing rivalry with Jacy Jayne and Fatal Influence, who had been recently called up from NXT for the post-WrestleMania edition of the show.

Triple H revealed the official poster for Backlash spotlighting Roman Reigns prominently, strongly hinting that he will play a key role at the event. The promotional material for that event features a stacked lineup of top WWE Superstars including Cody Rhodes, Roman Reigns, Becky Lynch, Liv Morgan, Oba Femi, Trick Williams, and Rhea Ripley, signaling another major international showcase on the horizon.

MISSING PERSON SEARCH

A concerning situation emerged following WrestleMania 42 weekend when Marc Izard, a wrestling fan from the United Kingdom, was reported missing after traveling to Las Vegas for the event. A missing person alert was circulated online in hopes of locating him. The situation gained significant attention after WWE veteran Natalya Neidhart shared the alert on her social media accounts, amplifying the message to a wider audience and lending her platform to the search effort.

According to the information provided, Izard was last seen leaving the April 17 episode of WWE SmackDown at T-Mobile Arena and was noted as using a walking stick at the time of his last sighting. His last photo was taken with Natalya at her signing appearance on April 16. Natalya posted: "A member of our wrestling family, Marc Izard, is currently missing after WrestleMania weekend in Las Vegas. His last known sighting was leaving the arena after SmackDown on April 17, his last photo taken was with me at my signing. Marc traveled from the UK and has not made it home or been in contact since. If you have any information at all, please reach out to the email listed in the poster as well as Las Vegas Metro Police Department. Let's come together and help bring Marc home safely."

The missing person alert included a contact email for tips: Cravedetest@hotmail.com. Fans and members of the wrestling community were encouraged to share any relevant information that might help locate Izard and ensure his safe return home to the United Kingdom.

Rodriguez expressed confidence in her abilities and accomplishments, stating that while she may not deserve the briefcase, she has earned the opportunity to win it. "Raquel Rodriguez, this year she's just she's undeniable. That's why because she is undeniable. I don't deserve to be the Money in the Bank winner. I've earned to be a Money in the Bank winner. I've earned everything that I have gotten up to this point and more and more. But, you know, um because of other circumstances or whatever may be happening, my timing has had what I would call bad luck. but no more. In 2026, that bad luck is gone. Raquel Rodriguez is going to be on top, baby."

Rodriguez also reflected on her journey and the advice she would give to her younger self. She discussed how her natural size initially made her come across as soft and how she had struggled with the desire to please others. However, she has come to realize that pleasing herself is what matters most. "I think would be take no kaka for a lack of better words. You know what I mean? I think like Raquel Rodriguez just from naturally being a bigger person came off a little bit soft in the beginning and there's definitely that want to please other people, but at this point of my life, I've realized that the only person I need to please is myself. And so what I would tell a younger Rak Rodriguez is I would be like, 'Take no kaka. Just don't take it.'"

I can't tell you how many times I might have like blacked out, you know. But definitely she amazing. She's that animal, you know."

JERRY LAWLER COMPARES PAT MCAFEE BACKLASH TO ANDY KAUFMAN HEAT

WWE Hall of Famer Jerry Lawler has offered perspective on the recent fan backlash toward Pat McAfee's involvement at WrestleMania by drawing parallels to the heat that Andy Kaufman generated when he entered wrestling decades ago. McAfee's participation in WrestleMania action has drawn significant negative reactions from fans, with many expressing displeasure at his involvement in the event and storylines.

🔥 WWE LFG Season 3 and Format Changes

WWE LFG Returns With Overhauled Format and New Coaching Staff

WWE LFG (Legends & Future Greats) returned for Season 3 on April 26 with significant changes to both its structure and coaching lineup. During the premiere's opening segment, Shawn Michaels revealed that WWE has completely overhauled the show's format, eliminating the team structure and points system that defined the first two seasons. The new approach removes the final contract prize and instead focuses on preparing talent for real NXT television appearances that could come at any moment. Contestants are no longer competing against each other within team structures but rather proving their readiness for immediate NXT television opportunities.

This represents a major departure from the reality competition style of previous seasons. Where Seasons 1 and 2 split competitors into teams led by coaches who earned points each week while working toward winning a contract, Season 3 emphasizes constant readiness. The pressure now shifts to proving ability at any time rather than winning a structured competition.

The Undertaker's Notable Absence Explained

One of the biggest changes fans noticed immediately was The Undertaker's absence from the coaching staff. The Deadman played a major role in earlier seasons but is not part of Season 3. During the premiere's opening segment, Shawn Michaels revealed the reason for his departure. According to the explanation, The Undertaker is currently focused on helping run the AAA brand and has been appointed as a producer with involved creative duties there, which appears to be keeping him busy away from WWE LFG programming.

With Undertaker not part of this season, Kevin Owens stepped into a coaching role while continuing his recovery from neck surgery. The move allows Owens to stay involved on WWE programming despite remaining sidelined from in-ring competition.

Season 3 features a revamped coaching lineup consisting of Booker T, Bubba Ray Dudley, Kevin Owens, and Natalya.
