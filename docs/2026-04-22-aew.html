21/04 – 22/04  ·  1 stories

TL;DR
  • After seven years as partners, AEW and Triller have officially ended their business relationship. Triller discontinued the AEW Plus subscription service and removed all AEW and ROH pay-per-views from their platform, with the company facing significant financial difficulties. Meanwhile, rumors that Pro Wrestling Tees severed ties with AEW have been firmly denied, with the company confirming it remains AEW's official merchandise partner.

🔥 AEW and Triller Partnership Ending

The AEW and Triller partnership has officially come to an end after seven years of collaboration. On Tuesday, Triller sent an email to current and past AEW PPV purchasers announcing the discontinuation of AEW Plus and the removal of all AEW and ROH content from their platform. The email stated: "We're writing to let you know that AEW Plus is being discontinued. AEW and ROH PPVs are also no longer available for purchase on TrillerTV. If you have an active AEW Plus subscription, it will end on your next billing date. You'll have full access until that date. Any PPVs you have previously purchased will remain available to watch in your account."

The partnership between the two companies began in 2019, with AEW Plus providing international viewers the ability to watch weekly Dynamite and Collision episodes, access archive AEW content, and receive discounts on pay-per-views through a monthly subscription service. The service represented a significant revenue stream for Triller, which has been under considerable financial duress for some time. According to recent SEC filings, AEW accounted for approximately 19 percent of Triller's consolidated revenue in 2023, totaling 10.3 million dollars. This figure increased to 24 percent in 2024, though the actual revenue amount decreased to 6.6 million dollars. The 2025 filings have not yet been released.

The discontinuation of AEW Plus appears to coincide with AEW's decision to launch its own streaming platform, MyAEW.com, in March with a new partner. This move gives AEW direct control over its content distribution and subscription revenue. Recent reporting has indicated that Orange Crush, headed by Adam Abdalla, is a potential buyer for the troubled Triller streamer, suggesting the platform may undergo significant changes in the coming months.

Separately, rumors have circulated regarding merchandise operations at AEW. A post on Squared Circle Reddit claimed that Pro Wrestling Tees had laid off their entire merch design team that worked with AEW as a direct consequence of the partnership ending. However, Pro Wrestling Tees has firmly denied these claims. In a statement to Fightful Select, the company said: "The person who made the Reddit post circulating has no knowledge of our business. Nothing on that post is true except that we unfortunately told some of our contracted designers that we would not need them for as many AEW designs anymore. Some of the AEW designers are still contracted. Nothing has changed otherwise and we are still fulfilling merchandise for AEW."

Pro Wrestling Tees has remained the official merchandise partner of Tony Khan's AEW since the company's inception in 2019. Despite the reduction in design work on certain projects, the partnership continues to operate normally with some contracted designers still working on AEW-related projects. The company's statement clarifies that no wholesale severance of the partnership has occurred and that merchandise fulfillment for AEW continues as scheduled.
