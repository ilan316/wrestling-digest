🔥 Mike Santana Steve Maclin Injury at TNA Sacrifice

---

A scary moment unfolded during the TNA World Championship main event at TNA Sacrifice 2026 when Steve Maclin was legitimately injured after taking a superkick from Mike Santana just minutes into their match on Friday, March 27, in New Orleans, Louisiana.

Santana connected with the superkick early in the bout, catching Maclin flush in the face. The move, reminiscent of the infamous superkick Goldberg delivered to Bret Hart in WCW that ended Hart's legendary career, immediately appeared to cause problems for Maclin. It was apparent almost immediately that something was wrong as Maclin stumbled and seemed to be knocked senseless by the impact.

Referee Alice Lane quickly threw up the X-sign, signaling a legitimate injury concern. The medical team rushed to the ring and checked on Maclin, who was unable to continue. The match was halted abruptly, and Maclin was assisted to the back for evaluation. Eddie Edwards then ran out for an impromptu confrontation with Santana, resulting in Edwards being put through a table to give the broadcast some closure.

Initial reports indicated that Maclin may have suffered a concussion from the impact. However, following the event, TNA President Carlos Silva provided an update stating that after TNA officials evaluated Maclin for a concussion, he was sent to the hospital for further testing. Silva confirmed that Maclin was not ultimately diagnosed with a concussion, as he passed all medical evaluations and was cleared to return to his hotel for the night.

Maclin himself addressed the situation on social media, expressing gratitude for the concern shown by fans and stating, "Thanks, everyone, for the calls, texts, and tweets. You can't F**king kill me. I'M BULLETPROOF. #MACLIN"

Mike Santana took some time before addressing the incident publicly. On Sunday morning, March 29, he issued a comprehensive statement on social media reflecting on what had transpired.

In his statement, Santana explained his pre-match ritual, stating: "Every time I'm getting ready to head out for a match, I always find a corner and take a moment to myself. I kneel and pray to God to protect me and my opponent. To watch over us as we head out to give the fans an escape and something worth what they pay their hard earned money for."

Acknowledging the inherent dangers of professional wrestling, Santana wrote: "But as we all know, freak accidents happen that are sometimes out of our control and in the moment. That's the unfortunate reality of what we do. All we can do is learn from it and take preventative measures moving forward, so that we can avoid making the same mistakes."

Santana was particularly effusive in his praise for how TNA handled the emergency situation. He stated: "I will say, I'm truly proud of how TNA handled the situation. And even more proud of referee Alice Lane. Her elite professionalism and quick thinking saved us. We were truly blessed to have had her out there with us."

The champion also expressed his concern for his opponent, emphasizing that their personal relationship and Maclin's wellbeing were paramount. Santana concluded: "I'm glad that Steve is safe and doing well. He's one of the toughest guys I know and someone I've considered a close friend for a long time. At the end of the day, our health and well-being come first and we showed exactly that."

The incident served as a stark reminder of the physicality and inherent risks involved in professional wrestling, even when performers take precautions and follow safety protocols. At the time of the updates, it remained unclear exactly when Maclin would be cleared to return to in-ring competition.

🔥 Leon Slater TNA/WWE Crossover Interactions

**TL;DR:** Leon Slater, the 21-year-old TNA X-Division Champion, has experienced significant crossover interactions with WWE and its talent. After AJ Styles praised him at TNA Slammiversary 2025, Slater later competed at WWE Saturday Night's Main Event in December 2025, where he teamed with Je'Von Evans against Styles and Dragon Lee, and received encouragement from Triple H backstage among other wrestling legends.

---

Leon Slater has quickly become one of professional wrestling's most promising young talents since joining TNA in October 2023, and his interactions with both established WWE stars and the company's leadership have left a lasting impression on the 21-year-old competitor.

One of Slater's most meaningful professional experiences came at TNA Slammiversary in 2025, when AJ Styles made his first appearance in TNA in over a decade. That night, Slater defeated Moose to become the youngest TNA X-Division Champion. Styles seized the opportunity to publicly endorse the rising star, coming out to the ring and speaking highly of him in front of the crowd. For Slater, this moment proved unforgettable. Beyond the public recognition, Styles pulled Slater aside for a private conversation that proved invaluable to the young wrestler's development. During their chat, Styles offered extensive guidance on match psychology, personal conduct in the wrestling industry, maintaining family values, and the professional principles that have made Styles successful throughout his career.

Reflecting on this experience, Slater expressed deep gratitude: "So the first major interaction I had with him was at Slammiversary where he returned to TNA. He came out and sung my praises in the middle of the ring, which I will never forget. That's the moment I'll always remember. He also pulled me aside for a chat after we came back from the ring. He spoke to me on everything, like match feedback, how to handle yourself in wrestling, how to handle yourself as a man, be a family man, how to operate, and how it's made him successful. He just gave me pure wisdom. So, getting to share that moment with him in my home at TNA was incredible. Then getting to share another moment with him in his home for the last 10 years, in WWE, and especially on the night that it was – it's still hard for me to believe I was there."

Slater's passion for wrestling runs remarkably deep. Having started wrestling training at age nine, he has been involved in the sport for over a decade. His love affair with wrestling began even earlier, around three or four years old, when his cousin lived next door and shared action figures, replica belts, DVDs, and video games. When his cousin eventually lost interest in wrestling, Slater inherited all his merchandise and never looked back. Speaking about his lifelong dedication, Slater said: "I can't remember a time in my life where the wrestling bug wasn't in me. I've never wanted to do anything else with my life, so I think I've had the bug since day one. I always tell people I slid out of the womb and took a back bump straight there. I was born with the bug."

The TNA star's crossover moments with WWE reached another pinnacle on December 13, 2025, at Saturday Night's Main Event in Washington D.C. This event held particular significance as it marked the conclusion of John Cena's year-long WWE farewell tour, with the 17-time world champion losing to GUNTHER in his final match. The card was designed to showcase younger talent from NXT, but WWE elevated the occasion further by featuring Slater in a high-profile match alongside his best friend, fellow 21-year-old Je'Von Evans.

Slater and Evans competed against AJ Styles and Dragon Lee, who were the reigning WWE World Tag Team Champions at the time. The match proved electric and packed with memorable moments. Describing the surreal nature of the experience, Slater said: "I'll see clips from that night of like John being stood in the ring and putting his armbands and his sneakers and I was like, bro, I was stood right there! It almost feels like a fever dream, but it was real. That whole day was just magical. For the match, I couldn't have asked to be around better people. AJ, who's one of my absolute idols. And then Dragon, another super hungry, young guy that's been smashing it and I used to watch his work when he used to wrestle all over the world. And then obviously, having Je'Von, one of my best friends as my partner was another dream come true."

The experience extended far beyond the in-ring action. Returning through the curtain after his match, Slater encountered an unprecedented gathering of wrestling royalty in Gorilla Position. Triple H occupied the chair there with Bruce Pritchard seated beside him. Michael Hayes was present, with The Undertaker standing at the back of the room and Shawn Michaels also in attendance. Outside Gorilla, Slater encountered Kurt Angle and Rob Van Dam, among others—essentially a who's who of wrestling legends he had watched throughout his life.

Triple H, WWE's Chief Content Officer, took time from what was obviously an incredibly busy evening to speak with Slater personally. Given the magnitude of the event and all the priorities competing for Triple H's attention, Slater found this gesture particularly meaningful. He recalled: "After the Saturday night's main event match, I had a really cool little chat with Triple H where he pulled me to a side for a couple of seconds, and spoke to me and Gorilla, and especially on a night like that where there's so much going on and I am far from the priority, I thought it was very cool of him to do that when he doesn't need to."

Slater has consistently praised his treatment during his time working with WWE over several months at the end of the previous year, describing the entire experience as validation of the respect the wrestling community holds for him and his home promotion, TNA. These crossover moments represent not just career achievements for Slater, but meaningful connections with the wrestlers and executives who have shaped the industry he has loved since childhood.

🔥 The IInspiration TJPW Tag Team Championship

**TL;DR:** The IInspiration (Cassie Lee and Jessie McKay) won the TJPW Princess Tag Team Championships at Grand Princess 2026 on March 29, defeating Ober Eats. This historic victory makes them the only tag team to hold women's tag titles in WWE, TNA, and TJPW, adding another major accomplishment to their résumé after recent momentum in other promotions.

---

The IInspiration have captured championship gold on the international stage, winning the TJPW Princess Tag Team Championships at Tokyo Joshi Pro's Grand Princess 2026 event held at the Ryogoku Kokugikan in Tokyo, Japan on March 29, 2026.

In the semi-main event, Cassie Lee and Jessie McKay defeated the reigning champions Ober Eats (Wakana Uehara and Yuki Kamifuku) to claim the Princess Tag Team Championships. The victory was particularly impressive considering it was only the team's second match in TJPW. Lee and McKay are now the 20th champions in the lineage of the Princess Tag Team Championships, which were first established in October 2017 when Yuka Sakazaki and Shoko Nakajima won the inaugural titles. They are the 17th team to hold the belts.

The Australian duo wasted no time celebrating their historic achievement on social media. Cassie Lee posted: "Global sensations! Worldwide phenomenons!" while Jessie McKay echoed the sentiment, writing: "Worldwide phenomenons!! 🌍 Global sensations!! 🤩 History makers!! 🏆"

With this win, The IInspiration have rewritten tag team history. The former Billie Kay and Peyton Royce—known collectively as The IIconics during their time in WWE—have now won tag team titles across three different major promotions. They previously captured the WWE Women's Tag Team Championships once and the TNA Knockouts World Tag Team Championships twice. The Princess Tag Team Championship victory makes them the only tag team in women's wrestling to have held tag team gold in all three promotions.

This championship win represents a significant turnaround for the team. Just weeks earlier, their AEW television debut resulted in a squash loss to The Brawling Birds (Jamie Hayter and Alex Windsor) in just two minutes on March 4, which led to questions about their booking and future prospects in the promotion. However, The IInspiration have since picked up momentum, securing their first win in Ring of Honor before capturing gold in TJPW.

The timing of this achievement is particularly notable given recent developments in AEW's tag team division. On March 28, the inaugural AEW Women's Tag Team Champions the Babes of Wrath (Harley Cameron and Willow Nightingale) amicably disbanded, with both members shifting their focus to singles competition. This creates a potential opening in the AEW Women's Tag Team Title picture.

The possibility remains that The IInspiration could make further history by winning the AEW Women's Tag Team Championships, which would make them the only team to hold women's tag titles in AEW, WWE, and TNA. The current AEW Women's Tag Team Champions are the Divine Dominion (Megan Bayne and Lena Kross), and only the Babes of Wrath have previously held the AEW titles.

From championship doubts to international gold, The IInspiration have demonstrated their resilience and staying power as one of women's wrestling's most accomplished tag teams. Whether they can continue their momentum and potentially add AEW gold to their collection remains to be seen, but their Princess Tag Team Championship victory has certainly reignited the conversation around their capabilities and potential future success across wrestling's major promotions.

TNA/WWE Crossover Match

---

A TNA Wrestling star has reflected on his match with WWE SmackDown star Carmelo Hayes earlier this year in London, England. The January 16 episode of SmackDown saw TNA Wrestling X-Division Champion Leon Slater make his first appearance on WWE's blue brand, answering Carmelo Hayes' United States Championship open challenge.

With the show taking place in Slater's home country of England, the TNA star received a hero's welcome from the fans in Wembley Arena before the pair stole the show with their work between the ropes. Speaking with The Sun, Slater reflected on the bout, saying that it is the "most comfortable" he's felt competing inside a WWE ring thus far.

"I'll be honest, that's probably the most comfortable I ever felt in a WWE ring because I felt like I was with my people, you know what I'm saying, like I remember there was one point where Carmelo was punching me in the face or something, like, I was getting battered, but I remember hearing 'He's one of our own, he's one of our own,'" Slater said. "And then I was like, 'OK, right, we're really at home. We've got the chants going and everything!'"

Slater didn't only feel comfortable in his home crowd; he also felt a connection with his opponent Carmelo Hayes. "Me and Melo hit it off, man, I feel like our chemistry in-ring was on a different level and I'm glad we got to deliver that night in Wembley," he reflected.

However, Hayes' United States Championship run came to an unexpected end on Friday's SmackDown (March 27). Hayes saw his title stripped by Sami Zayn, who capitalized on some inadvertent interference from Trick Williams, who aimed to hit Zayn but instead struck his former NXT tag team partner Hayes.

Hayes held the WWE United States Championship for a total of 98 days. He won the title from Ilja Dragunov on the December 19, 2025, episode of Friday Night SmackDown and successfully defended it multiple times through Open Challenges. His third televised title defense came against Slater at Wembley Arena.

With just three weeks to go until WrestleMania 42, the result has led to a negative response from a section of WWE fans, as well as former US Champion Bishop Dyer (Baron Corbin), who took to social media to share support for Hayes after he was "screwed." Zayn will put his new gold on the line against Trick Williams at WrestleMania 42, while Hayes currently looks set to miss out on the card unless something changes.

The loss to Zayn sparked speculation that Hayes might take time off from WWE television to marry NXT star Kelani Jordan. This speculation arose after a photographer and videographer hired by the couple shared pictures and videos on social media, leading fans to believe that Hayes and Jordan had tied the knot. However, this assumption appears incorrect.

According to recent reports, the images and videos actually stemmed from their engagement ceremony held last July, and Hayes is not expected to take time off during the WrestleMania season. His plans for the upcoming Show of Shows in Las Vegas, Nevada, next month remain unclear.

Last year's WWE WrestleMania 41 saw then TNA World Champion Joe Hendry answer Randy Orton's open challenge, with the match marking the first time a TNA contracted star had competed at "The Showcase of the Immortals." With WWE and TNA's working relationship going strong, fans have questioned whether we could once again see some TNA representation at this year's event as well.

Of all the talent on the TNA roster, Leon Slater seems the most likely option to fill that spot if WWE decides to showcase someone from the Impact Zone. With a potential multi-man match for the Intercontinental Title possibly on the cards, Slater would be a logical choice to join the fray.

MLP Canadian Championship and Global Wars

---

It's been a momentous weekend for Scott D'Amore's Maple Leaf Pro Wrestling, highlighted by significant championship debuts and the crowning of an interim titleholder following an unexpected injury to the promotion's top champion.

Josh Alexander, MLP's Canadian Champion, was forced to announce his absence just hours before the Global Wars Canada event last weekend after suffering a knee injury during an AES Collision taping. The injury will require surgery, leaving no confirmed timeline for his return. To address the vacancy during his recovery period, MLP scheduled the crowning of an interim Canadian Champion at their Uprising event.

At the Uprising show on March 28, Stu Grayson defeated five other competitors in a six-person Gauntlet match to claim the interim MLP Canadian Championship. The match followed traditional gauntlet rules, with competitors entering sequentially. Jay Lethal and Michael Oku started the bout, with Oku securing the first elimination by countering Lethal's top rope elbow with a crucifix pin. Rohan Raja entered next and quickly eliminated Oku with a leaping downward spiral. Rich Swann was the fourth competitor to enter, but Raja dispatched him with the same move that finished Oku. When Grayson entered, he eliminated Raja with the Knightfall. Jonathan Gresham was the final entrant, but Grayson outlasted him to secure the interim title.

It should be noted that the original card was subject to changes. TNA's Steve Maclin had been previously scheduled to participate in the match but suffered a concussion scare that forced his replacement. Michael Oku took his spot in the gauntlet.

The same weekend also saw historic achievements for MLP's tag team division. At Global Wars Canada, which took place in partnership with Ring of Honor, the Good Brothers—Karl Anderson and Doc Gallows from Bullet Club OC—were crowned the first-ever MLP Tag Team Champions. They defeated three other teams in a four-way match: Kaito Kiyomiya and Bishop Dyer, GOA (Bishop Kaun and Toa Liona), and Bryce Hansen and Sheldon Jean.

The Global Wars Canada card was stacked with championship action across multiple promotions. Ricochet defeated Rich Swann, while Athena successfully retained the ROH Women's World Championship against Taylor Rising. Michael Oku won a Six-Man Scramble that also included Jake Crist, Barbaro Cavernario, Michael Allen, Richard Clark, Ace Austin, and Brent Banks. Rohan Raja retained the PWA Champion's Grail against Stu Grayson, and Deonna Purrazzo successfully defended the ROH Women's Pure Championship against Gisele Shaw. Additional tag team action saw Jay Lethal, Blake Christian, and Lee Johnson defeat Evil Uno, Bhupinder Gujjar, and Psycho Mike, while Red Velvet retained the ROH Women's TV title against Alice Crowley. Eddie Kingston and Ortiz defeated Adam Priest and Tommy Billington in another contest.

Looking forward, MLP announced that the promotion will begin airing weekly broadcasts in Canada starting this summer, marking a significant expansion for the Toronto-based company.

At Uprising, the Good Brothers also defended their newly won MLP Tag Team Championship in a two-on-two match. Additionally, Kaito Kiyomiya faced Bishop Dyer after Dyer's turn on Kiyomiya following their tag team loss at Global Wars. The women's division saw Gisele Shaw challenge Deonna Purrazzo for the Canadian Women's Championship. Other matches included Jonathan Gresham vs. Psycho Mike vs. Rich Swann vs. Stu Grayson vs. Rohan Raja vs. Michael Oku for the interim Canadian Championship, Bishop Dyer vs. Kaito Kiyomiya, Máscara Dorada and Bhupinder Gujjar vs. Jake Crist and Michael Allen Richard Clarke, The Swirl (Blake Christian & Lee Johnson) vs. El Reverso & Mo Jabari, and Taylor Rising & Kris Chambers vs. Alice Crowley & Evil Uno.

D-Von Dudley Stroke Health Update

**TL;DR:** D-Von Dudley survived a severe 2020 stroke after experiencing a mysterious urge to unlock his hotel door, which proved lifesaving when he collapsed hours later. He initially failed to recognize his symptoms even as he lost feeling in his legs and fell face-first onto the floor, only accepting the situation when his wife recognized the signs and called for hotel intervention.

---

D-Von Dudley is revealing just how close he came to dying during his 2020 stroke—and the full story is even more intense than people realized.

Speaking to DJ Vlad, D-Von Dudley walked through the entire experience step-by-step, explaining how a completely normal evening in a hotel room slowly turned into a life-threatening emergency he didn't even recognize at first.

He began by describing how everything felt routine at the start—he was relaxed, hungry, and casually talking to his wife about getting food delivered, even joking about how it would work across states. "I remember going, 'Damn, I'm hungry.' And my wife at the time, I'm speaking to her and I go, 'Man, I would love if you could DoorDash me some food.' She goes, 'I could do that.' And this is how oblivious I am to technology — I went, 'Really? I'm in California. You're in Florida.' She goes, 'Yeah, I can do it.' So she did it."

That lighthearted moment quickly gave way to something far more unsettling. D-Von explained that while moving around his hotel room, he suddenly felt an overwhelming and persistent urge to unlock his door—something completely out of character for him. "As I'm getting out of the bathroom, something tells me — as clear as day — unlock the door. I don't know what it was… but it kept saying, 'Unlock the door.'"

At first, he resisted the feeling, sticking to his usual routine of keeping the door locked for privacy and safety. But the voice didn't stop—and eventually, it wore him down. "I was like, 'Nah, I want it locked. I don't want anyone making a mistake and coming into my hotel room.' But it keeps bothering me… something keeps saying, 'Unlock the door.' I'm like, 'Damn, whatever's inside me saying this is not going to stop.' So I said, 'F it. Ain't nobody coming in here.' I unlocked the door and went to bed."

That decision turned out to be critical. When he woke up later, D-Von believed it was still early in the night—but in reality, hours had passed. The moment he tried to stand up, his body completely failed him. "I got up — what I thought was the middle of the night — and I went to take my first step out of bed and fell face-first onto the floor. Busted my mouth open and everything."

As he lay there, he realized something was seriously wrong—but still couldn't process what was happening. Even as the situation worsened, D-Von admitted he wasn't reacting with urgency. His phone and the hotel phone kept ringing, but he brushed it off, unaware of how dangerous things had become. "All of a sudden, I couldn't feel my legs. I didn't understand what was going on. My cell phone is ringing. I can't get up fast enough to get it… Then the hotel phone is ringing. They go, 'Your wife is calling. She wants to know why you're not answering.' I said, 'Tell her I'm in the bathroom and I'll call her back.' I was being a jerk about it."

At that point, he was physically struggling just to move, attempting to crawl across the room while still not fully grasping the severity of his condition. "Now I'm trying to army crawl back to the bed… I finally get up, reach for my phone, and it falls to the other side. I'm like, 'You can't write a story better than this.' I finally grab it and go, 'What do you want? I'm sleeping. It's early.'… I don't even remember what she said — it sounded like a cartoon."

When he finally answered his phone, his confusion was obvious—and his wife immediately noticed something was off in the way he was speaking. As he explained his symptoms, she quickly realized what was happening—even as he continued to deny it. D-Von still didn't believe it—and even had his sense of time completely off. "Then she goes, 'What's going on with you?' I said, 'I fell, busted my mouth open, I can't feel my legs. Something's wrong.' She goes, 'You're having a stroke.' I said, 'No, I'm not. I'm too young to have a stroke.'"

Demolition on Modern Wrestling Style

---

During an appearance on The Demolition Podcast, legendary tag team members Ax and Smash offered their perspective on contemporary wrestling, taking issue with both the physical style and booking approach of modern promotions.

The veterans expressed frustration with how gimmick matches have become commonplace in wrestling programming. Ax explained that matches requiring major stipulations traditionally needed substantial buildup to justify their use as a climactic payoff. "Sometimes they just throw people into a gimmick match where there was no buildup for it," Ax said. "Sometimes that buildup was a year, a year and a half, and it culminated with, 'Now we're going to get a winner. Now it's finally going to be settled.'"

Smash elaborated on how the repeated use of these matches has created a dangerous escalation cycle. "Some of them are getting hurt so badly they can hardly work anymore," Smash noted. "Each guy has to beat what the next guy does. When we were doing it, the blowoff was a cage match or whatever, and then they wouldn't have a cage match again until somebody else had another blowoff. Now it's like, 'Hey, let's just throw a cage match in there. Let's have a ladder match.'"

Beyond booking philosophy, Ax raised concerns about the fundamental wrestling style itself. While acknowledging the athleticism of modern wrestlers, he drew a distinction between athleticism and wrestling. "They're great athletes, they're very athletic, but a lot of the stuff is like gymnastics. It's not wrestling," Ax stated. "They're taking so many bumps. Like Barry mentioned, the propensity for potential injury greatly accelerates."

Ax concluded by reflecting on the different mindset wrestlers of his era maintained. "We used to wrestle with the idea that we were going to be in this business for a long period of time. You have wrestlers now who feel like they have to outdo the match before them—or the two or three matches before." This commentary highlights a fundamental philosophical difference in how wrestlers approach longevity and sustainability in their careers.
