🔥 Aleister Black AEW Return Speculation

Aleister Black, formerly known as Malakai Black during his time in AEW, has become the subject of significant speculation following his recent WWE release in April 2026. Having spent four years in AEW from 2021 through 2024 before returning to WWE for a brief stint, Black's future in professional wrestling is now uncertain, with considerable backstage chatter surrounding where he might land next.

According to multiple reports, sources within AEW do not expect Black to be returning to the promotion in the near future. This belief is shared even among Black's friends within the company, who have cautioned others against expecting his return. Former AEW World Champion MJF and AEW talent coordinator and Ring of Honor Six Man Tag Team Champion Shawn Dean have separately expressed no desire to see Black back, which many view as a strong indicator that the door on his return is closed.

However, the situation may not be entirely permanent. Sean Ross Sapp noted that the bridge between Black and AEW is not believed to be completely burned, leaving at least some theoretical room for a future return down the line. This assessment suggests that while a near-term comeback is unlikely, circumstances could change in the future if Black demonstrates personal growth or a shift in perspective.

Black's mixed AEW tenure is often cited as the primary reason for hesitation about his return. While his presentation, theme music, and feud with Adam Copeland were praised, Black faced significant criticism for reportedly being unwilling to participate in certain storylines or lose matches. These issues created tension within the locker room and raised questions about his reliability and willingness to work within the company's creative vision.

Despite the controversies, Black did achieve some success during his AEW run. He won the AEW World Trios Championships alongside Brody King and Buddy Matthews. King has since gone on to achieve considerable success without Black, winning the AEW World Tag Team Championships with Bandido in 2025 and becoming a consistent contender for the AEW World Championship in 2026. A House of Black reunion would potentially be counterproductive to King's current trajectory and development as a singles competitor.

Several other factors complicate the possibility of Black's return. His injury history has been a concern throughout his career, and there is the fair question of whether he is currently more worthy of receiving television time than some AEW talent already on air who are fighting to get time. WWE SmackDown's ongoing format changes are now fully explained by a contractual agreement with NBCUniversal that builds in a seasonal schedule. According to Bryan Alvarez, WWE's current deal with USA Network stipulates that SmackDown will air three-hour episodes from January through the end of June every year, then revert to its traditional two-hour format from July through December.

This arrangement means the longer format is not a permanent change but rather a predictable, recurring seasonal adjustment. SmackDown first expanded to three hours on January 3, 2025, and returned to two hours on July 4 of that year. The same pattern repeated in 2026, with the show expanding again on January 2. These changes directly follow the contractual schedule built into the WWE and NBCUniversal partnership.

Dave Meltzer provided further clarity on the structure during Wrestling Observer Radio, confirming that the format split is embedded directly into the contract language. "The contract that they have signed now is January till the end of June would be three hours, and then the end of June, in the first of July to the end of December, it would be two hours. So that's the new, the way the deal is structured right now," Meltzer explained.

This cyclical arrangement is expected to remain in place for the foreseeable future, likely lasting for the next four years under the current media rights agreement. For viewers, this means adapting to three-hour SmackDown episodes during the first half of each calendar year and returning to the standard two-hour format in the second half. Fans still have approximately two more months of three-hour shows to experience before the July transition back to two hours.

Beyond the format changes, production elements are also expected to be modified in the coming weeks, though specific details about those changes have not yet been disclosed. The format adjustment represents a significant shift from how SmackDown operated in previous years, with the contractual guarantee ensuring that viewers can expect this pattern to repeat consistently through the duration of the deal.
