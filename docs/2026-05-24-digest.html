🔥 Skye Blue Ankle Injury

Skye Blue is opening up about the brutal ankle injury that sidelined her for nearly a year, detailing just how serious the damage was after speaking with her surgeon. During an interview with TMZ's Inside the Ring, the 26-year-old AEW star explained the horrifying moment doctors reviewed her scans and explained the severity of her condition.

"When I went to the surgeon and they did all the scans, he was like, surgery is this day. I was dumbfounded. He said, you don't have a choice. My ankle was literally detached from my leg. His exact words were, you look like you were in a car crash."

According to Skye Blue, the surgeon immediately told her that surgery was unavoidable because of how badly her ankle had been destroyed. The injury eventually required major surgery involving a steel plate, six screws on one side of her ankle and two long screws on the inside.

Skye Blue admitted the physical recovery was difficult, but mentally things became even harder once she was suddenly stuck at home after constantly being on the road with AEW. She described the transition from her fast-paced lifestyle to being immobilized at home.

"You're going from TV twice a week, being on the road, being go, go, go, to okay, you got to sit on the couch and you can't do anything."

Beyond the physical challenges, she revealed she constantly worried about whether fans and fellow wrestlers would see her differently once she returned to competition. The turning point in her recovery came when she secretly tried walking again without telling her physical therapist, discovering that the pain was manageable.

"I looked like a crooked pirate, I was hobbling and waddling, but I was like, oh, that didn't hurt that bad."

Even now, Skye Blue said she still feels the hardware in her ankle when it gets cold or rainy and the metal even sets off metal detectors at airports. Despite that lingering discomfort, she refuses to let the injury slow her down in the ring and maintains a mental approach of simply not thinking about it during matches.

"I just don't think about it, and I just go."

In a separate recent interview with TMZ, Skye Blue also revealed a full-circle moment with one of AEW's biggest stars. The 26-year-old talent reflected on meeting Jon Moxley years ago at a Comic-Con event, long before the two would eventually share the same locker room in AEW. Her mother had even pulled her out of school for the opportunity to meet the current AEW Continental Champion at the time.

"I think it was maybe like a Comic Con or something that me and my mom went to meet Moxley," Skye Blue said during the interview. "There's this photo, and the internet has seen it. I tried to bury it and hide it, but they found it. I have neon pink hair."

She went on to explain the story behind the now-viral photo and how nervous she was during the interaction with her childhood idol.

"My mom took me out of school so that we could go meet Moxley. I remember I walked up and I was terrified. I walked up to him and I was like, 'I want to be a wrestler one day. I'm going to train to be a wrestler one day.' And I don't remember exactly what he said, but he smiled and it was very quick. I was just so anxious the whole time. I remember walking away from that and I told my mom, 'I'm going to work with him one day.' And she was like, 'If you put your mind to it, I'm sure you can.' I had already proved how stubborn I was. I had started training. And now I work with the man."

Blue's prediction came true years later when she joined AEW and began working alongside Moxley. One of the coolest parts of working in AEW now is being able to learn directly from Moxley and receive feedback from someone she once looked up to as a fan.

"I can pick his brain about wrestling and I can get feedback from him. The other day I walked past him in the hallway and he was like, 'Good job, kid.' Little kid me was like screaming in the back of my brain. It's like a little full circle thing that I was like, 'Holy crap.'"

Blue has recently been active in AEW competition, teaming with Athena and her Triangle of Madness stablemates Thekla and Julia Hart in an eight-person tag victory on May 20 AEW Dynamite and Collision in Portland, Maine against the Brawling Birds, Mina Shirakawa, and Thunder Rosa. The week prior, the Triangle of Madness had lost a six-person tag via disqualification to Hikaru Shida and the Brawling Birds. Blue has had two singles matches in May as well, defeating Nixi XS at the May 2 Collision taping in Peoria, Illinois, and losing to Jamie Hayter on the May 6 Collision taping in North Charleston, South Carolina.

After hearing just how severe her ankle injury really was, Skye Blue's return to AEW television looks even more impressive than fans originally thought, especially considering her determination to overcome both the physical and mental challenges that came with such a devastating injury.

Gunther Discusses Potential Lesnar Match After WWE Return

Two of the most dominant men on the WWE roster, Gunther and Brock Lesnar, have never squared off against each other in singles action despite sharing the ring in Royal Rumble matches. This potential matchup has long captured the imagination of wrestling fans, and recent developments have reignited speculation about the clash.

Brock Lesnar's return to WWE came somewhat unexpectedly. After seemingly retiring following his loss to Oba Femi on Night Two of WrestleMania 42, WWE had removed Lesnar from the 'Now' section of its intro video and moved him to the alumni section on WWE.com. However, Lesnar made a surprise return on a recent episode of Monday Night RAW, laying out Oba Femi during an open challenge. Paul Heyman confirmed that Lesnar had already signed a contract for a rematch with Oba at Clash in Italy.

With Lesnar back on the roster, speculation about a potential showdown with Gunther has intensified. When asked about the possibility during an interview with the Italian Wrestling Channel, Gunther made his priorities clear. He noted that he did not even witness Lesnar's initial retirement at WrestleMania, as it occurred the day after his own match against Seth Rollins at the event. "I wasn't even there on that day at Mania, because it was the day after me, when a lot of it happened with Brock, so I only saw it in a headline, and that's literally all the info I had," Gunther explained.

Despite acknowledging Lesnar's return, Gunther emphasized his singular focus remains on his upcoming championship challenge. "Obviously, he's back now, but I haven't thought about it. I'm really focused on what I'm doing with Cody now. I will be challenging for the WWE Undisputed Championship in Italy, and that's my full focus right now," he stated. However, Gunther did not completely shut the door on a future encounter with The Beast Incarnate, saying "After I beat Cody then Brock can challenge me, and we'll do that."

Gunther's confidence in defeating Cody Rhodes at Clash in Italy reflects his status as one of WWE's most dominant competitors. Should he win the Undisputed WWE Championship, multiple challengers could be in line for title opportunities, including Lesnar, CM Punk, LA Knight, and Jacob Fatu. A potential rematch with Rhodes at SummerSlam is also being considered as an option.

European Advantage at Clash in Italy

Looking ahead to his championship match in Turin, Gunther also addressed the unique dynamic of competing in his home continent. European crowds are known for supporting wrestlers with connections to their region, regardless of alignment. This was evident when Drew McIntyre dethroned Cody Rhodes for the Undisputed WWE Championship in Berlin, where the Scottish star received overwhelming fan support.

Gunther, who built his reputation as a professional wrestler across Europe before joining WWE, acknowledged that he may receive babyface treatment from the Italian crowd despite playing a heel character on television. "Maybe, we'll see," he said when asked about potentially being a crowd favorite. "Obviously we're in Europe. That's my home turf. That happened similar before but it's nothing that shakes me or something."

Interestingly, Gunther views this dynamic as beneficial rather than problematic. "I even think it makes it a little bit more interesting to be honest. Might feel a little bit like an away game for Cody," he noted. This perspective reflects his confidence in his abilities and willingness to embrace the unpredictable nature of home-country advantage dynamics.

The Austrian star also emphasized the importance of his independent circuit experience in shaping his current position. When discussing his journey to WWE, Gunther reflected on his humble beginnings. "Yeah that's how I came up right? Gymnasiums and empty venues. But it's a great learning ground to be honest. Every match back then felt like WrestleMania, I remember that as well," he said. He credited those formative years as crucial preparation for his current role representing WWE on the European stage.

When asked about his feelings on currently not holding a title, Gunther framed championship gold as the ultimate professional goal. "Got to have goals to reach I guess right? But that's the ultimate goal of every professional wrestler is you want to be a champion. So that's what I'm here to do," he explained. For his match with Rhodes at Clash in Italy, Gunther assured fans of a high-quality contest, saying "I think it will not disappoint on any level."

WWE's Finn Balor Recalls NJPW Origins Of Demonic Body Paint

WWE star Finn Balor's Demon character is one of the most unique personas in pro wrestling, and he recently detailed how he came up with it. Balor debuted the persona during his time in NJPW, recalling how he came up with the idea for his match against Kota Ibushi at Wrestle Kingdom in 2014.
