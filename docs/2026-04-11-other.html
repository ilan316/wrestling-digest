🔥 TNA Talent Restrictions and AEW/NJPW Crossover

TNA WRESTLING'S TALENT RESTRICTIONS DRAW FIRE FROM WILL OSPREAY AND KENNY OMEGA

TNA Wrestling has sparked significant controversy by implementing a policy that prevents its contracted talent from appearing on independent wrestling shows opposite AEW wrestlers. The restriction, cited as "partner conflicts" by TNA President Carlos Silva, resulted in the cancellation of at least two high-profile matches scheduled for independent promotions.

The canceled matches included AEW World Champion MJF facing TNA's Nic Nemeth on May 1st for Create A Pro Wrestling, and Ricochet scheduled to face Leon Slater. The policy immediately drew criticism from multiple notable figures in the wrestling industry who questioned the rationale behind blocking wrestlers from independent bookings.

Will Ospreay, a former New Japan star now with AEW, provided perhaps the most vocal response to the restrictions. During an interview with Josh Martinez, Ospreay compared TNA's approach to a time when he faced WWE's Pete Dunne while holding the IWPG Junior Heavyweight Championship. He explained that New Japan had concerns about the cross-promotional match and wanted to know details about what would happen, but ultimately allowed the match to proceed after discussions between the companies.

"I remember New Japan were a little bit like, 'Hey, you can do the match but we're a little bit like wanting to know what's going to be happening in the match, if you know what I mean. And we had to get a lot of things ironed out and sorted. Pete's always a cool guy," Ospreay recalled.

Ospreay then directly addressed the contrast between how established companies handled cross-promotional cooperation and TNA's approach. He stated: "But the fact that WWE and New Japan let that match happen and TNA out of all people, like who the fuck do you think you guys are? I'm sorry but it's like all the shit talk that they were giving about being a number two and it's like you want to give permission for these guys to do that match and then pull them? I think that's cowardly shit, I'll be honest with you."

The AEW star emphasized that TNA's decision sends a troubling message about the company's view of its talent. "I think that says everything about what you view your talent as. You view them less than AEW talent and that they don't want to grow and that they don't want to develop and that they don't want to give back to fans," Ospreay said.

Despite his criticism, Ospreay made clear that his frustration does not extend to TNA's entire legacy. He acknowledged being a longtime fan of the promotion, particularly its early years. "That's me being a hard TNA fan. Those beginning years that I watched, that 04 to 05 era will always be my favorite era in wrestling," he noted before concluding: "But come on, man. We got all the best guys from there. Actually, to be fair, I like Steve Maclin as well—he's great. But man, I just think hang your head in shame, honestly."

Ospreay also suggested that the decision reflects poorly on TNA's standing in the industry, particularly given the company's previous assertions about being the number two wrestling promotion. The implication was that TNA's restrictive approach undermines its credibility as a major player that should be confident enough to allow its talent opportunities for growth and exposure.

Kenny Omega, AEW's EVP, also weighed in on the broader issue during an interview with SI.com's The Takedown. While less confrontational than Ospreay's response, Omega discussed the challenges of wrestlers from different companies working together. He acknowledged that some promotions struggle when isolated in what he called a "bubble," leading to insecurity and an inability to recognize mutual benefit.

"If you're in a bubble for too long and you failed to realize what exists outside your bubble and how small your bubble is, and in this case, we're talking about professional wrestling, I think you start to run into problems like this where someone will think that they're better than somebody else, and that there is no reason to work together, and they don't see the upside," Omega explained.

The AEW executive expressed optimism about the possibility of inter-promotional cooperation, noting that such arrangements exist successfully in other sports and media. He suggested that if companies worked together to solve logistical challenges, the entire wrestling industry would benefit. Omega indicated he would be willing to work with AEW President Tony Khan to find solutions to facilitate cross-promotional matches.

"I really do think that at the end of the day, if everyone were to really just sit down and figure out a way to make it happen, it's more than doable. We've seen it in other sports, other forms of media. This isn't just a thing that's unique to professional wrestling," Omega stated. He added: "I really do think that with how passionately I do feel about working together with everybody and anybody, that I think Tony would hear me out and also entertain the idea as well. I think it'd be great for fans, and I think at the end of the day, too, it's only a net-positive for all of professional wrestling."

The controversy adds to broader concerns about wrestling's competitive landscape and consolidation. AEW has positioned itself as a "challenger brand" against WWE's dominance in the industry, and the company views opportunities for its talent to work with wrestlers from other promotions as part of a strategy to grow at competitors' expense. AEW President Tony Khan has largely taken a diplomatic approach, but MJF and Create A Pro owner Pat Buck both criticized TNA's decision, with MJF being notably more forceful in his language.

The timing of the controversy is particularly notable as both TNA and AEW prepare for significant programming this weekend. TNA Rebellion takes place at the Wolstein Center in Cleveland, Ohio on Saturday, while AEW presents Dynasty from Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada on Sunday. Ospreay is scheduled to challenge Jon Moxley for the AEW Continental Championship at Dynasty.

The situation illustrates ongoing tensions within professional wrestling regarding territorial control and talent mobility. While TNA framed its decision as a matter of partner conflict management, critics argue that the approach is both unnecessarily restrictive and sends a negative message about how the company values its wrestlers' development and opportunities.

🔥 Nick Nemeth TNA and Law School

Nick Nemeth's career path could have been drastically different. The TNA wrestler, formerly known as Dolph Ziggler during his time in WWE, recently opened up about how close he came to pursuing a legal career instead of stepping into the professional wrestling ring. His reflections reveal a wrestler who has always been intellectually curious and strategic about his life choices.

During recent podcast appearances, Nemeth shared that his ambitions from 20 years ago were expansive and diverse. His initial plan included attending pre-law at Kent State University, becoming a lawyer while training for wrestling, winning two world championships, hosting Saturday Night Live, joining the UFC for one fight, and eventually moving into politics as the Mayor of Palm Springs before pursuing a career in the Senate. He admitted that he still maintains those political aspirations despite his successful wrestling career.

Nemeth's interest in law and politics began during his college years. He found himself naturally excelling in courses related to those subjects and became convinced that he would be an excellent lawyer. He explained that he believed every family and group of friends would eventually need legal representation, and he was genuinely interested in figuring out complex problems. His performance in these courses reinforced his belief that a legal career would have been realistic for him. Specifically, he had his sights set on entertainment law, which would have kept him connected to the world of sports and media even if wrestling never became part of his life.

Nemeth's original plan was ambitious but logistically demanding. He intended to attend law school while simultaneously pursuing wrestling training at night, a schedule that he later acknowledged would have been nearly impossible to sustain. That plan changed dramatically when he received an opportunity for a professional wrestling tryout while waiting for his law school classes to begin. This timing proved to be the pivotal moment that shifted his entire career trajectory away from the courtroom and toward the wrestling ring. The tryout led to opportunities that eventually took him around the world as a champion performer.

Even after committing to wrestling, Nemeth's intellectual curiosity about law remained active. He explained that he still tries to stay informed about legal matters, reading books designed to explain laws in accessible ways and making an effort to remain knowledgeable about the legal system. He expressed that he believes he could do good work in his home state of Ohio, potentially serving his hometown of Cleveland down the line. His passion for politics has only grown, though he acknowledged that public interest in politics has intensified significantly over the past decade.

Nemeth's commitment to maximizing his wrestling career also demonstrated his strategic thinking. Nearly three years ago, while still under a lucrative WWE contract with a year and a half remaining, Nemeth made the difficult decision to request an early release. Despite the financial security that came with sitting on the bench and accumulating additional money, he recognized that time, not money, was his most valuable resource. He understood that his window to be a believable world champion and main event performer on the global stage was limited.

The realization that drove this decision was both sobering and motivating. Nemeth calculated that as he aged, there would eventually come a point, likely around his 50s, when he would need to pass the torch to younger wrestlers and shift toward behind-the-scenes work. With that timeline in mind, he determined that sitting inactive under contract would be counterproductive to his career goals. He needed to remain visible, active, and competitive across multiple wrestling promotions to maintain his credibility as a top-level performer.

Making this request required Nemeth to approach WWE leadership with honesty and vulnerability. He had to go to the executive who had assured him years earlier that he would work there until he could no longer perform and that his position with the company was secure for life. Nemeth had to ask if that executive would release him from what was described as an insanely well-paid contract a year and a half earlier than scheduled. The conversation was difficult because Nemeth was essentially asking to walk away from financial security for the opportunity to work more actively elsewhere.

Nemeth's internal struggle with the decision was significant. He knew the value of the contract he was considering leaving behind. However, his passion for wrestling and his awareness of his limited window ultimately outweighed the appeal of financial comfort. He admitted that inactivity would cost him irreplaceable time that he could never recover later in his career. When he explained his reasoning to management, emphasizing that he couldn't remain on the bench because he was a workaholic who loved the business and only had a certain window to perform, the executive asked him to let them think about it.

To Nemeth's relief and gratitude, WWE granted his request for early release a couple of days later. This decision transformed the trajectory of his career during what might have been his final peak years as a performer. Instead of being locked into a contract working sporadically, he was able to compete regularly across multiple promotions, travel around the world, represent different championships, and re-establish himself as a top-level champion on the global stage. The opportunity proved that his instinct to prioritize activity and visibility over financial security was correct.

Nemeth's journey illustrates a wrestler who has consistently made decisions based on long-term career vision rather than short-term financial gain. Whether it was choosing wrestling over law school or requesting early release from a lucrative contract to remain active, he has demonstrated a clear understanding of what matters most at different stages of a career. His continued interest in law and politics suggests that those original ambitions have never truly left him, and they may still represent a future chapter in his life after his active wrestling career concludes.

A Fresh Coat of Paint

"Now is no time to think of what you do not have. Think of what you can do with what there is." This Ernest Hemingway quote frames the current moment for BodySlam, a wrestling news platform that has faced an unexpected crisis. In December 1922, Hemingway lost a suitcase full of short stories in a train robbery at the Gare de Lyon in Paris. Rather than surrender to despair, he recreated what he could and discovered that his writing improved through the process. His sentences became shorter, his paragraphs cleaner, and his prose more digestible. A personal disaster became a revelation that led to necessary upgrades.

BodySlam, founded by Cassidy Haynes, has been a significant force in wrestling journalism for years. The platform broke major news like Bryan Danielson's 2021 move to AEW. Its contributors have included photographers, writers, interviewers, podcasters, and scoopsters who have collaborated across multiple outlets. The site became known for witty pieces about questionable takes in the wrestling industry, comprehensive match recaps, wrestler profiles, and sharp commentary on the wrestling landscape.

In the first week of April 2026, BodySlam's server was deleted. While some work had been saved, years upon years of contributions vanished. This loss extends beyond mere data—it represents countless hours of reporting, education, and hard-hitting narratives that cannot be recovered. The heartbreak is real and substantial for everyone who invested their time and talent in building the platform's archive.

However, there is genuine hope moving forward. The writing team remains hungry and motivated to rebuild. The road ahead will be challenging, requiring renewed effort to rebuild web traffic and climb search rankings. While some contributors lost backups, others managed to preserve their work, creating a bittersweet silver lining. This preservation cannot replace the time invested in the original reporting, but it does provide a foundation.

The incident, while painful, has forced strategic improvements to the platform itself. Contributors like Corey Michaels have taken precautions for years, maintaining backups across multiple formats including Word documents, Google Docs, flash drives, hard drives, and various other storage methods. Michaels plans to revisit previous works that were lost, delivering them with the classic style readers remember but enhanced by the improvements he has made as a writer.

For readers, the relaunched BodySlam promises significant improvements over the previous iteration. The old platform had noticeable issues: ads that interrupted reading flow, short-form articles padded with keywords and buzzwords lacking substantive context, compromised integrity, and communication struggles. The overall experience felt chaotic and unfocused, though the team made do with what they had available.

The server deletion forced BodySlam's hand, and the result will ultimately benefit readers. The new website features a sleeker, more reader-friendly design. The responsive layout is easy on the eyes and provides helpful scannability for quick navigation. The new server offers enhanced security, and the search engine has been tailored to help readers discover content more easily. Most significantly, BodySlam has committed to longer-form content that provides substantial material for engaged readers to explore deeply. Post-show coverage may also receive graphics improvements to enhance presentation.

TNA Wrestling General News

I want to start this by writing a disclaimer that this column isn't about defending WWE or even TNA, but it's more about an opinion on the whole situation. A lot of what made this story such a hot topic was based solely on speculation from those in the wrestling media and social media bloggers. So just like them, this take is also based solely on speculation and might not please everybody, especially those anti-WWE fans who are looking for anything negative to criticize the company.

At the beginning of the week, the story broke that TNA pulled Moose off his Maple Leaf Wrestling date because he was booked to wrestle an AEW guy. Then later on, it was Leon Slater and Nic Nemeth's situations, and the reason given for all three was that it was due to partner-related issues. It didn't take long for everybody in the wrestling media and fans on social media to blame WWE for this, because let's face it, who's the biggest partner TNA has right now? WWE. And that will bring a lot more clicks and views than if it was somebody else that made the decision.

However, the opinion here is that this has nothing to do with WWE and has everything to do with another TNA partner: AMC. So let's start from the beginning. TNA has been partnering with WWE for two years now, as this partnership started in 2024 and became official last year. During that period of time, TNA talents did have matches with AEW talents. In fact, they had matches against AEW talents on a few indy shows in Montreal where they were live, and WWE never said anything. You also have to take into consideration that some of the WWE ID wrestlers did face AEW talents or have been booked on shows that featured AEW guys, and WWE never forbade them from doing those shows. So why would this have changed now? Why would they even care that one of their partners would let their wrestlers face AEW guys now when they haven't cared for two years? To anyone, it really makes no sense.

But what's new with TNA right now? They signed a multi-year TV deal with AMC. Now you might be thinking, why would AMC care about who TNA talents face on the indies? The answer is quite simple. They wouldn't care unless something made them realize that they might be losing money if a TNA talent loses on PPV against somebody from AEW, who they might see as competition. So let's go back to when this story broke. It was early this week, either Monday or Tuesday, and that's when the ratings for both AEW Collision and TNA IMPACT came out and were lower than usual. This may have made AMC realize not only that AEW is competition to their program, but also that when they go head-to-head, TNA isn't getting as many viewers as usual, which means less money in their pocket.

So it could clearly be seen as a suggestion on AMC's part, because if TNA wrestlers competed against anybody from another promotion and it was televised, they could risk their TV deal or be penalized for allowing their guys to wrestle for another televised promotion, which all three matches would qualify as. This kind of makes more sense because, in the end, when a wrestling company signs a TV deal with a major company, those TV executives, who may know nothing about wrestling, will sometimes meddle in company affairs. We saw that with TNA before during the Spike TV era, we saw it in AEW with WBD in the past, and we've even seen it with WWE and their TV partners before. So this type of practice isn't uncommon, and just by looking at the timing of all this, this feels like the more logical explanation for what happened.

In conclusion, there aren't all the answers on this story, and this opinion, just like everybody else's including those reporting this as real news, is based on speculation. The only person who knows the true reason why those talents were pulled is someone in the company making those decisions.

Eddie Kingston Independent Bookings

Eddie Kingston is heading back to Germany for what could be one of his final independent wrestling appearances. wXw announced that the former ROH Champion will return to the promotion at Drive of Champions, taking place on June 6 in Oberhausen. This marks Kingston's first match with wXw since competing at the 16 Carat Gold tournament in 2020.

The significance of this booking extends beyond a simple return to a familiar territory. In their announcement, wXw emphasized that this would likely be Kingston's last appearance with the promotion, as the AEW star is ending all of his independent dates after 2026. Kingston himself announced late last year that he is on his "final indy run," drastically scaling back his schedule moving forward.

"So this is my last indy run. Doing beyond and a show in Jersey this weekend," Kingston revealed at the time. "After that I am taking only one show a month. By next November I am done with the indies and yeah nothing more too it. As of now I [have] December and March, April, May."

Kingston's decision to wind down his independent work comes after a challenging period for the veteran wrestler. Following a leg injury that sidelined him for approximately a year, Kingston made his return to AEW last fall. However, he subsequently took additional time away from competition to address migraine issues. He returned to action late last month at Maple Leaf Pro, teaming with Ortiz.

The Drive of Champions appearance represents part of Kingston's carefully planned final chapter on the independent scene, with the wrestler committing to only select dates through November 2026 before stepping away from independent wrestling entirely.

Alberto Del Rio Legal Case

Alberto Del Rio's legal battle may be heading toward a potential agreement, and that possibility is now driving the biggest update in his case. Del Rio remains behind bars after a judge ordered pretrial detention following his domestic violence arrest in San Luis Potosí. But a sudden move to push his next hearing forward has sparked speculation that both sides could be exploring some kind of resolution.

According to reporting from Mexican media, the timeline around the case is shifting quickly. Mexican journalist Flor Rubio, who has worked with Del Rio and his wife, explained that the hearing may now happen sooner than expected, despite an earlier request to extend the legal timeframe.

"El Patrón is in pretrial detention. It is said that the hearing will be moved up tomorrow. They requested an extension of the constitutional term, which expires on Sunday at one in the afternoon, but it seems they are asking for an earlier hearing, and it could be tomorrow," Rubio stated.

That kind of move does not usually happen without a reason. Rubio pointed directly at what it might signal, noting that an agreement appears to be in play. "If that's the case, it's because I think there's some kind of agreement, because if you're asking for a doubling of the constitutional term to gather evidence and you're ahead of that term, it's likely that there will be some kind of agreement."

At the center of the situation is Marie Carmen Rodríguez, who Rubio says has been in direct contact with her. While talk of an agreement is now on the table, the personal reality behind the case is far more complicated. Rubio made clear that justice remains the priority and that releasing Del Rio could create further risk.

"I don't know what kind of agreement, she already told me that she doesn't want any kind of financial agreement, that she doesn't want to grant forgiveness, but what is that she is worried about the children of the Boss who are minors and if he were to stay in jail they would probably go to a children's home, because they have no one to take care of them. I don't know if the settlement means he'll face the trial while free and not in prison. She wants justice, that's why she reported him. She arrived at the hearing with a black eye and bruised face. We have to wait. I believe he should face trial in prison because otherwise Carmen is at risk."

Authorities have already confirmed that Del Rio was arrested in the act after his partner reportedly called for help during an alleged assault. Prosecutors are now evaluating multiple charges, including gender-based violence, which could carry serious prison time if proven. There is also a financial angle, with reports suggesting damages could reach up to one million pesos.

Right now, Del Rio remains in custody, but the sudden push to move the hearing forward has changed the tone of the case. If an agreement is being discussed, the next court appearance could change everything.

Liv Morgan On Milestone WWE Title Victory Against Ronda Rousey: 'So Surreal'

Liv Morgan On Milestone WWE Title Victory Against Ronda Rousey: 'So Surreal'

Since joining WWE over a decade ago, Liv Morgan has been on a steady climb through the company's ranks, building to her recent Royal Rumble victory and a prominent position on the card for WrestleMania 42. One of the most important steps in her journey was winning the Money in the Bank briefcase and using it to beat Ronda Rousey for the title the same night. Speaking to 8 News Now in Las Vegas, Morgan looked back on that historic night in 2022.

"It was so surreal," Morgan said. "Just being in WWE has felt enough for me. So to win my very first-ever World Championship, to win Money in the Bank and to cash in on Ronda Rousey to become the new SmackDown Women's Champion, it was just such a surreal moment. I remember holding my championship and looking out into the crowd and feeling like, 'Wow.' I had just made all my dreams come true."

Morgan then reflected on her upbringing as a pro wrestling fan, crediting her brothers for her interest in the sport. She named WWE Hall of Famer Lita as one of her favorites, with Morgan taking inspiration from Lita's fashion while she was growing up.

This month, following her Royal Rumble win in January, Morgan is set to face Stephanie Vaquer at WrestleMania, with the Women's World Championship on the line. The match between Vaquer and Morgan will take place on the first of the show's two nights, scheduled for Saturday, April 18.

Recently Released TNA Wrestling Star Dani Luna Releases Cryptic Social Media Video Teasing What Is Next

Dani Luna is speaking out following her TNA exit, and she's making it clear that her next move could be a major one. After her final match aired on Thursday's episode of TNA iMPACT, Luna took to social media with a reflective video that centered around one simple question: what's next?

The video features Luna back home in the United Kingdom, where she opens up in a voiceover about the uncertainty ahead and the fear that comes with it. "If it doesn't scare you, maybe it's not worth doing," she admitted during the piece.

That tone continues as Luna hints at bigger ambitions and a desire to push beyond her current limits. "And I know that you see it. I know that you do. But it's not enough anymore," she continued. "We must walk through the fire to truly come out inevitable this time. And I would kick through the crust of the Earth to prove to you that I am one of the best in the world. But maybe, maybe I don't have to."

That last line stands out as particularly significant. In her final appearance for the company, Luna came up short against Jada Stone on Thursday's broadcast. Luna had requested her release following TNA Sacrifice late last month, and at the time, it was noted that there was no bad blood between the two sides.

Now, with her departure official and her mindset clear, all eyes are on what Luna does next. Her cryptic messaging suggests she has her sights set on something bigger, though she has not yet disclosed her specific plans or destination.

Team Name For WWE Tag-Team Champions Damian Priest & R-Truth

Damian Priest and R-Truth's tag team partnership may have just received its official name. The current WWE Tag Team Champions appear to now be going by "Wepa Up," according to newly released merchandise on WWE Shop. The name blends Priest's signature "Wepa" catchphrase with Truth's longtime "What's Up?" slogan, something fans have seen play off each other during their on-screen segments.

Priest and Truth captured the Tag Team titles earlier in March with a victory over Tama Tonga and JC Mateo. Since winning the championship, they have continued their run as an unlikely but entertaining duo that has generated fan interest through their unique chemistry and contrasting personalities.

The new team name appears to be a natural fit for the pairing. By combining the two wrestlers' most recognizable catchphrases into one unified brand, WWE has created an identity that celebrates both competitors equally. The merchandise listings feature the name "Wepa Up" prominently, suggesting this is the direction the company intends to take with their promotional efforts.

While WWE has yet to formally acknowledge the name "Wepa Up" on television programming, the branding on official merchandise strongly suggests that this is the official direction moving forward for the reigning Tag Team Champions. The merchandise release effectively serves as a debut for the team's new identity, even as fans await an on-air acknowledgment of the name.
