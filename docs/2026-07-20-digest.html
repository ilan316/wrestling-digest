Kevin Knight AEW TNT Championship Record

Kevin Knight has officially set a new record for the most successful TNT Championship defenses in a single title reign. Following the July 18 episode of AEW Collision, the company took to social media to recognize Knight's accomplishment. AEW wrote via social media: "Congratulations to 'The Jet' Kevin Knight for holding the new record for successful defenses of the TNT Championship in a single reign."

Knight's latest title defense came against AR Fox in a match that aired on Saturday's Collision after being taped on Thursday at MGM Music Hall in Boston. The victory marked his ninth successful defense since capturing the vacant TNT Championship in a Casino Gauntlet match at AEW Dynasty on April 12.

During his record-breaking reign, Knight has retained the championship against Claudio Castagnoli, MJF, Hook, Brian Cage, Mike Bailey, Myron Reed, Desmond Xavier, Lio Rush, and AR Fox.

The milestone breaks the previous record established by Cody Rhodes during the inaugural TNT Championship reign in 2020. Cody successfully defended the title eight times between May 23 and August 13, a mark that had stood until Knight surpassed it with his victory over Fox. This is the first time the record has been overtaken since Rhodes set it.

Several other notable champions have tied the eight-defense record throughout the title's history. Darby Allin tied the record with eight successful title defenses during his reign from November 7, 2020 to May 12, 2021. Miro then defended the title eight times as well during his reign from May 12, 2021 to September 29, 2021. More recently, Kyle Fletcher tied the then-record with eight successful title defenses during his reign from July 31, 2025 to November 22, 2025. Knight is the 32nd champion in the title's lineage.

Claudio Castagnoli Dream Match Discussion

Claudio Castagnoli recently spoke with the Daily Star about his approach to professional wrestling and career philosophy. The AEW star and Death Riders member addressed the common question wrestlers face about dream matches, revealing that he operates with a distinctly different mindset than many of his peers.

When asked about his dream match, Castagnoli explained that he doesn't actually think in those terms. While he acknowledges that certain matchups would be cool, he is far more interested in the organic moments that naturally occur during his career. He emphasized that his best experiences have come not from long-held aspirations but from opportunities that simply presented themselves at the right time.

Castagnoli used his recent encounter with Blue Panther in CMLL as a perfect illustration of his philosophy. He noted that he was never sitting around thinking he desperately wanted to face the legendary wrestler. Instead, when the opportunity came up, he evaluated it in the moment and decided to pursue it. His willingness to say yes when Blue Panther was available led to what he considers special wrestling.

The veteran wrestler stressed that his approach has been to take whatever opportunities fall into his lap, enjoy the process, and work to make each match special regardless of whether it was something he had long desired. This method of accepting opportunities rather than chasing predetermined goals has, according to Castagnoli, served him exceptionally well throughout his entire career, and he intends to continue following this path forward.

For some longtime wrestling enthusiasts like Arizona James Walsh, TNA represented a lifeline when WWE was the only major option remaining after WCW and ECW closed in 2001. When TNA launched on June 19, 2002, as a pay-per-view only format at under $10 per week, it delivered quality wrestling that stood apart from WWE's offerings. The early years featured standout performers like Jerry Lynn and AJ Styles showcasing the X Division style that would eventually become standard in professional wrestling. The promotion also featured established main event talent including Jeff Jarrett, Scott Hall, Ken Shamrock, and Curt Hennig, creating compelling Wednesday night programming that rekindled wrestling fandom for viewers who had abandoned the industry.

However, the company's trajectory shifted with organizational changes that alienated some fans. In 2004, TNA transitioned from a traditional four-sided ring to a six-sided ring, a decision made to accommodate the promotion's expansion onto FOX Sports Net on Friday afternoons. While intended to modernize the presentation, the six-sided ring created technical problems for wrestlers and viewers alike. The different ring shape resulted in tighter ropes, a harder mat surface making bumps more painful, and awkward rope work that could lead to buckle bumps instead of traditional Irish whips. Despite these aesthetic concerns, TNA eventually secured television time on Spike TV in October 2005 following WWE RAW's departure from the network, establishing an 11 p.m. Saturday night slot that generated moderate viewership numbers. The arrival of Kurt Angle further shifted the company's direction and booking priorities.

TNA continues pursuing talent in today's wrestling landscape. According to sources within the organization, TNA expressed interest in signing recently released WWE performers Tyson Dupont and Tyriek Igwe, formerly known as High Ryze in WWE NXT. Both wrestlers have television experience and previously worked multiple dates for TNA, making positive impressions on company officials. They began teaming together in NXT in 2023 and later aligned with Wes Lee before both were released by WWE in April. Their availability and prior TNA work make them attractive candidates for the promotion's current roster needs.

One recent TNA talent, Mustafa Ali, has spoken positively about his experience with the organization. Ali intentionally signed a short-term deal when joining TNA in 2024, uncertain about how the company would treat former WWE performers. However, he discovered what he describes as a genuine family atmosphere within TNA, noting that wrestlers socialize together late into the night with camaraderie extending beyond typical professional relationships. Ali credits himself with revitalizing TNA's X-Division, citing his championship runs that generated media coverage and made the division a focal point of programming once again. While respecting the work Leon Slater performed with the X-Division title and the subsequent contributions of Cedric Alexander, Ali maintains that his tenure specifically reignited fan interest in the division through high-profile championship defenses and sustained visibility.

Historical Wrestling Title Changes July 20

July 20th stands as one of the busiest dates on the professional wrestling calendar when it comes to championship changes. Historical records indicate that 271 notable and major title changes have occurred on this specific date throughout wrestling history, demonstrating its significance across multiple eras and promotions.

The earliest documented title change on July 20th dates back to 1964, when Bull Curry and Fred Curry won the NWA International Tag Team Titles. This began what would become a consistent pattern of championship activity on this date. Nine years later in 1973, Jack Brisco captured the prestigious NWA World Heavyweight Title, marking one of the most significant championship reigns in professional wrestling history.

Throughout the 1980s, July 20th continued to see championship activity. In 1985, two title changes occurred: Chamaco Valaguez won the NWA World Middleweight Title while the NWA World Welterweight Title was vacated. By 1992, the national wrestling landscape had shifted as Earthquake and Typhoon captured the WWF Tag Team Titles on this date, signaling the growing importance of tag team wrestling in the World Wrestling Federation.

The 1998-2001 period proved particularly active for July 20th title changes. In 1998, two significant WCW championships changed hands when The Giant and Scott Hall won the WCW World Tag Team Titles and Bret Hart won the WCW United States Heavyweight Title. The following years saw New Japan Pro Wrestling championships change hands on this date, with Hiroyoshi Tenzan and Satoshi Kojima winning the IWGP Tag Team Titles in 2000 and Tatsuhito Takaiwa winning the IWGP Junior Heavyweight Title that same year. In 2001, Masayuki Naruse won the IWGP Junior Heavyweight Title while Gedo and Jado captured the IWGP Junior Heavyweight Tag Team Titles.

The mid-2000s brought international wrestling promotions into the July 20th title change picture. In 2007, Místico and Negro Casas won the CMLL World Tag Team Titles. The following year in 2008 proved particularly active, with three WWE title changes occurring: Shelton Benjamin won the WWE United States Title, Michelle McCool won the WWE Divas Title, and Curt Hawkins and Zack Ryder won the WWE Tag Team Titles.

From 2012 to 2014, July 20th remained an active date for championship changes across multiple promotions. Diamante won the Occidente Heavyweight Title in 2012. The year 2013 saw two notable changes with Shinsuke Nakamura winning the IWGP Intercontinental Title and Command Bolshoi winning the REINA/CMLL International Title. In 2014, Sadico won the Occidente Welterweight Title while The Miz captured the WWE Intercontinental Title.

The 2019-2020 period demonstrated the date's continued relevance. In 2019, Jay Briscoe and Mark Briscoe won the ROH World Tag Team Titles. The following year in 2020, Sasha Banks captured the WWE RAW Women's Title and Shelton Benjamin won the WWE 24/7 Title.

Recent years have shown a concentration of title changes on July 20th, particularly within TNA Wrestling. In 2024, five titles changed hands on this date. Ace Austin and Chris Bey won the TNA World Tag Team Titles, Mike Bailey won the TNA X-Division Title, PCO won the Canadian International Heavyweight Title, Nic Nemeth won the TNA World Title, and PCO also captured the TNA Digital Media Title in a notable accomplishment.

The 2025 iteration of July 20th continued this pattern with four titles changing hands. Leon Slater won the TNA X-Division Title, KENTA won the GHC Heavyweight Title, and Jeff Hardy and Matt Hardy won the TNA World Tag Team Titles. Most recently, Jacy Jayne, a former NXT star, won the TNA Knockouts World Title, rounding out the championship activity for this historically significant date.
