🔥 Chris Jericho AEW Return

Chris Jericho made his surprise return to AEW Dynamite on April 1, 2026, ending months of speculation about his future in professional wrestling. After being away from television for a year, Jericho appeared in his hometown of Winnipeg to declare he was "home," leaving fans curious about what his next chapter would entail. The return itself was significant because it came after widespread reports that Jericho was being courted by WWE for a potential retirement run and Hall of Fame induction.

Tony Khan addressed Jericho's decision in an appearance on the Coach & Bro Show, confirming that Jericho had been a free agent with multiple options available to him. Khan stated that he had been in constant communication with Jericho and that the wrestler specifically wanted to return to AEW, particularly on the date in Winnipeg that they had circled "a long time going back to last year." Khan emphasized that Jericho's choice to remain in AEW rather than pursue opportunities elsewhere was made deliberately and that Jericho has consistently expressed his love for AEW and his desire to continue there. Khan further explained that the decision was entirely Jericho's to make as a free agent.

However, the wrestling community's reaction to Jericho's return has been decidedly mixed. Bully Ray, speaking on Busted Open Radio, expressed strong disapproval of the decision, arguing that the AEW fanbase does not deserve Jericho. Ray pointed out what he viewed as hypocrisy among AEW fans, noting that many had previously booed and bad-mouthed Jericho to the point where he felt compelled to leave Twitter due to the negativity. Ray claimed that the sudden cheering for Jericho upon his return was not genuine appreciation for the performer but rather relief that he had not signed with WWE. Ray contended that Jericho has accomplished everything he could organically accomplish in AEW and questioned who he would have meaningful feuds with moving forward.

When asked about potential opponents for Jericho, Ray dismissed the suggestion of Kyle Fletcher as a credible challenger, claiming such a feud would only last about six months and that Jericho would have no one else to work with afterward. Ray emphasized that while he was happy for both Jericho and Khan securing the deal, he did not believe the arrangement benefited Jericho as a performer. Ray remained concerned that the AEW fanbase, which had previously turned on Jericho, could do so again in the future.

Jim Cornette offered even more scathing criticism of Jericho's decision, calling him "a fucking idiot" on his podcast The Jim Cornette Experience. Cornette argued that if Jericho had been free and clear to go to WWE and declined the opportunity, it represented a poor business decision. Cornette outlined the potential benefits of a WWE retirement run, including lucrative Hall of Fame opportunities, mainstream exposure, and a limited schedule that would involve only a few high-profile matches rather than a grueling full-time schedule in what he characterized as a "secondary company." Cornette suggested that even most AEW fans do not want to see Jericho wrestle in AEW and that the quality of his recent work in the promotion has been poor. He contrasted the prestige of ending his career on WWE's "strongest television program in the world" with what he saw as settling for obscurity in AEW, particularly at this stage of Jericho's career.

From an analytical perspective, a Wrestling Observer writer acknowledged surprise at Jericho's return, having previously expected him to sign with WWE. The writer noted that Jericho faced the challenge of overcoming what was termed "Jericho fatigue" among the AEW fanbase during 2024 and 2025. The columnist pointed out that Jericho's final run in AEW before his time away, involving his Learning Tree stable, had not resonated with audiences and felt similar to Cody Rhodes' forgettable later feuds in AEW.

The writer acknowledged that Jericho returned in excellent physical condition and appeared revived, having smartly made fans wait before explaining his return plans. However, the analyst expressed skepticism about whether AEW fans would genuinely accept Jericho at a main event level again. The writer noted that potential dream matches with figures like MJF, Kenny Omega, and Hangman Page have already been explored, and while Jericho has not wrestled Swerve Strickland, Andrade, Will Ospreay, Kazuchika Okada, and Kyle Fletcher multiple times, these matchups do not feel particularly compelling in 2026.

The writer proposed two possible directions for Jericho's return: a continuation of his involvement in the war against the Don Callis Family as a babyface, which could naturally align with his previous partnership with Kenny Omega as the Golden Jets, or a midcard role. However, the analyst expressed doubt about whether AEW fans were ready to embrace Jericho at the upper card level once again, given how much has changed in a year and how current top-tier competitors like Swerve Strickland, Andrade, and Konosuke Takeshita have elevated the World title picture.

Jericho was scheduled to address his return in more detail during a later episode of AEW Dynamite, with speculation that his story would continue to unfold in the coming weeks, possibly with his next significant appearance at AEW Dynasty or a date in his native Vancouver.

🔥 Tony Khan AEW CM Punk Incident and Company Direction

TONY KHAN REFLECTS ON BRAWL OUT AND RESHAPES AEW LEADERSHIP

During a recent appearance on The Coach and Bro Show podcast with Jonathan Coachman and Vince Russo, Tony Khan addressed one of the most controversial moments in All Elite Wrestling history: the infamous "Brawl Out" incident at AEW All Out 2022. The media scrum turned chaotic when CM Punk, fresh off winning the AEW World Championship against Jon Moxley, launched into a public tirade against fellow AEW stars Adam Page, Colt Cabana, and The Elite, including Kenny Omega and The Young Bucks. The situation quickly spiraled into a physical altercation backstage between The Elite and Punk, who was backed by Ace Steel. Reports indicated that Omega and at least one of the Bucks suffered injuries during the skirmish, leading Khan to suspend all parties involved and ultimately contributing to Punk's departure from the company in 2023.

Speaking several years removed from the controversy, Khan reflected on what he learned from the ordeal and how it changed his approach to running the company. He explained that his primary focus immediately following the incident was ensuring AEW's survival and continued operation. "I came out of that wanting for AEW to succeed and for AEW to continue more than anything, and what we were able to do to rebound from that," Khan stated. He acknowledged that while he had hoped everyone could work together, that proved impossible. However, he emphasized that over the past few years, AEW has remained strong, which he considers the most important outcome.

Khan admitted that the situation caught him off guard and forced him to reconsider his decision-making and communication strategies. "I learned a number of things. I probably would do things differently, say things differently, but like you said, it wasn't something I was expecting," he explained. He noted that despite being in the wrestling business for nearly four years at that point, the Brawl Out incident was a first for him. "You're very experienced in wrestling, and that was very much a 'welcome to wrestling' moment in some aspects, because I had been in wrestling for almost four years at that point, but that was certainly a first for me. As with all firsts, you learn from them, and you hope not to ever have a repeat."

VINCE RUSSO ON KHAN'S LEADERSHIP STYLE

Following the interview, Vince Russo reflected on his conversation with Khan and offered insight into Khan's character and leadership approach. Russo suggested that Khan's greatest asset could also be his biggest challenge. "One of Tony Khan's biggest assets could very well also be his biggest detriment. The dude doesn't have an unkind bone in his body. He is thoroughly and genuinely a good-hearted man that can be no other way—it's just his nature and make-up," Russo wrote. He compared Khan's kindness to the tougher personalities who have traditionally held power in the wrestling industry, noting that those at the top of WWE didn't typically get there by being "nice guys."

However, Russo insisted that Khan should maintain his character rather than change. "My advice to TK would be—don't ever change. Don't ever change one iota. The most important thing to any of us should be WHO we see when we look in that mirror every morning, because that's the real, honest to goodness TRUTH and measure of who we really are," Russo said. He expressed genuine appreciation for Khan's demeanor and positive energy, suggesting that such positivity is rare and valuable in today's world.

EXPLORING OTHER BUSINESS VENTURES

Beyond the Brawl Out discussion, Khan revealed details about other significant moments in his tenure as AEW president. When asked about a meeting with Shane McMahon in 2024, Khan described it as a pleasant conversation. The meeting had generated significant buzz when a photo of the two together leaked online, sparking speculation about a potential partnership or major role for McMahon within AEW. Behind the scenes, sources had indicated that McMahon expressed interest in a major role within the company, even mentioning a desire to "run things," though his unfamiliarity with AEW's product and business model reportedly became a sticking point.

Khan explained the circumstances of their meeting, which took place at an airport in Dallas. "I had one really pleasant conversation with Shane. Shane reached out to me and said, 'I'd love to get together. Where are you going to be?' I was going to be in Dallas. 'I can meet you here.' We had a nice conversation. We talked for a few hours in the airport," Khan recounted. He expressed surprise at how quickly the photo leaked and speculation emerged from what was meant to be a private discussion. "I thought it was really strange that a photo came out from it. I thought it was pretty unusual. It could just be somebody who worked at the airport, but I know I had nothing to do with it. It's very unusual," Khan said. He noted his surprise in the photo compared to McMahon's more composed expression.

Khan clarified that the meeting was a one-time interaction and that McMahon's interests didn't necessarily align with an on-screen wrestling character role. "I've only had the one really nice talk with him. I also thought it was quite impressive that in one conversation, it somehow got out that I talked to him. It was a pleasant chat and he seemed like a really interesting guy. I don't think the role of being an on-screen person in a wrestling show seemed like the thing he was particularly interested in, but he had a lot of interesting things and experiences that are totally different than mine," Khan explained.

THE WWE ACQUISITION BID

During the same interview, Khan addressed his previously disclosed attempt to acquire WWE during its 2023 sale process. Court filings revealed that Base 10, the ownership group behind AEW, had submitted a $6.9 billion bid for WWE, which ultimately went to Endeavor and led to the merger with UFC and creation of TKO. Khan confirmed his strong interest in the acquisition process and explained his reasoning for placing a bid.

"I was very interested in the process, and I think that if there was going to be a sale, I would at least want to have a chance to be involved. And if it had gone for any less than that, I would have really kicked myself," Khan said. When asked whether he was given a fair opportunity to compete in a genuine bidding process, Khan remained respectful of confidentiality agreements while acknowledging the process may not have been entirely transparent. "While respecting the confidentiality of that, I would say I don't have any complaints about the process from my side that I'd want to talk about right now. I do think there was some truth to that, but again, I want to respect the confidentiality of the whole thing," he stated.

Khan emphasized that he had no regrets about submitting his bid at the valuation level he did. "I would also say that at the time, it seemed to me—and I'll reiterate—that if it had gone for any less, I would have really beaten myself up, like, 'Why wouldn't you put in a bid?' I thought it was a very reasonable number that I put in," Khan explained. When Coachman confirmed the bid was in the $6 to 7 billion range, Khan acknowledged the reporting while maintaining discretion about specifics. He added that despite the failed acquisition attempt, he has focused his attention on developing AEW, noting that the years since have been "really good years for AEW."

Throughout his podcast appearance, Khan demonstrated a reflective approach to his leadership journey, acknowledging both his missteps during the Brawl Out crisis and his ambitious attempts to expand his wrestling empire through the WWE acquisition bid. His comments suggest a maturing understanding of the wrestling industry's complexities while maintaining his core values of integrity and respect, even when facing significant business challenges and conflicts.

🔥 MJF Fake Pilot Abduction Story

During his recent appearance on The Adam Friedland Show, AEW World Champion MJF recounted a bizarre and disturbing backstage incident involving a man who attempted to impersonate a pilot in order to lure him away from an AEW event.

According to MJF, the man confidently approached him backstage and claimed that Tony Khan had arranged for him to fly MJF home after the show. At the time, the story seemed plausible to MJF because his father was recovering from a heart attack and Khan was aware of the situation. MJF had also traveled on Khan's private jet on previous occasions.

MJF explained the initial encounter: "A pilot walks up to me and he goes, 'Mr. Friedman, I'm going to be flying you out back home after the show's proceedings.' Which, to be fair, I've flown on the jet a couple times with my boss up to that point anyway. Not to the island, well, to the island, as in Long Island, the only island that matters."

Believing the gesture was genuine, MJF went to thank Khan for the arrangement. However, when he knocked on Khan's door to express his gratitude, Khan was completely confused about what he was talking about. "And he goes, 'What the fuck are you talking about?' And I go, 'The pilot just told—' He goes, 'I didn't fucking book you a jet to go back home tonight,'" MJF recalled.

Realizing something was amiss, MJF returned with AEW EVP QT Marshall to confront the fake pilot. During this confrontation, Marshall questioned the man about who had hired him. The man maintained his story that Tony Khan had sent him. At that point, Marshall gave him an opportunity to prove his legitimacy by requesting to see his pilot's license.

"And QT goes, before the guy leaves, he goes 'Wait a minute, wait a minute, we'll let you stay if you show us your pilot's license.' And the guy, I shit you not, does this: 'Ah, I left it at home.' Get the fuck out of here," MJF said.

The situation escalated when security became involved and apprehended the man. What followed was even more disturbing. When security questioned the man about his intentions, he admitted the truth about what he was planning to do. "But they asked this guy, 'What were you planning on doing?' And he was honest. He said he was going to fucking abduct me. An adult man," MJF stated.

MJF noted that to this day, nobody knows how the man managed to gain backstage access to the event. He acknowledged that the security breach was likely due to building security rather than AEW's own security team, but also mentioned that AEW's security procedures have since been improved. "We still don't know how he got backstage. So, to be fair, our security team has changed since then. But it probably was the building security, not even AEW security's fault," MJF explained.

The story was later corroborated by multiple sources within AEW. Both Marko Stunt and QT Marshall have confirmed that the incident actually took place. While fans have questioned the validity of the story, these confirmations from people directly involved have lent credibility to MJF's account. Additionally, historical references suggest MJF has been discussing this incident publicly dating back to 2020, indicating this was not a recent fabrication.

The incident highlights serious concerns about event security and how individuals can potentially gain backstage access under false pretenses. It serves as a sobering reminder of the security vulnerabilities that can exist at large events and the importance of proper identification verification procedures.

Will Ospreay NJPW G1 Climax Return

AEW's Will Ospreay Shares Thoughts On Returning To NJPW For G1 Climax Tournament

AEW's cordial relationship with NJPW has led to many stars from both promotions featuring in each other's shows, and the recently returned Will Ospreay is keen to make good use of that arrangement as he is eager to wrestle in the G1 Climax.

Ospreay returned to AEW last month and a few weeks later wrestled in NJPW in a tag team match. After returning to in-ring competition in AEW, Will Ospreay made his first appearance in NJPW in over two years at the Sakura Genesis event in Tokyo on April 4. He teamed up with his United Empire partners, Henare and Great-O-Khan, to defeat El Desperado, Taichi, and Yuya Uemura in a six-man tag team match.

Will Ospreay spoke to Tokyo Sports after the event and confirmed that he couldn't appear at the Osaka-jo Hall on June 14 for the NJPW Dominion event because he's getting married to AEW's Alex Windsor the next day. However, he hopes to participate in many matches in Japan this year, and wants to win the NEVER Openweight 6-Man Tag Team Championship, teaming up with Henare and Great-O-Khan.

"I can't appear at Osaka-jo Hall (June 14) because my wedding (to AEW's Alex Windsor) is the day after the event, but I want to participate in as many big New Japan matches as possible this year. AEW is the best environment, but I have a special feeling for Japan, and I also want to spend more time with the members of United Empire," Will Ospreay said.

Speaking further, Will Ospreay noted that it's hard to ask Tony Khan for six weeks off to compete in this year's G1 Climax, which will run from July 11 to August 16, 2026. However, he hopes to enter the tournament at some point down the line. "It's hard to ask Tony (Khan) this year if I can take about six weeks off, but I've talked to my wife about it, and I do want to participate in the G1 Climax again someday. I've never won it, so I want to try again," said Ospreay.

Ospreay, who has won several titles in NJPW, is also targeting the IWGP Heavyweight Championship, which he previously won in 2021 when it was called the IWGP World Heavyweight Championship. The Heavyweight title was revived after it was combined with the IWGP World Heavyweight title in January, and Ospreay wants to get his hands on that belt.

"Also, the fourth-generation IWGP Heavyweight belt, which was revived this year, has been worn by Hiroshi Tanahashi, Kazuchika Okada, AJ Styles, Kenny Omega, and others, and I've always admired them. So I also want that belt," Will Ospreay said.

The former AEW International Champion knows the current IWGP Heavyweight Champion, Callum Newman, quite well, having mentored him from his teen years. Newman won the title from Yota Tsuji at NJPW's Sakura Genesis show, where Ospreay was ringside to watch his protege capture the championship and even congratulated him after the match.

Will Ospreay will be in action this Sunday at AEW Dynasty, where he takes on Jon Moxley for the Continental Championship.

Thunder Rosa Dual Contract with AEW and CMLL

Thunder Rosa Opens Up About AEW/CMLL Dual Contract, Says It's Been In The Works For 'About A Year'

Former AEW Women's World Champion Thunder Rosa is now operating under a dual contract arrangement with both AEW and CMLL. Rosa discussed this significant career development during an appearance on Busted Open, explaining the timeline and circumstances that led to the finalization of the deal.

According to Rosa, the groundwork for bringing her into CMLL on a more consistent and stable basis had been ongoing for approximately one year. However, the process experienced a setback following a personal injury. The negotiations were revived roughly two weeks before the official announcement, with talks occurring between Rosa's representatives and Mike Johnson, who oversees AEW talent relations.

The timeline moved quickly once both sides recommitted to the arrangement. Rosa learned that the deal would become official just five days after restarting serious discussions, which placed the announcement merely two days before her departure to Mexico City.

Rosa expressed the profound significance of this opportunity in her career. She stated: "Well, I heard about this news that was possibly gonna happen, like less than two weeks ago, and we had some talks with Mike who is the person in charge of all the AEW talent. But this is something that we've been trying to work on for about a year to do something more stable in CMLL, so I could go more often. But it didn't pan out after my injury so, we started talking again, I guess, about two weeks ago, and once they told me it was gonna be official was like five days or so (ago). Like two days before I went to Mexico City, and I mean, it is a huge deal for me because this is something that I've been wanting to do for, jeez, since I started wrestling. I wanted to be a part of CMLL since it's one of the oldest companies in Mexico and also is one of the most rigorous companies that you will work as a professional wrestler even in Mexico…"

Rosa's first slate of matches under this arrangement took place immediately, as she wrestled in three back-to-back shows for CMLL during the weekend of April 4th.

AEW Collision: Fairway To Hell is coming to television in May. The company announced that the Collision special will air on Saturday, May 9, from the SoFi Center in West Palm Beach, Florida. Britt Baker AEW to JCW

Vince Russo, who currently serves as an investor and lead booker for Juggalo Championship Wrestling, made a pitch to AEW President Tony Khan during an appearance on the Coach and Bro Show podcast. The request came during a broader discussion about Britt Baker's recent absence from AEW programming and her recent statements that her return timing is not within her control.

When asked about Baker's extended time off television, Khan confirmed that the accomplished AEW women's wrestler remains under contract with the company. He explained that no talent in AEW is overlooked and that booking decisions are made on a case-by-case basis, depending on multiple factors. Khan cited limited time slots, fluctuating creative directions, injuries, and shifting priorities as reasons why wrestlers may not appear regularly on the program.

As the conversation progressed, Russo made his pitch directly to Khan. He expressed genuine interest in bringing Baker to JCW Lunacy and framed it as a mutually beneficial arrangement. Russo told Khan that if AEW had too many performers on the roster, he would be willing to feature Baker on his show while simultaneously promoting AEW to his audience. Russo stated he would guarantee increased viewership for AEW through the appearance.

Khan's response was notably non-committal. He first clarified that he was not suggesting AEW had an overcrowded roster, reiterating that each situation requires individual evaluation. When Russo directly asked whether his question was a rejection of the idea, Khan left the door open. Instead of refusing the proposition, Khan simply replied "No, it's not. You never know," suggesting that a Baker appearance at JCW remained a possibility rather than a closed matter.

The exchange highlights the ongoing interest in Britt Baker across the wrestling industry despite her current absence from AEW television. Her status remains a topic of discussion among wrestling media and prominent figures within the business.

Megan Bayne and Lena Kross AEW Divine Dominion

Megan Bayne is opening up about a difficult change in AEW, while also looking ahead to the future of Divine Dominion. During a recent appearance on Close Up with Renee Paquette, Bayne addressed Penelope Ford's injury and the subsequent shift that now sees Lena Kross stepping in as her new tag team partner. Ford was injured on the February 25 episode of AEW Dynamite after taking a dive, with AEW President Tony Khan later indicating she could be sidelined for several months. The setback forced a shake-up in the team dynamic, leading to Bayne aligning with Kross going forward.

Speaking alongside Kross, Bayne didn't hide her disappointment over Ford being out of action, while also acknowledging the realities that come with competing in the ring. "Obviously, this current situation to me is a huge change and it's very upsetting," Bayne said. "But Penelope is an athlete, as we all are. So she knows that this sport comes with a lot of risk and you can't always avoid injury. It's never welcomed, but it's not always avoidable. So I'm obviously upset to not have her by my side."

Bayne went on to reflect on their close partnership and made it clear that Ford's recovery is the top priority right now. "Basically, since I debuted here, full time, we've been alongside one another. But at the moment, I think her focus just needs to be on getting back healthy, healing up at home, and my focus is going to remain at keeping the rest of the roster in line. I know that even if it's temporarily from the sidelines, she'll be supportive."

Meanwhile, Lena Kross is embracing the spotlight and the opportunity that comes with stepping into a high-profile role. "Pressure isn't a problem," Kross said. "It's a privilege. I know exactly who I am. I know that this is exactly where I'm supposed to be." Kross doubled down on that confidence, making it clear she intends to prove she belongs. "Some people might say I got it too early, but what I'm doing is proving exactly why I am here and exactly why I am one half of the AEW Women's Tag Team Champions."

MJF WWE Era Commentary

MJF recently appeared on Late Night Grin to discuss various topics surrounding his career and wrestling history, including which WWE era he would ideally want to be a part of competing in.

When asked about his preferred WWE era, MJF identified the Attitude Era as both the obvious and correct choice. He explained his reasoning by noting the exceptional quality of talkers available during that time period. "The easy answer is Attitude Era, and it's the right answer. Just is. And I watch that — to be honest, and there are some people who are going to agree with this because they're not smooth-brained and there's some people that aren't. I really think if you plop me into any era of professional wrestling, I'd be in the same spot that I'm in now. Genuinely. I do truly feel in my heart of hearts I'm the most complete professional wrestler right now in the world today."

MJF went on to elaborate on his Attitude Era preference, emphasizing that it was primarily about the level of competition on the microphone rather than in-ring ability. "Um and my body feels good for the most part. But Attitude Era — I think the reason I'd want to be involved in that is because the level of competition of guys that could talk. Forget the wrestling. We all know great wrestling is fun. But for me to have been able to go back and forth on the stick with The Rock, or with Steve Austin, or Mick Foley or Shawn. Not saying Bret was a good promo, but he was good in his own way. I think me and Bret would be interesting. God, there's so much, dude. There's just so much money there."

The AEW World Champion also discussed the prospect of facing Triple H during that era, suggesting such a feud would have been entertaining but potentially controversial. "Triple H would be really fun. I'd probably get in trouble in that era if we had a feud. 'What did he say? You can't say that!' No, I'm a big Triple H mark too, which is like sacrilege in my locker room because — you know."

MJF proceeded to offer praise for Triple H as a performer, arguing that the WWE legend deserves greater recognition than he typically receives. He suggested that Triple H's personal relationship with Stephanie McMahon has unfairly colored public perception of his accomplishments. "But I do think he will never get his flowers because of the fact that he was, you know, banging the f**king boss's daughter. Everybody just assumes that's the only reason why he got over. The fact of the matter is — and I've said it before in interviews, he made the guys that were hot hotter. He made sure there was nothing about him that was likable. And he didn't do it in a cheap way. Sorry, Shane Douglas. He didn't do it in a cheap way, there's a difference."

When the conversation turned to Mick Foley's past consideration of a comeback, MJF revealed that he had previously discussed the possibility with Foley directly. "Look, he's talked about it openly. We sat down and we had a discussion of what that would look like. And it was a very good talk. I still think he's a bitch, but you know. If whatever tirzepatide peptide he's on allows him to lose some more weight, maybe he can still make a comeback. We'll see what happens."
