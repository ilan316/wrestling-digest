🔥 WrestleMania 2026 Coverage and Aftermath

WRESTLEMANIA 42: IN-RING TRIUMPHS AND BACKSTAGE CHAOS

WWE's WrestleMania 42 weekend in Las Vegas delivered unforgettable in-ring moments alongside record attendance, yet the festivities were marred by widespread security failures that left talent shaken and frustrated.

PAIGE'S TRIUMPHANT RETURN

The most emotional story of the weekend belonged to Paige, who made her highly anticipated WWE return after more than eight years away. Cleared to compete following extensive rehabilitation and medical evaluation, Paige replaced an injured Nikki Bella in the Women's Tag Team Championship match on Night One. The surprise announcement sent shockwaves through the wrestling community as Paige and Brie Bella defeated Lash Legend, Nia Jax, Alexa Bliss, and Charlotte Flair to capture the titles.

Paige shared raw emotional footage of the moment she learned she had been cleared to wrestle again. In the video, she was visibly overwhelmed, describing the journey as a long road filled with doubt and stress. She admitted she never believed WWE would clear her to return to in-ring competition, making the news all the more meaningful. The moment proved transformative, as she expressed gratitude to those who supported her through her recovery and acknowledged the role sobriety played in her comeback.

Nikki Bella, while unable to compete due to an ankle injury suffered on the March 27 episode of SmackDown, was present at ringside with a crutch to help secure victory for her sister and Paige. Nikki revealed the emotional difficulty of having to keep her injury secret heading into WrestleMania, as she felt she was "lying" to the WWE Universe who expected to see both Bella Twins compete. She also clarified that Nia Jax actually helped her during the injury, contrary to online speculation, as Jax allowed Nikki to step out of the ring rather than being thrown out when she could not put weight on her damaged foot.

The ankle injury proved severe, with an MRI revealing a torn, sprained, and fractured ankle requiring immediate surgery. Nikki acknowledged this was likely her final WrestleMania opportunity in the United States, as future Mania events will take place in Saudi Arabia before her contract expires in early 2028.

OBA FEMI'S SHOCKING UPSET

In one of the most stunning results of the weekend, young star Oba Femi defeated Brock Lesnar in under five minutes on Night Two, leaving his boots and gloves in the ring in what appeared to be a retirement gesture. Lesnar's symbolic exit triggered immediate speculation about his WWE future, with Paul Heyman delivering an emotional promo on Raw the following night that further fueled retirement rumors.

However, sources within WWE suggest Lesnar may not have officially retired. While many believe he will step away at some point in 2026, possibly at SummerSlam in his home state of Minnesota, the situation remains unclear internally. The original creative plan had called for a multi-match feud between Lesnar and Femi, but those plans were altered in the weeks before WrestleMania.

Femi's victory positioned him as a major rising star, with the 23-year-old immediately calling out Roman Reigns and establishing himself as a top main event player moving forward. His dominant performance left little doubt about his credentials as a future world champion.

NXT CALL-UPS AND EMERGING TALENT

The Raw after WrestleMania saw multiple NXT stars finally receive their main roster promotions. Ethan Page made an impressive Raw debut, defeating Je'Von Evans in a match for an Intercontinental Championship opportunity. Page declared that "Monday Night Raw will never be the same" and promised to capture the Intercontinental Championship.

Sol Ruca also debuted on Raw, facing WWE Women's World Champion Liv Morgan in a competitive bout. Despite the loss, Ruca impressed with her in-ring work and mic skills. She revealed she was only informed of her main roster debut a few days into WrestleMania week and was surprised to receive a full match rather than just a backstage segment. She also named her dream opponents on the main roster, including IYO SKY and surprisingly Randy Orton.

Joe Hendry was announced for a concert segment on the following week's Raw, signaling an imminent main roster call-up. Multiple other NXT talents are expected to receive promotions in the coming weeks, including Jaida Parker, Kelani Jordan, Zaria, Blake Monroe, and Ricky Saints.

OTHER IN-RING STORYLINES

Cody Rhodes retained his Undisputed WWE Championship against Randy Orton in the Night One main event, though not before a fascinating dynamic shift occurred. Despite entering as the babyface, Rhodes received heavy boos while Orton garnered significant cheers. The match ended with Orton delivering a Punt Kick to Rhodes, leaving him standing tall.

Rhea Ripley defeated Jade Cargill for the WWE Women's Championship on Night Two. Though Ripley won decisively, Cargill called her championship reign a "practice run" and promised that next time would be different. She acknowledged wanting more competitive matches during her title reign and was eager for a rematch.

Trick Williams won the United States Championship from Sami Zayn, securing his first WrestleMania victory. Williams acknowledged his connection with fellow NXT alumni Oba Femi and Je'Von Evans, noting that all three had competed in the NXT Stand & Deliver main event a year prior.

Jacob Fatu defeated Drew McIntyre in an Unsanctioned Match on Night One, improving to 2-0 at WrestleMania. Fatu later revealed that Roman Reigns gave him important advice before the match, telling him to "calm down" and "enjoy the moment" before walking out to compete.

John Cena hosted the two-night event and participated in a memorable segment with Danhausen and the "Minihausens" on Night Two. Cena later called the moment "one of my favorite WrestleMania moments ever."

NIKKI BELLA'S INJURY AND RECOVERY

The ankle injury that sidelined Nikki Bella occurred just 30 seconds into her SmackDown match against Charlotte Flair and Alexa Bliss on March 27. Despite immediately recognizing the severity of the injury, Nikki pushed through the entire match, driven by the knowledge that the WrestleMania storyline was just beginning and not wanting to "ruin it for everyone."

Following MRI results the morning after the injury, doctors informed Nikki she had torn, sprained, and fractured her ankle and needed immediate surgery. She was unable to return home and flew directly to Birmingham for surgical intervention.

Nikki emphasized that keeping the injury secret was emotionally difficult for her, as she values transparency with the WWE Universe. However, she found a way to be part of WrestleMania 42 by appearing ringside on crutches to help secure the victory for Brie and Paige.

MAJOR SECURITY FAILURES AND FAN BEHAVIOR ISSUES

The most serious issue emerging from WrestleMania weekend involved widespread security failures at the MGM Grand hotel where WWE talent was staying. Multiple wrestlers reported incidents of invasive fan behavior ranging from inappropriate filming to physical confrontations.

CM Punk knocked a fan's phone out of his hand after the individual was recording AJ Lee and Bayley without permission. The fan later told TMZ he was not seeking legal action, only an apology and a handshake.

Booker T described the fan environment as the worst he has experienced at any WrestleMania, claiming fans were "camped out everywhere" and that one individual filmed him inside a bathroom. He emphasized the need for better security and said he could "only imagine" what active wrestlers experienced.

Sean Waltman reported being physically assaulted, having a camera shoved in his face, and being followed into a bathroom by a fan. He stated that while he has no problem with public photography, he draws a firm line when personal space is invaded.

Drew McIntyre took to social media to address the situation, expressing appreciation for fans who spent money on meet-and-greets while condemning those who "rushed" him and his family at the hotel, kept children up until 2 a.m., or shoved cameras in his face without consent. He stated these fans were "lucky I'm a calm natured person outside the ring."

Additional incidents involved Damian Priest protecting female talent from overzealous fans, Royce Keys being followed by a persistent fan asking for photos and autographs, and Randy Orton being surrounded by crowds in the hotel lobby.

Behind the scenes, WWE talent expressed near-universal frustration about the lack of security, with multiple wrestlers summarizing the situation with the blunt phrase "what security?" Some wrestlers chose to stay at different hotels to avoid fan crowds, creating travel complications for WWE's coordination.

Sources indicated that rising meet-and-greet prices under TKO Group Holdings may have exacerbated the problem by pricing out fans who would normally attend official events, leading them to seek interactions in uncontrolled hotel settings instead. One WWE source noted that meet-and-greet costs had become prohibitively expensive, limiting access in structured settings.

WWE staff reportedly joked darkly about the situation, with one remark suggesting they should "be allowed to superkick one annoying fan a day." Multiple talents reported that fans used children to pressure wrestlers into signing items, creating uncomfortable guilt-trip scenarios.

Kevin Nash addressed the lack of international attendance, noting that strict U.S. immigration policies and current political tensions have deterred foreign fans from traveling to events, resulting in a more domestic crowd than in previous years.

ADDITIONAL STORYLINES AND MOMENTS

Logan Paul admitted he was "wrong" for attacking teammate IShowSpeed after losing their six-man tag match. Initially blaming Speed for the loss, Paul later reflected on his harsh punch and attempt to put Speed through the announcer's table, ultimately taking accountability for his actions.

Bianca Belair announced her pregnancy during Night One, forcing John Cena to update the attendance number on the fly as she was no longer able to compete.

John Cena detailed his hair transplant surgery from November 2024, explaining the recovery process and acknowledging that fan pressure regarding his hair loss ultimately motivated him to pursue the procedure.

The Rock announced plans to adapt the Paige biopic "Fighting With My Family" into a stage musical in collaboration with director Stephen Merchant. The project will feature book and lyrics by Jon Brittain with music by Miranda Cooper and Nick Coler, with development underway for a potential 2027 debut.

WWE SmackDown viewership for April 17 drew 1.580 million viewers with a 0.42 demo rating, up from 1.412 million viewers the previous week, demonstrating strong interest heading into WrestleMania.

Brie Bella revealed that someone leaked Paige's return to WWE before WrestleMania, calling the person responsible a "stooge." Despite the leak being speculative, Brie expressed frustration about the breach of secrecy and vowed to identify who was responsible.

Bron Breakker suffered a legitimate head injury during a Raw segment when a chair shot from Seth Rollins meant for his back struck the back of his head instead, opening a deep cut. Breakker was checked by medical personnel but continued the segment as planned.

Jade Cargill and boxing champion Alycia Baumgardner teased a potential tag team partnership on social media, with both expressing interest in collaborating should Baumgardner join WWE.

Glenn Jacobs, known as Kane, reflected on his WWE Hall of Fame career and current role as Mayor of Knox County, describing himself as a "product of the American Dream."

Randy Orton publicly called out former Legacy stablemate Ted DiBiase Jr. for allegedly owing him $25,000, claiming the loan was made during their time together in WWE.

Jade Cargill reflected on her 169-day title reign, calling it a "practice run" and expressing a desire to have had more title defenses and competitive matches.

The Bryan & Vinny Show reviewed both nights of WrestleMania on the latest F4W episode, discussing technical difficulties at the start of the show before diving into comprehensive analysis.

Overall, while WrestleMania 42 delivered compelling in-ring action and several major storyline developments, the severe security lapses and resulting safety concerns for talent dominated post-event discussions and raised serious questions about WWE's preparation for future major events.

🔥 NXT Revenge Week 2 (April 21, 2026)

NXT Revenge Week 2 took place on April 21, 2026, from the WWE Performance Center in Orlando, Florida, and delivered several significant developments that will shape the brand's future direction.

ZARIA'S BRUTAL BETRAYAL ENDS RUCA RIVALRY

The main event featured Zaria and Sol Ruca in a Last Woman Standing match that served as the culmination of their long-running personal feud. This match came after Ruca had made her main roster debut on Raw the previous night, where she challenged new WWE Women's World Champion Liv Morgan. During that encounter, Zaria attacked Ruca and cost her the match, setting up this final confrontation on NXT.

The match itself was a hard-hitting affair with multiple "Holy shit" and "This is awesome" chants from the crowd. Both women fought all around the ringside area, utilizing weapons like trash cans and kendo sticks. Ruca demonstrated her athletic prowess early, hitting a spear that sent Zaria through the production set and a springboard corkscrew to the floor. The action was back-and-forth throughout, with Zaria hitting an F-5 off the barricade that sent Ruca through the announce table.

The dramatic finish came when Ruca set up two tables and placed Zaria on them before climbing to a balcony-like structure high above the ring. She executed a Sol Snatcher from that elevated position, appearing to have the match won. However, when Ruca tried to prevent Zaria from falling after a series of strikes, the emotional history between the two former partners momentarily surfaced. They embraced and appeared to reconcile, with Zaria claiming she loved Ruca. This proved to be another manipulation, as Zaria suddenly shoved Ruca off the platform, sending her crashing through one of the tables below while hitting her head on the other. Ruca could not beat the ten-count, giving Zaria the victory.

The match was not just violent but also served as an emotional resolution to their rivalry built on jealousy, broken trust, and the complicated history of their former friendship. Ruca believed she had always been loyal to Zaria, while Zaria felt used and hurt by their relationship. With this match, Zaria walked away as the victor, cementing her as a dominant force on the NXT brand moving forward.

POST-MATCH CONCERNS AND RUCA'S MAIN ROSTER FUTURE

The finish proved to be particularly dangerous, as Ruca appeared to suffer a cut to the back of her head during the fall. After the show went off the air, referees and medical staff rushed to the ring to check on her as she lay in the table debris. The situation escalated when Zaria made her way back to ringside instead of heading straight backstage, walking past the injured Ruca in a show of disrespect before leaving.

Despite the frightening fall, Ruca was eventually able to get back to her feet with assistance and made her way to the back under her own power while being escorted by officials. Before disappearing behind the curtain, she acknowledged the crowd by throwing up her signature hand sign, letting fans know she was still conscious after the scary moment.

Reports suggest that both Ruca and Joe Hendry are making their final appearances on NXT, with both losing their respective matches on the show. Ruca's loss to Zaria marked her final NXT appearance before transitioning to the main roster full-time as part of the Raw brand following her debut the previous night.

JOE HENDRY'S DECISIVE EXIT FROM NXT

Joe Hendry faced Keanu Carver in what many fans believe was his farewell match on the brand. While Hendry showed flashes of control early in the match, delivering chops, a fallaway slam, and a discus lariat, Carver's power proved too much to overcome. The match turned decisively when Carver mounted Hendry and unleashed repeated punches, forcing the referee to stop the match.

The beatdown-style finish sparked heavy speculation about Hendry's future, especially considering reports suggesting he would receive a main roster call-up to Raw. Michael Cole announced on the April 20 episode of Raw that Hendry would be present for a musical segment the following week, leading fans to connect the dots. The match ending served as the kind of storyline exit that typically signals a wrestler moving up from NXT development to the main roster.

LEXIS KING CAPTURES MEN'S SPEED CHAMPIONSHIP

In the Men's Speed Championship tournament final, Lexis King defeated EK Prosper to claim the vacant title. The Speed Championship had been vacated when the previous champion Elio LeFleur suffered a torn labrum during a house show match and required time off.

The finish saw Prosper attempting to handle all members of the Birthright faction at ringside, but once he returned to the ring, King connected with his Coronation finisher for the pinfall victory. Birthright's presence throughout the match proved crucial, particularly when Arianna Grace positioned herself between Prosper and a potential suicide dive. After the match, King posted a picture of himself with the title next to a photo of his father Brian Pillman, who won the WCW Light Heavyweight title years ago, doing the same pose.

This marks King's second title reign in WWE, as he had previously held the NXT Heritage Cup Championship from December 2024 until April 2025. The victory demonstrates the continued influence and success of the Birthright faction, which consists of second-generation talent including Channing "Stacks" Lorenzo, Arianna Grace, Uriah Connors, and Charlie Dempsey.

TATUM PAXLEY'S INNOVATIVE CASKET MATCH VICTORY

The women's mid-card featured Tatum Paxley defending her NXT Women's North American Championship against Blake Monroe in a Casket Match. This was a historic encounter, marking the first time since the 1998 Royal Rumble that a championship came with a Casket Match stipulation in WWE.

The match was a grueling affair with both competitors utilizing creative and occasionally unexpected weapons. Paxley retrieved her signature dollhouse from under the ring, and while Monroe initially used it against her by dropkicking Paxley into it, the match featured several other unique elements. A fire extinguisher came into play, diamonds from a pillowcase provided an unusual hazard, and both women competed with the casket itself as a weapon in creative ways, including a Spanish Fly executed from the casket by Paxley.

The finish came when Monroe was set up with a Cemetery Drive onto a pile of diamonds that she had intended to use against Paxley. With Monroe vulnerable on the apron, Paxley delivered a spinning kick that sent her into the casket. Paxley then firmly closed the lid to retain her championship.

There was a notable moment during the match when both women fell into the casket and the lid momentarily closed, but referees determined the match would continue. This match was also rumored to be Monroe's final appearance on NXT before her own main roster call-up to WWE's primary brands.

MYLES BORNE RETAINS NORTH AMERICAN CHAMPIONSHIP DESPITE NUMBERS GAME

In the opening match, NXT North American Champion Myles Borne successfully defended his title against Dion Lennox despite facing significant odds with the DarkState faction surrounding the ring. The match featured back-and-forth action with both competitors getting opportunities to control the tempo.

Borne overcame multiple interference attempts from Saquon Shugars and other DarkState members. He hit a flying crossbody over the top rope to take out both Lennox and Osiris Griffin on the outside. Lennox controlled portions of the match by exposing the iron bars underneath the ring and driving Borne back-first into them. The champion nearly fell to a sit-down powerbomb but mounted a rally with a DDT and multiple clotheslines.

The turning point came when Borne sent Lennox crashing into Shugars on the apron and hit the Borne Again finisher to retain his title. In a backstage segment later, Shugars revealed to Lennox that they had received a rematch opportunity from NXT General Manager Robert Stone, but the rematch would feature Shugars challenging for the title rather than Lennox, as Lennox had already received his shot. This decision temporarily upset Lennox but proved to be the right call to keep the championship within the DarkState faction's control.

LIZZY RAIN DEBUT VIGNETTE AND FUTURE NXT PROGRAMMING

A vignette aired promoting the arrival of Lizzy Rain, known previously as Rayne Leverkusen. The presentation emphasized her love of heavy metal music, with the former independent wrestler from Europe introducing herself as a rocker who will prove that "metal is forever." Rain has previously worked for promotions including Progress, Pro Wrestling EVE, and various Japanese promotions.

Rain is scheduled to make her NXT TV debut on the April 28 episode of NXT on CW, with her opponent to be announced. She had been present at NXT Vengeance Day in a limited capacity and worked a dark match before the April 14 NXT television taping.

UPCOMING NXT PROGRAMMING

The April 28 episode of NXT features several significant matches and appearances. Shiloh Hill will face Ricky Saints, though Hill's status became questionable after an attack by Ricky Saints left Hill pinned under Robert Stone's office couch. Myles Borne will defend the NXT North American Championship against Saquon Shugars, and the Birthright faction will face EK Prosper and Dorian Van Dux in tag team action. Prosper had claimed after his Speed Championship match loss that Birthright's involvement cost him the title, prompting the tag team match for the following week.

BROADER IMPLICATIONS FOR NXT

The show demonstrated NXT's continued evolution as a brand, with established stars like Paxley and Borne proving their credentials at championship level while new talents like King were elevated through tournament competition. The apparent departures of Ruca and Hendry to the main roster suggest that WWE continues to view NXT as a development pipeline for talent ready for Raw and SmackDown, though both had proven themselves effective performers during their time on the brand.

The mixed critical reception of some booking decisions, particularly regarding Izzi Dame's positioning as a challenger for Lola Vice's NXT Women's Championship, suggests that not every decision resonated equally with observers. However, standout performances like the Paxley-Monroe Casket Match received significant praise for innovation and storytelling, indicating that NXT continues to produce compelling wrestling content alongside character-driven narratives.

CM Punk Retrospective and Commentary

CM Punk Reflects On His Historic 2011 Clash With John Cena And Takes Justified Action Against Invasive Fan

Although fans don't look back fondly on WWE's programming during the early 2010s, one of the bright spots was CM Punk and John Cena competing to be the face of the company. Their clash at Money In The Bank 2011 in Chicago has been viewed as one of the greatest professional wrestling matches of all-time. According to "The Best In The World," the fans and the timing of the contest is what made it so memorable.

In an appearance on "All The Smoke," Punk explained the perfect conditions that led to their legendary encounter. "I ride the wave. I am carried to the position I'm in because of those fans, because of those people in Chicago. Just an unbelievable once-in-a-lifetime atmosphere where we capture that lightning, we're riding on that lightning bolt and all the ingredients are there ... Nobody really knew what was going to happen. They knew I was out the door. They knew I was leaving. So obviously he's going to lose and when you have that kind of perfect storm, you can create magic. And John is a guy who I'm very grateful for in my career ... he didn't have to be that nice or giving to me, a lot of top guys back then weren't."

Punk continued by saying that Cena is his wrestling soulmate, and that he was more generous than other top stars 15 years ago. He also compares his relationship with Cena to American football, stating that he was the lead running back while the "Never Seen 17" was the quarterback of WWE, and they always scored touchdowns.

In more recent news, CM Punk found himself in a different kind of spotlight at WrestleMania 42 in Las Vegas. A video surfaced showing an incident in the talent hotel where Punk smacked a fan's phone out of his hand. The fan had been recording a moment between Bayley and AJ Lee, with the phone getting closer to the two female wrestlers despite Bayley's requests for the fan to back up. Hotel security and Punk's longtime friend Ace Steele stepped in to defuse the situation before Punk left the lobby.

The incident highlights an ongoing problem with overzealous wrestling fans who mob talent at hotels and airports. Professional wrestling has been dealing with increasingly invasive fan behavior, particularly with the prevalence of smartphones. Earlier at WrestleMania 42, Randy Orton was surrounded by fans in the lobby, handling the situation as gracefully as possible while being swarmed for interactions.

While observers have noted that CM Punk is often self-absorbed and disingenuous, his action in this situation was completely justified. The wrestling business operates differently than it did in previous eras. TKO has monetized the WWE brand significantly, and fans who want to meet wrestlers are expected to purchase official meet-and-greet experiences through On Location, the organization that handles premium experiences for WWE. These experiences charge thousands of dollars for the privilege.

Wrestlers rarely perform free promotional appearances in hotel lobbies anymore since there are numerous other ways for the company to push the product. The expectation is that fans who truly want access to the stars should purchase tickets to official WrestleMania week events or meet-and-greet experiences, not stalk wrestlers in hotel lobbies or airports. The fan in question was not respecting basic boundaries, continuing to film despite being asked to back up, which made Punk's intervention the only reasonable way to resolve an escalating situation.

WWE AAA Acquisition and Signings

Since acquiring Lucha Libre AAA, WWE has added a number of stars to the company whether through free agency or from its own roster. Most recently, AAA welcomed in former CMLL star La Catalina, who has immediately positioned herself as a contender for Flammer's Reina de Reinas Championship. Looking at the current AAA roster, there is still room to grow and add more talent that could garner a great deal of the spotlight in the promotion in the near future.

Santi Rivera represents an intriguing prospect from WWE Evolve's new wave of talent. On WWE Evolve, the brand has welcomed in a number of new stars as of late with the ID talent taking center stage. Among the new stars introduced to the brand, Santi Rivera has been someone who has caught some eyes. With his impressive physique and notable look, Rivera has felt like someone who can be a star in WWE down the line. Through the ID program, Rivera might be able to embrace his hispanic culture by being signed by AAA where he can shine even more than he has in his first few weeks on Evolve.

Julissa Mexa offers a return opportunity for a former WWE talent with established experience. Back in 2021, Julissa Mexa signed with WWE and became Yulisa Leon on the NXT brand. Over two years with the promotion, Mexa made a name for herself, even appearing on Friday Night SmackDown at one point. After being released in 2023, Mexa has made her way to the independent scene, most notably in Mexico for The Crash Lucha Libre promotion. Winning The Crash Women's Championship, Mexa could once again get back under the WWE banner, but this time for Lucha Libre AAA where she would help to rebuild the women's scene.

Gianna Capri is among the newest WWE ID signees making immediate waves in the developmental system. One of WWE's newest signees under the ID program is the former Valentina Rossi. Now known as Gianna Capri, the WWE ID star recently made her debut on the Evolve brand. Capri has shown a great deal of confidence and charisma throughout her time on the independent scene. While she is making a name for herself in Evolve, Capri could become the latest ID talent to make the move to Lucha Libre AAA and further flesh out the women's scene right now.

Chazz 'Starboy' Hall has demonstrated remarkable potential across multiple WWE developmental platforms. Another WWE ID star who has been making an immediate impact wherever he appears is Chazz 'Starboy' Hall. In his first appearance on WWE Evolve, the former Starboy Charlie became the number one contender for the Evolve Men's Championship. Despite coming up short against Aaron Rourke in his eventual title shot, Hall then made his way to WWE NXT and impressed everyone with a strong showing against Lexis King in a Speed Championship Tournament bout. With all of his experience across the independent scene in promotions like GCW, DEFY and West Coast Pro, 'Starboy' is more than deserving of following in the footsteps of other WWE ID talent like Jack Cartwheel in heading to Lucha Libre AAA soon.

Soberano Jr. represents a major acquisition target from WWE's sister promotion CMLL. When looking at Lucha Libre AAA's biggest competition in CMLL, Soberano Jr. has to be among the most popular stars in Mexico today. For over 15 years, Soberano has made his presence felt as one of the biggest stars in CMLL. The former CMLL World Middleweight Champion has been speculated to potentially leave the promotion when his contract expires. WWE and Lucha Libre AAA would be hard-pressed to find a more decorated and popular star to add to their roster right now than Soberano, making him someone that should be highly sought after when he becomes available.

Persephone stands out as the top priority for potential signings given her elite status in the women's division. Another CMLL name that has been speculated as having interest from WWE is Persephone. Considered one of the fastest rising stars in wrestling today, Persephone has reached the mountaintop of the women's scene in Mexico as the reigning CMLL Women's World Champion. With victories over the likes of Mercedes Mone, Thekla and Tessa Blanchard, Persephone has more than made it clear that she deserves to be mentioned among the best women's wrestlers in the world today. At just 24 years old, Persephone should be a high priority for WWE AAA if she becomes available and look to rekindle her career-long rivalry with La Catalina in a new promotion.
