🔥 AEW New Pillars and Forbidden Door

TONY KHAN NAMES NEW PILLARS OF AEW

When AEW first assembled its roster, four young stars were designated as the unofficial "Four Pillars of AEW," a reference to the legendary "Four Pillars of Heaven" from All Japan Pro Wrestling's peak in the 1990s. The original four chosen were MJF, Darby Allin, Sammy Guevara, and "Jungle Boy" Jack Perry, all of whom have achieved varying degrees of success in AEW, particularly MJF and Allin as they have held, and in MJF's case still holds, the AEW World Championship.

However, as time has progressed, fans have started to create new groups of pillars based on who they believe will carry the company into the future. AEW President Tony Khan was asked about the pillars during a recent interview on "The Morning Q Crew" and listed four names representing the new "Four Pillars of AEW."

Khan acknowledged the subjective nature of selecting pillars, noting that asking 100 fans could yield 99 different answers given the abundance of great young wrestlers on the roster. If picking from a young group that has come in recent years, Khan would select Thekla, Kevin Knight, Kyle Fletcher, and Mercedes Mone. Khan emphasized that there are so many great young wrestlers who have joined AEW over the past few years, as well as experienced free agents who have integrated very well into the company. He stressed that Thekla, Mone, Knight, and Fletcher are extremely important to the future of AEW and are proving that now by being focal points of the weekly programming.

KHAN ADDRESSES TNA WRESTLING ACQUISITION INTEREST

During the Forbidden Door media call, Tony Khan was asked by Brandon Thurston of Wrestlenomics whether he would be interested in acquiring TNA Wrestling if Anthem Sports & Entertainment were willing to sell the company. Khan asked what price it would be at, and when the hypothetical figure of $40 million was mentioned, Khan responded with a firm "No" followed by laughter.

Khan elaborated on his position, noting that TNA has value, but any acquisition would have to be at the right price. He emphasized that acquiring TNA is not a top priority for him at this time and that he has not had any serious conversations about such a deal. Khan closed his remarks by noting that it could be interesting if the purchase price was right, but pointed out that the same applies to many things in life, comparing it to his interest in acquiring luxury automobiles for his personal garage. He noted that financial decisions always come down to pricing and value proposition.

Since founding AEW in 2019, Khan has made one notable acquisition of another promotion: Ring of Honor, which he continues to operate today with weekly TV on the WatchROH streaming service and quarterly specials. With WWE reportedly having a set-price purchase option for TNA through their working agreement with the company, Khan's lack of enthusiasm represents a significant statement about his current strategic priorities.

He emphasized that he respects Kingston and Woods' choice to step away from the company.

According to Big E, both men had assumed their entire wrestling careers would be spent with WWE and believed they would retire there. The Balance Between Storytelling and Moves in Wrestling

John Cena is absolutely correct that storytelling is pivotal to a great wrestling match. The emotional investment of fans is paramount to a match's success, and without compelling narrative, even the most technically impressive performances can fall flat.

However, there is a potential counterpoint to consider. Over the past several years, there have arguably been instances where WWE has leaned too heavily on the story aspect while delivering matches with too little physical action in some of its high-profile bouts. This raises questions about finding the proper balance between the two elements.

While it is entirely fair to say that storytelling is just as important, if not more important, than the moves performed in a wrestling match, that philosophy should not come at the expense of neglecting the physical aspect entirely. The most compelling matches typically blend both elements seamlessly—strong narrative combined with engaging in-ring action that reinforces and enhances the story being told.

Title Changes Wrestling History

June 25th has been quite a day when it comes to title changes in pro wrestling. According to available wrestling data, there have been at least 310 changes on this day in history. It definitely helps that there have been some major shows on this date in the past, including WWF King of the Ring 2000, WWE Vengeance 2006 and AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door 2023.

The earliest documented June 25th title change dates back to 1968, when Bobo Brazil won the NWA International Heavyweight Title. Nearly a quarter-century later in 1992, The Barbarian and Dick Slater won the NWA/WCW United States Tag Team Titles on this date.

The year 2000 stands out as one of the most significant for title changes on June 25th. WWF King of the Ring 2000 featured an exceptionally busy night for championship transitions. Christian and Edge won the WWF Tag Team Titles, while Crash Holly captured the WWF Hardcore Title. In the main event, The Rock won the WWF Title. Additionally, on the same date, Koji Kanemoto and Minoru Tanaka won the IWGP Junior Heavyweight Tag Team Titles, demonstrating that title changes were occurring across multiple promotions simultaneously.

The following year in 2001 continued the trend of multiple title changes on June 25th. Mike Awesome won the WWF Hardcore Title, X-Pac won the WWF World Light Heavyweight Title, and Rhyno won the WWF Hardcore Title, showing a particularly active night for mid-card championship changes.

Between 2004 and 2014, the pace of title changes on this date continued at a steady rate. Atlantis and Blue Panther won the CMLL World Tag Team Titles in 2004. Johnny Nitro won the WWE Intercontinental Title in 2006. Tara won the TNA Women's Knockout Title in 2009. The TNA X-Division Title was vacated in 2014.

The year 2015 proved to be another notably active date for title changes. Davey Richards and Eddie Edwards won the TNA World Tag Team Titles, Brooke won the TNA Knockouts Title, and Ethan Carter III won the TNA World Heavyweight Title. This demonstrated continued activity within TNA programming on this particular date.

More recent years have also featured significant title changes on June 25th. Taiji Ishimori won the GHC Junior Heavyweight Title in 2017. The TNA World Title was vacated in 2020. The most recent entry is from 2023, when Will Ospreay won the IWGP United States Heavyweight Title, continuing the historical trend of this date being significant for professional wrestling championship history.
