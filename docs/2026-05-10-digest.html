🔥 Chris Jericho Stadium Stampede at AEW Double or Nothing

Chris Jericho's Stadium Stampede Team Revealed with Elite Reunion at AEW Double or Nothing

Chris Jericho found himself in an unexpected situation when Ricochet proposed a Stadium Stampede match for AEW Double or Nothing. With no friends left in the locker room, Jericho had to resort to finding wrestlers who shared a mutual hatred with his opponents. The Hurt Syndicate members Bobby Lashley and Shelton Benjamin stepped up to fill two spots on the team, but two more positions remained unfilled.

The catalyst for the match's expansion came during AEW Fairway to Hell on May 9, 2026, when Ricochet interfered in the AEW National Championship bout between Jack Perry and Mark Davis. Perry hit Jack Perry with a golf club, enabling Mark Davis to capture the championship. The interference cost Perry the title he had worked hard to hold.

Perry was furious backstage after the loss and quickly made a decision to align himself with Chris Jericho's team for Stadium Stampede. Rather than settling for a traditional pairing, Perry had a bold idea. Capitalizing on the "Double or Nothing" name, Perry decided to double up on team members and bring four additional wrestlers instead of two, creating an unprecedented gathering of forces.

Perry unveiled an elite plan by announcing that The Elite would step up to right the wrong committed against him. Kenny Omega, The Young Bucks (Matt Jackson and Nick Jackson), and Perry himself would join Jericho's team, creating strange bedfellows in the process. This union is particularly ironic given the history between these factions. In the original Stadium Stampede match in 2020, Jericho led the Inner Circle into battle against The Elite, Hangman Page, and Matt Hardy, with The Elite ultimately emerging victorious when Omega hit a One Winged Angel on Sammy Guevara from the upper deck.

On the opposing side, Ricochet was game for the expanded match, and Don Callis took the news with a smile. Callis considered it a fantasy scenario that The Callis Family could beat up Jericho, Omega, and The Bucks all at once. Ricochet and Callis exited to discuss which family members would fill out the rest of their squad for Stadium Stampede.

The current roster for Jericho's team includes Bobby Lashley, Shelton Benjamin, Jack Perry, Kenny Omega, and The Young Bucks. The opposing team features The Demand members Ricochet, Bishop Kaun, and Toa Liona, along with newly crowned AEW National Champion Mark Davis and three additional members from The Don Callis Family, with one spot still to be announced.

Several other major developments were announced alongside the Stadium Stampede revelations. A previously advertised AEW World Championship bout underwent changes due to Kazuchika Okada traveling to Japan for a personal matter. Okada had originally been scheduled to challenge Darby Allin on the May 13 episode of AEW Dynamite, but Konosuke Takeshita will now represent The Don Callis Family in that world title match instead.

Additionally, Thekla was announced as the defender of the AEW Women's World Championship in a four-way match at Double or Nothing. She will put her title on the line against Hikaru Shida, Kris Statlander, and Jamie Hayter.

The Stadium Stampede match is set to take place on May 24, 2026, at Louis Armstrong Stadium in Queens, New York. With all the names already involved and the final member of The Don Callis Family team still to be revealed, the match already promises to be a spectacular display of chaos and athleticism. The bad blood between Perry, Don Callis, Ricochet, and Omega's faction could turn this match into total bedlam the moment it begins.

🔥 Jack Perry Loses AEW National Championship

Jack Perry is no longer the AEW National Champion. During the May 9 edition of AEW Collision: Fairway to Hell, held at the SoFi Center in Palm Beach Gardens, Florida, Perry put his AEW National Championship on the line against Mark Davis of The Don Callis Family in what marked Perry's fourth defense since capturing the title at AEW Revolution earlier in the year.

The match itself featured a clash of styles, with "Jungle Man" Perry using his high-flying agility and shiftiness to execute a variety of counter offense against the powerful Davis. In the early-going, Perry used his aerial prowess to keep the distance, hitting a Hurricanrana off the apron to the floor and another off the top rope back into the ring. However, Davis weathered the storm with his strength and raw power, using powerful strikes to swing momentum back in his favor whenever Perry seemed to gain control.

Down the stretch, the action intensified. Davis blasted Perry with a discus lariat and followed up with a piledriver attempt. Perry showed impressive toughness by kicking out of the cover. Davis went for a super maneuver out of the corner, but Perry countered into an avalanche hurricanrana. Perry sized up a running knee strike that appeared to finish the match, setting up what looked like a victorious moment for the champion.

That's when everything changed. Don Callis ran to the ring from commentary as Perry was setting up his finishing move. While Callis distracted the referee, Ricochet arrived from the crowd and hit Perry from behind with a golf club, a cheap shot that played into the golf-themed nature of the Fairway to Hell special. With Perry vulnerable from the attack, Davis seized the moment and hit his Close Your Eyes and Count To Three finisher, pinning Perry to win the AEW National Championship for the first time in his career.

The interference from Ricochet came full circle narratively, as it was Perry who had previously ended Ricochet's reign with the National Championship. The dots connected as well for the relationship between Callis and Ricochet, which had been building over recent months, including when they powwowed on the golf course back in January. Ricochet's involvement in the title change set the stage for major roster implications heading forward.

After the match, Perry cut a furious backstage promo about how things went down. However, he revealed that he had a very productive conversation with Chris Jericho, who needed some help dealing with Ricochet. Perry declared that himself, Kenny Omega, and The Young Bucks would be joining Jericho and The Hurt Syndicate in a Stadium Stampede match at AEW Double or Nothing on May 24. In response, Ricochet and The Gates of Agony will be joined by members of The Don Callis Family to even the odds in what is shaping up to be one of the most chaotic matches in AEW history. The title change has successfully elevated the stakes for one of AEW's biggest upcoming events.

🔥 Darby Allin AEW World Championship Matches

Darby Allin has proven himself a fighting champion willing to defend his AEW World Championship against any challenger who steps forward. His latest test came against PAC at the Fairway to Hell edition of AEW Collision, which took place at SoFi Center's golf course. The match featured a unique setting that allowed for unconventional spots, including both competitors utilizing the golf bunker as part of the action.

Darby started the match on the indoor golf course with a No Count Out stipulation in place. He landed a Code Red and a Jon Woo Kick that sent PAC flying into a golf bunker, but PAC responded with a Belly-To-Belly Suplex and a Gorilla Press that saw Darby land in the sand. PAC then applied the Brutalizer submission hold while both men were in the bunker as the match went to commercial.

When action resumed, PAC had tied Darby to the turnbuckle using a tag rope. After Darby countered a PAC Lariat and hit a Scorpion Death Drop for a two count, the challenger fired back. Darby went up top and landed an Avalanche Falcon Arrow that earned PAC a near fall. PAC then retrieved a table from underneath the ring but missed a Shooting Star Press to the outside. Inside the ring, Darby locked in the Scorpion Death Lock, but PAC managed to reach the ropes to break the hold.

The match escalated when the Death Riders came out to distract Darby, allowing PAC to take control. The faction built a stack of four tables near the entrance way while PAC delivered a Tombstone Piledriver to Darby on the ring steps. PAC then carried Darby up to the balcony overlooking the golf course. Despite Darby's attempts to fight back, PAC threw him off the balcony, sending him crashing through two of the four tables below in a devastating 25-foot fall.

It appeared that PAC had finally secured the AEW World Championship when he rushed down to capitalize on the damage. However, in a moment that exemplified Allin's resilience, he miraculously kicked out of the pin attempt. The Death Riders then distracted the referee, allowing them to bring the AEW World Championship belt into the ring. As PAC went on the attack, Darby sprang up and hit PAC with a low blow followed by a Coffin Drop to retain his championship.

Following his victory, the focus immediately turned to Darby's next World title defense. Originally scheduled to face Kazuchika Okada on the May 13 episode of AEW Dynamite, the match had to be changed. Don Callis announced that Okada was in Japan attending to personal family business, making him unavailable for the scheduled title shot.

To replace Okada, Konosuke Takeshita was announced as the new challenger for Darby's AEW World Championship on AEW Dynamite on May 13. Takeshita, like Okada, is a member of the Don Callis Family, though the two have had a contentious relationship throughout their time in AEW despite being in the same faction. Reports confirmed that Okada's trip to Japan for family business was legitimate, prompting the change in opponent.

The timing of this change added an additional layer of intrigue to the storyline, as Okada and Takeshita are already scheduled to face each other in a singles match at AEW Double or Nothing 2026. The tension that has existed between the two Don Callis Family members throughout their time on the roster now takes on added significance with Takeshita getting an opportunity to capture the AEW World Championship before their scheduled encounter at Double or Nothing.

Swerve Strickland Wants to Work with The New Day in AEW

Swerve Strickland is hopeful about the possibility of working with Kofi Kingston and Xavier Woods in AEW, though he made clear his primary concern is that the former New Day members do what's best for themselves. Speaking during an appearance on TMZ's Inside the Ring podcast, Strickland reacted to Kingston and Woods becoming free agents following their WWE departures in late April after opting against restructuring their contracts under a proposed pay reduction tied to TKO changes within the company.

When asked directly if he'd like to see the former New Day members sign with AEW, Strickland didn't hesitate to express his enthusiasm. "Oh yeah, of course. Absolutely," Strickland responded. However, he was quick to add important context to his answer, emphasizing that their autonomy matters more than his professional aspirations.

"I also just want them to do what's best for them. If they want to take like a five-year hiatus, they've earned it. They've more than earned it. They know their value as well, and that's the most important thing. I hope that's the lesson that everybody learns in this, not just like as a fan but as a professional yourself in this industry, they know their value, and they understand who they are, and they understand what they bring, and what they brought and their legacy they leave behind."

Beyond discussing the possibility of a future partnership, Strickland revealed that he reached out to Kingston privately after the news of his WWE departure broke. The former AEW World Champion went on to offer extensive praise for Kingston as a professional and a role model within the wrestling industry, particularly highlighting Kingston's significance to Black wrestlers in professional wrestling.

"He's like the epitome of professionalism when it comes to that," Strickland said. "Especially in our culture, you look at Kofi as one of the like top five greatest ever to do it. He's truly a model. A role model, but he's the model type of role model. He's the perfect type of role model."

Strickland reflected on how much Kingston has inspired his own career trajectory, particularly his historic achievement of becoming the first Black wrestler to hold the AEW World Championship in 2024. He highlighted the weight of Kingston's own groundbreaking accomplishment at WrestleMania, where Kingston became the only Black wrestler to win the World Championship at that flagship event.

"I meant every word I said when I won the championship and was like, 'Kofi's a big inspiration for that.' It's like, man, how do I do something so cool like that and make it my own?," Strickland said. "How do I do that in my own legacy, in my own career, something that Kofi did at WrestleMania. He's the only Black wrestler to win the World Championship at WrestleMania. You cannot take that away from him. He's the only one."

Harley Cameron Wardrobe Segment in AEW

Harley Cameron recently addressed the backstage segment from the May 26 episode of AEW Dynamite in which Mina Shirakawa ripped her shirt off during an on-screen altercation that generated significant online reaction. The moment was played for laughs, as Shirakawa apparently believed Cameron already had her wrestling gear on underneath, only for Cameron to reveal she was wearing just a bra.

Speaking with Good Karma Wrestling and ESPN South Florida, Cameron explained the circumstances surrounding the segment. "Look, Mina got all riled up and she just thought I was wearing my gear underneath, which I wasn't. And you know what? For everyone who had a heart attack over it, let me just preface the fact that my wrestling gear is actually like just a bra. I wrestle in a bra, if anyone takes care to notice."

Cameron also pointed out that the attire involved was not substantially different from what viewers regularly see during her matches. "So for everyone that was like, 'Oh my gosh,' I'm like, Mina is next to me in gear—our gear is bras. So, you know, she just thought I was in her wrestling gear. But I was in the wrong material."

She then joked about her immediate reaction after the shirt was removed during the segment. "When she ripped the shirt off, I was like, I gotta take the rest of it off now, you know? For anyone that was a little bit taken back, I mean guys, just watch my matches. I wear a lot less."

Some fans criticized the segment online and viewed it as unnecessarily objectifying, but Cameron made it clear she found some of the outrage overblown. The segment became a major talking point online following the episode, with fans debating whether the spot crossed a line or simply reflected the over-the-top presentation style often associated with professional wrestling storylines.

The pairing with Shirakawa comes after Cameron previously teamed with Willow Nightingale, where the duo enjoyed a three-month reign as the inaugural AEW Women's Tag Team Champions. Shirakawa had previously been aligned with Toni Storm before the new partnership was formed. The newly assembled team made their in-ring debut together on Wednesday's Dynamite, ultimately falling short against Kris Statlander and Hikaru Shida.

During the same interview, Cameron reflected on her rapid growth in AEW and credited Tony Khan for giving her an opportunity despite her lack of experience when she debuted in 2022. "Tony took a risk hiring someone so new as me. And I'm so thankful for him, because I owe him my career. And like I said, I was just lucky that I was prepared and that I was given an opportunity. And then he's been so amazing in letting me grow my wrestling skills in the company."

2025 has largely been viewed as a breakout year for Cameron, who earned "Most Improved" honors in the Wrestling Observer Newsletter Awards. The accomplishment reflects her continued development as a performer since her initial debut.

Bobby Lashley World Title Ambitions in AEW

Bobby Lashley remains committed to eventually pursuing the AEW World Championship, despite his current focus on tag team excellence. Throughout his AEW tenure, Lashley has maintained his standard of in-ring dominance, most notably holding the AEW World Tag Team Championship alongside Hurt Syndicate stablemate Shelton Benjamin in 2025. However, singles competition has largely taken a backseat since his rivalry with Swerve Strickland concluded in late 2024.

When discussing his future goals in AEW during an appearance on the Good Karma Wrestling podcast, Lashley made clear that pursuing the AEW World Championship remains on his radar. He expressed confidence that the fanbase would embrace such a pursuit and emphasized that it ultimately comes down to timing and opportunity aligning properly.

"I've won a World Title in TNA, I've won a World Title in WWE," Lashley stated (h/t Wrestling Inc.). "Now I'm in AEW...I think the fans see it. The fans see it. Whenever you put a title on someone else, the fans are looking like 'Oh man, I really like that title.' And then somebody else says 'Bobby Lashley,' and they're like 'Woah, wait a minute. You've got Bobby Lashley on the roster, why isn't he winning the World Title?' Timing, timing."

For now, Lashley is finding fulfillment in his current role within the Hurt Syndicate framework. He emphasized that being part of a tag team with Benjamin and working in a trios capacity with both Benjamin and MVP represents what he wants from his career at this particular juncture.

"Right now, I'm having the greatest time of my life, and that's being a tag team with Shelton Benjamin. It's about being trios with Shelton and MVP. That's what I want to be in my career right now. And then when the time's right, when the landscape opens up, when it gives me the opportunity to go in there, I'd like to go after that World Title. Because ultimately, in my career before I retire, to say I won World Title's in every organization that I've been in would be a huge accomplishment," Lashley explained (h/t Wrestling Inc.).

Lashley's perspective reflects both contentment with his present situation and a clear long-term objective. Capturing the AEW World Championship would represent a significant personal milestone, allowing him to claim world title victories across every major wrestling organization in which he has competed at the highest level.

Fatu left the ring but returned shortly after to apply the Tongan Death Grip one final time before eventually departing.
