🔥 MJF Wednesday Night Wars documentary pitch

AEW's Maxwell Jacob Friedman has been making numerous media appearances in recent weeks to promote upcoming projects, including the AEW Dynasty 2026 pay-per-view scheduled for April 12 in Vancouver, British Columbia, where he will defend his AEW World Championship against Kenny Omega. During his appearance on the Late Night Grin podcast, MJF discussed pitching a "Wednesday Night Wars" documentary to AEW President Tony Khan while reflecting on the ratings competition between WWE NXT and AEW Dynamite.

The Wednesday Night Wars began in 2019 when WWE strategically placed its developmental brand NXT on the USA Network on Wednesday nights to compete directly with AEW Dynamite's TNT debut. In their first head-to-head matchup, Dynamite decisively won with 1.4 million viewers compared to NXT's 891,000. AEW continued its dominance throughout the competition, winning most weeks until 2021 when WWE relocated NXT to Tuesday nights, where it has remained since.

MJF believes AEW should capitalize on this historic victory by producing a documentary. He emphasized the unique opportunity that exists because AEW now employs many of the talent who competed for NXT during the wars. "We literally won and we have guys that work in our company now that were on the other side. It would be fucking sick. You can sit down Adam Cole and Roderick Strong, Kyle O'Reilly and Swerve. You know, all these guys that were on the other side of the war," MJF explained. He added humorously, "You can't sit down Bobby Fish unfortunately. I mean folks, where's the lie?" borrowing Fish's infamous catchphrase.

In the same media appearance, MJF also discussed his catchphrase "I'm better than you and you know it." He revealed that he created the phrase at age 18 while standing in front of a mirror, and it came to him instantly, giving him chills. However, he later discovered that the movie Dodgeball contained a similar line: "Here at Globo Gym, we're better than you, and we know it." MJF acknowledged this coincidence and compared it to situations standup comedians experience, where they unknowingly repeat jokes from other sources they consumed years earlier.

During another recent appearance on Julian Edelman's Games With Names podcast, MJF reflected on his Iron Man match with Bryan Danielson at AEW Revolution 2023. He praised Danielson as one of the greatest professional wrestlers of all time and made an NFL comparison. MJF described Danielson as being at a "Tom Brady level" in terms of GOAT status, highlighting his technical prowess, athleticism, and toughness. He noted that Danielson is currently semi-retired but predicted the wrestling legend would return because of his addiction to competition, much like Brady himself.

🔥 Mascara Dorada AEW and CMLL dual contract

Mascara Dorada has officially joined AEW while maintaining his contract with CMLL, making him one of a select few wrestlers to hold dual contracts with two major promotions simultaneously. The announcement came as a surprise to the lucha libre star, who wasted no time accepting the opportunity when it was presented to him.

Speaking with interviewer Denise Salcedo, Dorada explained that at the time of the offer, Hechicero was the only wrestler with a dual contract arrangement. When the opportunity was presented, he didn't hesitate for a second. He described the deal as fulfilling a lifelong dream, saying that since childhood he always wanted to work for two companies. He emphasized that very few wrestlers in the world have two, three, or four contracts, and he was eager to become part of that exclusive group of performers.

For Dorada, the appeal of holding dual contracts extends beyond just the prestige. He expressed genuine pleasure at the prospect of wrestling all over the world, particularly highlighting his appreciation for audiences in the United States, whom he described as enthusiastic. He also noted the unique atmosphere of Mexican crowds, who create a different but equally enjoyable experience. The duality of performing for both AEW and CMLL audiences while facing top-tier wrestlers in both companies is something he finds truly rewarding.

AEW and CMLL have been strengthening their partnership in recent months, with multiple wrestlers signing similar dual deals. Beyond Mascara Dorada, other CMLL stars who have signed dual contracts include Persephone, Mistico, Hechicero, and Thunder Rosa, the former AEW Women's World Champion. This growing alliance between the two promotions could lead to additional crossover deals in the future, with speculation that current CMLL World Heavyweight Champion Claudio Castagnoli might also sign with AEW.

Mascara Dorada's wrestling style has been heavily influenced by several legendary performers. Mistico stands out as a primary reference point for him, as the luchador acknowledged that all Mexican wrestlers who claim otherwise about Mistico's influence are not being truthful. Mistico, along with Volador Jr. and Andrade El Idolo, revolutionized wrestling according to Dorada, and their style has always appealed to him. Beyond the Mexican wrestling scene, he drew inspiration from the aerial and acrobatic approach of international wrestlers like Will Ospreay and PAC, whose agility and technical completeness he deeply admires.

As Dorada moves forward in both promotions, he has already identified potential future opponents he would like to face. He expressed particular interest in wrestling Will Ospreay, describing him as a very well-rounded wrestler who delivers five-star matches regardless of his opponent. Ospreay's combination of strength and agility impressed Dorada greatly. Similarly, he named PAC as another wrestler he would love to face, praising PAC's strength, daring approach, and beautiful diving abilities.

Dorada's philosophy about facing top competition is straightforward: to be among the best in the world, you must face the best in the world. By competing against wrestlers like Ospreay and PAC, he aims to absorb their experience and continue developing his craft at the highest level. Currently, Dorada holds the Historic Welterweight title in CMLL and serves as one third of the CMLL Trios Champions alongside Mistico and Neon, solidifying his status as a significant performer in both organizations.

The dual contract represents validation from both AEW and CMLL that Mascara Dorada is a wrestler worth investing in long-term. With his impressive in-ring abilities and clear ambition to compete against the world's best performers, Dorada is positioned to make an impact on both the American and Mexican wrestling landscapes during what could be a defining period in his career.

The Acclaimed indie show and AEW reunion status

The Acclaimed's tumultuous relationship took an unexpected turn this past weekend at WrestlePro X, the independent promotion's tenth anniversary celebration. Anthony Bowens and Max Caster, once the respected AEW World Tag Team Champions, have spent the last year in conflict, either feuding outright or teaming together while maintaining their antagonistic dynamic. The pair's careers have declined significantly from their former glory, with Bowens still featured regularly on AEW television and Caster largely absent from the company since December 2025.

At the anniversary event, Bowens defeated Caster in singles competition via Crucifix Roll Up. Rather than leave things contentious, Bowens extended an olive branch by calling his former partner back into the ring. The two proceeded to reconcile, recreating their iconic scissor taunt and embracing in front of the crowd. The moment appeared to signal a potential reunion, but Bowens's words told a different story.

During his post-match remarks, Bowens acknowledged their year-long conflict while emphasizing the special nature of the WrestlePro anniversary event. He suggested that the truce was isolated to this particular occasion, stating he didn't believe they would ever compete together again. Most tellingly, Bowels made clear that nothing else needed to be said beyond the gesture itself, pointedly not extending the reconciliation to AEW television or other future engagements. The implication was clear: this was a one-time moment of respect between former partners, not the beginning of a full-fledged reunion.

Bowens has remained an active part of AEW's programming, recently competing in an AEW Continental Championship Eliminator match against Jon Moxley on AEW Collision. Caster's situation is far more uncertain, with the veteran having largely disappeared from the promotion's roster at the start of 2026. His wrestling appearances have been limited to scattered independent bookings with Beyond Wrestling, House of Glory, and WrestlePro, suggesting he may no longer be in an active AEW contract.

In a separate story of unlikely reunions, AEW's Jack Perry experienced a more elaborate reconciliation with former stablemate Marko Stunt. Perry, the current AEW Nation Champion, recently reformed Jurassic Express with Luchasaurus, but the original trio also included Stunt, who was released from AEW in 2022. Save for a single guest appearance in 2024, the two had not crossed paths for years.

That changed dramatically at GCW's Maniac 2026 event held in Los Angeles, Perry's hometown. Stunt was attacked by former AEW star KJ Orso, known to AEW audiences as Fuego Del Sol, along with Orso's partner Sam Stackhouse. The assault was interrupted when Perry unexpectedly arrived at the Ukrainian Cultural Center and came to Stunt's defense, reuniting the two before a shocked crowd.

The confrontation will continue beyond the immediate moment. Perry and Stunt have agreed to team together for the first time in years to face Orso and Stackhouse at Joey Janela's Spring Break X, scheduled for April 17 at the Horseshoe Las Vegas Hotel in Las Vegas, Nevada. The event will also feature Negative One, the son of deceased AEW star Brodie Lee, who will be facing Janela himself in a separate match.

Stunt's return carries additional intrigue given his recent history. After his AEW release, Stunt initially pursued an independent career but eventually stepped away from wrestling due to accumulating injuries. In 2024, he announced his retirement and transitioned to civilian life, taking a job selling cars at Homer Skelton Ford in Mississippi. His decision to return for the Spring Break X match with Perry represents an end to that retirement, confirmed by a "back in business" social media post from Stunt.

Perry's own trajectory took a dramatic turn at the end of 2025 when he returned from injury and reinvented himself, shedding the "Scapegoat" persona that had defined his recent run. This transformation proved successful, as Perry won the AEW National Title from Ricochet at the Revolution PPV last month, establishing himself as a championship-caliber competitor in the promotion.

WWE Hall of Famer on Chris Jericho AEW retirement tour

Chris Jericho made his highly anticipated return to AEW Dynamite on April 1st, 2026, after being away from television for an entire year. According to reports from PWInsider.com, the backstage consensus at Dynamite was that Jericho had reached terms on a new contract with AEW, solidifying his continued presence with the promotion.

During an appearance on the Something to Wrestle podcast, WWE Hall of Famer JBL addressed speculation surrounding Jericho's career trajectory and a potential retirement tour. JBL expressed his belief that there is simply no reason for Jericho to officially retire or embark on a farewell tour at this stage of his career.

In his comments, JBL highlighted that Jericho remains in excellent physical condition and continues to perform at a high level. "Jericho is still apparently very healthy. He's still on top of his game. He still looks great," JBL stated. This assessment suggests that from JBL's perspective, Jericho has many productive years ahead of him in professional wrestling.

JBL advocated for a different approach to how wrestlers handle their careers in the later stages. Rather than committing to a definitive retirement tour, JBL suggested that performers can keep things open-ended and flexible. "Have a tour, but if you want to come back again, come back again. There's no reason not to. I think sometimes you can just leave it open-ended. You come back for a while, people see you, then you disappear—and if you want to come back again, you come back again," JBL explained.

The Hall of Famer also reflected on what could have been offered to Jericho had he returned to WWE instead of re-signing with AEW. JBL suggested that the company had several significant opponents available for potential dream matches. "I think there were a lot of opportunities in WWE other than Gunther. I mean, you had CM Punk, you had Roman Reigns, you had Brock Lesnar, you had Seth Rollins—you had a bunch of guys that Chris has history with that could have been really good," JBL noted.

According to JBL's analysis, if a retirement tour was indeed part of WWE's pitch to Jericho, the legendary performer was right to decline the offer. JBL stated he would have made the same decision if presented with similar terms, suggesting that the creative opportunities alone may not have been enough to sway Jericho away from AEW.

AEW future with Paramount and Warner Bros merger

A massive new development in the potential Paramount-Warner Bros. Discovery merger has dramatically shifted the scale of this transformative media deal. According to reports from April 6, 2026, Paramount Global's Skydance-led bid to acquire Warner Bros. Discovery has reportedly secured nearly $24 billion in financial backing from multiple Middle Eastern sovereign wealth funds, with Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund leading the charge.

The financial structure of this unprecedented investment demonstrates the serious nature of the acquisition. Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund has agreed to provide about billion to Paramount, with the state-controlled funds of Qatar and Abu Dhabi providing the remainder of the billion. That level of investment signals just how serious this deal is and how far along it's moving, even as significant regulatory hurdles remain in place.

The merger itself carries an estimated $111 billion enterprise value and is still pending approval. It is not expected to be fast-tracked, as officials have already indicated that the deal will face substantial scrutiny before it can close. Currently, the companies have projected the deal to close by the end of the third quarter of 2026, though this timeline remains subject to regulatory outcomes.

Political and regulatory complications present notable challenges to the transaction. There are significant concerns raised by U.S. lawmakers regarding foreign investment and oversight of American media assets. The Paramount-WBD deal will "absolutely not" be on a fast-track for approval due to these political reasons, according to statements from officials. Even with these challenges, the financial backing shows strong confidence in the deal moving forward, especially with major investors stepping in to support the acquisition.

For All Elite Wrestling, this update adds another significant layer to an already evolving situation. AEW currently airs its programming across TNT, TBS, and HBO Max—all under the Warner Bros. Discovery umbrella—meaning any major shift in ownership could directly impact its future. The company's television home, distribution strategy, and overall business operations could all be affected by how the merged entity chooses to allocate resources and programming.

AEW President Tony Khan has already expressed optimism about the merger, viewing it as an opportunity rather than a threat to the company's operations. However, with billions now committed and the deal gaining clear momentum, the stakes are getting higher for everyone involved. This is no longer speculation or preliminary discussions—it represents a major financial move that could reshape the entire media landscape, including precisely where AEW fits into the new corporate structure and what opportunities or challenges may await.

AEW's Brody King On Current Popularity: 'People Are Frustrated'

AEW's Brody King has been vocal about his dislike of ICE and he has detailed why he isn't shy about expressing his opinions. King pointed to WWE Hall of Famer Mick Foley, who was also at the convention where he was interviewed, to highlight how there are others in the pro wrestling business who speak out against what they believe is wrong.

Mercedes Moné indie championship vacate

Mercedes Moné's run as Ultimo Moné has come to an end. After collecting various championships around the world, the former AEW star went on a tour to drop the titles one by one. However, terms couldn't be reached for her final independent championship, and Moné vacated the APAC Wrestling Women's Championship. The APAC title originates from Malaysia, and Moné had won the gold from Nor Phoenix Diana at a House of Glory event in New York. The vacation was attributed to budgetary and scheduling matters that prevented the two sides from reaching an agreement.

Moné explained her decision in the latest edition of her publication Moné Mag Volume 3, Issue 46. She described the situation as a tough call that she had to make while in London. According to her statement, her team made considerable efforts to find a workable solution. They had conversations, explored various options, and attempted to make things work with APAC Wrestling. However, the promotion ultimately told her team they didn't have the budget to fly her out and weren't open to working with other promotions to facilitate the title drop. For Moné, this approach didn't align with her vision for how the situation should be handled.

Moné went on to reflect on the broader lesson from the situation. She acknowledged that not every partnership in the wrestling business works out as hoped. However, she emphasized that she has learned to stand firm in her worth, her time, and what she brings to the table. According to Moné, every title she holds means something and every move she makes has purpose. While she noted that the decision wasn't easy, she described it as necessary. She ended her statement by noting that growth sometimes means letting go and affirming that she is always moving forward.

In response to the situation, APAC Wrestling founder Ayez Shaukat issued his own statement that expressed respect for Moné while addressing what he felt was an incomplete public narrative. Shaukat's statement revealed that he woke up to a flood of texts, tags, and questions about the situation, prompting him to clarify his side of events. He stated clearly that he has nothing but respect for Moné, acknowledging that she is a global star in high demand and that her schedule and travel limitations are real concerns. He also expressed that he does not blame her for the situation and appreciates the opportunity they had to work together.

However, Shaukat felt compelled to correct what he saw as a mischaracterization of events. He emphasized that the public narrative being pushed does not fully reflect what actually happened. According to his account, they did try to find solutions, and they did explore working with other promotions because that was what was asked of them. Shaukat stated that he personally reached out, followed up, and tried to make multiple scenarios work. He also needed to correct what he felt was a misunderstanding regarding responsibility for travel arrangements.

Shaukat clarified that he did not ask other promotions to fly his wrestler in for APAC Wrestling.
