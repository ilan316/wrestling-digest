🔥 Gunn Club returns to AEW

The Gunn Club is Back in AEW

The Bang Bang Gang are whole again. Austin and Colten Gunn made a surprise return on AEW Collision at the Peစီa Civic Center in Peoria, Illinois on Saturday, May 2, 2026, officially reuniting with Juice Robinson and Ace Austin after a lengthy absence due to injuries.

The return came during a pivotal moment on the episode. Juice Robinson and Ace Austin defeated Death Riders members Wheeler Yuta and Daniel Garcia in their match, with Juice delivering his DDT finisher for the victory. After the bout, Robinson caught the attention of Jon Moxley on commentary, motioning that he wanted a shot at Moxley's AEW Continental Championship. The situation quickly escalated when Yuta and Garcia attacked Robinson and Austin in retaliation. However, the Gunn Club's music hit, and Austin and Colten rushed to the ring for the save. Instead, Austin and Colten delivered the 3:10 to Yuma to Yuta, leaving the Collision cowboys standing tall as the crowd erupted.

In a backstage interview conducted after the show, Austin Gunn addressed his return, saying that while they usually have two words to say, tonight they had four: The Gunns are back. Colten acknowledged his serious knee injury, stating that while he may have ripped his patella in half, he refused to wait nine months to a year to return to in-ring action because the boys needed him. Juice Robinson capped off the promo by declaring that he had beaten Jon Moxley before and would do it again.

The Gunn Club's absence from AEW programming had stretched for an extended period. Both brothers had been dealing with significant injuries that derailed their momentum. Austin Gunn had been out with an injury dating back to late 2024, with his last match coming in March 2025 when he participated in a 21-man Blackjack Battle Royal at AEW Revolution. Colten's situation was even more dire, as he had suffered a severe knee injury in July 2025 after returning to the promotion for his first match since February of that year. The injury had initially threatened to sideline him for nine months to a year, but medical clearance came through last month, allowing for an accelerated recovery.

Their last program before the injuries had concluded with a title match loss to The Hurt Syndicate in February 2025. The team had struggled to regain traction afterward before the injury setbacks forced them away from television entirely.

AEW capitalized immediately on the return, releasing new merchandise featuring the Gunns with their catchphrase "Gunns Up" within hours of the segment airing. The promotion clearly viewed the return as significant enough to move quickly on commercial opportunities.

The return sets up several intriguing storylines heading forward. The reestablishment of the Bang Bang Gang as a complete four-man unit strengthens their numbers in the tag team division and provides additional depth to their interactions with the Death Riders. Additionally, Juice Robinson's implicit challenge to Jon Moxley for the Continental Championship adds another layer to the brewing tension, especially with the Gunn Club now positioned as support for Robinson and Austin going forward.
