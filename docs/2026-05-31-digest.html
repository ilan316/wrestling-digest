🔥 AEW announces new TBS Champion tournament with Survival of the Fittest match

AEW has unveiled an innovative plan to crown a new TBS Champion following the injury that forced Willow Nightingale to vacate the title. During the May 30, 2026 episode of AEW Collision, Tony Schiavone announced that the company will hold a series of six qualifying matches over the next month, with the six winners advancing to a Survival of the Fittest match.

Tony Schiavone explained the tournament structure during the broadcast: "We promised that we would tell you about the TBS title that was vacated because of the injury to Willow Nightingale. Well, over the next month, we are going to have a series of 6 matches, 6 matches, and the 6 winners will go on into a match that it could only be called the Survival of the Fittest match. You have seen it on Ring of Honor, Nigel."

The culmination of this tournament will take place on July 1, 2026, during the AEW Dynamite broadcast from San Diego. Nigel McGuinness provided additional context about the match's significance: "A Ring of Honor, a company that made the style famous. 6 matches which will lead to one final match. 6 women compete, and whoever wins that will emerge—the 6 women will emerge as the TBS Champion. The history speaks for itself. Chaos all over the ring. You never know what you're going to finally get, but ultimately one woman will remain victorious and take the TBS title with her."

The Survival of the Fittest match format has deep historical roots in Ring of Honor, where it first originated in 2004. Bryan Danielson was the first-ever winner of the men's version. The first-ever women's Survival of the Fittest match took place at Supercard of Honor on May 15, where Athena retained the ROH Women's Championship, defeating Maya World, Billie Starkz, Yuka Sakazaki, Trish Adora, and Zayda Steel. Since Tony Khan purchased ROH in 2022, he has brought back this prestigious match format.

Willow Nightingale, the previous TBS Champion, was forced to vacate the title just days before Double or Nothing, citing a shoulder injury. Her reign began on the final Dynamite of 2025, where she defeated Mercedes Mone. The injury also forced Nightingale to withdraw from the Women's Owen Hart Foundation Tournament, where she had been scheduled to face Alex Windsor.

Rather than simply handing out a title shot, AEW is making competitors earn their place in the championship match through the series of qualifying bouts. The company has not yet revealed all six qualifying matches, but the road to a new champion has officially begun. With the TBS Championship vacant and six spots up for grabs, AEW's women's division now has significant opportunity heading into July, with multiple competitors vying to prove they are the fittest in the company.
