🔥 AEW Double or Nothing 2026 - MJF vs Darby Allin Main Event

MJF REGAINS AEW WORLD CHAMPIONSHIP AT DOUBLE OR NOTHING, KEVIN KNIGHT SHOCKS WITH HEEL TURN

Maxwell Jacob Friedman captured the AEW World Championship for the third time in his career at Double or Nothing, defeating Darby Allin in a brutal and chaotic Title vs. Hair match that main evented the pay-per-view at Louis Armstrong Stadium in Queens, New York. The match was physically demanding for both competitors, but especially for Allin, who suffered multiple injuries throughout the bout including a hardway cut to the back of his head.

The path to this rematch began when Darby Allin defeated MJF in just 2 minutes and 15 seconds on the April 18, 2026 edition of AEW Dynamite in Allin's hometown of Seattle, capturing the AEW World Championship for the first time in his career. Since winning the title, Allin established himself as a fighting champion, defending it eight times against opponents including Tommaso Ciampa, Brody King, Kevin Knight, PAC, Konosuke Takeshita, Sammy Guevara, and Speedball Mike Bailey in a grueling 40-day title reign. MJF, desperate to reclaim his former championship, demanded another match and agreed to put his hair on the line as stipulation to earn a shot at the gold.

The main event itself was a war between these two long-time rivals who have faced each other dating back to the independent wrestling circuit. Early in the match, Allin crashed and burned on a high-risk dive to the outside, setting the tone for a night of punishment. MJF controlled much of the contest while Allin sold every move, but the former champion continued to press on despite the damage. Allin hit three back-to-back side headlock takeovers early in the match, a staple of their rivalry, but these weren't enough to secure victory.

As the match progressed, the punishment escalated dramatically. MJF hit a devastating package piledriver to Allin onto the turned-over steel steps. Later, MJF attempted to give Allin a forced haircut at the barber's chair but Allin fought back and attacked the Salt of the Earth, choking him out. MJF was then placed on a table at the entrance area as Allin climbed the scaffolding set up at the stage. Allin then paid tribute to Mick Foley by hitting his signature Coffin Drop from high above, crashing through the table. However, in the process, Allin hit the back of his head on a platform, causing him to bleed from that area. This was a hardway cut, as the New York State Athletic Commission does not permit blade work or blood on shows.

Despite the cut and massive damage from the spot, Allin dragged MJF back to the ring and locked in the Scorpion Deathlock submission hold. MJF appeared to be in trouble but managed to get his fingers on the ropes to break the hold. Allin attempted another Coffin Drop but MJF knocked him off balance from the turnbuckle. MJF then climbed to the top rope himself and connected with an avalanche tombstone piledriver, devastating Allin. Following the tombstone, MJF executed his trademark side headlock takeover to secure the three-count and regain the AEW World Championship.

After the match concluded, medical personnel rushed to check on Allin, who was barely moving and left battered from the punishing bout. Allin was eventually strapped onto a stretcher while MJF celebrated with the championship, even posing over his fallen rival in a cold post-match scene reminiscent of his previous title celebration over Hangman Page at Revolution. MJF then raised the championship above his head on the entrance stage while the new champion stood tall with the gold.

What happened next shocked the crowd and MJF himself. Kevin Knight suddenly sprinted down to ringside as medical personnel were still tending to Allin. At first, it appeared Knight was coming to help his friend and fellow wrestler, as he chased MJF away from the stretcher. However, Knight's intentions quickly revealed themselves as he climbed onto the top rope and launched himself onto the prone and injured Allin with a UFO splash, tipping the stretcher over and leaving Allin in even worse condition. MJF stood on the entrance stage with his mouth wide open in shock at what Knight had done, clearly not expecting the attack. Knight then stood over the fallen Allin while MJF applauded the destruction from nearby.

The heel turn was particularly shocking given recent context. On the May 20 edition of AEW Dynamite, just days before Double or Nothing, Kevin Knight had given Darby Allin a pep talk before his championship defense against Speedball Mike Bailey. Knight told Allin that there is nobody he trusts more to defend the honor of AEW against MJF. Additionally, it was revealed that Knight and Allin had trained together, which had been mentioned by the commentary team during the post-match angle. This made Knight's attack feel calculated, with the pep talk now appearing to be a setup to get Allin into the dangerous title match.

After the attack, AEW cameras attempted to get a statement from Kevin Knight regarding his shocking betrayal. However, Knight refused to comment and simply walked off. His first post-show social media activity came via Twitter when he posted only three dots: "…" This cryptic message offered no explanation for his actions but added to the mystique and calculation of the heel turn, making it seem premeditated rather than an emotional outburst.

MJF, meanwhile, took to social media to celebrate his championship victory and the fact that he kept his hair. He posted a series of tweets mocking fans who thought he might actually lose his hair and insulting Allin directly. MJF wrote: "WERE YOU EXPECTING ME TO ACTUALLY LOSE MY HAIR?!?!?!?!?!?!?!?!?" followed by extended laughter. He then directed his comments at Allin, stating: "BEAT THE LITTLE EMO TWINK WITH A HEADLOCK TAKEOVER! 3x WORLD CHAMPION AT 30 YOU F***ING MARKS!!!!!!!!" This arrogant celebration underscored MJF's dominance and set the stage for his third world title reign.

The finish of the match itself was significant for several reasons beyond just the title change. The side headlock takeover that MJF used to win has been a recurring element throughout their entire rivalry, from their early indie matches to their encounters in AEW. This callback to their history made the finish feel earned and deliberate on MJF's part. Additionally, MJF's ability to retain his hair was important to him personally, and he made sure to celebrate that fact as much as his championship victory.

Double or Nothing 2026 has now set up multiple major storylines heading forward. MJF begins his third reign as AEW World Champion and is presented as a serious, focused champion. Darby Allin will need significant recovery time after the brutal title match and the post-match attack, both of which left him bloodied and injured. Most importantly, Kevin Knight's shocking heel turn has created a new feud between him and Allin that will develop once the former champion recovers from his injuries. The cryptic nature of Knight's turn and his refusal to explain his motivations suggest that AEW has plans to explore the psychology and reasoning behind his betrayal in the coming weeks. Whether Knight was working with MJF, had his own agenda, or acted on some other motivation remains to be seen, but the attack has positioned Knight as a new threat to Allin's future championship aspirations.

🔥 AEW Double or Nothing 2026 - General Coverage

AEW Double or Nothing 2026 delivered a sold-out spectacle at Louis Armstrong Stadium in Queens on May 24, establishing itself as one of the promotion's finest performances. The event drew 14,028 fans and is tracking toward approximately $1.5 million in gate revenue, marking the second-largest non-WWE gate in United States pro wrestling history. Early pay-per-view advance buys were reportedly way above normal, with the AEW World Championship match between Darby Allin and MJF featuring a title vs. hair stipulation driving particular interest on the secondary market.

The main event delivered the expected drama as Maxwell Jacob Friedman defeated Darby Allin to reclaim the AEW World Championship while keeping his hair intact in a hard-fought street fight. The finish set up an immediate angle when TNT Champion Kevin Knight splashed the defenseless Allin, turning heel and creating intrigue about the champion's future direction. The card featured thirteen total matches across the main show and Zero Hour pre-show, with multiple championship matches producing title changes.

Kyle Fletcher's return proved to be a surprise highlight of the evening. After suffering a meniscus injury during a March 28 Dynamite match against The Rascalz, Fletcher returned within 60 days to shocking effect. During the International Championship match between Kazuchika Okada and Konosuke Takeshita, Fletcher made his presence known. Initially celebrating with the newly crowned International Champion Takeshita, Fletcher shockingly turned on his Don Callis Family stablemate, hitting him with the title belt while Mark Davis and Rocky Romero held him down. This shattering of the ProtoShita friendship created major storyline implications moving forward.

In the Owen Hart Foundation Tournament, Will Ospreay defeated Samoa Joe, Swerve Strickland bested Bandido, and Athena topped Mina Shirakawa to advance. During the post-show media scrum, Ospreay discussed a potential match against Strickland, noting they hadn't crossed paths in a year but were interested in seeing where it goes. He expressed his dream of headlining Wembley Stadium to win a title that means everything to him, stating nothing would stop him from getting that match. Athena's stablemate Thekla expressed confidence that Skye Blue would win the entire Owen Hart tournament, declaring herself the best in the company.

Stadium Stampede saw Chris Jericho's team triumph over Ricochet's squad in the unique multi-person spectacle. Team Jericho featured Chris Jericho, Bobby Lashley, Shelton Benjamin, Jack Perry, Kenny Omega, and Matt and Nick Jackson, while Team Ricochet included Ricochet, Bishop Kaun, Toa Liona, Mark Davis, Andrade El Idolo, Clark Connors, and David Finlay. The match highlighted AEW's ability to create large-scale cinematic wrestling experiences.

A significant moment came with Mick Foley's official debut for AEW. The legendary former WWE star co-hosted the Zero Hour pre-show alongside Renee Paquette, involving himself in an elaborate segment with Darby Allin and MJF. Following the appearance, AEW shared an "All Elite Wrestling" graphic confirming Foley's signing. During the post-show media scrum, Tony Khan praised the potential of Foley and Paquette as an on-air team, suggesting they would work together for an extended period.

In the Continental Championship match, Jon Moxley defeated Kyle O'Reilly in a close, hard-fought battle that saw both men show mutual respect for one another after the contest. The bout showcased the technical wrestling AEW aims to highlight regularly. For the World Tag Team Championships, FTR retained against Adam Copeland and Christian Cage in an I Quit street fight with massive stipulations attached, as a Cope and Cage loss would force retirement from tag team action.

Kazuchika Okada and Konosuke Takeshita delivered a hard-hitting 19-minute International Championship contest, with Takeshita capturing the title and prompting the Kyle Fletcher return and turn. Don Callis himself provided commentary support for both competitors, adding intrigue to the family dynamics at play.

However, the show's success was marred by significant logistical issues. AEW Double or Nothing ran extremely late, creating major problems for fans attempting to leave the venue and get home via public transportation. Video evidence showed large crowds stranded outside Louis Armstrong Stadium after the event, with some fans claiming exits had been shut down and train access was unavailable. Fans took to social media expressing frustration about the situation, with one attendee posting video and tagging AEW asking for help while noting the situation was "so bad" and "getting worse." While the sold-out crowd was a major success for the promotion, this post-show travel mess became a significant talking point online, raising questions about whether AEW needs to shorten its pay-per-view runtimes or if venues require better planning for late-night exits.

Tony Khan addressed numerous topics during the post-show media scrum held in Queens. Regarding potential counter-programming to WWE's WrestleMania 43, scheduled for Saudi Arabia in April, Khan expressed openness to the possibility. When asked if AEW could fill a gap on the domestic wrestling calendar during that weekend, Khan responded positively. "I think it is potentially viable," he said. "And it's a very interesting thought. We'll have to see what happens in the world landscape between now and then, but it's a very interesting possibility." This suggestion came after noting that April traditionally lacks a major domestic wrestling event when WrestleMania goes international.

Khan also announced plans for international expansion of MyAEW service while maintaining domestic components. He indicated that additional wrestling promotions would be included with the service, though specific details remain forthcoming. Regarding Madison Square Garden, Khan recounted the disappointing history of attempting to secure the iconic venue, describing it as similar to Lucy pulling the football away from Charlie Brown in the Peanuts comic strip.

The AEW President praised the New York State Athletic Commission for working cooperatively with the company, noting that while the commission did not permit blood or fighting through the crowd as a local regulatory requirement, AEW still achieved exceptional results. Khan stated he was "glad to keep the state of New York happy tonight" and believed they "hit a home run with the show." He emphasized his commitment to working within local regulations while still delivering compelling wrestling entertainment.

Khan elaborated on his vision for differentiating ROH as a separate entity from AEW programming. He explained that ROH shows feature a different presentation philosophy focused on younger talent, particularly women, with emphasis on pure wrestling rules. He also stressed the importance of maintaining veteran ROH stars' presence on television. Khan expressed pride in Darby Allin's World Championship reign, noting the departing champion "is just about done everything he has set out to do and had one of the greatest World title reigns in the company." He also expressed interest in hearing Kevin Knight's perspective following his attack on Allin, given Knight's heel turn implications.

Khan continued to praise the Paramount deal and his working relationship with David Ellison, emphasizing the partnership is finalized and discussing their prior collaboration on NFL projects through Skydance. He criticized Netflix's strategy of separating TBS and TNT from HBO Max, expressing his preference for maintaining AEW's presence on that platform.

Regarding immediate wrestling coverage, Khan indicated that Wednesday's AEW Dynamite would feature several matches, but acknowledged that the card cannot be fully assembled until wrestlers receive necessary medical clearances following Double or Nothing. Khan sent his best wishes to wrestler Rebel and expressed interest in organizing a Fight for the Fallen event to benefit ALS research, urging fans to support that cause.

A concerning incident occurred during the Buy-In pre-show when QT Marshall slipped off the top rope while attempting offense. Vita Coco Water product placement had made the rope slippery, resulting in Marshall taking a difficult fall to the outside. He was assisted to the back and did not participate in the post-match angle following the ten-man tag team match. Marshall's team, featuring Big Boom AJ and The Conglomeration members Mark Briscoe, Roderick Strong, and Orange Cassidy, defeated Shane Taylor Promotions. During the media scrum, Khan assured fans that "QT is going to be okay," though no additional medical details were provided.

When asked about potential usage of CMLL's micro wrestlers, Khan indicated he hadn't given the matter significant thought but would consider the possibility. This response suggested openness to exploring different wrestling formats and talent acquisition strategies.

The post-show media scrum also included colorful moments when vulgar chants erupted during the street fight. When Cage gave a vulgar response to being asked to quit, the crowd chanted "I Banged Your Mother!" Khan openly approved of the chants on social media, posting "Justified 'I banged your mother!' chants for the New York Street Fight I Quit Match World Tag Team Title fight FTR vs. Cope & Cage right NOW!" and thanking fans for watching the event.

Overall, Double or Nothing 2026 achieved significant commercial and critical success despite the post-event logistical problems. The coverage from multiple wrestling news sources indicated belief that the event ranked among AEW's finest in-ring performances to date, with compelling storylines, strong attendance, exceptional gate revenue, and above-average pay-per-view buys combining to validate the promotion's flagship event. However, the stranded fans situation highlighted an area requiring immediate attention for future large-scale AEW productions in similar venues.

🔥 AEW Double or Nothing 2026 - Owen Hart Cup Tournament

AEW Double or Nothing 2026 delivered an impressive card from Louis Armstrong Stadium in Queens, New York on May 24, featuring the continuation of the Owen Hart Foundation Tournament across both men's and women's divisions. The event showcased several high-stakes matches as competitors look to advance toward the finals at All In, which will take place August 30 at Wembley Stadium in London.

In the men's tournament, Will Ospreay defeated Samoa Joe in a hard-fought quarterfinal match that saw the two competitors trade back-and-forth offense throughout. Ospreay came out aggressively from the opening bell, jumping Joe in the corner to set the tone early. The Brit relied on speed and explosive offense while Joe brought his usual power-heavy style and attempted to slow the pace whenever possible. Joe recovered from Ospreay's early assault and played his signature offense, locking in the Coquina Clutch at one point that seemed to have the match won. However, Ospreay showed tremendous heart, fighting out of submissions and escaping the hold by grabbing the bottom rope. He also fought his way out of a Muscle Buster attempt. Joe kicked out at one after a Hidden Blade, but Ospreay connected with a second Hidden Blade to finally secure the pinfall victory. The win earned Ospreay a bow of respect from Joe and advanced him one step closer to the main event at Wembley. Ospreay will next face the winner of the opening round match between Mark Davis and Jack Perry in the semifinals.

Ospreay's path to training with Jon Moxley and the Death Riders added personal stakes to his bout with Joe. Before Ospreay left for neck surgery, Joe and The Opps had his back, with the Samoan Submission Machine believing Will should train with his team. Joe took Ospreay's decision to work with Moxley instead as an insult. Through his training with Moxley, who successfully defended his Continental Championship on the same card, Ospreay learned the value of not allowing a fired-up opponent time to settle in the ring.

The second men's tournament quarterfinal saw Swerve Strickland overcome Bandido in another closely contested match. Bandido brought his usual mix of speed and creativity while Strickland wrestled with a more aggressive and focused approach throughout the bout. Both competitors came out of the gate very hot and maintained high energy from the opening bell. Strickland carried on with his brutal reputation, showing Bandido no mercy in his assault while the masked challenger sold the damage as Swerve stalked his opponent. The action remained fast-paced and hard-hitting, with Bandido kicking out of a double stomp from the top rope. The competitors eventually battled on the ring apron, where Bandido connected with a reverse poisonrana to send Strickland to the floor. In the end, Swerve overcame the ROH Champion with a vertebreaker and connected with the House Call for the pinfall victory. This was a hard-fought win that took a lot out of both stars. Strickland will next face the winner of the match between Claudio Castagnoli and Brody King in the next round.

The Men's Owen Hart Cup has become one of AEW's most prestigious tournaments since launching in 2022. Past winners including Adam Cole, Ricky Starks, Bryan Danielson, and Hangman Page have helped turn the tournament into a major stepping stone toward the AEW World Championship picture. Winners of both Owen Hart tournaments will move on to challenge the AEW World Champion and the AEW Women's Champion respectively at All In.

In the women's tournament, Athena defeated ROH Women's World Champion Mina Shirakawa in the opening round. The two women took each other to the limit with a lot of near falls along the way. Shirakawa attempted to inflict damage on Athena's legs throughout the match, but the champion stood through it all. Athena used her size advantage against Shirakawa as much as possible, while Mina had no issue moving Athena around the ring and hoisting her up on her shoulders to bring her down to the mat. The two traded advantages multiple times with a slew of fast offensive moves. Toward the closing moments, Athena executed the O-Face on Shirakawa and secured the pinfall. With her victory, Athena booked her spot in the women's tournament semifinals. She will face the winner of the match between Sareee and Skye Blue later on in the tournament.

The women's Owen Hart Foundation Tournament has established itself as a prestigious event since its inception in 2022 alongside Martha Hart and the Owen Hart Foundation. Britt Baker became the first women's winner in 2022, giving the tournament immediate marquee credibility. Willow Nightingale scored one of the biggest wins of her career with an emotional tournament victory in 2023 that helped establish her as one of AEW's top babyfaces. Mariah May won the tournament in 2024 during her breakout run in AEW, while Mercedes Moné added her name to the list in 2025. Winners receive The Owen trophy and a special championship belt. Only time will tell who wins the tournament this year, but AEW is giving the women a prominent stage to display their talents.

Other women's tournament matches still to be announced include Alex Windsor facing a wild-card opponent, Hazuki facing Persephone, and the previously mentioned Sareee versus Skye Blue matchup.

In a non-tournament match, Jon Moxley successfully retained his AEW Continental Championship against Kyle O'Reilly in a no-time-limit match.
