🔥 Wardlow on American Gladiators

---

AEW star Wardlow is ready to spar, snarl, and bite his way through his rivals in the 2026 reboot of "American Gladiators." Under the name "Fang," the former three-time TNT Champion shared a video on X (formerly known as Twitter) of himself and his competitors ahead of the April 17 premiere. "Wardlow is officially FANG! One of the new #AmericanGladiators which debuts April 17th on @primevideo. Very excited for this, I grew up watching this show so little me is very proud to say, I am an American Gladiator! Pretty wild. Hope y'all are ready!" he wrote.

The reboot maintains the convention of the original American Gladiators series, where competitors receive specialized names rather than competing under their real identities. Wardlow's "Fang" moniker—complete with a wolf emoji—fits perfectly with the tradition established by the original show's iconic names like Ice, Nitro, Turbo, and Zap.

Wardlow isn't the only professional wrestler joining the American Gladiators revival. Former NWA World Women's Champion and AEW colleague Kamille (Kailey Latimer) will compete under the name "Hurricane." Beyond AEW, the cast includes former two-time WWE 24/7 Champion Eric "Rick Boogs" Bugenhagen as "The Bull," former two-time TNA World Tag Team Champion Jessie "Mr. Pectacular" Godderz competing as "Steel," and former OVW Women's Champion Jessica "J-Rod" Roden as "Supernova." Two-time WWE Champion Mike "The Miz" Mizanin will host the festivities.

Known for being "Mr. Mayhem" and one of Don Callis' adopted sons in AEW, Wardlow has been notably absent from in-ring action for an extended period. He hasn't competed since March 2024, when he challenged Samoa Joe for the AEW World Championship. Wardlow briefly resurfaced backstage at AEW Revolution earlier this month, but he has been sidelined since last summer after tearing his pectoral muscle. The injury occurred immediately after he returned from another year-long injury during a segment at AEW Forbidden Door in August 2025 as a member of the Don Callis Family. At this time, there have been no reports on whether he is cleared to compete in AEW.

His appearance on American Gladiators represents a notable next chapter for the former rising star, whose momentum had stalled in recent years. While fans wait to see if Wardlow returns to the wrestling ring, they'll have the opportunity to see him in action on Prime Video when the American Gladiators reboot premieres on April 17, 2026.

AEW Star Anthony Ogogo's Progress

---

Anthony Ogogo's journey in AEW has been marked by early promise followed by inconsistent television appearances and significant behind-the-scenes developments that shaped his wrestling career.

Ogogo, a former Olympic boxer, made his televised AEW debut in 2021 facing off against Cody Rhodes in a controversial Double Or Nothing bout. However, for various reasons, he has appeared infrequently on AEW programming since that match, though he has been seen more regularly working in Ring of Honor, where he appears as a member of Shane Taylor Promotions, often serving as a manager for the stable.

According to QT Marshall, who was involved in Ogogo's feud with Rhodes, the wrestler has continued working to improve throughout his entire tenure despite the lack of regular television exposure. "Anthony, like a lot of people in wrestling – if they're not on TV every week, they wonder what they could do better," Marshall said on the "What Happened When?" podcast. "It's not for a lack of trying, let's put it that way. ... Real nice guy. I think it's very hard for someone like him because he's used to boxing, and ... if you win, you win."

Marshall noted that he had actually requested to work with Ogogo as tag team partners in order to see how he performed in the ring. Looking back on that time period, Marshall recalled expressing concerns to Tony Khan about the storyline's implications for Ogogo's development. "I just remember telling him ... it's a huge spot for someone pretty new," he said. Marshall clarified that he wasn't attempting to take Ogogo's spot on the card, but was instead concerned about his long-term growth as a wrestler. "I just think he got a little bit too much, too early maybe. It's hard to go backwards once you're there. But he hasn't given up. He's trying the best he can."

What few knew at the time was that Ogogo's AEW introduction could have looked very different. According to Ogogo in a recent interview, he was originally planned to join Chris Jericho's Inner Circle faction during the early days of AEW, years before his 2021 debut. "I was supposed to be Jericho's bodyguard, part of his Inner Circle group," Ogogo revealed. "I think Tony Khan's idea was for me to work with Jericho and learn from Jericho. But I think Jericho wanted Jake [Hager], he knew Jake from their time in WWE, they had some real life situations with each other."

The Inner Circle debuted at the end of the first episode of AEW Dynamite in 2019, with Jericho joined by Hager, Sammy Guevara, Santana, and Ortiz. The faction lasted until 2022, when Jericho and Hager turned on the other members and formed the Jericho Appreciation Society.

Although the plan for Ogogo to join The Inner Circle never materialized, he still views Jericho as an instrumental figure in his professional wrestling career. According to Ogogo, Jericho's Learning Tree stable had its roots in reality. "I would always ask questions and he'd teach me stuff. He'd always watch my matches," Ogogo said. "[He'd] say 'do this, don't do that.' He's the man who knows how to get over so Jericho is someone I definitely learned a lot from."

Ogogo remains signed with AEW, though his in-ring appearances have been few and far between in recent times. His recent match at a Ring of Honor taping may signal more in-ring work in his future.

Kenny Omega Virtual Pro Wrestling 2

---

Kenny Omega has named Virtual Pro Wrestling 2 as his pick for the greatest wrestling video game ever created. The declaration comes from an interview where Omega discussed his favorite games of all time, naturally shifting to the topic of wrestling titles.

Virtual Pro Wrestling 2 was released on Nintendo 64 in 2000 and remained exclusive to Japan, setting it apart from the WWE games that dominated that era. Despite its limited regional availability, the game has maintained a devoted following among wrestling and gaming enthusiasts for over two decades.

When discussing what makes the game stand out, Omega emphasized two key factors: the roster depth and the gameplay mechanics. "Virtual Pro Wrestling 2, I would say, is the best wrestling game of all time. Both for the roster and for the wrestling and mixed martial art mechanics to it," Omega stated during the interview.

Omega's assessment is likely to resonate with longtime fans of the AKI engine era, as many gamers still consider those games the gold standard, even as modern wrestling titles continue to evolve. His take will likely spark debate among the gaming community about what truly constitutes the greatest wrestling game ever made.

Beyond naming his favorite game, Omega revealed his ongoing passion for gaming and its integration with professional wrestling. He mentioned that if AEW were to return to video game development, he would want deeper involvement from Geta-san, the director of the AKI engine games. According to Omega, Geta-san previously served as an advisor for AEW's original gaming project, but Omega expressed a desire for him to have greater creative control and narrative influence in future endeavors.

Kenny Omega's close ties to gaming culture make his opinions on the subject particularly noteworthy within both the wrestling and gaming communities, and his endorsement of Virtual Pro Wrestling 2 serves as a reminder of the lasting impact classic wrestling games continue to have.

AEW All In 2026 Ticket Sales and Main Event

---

AEW's biggest show of the year returns to the United Kingdom this summer as All In London once again takes place at Wembley Stadium on August 30, marking the third time in four years the company has run the iconic football venue. There is always talk regarding ticket sales when it comes to All In, and recent reporting broke down the opening day ticket sales, how many people are expected to attend, and what fans who do attend could see close the show.

After the first day of general public sales and all the pre-sales had wrapped up, All In London 2026 sold or distributed 19,883 tickets, a number that has since jumped to 21,141 tickets as of March 27. While that number is not one to be laughed at, industry analysis admitted that comparing the opening day sales of the 2023 and 2024 events could lead to some worrying signs. It was noted that no one is expecting AEW to hit the lofty heights of selling 81,035 tickets like what happened for the 2023 event, but the opening day sales of that show were in the 60,000 range, and the 2024 show had sold just under 34,000 tickets on the opening day.

The company is hoping to draw numbers similar to the 2024 event, which sold over 53,000 tickets, particularly with Will Ospreay back in action and the company seeming to be on the upswing in terms of momentum. However, tracking buying patterns, current projections don't see AEW getting near the 2024 numbers. In fact, projections indicate that 35,000 paid attendees would be what the company should expect, especially considering that tickets are much cheaper than 2024 and the numbers are nowhere near where they were two years ago. It was also noted that AEW decided to run The O2 Arena for last year's Forbidden Door pay-per-view, and while that show did eventually sell out, that wasn't until the day of the show. If AEW aren't in a position to sell out The O2 Arena ahead of time, running Wembley Stadium may not be the best idea, regardless of how impressive selling 35,000 tickets might be for a company not named WWE.

As we all know, if AEW hits on a hot angle or books a must-see match, those ticket sales will likely be a lot higher than what projections are predicting. That then leads to the big question of what match will main event the show. Now that he's back in action, all signs point to Will Ospreay not only being in the main event, but also challenging for the AEW Men's World Championship. As for who Ospreay could face, two names are currently leading the race.

MJF will defend the AEW Men's World Championship against Kenny Omega at Dynasty on April 12, with the winner of that match looking to be the man who will main event Wembley Stadium. Picking between MJF and Omega is interesting because they both have pros and cons. A lot of the core AEW fanbase are leaning more towards Ospreay facing Omega, with the two men already having two matches under their belt in recent years, and a trilogy match taking place on AEW's biggest stage makes a lot of sense. Plus, the matches at Wrestle Kingdom and Forbidden Door 2023 are considered to be two of the best matches of the 21st century by those fans.

On the other hand, AEW's business is improving so much that it almost feels too early to take the title away from MJF at this point, and perhaps shifting the title away from MJF in the summer might create more excitement if that's the direction AEW wants to go in. Ospreay and MJF did face each other at Wembley Stadium in 2024, albeit with MJF's AEW American/International Championship on the line, so the idea of running it back for the top prize in the company is one that could make more sense business wise.

Overall, AEW will go with whatever the company believes will shift more tickets and tell a better story.

Chris Jericho Forgotten WWE Feud

---

Chris Jericho Says This 'Forgotten' WWE Feud Deserves More Credit

Chris Jericho believes his 2001 feud with The Rock deserves far more recognition than it has received over the years. During a recent interview, the current AEW star recounted the storyline, which took place during the WCW invasion angle, when Rock repeatedly mocked Jericho for his inability to capture the world championship.

Jericho expressed his conviction that this particular rivalry stands out as one of the most underrated feuds of his entire career. "A really great one that I think deserves more recognition is Jericho versus the Rock," he said. "We could go toe to toe in the ring, and Rock was a great wrestler. Rock liked to talk things through in promos first, which I can do too, but there were very few people who could genuinely stand up to him on the mic. To have a great dragon slayer, you need a great dragon, and I think we were very good from that aspect."

The feud reached its climactic point when Jericho finally achieved what Rock had been taunting him about. "There was a whole run where he accused me of not being able to win the big one — and then I went ahead and beat him for the Undisputed Championship," Jericho explained.

Beyond their singles rivalry, the two competitors also shared tag team gold together. "We were even tag team champions together at one point, for about two weeks. I actually forgot about that until I saw a photo from Madison Square Garden recently and I thought, we were never tag champs — but there it is," Jericho recalled.

Jericho credited the quality of their in-ring work and promo exchanges as evidence of the feud's excellence. "Every time we were in the ring together or did a promo or a backstage segment, it was excellent. I think Rock's been gone from wrestling for so long, and people associate him so strongly with Austin and Triple H, that the Jericho-Rock rivalry kind of gets forgotten. But go back and watch it — it's very top-level stuff," he concluded.

Kenny Omega AEW NJPW CMLL Partnerships

---

Kenny Omega recently spoke with Justin Barrasso of Undisputed for an in-depth interview covering all things pro wrestling. During the discussion, Omega, the No. 1 Contender to the AEW World Championship, addressed the company's official partnerships with New Japan Pro Wrestling and CMLL, emphasizing how these collaborations have been instrumental in AEW's mission to showcase diverse wrestling styles to fans worldwide.

Omega reflected on how traditional wrestling promotions operated with a restrictive mentality. "You were taught that nothing else existed prior to what you were watching," Omega said. "The beginning was the beginning, and that it was the entire world. For us, we couldn't do that in AEW. There were a lot of great steps along the way."

Rather than following the conventional model, AEW chose to differentiate itself through international collaboration and co-promotion. Omega highlighted the significance of these partnerships in achieving the company's broader vision. "One that really sticks out is when we reached a point where we had an official partnership with New Japan and CMLL, and we were willing, able, and eager to show the various professional wrestling styles of the world to our American fans and international fans," Omega explained. "That was a main goal for me. I wanted people to experience all the different types of professional wrestling."

Acknowledging that not every initiative succeeds, Omega expressed pride in AEW's overall trajectory and resilience. "Very rarely did I feel like we took steps backward. You take pride in your work, so when we did, those were crushing. Not everything works, but wrestling has a circular momentum," he said.

Omega believes these strategic partnerships, combined with AEW's talented roster, position the company among the world's elite wrestling promotions. "So when something didn't work out, we have the roster to turn it around," he said. "That's the case now more than ever. This is a roster with a dedication to the art and sport of wrestling. That's why we'll always be in the conversation of the best professional wrestling company in the world."

Looking ahead, Omega is focused on his upcoming championship opportunity. He is scheduled to challenge MJF for the AEW World Championship at AEW Dynasty 2026, taking place on April 12 at Rogers Arena in Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada.

Zayda Steel AEW Signing

---

Zayda Steel had been positioned as one of the top prospects in WWE's ID program, frequently competing for the WWE ID Women's Championship. However, by fall, she made the significant decision not to renew her contract, and shortly thereafter joined AEW and Ring of Honor, where she quickly became integrated into the Sky Flight stable alongside Scorpio Sky, Christopher Daniels, and Dante and Darius Martin.

Speaking on "AEW Unrestricted," Steel explained the unexpected path that led to her signing, revealing that joining Tony Khan's promotion wasn't part of her original plan when she first became available on the open market.

"I chose to walk away from my previous employment," Steel said. "My choice. You know, I'm on my own journey. I love wrestling. I just want to embrace it and be around people that love it just as much as I do. So I chose to walk away. My plans, at first, were to go to Japan. I was going to take the last two to three months of the year as a little mental break, financial break, and I was going to go overseas and do it the right way this time. And five days after I tweeted that tweet, Tony Khan texted me. So plans changed."

The decision to leave WWE came after Steel began questioning her future with the company in the spring. She revealed that her mental health and love for the sport were suffering under her previous arrangement.

"I love wrestling, like I said, and this is my escape from reality," Steel explained. "And once I started to feel like my only escape was work, and I started to not like it and not have fun...I want to always love this, so I chose to walk away. As well as my mental [state], personally, I was losing myself. I was losing the love for wrestling, the only thing I've only loved. So it was...I was like 'Yeah, I need to do my own thing.'"
