🔥 WWE Hall of Fame 2026 - Bad News Brown

TL;DR: Bad News Brown (Allen Coage), the late Olympic judo medalist and WWE legend, has been announced as a 2026 WWE Hall of Fame inductee in the Legacy Class.

WWE has officially announced that Bad News Brown will be inducted into the 2026 WWE Hall of Fame as a Legacy Class inductee. Triple H made the announcement on March 26, 2026, via social media, stating: "From standing on the podium at the 1976 Summer Olympics to the center of the ring at #WrestleMania IV, Bad News Brown was a fierce competitor with an attitude to match. For his accomplishments in combat sports and contributions to @WWE, it is a pleasure to announce his induction into the 2026 Legacy Class of the #WWEHOF."

Brown's journey to wrestling stardom began well before his professional wrestling career. He won the bronze medal in judo for the United States at the 1976 Summer Olympics, establishing himself as a world-class athlete. He later combined his legitimate grappling expertise with vicious brawling techniques he developed growing up in Harlem, New York, to become one of sports-entertainment's most feared competitors.

Brown's professional wrestling career spanned multiple continents and promotions. He started with New Japan Pro-Wrestling shortly after his Olympic success before moving to Stampede Wrestling in Calgary, Alberta, Canada, where he competed against future WWE legends like Bret Hart. He made his WWE debut in 1988 and made an immediate impact, knocking out opponent after opponent with a devastating enzuigiri kick. At WrestleMania IV in 1988, one of his signature achievements came when he won a battle royal by eliminating Hart.

During his WWE tenure, Brown established himself as a fierce, independent competitor with an unwavering no-nonsense attitude. He famously kept to himself, making no friends, and even the most evil WWE Superstars couldn't warm up to him. At several Survivor Series events, the Harlem native opted to walk out on his team rather than work as part of a unit. Brown consistently demanded title opportunities and derided what he perceived as obstacles to his advancement, infamously referring to his perceived rivals as "beer-bellied sharecroppers" and "spineless cockroaches."

Brown feuded with some of WWE's biggest names during the late 1980s and early 1990s. He famously clashed with Hulk Hogan, "Macho Man" Randy Savage, "Rowdy" Roddy Piper, and Jake "The Snake" Roberts. His feud with Piper proved particularly memorable. After Piper eliminated him from the 1990 Royal Rumble Match, Brown climbed back into the ring and forced Piper over the top rope, eliminating him in return and triggering a brawl that no WWE official could halt. This led to a match at WrestleMania VI that ended in a double countout.

Brown's most humorous storyline came during his feud with Jake Roberts in 1990. Surprisingly, there was one thing the hardened New Yorker feared: snakes. When Roberts gave Brown a birthday gift in the form of his python, Damien, the surly Superstar was spooked. In an effort to combat Roberts and his dangerous snake, Brown went back to his roots in Harlem, searching through subway tunnels to find the biggest sewer rats in New York City. Though Brown trained the rodents to feast on snakes, they ultimately couldn't help him in the ring against Roberts at SummerSlam 1990. After his defeat at the hands of "The Snake," Bad News Brown disappeared from WWE.

Following his WWE career, Brown continued wrestling internationally. He competed for UWF-I and New Japan Pro-Wrestling in Japan, as well as several independent promotions. His wrestling career came to an end in 1999 due to a knee injury that forced his retirement.

Brown passed away from a heart attack in 2007, but his legacy lives on as his fellow legends welcome him into the WWE Hall of Fame. The 2026 WWE Hall of Fame ceremony will take place on April 17 at Dolby Live at Park MGM in Las Vegas, Nevada, during WrestleMania 42 weekend. Brown joins an impressive 2026 class that includes Stephanie McMahon, AJ Styles, Demolition (Ax & Smash), "Sycho" Sid Vicious (also a Legacy Class inductee), and NBA legend Dennis Rodman. The induction represents validation of a career built on legitimacy, toughness, and standing apart from the rest of the pack, recognizing not only his contributions to WWE but his broader accomplishments in Olympic athletics and professional wrestling worldwide.

🔥 John Cena Media Appearances and Interactions

TL;DR: John Cena has made multiple media appearances while addressing criticism about his interview style, and Roman Reigns is promoting WrestleMania 42 on mainstream platforms.

John Cena has been active across multiple media platforms recently, engaging in both mainstream entertainment and addressing public perception of his public persona. The 17-time World Champion appeared in a Netflix promotional video explaining Major League Baseball's new automated balls and strikes (ABS) system ahead of the 2026 MLB season launch. In the segment, Cena discussed his lifelong passion for baseball and how the new technology will impact the sport, explaining that while umpires will continue calling games, the ABS system provides objective accuracy that the human eye cannot match. He detailed the challenge system, noting that batters, pitchers, and catchers can appeal up to two times per game, with successful challenges retaining the team's challenge opportunity and unsuccessful ones resulting in the loss of a challenge.

The incident highlighted Cena's willingness to set boundaries while maintaining composure.

🔥 Chris Jericho Wyatt Family Storyline Pitch

TL;DR: Chris Jericho pitched a Wyatt Family storyline involving Shawn Michaels to Vince McMahon, but the idea was rejected after McMahon complained about his tough steak instead of evaluating the creative concept.

Chris Jericho has revealed a scrapped creative pitch from his WWE days that he still reflects on fondly. During a recent interview with GamesHub, the wrestling legend detailed a storyline concept involving Bray Wyatt and Shawn Michaels that he believed was strong enough to culminate in a SummerSlam main event.

The proposed angle was straightforward yet compelling. According to Jericho, the Wyatt Family would attack him, forcing him to seek help from an unlikely ally—Shawn Michaels, his former bitter rival. The narrative tension would come from uncertainty: would Michaels actually accept Jericho's offer despite their history? The angle would build to a moment where both men were attacked by the Wyatt Family together, setting up a tag team match at SummerSlam. This dynamic created intrigue around whether Michaels could be trusted and why Jericho felt compelled to call upon someone so unpredictable.

However, the pitch never had a fair chance of being seriously considered. Jericho explained that the circumstances of the meeting with McMahon were already working against him from the start. Triple H was present in the office—a situation Jericho noted was "never ideal" when trying to pitch ideas to Vince McMahon one-on-one. Compounding matters, McMahon hadn't eaten yet, and as Jericho pointedly observed, "when Vince is eating, he's not paying attention to anything else."

Despite these unfavorable conditions, Jericho went through the entire storyline, laying out what he described as "a really solid pitch" with complete structure and clear direction. However, McMahon's response had nothing whatsoever to do with the creative merits of the concept.

As Jericho recounted: "Vince just sat there eating his steak, and when I finished he said, 'Bad cow.' I said, 'What?' He goes, 'Bad cow — the steak is tough.' I said, what do you think of my pitch? He goes, 'What else you got?' The steak killed the whole angle."

This anecdote illuminates the unpredictable nature of WWE's creative process under McMahon's leadership. The rejection wasn't based on legitimate creative criticism or pushback on the storyline itself—it stemmed entirely from external distractions and McMahon's dissatisfaction with his meal. Jericho acknowledged this reality philosophically, stating: "But that's the nature of working for Vince — if he doesn't like it, you're done. Same with Tony, to a degree. You have to please your boss."

Interestingly, Jericho and Bray Wyatt would go on to feud throughout 2014 anyway, with matches taking place at WWE Battleground and SummerSlam that year, along with a steel cage match in September on Raw—which Wyatt won. While this provided a program between the two men, it lacked the creative hook and star power that the Shawn Michaels connection would have provided.

The story also underscores broader themes about how quickly ideas could fall apart in WWE's creative offices, a sentiment previously echoed by other wrestlers who have pitched concepts to McMahon. The unpredictability wasn't necessarily about creative debate or legitimate pushback on angles—sometimes it came down to timing, distraction, and circumstances entirely removed from the wrestling business itself. In this case, a less-than-satisfactory steak dinner derailed what Jericho still believes would have been compelling television involving three major wrestling figures.

🔥 WWE Saturday Night's Main Event at Madison Square Garden

TL;DR: WWE is planning to host Saturday Night's Main Event live from Madison Square Garden on July 18, 2026, marking the first-ever live broadcast of the show from MSG and its second appearance at the iconic venue.

WWE is set to run a major Saturday Night's Main Event special from Madison Square Garden on July 18, according to WrestleVotes Radio reporting on Fightful Select. This announcement carries significant historical weight, as it will mark only the second time the show has been held at MSG and, most notably, the first time it will air live from the venue.

The last time WWE hosted Saturday Night's Main Event at Madison Square Garden was in August 2007, when the show was taped ahead of time rather than broadcast live. CM Punk is the only active wrestler currently on WWE's roster who competed in that 2007 MSG event. The live presentation this year will represent a substantial upgrade in the show's presentation and significance.

The July 18 date is particularly strategic, as it falls on the same weekend as Fanatics Fest in New York City, an event in which WWE is expected to be heavily involved. By stacking Saturday Night's Main Event alongside Fanatics Fest, WWE is effectively turning that weekend into a multi-day attraction for fans, similar to how WrestleMania weekend functions. This positioning makes the July event one of WWE's biggest weekends outside of WrestleMania season, with multiple fan-focused experiences tied to the company converging in New York City.

This update builds on WWE's already confirmed plans for Saturday Night's Main Event in 2026. The company previously announced a May 23 edition at the Allen County War Memorial Coliseum in Fort Wayne, Indiana, which will take place two weeks after the Backlash PLE in Tampa, Florida. The Fort Wayne show marks the brand's continued expansion of NBC and Peacock specials, with tickets having gone on sale on March 20.

Saturday Night's Main Event has experienced a successful modern revival after originally thriving as a network TV staple in the 1980s and early 1990s. WWE's current approach to the special has leaned into delivering big matches and marquee appearances without branding the shows as full premium live events. The most recent Saturday Night's Main Event took place on January 24 in Montreal, Canada, and featured three matches, including AJ Styles' final one-on-one encounter against Shinsuke Nakamura.

With the historic first live broadcast at MSG, the strategic alignment with Fanatics Fest, and the continued rollout of multiple editions throughout 2026, WWE is clearly treating Saturday Night's Main Event as a key part of its calendar moving forward. The company is currently gearing up for WrestleMania 42 in Las Vegas, Nevada, on April 18 and 19, followed by the Fort Wayne event and then the MSG showcase in July.

🔥 NYC Preschool Director Embezzlement

TL;DR: A Brooklyn preschool director allegedly embezzled $2.75 million in tuition funds between 2022 and 2025, spending at least $350,000 on WWE tickets and VIP packages while maintaining a lavish lifestyle she documented on social media.

Murielle Misczak, 43, the director of Kinderhaus Brooklyn in Park Slope, was arrested on Wednesday, March 25, and charged with wire fraud, money laundering, and related offenses. Federal prosecutors allege that she diverted approximately $2.75 million in tuition payments into her personal bank account between January 2022 and October 2025, using the funds strictly for her own personal benefit.

Among the most striking details of the alleged scheme is Misczak's spending on WWE entertainment. According to law enforcement sources and federal indictments, she spent at least $350,000 on WWE tickets and VIP packages. This included purchasing exclusive VIP packages for her three sons to attend WWE RAW events and meet wrestlers backstage. She frequently bragged about these experiences on her public Instagram and other social media accounts.

The alleged embezzlement funded an extraordinarily lavish lifestyle that far exceeded her legitimate annual salary of approximately $40,000. According to a civil lawsuit filed by the school in December, Misczak "took frequent trips and vacations across the country – often every other weekend," enjoying "plush resorts, VIP access to WWE events, and first class amenities, much of which she documented on her public Instagram and other social media accounts." Federal authorities noted that she spent a total of $650,000 on travel and entertainment combined. Additionally, she spent $150,000 on food delivery services and substantial amounts on high-end luxury goods. She also maintained two rental apartments in Park Slope simultaneously.

When questioned about the missing funds, Misczak falsely claimed the money came from her wealthy mother in Switzerland. However, investigators discovered that she had manipulated the school's internal accounting system to cover her tracks, making the accounts show that funds had been deposited into the school's bank account when they had actually been diverted to her personal accounts. She also deleted emails that would have exposed her activities.

The scheme was discovered in October 2025 by the school's principal, who uncovered the financial discrepancies. The discovery created significant financial strain for the institution, forcing the principal to use personal savings to cover payroll and keep the school's operations running.

At her arraignment in Brooklyn federal court on Wednesday afternoon, Misczak pleaded not guilty. She was released on a $200,000 bond secured by a $50,000 wire transfer from her mother. However, she faces strict conditions: she must surrender her passport, wear an ankle bracelet monitor, and adhere to a home curfew. She could face up to 20 years in prison if convicted. The case remains ongoing.

🔥 Bron Breakker Original WWE NXT Plans

TL;DR: WWE originally planned to team Tony D'Angelo and Bron Breakker as a modern-day Steiner Brothers tag team in NXT before scrapping the idea during the NXT 2.0 reset.

When Tony D'Angelo first arrived in WWE NXT alongside Bron Breakker, the company had a specific vision for both men: they would work together as a tag team inspired by the legendary Steiner Brothers. The pairing was already being developed behind the scenes, with D'Angelo and Breakker training together and preparing for their eventual on-screen debut, though the plan never made it to television.

D'Angelo detailed the concept during an appearance on the No-Contest Wrestling podcast, explaining that the team was being primed as "the new age Steiner Brothers type of deal." He noted that wearing a singlet as part of this gimmick wasn't a significant departure for him, as he had already been wearing one before joining WWE. The company was clearly committed to establishing them as a powerhouse duo in the tag team division.

However, the creative landscape shifted dramatically when NXT underwent its major rebranding into NXT 2.0. According to D'Angelo, Road Dogg personally informed him that the original plans were being abandoned. The message was clear: the company was giving everyone a clean slate and reassigning them to different lanes. D'Angelo suddenly had to pivot quickly and figure out a new direction for himself.

That moment of uncertainty proved to be a turning point. During an in-house match, D'Angelo unleashed a different version of himself, complete with the accent and intensity that would eventually become his television character. The reaction from WWE management was immediate and positive—they saw something they hadn't expected and decided to run with it. This spontaneous performance led to the development of D'Angelo's "Don of NXT" character, which became far more successful than the original tag team concept might have been.

While the D'Angelo and Breakker pairing never materialized, both men's careers flourished in different directions. Breakker was pushed into the singles spotlight and eventually captured the NXT Championship multiple times before climbing the WWE ladder. D'Angelo, meanwhile, built his own faction with Channing "Stacks" Lorenzo and established himself as a prominent figure in NXT, eventually becoming involved in major storylines.

The full circle moment came years later when Breakker and Baron Corbin faced off against D'Angelo and Lorenzo for the NXT Tag Team Championships—a pairing that represented the kind of tag team competition that had been originally envisioned, just with completely different partners and outcomes. It's a striking example of how quickly creative decisions can shift in professional wrestling and how a single pivotal moment can completely reshape multiple careers.

Matt Cardona on Indies and WWE

TL;DR: Matt Cardona revealed how his wife Chelsea Green's conversation with WWE President Nick Khan led to his return to WWE, and he named Richard Holliday and Ben Bishop as the top independent wrestlers who are perfect WWE material.

Matt Cardona has opened up about his journey back to WWE and his perspective on the independent wrestling landscape following his six-year stint on the indie scene after his initial WWE release.

Speaking to Undisputed WWE Champion Cody Rhodes on his "What Do You Wanna Talk About?" podcast, Cardona revealed the pivotal conversation that led to his return to the promotion in January 2026. The catalyst came through an unexpected channel: his wife, Chelsea Green, who spoke to WWE President Nick Khan about him while doing media for the Royal Rumble in Saudi Arabia.

"Chelsea was doing, I guess, media for Rumble in Saudi, and Nick Khan was there. She said to Nick something like, 'You see my husband wrestle NXT?' Nick said something like, 'I've always wanted to meet your husband, give him my number.' She gave me Nick Khan's number," Cardona explained. "I know I'm not under contract, but I'm not gonna sneak in. Do that, and it leads to a match. I'm texting immediately. And then Nick called me right there, said, 'What do you want?' I said, 'I want to come back,' and the ball got rolling from there."

The WWE return opportunity came after Cardona worked for TTA as part of an NXT invasion angle. Despite not being under contract with TNA at the time, Cardona made sure to do things the right way before participating in the angle. Following his return to WWE, Cardona joined the SmackDown roster and has since been involved in storylines, including being attacked by Randy Orton after confronting the Viper over his heel turn against Cody Rhodes.

During the same podcast conversation, Cardona was asked to identify the top talent currently working on the independent scene. Having spent considerable time on the indies and establishing himself as the "King of the Indies," Cardona was well-positioned to answer. He highlighted two performers who stood out to him: Ben Bishop and Richard Holliday.

"Effort is very important to me. Ben Bishop and Richard Holliday are my two guys. They post a lot, advance their stories, and do the cell phone promos nobody wants to do. You have to. Those are my two guys. There are many other guys and girls out there, but those are the two who come to mind," Cardona said.

When Cody asked if those two wrestlers "are good," Cardona didn't hesitate with his endorsement. "I think they're good. They're perfect WWE superstars. I've wrestled them both," he replied. Both Holliday and Bishop have extensive independent wrestling experience, having worked across multiple promotions worldwide. Both have also participated in recent WWE tryouts, suggesting they remain on the company's radar as potential future signings. Cardona's endorsement carries significant weight given his firsthand experience competing against them and his recent transition back to WWE from the independent ranks.

Speaking on BTA Philly on March 25, 2026, The Sandman revealed the actual nature of his arrangement with WWE. According to him, he only recently learned the specifics himself after being informed by Brian Myers. "No, I just found this out. Somebody told me — I think Brian Myers — that I had a Legends deal, but I don't. I actually have a likeness contract with them. This clarification is consistent with statements The Sandman has made previously on other platforms. During an appearance on the Major Wrestling Figure Podcast, he made his position on WWE Legends deals abundantly clear. When directly asked whether he had signed one, he firmly declined: "No, I don't. No, they asked. I was like, 'no legends deal'."

The reasoning behind his refusal runs deeper than simple contrarian thinking. The Sandman has based his decision on feedback he's gathered from peers throughout his career in professional wrestling. "Well, because nobody ever said a good thing about a legends deal. I never heard one person say a good thing about them signing a nostalgia agreement," he stated.

WWE Legends deals are standard agreements in the industry designed to secure various rights and commitments from veteran wrestlers. These contracts typically lock in merchandising rights, guarantee appearances at company events, and provide licensing for wrestlers' likenesses in video games and other media — all in the name of capitalizing on nostalgia.

However, The Sandman's situation demonstrates that WWE can obtain the rights it needs through alternative arrangements. His likeness contract allows the company to feature him in video games without requiring him to accept the broader restrictions and terms associated with a traditional Legends deal. This approach appears to give him greater independence while still allowing WWE to profit from his iconic status.

The Sandman's decision underscores his commitment to maintaining autonomy even as a veteran of the industry. By passing on a deal that many of his contemporaries have accepted, he's choosing to operate on his own terms — a stance that reflects his long-standing philosophy as a performer who built his career in ECW, an organization known for pushing boundaries and rejecting WWE's corporate model.
