🔥 AEW Double Or Nothing 2026

AEW Double Or Nothing 2026 is shaping up to be one of the promotion's most significant events of the year, with the May 24 show taking place at Louis Armstrong Stadium in New York City. Given that this is one of AEW's tentpole pay-per-views, multiple title changes are expected throughout the evening, potentially setting a record-setting crowd for the event.

One of the most personal matches on the card is the AEW World Tag Team Championship bout between FTR and Adam Copeland & Christian Cage. The rivalry between these two teams has been building for months. After Copeland and Cage defeated FTR at AEW All Out last year, they stepped away before returning earlier this month to challenge for the titles. That rematch took place at AEW Dynasty 2026, where FTR scored the win to even the series between the teams.

Following their Dynasty loss, Copeland issued a challenge that FTR could not refuse by proposing a New York Street Fight rematch at AEW Double Or Nothing 2026. The major condition attached was that if Cope and Cage lose, they will retire as a tag team. Copeland and Cage winning the title in New York City would be a memorable moment where they finally get a measure of revenge on FTR for everything they have done to them over the past year.

However, the stakes just got even higher. When FTR responded on this week's AEW Collision, Dax Harwood and Cash Wheeler not only accepted the Street Fight challenge but added another stipulation. Dax called for an additional stipulation, turning the match into an I Quit match so there would be no doubt about which team is better once it's over. He wrapped up the promo by taking another shot at their opponents, saying that after Double or Nothing, Copeland and Cage could continue their nostalgia act while FTR would remain the greatest tag team of all time.

With the new I Quit stipulation now attached and Copeland and Cage's future as a team on the line, this match has become one of the most intense and personal bouts on the Double or Nothing card.

Beyond the tag team title picture, several other championship matches are expected to take place at Double Or Nothing 2026. Will Ospreay is anticipated to challenge Jon Moxley for the AEW Continental Championship. Ospreay suffered a heartbreaking loss to Moxley at Dynasty 2026 and has struggled since recovering from double fusion neck surgery, including losing to Mark Davis via referee stoppage on the April 22 episode of AEW Dynamite. Despite these setbacks, Ospreay could look to bounce back by finally defeating Moxley for the first time to capture the Continental Championship.

In the International Championship division, Konosuke Takeshita is set to challenge Kazuchika Okada. The tentative partnership between Don Callis Family members Okada and Takeshita reached a boiling point after their loss to The Young Bucks at AEW Dynasty 2026. After Takeshita walked off on his stablemate, Okada accepted the pre-match challenge from "The Alpha" for an AEW International Championship match at Double Or Nothing 2026. While Okada has been a thorn in Takeshita's side since joining the Don Callis Family in 2025 and may have what it takes to hold onto his gold come May 24, Takeshita is more motivated than ever to humble "The Rainmaker" and could do so by dethroning him for the International Championship.

The AEW Women's World Tag Team Championship is also expected to see action, with Kris Statlander and Hikaru Shida challenging Divine Dominion. Bringing two former AEW Women's World Champions together, Shida and Statlander have been on a winning streak as of late since forming their tag team. Divine Dominion could meet their toughest test yet as champions against Statlander and Shida at the Double Or Nothing pay-per-view.

The AEW World Trios Championship will also be on the line. The Conglomeration's Orange Cassidy, Roderick Strong and Kyle O'Reilly recently defeated The Dogs to win the championship at Dynasty 2026. Looking to capture their first championship since signing with All Elite Wrestling in January, The Rascalz, consisting of Dezmond Xavier, Zachary Wentz and Myron Reed, could cause another title change in the World Trios Championship scene with a win at Double Or Nothing 2026. Since signing with AEW, The Rascalz have been a consistent highlight of the company's television, with all three members standing out in singles, tag team and trios action.

With multiple title matches scheduled and the intensity of the Copeland and Cage versus FTR encounter reaching new heights, AEW Double Or Nothing 2026 is poised to be a landmark event for the promotion.

AEW Trademark Filings

AEW has filed an application to trademark the term "Royal Redemption" with the United States Patent and Trademark Office. The application was filed with USPTO on April 24, 2026, with the serial number 99785754 and is currently awaiting examination.

The filing description for the trademark reads as follows: Entertainment in the nature of wrestling contests; Entertainment services in the nature of live professional wrestling performances; Entertainment services in the nature of production of professional wrestling programs and events; Entertainment services, namely, production and distribution of ongoing television programs in the field of professional wrestling.

It is not yet confirmed whether the "Royal Redemption" name is planned to be used for an event, match type or otherwise. However, Andrew Zarian did report during Friday's Mat Men Pro Wrestling Podcast that the also recently trademarked AEW Redemption is a planned new pay-per-view coming at some point in 2026. If AEW Redemption is indeed the name of a new AEW PPV, one would assume that the Royal Redemption could be a match type feature on the event.

The term's similarity to WWE's Royal Rumble will have fans assuming that Royal Redemption could be AEW's answer to WWE's staple battle royal match. However, AEW's plans are not yet known at this time.

AEW has, for the most part, tried to separate itself from WWE since its inception in 2019. Whether it be the greater focus on in-ring action or more mature level of violence and language, AEW has been successful in doing just that, and its selection of match types have helped further separate them from their main competitor.

Rather than traditional battle royals, AEW has often put its own spin on things. The Casino Gauntlet Match sees stars enter the match individually in a sudden death, first pinfall or submission wins ruleset. The Blackjack Battle Royal is won by throwing people over the top rope, but has everyone present at ringside from the get go.

AEW even made a change to the Casino Gauntlet Match last year to make it even less like WWE's Money in the Bank Match. The winner is no longer able to cash-in their guaranteed world title opportunity at any time they want, and instead is forced to give one week's notice before doing so. MJF did this before capturing the AEW World Championship at Worlds End 2025 in December.

Fans love battle royals and AEW having its own Royal Rumble would be a welcome announcement for many fans. However, it is hard to imagine the company copy and pasting WWE's format for the match exactly. Tony Khan would anticipate that doing so would attract scrutiny, particularly if the name of the match was also very similar.

AEW Community Rankings

For a year's worth of Sundays, the community was asked to vote on their favorite wrestling performances in what has become a significant tradition. It all culminated in awarding the Cageside Cup to Timeless Toni Storm as the community's Performer of the Year. The voting system and methodology have evolved over the years, but the core concept remains unchanged: community members cast ballots weekly to recognize wrestlers who entertained them most.

The voting process is straightforward and accessible to all community members. Anyone who wrestled or appeared on a televised or streamed show during the preceding seven days is eligible for a spot on ballots. The criteria for voting is entirely up to the individual voter. Performers can be selected because they impressed voters, because something happened that changes their standing in their promotion, because they entertained that week, or simply because they are a voter's favorite. Community members are invited to explain their selections, but this is not mandatory. The site positions itself as a fan blog run by fans for fans, without judgment about different tastes and preferences.

The voting mechanics offer flexibility to accommodate different voting styles. Voters have five slots available for their weekly ballots. If voters place one performer in each slot, first place is assigned 5 points for the week, second place receives 4 points, third place gets 3 points, fourth place earns 2 points, and fifth place receives 1 point. Voters can list more than one wrestler per slot as either a tag team or a tie between unrelated performers. When wrestlers are tied in any one slot, those performers split the points assigned to that slot. For example, a ballot placing Kyle Fletcher in first place and a tie between Axiom and Nathan Frazer in second place would give Fletcher 5 points, with Axiom and Frazer each receiving 2 points for splitting the 4 points assigned to second place.

Voters can also strategically assign more points for a tie by having the tie replace one of their other slots. For instance, tying two wrestlers in second place while skipping third place would give each of those wrestlers 3.5 points, as they would split both the 4 points for second place and the 3 points for third place. However, the same wrestler cannot be placed in multiple slots. If a voter attempts this, that wrestler only receives the points for their placement, and the remaining points for that ballot are forfeited. This rule prevents voters from inflating one performer's score across multiple positions.

The annual Rankings are calculated similarly to the weekly voting. Finishing first in the weekly Rankings is worth 10 points in the Cageside Cup Standings, with points descending through tenth place, which is worth 1 point. Ties in the weekly Rankings split the points assigned between the number of people tied. This cumulative system across the entire year determines the overall winner of the Cageside Cup.

The competitive period for the 2025-2026 rankings ran from the Raw after WrestleMania 41 through WrestleMania 42. A total of 165 performers received votes during this year's competition. This represents a notable trend, as the number of performers who placed in a weekly Top Ten and earned a spot on the annual Rankings has decreased for the second straight year after years of increasing numbers. This year's Rankings included 15 fewer names than the previous year's standings.

The Top Ten performers of the year included several notable names from across wrestling's major promotions. Hangman Page of AEW placed sixth, noted as one of only two AEW representatives in the Top Ten despite an AEW wrestler winning the Cup. His booking and Rankings pattern has become established: he gets hot while chasing a title, then cools while holding it. As a two-time Men's World champion, Page's presence in the upper rankings reflects community appreciation for his work throughout the year.

Other top finishers included CM Punk and Seth Rollins, who together have won the Cup four times but couldn't generate momentum in this competitive period. Punk faced questions about whether excitement from his return had finally worn off, whether WWE overexposed him, or some other factor contributed to week-in week-out votes being difficult to secure. This held true despite Punk holding the World title and participating in a WrestleMania main event program. Rollins missed time due to legitimate injuries and worked through various physical limitations, with some community sentiment shifting negatively before he suffered actual injury.

Sami Zayn made the cut at ninth place. The 2022-2023 Cup winner maintained community support through a late Intercontinental title win and a compelling WrestleMania program. His presence in the Top Ten continued the tradition of recognizing performers who deliver across multiple storylines and high-profile matches throughout the year.

Higher-ranked performers showed different patterns. Oba Femi was identified as a later riser who appeared to be making a smart choice as a Cup winner candidate, though the community notes the risk of being fickle in future voting. Stephanie Vaquer also placed highly in the rankings, demonstrating strong community appreciation for her performances throughout the competitive period.

The editor made interesting observations about random ties that occasionally appear in the rankings. One tie that stood out was Randy Orton and Thekla at 39th place, which the editor found amusing as a potential Mixed Match Challenge pairing. While most ties were somewhat expected deeper in the rankings, this particular combination generated personal interest.

An interesting pattern emerged with several performers who had strong early runs that kept them in the Top Ten for much of the year but whose pushes faded, resulting in lower voting later in the competitive period. This phenomenon was likened to what happened with Damien Sandow, now known as Aron Stevens, during his time as The Miz's double. However, this year featured many candidates for this pattern. R-Truth and Ron Killings earned the distinction most notably, shooting to the top early around his re-signing and work with Heel Cena, only to end up outside the Top 20 by the year's end.

Naomi placed 11th despite not missing the Top Ten by much. She was highlighted as someone who didn't participate in the full year due to maternity leave. Similarly, Dominik Mysterio, the new AAA Mega champion, was noted as someone who might have placed significantly higher had he been around for the entire competitive period. The editor questioned how much higher both wrestlers would have ranked if they had been available all year.

The methodology and transparency of the voting system has been refined over the competition's 15-year history. The system was originally called the Smark's Shoot Standings (S3) for years before being renamed to its current designation. A few years back, the name was changed, though whatever the designation, the core purpose remains consistent: providing a way for community members to vote on which wrestlers entertained them most each week.

The editor invited community members to provide analysis of the full data set, offering to share the complete spreadsheet used to track performances week-to-week. The editor acknowledged that while they previously created fancy graphs in the early days of the competition, time constraints had prevented this in recent years. This invitation suggested the community values detailed examination of the data and patterns that emerge throughout the competitive year.

The Cageside Cup represents a significant achievement for winning performers. Previous winners are recognized and celebrated, with Toni Storm now joining a distinguished list of honorees. The fact that an AEW performer claimed the Cup for the third time in the competition's history underscores that while AEW may have fewer representatives in the Top Ten overall, the promotion continues to produce performers who resonate strongly with this particular community of wrestling fans. The 15th annual competition has concluded, and the community looks forward to the next year's voting cycle, beginning again after WrestleMania 42.

AEW Dynamite Viewership

AEW Dynamite viewership and key demo numbers are in for the April 22, 2026 episode of AEW Dynamite on TBS, as the show fell week over week. According to Programming Insider, AEW Dynamite on April 22, 2026 drew 617,000 viewers on TBS, with a 0.1 rating in the key P18-49 demographic.

For comparison, the April 15, 2026 episode drew 710,000 viewers with a 0.12 rating in the key P18-49 demographic, representing a decrease of 93,000 viewers and down by 0.02 in the demo. The week-to-week drop marks a notable setback for the program heading into the latter stages of spring.

Looking at year-over-year performance, AEW Dynamite drew 521,000 viewers and a 0.14 demo rating on April 23, 2025, versus 617,000 viewers and a 0.1 demo rating on April 22, 2026. This represents a year-over-year change of 18.4% growth in viewership and a 28.6% decline in the demographic rating. The mixed results suggest increased overall audience size but a shift in viewership composition.

The 4-week average stands at 677,750 viewers. The highest viewership of 2026 came on March 25, 2026 with 765,000 viewers, while the lowest viewership of 2026 occurred on January 21, 2026 with 498,000 viewers. These figures reflect only linear TV viewership on TBS and do not include streaming or digital viewership. Continued monitoring of AEW Dynamite will take place as the company builds toward its next major live event.

AEW's Jeff Jarrett Discusses Vince McMahon Joining Him In WWE HOF

For the second straight year, Vince McMahon's presence loomed large over the WWE Hall of Fame ceremony, with fans chanting "Thank you Vince!" after he was mentioned by his daughter, Stephanie McMahon, during her Hall of Fame speech. That reaction has prompted plenty of discussion, including whether or not McMahon should be inducted into the Hall of Fame himself, or how that could even be possible as McMahon continues to face accusations of sex trafficking and abuse from Janel Grant in her ongoing lawsuit.

As it turns out, at least one person was having that discussion before fans chanted for McMahon at the Hall of Fame ceremony. On last week's episode of My World with Jeff Jarrett, Conrad Thompson asked AEW's Jeff Jarrett, himself a WWE Hall of Famer, about the possibility of McMahon going into the Hall of Fame, perhaps at next year's WrestleMania in Saudi Arabia. Jarrett instead proposed it happen now.

"In my opinion, the sooner the better," Jarrett said. "Like, I get political, I get court cases, I get all of that. But the sooner the better. Life is short. I'd like to see it this year, I understand why it's not. Next year, if that's what it is...look, you know, Vince doesn't need the Hall of Fame to validate his career. But I think it would be fantastic.

"Again...at the end of the day, your audience is all that matters...That's what it's all about. If you deliver to your audience, then I do believe Vince has done so much for this industry, that even his worst critics and haters would say 'Okay, I still hate the son of a b***h. But yeah, he's a Hall of Famer.'"
