🔥 Ronda Rousey AEW involvement and Tony Khan's invitation

Ronda Rousey appears content keeping professional wrestling in the rearview mirror for now. While the former WWE and UFC star recently made a surprise appearance for AEW at Revolution in Los Angeles earlier this year, it does not sound like fans should expect her to become a regular part of the company anytime soon.

AEW President Tony Khan previously stated during a media call that the door remains open for Rousey to return whenever she wants. However, during a recent appearance on the Up & Adams Show with Kay Adams, Rousey made it clear that she currently has no plans to jump back into the wrestling world full-time. When asked directly about potentially returning to AEW, Rousey pointed to the demands of life on the road as the main reason she is not interested in resuming a wrestling schedule.

"No, that life of being on the road is probably more time-consuming than doing training camp at home," Rousey said. Kay Adams noted that Rousey appeared to have fun during her appearance at AEW Revolution. The former WWE star agreed but explained the circumstances that made that appearance different from a long-term commitment. "I had a great time, but it just happened to be in town, and my best friend was there. You know what I mean?" Rousey added.

Rousey made a surprise appearance at AEW Revolution in Los Angeles in March, helping her longtime friend and training partner Marina Shafir after Shafir's match with Toni Storm. The appearance quickly generated speculation about a possible future with the promotion, especially after Tony Khan later stated that she would be welcomed back at any time. She did not do anything physical in the ring during the appearance, given the risk that would have posed to her MMA return in May against Gina Carano.

Rousey previously explained on her vlog that the AEW Revolution appearance came together because she wanted to support Shafir during her first pay-per-view match and also tie it into her own upcoming fight. She asked Marina if she could do a run-in to help her out while promoting her own fight. Marina told her she would be at Staples Center doing her first-ever pay-per-view match, which made the situation work perfectly.

The former WWE Superstar admitted the cameo had a rebellious edge because of the TKO connection, but she felt it was easier to ask for forgiveness than permission. "It's kind of a cool little bit of a f*** you to the TKO Group, which is kind of funny because WWE is on Netflix. But I kind of figured it'd be easier to ask for forgiveness instead of permission on this one. Like, I'm promoting your show. It's fine. We didn't advertise it. It's not like we boosted the ratings, so it should be fine."

Rousey's latest comments make it clear that her AEW appearance was about convenience, friendship, and timing, not the start of a full-time wrestling comeback. Tony Khan may be open to using her again, but Rousey does not sound interested in signing up for the road life that comes with professional wrestling. For now, AEW fans should not expect Ronda Rousey to become a regular presence. If she does show up again, it sounds like it would have to fit her schedule and personal life first, making another appearance unlikely in the near future.

🔥 Jim Ross AEW contract expiration and health issues

Jim Ross Reveals AEW Contract Set To Expire This Summer With Health Concerns

WWE Hall of Famer Jim Ross has revealed that his current contract with All Elite Wrestling is expected to expire in August, and he remains uncertain whether the company plans to offer him a new deal. Speaking on his Grilling JR podcast, Ross discussed his current role in AEW and whether he would like to be more involved behind the scenes. The Hall of Fame broadcaster indicated that he is content with his current position but would be willing to take on additional responsibilities if asked.

"I'm pretty content, Conrad. To be honest with you, would I accept more responsibility and do more work? Of course, if asked to perform in that world, I'm happy to do it," Ross stated during the episode. He also praised AEW President Tony Khan for the way he has treated him throughout his tenure with the company, saying "Tony Khan's been really good to me."

The conversation then turned to Ross' contract status, where he acknowledged that his future remains undecided. "But I don't have a lot more time left on my contract," he noted. Ross revealed he recently discussed the situation with his longtime agent Barry Bloom, but is taking a relaxed approach to the negotiations. "I talked to Barry Bloom, my agent, he was in New York, and we chatted about my status, and I'm just gonna let it play out," Ross explained.

The legendary announcer admitted he does not know what Khan's plans are. "I don't know when Tony Khan wants to keep me, well, if he doesn't, then he won't." Ross reflected on his remarkable longevity in the wrestling business and suggested he is no longer stressing about contract situations. "God damn, Conrad, I've been doing this 50 years, long time, and I enjoyed virtually every minute of it. I'll keep everybody posted whenever my deal is up, I think sometime in August, I believe that's true. That's how focused I am on my contract. I just passed the worrying about it stage."

Despite the uncertainty surrounding his future, Ross spoke positively about AEW's current structure and leadership. "I hope I stay with them, and I enjoy the hell out of Tony Khan," he said. He also praised the company's current support staff, calling it one of the strongest teams assembled during his time with AEW. "I believe that presently AEW has their strongest support staff assembled as we've ever had."

Ross further revealed that he is putting future book-writing projects on hold while he waits to see what the next chapter of his career looks like. "I'm gonna take a little break in it. After this summer, I'm not sure what my future holds, you know, with AEW," he stated. Ross has been with AEW since the promotion launched in 2019 and remains one of the most recognizable voices in wrestling history.

Health Challenges Continue for the 74-Year-Old Broadcaster

The contract uncertainty comes amid serious health challenges for the 74-year-old cancer survivor. Ross recently disclosed that he suffered a fall before AEW Double or Nothing, which took place on May 26. His appearance at the event was notably affected by his health issues, and he later recounted a difficult travel experience on his podcast.

"It was not a good travel weekend for me. I was supposed to get picked up Saturday morning at like 6 a.m. I'll have a chat with my travel guy regarding that matter. Why would you fly me out at 6 in the morning for a Sunday night show? It makes no sense. You know, I'm not 17 anymore," Ross explained. He questioned the logistics of being booked on such an early flight for a Sunday evening event, noting that the schedule was particularly demanding given his age.

The trouble started before he even left home in Oklahoma. According to Ross, he unexpectedly fell before leaving for the airport and was unable to immediately get help because his phone was not nearby. The incident ultimately caused him to miss his scheduled flight and forced him to rebook later in the day. "So, anyway, we had the alarm set. My driver was going to pick me up, and all of a sudden, I fell. I hadn't had a fall in weeks. So I fell and busted my ass away from my phone. I don't know how you do it. There's got to be a way to, I guess, just put it in my pocket. Hell, I don't know. If I had my phone with me, I could have got a little bit of help. As a result of that, I missed my flight," he recounted.

Ross also vented about the current state of air travel, calling it the most miserable he has ever experienced in his long, travel-filled career. After eventually arriving in New York on Saturday, he spent some time socializing with Excalibur before returning to his hotel room, where another significant issue surfaced. His back reportedly gave out while he was trying to rest. "I got in my room on Saturday night, I think it was. I laid on the bed, put my ass on the edge of the bed, and just relaxed. Well, little did I know, my back was going to go out on me. So now I'm in the hotel with my back out and I can't function. So it was just a horrible experience," Ross described.

On Monday, Ross returned home and provided another update on how he was feeling physically following the difficult weekend. While he described himself as extremely sore, he also revealed he is currently dealing with weakness in his legs and is looking for medical help moving forward. "Sore. It's like taking a big hit in the football game. I'm just sore as hell. But, you know, I asked to go to work. I can't blame anybody but myself. I've got to find a doctor. I got a neurologist now, he's talking to me about risk area for dementia and all that bullshit," Ross said.

Despite his challenges, Ross expressed his determination to continue working. "I have the worst luck sometimes. Golly. But just real sore. I got to find the right doctor that can get me back rolling, because my legs are really weak. And on Saturday morning, one of my legs just went out from under me. It went like a piece of spaghetti. So, anyhow, flying back — I could have stayed home, maybe I should have. But I like working, and I love pro wrestling," he stated.

The combination of physical ailments and ongoing neurological testing for dementia concerns has understandably affected Ross, though he appears determined not to let these issues completely derail his career. He is currently seeking proper medical attention to address his leg weakness and neurological health.

Ross Weighs In On McMahon's Legal Troubles

Beyond his own situation, Ross also recently commented on the significant legal challenges facing Vince McMahon. During the latest episode of Grilling JR, Conrad Thompson brought up the Delaware Chancery Court ruling that sanctioned McMahon and WWE leadership over deleted Signal messages tied to the WWE-UFC merger process.

The lawsuit centers on whether WWE shareholders were denied the best possible deal because McMahon allegedly pushed the company toward Endeavor in order to protect his own power. After Thompson laid out the situation, Ross did not sugarcoat how serious he believes the case could become.

"Well, as the old cliche goes, where there's smoke, there's fire. And there's fire here. I think that's a very unique case. It's going to expose a lot of conversations and things of that nature. I think it'll be very wordy. It will be very hostile, I think, because you are talking about, if I'm thinking the right way, a ton of money. A ton of money," Ross stated.

Ross also made it clear that while he does not personally know Nick Khan, he believes Vince McMahon is in a very difficult position. "I feel bad for, you know, I don't know Nick, I've never met Nick Khan. I don't know what kind of guy he is. He's done well for them. But Vince is — he's got a noose around his neck, it would seem to be so. And it won't be a case that's over quickly. I don't think. Too much verbiage, too much 'what you mean by this' and whatever. It's taking people that have major responsibilities in WWE away from their designated task. And right now is not the time to have your eye off the ball," he explained.

Vice Chancellor J. Travis Laster ruled that certain damaging facts will now be presumed true against McMahon and Khan unless they can overcome a higher legal standard at trial. The court found that key Signal messages were deleted after WWE executives had already been instructed to preserve communications. The trial is scheduled to begin June 8 in Delaware Court of Chancery, and Ross believes the case could drag WWE leadership into a messy, expensive fight at a time when the company needs its top executives focused on business. McMahon and the other defendants have denied the allegations.

Whether Ross remains with AEW beyond August is likely to become one of the most closely watched contract situations in wrestling over the coming months, particularly given his iconic status in the industry and his ongoing health concerns.

🔥 Hazuki AEW Collision debut

Hazuki, the STARDOM performer, is officially headed to AEW television this weekend. Tony Khan announced on social media that Hazuki will make her AEW debut during Saturday's episode of Collision when she takes on rising talent Maya World ahead of the 2026 Owen Hart Foundation Tournament quarterfinals. The announcement came on May 28, 2026, confirming the match would take place live nationwide at 8 p.m. ET/5 p.m. PT on TNT and HBO Max.

The match features an added layer of intrigue with Persephone scheduled to sit in on commentary to scout her first-round Owen Hart Cup opponent before their upcoming quarterfinal clash. Khan's announcement emphasized this dynamic: "With Persephone on commentary before their upcoming Owen Hart Tournament quarterfinal fight, HAZUKI will make her AEW debut when she collides vs rising star Maya World THIS SATURDAY!"

However, the announcement immediately created a notable scheduling conflict. Joshi content creator Scott E. Wrestling pointed out on social media that Hazuki was already booked to compete at STARDOM's events in Kobe and Kyoto on May 30 and May 31 respectively. Most significantly, she was scheduled to participate in the main event of the Kyoto show on May 31 in an eight-woman tag team match. This put AEW's May 30 debut directly opposite STARDOM's May 30 Kobe event, creating an impossible scheduling situation for Hazuki.

Following the public discovery of the conflict, STARDOM acted quickly to resolve the issue. The company announced that Hazuki would be removed from the May 30 event in Kobe, allowing her to make her AEW debut as scheduled. Her main event match in Kyoto on May 31 remains set to take place, providing a workable solution that allows Hazuki to fulfill both commitments.

The Owen Hart Foundation Tournament, which serves as the context for Hazuki's debut, officially kicked off at AEW Double or Nothing. The tournament has already seen significant progress, with Athena advancing to the semifinals after defeating Mina Shirakawa in the opening round. Several first-round matches remain to be completed, including Hazuki versus Persephone, Skye Blue versus Sareee, and Alex Windsor facing a mystery wildcard entrant.

The tournament has already dealt with injuries affecting its composition. Willow Nightingale was forced to withdraw from the tournament due to an injury, which also led to her vacating her AEW TBS Championship. Additionally, Sareee is not yet medically cleared to compete due to neck problems, though her match against Skye Blue is still scheduled. The mystery wildcard entrant facing Alex Windsor will be revealed on the June 3 episode of AEW Dynamite.

The tournament finals are scheduled to take place next month at the AEW x NJPW Forbidden Door pay-per-view event. This gives Hazuki a clear path through the bracket if she can defeat both Maya World this Saturday and Persephone in her quarterfinal match.

Saturday's Collision episode will mark AEW's return to its normal standalone format after a special combined three-hour Dynamite and Collision broadcast from Philadelphia earlier in the week. The show takes place in Huntsville, Alabama, where AEW is also scheduled to honor late Midnight Express legend Dennis Condrey during the broadcast, adding additional significance to the weekend's programming.

🔥 Cody Rhodes reflects on 2016 WWE exit and Hollywood aspirations

The wrestling world mobilized on social media to support him, and when the list of wrestlers he would face emerged, fans didn't just show their support online—they backed it with their wallets, attending the small independent shows Rhodes worked.

Rhodes characterized this period as "a very big bluff" and "a big card game." The braggadocio, the suits and ties, the confidence that defined his character—all of it began as performance and projection. However, the narrative shifted fundamentally when the fans actually stood behind him. "The suit and ties, the braggadocio, the confidence, all of it ceased being a bluff the moment the fans actually stood behind me," Rhodes explained. "I'm very much propped up by that fandom, and I've continued to be through every endeavor I took."

What made the difference, according to Rhodes, was surrounding himself with people who would support his vision. The subsequent years saw him reinvent himself across indie promotions, Impact Wrestling, and New Japan Pro Wrestling. These experiences culminated in the inaugural All In event with The Young Bucks, Kenny Omega, and Adam Page, which eventually led to the formation of All Elite Wrestling. That one decision in 2016 fundamentally altered the trajectory of professional wrestling.

Now, as the current Undisputed WWE Champion, Rhodes finds himself in a vastly different position from that uncertain period a decade ago. Yet his ambitions extend beyond the wrestling ring. Rhodes has already dipped his toes into acting, having studied at the Howard Fine Acting School for a year and appeared in projects including Arrow and The Naked Gun.
