Disney has officially released a trailer for its highly anticipated live-action adaptation of "Moana," featuring Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson reprising one of his most beloved and recognizable acting roles. Johnson is stepping back into a character he has already made iconic through his voice work in the animated films. He originally voiced Maui in both "Moana" and "Moana 2," establishing the role as one of the most memorable performances of his acting career. Now, fans finally have a glimpse of what the character looks like in live-action form.

The live-action "Moana" film is currently scheduled to hit theaters on July 10. The trailer, which Johnson shared on social media, includes the tagline "The ocean chose her for a reason" along with a wave emoji and a hook emoji, referencing the character's iconic weapon. The post was captioned "Experience @Disney's MOANA only in theaters JULY 10."

The project represents a significant moment for Johnson as he transitions from voice acting to physically embodying one of Disney's most popular characters. The live-action remake joins Disney's growing slate of live-action adaptations of their animated classics. As fans await the film's summer release, the trailer has already begun generating excitement for seeing how Johnson's charismatic presence translates to the live-action format.

According to White, Jones was not part of the plans for the event scheduled at the White House in June. When asked about any involvement between the fighter and White House plans, White made his position unmistakably clear, stating "No way in hell."

These announcements reflect White's continued management of the UFC's direction and his willingness to publicly clarify the organization's positions on potential partnerships, fighter negotiations, and high-profile events.
