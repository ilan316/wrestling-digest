Brock Lesnar vs Oba Femi WrestleMania 42 Match

# Brock Lesnar Faces Oba Femi at WrestleMania 42 Amid Conflicting Predictions and Career Reflections

Brock Lesnar is preparing for what could be one of the final matches of his WWE career as he squares off against rising star Oba Femi at WrestleMania 42. The matchup has generated significant debate about whether the veteran will maintain his dominance or if the former NXT Champion will achieve a career-defining victory.

## The Build-Up on Raw

The confrontation between Lesnar and Femi has escalated over consecutive weeks on WWE Raw. During the March 16 and March 23 episodes, Femi demonstrated his physical superiority, first dropping Lesnar with a Fall From Grace powerbomb and later clotheslining him over the top rope. These dominant displays have positioned Femi as a legitimate threat heading into WrestleMania, despite Lesnar's legendary track record at the showcase of the immortals.

## Heyman's Confidence in The Beast

Paul Heyman remains undeterred by Femi's recent successes, citing video game simulations as evidence of Lesnar's inevitable victory. According to Heyman, he has tested the matchup "every single time" in WWE 2K26, with Lesnar emerging victorious in each scenario. Heyman acknowledged that Femi's initial attack on Raw caught them off guard, attributing the success to the element of surprise.

"When you have the element of surprise, you have the advantage," Heyman explained. "But when you are a marquee match in what I consider to be the main event of WrestleMania, because anytime Brock Lesnar appears at WrestleMania, that's the main event. When you're going to step into that spotlight, Brock Lesnar knows you're coming."

Heyman emphasized that Lesnar will enter the match with a game plan and undivided focus, suggesting that the advertised nature of their WrestleMania bout will prove decisive.

## Hall of Famer Predicts Upset

Not everyone shares Heyman's conviction. Rikishi, speaking on his "Off the Top" podcast, believes WWE should capitalize on Femi's momentum and push him to victory. The Hall of Famer pointed to the crowd's enthusiastic reception of Femi, drawing comparisons to legendary pushes of the past.

"When he comes out, the wrestling fans, if they love you, they're going to let you know," Rikishi said. "The last time I heard people yell 'Oba! Oba!', it almost kind of sounded like 'Goldberg! Goldberg!'"

Rikishi argued that having Femi defeat Lesnar would be the optimal booking decision to establish the young star at the main roster level. "WWE, don't kill this man right off the bat. Let him get his run. Let him get his opportunity," he stated.

Matt Hardy echoed similar sentiments, calling the match a "career-defining moment" for Femi. Hardy expressed confidence that WWE would "do the right thing" by having Femi emerge victorious, praising how the company has presented the former NXT Champion since his main roster debut.

## Lesnar's Temporary Return

Recent interviews have shed light on Lesnar's current mindset regarding his WWE career. Speaking on the Spittin' Chiclets podcast, Lesnar revealed that his 2025 return to WWE was primarily motivated by financial necessity rather than nostalgia or legacy concerns.

"I went back to work so I could feed my kids. You guys should see my grocery bill," Lesnar stated. "I had left the company years ago, and I'm grateful that I'm back for a short time here so, I'm really grateful for all the opportunities that I've had in my life."

This comment about his return being "for a short time" aligns with rumors that Lesnar could retire in 2026, with some speculation pointing to SummerSlam in Minneapolis as a potential final match. John Cena previously hinted at this possibility, suggesting that Lesnar could "ride into the sunset" in Minnesota, where he built his name as a collegiate wrestler at the University of Minnesota.

## Legacy Beyond Championships

In the same interview, Lesnar offered reflections on what truly matters to him at this stage of his life. Despite his unprecedented accomplishments across WWE, UFC, and amateur wrestling, Lesnar minimized the significance of his titles and accolades.

"For me, none of my championships or none of my accolades, they all mean nothing. It means nothing to me," Lesnar said. "My kids are my legacy. I mean, in today's dark-ass world that we're living in, if you can come out and have good kids — I take a lot of pride in that. When parents come to me and say, 'Man, your boys are really respectful' — that pumps my tires, you know?"

Lesnar further elaborated on the separation between his public persona and his private self, noting that people see "the entertainment Brock Lesnar" but not who he actually is. He acknowledged maintaining a mystique throughout his career while remaining honest about his personality.

## Reflections on the Undertaker's Streak

During his podcast appearance, Lesnar also discussed one of the most controversial moments of his career—ending The Undertaker's undefeated WrestleMania streak at WrestleMania 30 in 2014. Lesnar emphasized that the decision was not his to make, with Vince McMahon making the final call.

"For me, guys, it's really simple. Like, this is a business," Lesnar explained. "At the time, Vince McMahon, if there was a person that could take the streak away with a legitimate background and people could believe it, I guess it was me. There's a lot of people that are pissed that it's me, but it's not my call."

Lesnar revealed mixed emotions about the outcome, stating he was "excited" but also "felt bad" given that The Undertaker was his mentor. He noted the particularly difficult circumstances, as The Undertaker was told of the change just two hours before their match. Out of respect, Lesnar visited The Undertaker in the hospital following the bout, which had resulted in a concussion.

## The Stakes at WrestleMania 42

The clash between Lesnar and Femi carries considerable weight for both competitors. For Femi, a victory would represent a massive breakthrough moment early in his main roster career and could establish him as a major player on WWE's roster. For Lesnar, it would mark another successful WrestleMania appearance as he winds down what appears to be a final run with the company.

As WrestleMania 42 approaches in Las Vegas, the wrestling world awaits what could be a career-defining match for one of WWE's most promising young talents against one of its greatest attractions.

Sid Vicious WWE Hall of Fame 2026 Induction

# Sid Eudy Posthumously Inducted Into 2026 WWE Hall of Fame as Legacy Honoree

The late Sid Eudy, known throughout his legendary career as Sid Justice, Sid Vicious, and Sycho Sid, will be posthumously inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame as part of the 2026 Legacy Class. The announcement was made by WWE Chief Content Officer Triple H via social media on March 24, 2026.

"No matter which moniker you knew him by, Sid's intensity was palpable across the ring and through your TV screen," Triple H wrote in his announcement. "A multi-time champion in WWE and WCW, and a two-time WrestleMania main event, it's a pleasure to announce that he will take his rightful place in the 2026 Legacy Class of the WWEHOF."

Eudy passed away in August 2024 at the age of 63 following a private battle with cancer. Prior to his death, the wrestling legend expressed optimism about the possibility of a Hall of Fame induction, believing that Vince McMahon's departure from WWE leadership would make such an honor more likely given their contentious history.

## A Career Defined by Presence and Championship Success

Sid's remarkable career spanned multiple decades and major wrestling promotions. He was a two-time WWE Champion and a two-time WCW World Heavyweight Champion, establishing himself as one of the most imposing and visually captivating competitors of his generation.

His WWE tenure included two main event appearances at WrestleMania. In 1992, Eudy headlined WrestleMania VIII in a match against Hulk Hogan. Five years later, he returned to main event WrestleMania 13 against The Undertaker, cementing his status as a main event performer during WWE's most prominent shows.

During his initial WWE run in the early 1990s, Sid debuted as Sid Justice and made his mark immediately. He served as special guest referee at SummerSlam 1991, where WWE Champion Hulk Hogan and The Ultimate Warrior faced off against The Triangle of Terror in a handicap match. Later that same evening, Sid saved Randy Savage and Miss Elizabeth from an attack by Jake Roberts and The Undertaker at Savage and Elizabeth's wedding reception.

## The Return and Championship Glory

After a hiatus from WWE, Sid returned as "Sycho Sid" and forged an alliance with Shawn Michaels, serving in The Heartbreak Kid's corner at WrestleMania XI when Michaels challenged Diesel for the WWE title. This match sparked an iconic rivalry between Sid and Michaels that would define much of his second WWE run.

The pinnacle came at Survivor Series 1996 at Madison Square Garden, where Sid defeated Michaels to capture his first WWE Championship. The moment proved particularly memorable for the crowd's reaction to his entrance. After losing the title back to Michaels, Sid reclaimed the WWE Championship on the February 17, 1997 edition of Raw by defeating Bret Hart, becoming a two-time champion.

Established as one of the biggest competitors in the world, Sid ventured to WCW in 1999, where he immediately entered the main event scene. He battled wrestling legends including Hulk Hogan, Sting, Goldberg, and Booker T, further enhancing his reputation across the wrestling landscape.

A catastrophic injury in 2001 threatened to end Sid's career prematurely. Despite this setback, he refused to remain sidelined and eventually returned to the independent wrestling circuit. His return match notably featured current WWE Superstar Sami Zayn. Sid continued making occasional appearances throughout the years, including at RAW 1000 in 2012, before retiring in 2017.

## Family Reaction and Legacy

Sid's family responded with gratitude and pride to the Hall of Fame announcement. His eldest son Frank Eudy, a former contestant on the CBS reality series "Big Brother," took to social media to express his emotions about the honor.

"Congratulations to the Master and Ruler of the World! Grateful for the recognition by WWE inducting my dad into the Hall of Fame! He deserves this and I wish he was here to be a part of the celebration! I love and miss you dad! #WWEHOF," Frank posted.

Sid's younger son, Gunnar—who has competed in the ring under the name "Two Psycho"—also celebrated the recognition. "It's official. My father, Sycho Sid Vicious, is going into the WWE Hall of Fame. The Master and the Ruler of the World is finally getting his flowers. Proud to be his son and to carry that legacy forward. The Hall of Fame just got a whole lot more vicious," Gunnar wrote.

Gunnar is planning a memorial show for his father in August 2026, with all proceeds benefiting an animal rescue charity in honor of Sid's well-known love of animals.

## The 2026 Hall of Fame Class and Ceremony Details

Sid joins an impressive class of 2026 inductees that includes Stephanie McMahon, AJ Styles, Demolition (Ax & Smash), and NBA legend Dennis Rodman. The induction ceremony will take place on Friday, April 17, 2026, at Dolby Live at Park MGM in Las Vegas, marking the eve of WrestleMania 42.

The ceremony is scheduled to begin at 9 p.m. Pacific Time / midnight Eastern Time. WWE's official announcement emphasized that Sid's reputation as one of the toughest and most visually captivating Superstars cemented his legacy, with his influence continuing to be seen in wrestling rings across the world despite his passing.

# CM Punk Takes Heat for Controversial RAW Promo Ahead of WrestleMania 42 Main Event Against Roman Reigns

CM Punk's tenure as WWE World Heavyweight Champion continues to generate significant controversy on RAW, particularly following his heated exchanges with Roman Reigns and inflammatory comments that have drawn criticism from both inside and outside the company.

## Punk Embraces Age in Response to Reigns' Insult

During this week's RAW broadcast, Punk responded to Roman Reigns calling him "old" by taking a defiant stance on his age. Rather than dismiss the insult, Punk doubled down, declaring pride in his status as a 47-year-old professional wrestler heading into WrestleMania 42.

"If I told you all here to your face that I wasn't, you would know that I'm a liar," Punk stated in the closing segment. "Am I old? Yeah, yeah, I'm old. Am I ashamed of it? Hell no. Not no. Hell no. In fact, I'm proud of it."

Punk continued by taking a shot at Reigns, suggesting the 40-year-old (two months shy of 41) covers his gray hair with dye. "If I was ashamed of how old I was, well, I'd be covering up these grey whiskers with Just For Men, just like Roman does. I am proud of who I am, my age, what I've done and where I come from."

## NSFW Promo Marks WWE RAW First

The exchange escalated when Punk delivered a scathing promo that included language previously unseen on WWE programming. In a segment that drew audible gasps from the TD Garden crowd in Boston, Punk made crude references to Reigns' physical appearance and conditioning.

"At WrestleMania, 20 minutes deep when you're gasping for air and all that oil and jizz you cover yourself in dries up, just like your chances of ever being Heavyweight Champion are gone," Punk said, later adding, "And I hit you and jack you with a GTS, and you go to sleep and you wake up Monday morning and you realize that I am the best in the world."

The use of explicit language surprised fans and commentators alike, with many noting on social media that this marked a new frontier for WWE on Netflix. Some praised the moment as part of a "golden era," while others criticized it as unnecessarily lowbrow.

## Controversial Comments About Sika Anoa'i Draw Family Ire

What many overlooked amid the profanity was an even more serious transgression: Punk's mention of Roman Reigns' late father, WWE Hall of Famer Sika Anoa'i. The comment did not sit well with the Anoa'i family, particularly Rikishi, who expressed his disappointment on his "Off The Top" podcast.

"You know, I felt that by CM Punk bringing up my uncle's name was just a disrespectful thing for him to do," Rikishi said. "I would have thought CM Punk that you're smart enough to even tap on something like that. If you tried to get up underneath our skin, if you tried to get up underneath the family skin, you got up underneath this one, man, because I had nothing but respect for CM Punk."

Rikishi acknowledged Punk's contributions to professional wrestling and his current accomplishments as champion, but stressed that invoking the memory of a deceased family member crossed a line. He characterized the move as cheap and disrespectful to Sika, described as a paramount chief held in high regard by the family.

"When you mention a name of a family member that is no longer here, a paramount chief in this family who we all respect and love, and then you want to dig that up. What was it for you to get some heat?" Rikishi asked pointedly. "I want to tell you something, CM Punk. If you done turned something over, that is going to be hard for you to come back from, and I'm going to leave that in the hands of Roman Reigns."

Punk has shown no signs of backing down or apologizing for the comments, instead doubling down on his animosity toward Reigns and the Anoa'i family.

## Physical Confrontation Ends RAW

The tension culminated in a physical confrontation that closed Monday's broadcast. After Jey Uso made his way to the ring in response to Punk's taunts, Jimmy Uso attempted to calm his brother down, reminding him of their upcoming WWE Tag Team Title match. However, Roman Reigns' arrival only intensified the situation.

When Punk mouthed off at Jey again, the Uso attacked the champion, and Reigns followed suit with a Superman punch to the back of Punk's head. After dragging the Usos down the ramp, Reigns put Punk through the announce table with a powerbomb and spear combination.

Notably, Punk was shown laughing as the broadcast faded to black, suggesting the champion may view the physical beatdown as part of his broader narrative leading into WrestleMania.

## Seth Rollins Reflects on Punk's Character Evolution

In a separate development, Seth Rollins appeared on an interview where he reflected on his complicated relationship with CM Punk, whom he credits as a major influence on his career path from the independent scene to WWE.

"There's no secret, he was a major influence on my career. First of all, he was a pioneer," Rollins acknowledged. However, he noted that Punk's character changed significantly after leaving WWE in 2014, describing him as increasingly bitter and self-centered.

"The problem with him was the person he became was this — I mean, he's always been a curmudgeon and he'd probably admit that himself — but, he was not helpful when it came to moving the business forward," Rollins stated. "He was so bitter and he was so self-centered, that it was about him and it was about how do I make this right, and he did that at the expense of the industry."

Rollins emphasized that while he respects who Punk was before 2014, reconciliation between the two seems unlikely given their fundamental philosophical differences about the business.

## WrestleMania 42 Main Event Set for Vegas

The WrestleMania 42 main event between Punk and Reigns is scheduled for April 18-19 at Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas, Nevada, with Punk's World Heavyweight Championship on the line. The storyline heading into the bout shows no signs of cooling, with the Anoa'i family now directly invested in Reigns' pursuit of the title.

Braun Strowman Reflects on WWE Creative Process

# Braun Strowman Criticizes WWE Creative Process, Praises New Freedom in Production Venture

Adam Scherr, known to pro wrestling fans as Braun Strowman, has opened up about his experience with WWE's creative process, revealing frustrations he harbored during his 12-year tenure with the company. Speaking to The Hollywood Reporter, the former WWE star reflected on the constraints he faced while working for the promotion and contrasted them with the creative freedom he now enjoys.

Strowman, who was released from WWE in May 2025, didn't mince words when discussing the scripts he was given during his time with the company. "For me, the big eye-opening thing was not having my hands tied," he explained. "They've allowed me to be me, whereas my hands were tied creatively for the last 12 years (in WWE), and while that's not a bad thing — I was very blessed with what I got to do — but at the end of the day, I was playing a character on a show, and sometimes the script sucked."

The 6'8" monster acknowledged his feelings were strong on the matter, adding, "I mean, no offense, I hated it, but I had to go in there and make the best of it because that was my job." Despite his criticisms of the creative side, Strowman made clear that his time in WWE was meaningful to him and that being frustrated with some scripts was part of the overall experience.

## New Opportunities and Creative Freedom

Since departing WWE, Strowman has found outlets where his creative input is valued. He is co-launching a production company called Meat Castle Media with his manager and business partner Nick Antonicelli. The new venture represents a significant shift in how Strowman operates professionally.

"Well, now with this opportunity… my creative input is welcomed, it's not dismissed," he said. Strowman elaborated on his creative process, describing himself as someone who thinks outside the box and operates ahead of the curve. "My brain is crazy. I think outside the box, I'm moving 10 steps ahead of where I'm at in life, always, which is good and bad. But having this team behind me… to help with my vision — my brain's kind of like a disco ball with a light shined on it. There's beams just going everywhere."

He credited his team at Meat Castle Media with helping to channel his expansive creativity while maintaining his creative freedoms. "With this team, they're able to dial me in and steer me in the right direction while still allowing my creative liberties and my freedoms to be," Strowman explained.

## Current Projects

Strowman has already launched a new show on USA Network titled "Everything on the Menu with Braun Strowman," which has been produced through a partnership with WWE. The show has been picked up for a second season, suggesting his post-WWE ventures are gaining traction in the entertainment industry.

The comments from Strowman provide a rare behind-the-scenes perspective on how WWE's creative system operates and suggest that even top-tier talent felt constrained by the company's scripting process during his tenure.

Finn Balor WWE RAW Return and Judgment Day

# Finn Balor Returns to WWE Raw With Vengeance Against Judgment Day

Finn Balor made a dramatic return to WWE Raw on March 23 at the TD Garden in Boston, exacting revenge on his former faction Judgment Day in a chaotic Intercontinental Championship match between Penta and Dominik Mysterio.

## The Return and Interference

Having last competed at Elimination Chamber on February 28, Balor had been absent from in-ring competition since his removal from Judgment Day earlier this month following escalating tensions with Dominik Mysterio that erupted on the March 9 episode. His return came at the perfect moment, as Mysterio was attempting to reclaim the Intercontinental Championship from defending champion Penta.

The match took an unexpected turn when Balor's entrance music hit just as Mysterio appeared poised to gain momentum. JD McDonagh rushed to the entrance ramp to defend his Judgment Day brethren, but Balor intercepted him and sent him over a roadcase. The distraction proved costly for Mysterio, allowing Penta to secure a rollup victory and retain his title.

## Post-Match Destruction

The match was only the beginning for Balor, who entered the ring immediately after to launch a physical assault on Mysterio. He connected with a slingblade and dropkick before attempting to hit his finishing Coup de Gras. McDonagh pulled Mysterio out of the ring to safety, but Balor pursued both men, landing a crossbody on them before Mysterio escaped into the crowd.

McDonagh bore the brunt of Balor's fury, taking a dropkick and a Coup de Gras that sent a clear message. Balor stood on the ropes, staring down the fleeing Mysterio as the segment concluded.

## Breaking His Silence

Following the chaotic events, Balor addressed his actions on social media, sharing images of him hitting McDonagh with the Coup de Gras and captioning them simply: "One Down." This cryptic message suggested his vendetta against Judgment Day was far from over.

The post came shortly after Balor had previously tweeted "1,372"—the number of days he had been a member of Judgment Day before his ejection. His departure marked another significant shift in the faction's composition, which has undergone dramatic changes in recent months.

## Judgment Day's Changing Landscape

Judgment Day has become a revolving door of membership in recent times. Balor was instrumental in the removal of Damian Priest from the faction at SummerSlam in 2024. Mysterio himself had previously betrayed Rhea Ripley at the same event, choosing instead to side with Liv Morgan. The current serving members include Mysterio, Morgan, and McDonagh, though Morgan has publicly called Balor "dead weight" in recent interviews.

The faction's struggles extend beyond internal conflict. Currently, Judgment Day holds no WWE championships. Mysterio recently lost the Intercontinental Championship following being "cursed" by Danhausen, and the group's prospects appear uncertain heading into their fourth anniversary as a stable next month and their third major iteration since forming in 2022.

## WrestleMania 42 Implications

Liv Morgan will attempt to restore championship prestige to Judgment Day when she challenges Stephanie Vaquer for the WWE Women's World Championship at WrestleMania 42. Morgan has been vocal about her preparation for the match, making disparaging comments about her opponent's longevity in WWE.

Vaquer has already made her presence felt in recent weeks, attacking Morgan brutally on the March 23 Raw before the Intercontinental Championship match. The assault came as Morgan was making her way to ringside to support Mysterio, leaving her laid out in the Gorilla Position.

## Looking Ahead

Whether Judgment Day can maintain its cohesion through its fifth anniversary at WrestleMania 43 in Saudi Arabia—though the date has yet to be officially confirmed—remains uncertain. Balor's return has clearly destabilized the faction, and with his focus now turned toward revenge against his former allies, the internal dynamics of Judgment Day will likely continue to deteriorate in the coming weeks.

Paul Heyman on Zilla Fatu WWE Signing

# Paul Heyman Endorses Zilla Fatu For WWE Signing, Says Company 'Would Be Remiss' To Pass On Rising Star

Paul Heyman has given a resounding endorsement to independent wrestling prospect Zilla Fatu, suggesting that WWE should sign the rising star at the earliest possible opportunity. During a recent media appearance promoting WWE 2K26, the veteran wrestling personality made his thoughts on Fatu abundantly clear.

When asked by interviewer MuscleManMalcolm about Zilla Fatu and the possibility of him joining WWE, Heyman didn't mince words. "I think WWE would be remiss if WWE doesn't sign Zilla Fatu at the very first opportunity to do so," he stated. Coming from someone of Heyman's stature and influence within the wrestling industry, the endorsement carries significant weight.

## Who Is Zilla Fatu?

Zilla Fatu is the son of the late Edward Fatu, better known to wrestling fans as Umaga, a former WWE Champion and multiple-time Intercontinental Champion. The young wrestler is first cousins with notable WWE talents The Usos, Solo Sikoa, and Jacob Fatu, making him part of the prominent Anoa'i/Fatu wrestling dynasty that has long been represented in WWE.

At 26 years old, Zilla Fatu has been actively wrestling for approximately two years, having made his in-ring debut in July 2023. He has trained under the tutelage of Booker T at Reality of Wrestling and has competed in several independent promotions, including House of Glory, GCW, and recently appeared on TNA Wrestling's 2025 Slammiversary pre-show.

## Building Momentum In Independent Scene

The young wrestler has already generated considerable interest from WWE fans. During an appearance at the January 6 New Year's Evil NXT special, the crowd chanted for Zilla Fatu during a segment involving Ricky Saints, an experience that Fatu himself has described as humbling and special.

Heyman's previous connections to members of the Fatu and Anoa'i families make his endorsement particularly notable. The veteran has extensive history working with this wrestling bloodline and currently serves as the "Oracle" or wiseman for The Vision faction on WWE Raw, where he manages and advises multiple talents including Logan Paul, Bron Breakker, Austin Theory, and Bronson Reed.

## Strategic Fit For WWE

Given Zilla Fatu's family connections and lineage, he has multiple avenues for alignment within WWE's current roster. The presence of his cousins The Usos and Solo Sikoa—both significant players in the company—could facilitate a natural integration into existing storylines and factions.

Following Heyman's endorsement, speculation about a potential pairing between the wiseman and the up-and-comer has emerged, with observers noting that Heyman's track record of developing younger talent could benefit Fatu's WWE journey significantly. The combination of family legacy, independent wrestling experience, and now high-profile endorsement suggests that a WWE signing could be on the horizon for the rising star.

Celebrity Training with WWE Stars

# WWE Star Natalya Extends Training Invitation to Music Artist Yungblud

WWE star Natalya has revealed an exciting development in the crossover between entertainment and professional wrestling. The veteran performer has invited British alternative rock star Yungblud to train at The Dungeon, her private wrestling facility. Sharing a photo of the pair in an aggressive choke hold on social media, Natalya announced the invitation with confidence: "I invited @yungblud to wrestle in The Dungeon. He accepted… I think he'd fit in perfectly."

Yungblud, born in Doncaster, England, in 1997, is a Grammy Award-winning artist who released his fourth studio album, Idols, in June of last year. His involvement with WWE has already begun, as he provided the theme for WrestleMania 42 alongside legendary rock band Aerosmith, creating a collaborative remix of their track "Back in the Saddle."

## The Hart Dungeon Legacy

Natalya runs The Dungeon with her husband, TJ Wilson, at a private, invite-only training facility in Tampa, Florida. The modern facility is inspired by the original Hart Dungeon in Calgary, where the legendary Hart family trained for years and produced some of the toughest wrestlers in history. The contemporary version focuses on high-level training including in-ring work, conditioning, and rehabilitation for injured wrestlers making comebacks.

The facility has earned a strong reputation in the wrestling industry. Many notable WWE stars have trained there, including Liv Morgan, Maxxine Dupri, Apollo Crews, Ilja Dragunov, and Nikki Bella. Natalya has been instrumental in developing younger talent like Maxxine Dupri through the facility, establishing it as one of the best training environments available today.

## Yungblud's Path Forward

While details of when the training session will take place have not been revealed, the possibility of Yungblud appearing on WWE television looms large. With WrestleMania 42 scheduled for April 18 & 19 in Las Vegas, the timing could align with his potential involvement in the company's programming.

A tangential connection to WWE already exists through Yungblud's work. The official music video for "Zombie," a track on the Idols album, featured actress Florence Pugh, who previously played Paige in the movie Fighting With My Family, a production that featured Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson in a cameo role.

## Celebrity Crossovers in WWE

Yungblud's training invitation continues a broader trend of celebrity involvement in professional wrestling. Recent examples include musicians Jelly Roll and Bad Bunny, both of whom have crossed over into WWE. Jelly Roll, who is currently mixing it up on WWE SmackDown with Kit Wilson and The Miz, has already demonstrated his commitment to the craft.

WWE star Jacob Fatu recently praised Jelly Roll's work ethic during a training session at the WWE Performance Center. Fatu recalled witnessing Jelly Roll insist on cleaning the ring after training, refusing to let others do the work. "He's not making a scene about it," Fatu explained. "Man, bro cleaned the whole ring, did what he had to do, wiped his feet—but also, you could see the love and the passion."

## Natalya's Expanding Role

Beyond her work at The Dungeon, Natalya is expanding her influence in the wrestling world. She will be joining Kevin Owens as a new coach for WWE LFG, the A&E series, in its third season, alongside Booker T and Bubba Ray Dudley. This appointment reflects her status as a veteran who forged her "Nattie" personality through matches on the independent scene before bringing her talents to WWE television.

While a training session at The Dungeon doesn't necessarily guarantee a move into professional wrestling, Yungblud's acceptance of Natalya's invitation suggests genuine interest in the sport. Given his WrestleMania 42 involvement and Natalya's endorsement, fans should watch for potential WWE television appearances in the coming weeks.

# WWE's WrestleMania 42 Faces Ticket Sales Challenges as Company Doubles Down on Premium Experiences

With WrestleMania 42 set to take place on April 18 and 19 at Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas, WWE is pursuing an aggressive multi-pronged strategy to boost attendance and revenue amid reports of struggling ticket sales. The company has implemented price adjustments, discount codes, and increasingly expensive VIP experiences in an effort to fill the venue for its flagship annual event.

## Price Reductions and Discount Codes

WWE has made notable adjustments to its ticket pricing structure in recent weeks. Entry-level tickets for night two have dropped from $254 to $177, while night one tickets fell from $228 to the same $177 starting price. These reductions signal WWE's recognition of sales challenges as the event approaches.

The company is also running a limited-time promotion offering 25 percent off tickets using the code 25DAYS. This discount applies not only to WrestleMania 42 but also extends to the accompanying SmackDown broadcast, the 2026 WWE Hall of Fame ceremony, and the Raw that follows. With 25 days remaining until the two-night spectacle, this represents the latest move in an ongoing effort to boost attendance across all major events during WrestleMania weekend.

## Premium VIP Packages and Experiences

Despite discount offerings for standard tickets, WWE continues to market high-end VIP packages. The company is promoting $37,500 Elite packages, with an even more exclusive offering now capturing attention—a Cody Rhodes Bus Tour experience priced at $9,950 per person with only 12 spots available.

# Match Spotlight: Bret Hart Vs. Shawn Michaels, WWE WrestleMania XII

In the long history of WWE, 1996 was a pivotal year. After "WCW Nitro" premiered head-to-head against "WWF Raw" the year before, the Monday Night War was in its early stages. The 12th edition of WrestleMania took place on March 31, 1996, just a short time before WCW would pull ahead in the ratings for an extended period of time.

WrestleMania 12 is considered middle-of-the-road in terms of overall quality, as it is dominated by a single match: the main event Iron Man match between WWF Champion Bret Hart and challenger Shawn Michaels. This hour-long battle proved to be a defining moment in wrestling history, earning its place as a favorite in WrestleMania history despite the specific appeal of the Iron Man format. The match became a key part of one of the company's most storied rivalries.

## Building Toward the Clash

Earlier in 1996, Michaels won the Royal Rumble to earn his shot at Hart, who had held the promotion's top title since he defeated Diesel (AKA Kevin Nash) for it the previous November. The video package preceding the match framed this as a contest to determine the best in the world, after both men steadily climbed the company's ranks over the previous decade through hard work and determination. While Hart was in the midst of his third world title reign, Michaels had yet to win the big belt, which informed the dynamic between the two before the match, during, and in the years to come.

## Inside the Ring

The match began with Michaels arriving via zipline, before Hart made his entrance in more standard fashion, but with the belt around his waist. Earl Hebner, who would later play an even bigger role in the Hart-Michaels story, was the referee. Although Iron Man matches happened occasionally at live events, this was WWE's first televised Iron Man match, so Hebner carefully explained the rules before signaling for the bell.

Over the next methodically-paced hour, Michaels and Hart showcased their in-ring acumen. The match began with technical wrestling, which is supposed to be Hart's specialty. However, it was Michaels who maintained most of the advantage at first. As things progressed, the wrestlers integrated more brawling elements, with Hart ratcheting up the violence compared to his usual demeanor. As the performers alternated between styles of wrestling, they steadily quickened the pace, with Hart establishing himself as the heel so Michaels had more to gain out of the eventual conclusion.

Despite its length, the match is never boring, which is a credit to both Hart and Michaels. The announcers, led by Vince McMahon and Jerry Lawler, did a fair job of explaining the story as things slowly escalated. When they finally reached the home stretch, the crowd remained fully invested, which wasn't a given considering the risks inherent with an Iron Man match format.

## A Historic Conclusion

In the end, both men reached the time limit with Hart having Michaels in the Sharpshooter. Authority figure Gorilla Monsoon appeared and ordered both men to get back in the ring for a sudden death finale. Michaels then hit Hart with two Sweet Chin Music superkicks and pinned him to win the WWF World Heavyweight Championship, with McMahon calling out Michaels' "boyhood dream" on commentary.

## A Legacy-Defining Match

There are various reasons why this match remains an important part of WWE's history and the legacy of both Hart and Michaels. A significant factor is that it served as Michaels' final step into the world title scene. While he is undeniably an instrumental figure in WWE history, this match represented a turning point in his career trajectory as a main event player.

David Sahadi WWE Promo Creation Stories

# David Sahadi on Creating Wrestling's Most Iconic Promos: Edge's Mysterious Debut and More

Legendary TV producer David Sahadi recently sat down for an in-depth interview to discuss his groundbreaking work in professional wrestling history. The accomplished creative force behind some of WWE and TNA's most memorable vignettes, promos, and commercials spoke with host James Walsh on the Wrestling Epicenter platform, marking his second appearance on the show.

During the conversation, Sahadi promoted his newly released book "Backstage Pass," which offers an intimate behind-the-scenes look at life in the fast-paced world of professional wrestling. The book features stories spanning decades of wrestling history, including anecdotes about legendary names like Hulk Hogan, The Rock, and Steve Austin. Host James Walsh praised the work as "the best inside baseball… inside wrestling book I've read in years," a sentiment echoed by most professional reviewers.

## The Creative Process Behind Edge's Legendary Debut

One of the most revealing moments from the interview centered on Sahadi's creative decisions regarding Edge's debut vignette—work that didn't even make it into his book. Sahadi explained that WWE initially wanted a Jim Morrison-style character with Edge reading poetry by candlelight in Sahadi's New York City apartment.

"I didn't feel it at all, James," Sahadi recalled. "He didn't have the kind of voice to where he seemed like a wordsmith. He didn't come off like a Jim Morrison type character, he was almost too good looking."

Rather than accept the initial concept, Sahadi pivoted creatively. He took Edge outside into the cold and lent him his long leather trenchcoat to film the vignette. The trenchcoat, which was too large for Sahadi's frame, extended only to Edge's knees—a detail that added to the overall effect. Sahadi filmed Edge mostly from behind, presenting him as a mysterious figure emerging from the subway.

The crucial creative decision came in post-production. Instead of featuring Edge's own voice reading poetry, Sahadi brought in a female voice artist to deliver the now-iconic line: "You think you know me?"

"If you had heard him speak, him reading poetry, I don't think his character would have ever really taken off," Sahadi explained. "It might have been dead on arrival! But, that was one of the best work of my career, of my early career especially."

By keeping the voice-over minimal and leaving much to interpretation, Sahadi maintained the mystique that would define Edge's character throughout his career. He used V-roll editing techniques to enhance the vignette's visual impact while keeping dialogue sparse.

## A Creative Philosophy Fading from Modern Wrestling

Sahadi expressed concern that this particular approach to character development has become increasingly rare in contemporary wrestling production. "I don't see that very much anymore as well," he said. "It is almost like it is the same 'cut and paste' formula for the past 15 years."

The interview highlighted Sahadi's willingness to trust his instincts and deviate from initial creative directions when something wasn't working. His ability to transform what could have been a failed concept into one of wrestling's most iconic character introductions underscores his reputation as one of the industry's most innovative producers.

"Backstage Pass" is available on Amazon and through major book retailers, offering fans and industry professionals alike a deeper understanding of the creative minds behind professional wrestling's most memorable moments.
