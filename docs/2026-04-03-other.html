🔥 TNA Impact April 2026

TNA Impact delivered a mixed bag on April 2, 2026, sandwiched between two major events with plenty of storyline setup for next week's programming. The show featured four matches and extensive promo segments that built toward TNA Rebellion scheduled for April 11 in Cleveland, Ohio. While the evening had its moments of excitement, it also drew criticism for how it handled certain plot developments and reveals that fans felt came too quickly.

The main event saw Arianna Grace successfully defend her TNA Knockots World Championship against Xia Brookside in a hard-hitting contest that marked a historic moment for the promotion. This was the first-ever Knockouts championship match to main event Thursday Night Impact since the show moved to AMC in January. Brookside proved herself a credible challenger by demonstrating early in the match that there are other multi-generational talent stars in professional wrestling beyond Grace and her associates from WWE NXT. However, Grace managed to secure the victory with assistance from her fiancé Channing "Stacks" Lorenzo, who provided a crucial distraction that allowed Grace to capitalize and finish Brookside with a knee to the face. This was Grace's third-ever TNA Knockouts World Title match and another successful defense of her championship.

Looking ahead to TNA Rebellion, Grace will defend her title for the fourth time against Lei Ying Lee, the woman from whom Grace won the championship at No Surrender this past February. Interestingly, Grace, Lee, and former TNA star Dani Luna recently faced off in a triple threat match at No Surrender last Friday, with Lee and Luna having previous history with the current champion.

In addition to the main event title defense, Tessa Blanchard earned an impressive victory over Jody Threat in a Bunkhouse match, securing her win via knockout in another historic moment as the first-ever Knockouts Bunkhouse match on the program.

The evening also featured significant storyline developments that drew mixed reactions from observers. The Righteous, consisting of Vincent and Dutch, revealed themselves as the mystery attackers targeting Matt Hardy. This revelation capped off a series of attacks that saw Matt laid out by The Righteous last Thursday and again at TNA Sacrifice this past Friday when Dutch attacked him during a tag team match between Jeff Hardy and Vincent against The System. However, some felt this reveal came too quickly, with critics noting that the mystery could have benefited from playing out for at least another week to build additional intrigue and hype. The promo segment delivering the reveal was criticized as simple and brief, with the revelation feeling somewhat easy to piece together in retrospect.

The storyline does set up an intriguing match for next week, as The Hardys will defend their TNA World Tag Team Championships against The Righteous in a tables match on April 9's Impact. This stipulation elevates the rivalry and suggests that the animosity between these teams runs deeper than money and titles alone. The Righteous have reportedly dug deeper psychologically with The Hardys than The System has managed, creating psychological wounds that may linger longer than physical injuries. Some observers theorized that if The Hardys retain their titles at Rebellion against The System, they might not be mentally prepared for that defense if they first have to go through a grueling tables match with The Righteous. This dynamic could lead to an extended summer-long feud between the teams, with speculation that The Righteous could eventually capture the titles if the rivalry continues to escalate.

The episode also saw the reveal of AJ Francis as the attacker who laid out Nic Nemeth, with the revelation coming after Nic was found backstage by his brother Ryan Nemeth at TNA Sacrifice. This reveal similarly drew criticism for coming too soon and for feeling underwhelming in execution. Francis's justification for attacking Nemeth came across as underdeveloped, feeling as though it was conceived at the last second rather than being part of a carefully constructed long-term storyline. Critics felt both reveals happening on the same night created an overwhelming amount of information to process, and suggested that spacing them out over multiple weeks would have created more compelling storytelling.

Several new matches were announced for next week's April 9 episode of Impact ahead of the TNA Rebellion event. In addition to The Hardys versus The Righteous tables match for the Tag Team titles, the card will feature Dani Luna facing Jada Stone. Notably, Luna was recently granted her release by TNA after she had requested it, making this appearance noteworthy given her departure status. The episode will also feature BDE facing Frankie Kazarian in a singles match. Additionally, digital exclusive footage announced that Elayna Black will take on Myla Grace next week on Impact.

Despite the criticisms regarding pacing and execution of certain reveals, there was appreciation for how The Righteous storyline was being developed. The heel turn and reveal that their previous admiration for The Hardys was merely an act added a psychological dimension to the rivalry. The commentary about them facing "apocalyptic consequences" in the tables match next week generated enthusiasm among some observers, particularly with the suggestion that their rivalry could extend well beyond a single match. The intrigue surrounding what the Righteous ultimately want to prove to The Hardys, beyond money and titles, created anticipation for how the feud would develop through the spring and summer months ahead.

Nattie Bloodsport XV Match

Nattie Lobbied Hard for Shayna Baszler Match at Bloodsport XV

Nattie is ready for her return to Bloodsport. The event, which will take place on April 17 in Las Vegas, will feature a singles contest between Nattie and Shayna Baszler. This will be the first time that they have shared the ring since the April 11, 2025 episode of SmackDown.

In a recent interview with the Toronto Sun, Nattie spoke about the match and revealed that she handpicked Baszler to be her opponent in Vegas. According to Nattie, she actively campaigned for this specific matchup, stating that she fought to wrestle Shayna Baszler and lobbied extensively for the bout.

Nattie emphasized the significance of Bloodsport to her career, noting that the event is where her current persona was born. She explained that a year ago, while in a place in her career where she felt she needed more and wanted to grow, she took matters into her own hands by seeking opportunities outside of WWE. With the blessing of Triple H and Nick Khan, she was able to appear at Bloodsport for the first time in her 18-year tenure within WWE at that time.

Speaking to her rationale for choosing Baszler specifically, Nattie stated that in her 19 years in the WWE locker room, there is no other woman she has worked with who is as dangerous as Shayna. She described Baszler as someone that both men and women fear, emphasizing that when Baszler claims to be a submission magician, she is not exaggerating. Nattie highlighted Baszler's background as a pioneer in women's MMA and noted her training under Billy Robinson and Josh Barnett at the Snake Pit, contrasting this with Nattie's own Dungeon training from the Hart family.

Nattie also credited Baszler with paving the way for other women in combat sports. She suggested that figures like Ronda Rousey may not have pursued their paths if not for the inspiration provided by Shayna Baszler, and that many successful women in MMA would not have had the road paved for them without Baszler's pioneering efforts.

According to Nattie, she handpicked Shayna because their matchup will bring out the very best in her while also pushing her to her limits. Nattie stated that she needs someone who will get in her face and challenge her at the highest level.

The stakes for this match are particularly high. Nattie acknowledged that if the match does not go well, it could mark the end of her Nattie persona. She emphasized the gravity of the situation, explaining that a year ago at Bloodsport is where Nattie was born, but if she does not win this year, that is where Nattie will die.

With two Bloodsport matches already under her belt, Nattie remains undefeated, having secured victories over Miyu Yamashita and Masha Slamovich. Her evolution from the friendly, fan-favorite persona she maintained for years within WWE to this harder-edged Nattie character has given audiences a renewed reason to pay attention to the veteran star.

Lady Frost MLW Debut

Lady Frost is aiming for the top stars in MLW. Just days after signing with the company, Frost will make her debut for MLW at their Charleston event on Sunday, April 12. If you were expecting her to start at the bottom of the division, you've got things all wrong.

Speaking with Sean Ross Sapp, the eight year veteran noted that she's ready to call out the MLW Women's World Featherweight Champion, Shotzi Blackheart. Frost expressed her ambitions clearly during the interview, stating "I think there's a lot of women around the world. Hopefully that I get to travel more. I know that MLW has a working relationship with the girls from Japan. I know Shotzi is the champ right now. So obviously my target's huge. I don't want to start low. I'm going to right for the top, calling her out. We've never wrestled. We've been in independent locker rooms together actually years and years ago. So I think that will be interesting."

Frost acknowledged her previous experience, noting that she and Shotzi have crossed paths in independent wrestling locker rooms years ago, adding an interesting dynamic to a potential future matchup between the two.

Elsewhere in the interview, Frost said that she's not bitter about her time with AEW and ROH, suggesting she is moving forward positively with this new chapter in her career at MLW.

AAA WWE Mattel Action Figures

WWE's Mexican wrestling brand will soon have representation in Mattel's toy lineup. According to reports from WrestleVotes Radio on Fightful Select, the Mattel WWE team has received approval to begin producing action figures for AAA stars. This marks a significant milestone for the promotion following its acquisition by WWE.

Mr. Iguana has been prioritized as the first talent to be featured in an AAA-branded Mattel action figure. A target release is planned for the fall. The legendary luchador's figure will serve as the inaugural offering in what is expected to become an expanded line of AAA merchandise.

WWE announced the acquisition of Lucha Libre AAA Worldwide nearly a year ago during the first night of WrestleMania 41. The move represented a major expansion of WWE's international operations and brought AAA's storied roster under the WWE umbrella. Now, the stars of AAA will have access to high-quality action figures that resemble those of top WWE Superstars, thanks to the partnership between WWE and Mattel.

Mattel has been WWE's primary toy partner since early 2010, establishing itself as the company's trusted manufacturer of wrestling merchandise. The two companies renewed their global licensing partnership in April 2025 with a multi-year extension of their agreement. Mattel produces a wide range of WWE products, including action figures, accessories, playsets, roleplay items, vehicles, and vehicle accessories. The expansion to include AAA talent demonstrates the strength of this partnership and WWE's commitment to elevating the Mexican promotion's global profile.

Tessa Blanchard TNA Hand Injury

Tessa Blanchard Reveals Hand Injury After Brutal TNA Bunkhouse Match

Tessa Blanchard emerged victorious from her Bunkhouse match against Jody Threat on the April 2 episode of TNA iMPACT on AMC, but the victory came with a physical cost. Following the match, Blanchard revealed on X/Twitter that she had sustained a hand injury, posting a photo that displayed bruises on her right hand.

Rather than attributing the injury to her opponent or the nature of the match itself, Blanchard made a bold statement by claiming responsibility for the damage. She noted that she inflicted the injury upon herself and used the moment to put the entire Knockouts division on notice regarding her intentions and capabilities moving forward.

In her social media post, Blanchard wrote, "My hand? I did that to myself. Not Jody. Not the match. Me. Because when I step into that ring, I don't hold back… I let every ounce of frustration, every ounce of pressure, everything this industry has tried to put on me… loose. You can talk about birthrights. You can talk about what you think you deserve. The sacrifices, the waiting, the excuses… But I don't wait. I take. So to the entire Knockouts division… you better buckle tf up. I'm not here to fit in… I'm here to remind you exactly who runs this."

Beyond her written statement, Blanchard recorded a promo video in which she elaborated on her mindset and approach to professional wrestling. She emphasized that she doesn't follow trends or wait passively for opportunities to present themselves, instead choosing to actively seize them when they arise. Blanchard used the Bunkhouse match as a platform to remind the wrestling world of her identity and her capacity to dominate.

Speaking directly about her opponent's performance, Blanchard addressed Jody Threat, saying, "Jody, you didn't lose tonight because you're weak. You're not weak. You lost tonight because you stepped into the ring with the woman who refuses to hold anything back. The frustration, the anger, it's precisely what makes me so damn dangerous."

Blanchard continued by directly challenging the narrative surrounding competition in the Knockouts division. She stated, "The Knockouts division is constantly talking about who's next, what's next, who's rising, but let me make something abundantly clear: There is no next when I'm standing right here. I don't follow trends. I don't wait for opportunities, I take them, and tonight, I took that match, I took that spotlight, and I reminded everyone exactly who Tessa Blanchard is."

Through both her social media presence and video promo, Blanchard conveyed a message of uncompromising intensity and self-determination. Her willingness to attribute her hand injury to her own unbridled approach in the ring served as a metaphor for her overall wrestling philosophy: complete commitment with no holding back. The statement sent a clear signal to the rest of the Knockouts division that Blanchard views herself as the primary force to be reckoned with and that she will not wait for circumstances to align in her favor.

Matt Hardy TNA Nose Injury

Matt Hardy recently opened up about an injury he sustained during a high-octane tables match on TNA Impact. While discussing his latest wrestling endeavor on The Extreme Life of Matt Hardy podcast, Hardy revealed that he picked up a nose injury during the taping, which came from a match that escalated from a simple segment into full-blown chaos.

Hardy first mentioned the injury in passing before deciding to provide a full explanation of what transpired. He detailed how everything began with an in-ring promo featuring The Hardys and The Righteous, which quickly turned more physical than initially expected. That segment directly led to a tables match the following week, which Hardy described as delivering exactly what fans would anticipate from that type of bout.

According to Hardy, the in-ring promo was entertaining and featured good content, with The Righteous interrupting the segment, leading to an engaging back-and-forth between the teams. At the end of the promo, both teams built toward the tables match that would follow on the next episode of TNA Impact. Hardy spoke positively about the match itself, calling it a can't-miss encounter.

Once the match got underway, the action never let up. Hardy described the sequence as nonstop, with both teams going all out from the opening bell to the final moments. During the intensity of the bout, the injury occurred when one of the table spots didn't go exactly as planned. Hardy explained that while executing a table spot on an opponent, he ended up catching part of the damage himself.

Hardy provided specific details about how the injury happened, noting that a table he put somebody through nicked his own nose. He emphasized that the tables were extremely sharp and had become very splintered during the match, with wood splinters flying everywhere throughout the bout. Hardy characterized the overall match as wild, highlighting the unpredictable nature of tables matches and how quickly things can go sideways when chaos is the centerpiece of the action.

This injury exemplifies the risks that come with executing high-impact spots in matches designed around destruction and hardcore wrestling. Even when delivering the kind of entertaining moments fans expect from a tables match, wrestlers can find themselves on the receiving end of collateral damage. Hardy's willingness to continue putting his body on the line in such matches demonstrates his commitment to providing compelling television, even at this stage of his career, though it raises questions about whether such risks are necessary or advisable for veteran performers.

Kazusada Higuchi DDT Retirement Event

Kazusada Higuchi, a prominent figure in DDT Pro-Wrestling, is stepping away from in-ring competition following medical advice that his continued wrestling career poses a significant health risk. Higuchi is retiring due to a subluxation of his first and second vertebrae, a serious spinal condition that doctors have warned could have life-threatening consequences if he returns to active competition.

The retirement event will be hosted at Korakuen Hall on April 5th as part of a DDT Pro-Wrestling show. Rather than competing in his final match, Higuchi's faction-mates from Harimao will wrestle on his behalf during the main event. Following the conclusion of the main event, a retirement ceremony will take place to honor Higuchi's career.

Higuchi has made several specific requests regarding how his retirement will be celebrated and commemorated. He has requested that there be no photo or autograph session held at the event. Additionally, he does not want paper streamers to be thrown into the ring during his ceremony. These requests suggest Higuchi prefers a more subdued and dignified farewell rather than the traditional celebratory elements often associated with retirement events in professional wrestling.

John Skyler & Jason Hotch Feared TNA Wrestling Departure Before Mustafa Ali Partnership

John Skyler has revealed that The Great Hands were once 'job scared' during a period of time in TNA Wrestling. Members of Order 4 in TNA Wrestling, John Skyler and Jason Hotch are also referred to as the Great Hands.

Looking back at their history together in an interview with Wrestling Life Online, Skyler opened up about the evolution of the pairing. When he and Jason Hotch were first thrown together as a team four years ago, the initial direction was simple: "Hey, there's this veteran and this kid that just won the Gutcheck, they kind of look alike, let's see what they offer as a team." That was about the direction they were given at the time.

Skyler acknowledged that at one point they were directionless in TNA. The team didn't know what the future held and were genuinely fearful about whether they would last and survive in the company. The turning point came with the arrival of Mustafa Ali. Under Ali's leadership, the team transformed from being directionless to having a clear purpose and identity.

The name change reflected this transformation, evolving from "Good" to "Great" because, as Skyler explained, Mr. Ali made them great again. Over the last twelve to sixteen months, Order 4 has been able to show the world just how great they can be. Skyler pointed to their matches against teams like The Rascalz, The Hardys, The System, Elijah, and Cedric Alexander as evidence of their consistent quality. He emphasized that every time Order 4 steps in the ring, regardless of where they are on the card or where the match takes place, they hit a home run.

Skyler concluded by noting that they started off as Good Hands and are now The Great Hands. His ultimate goal is clear: once they win the TNA World Tag Team Titles, they will simply be known as the best.

With a return to TNA Wrestling in January 2024, Skyler would team with Hotch for the first time as The Good Hands the following month, setting the stage for their eventual rise under Ali's leadership.

The lack of creative direction that plagued Skyler and Hotch is not an unfamiliar story in professional wrestling. Prior to his WWE release, Mustafa Ali found himself as the leader of Retribution, a faction of renamed stars that was widely criticized by the wrestling community. Ali had previously commented that the group "never had a why" for their existence or purpose, with that lack of direction being a contributing factor to the failure of the faction.

It appears that a similar lack of direction in TNA Wrestling was how Skyler and Hotch felt, leaving them fearful that they might be heading toward the end of their time in the company. Fortunately, direction was found, and the arrival of Ali in early 2024 proved beneficial to both stars, giving them the purpose that they needed to successfully ply their trade on television.

This is not an unusual story in any creative field. A seemingly simple change, like the addition of a strong creative direction and a purposeful leader, can alter the trajectory of performers toward something more appealing, enjoyable, and rewarding. Skyler and Hotch's journey demonstrates how the right leadership and creative vision can transform struggling performers into consistent main event contributors.
