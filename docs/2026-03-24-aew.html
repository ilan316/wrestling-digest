Thunder Rosa WWE Contract Offers and AEW Reflections

# Thunder Rosa Opens Up About WWE Contract Rejection and AEW Journey

Former AEW Women's World Champion Thunder Rosa has revealed details about a significant career crossroads in 2019 when WWE approached her with a contract offer that would have fundamentally altered her wrestling trajectory.

During an appearance on The Cruz Show on Real 92.3 LA, Rosa disclosed that WWE offered her a $60,000 annual salary to become a referee, but the position would have required her to retire from in-ring competition. At the time, the offer represented financial stability she desperately needed after years of struggling as an independent wrestler.

## Financial Struggles Led to WWE Consideration

Rosa explained the difficult circumstances that made the WWE offer initially appealing. "In 2019, they called me and they were like, 'Are you ready to hang up the boots?' I was like, 'Well, you know, I needed money,'" she recalled. "Being an independent professional wrestler is a hustle. I had a job when I was in the Bay Area, but when I decided to do wrestling full time, I was depending on my ex-husband and my hustle. I was hustling all the time and I was tired."

The $60,000 yearly salary seemed promising at the time, but circumstances beyond Rosa's control prevented the opportunity from materializing. "There was a hurricane that happened and then they just kept pushing my tryout and then it never happened," Rosa explained. "But it was meant to be that way."

## The Path to AEW

When WWE's referee opportunity fell through, Rosa made a pivotal decision that would eventually lead her to AEW. She signed an MMA contract with Combate Americas for $3,000—a move that represented a different direction for her career. Rosa signed with AEW in 2020 and achieved significant success, capturing the Women's World Championship two years later in a steel cage match against Britt Baker. She was forced to relinquish the title in November 2022 due to a serious back injury and subsequently retired from MMA around the same time.

## Reflecting on AEW's Growth and the Britt Baker Rivalry

Looking back on AEW's trajectory, Rosa expressed pride in the company's evolution. "For the longest time we were the alternative," she said. "We're no longer the alternative—we're here. We've been here for six years and the stuff that we have been able to accomplish, and I am very proud of this. I was part of one of the biggest changes in mainstream wrestling."

A significant moment in that journey was her legendary Lights Out match against Britt Baker on the March 17, 2021 edition of AEW Dynamite. The match was the first women-exclusive main event in company history and became an extreme, brutal affair featuring ladders, chairs, and thumbtacks. It marked the first time two women bled on television during a mainstream wrestling broadcast and earned match-of-the-year honors.

However, Rosa's perspective on the match differed from Baker's intentions, at least in her assessment. "I remember Britt was telling me, 'We're going to make sure that this goes great and we don't mess up or whatever,'" Rosa recounted. "She said something else, and I'm just thinking like, 'Girl, I know you're doing it for the clout.' I'm doing it because people don't understand that this is not only gonna change my life, but this is gonna change a lot of morenitas and brown people's lives."

Rosa's comments reflect her perspective that while Baker may have been motivated by personal advancement and reputation, Rosa herself was driven by the broader significance of the match for representation in mainstream wrestling.

AEW Backstage Personalities and Culture

# Life Behind the Scenes at AEW: Comedy, Politics, and Purpose

All Elite Wrestling's backstage environment has become an increasing focus of wrestler commentary, revealing the personalities and perspectives that define the company's culture away from the cameras.

## The Funniest Faces Behind the Curtain

When asked about the funniest people backstage at AEW, rising stars Skye Blue and Kyle Fletcher offered differing perspectives on who brings the laughs to the locker room. Blue nominated Claudio Castagnoli for the title, though Fletcher immediately countered that Castagnoli's humor comes in the form of dad jokes—material that Blue admitted goes over the heads of others but lands with her.

Fletcher ultimately sided with Don Callis as AEW's funniest personality, praising the executive's blunt humor that rarely gets public exposure. "Not a lot of people get to see that side of him, but he is hilarious," Fletcher explained. "He is not afraid to say the things that everyone else is afraid to say, and he is very, very blunt and I always get to hear how blunt he is and I just find it hilarious."

## A Debate Over Wrestling's Purpose and Politics

The early months of 2026 have brought political activism into AEW programming, with anti-ICE chants becoming regular occurrences at shows driven by rising star Brody King's activism. While AEW owner Tony Khan has maintained that the company isn't inherently political, he has allowed wrestlers the freedom to express themselves without restriction.

However, not all AEW talent embrace this direction. Anthony Ogogo expressed reservations about mixing politics with professional wrestling, arguing that the medium should serve as an escape from real-world concerns. "When you start bringing up political things into the show, it then brings real life into the show, which should be an escape," Ogogo said.

The veteran wrestler acknowledged the passion of AEW's fanbase and their desire to voice opinions, but suggested a separation between wrestling and activism. "That's one perspective to have, like there's lots of crazy crap going on in the world, I want to avoid that and watch wrestling for two hours," he explained, citing comedian Ricky Gervais's approach of keeping performances focused and free from extended monologues.

Interestingly, Ogogo acknowledged his own history with political promo work, having worked with Cody Rhodes five years prior on anti-ICE messaging. "I did a promo, five years ago, I was obviously bashing America and I was talking about ICE, so all about leaving politics out and all that, I have done it," he admitted. "I did it five years ago before it was cool to talk about it. It is what it is at the end of the day. Wrestling is all about emotion."

## Making Yourself Indispensable: Colt Cabana's AEW Journey

Colt Cabana has carved out a unique niche within AEW that extends far beyond in-ring competition. Operating in multiple capacities, Cabana serves as a producer, works with the merchandise team, handles VIP experiences for premium ticket holders, and maintains his roster status despite his in-ring wrestling opportunities diminishing.

"I'm trying to make myself irreplaceable. I don't ever want to leave this job," Cabana expressed, revealing his strategic approach to job security within the organization.

On show days, Cabana's responsibilities vary considerably. He waits for his producing assignment before working with assigned wrestlers on their matches. Beyond that, he assists the merchandise department by obtaining autographs, creates promotional content for social media, and communicates with the director's truck during matches.

Cabana's philosophy as a producer prioritizes artist autonomy over directorial control. "You're the artist, you're the wrestler, you're the one that got yourself here," he explained. "I'm only here to help your ideas, maybe help them grow, help put them together, help form them, make suggestions. But ultimately, it's your match and I want you to either do great for your match or I want you to eat sh*t. So you learn."

Before televised shows, Cabana hosts the VIP experience for premium ticket purchasers, bringing in wrestlers and industry figures like Jeff Jarrett to meet fans. This responsibility taps into his decades of wrestling knowledge while providing value to the company's business operations.

Cabana signed with AEW in 2020 after considering three competing offers: a deal with Ring of Honor, a full-time partnership with New Japan Pro Wrestling teaming with Toru Yano, or joining the upstart AEW. He expressed satisfaction with his choice, particularly given how the COVID-19 pandemic shortly thereafter impacted the entire wrestling industry. "I'm pleased with the decision I made," Cabana reflected on his now-six-year tenure with the company.

Ronda Rousey AEW Revolution Appearance

# Ronda Rousey's AEW Appearance Was One-Off, Not Start of Long-Term Storyline

Ronda Rousey's surprise appearance at AEW Revolution on March 15, 2026, may have left fans wanting more, but according to reports, there won't be an immediate follow-up. The former UFC and WWE star's involvement with Tony Khan's promotion has been revealed as a one-time appearance rather than the beginning of an extended wrestling storyline.

During the Revolution pay-per-view event held in Fresno, Rousey made a shocking appearance where she confronted Toni Storm and helped Marina Shafir lay her out. The moment instantly sparked speculation among wrestling fans about what could come next and whether Rousey was signing with AEW for a significant push.

However, according to reports from PWInsider on March 24, 2026, Rousey is not expected to return to AEW anytime soon. Her Revolution appearance was designed primarily to promote her MMA return and to support Marina Shafir, with whom she has maintained a close friendship for many years. The involvement was tied to timing and real-life factors rather than creative long-term planning.

The decision to book Rousey at Revolution made practical sense due to several converging circumstances. Since the event was held locally for Rousey in the same market as her scheduled May fight against Gina Carano, all parties involved found the timing and logistics favorable.

## No Immediate Feud With Toni Storm

Many fans anticipated that Rousey's confrontation with Toni Storm would lead to a follow-up angle or potential feud. Those expectations have been tempered by reports confirming that no such storyline was scheduled post-Revolution. Storm subsequently took a break from in-ring competition, further eliminating any immediate booking prospects.

However, the door to a future program between Rousey and Storm hasn't been completely shut. While nothing was scheduled when the Revolution event took place, the possibility remains that their paths could cross again at some point down the line.

## Praise From Wrestling Legends

Despite being a one-off appearance, Rousey's AEW moment has earned respect from wrestling veterans. WWE Hall of Famer Rikishi recently praised Rousey as a "marketing genius" on his "Off The Top" podcast, commending her ability to capitalize on opportunities across multiple entertainment platforms.

Rikishi noted how Rousey has successfully navigated different industries, first earning substantial compensation in UFC before moving to WWE and securing a featured position there. He predicted that her AEW appearance would likely conclude her current wrestling run, positioning her to move forward with other endeavors.

"You talk about a marketing genius, she surpassed a wrestling fan, she surpassed the UFC fan, she [is] coming after the bag," Rikishi said. "At the end of the day, every single wrestler, every single UFC fighter, that's what you should be concerned about."

The Hall of Famer also defended Rousey against criticisms from some within the wrestling community, noting that jealousy often surfaces when individuals find success in competitive industries. He acknowledged that while she may not connect with every wrestler in the locker room, Rousey possesses legitimate wrestling skills and the ability to perform at a high level.

"I believe that she can handle her own. Is she a skilled wrestler in professional wrestling? I feel she is," Rikishi stated. "But this is the industry we in. If you're successful, you got jealousy people; when you're not successful, not a damn soul going to reach out to pick you up."

Rousey has faced criticism from some wrestling peers and fans throughout her time in the industry, yet her business acumen and crossover appeal continue to generate significant interest whenever she appears in wrestling contexts.

Wardlow AEW Revolution Appearance

# Wardlow Spotted Backstage at AEW Revolution, No Imminent In-Ring Return Expected

Former AEW TNT Champion Wardlow was in attendance at this month's AEW Revolution pay-per-view held at the Crypto.com Arena in Los Angeles, CA, though his visit was strictly a backstage one with no official capacity on the show.

According to reports, Wardlow was there simply to visit with colleagues, and there are currently no plans for an in-ring return in the immediate future. The powerhouse has been sidelined while recovering from an injury he sustained in 2025, shortly after making a brief return to action last August.

## History of Recent Injuries

Wardlow's injury troubles have been extensive over the past year and a half. His most recent setback came during an August 2025 appearance at Forbidden Door, where he shocked the wrestling world by attacking Swerve Strickland and Prince Nana while pledging allegiance to the Don Callis Family. That incident resulted in a torn pectoral muscle that forced him out of action once again.

Prior to that appearance, Wardlow had been recovering from a knee injury that kept him sidelined for approximately a year. During his time away from the ring, he remained active outside of wrestling by filming for the American Gladiators reboot series hosted by WWE's The Miz, which debuted in April. He has since filmed two seasons of the competition show.

## Career Overview

Wardlow's AEW tenure dates back to his 2019 debut when he was introduced as MJF's bodyguard. That partnership lasted until 2022, when Wardlow refused to hand over the Dynamite Diamond Ring to MJF, leading to a split from The Pinnacle and the beginning of his singles career.

Following his departure from the group, Wardlow captured the TNT Championship in July 2022, holding the prestigious title for nearly 140 days. As of now, there is no confirmation regarding when he might be cleared to compete again, though further updates are expected once more details become available.

Lio Rush AEW New Gimmick

# AEW Star Lio Rush Fires Back At Critics With Impressive Social Media Numbers

AEW's Lio Rush is letting his success speak for itself as he responds to criticism surrounding his newly debuted "Blackheart" gimmick.

Rush introduced the striking new persona at AEW Revolution when he entered the 21-man Blackjack Battle Royal for the National Championship. The demonic character features a bald look, white contacts, and eerie, creature-like movements, with Rush crawling around on all fours during his performances. Commentary comparisons to a fast-moving Gollum from Lord of the Rings helped establish the unique presentation of the gimmick.

## Mixed Reaction From Fans

The wrestler followed up his Revolution debut with another similar performance against Tommaso Ciampa on the Slam Dunk Sunday edition of AEW Collision on March 22, though he came up short in that matchup. While the new gimmick generated significant attention, wrestling fans online remained divided on the character direction. Some expressed confusion and criticism about Rush's sudden shift in presentation, particularly questioning why the character change came without any storyline explanation.

## Massive Social Media Success

Despite the polarizing reception, the numbers tell a different story. Lio Rush's new gimmick has been performing exceptionally well across AEW's social media platforms, accumulating over 20 million views in under 24 hours following his Collision appearance.

Rather than ignoring the criticism, Rush took to X/Twitter to address his doubters directly, posting: "Less than 24hrs. 20M+ HE hears you."

## Expanding His Reach

Beyond AEW, Rush made an impact in the company's sister promotion, Ring of Honor, defeating Nathan Cruz during a ROH TV taping in Jacksonville, Florida, on March 22. The success across multiple promotions underscores the potential of his new character direction despite early skepticism from some members of the fanbase.

Roderick Strong and Orange Cassidy AEW News

# Roderick Strong Officially Joins The Conglomeration Following Tag Team Victory

Roderick Strong made a significant announcement on AEW Collision, declaring that he is officially a member of The Conglomeration. The declaration came after Strong and Orange Cassidy earned an impressive tag team victory against Jay Lethal and Lee Johnson on the Slam Dunk Sunday episode of Collision (Mar. 22, 2026).

## Strong and Cassidy Demonstrate Chemistry in the Ring

Strong and Cassidy displayed impressive synergy during their first tag team outing, executing a strategy that had Cassidy absorb early offense before building momentum for Strong's hot tag. The plan worked to perfection, with Strong unleashing a series of impactful strikes that found their mark against tired opponents.

The victory was sealed when Strong connected with a devastating backbreaker on Johnson, then tossed Cassidy onto his fallen opponent for the pinfall. The smooth teamwork and chemistry between the two wrestlers set the stage for Strong's subsequent announcement.

## A Change of Heart

Following the match, Strong took time to explain his decision to fully commit to The Conglomeration. Initially, Strong had been associating with the group primarily to be alongside Kyle O'Reilly. When O'Reilly was sidelined due to injury, Strong found himself questioning his continued presence with the faction.

Upon reflection, Strong realized that what needed to change wasn't his alignment with the group, but rather his own perspective. This realization led Strong to declare his official membership in The Conglomeration, signaling a genuine commitment to the faction and its ideals.

## New Challenge Emerges

The celebratory moment was interrupted when David Finlay and Clark Connors of The Dogs appeared on the big screen with a challenge. The two teams will face off in a tag team match on AEW Dynamite, with The Dogs making it clear that their business with Strong and Cassidy is far from finished. AEW has made the bout official.

Young Bucks AEW Tag Team Championship

# The Young Bucks Discuss Special Family Entrance, Praise Adam Cole, and Crown The Usos as Best Tag Team Outside AEW

The Young Bucks are set for a major championship showdown as they prepare to defend the AEW World Tag Team Championships against FTR at AEW Dynasty 2026 on April 12 in Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada. The bout will also feature involvement from Adam Copeland and Christian Cage.

During a recent edition of Being The Elite Mailbag, Matt and Nick Jackson opened up about various topics impacting the professional wrestling landscape, including their thoughts on talent both within and outside of All Elite Wrestling.

## Praise for Adam Cole

Matt Jackson expressed his deep affection for Adam Cole, calling him one of the most talented and charismatic wrestlers he's ever encountered. "I love Adam Cole so much. He is the sweetest man I think I've ever met, not in the business, but in life, and I think every day he's improving and he's getting better," Jackson said. "I think he's one of the most talented, gifted, charismatic wrestlers that ever was and a much better human being."

Jackson noted that Cole's warm personality extends beyond his professional abilities, prompting him to reach out to the wrestler. "This actually, thank you for bringing this up because I need to actually text him right now and check on him because I miss him so much," he added.

## The Usos Recognized as Top Team

When discussing the best tag teams in professional wrestling today, Matt Jackson acknowledged that while AEW houses most of the elite teams in the industry, The Usos deserve top billing outside the promotion. "We've got most of them, right? I mean, I guess you have to say the Usos," he said.

Nick Jackson explained his perspective differs slightly, as he primarily focuses on AEW content. "For me, it'd be unfair to answer that because I don't really watch anything outside of AEW. AEW has so much content on a weekly basis, it's hard to see anything outside of it," Nick stated.

Matt elaborated on his history with The Usos, recalling a backstage encounter years earlier. "Years and years and years ago, this was probably in 2011, I believe, we got to go backstage at, I think it was SmackDown when we actually did this. It was prior to the show, so all the boys were out there watching, and we got to do a sort of like a tryout match in front of the boys, and we got to wrestle the Usos," he explained.

That early experience left a lasting impression on Jackson. "Even way back then, those guys were awesome. They're really good. Next level, they were calling great stuff on the fly, and I remember afterward thinking like, 'whoa, those guys are good, I would really like to have a proper match with them one day,'" Matt recalled. "Now, fast forward all these years later, it's probably not possible. But hey, you know, I recognize that they've done some great stuff."

## Family Entrance Insight

The Bucks recently debuted a special entrance featuring their children at AEW Revolution, and they shed light on how that moment came together. Nick Jackson revealed that his daughter Allison had been requesting to participate in an entrance for approximately three years, and he was waiting for the perfect opportunity.

"My daughter Allison's been asking me for probably the last three years, and, well, 'we've been bad guys, honey,' and I was like, you got to be patient. We got to wait until the spot works, and I felt like this was the perfect time to do it," Nick explained. "In Los Angeles, our home state, West Coast. We could easily have all the family there. So the perfect ingredients mixed well, and we were ready for it on the pay-per-view."

Matt Jackson credited their children as the driving force behind the entrance concept. "We should probably credit our children," he stated.

The special moment clearly resonated with the Bucks' family. Matt revealed that his son was so excited about the experience that his wrestling enthusiasm has reached new heights. "My son has slept in his gear for the last two nights straight, and he is obsessed. I think he's been bit by the bug," Matt said.

Toni Storm AEW Absence Update

# Toni Storm's AEW Absence Sparks Speculation as New Details Emerge

Toni Storm's sudden departure from AEW programming has left fans searching for answers, with recent reports suggesting the circumstances surrounding her write-off may not involve a physical injury.

Storm, a four-time AEW Women's World champion, was unexpectedly removed from television on March 18's episode of Dynamite. In the lead-up to that show from Fresno, California, AEW released a backstage segment depicting Storm's lifeless body in a pool of blood, effectively writing her off the program. Following the airing of that scene, reports indicated that the veteran performer would be unavailable for an extended period, with the expectation that she would not compete for the remainder of 2024.

## The Timing Raises Questions

According to Wrestling Observer Radio, Dave Meltzer reported that Storm's unavailability was not due to injury. More significantly, Meltzer revealed that AEW management only learned of Storm's situation on Wednesday — the very day of the episode — forcing the company to quickly devise the emergency angle that removed her from the storyline.

"It appears [Storm's] not gonna be wrestling for a long, long time, and this was something that they found out on Wednesday," Meltzer explained. "It was not an injury or anything like that. On Wednesday, she is there. She is set up to wrestle in this match and they had to do this emergency angle to take her out and put Mina Shirakawa in. On the day, on Wednesday — earliest Wednesday morning, certainly all day Tuesday — they were under the impression that she was going to be wrestling."

The unexpected decision forced AEW to quickly pivot, with Shirakawa taking Storm's place in her scheduled match.

## Additional Clues Offer Limited Insight

Details regarding Storm's absence remain scarce, though additional information has surfaced in recent days. Storm was booked for the Big Event EXS convention in New York over the past weekend, and attendees who shared photos from the event noted nothing unusual or indicative of a specific injury.

The company has allowed the absence to remain largely mysterious, respecting Storm's privacy on the matter. As the wrestling community awaits clarity, fans and industry observers continue to speculate about the nature of her time away from competition.

For now, hope remains that Storm is doing well, with the expectation that she and possibly her husband, professional wrestler Juice Robinson, will determine when and if to share further details about her hiatus.
