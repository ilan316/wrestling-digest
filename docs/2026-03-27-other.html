🔥 Buff Bagwell TNA iMPACT Return

**TL;DR:** Buff Bagwell made an emotional return to TNA iMPACT on March 26, 2026, marking his first appearance in nearly 20 years. The former WCW star disclosed that he had his right leg amputated above the knee on July 10, 2025, following a 2020 car accident and five years of complications. Despite his physical challenges, Bagwell apologized for his past behavior and expressed his desire to wrestle one final match for TNA if fans would support him, though the segment was interrupted by Frankie Kazarian's mocking comments before Elijah came to his defense.

**Buff Bagwell Returns to TNA iMPACT After Nearly Two Decades**

Buff Bagwell made a surprise appearance on the March 26, 2026 episode of TNA iMPACT, delivering an emotional and heartfelt segment that marked his return to the company for the first time in almost 20 years. The appearance took place at the Gateway Center Arena in Atlanta, Georgia, and was taped several weeks prior to air on AXS TV. During his time on the show, the former WCW and WWE veteran conducted an interview with backstage interviewer Gia Miller, where he opened up about his difficult personal journey and his hopes for one final match.

**A Candid Discussion of Health Struggles**

Bagwell's appearance came in the aftermath of significant health challenges that have defined the past several years of his life. He disclosed that he had his right leg amputated above the knee on July 10, 2025, a decision that came after years of serious complications stemming from a 2020 car accident that occurred while he was under the influence. The accident severely damaged his right knee, with his kneecap nearly destroyed and his leg unable to bend properly.

Over the five years following the accident, Bagwell endured an arduous recovery process that included more than 40 surgical procedures in an attempt to save his leg. These surgeries included a knee replacement and a procedure where part of his calf muscle was transferred to improve blood flow. Despite these extensive efforts, complications persisted. Bagwell suffered from multiple infections, including a serious staph infection, and was hospitalized repeatedly. At one particularly difficult point, he underwent 21 surgeries in just seven weeks.

The combination of physical pain and emotional toll took a significant impact on Bagwell's quality of life. He became self-conscious about the appearance of his leg and avoided public appearances, preferring to wear long pants rather than shorts. Walking, sitting, and exercising became increasingly difficult, and depression set in as the situation deteriorated.

By 2024, Bagwell faced a critical decision. Doctors presented him with two options: undergo additional surgeries with only a minimal chance of success, or proceed with amputation. Even if the surgeries were successful, his leg would remain stiff and provide limited functionality. After careful consideration, Bagwell chose to have the amputation performed above the knee.

In the immediate aftermath of the July 10, 2025 surgery, Bagwell experienced a moment of shock and anxiety. However, he came to realize that amputation was the right decision. He later explained that after waking up, he reflected on the condition his leg had been in, and within moments, he knew he had made the correct choice. Following the procedure and receiving a prosthetic leg, Bagwell reported feeling as though he had "gained two legs out of this," since his injured right leg's condition had previously limited the use of his left leg as well.

**A Heartfelt Apology and Statement of Intent**

During his segment with Gia Miller, Bagwell addressed the fans directly with a sincere and emotional message. He began by thanking God for guiding him through his difficult journey. He then delivered a heartfelt apology, acknowledging his past behavior over the previous two decades.

"I've got a big apology," Bagwell stated. "I've got to apologize to each and every fan out here for my behavior over the last 20 years. There's been some ups and some downs, but more downs than ups."

Despite the severity of his physical challenges, Bagwell expressed his desire to compete one final time for TNA. He told the fans that if they were willing to support him, he would make his way to the ring and wrestle for the company one more time.

"If you'll get behind me just one more time, I will come down this ramp. I will walk down this ramp. I will get in that ring, and I will wrestle for TNA one more time," Bagwell said, his remarks eliciting a strong positive response from the live crowd.

**Frankie Kazarian's Interruption and Mocking Assault**

The emotional moment was quickly interrupted by Frankie Kazarian, who emerged to confront Bagwell. Kazarian's segment was marked by harsh words and mocking comments directed at the legendary wrestler. He criticized Bagwell's physical condition and ability to perform, making light of the amputation and Bagwell's famous catchphrase.

"Walk down that aisle? I hate to break it to you, my friend—you're not walking anywhere. I mean, maybe you can hop down that aisle, but you're not walking, bud," Kazarian said, setting a dismissive and mocking tone for his remarks.

Kazarian continued, "Don't get me wrong, I was a huge Buff Bagwell fan. You had it all—the physique, the swagger. You used to come out doing your thing. I liked the Buff from 20 years ago, but this… this is pathetic. What are you even doing here? Do you want us to start a GoFundMe page for you?"

He then referenced Bagwell's catchphrase, twisting it in a derisive manner. "What was that catchphrase you used to say? 'Buff is the stuff.' It looks to me like Buff lost some stuff. What's wrong? You don't look well. You getting a little weak in the knee?" Kazarian paused before delivering his final insult: "You're half the man you used to be, and I'm twice the man you ever were."

**Elijah's Show of Support**

Before the confrontation could escalate further, Elijah made his way to the ring to even the odds and chase Kazarian away. Elijah then showed his support for Bagwell, offering encouragement and solidarity. He told Bagwell that he would be willing to walk to the ring alongside him whenever he was ready to make his comeback match, providing a moment of positive support that contrasted sharply with Kazarian's mockery.

**Historical Context with TNA**

Bagwell's return to TNA carries historical significance for the promotion. He competed for the company in its earliest days, appearing on TNA's very first show in 2002 and competing four times for the promotion that year and into 2003. He made additional appearances in 2006. During his time with the company, Bagwell held the TNA World Championship. His return marks a full-circle moment for a wrestler who has been a part of TNA's journey since its inception.

**Looking Forward**

While Bagwell has expressed his desire to compete one final time for TNA, the specific timing and details of any potential match have not yet been finalized. His return demonstrates his commitment to overcoming the physical and emotional challenges he has faced, as well as his determination to provide fans with one last opportunity to see him perform in the ring. The positive response from the TNA crowd and the support from fellow wrestler Elijah suggest that fans remain interested in seeing Bagwell's comeback match come to fruition.

Goldy Locks TNA Return Plans

---

Goldy Locks is looking toward the future, and it sounds like she's not done with TNA just yet. During an appearance on Jasmin St. Claire's Off The Rails podcast, the former TNA personality made it clear that she still has unfinished business with the promotion that helped launch her wrestling career. While discussing her past experiences and current projects, Goldy Locks revealed that returning to TNA is something she would welcome if the opportunity presented itself.

Her interest in coming back isn't just about nostalgia. Goldy Locks noted that she already has creative ideas in mind and feels there's still more she can offer if given the chance to step back into the spotlight. "I went back and did my thing. And it's not like I'm just sitting around waiting for a call, but I mean, creative production, they know like I would I would love to be back," she explained during the podcast. "So, you know, whatever's in the cards is in the cards. You just move forward."

"I've made it clear that I would love to come back and I've got some angles, some story lines that I'd like to do. And we'll see if it happens or something else. I'm open to whatever they would want to do with me, but I would love to be back. It was really, really fun. It was awesome," Goldy Locks continued.

However, Goldy Locks also opened up about the challenges she faced during her early days in TNA, painting a picture of chaos behind the scenes during the company's formative years. According to her account, her early assignments often involved being sent into live television situations without preparation, guidance, or even basic instructions about what was expected of her.

She explained that critics often judged her performance harshly at the time, but many viewers never realized how little direction she was actually given before stepping into the spotlight. "I didn't know what I was doing. Nobody told me what to do. They just threw me to the wolves. And they just didn't tell me what was going on. They gave me a microphone and just said, 'Go out there.' So, I did the best that I could," Goldy Locks said.

Her comments offer a rare behind-the-scenes look at how early TNA operated and how quickly talent were expected to adapt in a live environment. With wrestling companies continuing to revisit past talent and revive classic personalities, Goldy Locks positioning herself for a potential comeback adds another intriguing possibility to the mix.

Netflix Price Increase

Netflix subscribers are once again being asked to dig deeper into their wallets if they want to continue to "Netflix and Chill" in the future. This week, it was announced that the international streaming giant will be raising its subscription prices across multiple tiers in the United States.

The price increases affect all four subscription options available to American subscribers. The Standard With Ads tier will increase from $6.99 to $7.99 per month, marking the first price increase this ad-supported option has experienced. This tier provides one stream in HD with advertising included.

The Standard plan, which offers two streams in HD without ads, will see the most significant proportional increase, jumping from $15.49 to $17.99 per month—a roughly 16% increase. The Premium tier, which provides four streams in 4K quality without ads, will rise from $22.99 to $24.99 per month. While this represents a $2 increase in absolute terms, it is proportionally the smallest jump of the three main tiers.

Additionally, the Extra member add-on for ad-free plans will increase from $7.99 to $8.99 per month.

For wrestling fans, it's worth noting that WWE Raw airs live on Netflix every Monday night at 8/7c, with live coverage available from this outlet every Monday evening.

WWE Star Addresses Challenges Of Not Having Wrestling Background

---

Approaching her fifth year since taking part in a WWE tryout, Maxxine Dupri has seen an early main roster call-up and subsequent championship reign that has catapulted her to success. In reflecting on her journey, Dupri has addressed the challenges of joining the company with no background in professional wrestling, discussing her sense of imposter syndrome and feelings of not quite being prepared at the point of her call-up.

Speaking on the Reality of Wrestling The Collection podcast, Dupri reflected on the skills she was able to bring across as a dancer, cheerleader and model. She emphasized that performance, acting, and character work have always been her strengths, noting: "I think for me, the performance, the acting, the playing a character, that's my bread and butter. I love that, and that's what I think I've always been good at growing up."

However, Dupri highlighted the significant challenge of learning professional wrestling as a physical art form. "And then the part that's so challenging is learning this new art that's physical, and you need it to feel real, and you need it to feel raw and not like a dance, not planned, not choreographed," she explained. She emphasized the importance of authenticity, saying: "You want somebody to sit in the front row and be shocked to their core at every step and turn. And I think that's the part that's such an art form that takes so long to master."

Dupri offered practical advice for aspiring wrestlers without a background in the industry, suggesting that watching wrestling product helps develop understanding and intuition. "And I do believe the more you watch it, the more it makes sense in your head," she said. "It's like the more product you watch, the more you're like, 'Ah yes, I feel that.' And then you relate to it, and then it's easier to apply to yourself. But it is challenging when you first start, and you come into this world, and you didn't grow up in it, and you're like, 'Wait, where do I even begin?'"

Dupri was recruited through the Las Vegas tryouts in 2021, becoming one of a number of candidates from non-wrestling backgrounds. The company's recruitment efforts at the time noted that prospects "ranged from former football and basketball players to gymnasts and bodybuilders," demonstrating WWE's commitment to casting a wide net.

Attracting talent from beyond the wrestling world is not unusual for WWE, with the search for future stars being as much about having a bankable presence as it is having in-ring ability. The logic behind this approach is sound: abilities that can be fostered or taught, especially through a facility such as the WWE Performance Center, while the less tangible soft skills – charisma, confidence or presence – are things someone either has or doesn't.

WWE operates a multi-pronged development system designed to identify and cultivate talent from various sources. WWE Recruit brings in established names, the WWE ID program focuses on up-and-coming stars, and Next In Line (NIL) targets elite athletes. The initial tryout, contract, or NXT signing is just the first step in a wrestler's journey, with many not delivering on what was perhaps initially seen in early stages. However, the door doesn't necessarily close permanently for those who fall short, as long as future stars are willing to put in the necessary work to achieve their goals.
