TNA Wrestling New York Return

TNA Wrestling Returns To Albany, New York For Two Nights of Action

TNA Wrestling is gearing up for their return to New York with an upcoming back-to-back iMPACT taping in Albany. The company has announced live events scheduled for Wednesday and Thursday, July 1-2 at Broadview Center, with tickets going on sale this week.

The TNA shows are set for Wednesday and Thursday, July 1-2, to be broadcast as episodes of Thursday Night iMPACT on AMC and AMC+ in the U.S., Sportsnet 360 in Canada, and TNA+ worldwide. The July shows mark a return to Albany, which was the host city for TNA Bound For Glory 2022, where Josh Alexander defeated Eddie Edwards to retain the TNA World Championship and Jordynne Grace beat Masha Slamovich to keep hold of the TNA Knockouts World Championship.

The TNA ticket pre-sale in Albany begins Wednesday, April 22, at 10 a.m. ET. The public on sale begins Friday, April 24, at 10 a.m. ET. Fans can sign up for priority alerts at TNAWrestling.com to ensure they don't miss out on tickets.

Both nights in Albany will include post-show Meet & Greets, giving fans direct access to their favorite TNA stars for autographs, photos and one-on-one moments. Stars on the TNA roster scheduled to appear include wrestling legends Jeff and Matt Hardy, TNA World Champion Mike Santana, TNA X-Division Champion Leon Slater, TNA International Champion Mustafa Ali, plus Nic Nemeth, Ricky Sosa, Elijah, The Elegance Brand, Elayna Black, Tessa Blanchard, Rosemary, Eddie Edwards, Frankie Kazarian and Moose, among others.

To celebrate TNA's return to New York, the company highlighted three special TNA moments held in the state. On August 7, 2014, at the Hammerstein Ballroom in Manhattan, Bully Ray power-bombed then-TNA Wrestling President Dixie Carter through a table. The culmination of a year-long promise from Bully, which remains one of the most famous moments and loudest reactions in wrestling history. On September 9, 2023, at the Westchester County Center in White Plains, Chris Sabin defeated Lio Rush to capture the X-Division Championship for a record-setting 10th time as part of the 1,000th episode of TNA iMPACT. On April 23, 2022, at the Mid-Hudson Civic Center in Poughkeepsie, Josh Alexander defeated Moose to win the TNA World Championship in the main event of TNA Rebellion.

TNA Wrestling is one of the world's top professional wrestling brands and one of the fastest growing in the industry. The company produces over 200 hours of original content annually, televised in 200 countries and broadcast in the United States on AMC. Founded in 2002, TNA Wrestling's deep library of premium content is streamed globally on key OTT platforms including Pluto TV, Roku, Samsung TV Plus, and its own subscription service TNA+. With over 10 million engaged followers across social media and a rapidly expanding live event footprint, TNA Wrestling is entering a new era of growth.

CMLL Card Announcements and Talent Updates

CMLL has officially announced the full card for their Viernes Espectacular show on Friday, April 24th, 2026. The event marks the official 70th anniversary of Arena Mexico and will feature an exciting lineup of matches designed to celebrate this significant milestone.

The main event of the evening will showcase the finals of the Men's Campeonato Universal 2026 Tournament. Three competitors will vie for the championship: Hechicero, Mascara Dorada, and Black Tiger (Magnus). The Campeonato Universal is an annual Lucha Libre tournament that has taken place since 2009 and is traditionally billed as a "tournament of champions," with sixteen male CMLL Champions participating throughout qualifying matches. Ultimo Guerrero and El Terrible are recognized as the only two-time winners of the tournament to date. The 2026 edition will crown a first-time winner. Hechicero enters as the most recent champion on the participants list, having recently defeated Claudio Castagnoli for the CMLL World Heavyweight Championship at Homenaje a Dos Leyendas last month. Black Tiger currently holds one half of the Mexican National Tag Team Championships, while Mascara Dorada is the current World Historic Welterweight Champion.

The card features several colossal multi-participant matches spread throughout the evening. One standout bout will pit a CMLL vs Don Callis Family Copa Aniversario 70th Anniversary of Arena Mexico match, featuring Angel de Oro, Ultimo Guerrero, Neon, and Mistico against Volador Jr, El Clon, Rocky Romero, and Mark Davis. This match represents a special international collaboration for the anniversary celebration.

Additional matches on the card include a Trios Match featuring Dragon Legendario, Dragon de Fuego, and Dragon Rojo Jr taking on Los Villanos (Villano III Jr and Hijo del Villano III) and El Elemental. A Copa Aniversario 70th Anniversary of Arena Mexico Amazonas Edition match will showcase India Sioux, La Jarochita, Lluvia, and Tessa Blanchard competing against Zeuxis, Reyna Isis, Olympia, and Keyra. An International 8 Man Tag Match pitting Mexico vs Japan will see Esfinge, Barbaro Cavernario, Soberano Jr, and Templario face off against KUSHIDA, Yutani, Okumura, and Shoma Kato.

The event will take place at Arena Mexico on Friday, April 24th, 2026, with doors opening at 8:30 p.m. Tickets are available through Ticketmaster. The show will also be available for live streaming exclusively for members at the "Campeon Mundial" and "Leyenda" levels.

Jey Uso Wants One More Run As Roman Reigns’ Right-Hand Man in WWE

Jey Uso Wants One More Run As Roman Reigns' Right-Hand Man in WWE

Jey Uso may be firmly in his "YEET Man" era, but his latest comments suggest he hasn't completely closed the door on his past, especially after recent developments on WWE RAW following WrestleMania 42. Speaking in a recent interview, Uso opened up about revisiting a role that once defined The Bloodline's dominance. He hinted that the particular story thread might not be finished just yet.

During an interview with House of Solo, Uso made it clear that he's interested in stepping back into familiar territory, but with a new perspective shaped by his singles run. "I would like to give one more 'right hand man' run. One more run, after this YEET man run. It would be really special and feel really real. I think that is one version of me that I think I would like to give the WWE Universe," he stated.

This statement lands at an interesting time. On the April 20 episode of WWE RAW, the original Bloodline reunited, with Roman Reigns, now World Heavyweight Champion after WrestleMania 42, standing alongside both Usos. However, the dynamic appears different this time, with Reigns stressing unity rather than hierarchy. This shift in approach could potentially pave the way for a different kind of "right-hand man" storyline should Uso decide to pursue that direction.

Uso's journey to this point has been anything but a straight line, more of a zigzag than a spear. Reflecting on his evolution, he highlighted how family and independence have shaped his career. "Family has always influenced me regardless of my career. Family first, always. Before money, before fame, all you have left is your relationships with your family," he emphasized.

He also pointed to his singles breakout as a turning point in his career trajectory. "I feel like we made OUR legacy when I debuted with my brother. The truth is, I started a new legacy in 2023-2024 when I started to step out on my own and built the 'Yeet Man,'" Uso explained.

With The Bloodline back together and new challengers like Jacob Fatu emerging within the faction, Uso's potential return to his "right-hand man" role could add another layer to an already shifting dynamic. This could prove that in WWE, some roles never truly get future endeavored, and character arcs can be revisited and reinvented depending on timing and creative direction. For now, Uso seems content with his current run while leaving the door open for what could be a meaningful return to one of his most impactful roles.

Drew McIntyre Calls Out Fans Over WWE WrestleMania Incident

Drew McIntyre has spoken out about fan behavior during WrestleMania 42 week, echoing growing concerns raised by multiple WWE talents regarding security and fan interactions.

Taking to social media, McIntyre drew a clear distinction between respectful fan interactions and behavior that crossed personal boundaries. He expressed appreciation for fans who engaged appropriately, saying "For those fans who spent hard earned money on a chance to say hello, take a photo, have something signed, I appreciate each and every single one of you." He also mentioned fans giving him one of his many bracelets as part of positive interactions.

However, McIntyre detailed several troubling experiences involving fans at the talent hotel. "To those who rushed my family and I in the hotel, kept your kids up till 2am throwing them in front of us, or shoved a camera in my face without asking, please don't do that ever again," he stated. McIntyre added that the situations could have escalated further under different circumstances, noting "You're lucky I'm a calm natured person outside the ring."

His comments align with multiple reports from WrestleMania week suggesting a lack of adequate security at the hotel, where talent were reportedly accessible in public areas without additional protective measures. Several WWE figures have described similar incidents, including fans crowding lobbies, following talent, and attempting to film them without consent.

The incidents raise serious questions about talent safety and fan access during major wrestling events. The pattern of behavior described by McIntyre and other WWE talents suggests systemic security gaps that allowed fans to approach wrestlers and their families in unsupervised settings without proper boundaries or consent protocols.

As of now, WWE has not publicly addressed the concerns raised regarding security during WrestleMania 42 week. The company has not issued any statements about implementing additional protective measures or reviewing their hotel security protocols for future events.

AEW's Jeff Jarrett Assesses Opportunities From WWE's Saudi WrestleMania Weekend

WWE has announced plans to hold WrestleMania 42 in Saudi Arabia in 2027, despite ongoing concerns about the region's geopolitical situation and Saudi Arabia's human rights record. This decision has raised questions about what will happen with WrestleMania weekend, as most independent promotions are not expected to travel to Saudi Arabia to run shows during that time.

The situation has created speculation that a major alternative event could take place in the United States during WrestleMania weekend 2027. This alternate event could potentially be run by AEW, another US promotion, or a collaboration of multiple promotions, with smaller wrestling companies converging around it to create a counterpoint to WWE's international WrestleMania.

During an appearance on "My World with Jeff Jarrett," the AEW executive discussed the potential for such an event. Jarrett acknowledged that a major US alternative to WrestleMania could indeed happen and would be significant if it did occur. He expressed that if WWE does hold WrestleMania in Saudi Arabia, the entire independent wrestling industry would have major opportunities available.

"When you run something for the first time, it's always big and new," Jarrett said on "My World with Jeff Jarrett." "If Mania really goes to Saudi, which I think...it's so disconnected. It's not like it's London or Toronto or something like that. I just think the opportunity's wide open for the entire industry. Like, if you're not affiliated with WWE, it's going to be wide open. So yeah, I do...But I also think, if I'm a betting man, they [WWE] don't go to Saudi next year."

Jarrett expressed skepticism about whether WWE will ultimately proceed with the Saudi Arabia plan, noting that the location feels disconnected from traditional WrestleMania destinations like London or Toronto. Whether an alternative US event materializes, according to Jarrett, will largely depend on whether WWE actually goes through with holding WrestleMania in Saudi Arabia in 2027.
