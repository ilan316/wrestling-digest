🔥 Tony Khan addresses New Day interest and WWE speculation on AEW TV deal

Tony Khan addressed two major storylines during the post-show media scrum following ROH Supercard of Honor, directly confronting WWE speculation while carefully navigating questions about potential talent acquisitions.

Khan responded to reports from the Wrestling Observer Newsletter claiming that people within WWE have been spreading doubts about AEW's ability to secure another major television deal once its current Paramount agreement expires at the end of 2027. The reports suggested that some WWE officials at the highest levels have privately discussed the belief that AEW will not land another significant media rights contract, particularly following the Paramount/Skydance merger finalization.

"I saw a report today saying somebody in WWE is telling people within WWE talking about AEW's next TV deal and talking about what we're doing. And I was surprised by that and I'll say what they said could not be further from the truth and they're entitled to their opinions," Khan stated. "I've been trying to be respectful to them and try to conduct myself as a gentleman and then for them to go on background and spread these things…I'll just turn the other cheek, and maybe a couple of years ago I wouldn't have done it like that."

The WWE speculation appears to mirror similar predictions made before AEW's previous media rights negotiations, which ultimately resulted in the company securing a significant increase on its last agreement. Despite the backdrop chatter, AEW continues to perform strongly in key demographics and generates solid pay-per-view revenue, while remaining considerably less expensive than UFC from a television rights perspective.

On the talent acquisition front, Khan was asked directly about the possibility of signing Kofi Kingston and Xavier Woods, who officially confirmed their WWE departures on May 4. The former New Day members are expected to use the names Kofi and Austin Creed going forward outside of WWE.

Khan took a cautious legal approach to the question, declining to comment on wrestlers with ongoing contractual obligations to WWE. "I cannot comment, or I don't think I should comment on wrestlers that still have a contractual obligation. As I understand it, I believe they are still obligated, and I don't believe I'm allowed to talk to them," Khan explained. "As such, I don't feel it would be right to comment on them under the circumstances except to say that I think they are great wrestlers."

Wrestling has always been larger than life. With colorful characters, dramatic rivalries, and exaggerated storytelling, it naturally translates well into gaming. Below is a look at the most popular wrestling-themed game types and why they continue to resonate with fans.

Console Wrestling Games: The Cornerstone of the Genre

Console wrestling games remain the most recognizable wrestling-themed titles. These games focus on recreating the in-ring experience with varying levels of realism. Why they remain popular: Roster-based gameplay tied to well-known personas, signature moves and dramatic finishing sequences, career and story modes centered on rivalries, and local and online multiplayer options. These games appeal to fans who enjoy immersion and extended play sessions, offering deep mechanics and presentation designed to mirror televised wrestling.

Arcade-Style Wrestling Games

Arcade wrestling games strip away realism in favour of fast, exaggerated action. They often feature simplified controls, colorful visuals, and exaggerated physics. Key appeals include: Easy-to-pick-up gameplay, short energetic matches, focus on spectacle rather than simulation, and suitability for casual or group play. These games are popular with players who prioritize fun and accessibility over authenticity.

Mobile Wrestling Games

Mobile games have helped wrestling reach new audiences. Designed for touch screens and short sessions, mobile wrestling games often simplify mechanics while retaining character identity. Common features include: One-tap controls, quick matches or challenges, progression through collectibles or upgrades, and regular content updates. Mobile wrestling games suit players who enjoy brief, portable gaming experiences.

Fantasy and Custom Wrestling Games

Some wrestling-themed games move beyond real-world inspiration and dive into fantasy. These titles introduce fictional characters, supernatural arenas, or exaggerated powers. Why fantasy wrestling works: Creative freedom beyond realism, visual variety, appeal to non-hardcore wrestling fans, and emphasis on storytelling. Fantasy settings often attract players who enjoy creativity more than sport simulation.

Manager and Booking-Style Wrestling Games

Not all wrestling fans want to play in the ring. Management-focused wrestling games allow players to book events, manage talent, and build fictional promotions. Popular mechanics include: Storyline creation, roster management, event planning and scheduling, and reputation and popularity systems. These games appeal to fans interested in wrestling's behind-the-scenes side.

Retro Wrestling Games

Older wrestling games continue to maintain cult followings. Retro-style titles focus on simplified mechanics, pixel art, and nostalgic design. Why retro wrestling remains popular: Simple controls, focus on character identity, high replay value, and nostalgia for earlier gaming eras. These games are often revisited by long-time fans and discovered by new audiences.

Wrestling-Themed Casual and Party Games

Wrestling themes have also been adapted into party-style games. These titles focus on mini-games, quick challenges, and social interaction rather than full matches. They often include: Competitive mini-rounds, local multiplayer, humorous takes on wrestling tropes, and broad appeal beyond wrestling fans. Party games help extend wrestling's reach into mainstream gaming communities.
