🔥 Undertaker AAA Mask vs Mask Match

The rivalry between the two versions of El Grande Americano is officially set to culminate on May 30 at AAA Noche De Los Grandes, with The Undertaker confirming the mask vs. mask match through social media on April 14, 2026. The Undertaker, serving as an executive producer for AAA, announced the bout would headline the event taking place at Arena Monterrey in Monterrey, Nuevo Leon, Mexico. In his announcement, Undertaker wrote "Circle the date on your calendars! Mask vs. Mask will take place at AAA Noche De Los Grandes on May 30th at Arena Monterrey. This will be intense!"

The feud between both WWE stars wearing the El Grande Americano mask has been building for nearly a year. Chad Gable, the original character bearer, was forced out of action in 2025 due to a shoulder injury, prompting Ludwig Kaiser to take over the gimmick. Kaiser not only continued the character but expanded it significantly, introducing sidekicks Bravo Americano (Tyler Bate) and Rayo Americano (Pete Dunne) as part of the Los Americanos stable. The feud began properly when Gable returned at the 2026 Royal Rumble, leading to multiple confrontations between the two Americanos in both AAA and WWE rings. The two squared off in a singles match on the March 16 episode of WWE Raw, with Kaiser's version of Americano defeating the original.

To build toward the mask vs. mask challenge, Kaiser and Gable had an intense brawl in Mexico where Gable threatened AAA broadcast team member Andrea Bazarte, who is Kaiser's girlfriend. Kaiser made the save and laid down the mask vs. mask challenge, setting up this climactic encounter. This will mark the first time in either Americano's career that they will compete in a Lucha de Apuesta match, adding significant stakes to the competition.

The match represents a major moment for AAA since WWE purchased the promotion one year ago, with the announcement arriving just before WrestleMania 42 weekend. WWE has been building up AAA with major matches, and this mask vs. mask encounter will be the first major Apuesta match in AAA since the purchase. The event will air as a full live special rather than being broken up into one-hour blocks, demonstrating WWE's commitment to making this a significant wrestling event.

El Grande Americano is a character that transcended what many believed should have worked in professional wrestling. What began as a potentially temporary gimmick for Gable became a successful long-term character when Kaiser took it over. Kaiser's commitment to the character, including speaking Spanish in promos and showing respect to luchador traditions, helped the character resonate with AAA's Mexican fanbase. The addition of Bravo and Rayo to Los Americanos brought humor and depth to the group, with the team showcasing lighter moments and surprising proficiency with the Spanish language on social media. With one version of El Grande Americano set to lose their mask at the May 30 event, the future for both Kaiser and Gable in WWE remains to be determined, but this character will likely be remembered fondly by fans for years to come.

🔥 Will Ospreay United Empire Trademark

Will Ospreay has submitted a trademark application for the term "United Empire," the name of his professional wrestling faction from New Japan Pro Wrestling. The filing was made on April 10 with the United States Patent and Trademark Office, with the application covering two separate categories of goods and services.

The first category covers merchandise including shirts, sweatshirts, hooded sweatshirts, hats, bandanas, and socks. The second category encompasses a wide range of entertainment and information services: providing wrestling news and information via a global computer network; entertainment services, namely, wrestling exhibits and performances by a professional wrestler and entertainer; entertainment in the nature of wrestling contests; televised appearances by a professional wrestler and sports entertainer; personal appearances by a professional wrestler and sports entertainer; live appearances by a professional wrestler and sports entertainer; and providing online interviews featuring professional wrestlers and sports entertainers in the field of professional wrestling and sports entertainment for entertainment purposes.

According to the filing details, the "First Use Anywhere" and "First Use in Commerce" for the term United Empire were both noted as December 2019, indicating the faction's origins predated its official NJPW debut in October 2020.

United Empire was founded by Ospreay in October 2020 and has remained a staple of NJPW programming. Following Ospreay's departure to AEW, the faction has been led by Callum Newman, who recently achieved historic status by defeating Yota Tsuji to become the youngest IWGP Heavyweight Champion in history at just 23 years old. The faction's current membership includes Newman, HENARE, Francesco Akira, and Great-O-Khan.

The faction recently crossed over into AEW programming when Newman, Akira, and HENARE appeared on AEW Dynamite on April 8. During the broadcast, they assisted Ospreay and Alex Windsor in a backstage confrontation against the Death Riders faction, which consists of Jon Moxley, Claudio Castagnoli, Wheeler Yuta, PAC, Daniel Garcia, and Marina Shafir. The United Empire members went on to achieve victory as part of a Chaos in Canada match that evening.

Ospreay is no longer a full-time NJPW performer but has indicated his intention to return to Japan more frequently following his recovery from neck surgery. The trademark filing does not prevent United Empire from continuing to operate under that name in NJPW, as Ospreay has demonstrated continued respect for his former home and its roster. Rather, securing the trademark represents a business-savvy move that protects his intellectual property while allowing the faction to grow in prominence, particularly outside of Japan through his standing in AEW and the close working relationship between AEW and NJPW.

The filing highlights Ospreay's business acumen in professional wrestling, as holding the trademark himself provides him with control over how United Empire is commercialized and used across different platforms and jurisdictions. While the ultimate implications of the trademark filing for the faction's future direction remain to be seen, it positions Ospreay to potentially leverage United Empire's brand across multiple wrestling promotions and merchandise opportunities.

Vampiro Retirement Match

Vampiro isn't going out exactly the way he wants to, but he's still making the best attempt to go out on his own terms. The legendary wrestler previously did a retirement tour in Mexico across multiple events in 2024, structured that way because he didn't think one show was enough to say goodbye to fans. Outside of his time in WCW, Vampiro didn't spend much time working in the United States. However, he recently found a home with Juggalo Championship Wrestling, which is giving him the chance to say goodbye on his own terms.

Vampiro praised JCW owner and ICP founder Violent J for his lifelong passion for pro wrestling, sharing that he's also excited to support his friend's vision. "So, more than it being Vamp's last match, it's that I'm part of something that's so awesome. It's so big. And if you're a wrestling fan, come on out and see what is authentically a true grassroots movement for people who really care about wrestling, not so much the business. The business, of course, we all want to make it," he noted. "But just to be a part of something so real, that's the biggest reason for me."

Vampiro compared the experience of a JCW event to that of a Rocky Horror Picture Show screening. The audience participation is key, and you're starting to see the crowds get bigger and bigger now that JCW is a full-time promotion. "When we first started doing JCW and trying to make it a more permanent thing, we would do some dual touring together and try to put it out there and make it work. The fans would put up with it. Twenty people would come. We'd have a few stragglers here and there. Now, there's a waiting line to get in," he explained. "The Rocky Horror Picture Show was a really big movie in the '70s, and the audience participated with what was on the screen. So everybody became a hardcore fan for life. That is what JCW is like," he continued. "We never went away. We have been around for over 30 years. We've gone up against everybody. We've had everybody come through our doors, gone through everybody else's doors. We put our time in. And all of that is because of Joe Bruce, Violent J's dream."

Vampiro's final match is a triple threat match against PCO and Big Vito. He'll have ICP in his corner, but Vampiro also has to contend with some of the health issues that have plagued him in recent years. Vampiro was diagnosed with early-onset Alzheimer's disease in 2019, and he's also dealt with a broken back, a brain tumor, and stomach cancer, among other issues.

Vampiro says his doctors and family are not in favor of him competing again, and he knows he probably shouldn't, either. "Oh yeah. My partner, my girlfriend, she's not happy about it. My daughter is 100% against it. I am having to lie to them, you know. 'I'm not going to do anything. I'm not going to get hurt. Don't worry.' All that kind of stuff," he joked. "My doctors are like, there's no way, because I have nerve damage, right? The last time I broke my neck, my hand doesn't really work. I can't really walk correctly. My brain and my lower back and my legs are not connected. There's a pause. Sometimes I want to take a step and I can't, and I fall. And then, of course, there's the broken back, the broken neck, the knee damage, the 26 or so concussions. Should I be doing this? Absolutely not. But what am I supposed to do?"

Vampiro is feeling very emotional going into the match. He's disappointed about his physical health, noting how he needed help getting in the ring due to nerve issues during his last retirement tour. Vampiro says he wants to wrestle, and more specifically, doesn't want to stop or grow old. He enjoys his life as a wrestler and is determined to end his storied career on his own terms.

Police Close Case in Wild Berwyn Eagles Club Incident, Confirm No Stabbing Took Place

A bizarre and heavily discussed wrestling incident from earlier this year has now reached its conclusion, with police confirming there was no stabbing at the Berwyn Eagles Club in March. According to a report obtained via a Freedom of Information request, the Berwyn Police Department has officially closed the case after determining the alleged stabbing did not take place. The situation stemmed from a March 28 Ruthless Pro Wrestling event at the venue, where early reports claimed a fan had stabbed wrestler Krule, also known as Mads Krule Krugger.

However, that narrative quickly unraveled as additional footage surfaced. The first video to emerge saw wrestlers and fans attacking the fan and removing him from the building. Subsequent clips revealed the fan approaching Krule at his merchandise table after the show, and Krule throwing the first punches before the fan was swarmed. The video did not show any sign of a weapon.

Inside the police report, Detective William Massuci of the Berwyn Police Department noted that he spoke with an RPW manager whose name was redacted in the filing. The March 28 event was the final show under Chris Kohlenberg's leadership, with the promotion now being run by Rickey Shane Page, SHLAK, and Dred.

The unnamed manager stated the fan was causing problems early in the night and was intoxicated and warned by management to calm down or would be removed. The situation escalated after the show concluded, according to the manager's statement included in the report. The fan agreed to calm down but approached Krule after the show and started causing more issues. The manager states that the fan ended up attacking his wrestler and tackled him to the ground. The fan continued to punch the wrestler several times. Then the rest of the wrestlers and employees came in to help and got the fan off of him and escorted the fan out of the building.

The report also directly addressed the most serious allegation. The unnamed manager stated that the individual in question was not stabbed in reference to Krule, who isn't listed by his real name or ring name in the redacted portion of the document. Krule was later contacted at the airport while traveling home, where he provided his own account of the incident to Detective Massuci. Some guy was causing problems all night and came up to him at the end of the night and struck him several times and tackled him to the ground. His peers then got the fan off of him and took matters into their own hands.

Ultimately, authorities concluded there was no stabbing and no criminal case to pursue. The Berwyn Police Department has closed the case, stating there was no stabbing and that the wrestler doesn't want to pursue any criminal complaints.

In the aftermath, the Berwyn Eagles Club announced it will no longer host professional wrestling events due to licensing issues, with its final scheduled show being an AAW Wrestling event on April 24. A GoFundMe campaign has since been launched in an effort to help keep the venue operational.

Bully Ray isn't pleased with TNA Wrestling for pulling Nic Nemeth out of his match with MJF, and says he's legally allowed to wrestle the AEW star.

Unless Nic's TNA contract specifically says you are in no way, shape and form allowed to be on the same show as, be in the same ring as, work with in any way shape or form AEW contractual talent, unless that very specific wording is in the independent contractor's contract, he can go do whatever he wants and wrestle whoever he wants, declared Ray on Busted Open. Because right off the bat, they understand the scope of what they're dealing with. Now it'll come down to nuance and gray area and the actual, the verbiage.

Ray urged TNA and its president, Carlos Silva, who many assume decided to bar TNA stars from wrestling against and on the same card as AEW wrestlers, to let the match between MJF and Nemeth go ahead, but be clearer with restrictions in the future.

If the perception is that Carlos Silva is to blame, and if the reality is that Carlos Silva is to blame, if I'm Carlos and I'm taking all this backlash, I'm just saying, you know what, let the match happen. For next time, we're going to be a little bit more careful about it, he added.

CM Punk Admits He Didn’t Miss Wrestling After Leaving WWE

CM Punk has opened up about just how disconnected he truly felt from wrestling after his departure from WWE, revealing a level of detachment that surprised many in the industry. While speaking on the All The Smoke podcast, Punk explained that after leaving WWE, he didn't immediately feel the urge to return to the business. In fact, he said wrestling barely crossed his mind during that time away.

According to Punk, he was completely out of sight, out of mind with wrestling. He didn't watch wrestling, didn't pay attention to wrestling, and didn't really have any itch to get back involved. The first time he really paid attention to wrestling again was when his wife was wrestling at WrestleMania, and he wanted to watch her match. That was probably the first time he tuned back in after he left WWE.

Punk's exit from WWE in early 2014 turned into one of the most talked-about departures in modern wrestling history. He walked out following months of frustration with injuries, creative direction, and his place on the roster. Not long after leaving, he shifted his focus to mixed martial arts and signed with Ultimate Fighting Championship, stepping into the Octagon for two professional fights before eventually moving on from that chapter.

Years later, Punk shocked fans again when he returned to wrestling in 2021, this time signing with All Elite Wrestling. His debut quickly became one of the most talked-about moments in the industry. However, his run there was filled with injuries and backstage controversy that ultimately led to his departure from that company as well.

After nearly a decade away from WWE, Punk completed the full-circle moment in late 2023 when he made a surprise return at WWE Survivor Series in Chicago. That comeback instantly reshaped the WWE landscape and set the stage for the WrestleMania run he's currently on.

Punk's admission sheds light on how far removed he really was during his hiatus. Instead of staying connected to the business or watching from afar, he stepped away entirely until a personal reason finally pulled him back in. It also makes his eventual return feel even more significant. Rather than chasing unfinished business or revenge storylines, Punk's comeback came after years of genuine distance from the industry he once walked away from.
