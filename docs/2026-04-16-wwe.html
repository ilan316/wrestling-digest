THE STATE OF WRESTLEMANIA 42: TWO CHAMPIONSHIP STORIES, TWO VERY DIFFERENT RECEPTIONS

WrestleMania 42 will showcase two championship main events on consecutive nights in Las Vegas, but they could not be more different in terms of fan reception and creative execution. The contrast between these feuds tells a story about wrestling storytelling, corporate interference, and what happens when one rivalry is allowed to develop organically versus another that becomes bogged down by outside interests.

THE PUNK VERSUS REIGNS FEUD: PERSONAL, PSYCHOLOGICAL, AND EARNED

The World Heavyweight Championship match between CM Punk and Roman Reigns on Night 2 has become one of the most personal and layered feuds heading into the event. Punk has spent months building this rivalry with surgical precision, focusing on philosophy, consistency, and the very nature of what it means to be a professional wrestler in the modern era.

Punk has consistently attacked Reigns' part-time status, arguing that being a top WWE star carries responsibilities that Reigns is not fulfilling. In interviews with Corey Graves, Punk pointed out that other top stars like Seth Rollins and Cody Rhodes balance family life with consistent WWE appearances, questioning why Reigns cannot do the same. Punk framed this not as a personal attack but as a debate between "pro wrestling versus cinema," suggesting that Reigns treats his WWE role as something to dip into rather than commit to fully.

Beyond schedule complaints, Punk has questioned whether Reigns has ever accomplished anything truly on his own. Punk recalled his early interactions with the members of The Shield, explaining that when he first spoke with them, he told them to forget everything they had done up until that point because nobody knew who they were. Punk said Reigns' first goal was to provide for his daughter, which Punk respected. However, Punk has since pointed out that from The Shield to the Big Dog to the Tribal Chief, Reigns has been surrounded by management support, office backing, and family connections. According to Punk, Reigns is "somebody else's idea that had to get pushed through and cross the goal line and score."

Punk has also suggested that Reigns is driven by ego and envy, describing how Roman "moves like he's Genghis Khan" and needs not just to win but to see everybody else lose. Yet Punk acknowledged that this mentality likely contributed to Reigns reaching his current level, suggesting it is a strength rather than a weakness.

The psychological warfare escalated when Punk referenced Reigns' late father, Sika Anoa'i, during a promo. Punk later admitted in interviews that he crossed the line with this moment, describing it as "low-hanging fruit" and saying he "legitimately feel a little bit gross about it." However, Punk explained that this is his method, calling himself a "habitual line stepper" who will get into someone's head. He acknowledged this was intentional, part of his game plan to gain a psychological edge before WrestleMania.

When Reigns claimed that he had influence in Punk's WWE return and could have blocked it, Punk responded by suggesting this was either laughable or concerning, depending on interpretation. Punk joked that if Reigns really had that power, he should have said no, as it would have made things easier for him heading into WrestleMania.

Throughout this feud, both men have acknowledged envy and ego as driving forces. Punk pointed out his own ego while describing Reigns' dominance-driven mentality. The rivalry has become deeply rooted in their history, Punk's perception of Reigns' privilege as a second-generation star, and fundamental differences in their philosophies about wrestling versus entertainment.

THE RHODES VERSUS ORTON FEUD: A BEAUTIFUL STORY INTERRUPTED

In stark contrast, the Undisputed WWE Championship match between Cody Rhodes and Randy Orton should have been one of the most anticipated main events in WrestleMania history. These two had a long, layered history spanning decades, and their reunion as adversaries promised to deliver emotional payoff to years of storytelling.

However, the insertion of Pat McAfee as Randy Orton's mystery supporter in recent weeks has fundamentally altered the narrative in ways that have provoked unprecedented fan backlash. McAfee's reveal as the person who has been advising Orton and helping him in his rivalry with Rhodes came after weeks of mystery building. Initially, the idea was to add an unexpected wrinkle to the story. Instead, it appears to have derailed it entirely.

Cody Rhodes himself acknowledged the situation bluntly while promoting the match on the SI Media Podcast. Rhodes stated that this storyline was "the most ill-received thing in the history of wrestling," comparing it unfavorably even to infamous moments like the Gobbledygooker and the Shockmaster. Rhodes explained that the entire fanbase, across all demographics including independent wrestling fans, casual fans, cinema fans, and everyone in between, has received this development with confusion and disappointment. He used the phrase "Huh? What? Okay?" to describe universal fan sentiment.

Notably, Rhodes was diplomatic about McAfee himself, acknowledging that the ESPN personality is a huge star, arguably the face of the network, a great athlete, and someone who genuinely loves wrestling and is trying to help the industry. However, Rhodes was clear that none of this changes the fact that fans did not want to see this angle inserted into his match with Orton.

When asked whether he could have pushed back against the McAfee insertion, Rhodes gave a notably evasive answer, saying "that's an interesting question" and leaving it at that. This vagueness suggested either that he was not given much choice in the matter or preferred not to directly address the decision-making process publicly.

Industry veteran Bully Ray echoed these concerns on Busted Open Radio, criticizing that the Rhodes-Orton storyline was "tampered with for no good reason." Ray found it particularly odd that WWE and TKO leadership chose to heavily intervene in this specific feud while leaving other main event programs untouched. He pointed out the irony that the CM Punk versus Roman Reigns match, which is also a major championship program, was allowed to develop organically, leading him to speculate that executives may not have had confidence in Rhodes versus Orton as a standalone main event.

Reports have suggested that the decision to insert McAfee came from TKO executive Ari Emanuel rather than WWE creative, implying that this was a corporate synergy decision rather than a creative one. This perception has damaged the storyline in the eyes of fans who can sense when wrestling is being used as a vehicle for outside interests rather than when compelling narratives are driving wrestling.

McAfee's own promos have contributed to the problem. His early commentary came across as dismissive of the product and the fanbase, with one segment that left him making a major announcement that WWE WrestleMania 42 tickets would be 25 percent off. This coupon offer after weeks of building mystery struck many as tone-deaf and corporate, undermining the dramatic stakes of the angle. The combination of McAfee being positioned above the current landscape while simultaneously begging fans to purchase discounted tickets sent mixed messaging that felt exploitative rather than entertaining.

THE STORYLINE STRUCTURE DIFFERENCE

What separates these two feuds dramatically is creative confidence and organic development. The Punk-Reigns match grew from their history, their contrasting philosophies, and personal grievances that built week by week. Even when Punk pushed boundaries, it felt like part of a calculated psychological strategy within the context of their rivalry.

The Rhodes-Orton program had decades of legitimate history to draw from. Their friendship that turned to tension, the respect between two generational wrestlers, the storyline of whether they could overcome their differences or would face off as adversaries all had deep roots. This was earned storytelling.

McAfee's insertion broke that narrative flow. Instead of the story being about Rhodes and Orton's relationship and competition, it became about McAfee's motives, McAfee's promos, and McAfee's presence. The dynamic shifted from wrestler versus wrestler to a situation where outside personalities were driving the action. For fans who had been waiting years for this specific match, this felt like a bait-and-switch.

WHAT'S AT STAKE: WRESTLEMANIA AS PAYOFF VERSUS PIVOT

Multiple voices in wrestling commentary have pointed out that WrestleMania serves a specific narrative function: it is where stories pay off, not where they pivot to new directions. WrestleMania is supposed to be the culmination of long-term storytelling, the moment where years of investment in character arcs and rivalries reach their climax.

The Punk-Reigns feud, despite its personal nature and boundary-pushing moments, fits this model. These two have a connection to WWE's recent history, real grievances, and philosophical differences that have been articulated clearly. Their match represents a clash of worldviews coming to a head on wrestling's biggest stage.

The Rhodes-Orton program should have fit this model perfectly, with two respected veterans with shared history settling matters in a way that would feel earned and satisfying. Instead, that narrative got complicated by external insertion.

CODY RHODES' PERSPECTIVE: OPTIMISM AMID CONTROVERSY

Despite the backlash, Rhodes has maintained an optimistic public stance. He has pointed out that Randy Orton is arguably the best he has ever been as a performer, with the crowd reacting to him very positively before his turn against Rhodes. Rhodes noted that Orton is the hottest he has ever been and potentially at his finest in-ring capabilities. Because of Orton's quality as a performer, Rhodes expressed confidence that the match itself will deliver strong in-ring work, regardless of the storyline complications surrounding it.

Rhodes also revealed that he has a plan regarding McAfee, comparing his situation to the Red Dead Redemption character Dutch Van Der Linde. He suggested that he is not going to keep getting kicked around and has something up his sleeves to address the McAfee situation at WrestleMania.

Additionally, Rhodes pointed out that he has experience handling difficult creative situations, comparing McAfee to his Stardust period or other instances early in his career when he was given less than ideal angles. He said he has always looked at such situations and asked himself how to make something out of them. Rhodes even suggested that dealing with unwanted creative elements is something John Cena had to handle for years and years, and that Rhodes sees himself as someone willing to step up to handle such challenges rather than running from them.

THE BROADER IMPLICATIONS

The contrast between these two title feuds at WrestleMania 42 raises questions about creative philosophy in modern WWE. The Punk-Reigns match suggests that when wrestlers are given freedom to develop feuds based on legitimate character differences and history, compelling and layered storylines emerge. The rivalry has personal stakes, philosophical debate, and psychological warfare that feels earned.

The Rhodes-Orton situation suggests what happens when corporate interests and outside personalities become inserted into main event storytelling. The fan reaction has been remarkably consistent across all demographics and online and offline audiences, with nearly universal criticism of the decision.

Whether the Rhodes-Orton match can overcome the storyline complications through in-ring excellence, as Cody suggested, remains to be seen. What is clear is that the wrestling community had a strong preference for how this match should have been built, and that preference was not respected in the final creative decisions made.

McAfee has maintained that if Orton loses at WrestleMania 42, he will never appear on WWE television again, which has led many observers to speculate that Orton is favored to win the championship. However, the reaction has been so negative that some wonder whether WWE might reconsider the direction. What seems certain is that the wrestling fanbase would prefer that the final nights of WrestleMania focus on the wrestlers involved rather than on celebrity guests or outside personalities stepping into major storyline roles.

🔥 WWE WrestleMania 42 card, matches and storylines

WWE WrestleMania 42 is set to take place this weekend at Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas, Nevada, marking the company's second consecutive year hosting the flagship event in Sin City. The two-night premium live event will feature some of the biggest matches and storylines in professional wrestling, though the road to the showcase of the immortals has not been without complications.

The card is headlined by several championship matches across both nights. Roman Reigns will defend his World Heavyweight Championship against CM Punk in a match that has become increasingly personal. The rivalry intensified when Punk invoked the memory of Roman's late father, drawing the ire not only of Reigns but also his family, The Usos. Despite The Usos' involvement in the feud, WWE appears intent on telling the story of Roman finally facing this challenge on his own, making their potential interference a significant talking point heading into the match.

Cody Rhodes will defend his Undisputed WWE Championship against Randy Orton on night one. Orton's recent heel turn included a brutal and bloody beatdown of Rhodes, and the veteran has aligned himself with ESPN personality Pat McAfee. This has become a point of contention among fans and observers, with Cody himself addressing the fan reaction as "the worst received thing in wrestling." McAfee and rapper Jelly Roll's involvement in the buildup has overshadowed much of the in-ring storytelling between the two competitors, raising concerns that their presence could negatively impact the match itself.

Finn Balor will face Dominik Mysterio in what has been billed as a highly personal encounter. Balor is bringing back his Demon persona for the first time in three years. In an appearance on ESPN SportsCenter, Balor described the character as something beyond words. He stated, "It's not something I can really describe in words. It's a feeling. It's an emotion. It's an energy. And it's something that I can only tap into very occasionally. It can be very draining." The rivalry dates back over a year to WrestleMania 41 when Mysterio pinned Balor to win the Intercontinental Championship. Balor has since signaled a shift in tone, indicating that "the time for talking is over" and it's time to show action. The Demon has lost its last two matches to Roman Reigns and Edge, meaning this bout is crucial for restoring the character's credibility. Rey Mysterio, Dominik's father, has expressed enthusiasm for the match and stated that Finn is "ready to whoop Dom's butt and, more importantly, to teach him a lesson."

Rey Mysterio himself will compete in an Intercontinental Championship ladder match. When speaking to ESPN SportsCenter, the WWE Hall of Famer expressed his excitement about being in a ladder match on the grandest stage of them all, despite the inherent risks involved. Rey noted that he has been Intercontinental Champion twice before and that "the prize is worth the risk."

Liv Morgan will challenge Women's World Champion Stephanie Vaquer despite recovering from a significant head injury. On the April 6 episode of Monday Night Raw, a backstage segment involving Vaquer and Roxanne Perez turned physical, resulting in Morgan sustaining a black eye, swelling, and a golf ball-sized hematoma. Both Morgan and Perez were placed in concussion protocol following the incident. Morgan appeared on ESPN's First Take on April 15 with visible injuries and stated, "You cannot fake this bruising, you cannot fake this swelling." Despite the setback, Morgan has continued to make media appearances and was even spotted at a San Antonio Spurs game, signaling steady recovery. Reports indicate there is optimism she will be medically cleared in time for her WrestleMania opportunity.

Oba Femi will make his WrestleMania debut against Brock Lesnar after accepting the Beast's open challenge. The rising star has emerged as a top contender in recent weeks, and many view this as a potential coronation moment for the Nigerian wrestler. In an appearance on CNN, Femi explained the meaning of his ring name, stating that "Oba" translates to "The King" while "Femi" means they desire or want someone. He explained, "So it's a way of saying, 'The one who's after the King's heart,' or, 'The one the King loves.' So, 'God loves me.'" Femi expressed his desire to showcase his Nigerian culture and heritage through his character development, noting that he wants to display the layers to his character beyond just being "a Nigerian guy." Many observers believe Femi should defeat Lesnar to continue his momentum as a rising star.

Sami Zayn will defend his United States Championship against Trick Williams, who has been feuding with the champion since the start of 2026 on Friday Night SmackDown. Williams was called up to the main roster and has clashed with Zayn on multiple occasions. After Zayn won the championship by defeating Carmelo Hayes on March 27, the match against Trick was announced. Many view this bout as a potential coronation for Williams to win his first singles title on the main roster.

The card has faced notable criticism regarding celebrity involvement. Grammy-nominated artist Joe Jonas will perform the American National Anthem before night two of WrestleMania. This follows previous celebrity additions including Pat McAfee's involvement with Randy Orton, rapper Lil Yachty's alignment with Trick Williams, and popular streamer iShowSpeed teaming with Logan Paul and Austin Theory against The Usos and LA Knight. Many wrestling fans have expressed frustration with what they perceive as an overemphasis on celebrity involvement at the expense of in-ring storytelling.

WWE head of creative Paul Levesque, known as Triple H, addressed the unpredictability of professional wrestling compared to other sports in an interview with Joe Tessitore. He explained that unlike sports teams with backup players, wrestling lacks substitutes for its major stars. "There is no backup Cody Rhodes. There is no backup Randy Orton. There's no backup Roman Reigns. There's no backup CM Punk," he stated. Levesque acknowledged the challenges inherent in the business, noting that injuries can occur even after perfect planning. He referenced advice from the late Dusty Rhodes, explaining that if you can achieve 70 percent of your creative vision on television, that should be considered a success. Despite these challenges, Levesque emphasized that WWE must remain ready for any variable.

Several potential NXT callups and debuts have been discussed for the event. Joe Hendry teased a main roster callup with an in-concert segment on NXT Revenge, and observers believe his debut at WrestleMania would be fitting after his appearance at WrestleMania 41. Jacy Jayne, who recently lost her NXT Women's Championship rematch, could receive the stamp of approval that comes with a WrestleMania debut.
