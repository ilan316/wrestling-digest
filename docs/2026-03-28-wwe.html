## Sami Zayn Captures United States Title from Carmelo Hayes Amid Major Fan Backlash

The road to WrestleMania 42 took an unexpected turn on the March 27, 2026 episode of WWE SmackDown when Sami Zayn defeated Carmelo Hayes to capture the WWE United States Championship. The victory came after interference from Trick Williams, who accidentally struck Hayes while attempting to attack Zayn, setting up the Helluva Kick finish that crowned Zayn as the new champion. Following his win, SmackDown General Manager Nick Aldis confirmed that Zayn will defend the United States Championship against Trick Williams at WrestleMania 42.

### The Build to the Match

Earlier in the night, tensions boiled over when Aldis informed Trick Williams that he had secured a WrestleMania 42 match. Sami Zayn, who overheard the conversation, immediately asked about his own status at the event. Aldis' response was deflating—there were currently no plans for Zayn at WrestleMania. A dejected Zayn walked away, but quickly returned after Williams taunted him about going to catering. The confrontation escalated when Zayn struck Williams and declared to Aldis that he would find a way onto the WrestleMania card.

Carmelo Hayes had been conducting open challenges for the United States Championship throughout recent weeks, continuing what had become a strong reign. When Zayn answered the challenge, Williams positioned himself at ringside to watch the proceedings, initially cheering on Hayes in hopes of seeing Zayn fail.

### The Match and Controversial Finish

The match itself proved to be highly competitive. Hayes controlled the early portions with his technical prowess and high-flying offense, but Zayn fought back with heavy strikes and impactful moves. Both competitors landed near-fall sequences throughout. Hayes connected with his finisher, The First 48, which appeared to secure victory, but Zayn managed to kick out. Zayn followed with a Blue Thunder Driver that Hayes also kicked out of, keeping the match alive.

The decisive moment came when Williams grabbed the United States Championship belt and threw it into the ring in front of Zayn. As the referee was distracted removing the title from the ring, Williams attempted to strike Zayn with a metal can from the apron. Zayn managed to dodge, but the shot struck Hayes instead. With Hayes stunned, Zayn capitalized by connecting with the Helluva Kick and securing the pinfall victory.

### Significant Fan Backlash

The decision to have Zayn defeat Hayes has generated substantial negative reaction from the wrestling community. On YouTube, the match finish video accumulated notably poor engagement metrics, with significantly more dislikes than likes. One video showed 12,200 dislikes compared to 7,800 likes, representing approximately 61% of viewers hitting the dislike button. Another version recorded 12,000 dislikes against 8,000 likes.

Fan comments reflected widespread disappointment, with viewers writing "So we just couldn't do Carmelo VS Trick at Mania my gosh man," "Poor Carmelo Hayes," "The 'underdog' strikes again," and "Ruthless Depression." The episode of SmackDown received an overall rating of just 1.74 on the Cagematch.net wrestling database.

### Former Champion Calls for Change

The backlash extended to former WWE stars. Bishop Dyer, formerly known as Baron Corbin and a former United States Champion himself, voiced his displeasure on social media. Dyer called for WWE to add Hayes to the WrestleMania 42 title match, suggesting it be converted into a Triple Threat.

"Make it a 3 way!! @Carmelo_WWE has carried that for months and has delivered!!" Dyer wrote.

He followed with another message expressing his belief that Hayes was treated unfairly: "Just my opinion but @Carmelo_WWE got screwed. He deserves a mania match!! Him and Ilja made that title matter again! Shit give me Ilja vs @_trickwilliams vs Melo at mania."

Dyer's perspective resonated with many fans online, who similarly argued that Hayes had done significant work to rebuild the credibility of the United States Championship through his lengthy reign and consistent open challenges.

### Hayes' Championship Reign

Carmelo Hayes' title reign had been notable for its activity and consistency. The March 27 match marked his ninth televised title defense and 15th overall. He had held the championship for 89 days after winning it from Ilja Dragunov on December 26, 2025. Hayes and Dragunov had been credited by fans and observers alike with elevating the prestige of the United States Championship during the preceding months.

### WrestleMania 42 Plans

With Zayn now confirmed as the United States Champion defending against Trick Williams at WrestleMania 42, the question of Hayes' status at the event remained uncertain. Several observers and fans suggested potential solutions, including adding Hayes to the title match to create a Triple Threat, or potentially including Ilja Dragunov as well given his prior involvement with the championship.

The match came exactly 22 days before WrestleMania 42 is scheduled to take place in Las Vegas. While Zayn had secured his spot on the card, the manner of his arrival—by defeating a champion who had worked extensively to build credibility for the title—has left a significant portion of the fanbase questioning WWE's booking decision on the road to the company's biggest annual event.

🔥 ESPN Lawsuit Against WWE PLE Marketing

---

A significant legal twist has emerged in the ongoing case surrounding WWE's Premium Live Events and ESPN's streaming platform. ESPN has officially stepped into the dispute by filing a motion to intervene in the class-action lawsuit that was originally filed in January 2026 in Connecticut, adding a new layer to an already complex legal matter.

According to reporting by Brandon Thurston, ESPN submitted a memorandum on Friday requesting permission from the court to intervene in the case. The lawsuit itself was brought by two consumers and centers around allegations of deceptive marketing, specifically claiming that WWE advertised that ESPN subscribers would receive access to premium live events at no additional cost—something the plaintiffs argue was not universally true.

The strategic placement of defendants appears deliberate. WWE was originally named as a defendant in the suit, while ESPN was notably not included. "Plaintiffs did not name ESPN as a defendant, apparently to get around the arbitration clause in the subscriber agreement," according to available reporting. That detail appears to be a key factor in ESPN's latest move, as the network is now pushing for the case to be moved out of public court and into private arbitration, citing the terms outlined in parent company Disney's subscriber agreement. If granted, that shift could significantly change how the case proceeds, potentially breaking up the class action into individual claims rather than maintaining it as a consolidated lawsuit.

The heart of the dispute lies in how the WWE-ESPN streaming deal was marketed and implemented. When the partnership was first announced, ESPN indicated that it expected to eventually secure agreements with all major cable and streaming providers, with the goal of allowing consumers to access ESPN Unlimited without paying anything extra. However, that rollout remains incomplete. As of now, subscribers who do not have a participating provider still need to pay $30 per month for ESPN Unlimited, which sits at the center of the plaintiffs' argument regarding deceptive marketing practices.

The lawsuit aims to represent a specific group of consumers—those in the United States who were already ESPN subscribers between August 6 and the WWE Wrestlepalooza premium live event on September 20, as well as anyone who paid for the service during that window. There are, however, notable exceptions. Customers who subscribed through Hulu + Live TV, Spectrum, Verizon FIOS, DIRECTV, or FuboTV are excluded from the class, as those providers did offer access to the service at no additional cost during the timeframe in question.

In its motion to intervene, ESPN argues that the claims directly involve its platform and user agreements, making arbitration the appropriate forum for dispute resolution. The outcome of ESPN's motion could determine whether this dispute plays out in a public arena or stays locked behind legal curtains. WWE has until April 13, 2026, to formally respond to ESPN's complaint.

This legal battle carries major implications for how streaming access to WWE events is marketed and delivered in the future, especially if the case expands to include ESPN directly as a defendant. The motion to compel arbitration represents a significant strategic shift that could fundamentally alter the course of the litigation.
