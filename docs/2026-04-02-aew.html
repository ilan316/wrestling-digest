🔥 Chris Jericho Returns to AEW

After months of uncertainty regarding his professional future, Chris Jericho has returned to All Elite Wrestling. The legendary wrestler made his surprise appearance during the opening segment of AEW Dynamite on April 1, 2026, performing in his hometown of Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada. Jericho had been absent from AEW programming for nearly a year, having last appeared after dropping the ROH World Championship to Bandido at Dynasty the previous spring.

The return came as a shock to fans and industry observers alike, as rumors had been circulating since late 2025 that Jericho would be heading to WWE for what many believed would be his final run. Some within the wrestling industry had expected Jericho to appear at the Royal Rumble in January or at WrestleMania, but he did not materialize at either event. The surprise intensified when Jericho filed a trademark for the term "The Cornerstone" last week, fueling immediate speculation about an imminent return.

Jericho's entrance was orchestrated as an interruption during a contract signing segment between AEW World Champion MJF and number one contender Kenny Omega for their Dynasty match. As Tony Schiavone began introducing the signing, pyro began firing on the stage. When the camera cut to reveal the source, Jericho's iconic theme song "Judas" hit the speakers. The former AEW World Champion walked to the ring with a smile on his face, soaking in the crowd's enthusiastic reaction as fans sang along with his entrance music.

Once in the ring, Jericho kept his message brief. After allowing the crowd to continue celebrating his return for several minutes, he took the microphone and delivered just four words: "Winnipeg! AEW! I'm home." He then exited the ring, hugged fans at ringside, and made his way to the back before the contract signing could proceed. The segment was notable for its simplicity, with Jericho making no further attempts at explanation or character development.

According to Dave Meltzer's reporting on Wrestling Observer Radio, Jericho's decision to return to AEW rather than join WWE came after months of negotiations with both companies. Meltzer revealed that he had been tipped off to Jericho's AEW plans a few days before his appearance. When Meltzer checked with WWE sources to confirm whether Jericho was heading to the company, he received a clear response: "He is not coming to WWE now, any time soon, and maybe never."

The reasons behind Jericho's choice appear multifaceted. According to Meltzer, WWE was interested in signing Jericho for what the company described as a retirement tour. This proposition apparently did not appeal to the veteran wrestler. Meltzer stated, "I don't know what they told him. I know what I was told, and I was told that, as far as they wanted him for a retirement tour, okay? And maybe that's not what he wanted."

Additional insight came from Sean Ross Sapp, who reported that Jericho may have been reluctant to give up his outside projects, which WWE would have likely required him to relinquish. This factor, according to Sapp's sources, played a significant role in Jericho's decision, though it was not solely about financial compensation. Sapp noted that there was a week-long period where it seemed Jericho might be WWE-bound, but ultimately the decision swung back toward AEW.

Mike Johnson of PWInsider reported that the general belief backstage at Dynamite was that Jericho had come to terms with AEW on a new deal. Jericho was not present at the venue until moments before Dynamite went on the air. He was driven directly to the arena approximately two minutes before the show began broadcasting and went straight to the gorilla position for his ring entrance. After his return segment, Jericho filmed additional content for the company, participated in a photo shoot, and made the rounds backstage introducing himself to talents he did not previously know.

Regarding the creative reasoning behind staying with AEW, Meltzer offered perspective on the various factors that likely influenced Jericho's decision. He explained that while WWE would have provided a larger audience, AEW offered several advantages, including potentially more money and greater creative freedom. Meltzer suggested that at this stage of his career and life, Jericho was likely prioritizing creative fulfillment and enjoyment over other considerations. He noted that Jericho would have more creative input in AEW than he would have received in WWE, though WWE's larger viewership presented its own appeal.

Meltzer also addressed the security of Jericho's return, noting that by the afternoon of his appearance, news of his comeback had spread throughout the wrestling industry and even reached people within Warner Bros. Discovery, the parent company of AEW. The fact that the surprise was not completely contained to air time itself was somewhat unusual for such a significant event.

The physical appearance Jericho presented upon his return generated considerable positive commentary. Jonathan Coachman, a former WWE personality, praised the veteran wrestler's condition, stating that Jericho "looks fantastic" and "has abs. Looks younger. Looks refreshed." Coachman suggested that Jericho's physical transformation would be a major story the following day. This observation was consistent across multiple reports, with observers noting that Jericho appeared to be in the best shape of his recent years.

Regarding the crowd reaction, Meltzer offered a mixed assessment. While acknowledging that the crowd in Winnipeg, numbering over 4,500 people, was genuinely excited and gave Jericho a pop for the surprise, Meltzer suggested the reaction might not have been as loud as it would have been on a WWE program. He hypothesized that WWE's larger mainstream audience would have generated a more thunderous response. He also noted that Jericho's choice to enter to his classic "Judas" theme rather than a newer entrance song was fortuitous, as the familiar song generated more fan engagement than a new composition might have.

The timing of the return in Winnipeg held particular significance. As Jericho's hometown, the location provided an emotionally resonant backdrop for his reemergence. His father and family members were seated in the front row, adding a personal element to the moment. The city where he grew up provided the perfect setting for a return that emphasized homecoming and belonging.

Regarding future creative direction, multiple speculative pieces emerged about where Jericho might be headed. Potential storylines discussed include a feud with Kenny Omega, involvement in MJF's championship program, conflict with the Don Callis Family, programs with rising talent, or development of a new gimmick centered around "The Cornerstone" trademark he recently filed. However, at the time of his return, AEW had not specified what creative plans were in store for the veteran wrestler.

Jericho actively promoted his return through social media, posting behind-the-scenes footage of himself in the parking garage of the arena moments before his entrance, captioning it with "Alright, here I am in the parking garage of the MTS Centre in Winnipeg. It is 6:50 and in 10 minutes I am going to shock the world by returning to AEW. Join the ride." He also shared clips of his return segment and promoted new merchandise being released through AEW's shop.

The broader context of Jericho's absence came from his last on-air appearance nearly a year earlier. After losing the ROH World Championship to Bandido at Dynasty, Jericho appeared on the following Dynamite episode and told his stable, The Learning Tree, that he needed to take time away from the company. This absence lasted approximately twelve months before his surprise return, during which he did not appear on AEW television.

One critical observation came from wrestling journalists and fans regarding the overall impact of the segment. While the surprise itself was effective and the crowd reaction was positive, some felt the execution could have been stronger. The segment was notably brief, with Jericho offering minimal explanation or storyline development. This left viewers with more questions than answers about his motivations, his timeframe away, and what his return would mean for AEW's ongoing narratives.

The return does mark a significant moment for AEW as the company prepares for its Dynasty pay-per-view event. Jericho's presence adds another major star to the card and potentially opens multiple storytelling avenues moving forward, particularly given the ongoing championship programs and various feuds currently playing out on the program.

🔥 Kenny Omega AEW Ticket Prices and Commentary

Kenny Omega recently sat down with CBC News Manitoba to discuss AEW's approach to ticket pricing and how the promotion differentiates itself from WWE and independent wrestling organizations. The interview took place before AEW Dynamite in Winnipeg, a city with a strong wrestling history.

Omega reflected on Winnipeg's significance as a wrestling market, noting that WWE had regularly run shows there throughout his childhood with house shows, Monday Night Raw, and TV tapings. However, he also acknowledged that the city has hosted numerous independent promotions over the years, giving wrestling fans exposure to everything from small indie operations to large-scale productions.

This diverse wrestling landscape informed Omega's perspective on AEW's positioning. "We're sort of like the alternative," Omega explained. "We're not quite WWE and we're not an indie either. We're just AEW. What we try to be is the most fun live show that you can watch available to you."

The core of Omega's message centered on AEW's commitment to affordability. He emphasized that for family-friendly shows, ticket prices are genuinely accessible without compromising on content quality. "I do think that for family friendly shows, mostly family friendly, ticket prices are absolutely friendly. We never skimp on the content. You'll always get a ton of wrestling for your money value."

Omega highlighted the specific value proposition for Winnipeg fans, noting that the city was receiving a double taping of both AEW Dynamite and AEW Collision. "So you are getting a whole heck of a lot of AEW and you're not having to mortgage your first house or sell off your first child. That's not even a knock on any of the promotions or anything. It's just that if you want to check out a television product, see yourself on TV, have fun, see what makes AEW so special, you can do it. And you can do it at an affordable price."

Omega stressed that AEW's pricing philosophy extends to fans at all economic levels. "I'm just really glad that if there's any interest whatsoever, whether it be because you're a diehard AEW fan or you just want to go out and check out a night of wrestling and do something fun with the family or friends, I'm glad that there's price point options available for all walks of life. That's one thing I'm very proud of with AEW."

His comments arrive during a period of intense scrutiny over WWE's ticket pricing strategy. WWE has faced considerable criticism for high prices on major events, with reported decreases in WrestleMania ticket sales attributed in large part to cost concerns. Multiple wrestling industry figures, including AEW personnel like MJF, have previously highlighted AEW's lower pricing as a competitive advantage.

Omega's statements effectively position AEW as the more financially accessible alternative without directly attacking WWE's business model. Several sources noted that Omega went out of his way to clarify his remarks weren't meant as a knock against other promotions, instead framing them as a reflection of AEW's core values around accessibility and fan experience.

Beyond the pricing discussion, Omega touched on his recovery from diverticulitis, which sidelined him for much of 2024. He explained that accepting the possibility of transitioning to a backstage role actually helped him mentally approach his rehabilitation with greater focus and purpose. This acceptance of uncertainty paradoxically motivated him to pursue his in-ring return more effectively.

"I think a big part of it which helped me a lot on this journey was that I was at peace with being able to take a huge step back if I needed to," Omega said. "If I needed to have a completely non-physical role within the company and just be an office guy in the back that's never seen but is still contributing, I was happy to have just been involved with this project that is AEW."

Omega credited his recovery team at AEW, modern medical science, supplementation advances, and access to cutting-edge treatments for his successful comeback. He emphasized that the combination of support from AEW staff and contemporary health resources played crucial roles in his rehabilitation journey.

At the time of the interview, Omega was preparing for a major World Championship program against MJF at AEW Dynasty. The two had a contract signing on the April 1 edition of Dynamite that escalated into personal confrontation, with MJF making remarks about Omega's diverticulitis that prompted a heated table flip and the subsequent scheduling of an additional singles match with interference from other wrestlers.

The broader context of Omega's comments reflects an ongoing industry conversation about fan accessibility and pricing strategy. While WWE continues to charge premium prices justified by major production values and star power, AEW is leveraging affordability as a key differentiator in its marketing and positioning. Omega's enthusiastic endorsement of this approach from a major AEW star and former World Champion adds significant weight to the company's messaging on this issue.

Thunder Rosa Dual Contract Discussion

Thunder Rosa Says It Would Be An Honor To Have A Dual Contract With AEW And CMLL

Thunder Rosa has made it clear that she would welcome the opportunity to sign a dual contract with both AEW and CMLL. The dual contract arrangement has become an increasingly popular trend in professional wrestling, with wrestlers like Mistico and Persephone recently signing deals with AEW despite already maintaining contracts with CMLL.

When asked about the possibility during an interview with The Takedown on Sports Illustrated, Rosa responded enthusiastically without hesitation. "Really, you gonna ask me that question? Of course I'm interested in something like that," she said. "It's a big deal, it is such a big deal and it's such an honor when they recognize you to be somebody to be part of their organization. Absolutely, it would be an honor."

Should the opportunity materialize, Rosa would be following the recent precedent set by Claudio Castagnoli, who signed his dual contract with the promotion back in March. The move represents a growing trend of AEW talent expanding their reach into the Mexican wrestling scene.

Beyond the professional achievement, a CMLL contract would fulfill a personal dream that Rosa has harbored since the beginning of her wrestling career. "That's like one thing that I always, when I started wrestling, I always wanted to do is wrestle in Arena Mexico, to be part of some of the trainings in Arena Mexico, which I will survive when I do them. I will survive them all (laughs)," Rosa explained. "It's like pushing myself to the limit and it's like one of those things that I'm really enjoying the process because it's like pushing yourself in a different way."

Rosa has already begun her journey with CMLL, making her promotion debut at a cross-promotional event featuring AEW and ROH in 2025. She is scheduled to make her return at Arena Coliseo's 83rd Anniversary Show on April 4, where she will team with Garra Negra and La Jarochita against Zeuxis, Keyra, and Sanely in a six-woman tag team match.

Kyle O'Reilly Father Passing

Kyle O'Reilly recently shared the heartbreaking news that his father, Rick Greenwood, has passed away. The All Elite Wrestling star posted a lengthy and emotional tribute via Instagram to honor his late father's memory and celebrate his remarkable life.

According to O'Reilly's statement, Rick Greenwood passed away peacefully on March 27, 2026, surrounded by loved ones while listening to his favorite Pink Floyd album. Rick was born on Valentine's Day in 1952 and spent his childhood in Penticton, BC, where he grew up as a sickly child alongside six siblings. The family instilled strong values of hard work during his early years, as all the children had paper routes and worked in the cherry orchard.

As a teenager, Rick entered an apprenticeship program and became a master craftsman cabinet maker. His exceptional talent for woodworking was unparalleled, with an extraordinary ability to transform imagination into tangible creations using nothing but wood and his bare hands. Rick met his spouse and Kyle's mother, Janie McLoughlin, in 1975, and the couple would go on to build a life together. O'Reilly expressed comfort in knowing that his parents are now reunited beyond this earthly realm.

Rick became a father in 1982 when his daughter Kelli was born, followed by Kyle in 1987. He was a devoted father who ran his own business building staircases and handrails while also constructing the cribs and the house in which his children lived. His craftsmanship and dedication to his work were evident in every project he undertook.

Later in his career, Rick became a shipwright for BC Ferries, where he ran maintenance and refits on an entire fleet of ships. Even in this role, he added his perfected handiwork to the most unlikely places that went unnoticed by daily passengers. However, Rick ensured that every job was executed with meticulous attention to detail and artistic beauty. He was, in every sense, a true artist.

The legacy of Rick's work is visible throughout his home. From the dinner table where family memories were shared to fine china cabinets to the cedar strip canoes and kayaks he built with his brother Steve during Kyle's childhood, his fingerprints are everywhere. These tangible creations serve as lasting reminders of his skill, dedication, and love for his craft.

O'Reilly highlighted the greatest lesson his father taught him: "If you do something you love, you'll never work a day in your life." This philosophy deeply influenced Kyle, as his father worked relentlessly hard simply because he loved what he did. This inspiration gave Kyle the courage to pursue his own seemingly impossible dream in professional wrestling, driven by his passion for the sport.

In his tribute, O'Reilly expressed gratitude for the outpouring of support and messages from people who knew his father. Many shared stories about Rick being a kind and gentle soul. Kyle expressed pride in his father's character, noting that in a world full of malice and hatred, Rick always chose to be kind. "Be kind and create" appeared to be his father's life mantra, and Kyle honored this legacy in his final words to his father: "Love you forever, Dad."

O'Reilly's last in-ring competition in AEW took place at Full Gear 2025, where he competed in a No Holds Barred match against Jon Moxley. A month later, he appeared backstage at a December edition of Dynamite, confirming that he was dealing with a bulging neck disc injury and an arm fracture that had sidelined him from in-ring action. The wrestling community and fans extend their condolences to Kyle O'Reilly and his family during this difficult time.

Don Callis AEW Storylines

Don Callis continues to be a pivotal figure in AEW's world championship picture, serving as a powerful ally to MJF while simultaneously waging war against Kenny Omega. In recent comments to CBC News Manitoba, Callis reiterated his deep-seated animosity toward Omega, declaring his willingness to assist anyone in destroying the wrestler he views as fundamentally flawed.

"Now, Kenny Omega is once again looking like maybe the best wrestler in the world. I will give him credit, for the first time in a year, he looks like the old Kenny Omega. Now he's got his sights set on MJF. I'll say this, I will do anything I can to help MJF to destroy Kenny Omega. I would help anyone to destroy Kenny Omega because Kenny Omega is at the very bottom of the food chain from how I look at things," Callis stated. These comments come as Omega prepares to challenge MJF for the AEW World Championship at Dynasty, one of the year's biggest matches. With Callis firmly in MJF's corner and actively working to sabotage Omega, the champion appears to have a significant advantage heading into the encounter.

Beyond his involvement with the world title picture, Callis has also taken control of the pathway to championship contention. On AEW Dynamite, when Darby Allin emerged to demand his own title opportunity, Callis appeared alongside Andrade El Idolo, Lance Archer, El Clon, and Hechicero to deliver a message from MJF. According to Callis, MJF has granted Allin the chance to earn a title shot on one condition: he must defeat a hand-picked member of Callis' family at Dynasty.

That chosen opponent is Andrade El Idolo, marking the first singles encounter between the two wrestlers since Andrade's return to AEW. Their history is mixed, with Andrade defeating Allin in 2022 before losing to him in a Coffin Match. The matchup sets up an intriguing dynamic, as Andrade has made clear his own desires for a title opportunity while also expressing dissatisfaction with being forced to operate under MJF's umbrella.

The arrangement demonstrates how thoroughly Callis and MJF have structured the title picture around themselves. Rather than allowing challengers a direct path, they have created gatekeeping scenarios where aspirants must first overcome Callis' handpicked representatives. This strategy not only protects the championship but also elevates the profile of Callis' faction, making them indispensable to anyone seeking a shot at gold. As Dynasty approaches, the influence of Don Callis and his family continues to grow, positioning them as the true power brokers in AEW.

Kyle Fletcher AEW Apology

Kyle Fletcher has issued an apology for recent comments he made regarding AEW All In: Texas, acknowledging that his initial remarks were poorly worded and insensitive considering the circumstances surrounding Adam Cole's career-threatening injury.

Fletcher was originally scheduled to defend his TNT title against Cole at AEW All In: Texas last July. However, an injury Cole sustained during their match forced him to withdraw from the bout. The match was subsequently restructured into a four-way competition. When Fletcher recently spoke about how the change in match stipulation affected him, his comments drew criticism from the pro wrestling community.

In a new interview, Fletcher expressed regret over the way he had framed his feelings about that day. "That comment that I made the other day, I was very upset with myself for the way I worded it. I would like to apologize for the way I spoke and the words that I used. I think the words I used are a bit insensitive, saying it was one of the worst days of my life," he stated.

Fletcher emphasized that Cole's injury is Cole's story to tell, and he should not have centered his own experience around that narrative. However, he maintained that the day was indeed traumatic for him, and he proceeded to explain the deeper reasons behind those feelings in greater detail.

"Truthfully, that was one of the worst days of my life," Fletcher revealed. "I've tried my best to stay away from talking about it, but one of the reasons it was one of the worst days of my life is because, think about it, I had to watch someone that I admire very much and I look up to stand in the ring and cry and basically say that he may never wrestle again. And then I had to go out into the ring and perform, basically knowing that it was possibly my fault and that everybody in the arena knew it was my fault."

The TNT Champion further disclosed that some individuals had encouraged him to capitalize on the incident by using the fact that he injured Cole to generate villain heat during future promos. Fletcher made clear that he was uncomfortable with that approach and chose not to pursue it.

Brody King Peter Rosenberg Diving Debate

Brody King Reacts To Peter Rosenberg's Rant About Wrestlers Dives: "Don't Tell Me What To Do, Peter"; Rosenberg Replies

Telling Brody King what to do is not a wise move. He doesn't like it. The "Abolish ICE" t-shirt-toting AEW star recently surfaced via social media to respond to criticism from WWE broadcast team member Peter Rosenberg, who ranted in general about wrestlers taking certain dives.

Rosenberg took to social media to express his concerns about the risky nature of high-flying maneuvers that have become staples in professional wrestling. "This is why I tell whoever I can at evolve and even on the main roster — don't bother with these dives," Rosenberg wrote. "They are all risk — no reward. If you successfully hit a suicide dive in 2026 no one cares, but if you mess it up you could truly harm yourself!"

The WWE personality made clear he was speaking from an observer's perspective rather than someone with in-ring experience. Rosenberg added, "Don't do it. Meant to add the caveat — I am NOT a wrestler of course lol.. but when I watch this up close I cringe every time." His concern centered on the minimal payoff of successfully executing such moves compared to the potential for serious injury.

Reacting to Rosenberg on social media was King, who bluntly responded with a direct message: "Don't tell me what to do Peter." The AEW wrestler's response was characteristically straightforward, making clear he wasn't interested in unsolicited advice about his in-ring work.

Rather than escalate the exchange, Rosenberg kept things light and playful. Peter Rosenberg replied to King with a good-natured jab, writing, "You especially you big S.O.B." The back-and-forth demonstrated that despite their differing perspectives on wrestling psychology and safety, both parties were able to keep the conversation friendly.

The exchange showcases the ongoing debate within professional wrestling about the role of high-risk offense and its necessity in modern wrestling. While Rosenberg advocates for a more conservative approach to minimize injury risk, wrestlers like King continue to defend their right to perform the moves that are part of their arsenal and their identity as performers.
