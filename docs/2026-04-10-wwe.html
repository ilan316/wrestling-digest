🔥 Pat McAfee storyline with Randy Orton on WWE SmackDown

MULTIPLE REPORTS REVEAL BACKSTAGE DRAMA SURROUNDING PAT McAFEE'S WWE INVOLVEMENT

Pat McAfee's shock return to WWE programming as Randy Orton's mystery phone caller has created significant controversy both on and behind the scenes. Revealed during the April 3, 2026 edition of WWE SmackDown, McAfee's involvement in the Randy Orton versus Cody Rhodes storyline has already generated mixed reactions from the wrestling community.

Damian Priest was among the first to publicly express his disapproval of the angle. During an interview with Gabby LaSpisa, Priest stated bluntly that the storyline "sucks" and revealed that sentiment was shared among the locker room. "Not a fan. You know, somebody who knows Pat, get along with him. But for me, that's not my taste. I don't think it helps anything. He didn't need it," Priest explained. The Archer of Infamy went further to criticize the apparent double standard regarding on-air language, noting that McAfee was permitted to use language that other talent would not be allowed to use on the PG-rated show. "We're a PG show, right? But he could come in and he could throw F-bombs and say whatever he wants. For me, that's like one of those things that so many other talent, even from one word, can benefit. And it's like, wow, now they're up here. Instead, you take somebody who's not even here all the time and gets away with saying something like that, who doesn't even need it. Not going to lie, it kind of pissed me off," Priest stated.

Behind-the-scenes reports indicate that negative reactions extended well beyond Priest's comments. According to reporting, multiple individuals within WWE described the segment as counterproductive, with one source calling it "the most counterproductive thing in a long time." Dave Meltzer reported that "many" within the company were unhappy with how the segment played out and felt the angle worked against the company's broader creative direction.

The reporting also suggests that McAfee's involvement may not have originated from WWE's core creative team. Instead, there is a strong belief internally that the decision came from TKO leadership, specifically CEO Ari Emanuel, rather than being initiated by WWE's lead creative figure Paul "Triple H" Levesque. Emanuel is reportedly pitching McAfee as a "new Stallone-type action movie star for Hollywood" and wanted to ensure high ratings for WrestleMania on ESPN television by using a major ESPN personality in one of the main event angles.

McAfee's promo was not fully scripted by WWE writers, according to the reports. While some within the company were aware discussions involving McAfee had taken place, far fewer knew he would physically appear on the show. His involvement was kept tightly controlled, with talent being kept unaware and McAfee brought into the arena late to prevent leaks. Several of McAfee's on-air remarks, including references to WrestleMania ticket sales and ratings criticism, were said to be unscripted, with McAfee largely crafting the promo himself and getting approval from higher up in the organization.

Christian Sowell was listed as the writer assigned to the segment, and while creative was aware McAfee would be involved, the promo delivered reportedly differed from what had initially been communicated to Triple H beforehand. WWE President Nick Khan was also present throughout the process.

PLANS MOVING FORWARD AT WWE BACKLASH

Despite the negative backstage reaction, WWE has already mapped out future plans for McAfee's involvement in the storyline. According to multiple reports, WWE is planning for Randy Orton and Pat McAfee to team up against Cody Rhodes and Jelly Roll at WWE Backlash on May 9 in Tampa, Florida.

The current plan appears to be that McAfee will be in Orton's corner at WrestleMania 42 as the challenger faces Rhodes for the Undisputed WWE Championship on night one of the two-night event at Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas on April 18 and 19. Jelly Roll, who recently competed in his second WWE match with a victory over Kit Wilson, would be in the corner of Rhodes. This setup would kick-start the storyline that could carry into the expected tag team match at Backlash.

McAfee himself has previously stated that he will never appear on wrestling television again if Orton loses at WrestleMania. However, the reports suggest there are "plenty of storyline ways" to bring McAfee back at Backlash if Rhodes is victorious, with the simplest option being McAfee simply saying "I lied."

IMMEDIATE DEVELOPMENTS

The angle is set to continue developing during the April 10 episode of WWE SmackDown from San Jose, California. WWE has advertised that Rhodes will respond to McAfee's recent comments on the show. McAfee has teased his own appearance on the episode, writing via social media that he has a "massive surprise" to announce.

In his statement, McAfee doubled down on his support for Orton, writing that "a man sent DIRECTLY from the Wrestling AND Sports Entertainment Gods will be properly leading the business once again" and that he would serve to motivate Orton to "not only become 15 TIME WORLD CHAMPION but also, become a man who saved his family's business." McAfee also declared that "WrestleMania Saturday Night is gonna be The most Historic Night in the HISTORY of sports."

According to reporting, given the negative reactions both online and internally to McAfee's involvement, WWE's creative team has gone back to the drawing board to discuss new possibilities for the storyline. Some immediate plans for the April 10 SmackDown episode were reportedly "reworked" as a result of the backlash.

BROADER CONTEXT

McAfee has been associated with WWE since 2018 and had previously been viewed as having a positive effect on the company, whether appearing on commentary, as an analyst, or in the ring. He famously challenged GUNTHER at last year's Backlash and has worked alongside veteran commentator Michael Cole. However, this latest run has been met with significantly less enthusiasm from both WWE fans and talent.

The clues leading up to McAfee's reveal included references to the "wrestling royal family," making his eventual appearance something of a curveball to many observers. During his segment on SmackDown, McAfee delivered a low blow to Rhodes before launching into his tirade that heavily criticized the current WWE product and Rhodes as Undisputed WWE Champion.

Time will tell whether the angle ultimately succeeds or continues to generate the significant backstage controversy it has already created in its early stages.

🔥 IYO SKY vs. Asuka WrestleMania 42 match status

IYO SKY vs. Asuka WrestleMania 42 Match Status Remains Uncertain

The status of the highly anticipated IYO SKY versus Asuka singles match for WrestleMania 42 continues to remain up in the air, according to recent reports. While WWE had internally listed the match on its WrestleMania 42 lineup, it notably did not appear when the official card was announced on ESPN earlier this week.

WWE announced the official lineup for WrestleMania 42 with seven matches scheduled for night one on April 18 and six matches for night two on April 19. The SKY versus Asuka match would be the 14th and final match added to the card, but its status depends on several factors that have yet to be finalized by WWE Creative.

According to reports, the decision on whether the match gets officially announced will reportedly depend on the runtime for night two. The match is listed on WWE's internal lineup for WrestleMania 42, but the time allotted for each match on Sunday will determine if SKY and Asuka will be given a spot on the card. Essentially, this decision hinges on how long WWE wants Night Two to run and how much time is allocated for the six matches already announced.

There is reportedly a backup plan for the feud if SKY and Asuka are unable to clash at WrestleMania. WWE is considering the option of doing the match at Backlash on May 9, which is the Premium Live Event following WrestleMania. In fact, some reports suggest this might not even be a backup plan but rather WWE's preferred plan, though the decision remains uncertain at this time.

Both competitors have already begun building toward the matchup publicly despite the uncertainty surrounding its status. On the April 6 episode of Raw, Asuka delivered a taped interview to build towards the match, while SKY took to social media to tease a future battle with the "Empress Of Tomorrow." SKY posted a photo of Allegiant Stadium on her social media, teasing a WrestleMania appearance. Meanwhile, Women's Intercontinental Champion AJ Lee noted on social media that she has been "fangirling over Asuka, Kairi, and Iyo for months, and I am unhealthily invested in this now."

Asuka and SKY have only competed in singles action against each other once in WWE, with the "Genius Of The Sky" having secured the victory on an episode of WWE SmackDown in 2023. If this match does happen at WrestleMania, it would potentially make WWE history as the first singles match between two Japanese women at the Grandest Stage of Them All.

An additional complication exists that could further affect the situation. If Nikki Bella is not medically cleared to compete in the Women's Tag Team Championship Fatal 4-Way, The Kabuki Warriors could be inserted into that match, pairing Asuka with Brie Bella. This scenario would effectively remove Asuka from a potential singles bout with SKY altogether, further clouding the status of the planned match.

WrestleMania 42 is scheduled for April 18 and 19 at Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas, Nevada. Backlash will follow on May 9 at the Amalie Arena in Tampa, Florida. At this time, fans will have to wait for further updates from WWE Creative to see if this intriguing matchup finds its way onto one of these cards.

🔥 Charlotte Flair comments on WWE career and fans

CHARLOTTE FLAIR ADDRESSES WWE CAREER, UNDERSTANDS FAN REACTIONS

Charlotte Flair has shared candid reflections on her WWE career and the fan reactions she has received throughout her tenure as one of the most decorated women's wrestlers in company history. Speaking on Casuals with Katie Nolan, Flair acknowledged and understood why audiences wanted to boo her character for the majority of her career, regardless of whether she was being presented as a heel or babyface.

With 14 singles championship reigns on the main roster, Flair's dominance made her an easy target for crowd disapproval. She explained her perspective on this reaction, stating: "I mean, it felt good for most of my career. Also I think, because I was looking at it like, I'm Ric Flair's kid. I get it, there's nothing to like. I'm really gifted athletically because I've learned so much over the years, but you never want the same... Alabama wins again, Yankees win again, the Patriots win again. I get that, but then that was my job to roll with it."

Flair noted that her character was intentionally built as an undefeated force, which naturally drew negative reactions from audiences fatigued by constant winning. However, she revealed that a recent storyline involving losses last year provided the character with much-needed dimension. She explained: "It's a character, the character wasn't built to lose. Yes, the storyline I've been doing and losing last year, I wouldn't change it for the world, but I think it was just easier to dislike that character up until now than it was to like that character."

When asked if she hates Charlotte Flair sometimes, the real-life Ashley Fliehr distinguished between the character and herself, noting that she actually built the character traits she wished to embody in her personal life. She expressed: "Do I hate her sometimes? No, because I really did build this woman that I wanted to be in my personal life. So it's like, I wish I was more like my character sometimes, take no s**t. I just wish I was like, she's so put together and the investment, I wish I had invested in myself as much as I did the character. But now it's both."

Flair credited the losses and imperfections introduced into her character's storyline for making the character more compelling. She stated: "I've had the most fun I've ever had this past year, showing layers of what made Charlotte so perfect was the imperfections and the losses versus all the wins. Tying that in has been so much fun."

WWE UNREAL ON NETFLIX TRANSFORMS FAN CONNECTION

A significant factor in Flair's successful transition from heel to babyface has been WWE Unreal on Netflix. Speaking on ESPN's Unsportsmanlike with Evan, Canty and Michelle, Flair explained the dramatic impact the docuseries has had on her career and how fans perceive her. She credited the show with allowing fans to connect with her as a person rather than just a character after nine years of playing a heel so effectively that audiences believed she actually was that antagonist.

Flair discussed the evolution of professional wrestling, comparing the current era to the past when wrestlers maintained the illusion of their storylines by avoiding being seen together in public. She explained: "Every business needs to evolve and we're here to sell conflict. The older generation, everyone lived in gimmick. Either you play the good guy or the bad guy. You weren't traveling with the person you were in a feud with. Now, you can see me and whoever I'm wrestling at the gym the next day. We know it's scripted."

The Queen emphasized the value of the documentary series in humanizing performers: "For me, Unreal did so much in terms of the fans being able to connect with the person and not just the character. I played my character so well that they believed that I was that bad guy for the last nine years. For me, it did wonders. Everything has to evolve. If you're really good at your job, it shouldn't be an issue."

Flair's partnership with Alexa Bliss has become a focal point of the women's division. The team previously won the Women's Tag Team Titles at SummerSlam but lost them after a nearly 100-day reign. Despite the title loss, Flair and Bliss continue to stand out as top contenders and have earned another opportunity to reclaim the championship at WrestleMania 42, where they will face The Irresistible Forces (Nia Jax and Lash Legend), Bayley and Lyra Valkyria, and The Bella Twins in a Fatal Four-way match.

CHARLOTTE FLAIR DISMISSES RETIREMENT, REDEFINES PRIME AT 40

Charlotte Flair has dismissed any notion that she is approaching the end of her career, calling age 40 "new territory" and refusing to accept societal limitations placed on women her age. When asked about retirement thoughts during her appearance on Casuals, Flair rejected the premise entirely, noting that such questions reflect outdated industry thinking.

Flair challenged the double standard regarding age in professional wrestling and sports in general. She stated: "It's unfortunate that those sentences happen because I feel like in our industry, men don't reach their prime until 40. Because I started late, I didn't win my first title until I was 30. Where the girls are now, which is awesome, a lot of people are in their younger 20s. But I graduated, had my awkward 20s of not knowing what to do, and then wrestling came later in life. But saying that the men don't hit their prime till 40, why am I not hitting my prime now?"

Rather than accepting societal expectations about aging, Flair expressed determination to define her own timeline: "I don't look at it like, yes, I realize that I think it would be easier for people to label me in that box, but I'm not going to let anyone put me in that box. And I hope women in any profession who are about to turn 40 don't think that is a slowdown or an end game."

She drew parallels to other athletes who have continued performing at elite levels well into their 40s, citing skier Lindsey Vonn as an example. Flair emphasized that longevity in sports depends on individual capability and willingness to invest the necessary time and effort. She declared: "If anything, whatever industry that you're in, and in sport like Lindsey Vonn did, it's whatever you are capable of and are willing to do and put in the time. I'm very fortunate. I started out on top. It might seem like my career is longer, but I just have had the opportunity to be at the top of the game."

Looking forward, Flair stated her intentions unequivocally: "And now I'm not gonna say sorry anymore for winning, I'm not gonna age out of my career, I'm gonna quit when I want to quit. I'm gonna lace it up when I want to lace it up."

Flair also addressed the stigma surrounding age 40 for women, calling out the disparity in how male and female athletes are perceived as they age. She noted that if viewers did not know her age and watched her perform alongside younger competitors, they would not necessarily recognize a significant difference in ability or presence. She expressed: "I do feel like if nobody knew how old I was and you saw me out in the ring, you wouldn't look at me with the group of girls and be like, 'Oh, she's been here for 10 years, and she's 40.' It's just unfortunate because that's how society is, and it's changing slowly, but like 40 has had such a stigma around it. What is it? Men get better, women get older? Yeah, no, I'm only getting better... Here's the thing: it's new territory."

Flair noted that turning 40 mirrors her situation when she debuted in 2015, a period she describes as navigating new territory. She hopes her continued success will contribute to changing societal perceptions about women and aging. Her peers in WWE, including The Usos, Drew McIntyre, Cody Rhodes, Rusev, and Roman Reigns, are all around the same age and remain prominent figures on major WWE cards including WrestleMania 42, yet their ages receive significantly less scrutiny and retirement speculation.

🔥 Dwayne 'The Rock' Johnson Forbes greatest self-made Americans

Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson has secured a place on Forbes' prestigious list of the 250 greatest living self-made Americans, earning the number 70 ranking on a list that celebrates the American Dream and those who have risen to the top of their fields from nothing. The list was compiled to commemorate the 250th birthday of the United States and includes individuals recognized for their grit, hustle, and resilience in pursuing the American Dream.

Johnson's inclusion on the list is particularly noteworthy given his well-documented struggles from a young age. He has been open about his humble beginnings, including financial hardship, frequent evictions, a failed football career, and battles with depression. His story represents the quintessential American Dream narrative, starting from nothing and reaching the pinnacle of success across multiple industries.

Forbes' description of Johnson's achievement references the now-famous story of his financial rock bottom: "The Rock named his production company Seven Bucks as a reminder of a bleak time: cut from the Canadian Football League, he arrived broke in Tampa with 'a five, a one and change.'" This reference to arriving with just seven dollars has become emblematic of Johnson's journey and serves as a constant reminder of where he started.

The Forbes criteria for selecting the 250 greatest self-made Americans emphasized financial success, obstacles overcome, and enduring impact. The selection process was rigorous, involving analysis of Forbes' 109-year archive of entrepreneurial stories, input from current beat reporters, consultation with artificial intelligence systems, and evaluation by a panel of expert judges including the CEO of the Kauffman Foundation, various venture capitalists and entrepreneurs. The methodology relied heavily on Forbes' Self-Made Score, a 1-to-10 ranking that measures the "distance traveled" by each individual, with only those ranking nine or ten making the final cut.

Johnson's multifaceted career trajectory demonstrates the breadth of his success. Beginning in WWE, where he became a legendary performer, he transitioned to Hollywood and became one of the most profitable stars in the entertainment industry. Beyond acting, Johnson has expanded into the beverage industry and serves as a member of the TKO board of directors. His production company, Seven Bucks Productions, has become another significant business venture, allowing him to control creative projects across multiple entertainment platforms.

The ranking places Johnson in distinguished company. Oprah Winfrey topped the list at number one, while other notable names in the top ten include NBA star LeBron James, musician Dolly Parton, former President Bill Clinton, and Vice President JD Vance. Johnson ranked between musician Stevie Wonder and businessman Norm Asbjornson, founder of HVAC company AAON.

It is worth noting that another wrestling-connected figure appeared on the list. Shahid Khan, an investor in AEW and father of Tony Khan, ranked at number 21. Khan's story involved arriving in the United States as a Pakistani immigrant with just $500 in his pocket, working nights as a dishwasher while studying at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, before designing a one-piece truck bumper that became the foundation of his 15 billion dollar fortune.

Johnson's current endeavors continue to demonstrate his ongoing impact across entertainment and business. He most recently competed in the ring at WWE WrestleMania 40 and was nominated at the 2026 Golden Globes for his performance in "The Smashing Machine." His upcoming projects include new movies in the "Moana" and "Jumanji" franchises, as well as a Hawaiian crime drama in development with acclaimed director Martin Scorsese.

The Forbes recognition underscores how Johnson's influence extends beyond pure financial metrics. While his wealth across multiple ventures is substantial, Forbes acknowledged that some honorees on the list, like Johnson, have "wealth" measured in influence and impact rather than solely in dollar signs. His perseverance through adversity and his ability to build successful enterprises across different industries exemplify the values that the Forbes list seeks to celebrate.

Scarlett comments on Vince McMahon and WWE

Scarlett Bordeaux has opened up about the tumultuous period when Vince McMahon secretly returned to influence WWE creative decisions after his 2022 retirement. In interviews with Denise Salcedo, Bordeaux detailed how talent began noticing changes in the company's booking even though McMahon had no public presence.

Bordeaux explained that after she and Kross returned to WWE in 2022, the first few months felt amazing. However, everything changed when McMahon began secretly exerting control over creative decisions. She revealed that producers privately confirmed McMahon's involvement, though there was no official announcement. "He wasn't publicly out in the company. But we started talking to different producers and were like, 'This show's starting to feel really weird. The booking's starting to feel weird. Things are changing last minute. This is starting to feel like it did before Vince left. Like, it feels exactly, exactly the same.' And then a few producers privately told us, 'He's back.' But it was one of those things like, people knew but didn't talk about. Because there was no certainty about it. We didn't see him around or anything."

The effects of McMahon's return were immediate and detrimental to Bordeaux and Kross's careers. Their planned involvement in a storyline with Roman Reigns and Drew McIntyre was completely scrapped. Additionally, Kross had a WrestleMania match against Bray Wyatt that McMahon "killed." The uncertainty surrounding McMahon's actual involvement in the company made it difficult for talent to address the situation publicly or fight back. According to Bordeaux, only talent with inside connections learned about McMahon's return, creating an atmosphere of confusion and frustration throughout the roster.

The most devastating blow came at WrestleMania 40. Bordeaux and fellow wrestler B-Fab were originally scheduled to wrestle in the tag team Philadelphia Street Fight match featuring The Pride vs. The Final Testament. Bordeaux recalled the emotional toll of this opportunity being dangled and then snatched away. She admitted she had expressed her fear to Kross before learning they would be in the match: "I was crying, telling Kevin 'What if I get really excited about this and they take it away? I'm really, really afraid to be excited about this right now'. And they did. And I just remember, just kind of going numb."

The decision to remove Bordeaux and B-Fab from the match came last minute with no explanation provided. Both Kross and Bobby Lashley fought backstage to have them included in the match, especially given that it was a No DQ stipulation where their presence made logical sense. Despite these efforts, the decision was reversed without justification. Bordeaux was still able to participate in some capacity, going through a table at the event, but the sting of losing her wrestling spot on wrestling's biggest stage remained.

Reflecting on the situation, Bordeaux acknowledged that she may not have fought hard enough for her opportunities. She praised Chelsea Green for her determination to advocate for herself within WWE and expressed some regret about her own approach. "I do give credit to Chelsea Green. She said every single thing she's done, she's fought for it. I give her so much credit because maybe I could have fought more. Maybe."

The situation ultimately led to Bordeaux and Kross leaving WWE. When Kross's contract came up for renewal following his final match against Sami Zayn at SummerSlam 2025, he received an offer for a contract extension while Bordeaux did not. This disparity in treatment led to their decision to depart the company together. Despite the frustrations, Bordeaux expressed gratitude for her time in WWE and acknowledged that remaining healthy and able to perform elsewhere has been a positive outcome. McMahon retired from WWE in July 2022 amid sexual misconduct allegations but later inserted himself back into power. His final resignation came in January 2024 after former WWE employee Janel Grant filed a lawsuit alleging physical and emotional abuse, sexual assault, and sex trafficking, a case that remains ongoing.

Triple H defense of WWE creative decisions

Triple H recently addressed WWE's leadership transition in an interview, clarifying the nuances of his takeover as Chief Content Officer. The situation was far more complicated than a simple power transfer from Vince McMahon to Triple H. Triple H assumed the role in September 2022 after McMahon stepped away amid controversy. However, McMahon's departure was not permanent or clean, as he returned in early 2023 during WWE's sale process to Endeavor.

During this transitional period, both McMahon and Triple H were actively involved in the company's creative direction behind the scenes. McMahon continued to attend meetings, provide input, and direct decisions from the background, which created an unusual and undefined power structure. This overlap made the transition period exceptionally complex and difficult to navigate.

Speaking on the podcast What Do You Wanna Talk About? with Cody Rhodes, Triple H was asked whether WrestleMania 40 represented his first event in complete creative control. He responded, "I think so, though, you know, there was, and again, this is very terrible at times, but it's not like one day, here's yours, and everything else went away. There's so many aspects to that of, you know, hey, Vince's stepping away, you're going to take this spot, but he's trying to get in, he's still meeting with me all the time, he's still, you know, directing traffic from the side, and there's no real, it's weird, no real clear moment for me, but I would consider it that, yes."

Triple H emphasized the unique pressures he faced during this ambiguous transition. He explained that although people attributed decisions to him, the reality was more nuanced. "The only thing that is difficult in that transition, you're trying to, at the end of the day, when people are like, yeah, it's your decision, right? Yeah, sort of. You know what I mean? Like, you're, yes, and you have to defend your position, and you have to be able to sell that to people, explain it to people, if it's a little bit not your position, or a little bit, well, why did this happen?" Triple H described the delicate balance of maintaining authority while not explicitly disclaiming responsibility for decisions that were partially influenced by McMahon.

"And you don't want to say, well, You don't want to say, 'Well, it wasn't totally my decision.' Not because you don't want to seem like you don't have power you just have to position that way," Triple H continued. "It's just so complex. There are so many aspects even on just a regular, general, daily basis you're putting something out there, and in some manner, in an ideal world, I would do this."

Triple H further elaborated on the reality of WWE's creative process, which involves far more constraints than fans typically understand. "We don't live in an ideal world it's a realistic world. I can't do that because of this or that. It can be something as simple as these two people don't get along. I know this person has an injury that I can't put out there whatever the moment, the sequence is." His comments shed light on the numerous variables that influence creative decisions, from personal conflicts between talent to unexpected injuries and logistical constraints.
