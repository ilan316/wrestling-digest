MVP MMA Netflix Event

# Netflix Announces Star-Studded MMA Card Led By Rousey vs. Carano

Netflix is officially entering the mixed martial arts world with an ambitious debut event featuring some of the biggest names in combat sports history. Jake Paul's Most Valuable Promotions (MVP) has announced a comprehensive fight card for their Netflix MMA event scheduled for Saturday, May 16, 2026, at the Intuit Dome in Los Angeles, California.

## Headliner and Triple Main Event

The card is headlined by a highly anticipated matchup between Ronda Rousey and Gina Carano, marking the returns of both fighters for what is expected to be their final MMA bouts. The event has been billed as featuring a "Triple Main Event," with two additional marquee fights complementing the headliner.

Francis Ngannou will face Philipe Lins in the second major attraction, while Nate Diaz takes on Mike Perry in the third. These three fights represent the promotional centerpiece for Netflix's debut into live MMA broadcasting.

## Notable Fighter Appearances

The undercard features an impressive roster of established combat sports athletes. Junior dos Santos, the former UFC heavyweight champion, returns to MMA competition for the first time since December 2020, when he suffered a loss to Cyril Gane at UFC 256. Dos Santos has lost his last four fights prior to this booking. He will face Cuban heavyweight Robelis Despaigne.

Other seasoned veterans on the card include Muhammad Mokaev, a former UFC fighter, who will compete against Adriano Moraes, a former ONE Championship champion, in a three-round flyweight contest with significant "fight of the night" potential. The matchup between Lorenz Larkin—a veteran of UFC, Strikeforce, and Bellator—and Bellator and PFL veteran Jason Jackson is also featured on the lineup.

## Additional Bouts and Family Connection

Aline Pereira, sister of noted fighter Alex Pereira, will compete on the card against Canada's Jade Masson-Wong. Additional fights include Salahdine Parnasse facing Kenneth Cross, David Mgoyan against Albert Morales, Namo Fazil taking on Jake Babian, and Chris Avila in a match against an opponent to be determined.

The complete fight card was officially announced on March 24, 2026, and will stream globally on Netflix from the Los Angeles venue. The announcement came after a fraudulent card had circulated on social media the previous day, but the promotion clarified the authentic lineup with its official Tuesday morning reveal.

General Wrestling Commentary and Reflections

# The Sandman Reflects on Wrestling's Evolution, Names Early Favorites

The Sandman recently sat down with FOX 29 Philadelphia to discuss his extensive career in professional wrestling, touching on how the industry has transformed over the decades and reflecting on the wrestlers who inspired him as a fan.

When asked about the differences between modern wrestling and the product from 20 or 30 years ago, The Sandman drew a vivid comparison to technological advancement. "Well, everybody's smaller now," he explained. "Back in the day it was like a big man's game. Now it's more, it's like a little man's game now. If you look at the business, it's so much more athletic today than it was 20, 30 years ago. Forget it. It's like night and day. Like the stuff that the guys are doing in the ring now compared to then, it was like black and white TV to like color TV."

The observation highlights a significant shift in professional wrestling's approach and execution. Where size once dominated the landscape, modern wrestling has embraced athleticism and technical proficiency as primary attributes, allowing competitors of smaller statures to headline major events and captivate audiences with increasingly complex in-ring performances.

## Early Wrestling Fandom

The Sandman's appreciation for wrestling runs deep, stemming from his childhood. "I watched it every day," he recalled. "Saturday afternoon, one o'clock, I was sitting in front of my television watching either like Channel 48 or Channel 29 back in the day."

When asked to identify his first favorite wrestler, The Sandman named Superstar Billy Graham. "Superstar Billy Graham was my very first favorite and then the next was Dusty Rhodes," he said, establishing a clear lineage in his wrestling influences.

As one of ECW's original figures and integral to the promotion's identity, The Sandman has become a legendary name in professional wrestling history. Looking forward, he is set to retire from the ring next month in a match against The Invisible Man.

# Kazusada Higuchi Announces Retirement from Professional Wrestling

It is a somber day for Japanese professional wrestling as DDT Pro Wrestling has announced the retirement of veteran wrestler Kazusada Higuchi due to a serious cervical spine condition.

The promotion revealed that during routine health checkups, Higuchi was diagnosed with a subluxation of the first and second cervical vertebrae. Medical professionals have determined that continuing to participate in contact sports poses a significant health risk, prompting the decision to step away from in-ring competition.

## No Final Match, But Ceremony Planned

While Higuchi will not compete in a final match, DDT Pro Wrestling has arranged a retirement ceremony to honor his contributions to the sport. The event will take place on April 5th at Korakuen Hall in Tokyo, providing fans and peers an opportunity to pay their respects.

## A Decorated Career

Higuchi leaves behind an impressive legacy spanning over a decade in professional wrestling. His accomplishments include:

**DDT Pro Wrestling Championships:**
- 2x KO-D Openweight Champion
- 4x KO-D Tag Team Champion (alongside partners Shigehiro Irie, Daisuke Sekimoto, Yukio Sakaguchi, and Naomi Yoshimura)
- 4x 6 Man Tag Team Champion with various partners
- 2x King Of DDT Winner (2022 & 2025)

**Additional Accolades:**
- 2015 Pro Wrestling Koshien Winner
- 1x Iron Fist Tag Team Champion (Basara)
- 2022 Japan Indie Awards MVP Award Winner
- 2014 Newcomer Award Winner
- Ranked 55th in Pro Wrestling Illustrated's 2023 PWI 500
- 4th Place in Wrestling Observer Newsletter's 2015 Rookie Of The Year Award

Higuchi's career achievements and competitive spirit have left an indelible mark on DDT Pro Wrestling and the broader Japanese wrestling landscape.

Stardom Tokyo Dome Ambitions

# Stardom Sets Sights on Tokyo Dome as Major Expansion Goal

Stardom president Taro Okada has outlined an ambitious vision for the promotion's future, with capturing the Tokyo Dome emerging as the company's ultimate objective. In a recent interview, Okada detailed the strategic approach Stardom is taking to expand both domestically and internationally while working toward this landmark achievement.

Okada emphasized that the promotion's international expansion strategy will rely heavily on partnerships rather than independent ventures. Given the substantial financial risks and costs associated with organizing standalone international events, Stardom has opted to collaborate with established promotions overseas to maximize exposure and minimize risk.

"Regarding our international expansion, our policy is that, given the costs and risks involved in organizing events on our own, we should actively seek partnerships with local organizations," Okada explained. He pointed to Stardom's work with CMLL as an example of this philosophy in action, noting that sending wrestlers to partner promotions enhances their experience while simultaneously spreading Stardom's brand globally.

The president also highlighted the reciprocal benefits of these partnerships. "Sending wrestlers to CMLL not only enhances their experience but also helps spread the name of Stardom worldwide. Conversely, when unknown top-tier wrestlers from overseas come to Japan, it serves as a major source of excitement for Japanese fans. Streamlining our respective businesses and strengthening our collaboration is essential to prevent the industry as a whole from shrinking."

Okada, who also serves as a director for New Japan Pro Wrestling, believes that strengthening internal operations is equally critical. Improving communications and advertising have become priorities as Stardom works to build toward its bigger ambitions.

The Tokyo Dome, however, remains the true north star for Stardom's expansion efforts. Okada stressed the unparalleled cultural significance the venue holds in Japanese entertainment.

"In the Japanese entertainment industry, the 'Tokyo Dome' brand is unrivaled," Okada said. "Whether it's an overseas artist performing in Japan or a comedian holding a live show, the fact that it took place at the Tokyo Dome is the ultimate status symbol and the most compelling argument to the public. Making the Tokyo Dome event a success — that is the proof of being number one in the Japanese pro-wrestling industry as a world-class entertainment business, and it will completely set us apart from the rest. I want to achieve this goal head-on, no matter what it takes."

The Tokyo Dome ambition resonates throughout the Stardom roster. Saya Kamitani, who earned MVP honors at the 2025 Tokyo Sports Awards, and recent roster addition Maki Itoh have both cited a Tokyo Dome show as one of their personal goals with the promotion, indicating that Okada's vision is shared among the company's top talent.

# Lash Legend More Nervous Watching Fiancé Trick Williams' WWE Matches Than Her Own

Lash Legend has found herself experiencing a different kind of nervousness in her WWE career — not from performing herself, but from watching her fiancé Trick Williams compete on the main roster.

During an interview with Esteban Ramirez, Legend opened up about her pride in Williams' rapid ascension through WWE's ranks, from NXT to SmackDown, where both performers now work together as members of the blue brand. The couple announced their engagement in November, around the same time Legend was promoted to the main roster alongside Williams.

"It feels so good. Honestly — and I tell him this all the time — I'll be more nervous to watch him than for me to go out there," Legend explained. "I'm pretty chill, I usually don't get too anxious with things, I'll just be excited more than anything and ready to do it. But I feel like with him, I'm actually like 'Oh my gosh.'"

Legend attributed her heightened anxiety to the emotional investment she has in Williams' success. "It's because it's a different type of excitement when you see somebody that you really care about, and you know somebody who's been working their butt off his whole life," she said.

The SmackDown performer emphasized how Williams' current opportunities represent the culmination of years of dedication. "I feel like everything that he is doing, and the opportunities that he is getting right now, he has prepared for his entire life. It's really crazy to see, just all the work you put in and finally reaping those benefits. It's a beautiful thing to see, and I'm just glad I get to support him and be by his side and just cheer him on and give him his flowers."

Legend herself has had a notable WWE journey since making her NXT debut in 2020 following an accomplished college basketball career and a brief stint in the WNBA. On SmackDown, she has formed an unlikely tag team with former rival Nia Jax, and the two currently hold the WWE Women's Tag Team Championship.

Brock Lesnar: If I Didn’t Win A Little Kids’ Wrestling Tournament, My Mother Left Me At The Venue To Find My Own Ride

# Brock Lesnar Reveals Extreme Parental Pressure During Youth Wrestling Career

Brock Lesnar has opened up about the intense expectations his mother placed on him during his childhood wrestling days, revealing a rather extreme form of motivation that pushed him to excel in the sport.

During an appearance on Spittin' Chiclets, the WWE superstar recalled a period when his mother would leave him stranded at wrestling venues if he failed to win tournaments. According to Lesnar, this harsh consequence was his mother's way of ensuring he maintained a winning mentality from an early age.

"Even my mom, my mom had so much expectations out of me. My mom, bless her heart, and I thank her to this day even. If I didn't win a little kids' wrestling tournament, she left me there to find my own ride," Lesnar explained. "She was pissed, and I didn't wanna ride home with her for three hours and in the backseat of that car — she just left me and I was like, 'I can't believe my mom. She left me here.'"

The harsh parenting approach apparently resonated with Lesnar, as he expressed gratitude toward his mother despite the unconventional methods. He emphasized that while the tactic was extreme, it motivated him to pursue victory at all costs.

Lesnar's amateur wrestling resume reflects the results of such intense pressure. His accolades include an NCAA Division I Championship, two NCAA All-American honors, and an impressive 106-5 record before transitioning to professional wrestling and eventually becoming one of WWE's biggest stars.

Currently, Lesnar is competing in WWE programming, with his next scheduled match featuring a singles bout against Oba Femi at WrestleMania 42.
