🔥 Orange Cassidy AEW Pockets Spot and Comedy Wrestling

Orange Cassidy has emerged as one of AEW's most distinctive performers since signing with the company in 2019, and much of his appeal stems from his willingness to experiment and take risks in the ring. Speaking in recent interviews, Cassidy reflected on how his trademark spots developed and his overall philosophy toward professional wrestling.

The hands-in-pockets routine, perhaps Cassidy's most recognizable spot, came about almost accidentally. During a match with Marko Stunt over a decade into his wrestling career, Cassidy tried the spot and noticed it was getting over with the audience. He began incorporating it into more matches, and it eventually became something fans identified him with immediately. When asked about the spot, Cassidy explained that he never anticipated people would embrace such a seemingly simple gesture. He views it as what makes a great professional wrestler: something that stands out and allows people to identify with you. In other interviews, he elaborated on the reasoning behind the spot, explaining that putting his hands in his pockets while beating opponents represents the ultimate form of disrespect, similar to beating someone with their hands tied behind their backs.

The origins of his Orange Punch finisher are equally straightforward. Cassidy noted that because he is not a particularly large wrestler, he needed a way to knock out bigger opponents. His solution was simple: jump in the air and punch them in the face, which he called the Orange Punch.

Regarding the "comedy wrestler" label that has followed him throughout his AEW tenure, Cassidy does not shy away from it. He openly acknowledged that when people refer to him as a comedy wrestler, that is accurate because comedy is an element he uses intentionally in his matches. However, he emphasized that comedy is only one component of his wrestling arsenal. Cassidy was trained alongside and wrestled with many performers from different styles, and he has found his greatest success by incorporating as many different wrestling styles as possible into his matches. He acknowledged that this approach carries risk because it is never certain whether something will work, but he does not concern himself with that uncertainty. His approach is pragmatic: he keeps what works and discards what does not.

Since returning from injury in the fall, Cassidy has continued his association with The Conglomeration, a faction he describes as "me and a bunch of other dudes that lost partners or friends or whoever it may be." The group came together and won the AEW Trios Championships, with Cassidy teaming alongside Roderick Strong and Kyle O'Reilly. They have remained successful, retaining the titles as recently as a weekend episode of Collision against Rush, Dralistico, and Sammy Guevara.

One of Cassidy's most recent viral moments came during a June 3 episode of AEW Dynamite when he and The Conglomeration froze in place during Lio Rush's entrance. When asked about this moment, Cassidy admitted he was improvising. He said he may have been thinking of Jurassic Park, explaining that freezing was his first gut instinct. He theorized that if he did not move, Lio Rush might not be able to see him, though he acknowledged he did not know if this logic was sound. The strategy appeared to work, as Rush moved past the frozen figures and entered the ring. When asked if he feared Lio Rush, Cassidy expressed genuine concern, noting that Rush's unpredictable nature and the fact that he "just shows up whenever" makes him difficult to read or predict.

In other recent news, Cassidy revealed that his new viral t-shirt was not his own creation but was conceived by Matt Sydal. Cassidy praised Sydal for bringing the idea to him and joked that he would be sending Sydal checks for the rest of his life as payment for the contribution.

Cassidy also mentioned a friendship with a figure known as The Rizzler, describing him as "the best" and saying they are close associates who discuss various topics including airplane food and video games.

Throughout all these interviews, a consistent theme emerges: Cassidy's success in professional wrestling has come from years of trial and error, from being willing to take risks, and from understanding that in wrestling, sometimes the simplest ideas resonate most strongly with audiences. His decade-plus journey to finding what connects with people has made him one of AEW's most over and recognizable performers.

Keith Lee's Status and Return Discussion in AEW

Keith Lee has been absent from AEW television for more than two and a half years, creating significant speculation about his future with the company. The last time Lee competed on AEW programming was in December 2023 at Collision: Holiday Bash, where he defeated Brian Cage. In late 2023, Lee publicly revealed that he had been working through an injury since 2022 that had worsened over time, ultimately leading to his removal from the 2023 AEW Worlds End PPV event.

According to reports from Fightful Select, AEW sources confirmed that Keith Lee was discussed for a return to television several months ago. However, those plans have yet to materialize. The report noted that tentative creative ideas were in place at the time, but if those plans had moved forward as originally intended, Lee would likely have already returned by now. It remains unclear whether the discussions ever reached Lee himself or whether he was made aware of the internal conversations about his comeback.

One significant detail from the reports is that Lee has not been backstage to undergo the standard clearance process, which is typically one of the first steps toward an in-ring return. This process usually takes several weeks and is considered a strong indicator that a comeback is approaching. The absence of this formal clearance suggests that despite internal discussions, there has been no concrete movement toward bringing Lee back to competition.

Additionally, sources indicated that several wrestlers within AEW were said to be unaware of Lee's status and had been asking questions about his situation themselves. Lee has reportedly not been a regular presence backstage at AEW television tapings during his extended absence, which may explain why his current condition and timeline remain somewhat mysterious to those within the company.

In June 2025, Lee publicly addressed his status after a fan reached out to wish him well. He stated that he has been "good for quite some time" but made it clear that his wrestling return is not something he controls. When responding to a supporter who suggested launching a fan campaign to push for his return, Lee appreciated the concern but said that was not necessary. He expressed faith that things would happen as they were meant to, and that eventually the purpose would show itself.

Lee's response indicated a level of acceptance regarding his situation, even as the timeline for his return remains completely uncertain. While AEW has reportedly explored the idea of bringing him back, there is currently no confirmed timetable for a return and no official update on when fans might see him back in the ring. For now, Keith Lee's future with AEW remains in limbo, with internal discussions having occurred but no concrete plans moving forward.

Mick Foley Potential Wrestling Return

Jeff Jarrett Believes Mick Foley Will Wrestle Again, Citing Decades-Long Friendship and Storytelling Potential

Jeff Jarrett believes Mick Foley has another match left in him. Following Foley's surprise appearance at AEW Double or Nothing, speculation has continued to grow about whether the WWE Hall of Famer could return to the ring for one final bout. Speaking on My World, Jarrett said he would be surprised if Foley's recent AEW involvement does not eventually lead to a match. After co-host Conrad Thompson asked whether he expects Foley to wrestle in AEW, Jarrett made his feelings clear, stating "There is no way in hell I would bet that he's not going to pay this off in something."

Jarrett has known Foley for nearly four decades and described him as someone whose passion for wrestling and storytelling has never faded. "Mick, we've known each other 40 years," Jarrett explained. "He is a lifer." While acknowledging Foley's legendary in-ring career, Jarrett said what stands out most is his ability to emotionally connect with an audience. "He's a storyteller. Mick's going to be able to capture emotion as long as he can breathe."

Jarrett emphasized that if Foley does return to the ring, the match itself may not be the most important part of the story. Instead, he believes the journey leading up to the bout could be just as compelling. "Can he have one last match? Hell yes, he absolutely can. I'm more here for the story on how we're going to get there and the promos and the meeting and the emotion and what goes on into it." Jarrett then began outlining several ideas that could be incorporated into a potential retirement storyline, including Foley's family, his career struggles, and his rise through the wrestling business. "I can see Mick doing some Rocky training videos. There's so many things you can do that are going to be heartfelt. There's things to bring in family."

According to Jarrett, Foley's life story provides more than enough material for a meaningful final run. He pointed to Foley's early days driving long distances for training, working enhancement matches, and eventually becoming one of the most beloved performers in wrestling history. "What a journey," Jarrett said. Jarrett also questioned whether there is another star from Foley's era whose final match would generate as much interest among longtime wrestling fans. "If you're a wrestling fan over the last 20, 30, 40 years, is there an individual that's walking today that has the career that you would want to see one last match?" With AEW already planting seeds through Foley's interaction with MJF at Double or Nothing, Jarrett believes the company has a unique opportunity in front of it. "I think the opportunity to really, really, really bite into Mick Foley... it's coming. And I'm here for it."

Concerns Mount Regarding Foley's Return to In-Ring Competition

However, not all wrestling observers share Jarrett's enthusiasm. One columnist raises significant concerns about the prospect of Foley returning to active competition, pointing to the substantial time that has elapsed since his last match and his previous injury setbacks.

Foley's last official match as a participant in any wrestling competition took place on January 29, 2012, when he was a surprise entrant in the 2012 Royal Rumble match. During that match, he made three eliminations before being eliminated by Cody Rhodes after spending 6:34 in the contest. His last non-battle royal match occurred on August 12, 2011, when he teamed with Terry Funk to face "Rowdy" Roddy Piper and "Cowboy" Bob Orton at a Juggalo Championship Wrestling show called Legends & Icons, losing in a four-minute main event.
