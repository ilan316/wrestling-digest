🔥 WWE Raw April 20 2026 review

THE RAW AFTER WRESTLEMANIA 42: MAJOR DEBUTS, RETURNS, AND TITLE PICTURES ESTABLISHED

The April 20, 2026 episode of WWE Raw emanated from the T-Mobile Arena in Las Vegas, Nevada, serving as the annual night after WrestleMania. As expected from this signature WWE event, the show delivered surprises, debuts, returns, and several storyline developments that will reverberate through the next phase of competition.

OBA FEMI DELIVERS POWERFUL STATEMENT

The show opened with a statement-making moment as Oba Femi made his entrance following his historic victory over Brock Lesnar at WrestleMania 42. Lesnar, who removed his gloves and boots in the ring after the loss—a traditional signal of retirement in professional wrestling—appeared to have fought his final match. WWE reinforced this narrative by running a career-spanning tribute package for Lesnar on Raw, complete with "Thank you, Brock" chants from the crowd.

However, Femi's opening promo was remarkably brief and equally powerful. The 27-year-old stepped to the ring in his signature three-piece suit, strutted to the center, and delivered just four words before dropping the microphone: "Your Ruler has arrived." The brevity of the statement proved more impactful than any extended monologue could have been. Having dominated Lesnar in under five minutes at WrestleMania, Femi had already accomplished the necessary work through his in-ring performance, making additional words seemingly unnecessary.

The moment was intentionally designed to mirror Roman Reigns' promo on Raw after WrestleMania 33, utilizing silence and confidence as psychological tools. Femi's statement signaled that he was not simply celebrating one victory but announcing his arrival as WWE's next dominant force. In post-WrestleMania interviews, Femi stated he had rendered 25 years of Lesnar's dominance "null and void" and made it clear that Roman Reigns could be in his sights, suggesting the OTC could "get some" before the year concludes. Additionally, Femi announced plans to enter the King of the Ring tournament to secure a championship opportunity at SummerSlam.

PAUL HEYMAN'S EMOTIONAL DEFENSE OF BROCK LESNAR'S LEGACY

Later in the show, Paul Heyman appeared alongside The Vision (Logan Paul and Austin Theory) and delivered an unexpected emotional moment. Heyman called out WWE's production team for placing Lesnar in the "Now" portion of the company's "Then, Now, Forever" intro rather than the "Forever" section. Visibly moved, Heyman demanded that Lesnar be reclassified, insisting his legacy transcends the present and asserting that Lesnar will "forever be the baddest [in WWE]." The moment highlighted the genuine significance of what had transpired at WrestleMania and reinforced Lesnar's legacy regardless of his apparent retirement.

THE STREET PROFITS RETURN AFTER SIX-MONTH HIATUS

One of the night's most significant returns came when The Street Profits made their surprise comeback to WWE television. Angelo Dawkins and Montez Ford had been absent since October 2025, following a loss to the Wyatt Sicks on the October 10 episode of SmackDown. The time away was reportedly due to a "creative reset," allowing the tag team to refresh before returning to action.

The Profits made their entrance during a chaotic segment involving Seth Rollins and The Vision. Paul Heyman and Vision members Logan Paul and Austin Theory had been dominating the segment when Bron Breakker appeared and confronted Rollins about his WrestleMania performance. Rollins attacked Breakker with a steel chair from behind, initially gaining the upper hand by hitting Breakker and Paul with multiple chair shots. However, the numbers game quickly caught up to Rollins as Theory and Paul began to overwhelm him.

At the moment when defeat seemed imminent, The Street Profits rushed to the ring and attacked The Vision, forcing Paul and Theory to retreat. Ford delivered a significant tope con giro to both opponents outside the ring, and the action continued through the crowd. While the Profits' intervention helped even the odds, the segment ultimately concluded with Breakker gaining control of Rollins and delivering two spears, with a warning that he would break Rollins down completely.

The return was significant not only for reestablishing the Profits but for addressing lingering questions about their future as a team. Both Ford and Dawkins had previously expressed their commitment to remaining together as a tag team rather than pursuing singles careers, and their return solidified that direction.

NXT CALL-UPS AND MAIN ROSTER DEBUTS

Post-WrestleMania Raw lived up to expectations by featuring multiple NXT call-ups to the main roster. Two prominent NXT stars officially joined Raw, and their debuts immediately thrust them into competitive situations.

Ethan Page, a former NXT Champion with an 86-day reign as NXT Champion and a record-setting 273-day run as NXT North American Champion, was officially introduced as Raw's newest superstar. General Manager Adam Pearce made the announcement during a backstage segment involving WWE Intercontinental Champion Penta and Je'Von Evans, who was expressing his desire to pursue another shot at the title following his performance in the WrestleMania ladder match.

Page immediately made his intentions clear by calling for a shot at the Intercontinental Championship, setting up a match against Evans for later in the night. The match proved controversial among observers. While Page's debut victory was important for establishing his position on the main roster, some felt the booking diminished Je'Von Evans' considerable momentum after his strong ladder match performance at WrestleMania. Evans had been viewed by many fans as a potential IC Champion following his performance on the grandest stage, and losing to Page in his immediate post-Mania encounter removed momentum from the "Young OG."

Rusev's interference during the match proved pivotal, as he attacked Evans and prevented him from executing his offense effectively. Page capitalized on the distraction, delivered his Twisted Grin finisher, and secured the victory. After the match, Rusev continued the assault on Evans, locking in the Accolade submission while Page, standing nearby, ensured that Penta's attempt to make the save resulted in further damage. The segment positioned Page as an immediate challenger for Penta's Intercontinental Championship while setting up what promises to be a three-way dynamic in the IC title picture.

Sol Ruca also made her Raw debut and faced Liv Morgan, who was celebrating her WrestleMania victory over Stephanie Vaquer with her Judgment Day associates. Morgan had been the aggressor in their interaction backstage, and Ruca stood her ground despite being insulted. General Manager Adam Pearce quickly made the match official.

Ruca showed impressive resilience during her debut, kicking out of Morgan's Oblivion and delivering her Sol Snatcher move. She also executed a springboard dive to take out Roxanne Perez and Raquel Rodriguez. However, Zaria's interference from ringside shifted momentum decisively, allowing Morgan to regain control and ultimately defeated Ruca with a second Oblivion. Despite the loss, Ruca's performance earned praise for her athleticism and resilience.

THE BLOODLINE'S REUNION AND JACOB FATU'S CHALLENGE

The final segment of Raw featured a major storyline development as The Bloodline reunited. Roman Reigns, coming off his World Heavyweight Championship victory over CM Punk at WrestleMania 42, made his way to the ring with Jimmy Uso and Jey Uso. During the segment, Reigns addressed the fractured state of the family, acknowledging he had missed the bond they once shared.

Notably, Reigns had to address a real-life incident that occurred during the WrestleMania build. Jey Uso had appeared on rapper Cam'ron and Ma'se's podcast "Come And Talk 2 Me," where the interaction became uncomfortable. Cam'ron made disparaging comments about Uso's match placement and even questioned Uso's status within the Anoa'i family, leading to a physical confrontation before others intervened.

During his Raw segment, Roman Reigns took a clear shot at Cam'ron, calling him "some rapper from the '90s running his mouth." Reigns stated, "You're Main Event Jey Uso, you're the Yeet man. YOU HAD SOME RAPPER FROM THE 90S RUNNING HIS MOUTH TO YOU... THAT'S CRAZY TO ME!" He used the moment to discuss outsiders attempting to create problems within their family, spreading his fingers to demonstrate division before making a fist to show unity and strength. Reigns reminded The Usos how powerful they were together when The Bloodline was at the top of WWE and asked them to stand with him again as family.

Jimmy and Jey both agreed and acknowledged Reigns as their Tribal Chief, executing their signature "ones up" gesture together. However, this reunion moment was interrupted when Jacob Fatu made his entrance. Fresh off his WrestleMania victory over Drew McIntyre, Fatu was in no mood for celebration or acknowledgment.

Fatu quickly made clear he was not there to acknowledge Reigns and instead demanded everything the Tribal Chief possessed. He stated, "I don't want that title, I NEED that title," emphasizing his hunger for the World Heavyweight Championship. Fatu explained he wanted everything Reigns had, including the championship status, shoe deals, vehicles, and the lifestyle that accompanied being the OTC. He made clear he was doing this for his family and would not stop at any cost.

Fatu issued a direct challenge for a title match at WWE Backlash on May 9. However, Reigns did not immediately accept or decline. Instead, he questioned whether Fatu could handle the "burden" of being champion if he won or the "burden" of acknowledging Reigns if he failed. Calling Fatu "little cousin," Reigns stated he would take the week to consider the challenge before providing his answer on the following Raw.

As Reigns departed the ring, he notably instructed The Usos to "talk some sense" into Fatu. This exchange suggested that while Fatu's family connection to Reigns granted him consideration, the OTC was not taking the challenge lightly. Based on post-WrestleMania interviews, WWE appears to have plans for Reigns to face multiple challengers during his reign, including both GUNTHER and Oba Femi, suggesting Backlash could mark the beginning of a significant title program.

CM PUNK ADDRESSES PERSONAL LOSSES AND EYES FUTURE OPPORTUNITY

CM Punk made a significant appearance on Raw to address his WrestleMania loss to Roman Reigns, which cost him the World Heavyweight Championship. However, Punk's promo extended beyond simple title loss commentary. He opened up about the difficult personal losses he had experienced in recent months.

Punk mentioned the loss of his longtime MMA trainer Duke Roufus, his beloved dog Larry, and several WWE colleagues who had passed away. He thanked the fans for helping him process his grief throughout his title reign, noting that holding the championship provided something to hold onto during this difficult period. Despite the heartfelt nature of his words, Punk made clear he was not going on vacation or sulking over his loss.

Instead, Punk stated he would "stay ready" because championship opportunities can arise unexpectedly. He emphasized that while he had lost at WrestleMania, he remained confident in his abilities, noting he still possessed "a skip to my step" despite the loss. The implication was clear: Punk intended to pursue another world title opportunity in the near future.

Shortly after Punk began to leave the ring, Cody Rhodes made his entrance with the WWE Championship. Rhodes' appearance immediately drew attention for the severe injuries he displayed from his WrestleMania match against Randy Orton. Rhodes' left eye was swollen shut with heavy bruising, surrounded by visible lacerations that had been treated with sutures. Medical staff had confirmed an orbital injury, and he carried multiple facial cuts requiring three stitches and four staples to the back of his head. The damage came from a late-match striking exchange with Orton that left Rhodes busted open, followed by a brutal post-match assault including a title belt shot and a Punt Kick that placed Rhodes in concussion protocol.

Despite his injuries and not being medically cleared for WWE's scheduled world appearances, Rhodes showed up on Raw for this segment. When Punk asked if he went over his time, Rhodes told him not to leave, stating Raw was Punk's time. Punk asked if Rhodes thought he would "crash out," and Rhodes honestly answered, "Yeah."

In an intriguing moment, Rhodes said he should feel like a loser despite his WrestleMania victory. Punk responded by noting he was a loser but emphasized it was acceptable. As Punk prepared to exit, he once again mentioned he would stay ready for a title opportunity. This time, he made clear he was looking directly at Rhodes and his WWE Championship.

As Punk walked up the ramp, Rhodes grabbed his microphone and simply stated, "Just say when." The two-word response suggested Rhodes was open to facing Punk for the WWE Championship whenever Punk felt ready to make the challenge. WWE's treatment of this exchange strongly suggested a future title match between the two is in the works, with the timing to be determined at a later date.

🔥 Sol Ruca WWE Raw Debut

Sol Ruca made an unexpected appearance on WWE Raw following WrestleMania 42, interrupting Liv Morgan's championship celebration in Las Vegas. Morgan had just captured the Women's World Championship by defeating Stephanie Vaquer at WrestleMania, and was in the ring with Judgment Day when Ruca's music hit.

Ruca approached the ring with respect, telling Morgan that she had been following her journey for the last decade and considered her an inspiration. She mentioned that they had faced off in the Royal Rumble match earlier this year when they were the final two competitors. However, Morgan responded dismissively, telling Ruca that she had some nerve interrupting the celebration and warning her not to jeopardize her chances of making it to next year's WrestleMania, saying "you are no Liv Morgan, sweetie."

Ruca stood her ground, stating that she didn't come out to pick a fight but wouldn't back down from one either. This exchange prompted Raw General Manager Adam Pearce to make his way to the ring and officially announce an impromptu singles match between the two women.

The match itself was competitive and impressed the crowd with displays of Ruca's high-flying ability. She executed several impressive moves including a standing moonsault, German suplex, X-Factor, missile dropkick, and codebreaker counter. She also hit her Sol Snatcher finisher on Morgan, though Morgan managed to roll out of the ring before being pinned. Ruca then landed a massive springboard dive over the top rope onto Judgment Day members Roxanne Perez and Raquel Rodriguez, drawing "holy shit" chants from the crowd.

However, the match did not end fairly. With the referee distracted by checking on Morgan, Zaria rushed down to the ring and attacked Ruca at ringside, slamming her into the ring post and hitting a spinebuster. Ruca barely made it back into the ring before the count of nine, but the damage had been done. Morgan capitalized on Ruca's weakened state and hit Oblivion for the pinfall victory.

Despite the loss, Ruca's debut made a significant impact on the Raw crowd, who chanted her name during the match. After the match concluded, Stephanie Vaquer came out and stared down Liv Morgan, hinting at a potential future matchup.

In her reflections on the appearance, Ruca expressed that the opportunity was completely unexpected. She explained that she had arrived in Las Vegas for WrestleMania weekend expecting to work WWE NXT matches and participate in some media duties. Only a few days into the event did WWE inform her she would be appearing on Raw. She initially thought it might be a brief segment or backstage appearance, but was pleasantly surprised to learn she would cut a promo, compete in a match, and do interviews.

Ruca stated that the short notice actually worked in her favor, saying she preferred not knowing in advance because it prevented her from getting too nervous. She expressed that being placed in a match against the newly crowned Women's World Champion was a confidence booster, as it signaled to her that WWE management believed in her abilities.

What particularly stood out to Ruca was the crowd's reaction. Coming from NXT, where many Raw viewers don't regularly watch, she was uncertain how the audience would receive her. She acknowledged the risk of appearing on Raw as an NXT talent, recognizing that casual viewers might not be familiar with her work. While Morgan is billed as a heel, Ruca noted that fans still love her, so she prepared herself for the possibility of being booed.

Ruca revealed that she was actually "thrown off" when the crowd popped immediately after she delivered her first line in her promo. She said, "As soon as I said my first line they popped, and I was like, 'Okay, they know who I am.'" This positive reception surprised her positively and gave her confidence for the match that followed.

Ruca characterized the transition from NXT to the main roster as a learning experience. She noted that performing on Raw requires listening to the crowd more carefully and adjusting her pace accordingly, allowing herself to feed off the audience's energy and create meaningful moments. She emphasized the importance of having confidence and committing fully when stepping into that larger arena.

Ruca is scheduled to face Zaria in a Last Woman Standing match on NXT, which could potentially be her final match on the developmental brand before an official call-up to the main roster. While her Raw appearance hasn't been officially confirmed as a call-up like Ethan Page's earlier that same evening, the significant spotlight placed on her suggests WWE has major plans for her future.

Rob Van Dam Hall of Fame 2026

The journey to appreciating Rob Van Dam begins like many wrestling fan origin stories: through the mystique of ECW. This became his calling card: the ability to make every week feel like the most important wrestling show you'd ever see.

The question of whether RVD could translate his ECW magic to a larger stage seemed impossible in 2001. Yes, he had appeared on Raw in 1997, but by the end of his ECW run, RVD operated with a level of creative freedom that seemed unimaginable for any other promotion to grant. The use of weapons, integral to ECW's identity, combined with his ability to leap high off the turnbuckle and crash land directly on an opponent's core made RVD the very personification of ECW itself. When discussion turned to him leaving ECW, despite his Mr. Monday Night gimmick suggesting exactly that outcome, it seemed impossible that WCW or WWE would ever allow him to be the RVD that ECW had cultivated.

Like all great things, ECW couldn't last forever, and in the spring of 2001, the promotion went out of business. To compound the problem, WWF bought WCW, meaning there was only one place for RVD to work. This represented a massive crossroads in his career, comparable to a favorite indie artist signing with a major record label and being forced to sing radio-friendly songs. While RVD's high-flying style was welcomed in WWE, the landscape was drastically different. He could feature in a ladder match with Jeff Hardy or carry the Intercontinental Championship to boost the mid-card, but the weapons and limitless creativity that defined RVD in ECW gradually disappeared.

RVD's career in WWE was marked by constant tension with Vince McMahon. Paul Heyman once described his booking philosophy as simple: do a bunch of things, then put RVD out there for the last 30 minutes and let him work. There came a point when RVD won the WWE Championship from John Cena, and his connection and popularity with the audience reached an all-time high. But the title came off him almost as quickly as it went on when he was caught with weed during a traffic stop with Sabu. It echoed Jim Duggan and the Iron Sheik getting caught with drugs in the 1980s, though instead of being fired, both wrestlers were depushed, and RVD lost his WWE title almost immediately.

Yet RVD refused to live by the rules others followed. While primarily known as a mid-carder, his resume was remarkably successful. In ECW, he won the television title. In WWE, he held the Intercontinental Championship six times. In TNA, while he did hold the world title, he also held the X-Division Championship. In many ways, he became the perfect secondary champion: so much of an attraction that promotions could sell out arenas across the country with him on top of B-level cards. He was dependable, even when clashing with management behind the scenes.

Clashing with management represented old school wrestling mentality, and even a promoter like Vince McMahon wasn't going to take it personally. RVD had accomplished something remarkable: he brought his indie style out of bingo halls and into well-lit, fully sold-out arenas. While forced to compromise, he fought for every bit of creative freedom available to him. That determination, combined with his revolutionary in-ring style, made him one of wrestling's most unique and enduring performers.
