🔥 WWE WrestleMania 42 Main Event Coverage

WWE WrestleMania 42 Main Event Coverage

WWE is set to present WrestleMania 42 this weekend at Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas, Nevada, with the two-night premium live event featuring championship matches that emphasize personal history and long-standing rivalries between competitors.

The first night will be headlined by Cody Rhodes defending his Undisputed WWE Championship against Randy Orton. The match carries significant historical weight, as Rhodes and Orton have nearly two decades of shared history dating back to their time together in The Legacy stable. Orton mentored Rhodes early in his career, was involved in the final match of Rhodes' father Dusty Rhodes, and faced Rhodes in his first match with the company. However, the storyline has been complicated by the late addition of Pat McAfee to Orton's corner, a development that has drawn widespread criticism from the wrestling community.

Cody Rhodes has been vocal about his displeasure with McAfee's involvement. In interviews, Rhodes repeatedly invoked the Mariah Carey "I don't know her" meme when asked about McAfee, refusing to acknowledge the broadcaster's presence in the storyline. Rhodes explained that while McAfee is clearly a serious person with significant success in his career, someone in his orbit needs to tell him no occasionally. Rhodes compared McAfee's insertion into the main event to the famous internet meme of a clown trying to blend in with soldiers, describing it as "an interesting fit." He also criticized McAfee's in-ring presentation, comparing it to watching someone at a bar do a Stone Cold Steve Austin impression and noting that the emphasis on the Attitude Era felt out of place when WWE has been thriving for the past four years.

Rhodes acknowledged that McAfee offered to discount WrestleMania 42 tickets during his appearances on television, and that his involvement seems tied to boosting ticket sales and cross-promotional interests with ESPN. CM Punk has also weighed in on the situation, cutting promos that included criticism of McAfee before their own WrestleMania match. WWE announcer Joe Tessitore has noted that McAfee has generated what he calls "nuclear true hate heat" with fans in just two weeks, creating genuine emotional investment in the Rhodes versus Orton match through negative reactions to McAfee's presence.

McAfee has pledged that if Orton loses to Rhodes at WrestleMania 42, he will leave the wrestling industry forever. If Orton wins, WWE will need to develop plans for McAfee's continued involvement with the company. Speculation includes potential matches with CM Punk, the formation of a new stable led by Orton and McAfee, or McAfee transitioning into an authority figure role within WWE.

The second night of WrestleMania 42 will be headlined by CM Punk defending his WWE World Heavyweight Championship against Roman Reigns. This match has been built around long-standing personal issues between the two competitors. Punk recently made controversial comments about burying Reigns next to his late father, which led to The Usos confronting Punk and demanding an apology. This involvement of The Usos has sparked speculation about a potential Bloodline reunion, with reports suggesting that Jey Uso may have called for Reigns to take action against Punk in the weeks leading up to their championship match.

CM Punk has reflected on the significance of headlining WrestleMania in back-to-back years. In interviews, Punk explained that main eventing WrestleMania means far more now than it would have 10 to 15 years ago, when he had largely resigned himself to the belief it would never happen. The sweeter taste of the accomplishment now, combined with successfully climbing the mountain and planting his flag at the top, has created an additional layer of meaning to the achievement. Punk noted that headlining two consecutive WrestleManias is comparable to being on the cover of the WWE 2K video game for a second time—it remains a significant accomplishment despite the recency of his previous main event appearance.

Punk has also addressed retirement plans, firmly stating that he has no intention of retiring anytime soon and asking fans to stop asking about it. He noted that his appearance on the WWE 2K26 cover represents his entire career, from beginning through middle, and while the end is creeping closer, it is "not anytime soon." Punk emphasized his consistency as a top talent and significant draw for the company.

Roman Reigns has similarly discussed his future in the company. At 40 years old, Reigns stated that while he still has "a little more juice to squeeze," retirement is approaching. He acknowledged that with modern advances in sports science and nutrition, careers have been extended, and that the financial equity that comes with tenure in the industry is a significant factor in his decision to continue. Reigns expressed confidence that creatively there is still much left for him to accomplish and substantial earnings potential remaining before he steps away from competition.

In related WrestleMania 42 news, the status of Nikki Bella for a high-profile four-way WWE Women's Tag Team Championship match remains uncertain. Bella has been dealing with lingering issues from a late-March match on SmackDown, including a walking boot injury. As of the final week before WrestleMania, her medical clearance remained "too close to call." If Bella cannot compete, Asuka and Kairi Sane are expected to replace her and Brie Bella in the match against reigning champions Nia Jax and Lash Legend, Charlotte Flair and Alexa Bliss, and Bayley and Lyra Valkyria.

Additionally, the women's championship match between Rhea Ripley and Jade Cargill represents another marquee bout for the weekend. Ripley, who won her championship opportunity through an Elimination Chamber victory earlier in the year, has expressed her desire to provide Cargill with a quality match. While Ripley confidently stated that she will take Cargill's championship at WrestleMania 42, she emphasized that her goal extends beyond merely winning. She expressed hope that regardless of the match's outcome, Cargill will feel proud of the bout they deliver, particularly given that this match represents the collision between two powerhouses that fans have anticipated since Cargill's arrival in WWE.

Other wrestlers have also noted the significance of competing at WrestleMania. CM Punk reflected on his first WrestleMania moment, when he served as an extra for John Cena's entrance at WrestleMania 22 in Chicago. Punk recalled riding to the ring in a gangster car, standing outside the ring, and watching Cena and Triple H face off. He stated that he thinks about that moment every day he walks into work, as it helped him see the path forward to his future in the industry. This journey from extra to main event headliner in back-to-back years underscores the significance of WrestleMania as the pinnacle event in professional wrestling.

The road to WrestleMania 42 has revealed several trends within WWE. The company has demonstrated awareness of fan perception regarding the current on-screen product, with talent like Pat McAfee directly criticizing the company's creative offerings. The insertion of celebrities into prominent roles continues to be a major part of WWE's strategy, though the backlash to McAfee's involvement suggests the company must carefully balance appealing to casual audiences with maintaining the goodwill of its passionate core fanbase. The storylines emphasize that WWE remains committed to building narratives around legitimate personal history and long-standing rivalries, even as it experiments with celebrity crossover appeal.

🔥 AJ Lee WWE Return and WrestleMania 42

AJ Lee is walking into WrestleMania 42 with a completely different mindset than the one she carried during her first WWE run. In interviews with The Takedown on Sports Illustrated, Lee opened up about how her mentality today is nothing like what she experienced in her twenties. Back then, she felt like she had to fight for every opportunity and constantly prove she belonged in the company. Now, after years away from the ring, confidence has replaced that pressure.

Lee credits her decade-long absence with allowing her to grow both personally and professionally. She explained that maturity has reshaped her entire approach to wrestling. "I know who I am. I know what I've done. I can walk into this place with a new kind of confidence and a calmness of just being a grown-ass woman. And there is a different level to performing that way, with that mindset of, yeah, I know what I can do. And so it makes it a little bit more fun. I get to really just enjoy and soak up the moments versus feeling like you were scrapping every day back then."

The physical preparation for her return has been equally significant. Lee stated that she is in the best shape of her life, a development she attributes to spending the past ten years prioritizing her mental health and finding the right balance between being healthy and strong in her everyday life. She brought that improved version of herself into her wrestling comeback. "Thankfully, I am in the best shape of my life and part of that is because I've spent the past 10 years taking care of my mental health and finding that right balance of how to be a healthy, strong person just in real life. And so to be able to bring that into wrestling, I've come in as the best version of an athlete I can be."

The decision to return to WWE was fundamentally driven by fan loyalty. During meet-and-greet experiences tied to her outside projects, Lee encountered lines that were legitimately seven hours long, which made her realize the connection with her audience had never faded. "I knew that coming back was the right decision because of my fans. I was getting these lines that were legitimately seven hours long and that's when I realized, 'oh, they're still here'. There's still this hunger here. This support that only comes with wrestling. It only comes with this really loyal fanbase who watched me from day one, were a part of the mission statement of underdogs for the win and stuck with me, and were waiting for me to come back."

Lee's return quickly became a major storyline when she aligned with her husband CM Punk in his rivalry involving Seth Rollins and Becky Lynch in September 2025. That momentum carried into a featured singles feud where Lee challenged Lynch for the Women's Intercontinental Championship. She defeated Lynch to capture her first singles WWE title in over a decade at Elimination Chamber in Chicago. On the March 23 episode of Raw, Lee issued the challenge for a rematch against Lynch at WrestleMania 42, and Lynch accepted, noting it played directly into her plan to provoke the showdown.

Working alongside the current generation of WWE women's wrestlers has been particularly meaningful for Lee, as many of them were inspired by her work during her first run in the company. She reflected on this unique experience: "It's kind of like being at your own funeral a little bit. Everyone has something really nice to say about how I helped them in their career, and it's just meaningful and very surreal and cool." Lee had always wanted to leave wrestling better than she found it, fighting for change long before the WWE-branded Women's Revolution kicked off in July 2015. That advocacy for the next generation now comes full circle as she works with wrestlers who benefited from the foundation she helped establish.

Lee also reflected on the legacy she left behind after stepping away in 2015, particularly within the women's division. She explained her original philosophy: "Whatever you're an advocate for, or you're fighting for, it's always for the next generation. You kind of never think it's going to be for you, that change. You wanna start the change, and it makes the world better for people to come after you. You leave the world better than you found it. And so that was my idea with wrestling." Now that she has returned, hearing stories from members of the current roster has only reinforced how much she means to the women's division and the wrestlers within it.

Looking ahead to her WrestleMania 42 match against Becky Lynch, Lee emphasized that storytelling is more important than match length. She noted that the feud has resonated deeply with fans, and she can feel that investment in real time. "It's all about telling the best story that keeps people captivated the entire time. And there's a lot of it you cannot plan for. It has to feel organic, it has to feel like you are communicating with the audience in real time. And that [is] something I feel has always been something I'm in tune with and is probably a big part of my success. Making sure their hearts are with us. And so far, they've just been so into this rivalry with me and Becky, and to feel that passion, you can feel like heat when you're in the ring. You feel it in your skin."

The upcoming WrestleMania 42 match will mark Lee's first appearance on the Grandest Stage of Them All since WrestleMania 31 in 2015, when she and Paige teamed up to face The Bella Twins. Many fans are predicting that Lynch will finally be victorious over Lee on the biggest stage of the year, but Lee could potentially earn her first WrestleMania one-on-one victory. The match is scheduled for Night One of WrestleMania 42, taking place on April 18 and 19 from Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas, Nevada.

In a lighter note, fellow WWE star Rhea Ripley recently spoke about AJ Lee's signature footwear choice during an interview on Complex's Sneaker Shopping. Ripley noted that Lee continues to wrestle in Chuck Taylors and confirmed that she herself used to wrestle in them as well. When asked if she could still wrestle in Chuck Taylors, Ripley said she could, especially in the high-top variety. "I think I could still wrestle in them. Especially the high top ones. Low boys might be a little hard, because there's no ankle stability there. But the higher ones, for sure."

Ripley also shared a humorous anecdote about doing a shoey, an Australian drinking custom, with Grayson Waller during the Elimination Chamber 2024 Premium Live Event. She poured beer into her brand new Chuck Taylors on stage in Australia and drank from the shoe as part of the celebration. "I did one on stage in Australia with Grayson and I had my brand new Chucks on. They were so new. Like it was probably the first week of me wearing them, and I was so sad because then after that it just smelled like beer. I was so upset." Ripley acknowledged that while she considers Waller more of a pro at the tradition, she has participated in the custom herself.

🔥 Rhea Ripley at WrestleMania 42

Rhea Ripley is reflecting on a surreal moment in her career: meeting Drake backstage at a major WWE event and being called his "muse" by the Canadian rapper. During her recent appearance on Complex's Sneaker Shopping, the WWE superstar opened up about the encounter and what it meant to her.

Ripley recalled the backstage meeting at Elimination Chamber 2025 in Toronto, describing how unexpected and wild the moment felt. "It was wild. Like, just, I remember meeting Drake for the first time and we were in Gorilla, and he just kind of, like, snuck in. She confirmed that Drake was backstage with Triple H and greeted everyone in the area.

Drake had originally made his comments about Ripley during a September 2025 appearance on Not This Again with Bobbi Althoff, revealing that his Instagram For You Page was flooded with content about her. "My F.Y.P. page is just all Rhea Ripley. She's a wrestler. She's like my muse but — I'm the opposite of probably everything that she likes," the rapper said at the time.

Beyond Drake's recognition, Ripley reflected on how mainstream wrestling has become, noting that various celebrities now know who WWE superstars are. She expressed excitement about receiving praise from rapper Lil Yachty, who attended RAW a few months prior and mentioned being an admirer of the former Women's World Champion. "It's wild. During the same interview, Ripley addressed a viral fake AI-generated story claiming she had signed a $30 million endorsement deal with Michael Jordan. The fabricated post even included fake quotes, and surprisingly, many people in the comments believed it was real, with some saying they'd buy Jordans for the first time because of her involvement. Ripley reacted with humor and disbelief, saying she wished the deal were real. "I mean, I wish it wasn't," she joked. The conversation then shifted to actual endorsement opportunities. Ripley expressed strong interest in securing a deal with Vans, the shoe brand she wears every single day. These revelations show a more vulnerable side of one of WWE's biggest stars. While Ripley is now known for her confidence and iconic look, she's demonstrated that her journey to becoming one of the most recognizable superstars on the roster involved overcoming significant personal struggles and insecurities. Her appearance at WrestleMania 42 Night 2, where she will challenge Jade Cargill for the WWE Women's Championship, continues her rise as one of WWE's most prominent figures.

Myles Borne Discusses Emotional Road To WWE NXT NA Title

WWE NXT star Myles Borne has explained what it meant to win the North American Championship. Borne won his first title in pro wrestling when he got the better of Ethan Page, and he recalled the numerous emotions he experienced after the win in a recent interview with Denise Salcedo.

As soon as I hit the Borne Again and I heard the one, two, three, it was very emotional. I felt so many different emotions because I had worked so hard and, you know, all my life — I remember I used to play the video game, SmackDown vs. Raw, and I'd sit in front of my TV on the carpet, and I remember I was like, 'These guys are so awesome. I want to do this.' And, you know, all my life I was inspired by it.

After his North American title win, Borne went home to his garage to train, which is when the magnitude of his achievement hit him.

I just stopped for a second, I said — I had the title — 'Holy crap. Not only did I achieve my dream of coming to the WWE, I said, Myles, you are now the men's North American Champion.' And, you know, that's when it hit me 'cause I've been training and working so hard and I'm surrounded by amazing talent, amazing coaches. And it just becomes, you know, a whirlwind. And then once it all happened in front of you, there's so many emotions to where I can't even — I wanted to tear up and I couldn't tear up 'cause I was too excited.

Borne, who was born deaf, said that there was doubt and numerous obstacles while he was growing up, but he believes that his disability is a boon in a way, as it pushed him to prove himself. He added that he was often left out in his younger years, which drove him to work extra hard to overcome the situations he was put in. Since his title win, he has successfully defended it a few times, with one victory over former NXT Champion Johnny Gargano.

Cody Rhodes On WWE's Developmental System Evolution

Cody Rhodes is reflecting on how far WWE's developmental system has come, and why today's version may be better suited for the next generation. Speaking on Matt and Shane's Secret Podcast ahead of WrestleMania, Rhodes opened up about the evolution of WWE's training structure, comparing his own experience coming up through OVW to the current system in NXT and the Performance Center.

While Rhodes admitted he has an appreciation for the more independent, sink-or-swim style of training he experienced early in his career, he made it clear that today's setup offers a more complete and healthier path for aspiring talent.

Today, they've got it all mapped out really well. They've got exactly what you mentioned. They've got agility. They've got multiple rings, including one that has an extra layer of pad and bounce if you're going to try big high spots. They've got Shawn Michaels. Again, we're talking about probably the greatest in the ring ever. So that's your head coach. How could you not be good? The system they have down there with the Performance Center now is designed for the NIL athlete because they're used to it. 'I came from baseball, I came from football, I came from gymnastics.' So they need all that. They need a room that's a recovery suite. They need a full-blown weight room, which they have. They need all that.

It's a far cry from the environment Rhodes broke into. Rhodes went on to explain that while the older system forced talent to find their own path and build connections organically, the modern approach is ultimately more beneficial across the board.

When I came up, you were just doing it yourself, which I liked because you found like-minded people. I remember my whole class of coming up from OVW, but the system now, it's a lot healthier. A lot healthier to get everybody ready.

Rhodes added that the introduction of WWE's wellness policy in 2007 has also led to a change where overall athleticism is more of a focus than just having the biggest physique. When he started his WWE career in 2006, Rhodes trained in Ohio Valley Wrestling before coming to TV. He's now on his second stint with the company and his third WWE Championship reign. The main event of WrestleMania 42 night one this Saturday will see Rhodes defend the Undisputed WWE Championship against Randy Orton.

According to Bold, preparation for each WrestleMania began just one or two weeks after the previous show concluded. Once WWE locked in a city and venue for the next WrestleMania, the merchandising and branding process immediately moved into high gear. The company would design unique logos and create product collections focused on both the WWE and WrestleMania brands as well as individual talent.

Bold explained that the mission of his merchandising team was to ensure talent were properly represented with the right products in the right place at the right time to capitalize on their live television appearances and in-arena presence. This required staying several steps ahead of storyline developments and having merchandise ready before fans even realized a wrestler was being pushed into a major angle.

This approach made particular sense during the Attitude Era, when WWE was pulling record attendance and merchandise numbers and creating genuine pop culture phenomena out of its wrestlers. If a performer caught fire on television, the merchandise had to be ready to sell immediately rather than weeks later. The year-round operation of WrestleMania planning was essential to WWE's profitability during its most successful period.

THE HALL OF FAME HONORS HOGAN VS. ANDRE

WWE Storyline and Insider References Criticism

WWE is facing criticism for leaning too heavily on insider terminology and behind-the-scenes references in its storytelling approach. Speaking on Vince Russo's The Brand on April 13, 2026, veteran Al Snow addressed what he sees as a fundamental flaw in WWE's current creative strategy, particularly regarding promos that blur the lines between reality and storyline, such as segments featuring CM Punk.

Snow acknowledged that WWE's intent appears to be creating a sense of realism through "shoot-style" promos and insider-heavy dialogue. However, he believes the approach is fundamentally misguided in its execution and ultimate impact on the viewing audience.

"I know what they're attempting to do with this—blur the lines, you know, and we're going to shoot, and these pipe bomb things—and we think that's cool, but it puts a bad taste in the audience's mouth. And it only really appeals to a very niche audience that everyone seems to be trying to go for these days, which is just that internet wrestling community audience. That's it," Snow explained.

The crux of Snow's argument centers on the disconnect between WWE's creative choices and the average viewer's level of knowledge. He used the example of wrestlers referencing ticket pricing as a way to generate heat, questioning why performers would bring up issues that casual fans may not even be aware exist as a problem.

"And there's a whole large world of fans out there outside of those that would love to be a part of things and enjoy the show, but you can't reference things that they aren't aware of. So when you're a talent and you're referencing that to get a reaction, the average person at home is like, 'The cost of tickets are high? I didn't know that.' And that it's an issue," Snow stated.

Snow further criticized WWE's practice of referencing behind-the-scenes figures and management decisions that hold no relevance to actual in-ring storytelling. He specifically cited WWE's references to executive Ari Emanuel as an example of content that only resonates with the most dedicated insider audience members.

"Carl the plumber doesn't know Ari Emanuel is now a part of WWE management. That's a definitive insider thing that only appeals to those that know any of that information. So, you know, whenever you tell an inside joke, only the other person that knows the inside joke gets the punchline. And these promos that they're doing, where you're referencing inside material, only those that are inside get it," Snow said.

The former ECW and WWE performer emphasized that WWE is building storyline heat around figures who will never physically compete in the ring, which he views as weakening the fundamental structure of professional wrestling storytelling. He also pointed to references involving wrestling history, such as mentions of Disco Inferno, arguing that contemporary viewers lack the context to understand or appreciate such callbacks.

"And referencing ticket prices as a way to get heat on Pat McAfee makes no sense. And why are you referencing his agent? You're not going to meet him in the ring… Ari Emanuel is not going to come to the ring and take a bump for you. You're referencing a person that Carl the plumber today is like, 'Who was that? Disco? Who?' To a niche audience, to an inside audience, that reference gets a reaction—but to the average audience these days… none. It's been years," Snow explained.

Snow's criticism extends beyond individual promos and addresses what he views as a troubling broader creative philosophy within WWE. He contends that while the company may be attempting to inject realism into storylines, the execution has become too insular and focused on appeasing the hardcore internet wrestling community at the expense of mainstream audiences.

Snow's core message is unambiguous: WWE risks significantly diminishing its mainstream appeal if it continues prioritizing insider reactions over universal storytelling comprehension. According to Snow, the company needs to recognize that while certain references generate reactions from dedicated fans, they actively alienate the larger casual audience that should represent the foundation of professional wrestling's broader entertainment appeal. Without a recalibration of this approach, Snow suggests WWE may find itself effectively catering exclusively to an increasingly narrow demographic while losing the substantial casual audience that has historically driven the industry's financial success and cultural relevance.

Shawn Michaels Reflects On Changes In WWE From The 1990s To The 2000s

Shawn Michaels' legendary career spanned 30 years, beginning in 1984 and ending with his official retirement match at WWE WrestleMania XXVI against The Undertaker. "A lot of it was very 'Wild West.' HBK noted how things like "The Jerry Springer Show" and reality shows started taking off in popularity, so WWE naturally followed suit in order to appeal to the culture.
