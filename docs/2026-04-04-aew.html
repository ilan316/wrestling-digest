🔥 Thunder Rosa AEW CMLL dual contract

Thunder Rosa, the former AEW Women's World Champion, has officially joined the CMLL roster under a dual contract agreement with All Elite Wrestling. The announcement was made official during CMLL's April 3 Super Viernes show, where Rosa competed in a Two out of Three Falls tag-team match alongside India Sioux and Marcela against Olympia, Persephone, and Princesa Sugehit.

CMLL confirmed the news through an official announcement, stating that Thunder Rosa would be part of the CMLL roster under a dual contract modality with All Elite Wrestling. The deal allows Rosa to maintain her AEW status while simultaneously working for CMLL, similar to arrangements other wrestlers have secured with the two promotions.

Rosa's interest in pursuing such an arrangement had been publicly known. In a recent interview with The Takedown on Sports Illustrated, when asked about the possibility of a dual contract, she responded enthusiastically: "Really, you're gonna ask me that question? Of course I'm interested in something like that. It's a big deal, it is such a big deal, and it's such an honor when they recognize you to be somebody to be part of their organization. Absolutely, it would be an honor."

According to reports from Fightful Select, the working plan for the deal was confirmed prior to the official announcement. Sources indicated that the CMLL contract would run concurrently with her existing AEW deal, which is believed to expire at some point in 2027, though an exact date has not been confirmed.

Rosa has been rebuilding her career following a significant injury that sidelined her for seven months. She returned to action in February 2026 after overcoming an injury she previously thought could have ended her in-ring career. Her return to competition included an unsuccessful AEW Women's World Championship challenge against Thekla on the March 4 episode of AEW Dynamite.

Prior to signing with CMLL, Rosa's only other appearance for the promotion came at the June 2025 CMLL Presenta NJPW Fantastica Mania Mexico event, where she lost a match against La Jarochita. Her most recent non-crossover independent appearance before the CMLL deal was at HOL Hate Me Now in June 2025, where she defeated Zyra.

Rosa follows in the footsteps of other AEW talent who have secured dual contracts with CMLL. Claudio Castagnoli became the first AEW talent to sign such an arrangement and has found notable success, becoming the CMLL World Heavyweight Champion. Other wrestlers on dual contracts include Mascara Dorada, Persephone, and current CMLL World Heavyweight Champion Hechicero.

These dual contract arrangements represent a strategic partnership between AEW and CMLL to increase crossover appearances and introduce top talent from both sides to each other's fan bases. The approach differs from WWE's venture into Mexico, where WWE officially acquired AAA in April 2025 and operates it as a separate brand. However, the end result is similar, exposing US-based audiences to top Latin American wrestlers while giving Mexican fans opportunities to see US-based talent perform in their market.

Chris Jericho AEW return

Chris Jericho is officially back in AEW. All signs point to this being more than just a short-term return. The Ocho made his return on Wednesday's episode of AEW Dynamite, emanating from his longtime hometown of Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada, marking his first appearance in nearly a year. The comeback immediately sparked speculation about his future, particularly with ongoing chatter in recent months about a potential jump to WWE.

According to Dave Meltzer in the latest edition of the Wrestling Observer Newsletter, that speculation can likely be put to rest. Meltzer reports that the belief within the industry is Jericho is back with AEW for the long haul, and any possibility of him heading to WWE is now dead. Jericho's deal reportedly expired at the end of 2025, but Tony Khan extended the contract due to time Jericho missed for outside commitments, including acting work.

Meltzer reported that WWE only wanted to bring Jericho in for a retirement tour and farewell. At one time, it was stated that the 2026 year would be built around retirements for AJ Styles, Brock Lesnar, and presumably Jericho. However, even though there were teases by Pearce and another by CM Punk, it was repeatedly stated that the stories going around involving WWE and Jericho should not be believed.

There are more questions than answers right now as nobody is talking about the details. The belief is that him going to WWE is now dead and he's back in AEW for the long term. What he'll be doing and how the crowd will react to what he's been doing will be a lot more clear in two weeks when they are out of Canada.

Jericho is scheduled to speak next week, as he will address fans live on AEW Dynamite from Edmonton, Alberta, Canada. During his initial return, he gave fans only three words, which was enough to get people talking. Interestingly, during the on-air announcement on AEW Collision, he was referred to simply as Jericho, adding another layer of intrigue following his brief, buzzworthy appearance in Winnipeg.

Lady Frost praises Toni Storm

Lady Frost has nothing but positive things to say about her time working with Toni Storm. In a recent interview, Frost discussed her experiences throughout her time in AEW and ROH, where she worked with several top-tier talent including Storm, Kris Statlander, Billie Starkz, and Athena.

When discussing Toni Storm specifically, Frost was full of praise for the wrestler's work ethic and collaborative spirit. "Toni's incredible with everyone. She is a great worker. She's easy to work with," Frost stated. She emphasized that working with Storm made it simple to develop storylines, attributing this to Storm's strong character work and ability to build meaningful moments throughout their matches.

Frost elaborated on her philosophy regarding professional wrestling matches, noting that while she considers herself a "spot girl," the most important element is making those moments matter within the context of the overall narrative. "It's not just moves, it's not just choreographed things, and you know me, I'm a spot girl. But at the end of the day, is making those spots matter, telling that story," Frost explained. She reflected fondly on her time with Storm, saying "We always had a blast working together."

Beyond her work with Storm, Frost also celebrated her chemistry with Lena Kross, noting that she has worked with Kross more frequently than anyone else on the independent wrestling circuit. Frost expressed genuine enthusiasm about Kross's recent signing to a major promotion, believing that Kross has tremendous potential. "I think I've worked her more than anyone else on the indie scene. I'm so pumped that she got signed. We did a lot in Wrestle Pro and I think she's gonna be incredible. Obviously already is, but we always worked well together," Frost said, underscoring her confidence in Kross's abilities and future success.

Frost also shared her perspective on professional chemistry in wrestling, noting that it is impossible to predict who will work well together until they step into the ring. "I always say you never know who you're gonna have chemistry with until you get in there," she remarked.

Looking toward her future endeavors, Frost indicated that she is currently focusing on MLW, where she has set her sights on a match against Shotzi Blackheart. This demonstrates her continued ambition to work with top-tier talent and create memorable moments in her wrestling career.

Eddie Kingston Refuses To Face MJF Unless AEW World Title Is On The Line

Eddie Kingston has faced nearly everyone there is to face in AEW, but on the shortlist of performers he has yet to cross paths with, one name stands out from the rest. That being the current AEW Men's World Champion and fellow New York native MJF. The two men have miraculously never faced in AEW, or even on the independent scene before their separate AEW journeys began, and it's a match a number of people have wanted to see for some time.

There's just one problem, Kingston has a condition. During a recent interview on Sports Illustrated's The Takedown, he revealed that the condition was that he would only face MJF if Friedman's beloved "Triple B" was on the line.

"He's not worth it unless the AEW Title's on the line," Kingston said. "He's not worth it unless it's a number one contenders match, or the world title. Other than that, no, I have no desire. I won't give him–I won't give MJF the privilege to fight me. He hasn't earned that. He may think he has, fans may think he has, that's fine, everybody has an opinion, opinions are like a*******, everyone's got one, they all stink, that's fine. My opinion is no. So when he earns it and if he doesn't have the title and he's not up for a number one contenders thing and he earns it, he earns the privilege to fight me, then maybe. But other than that, no. There's a lot of other people I'd rather [fight]."

If Kingston was to go after MJF and the AEW Men's World Championship, he would join the long line of AEW stars who are looking to take the "Triple B" away from Maxwell Jacob Friedman. At the time of writing, he has been pursued by the likes of Brody King, Andrade El Idolo, and Darby Allin. At the upcoming Dynasty pay-per-view on April 12, he'll have to defend the title against Kenny Omega.

Leila Grey ACL injury

AEW's Leila Grey has shared an update following her recent surgery, after suffering a torn ACL injury in late 2025. Leila Grey's most recent match saw her unsuccessfully challenge for Mercedes Mone's TBS Championship on the December 6, 2025 AEW Collision episode. With Grey revealing that she had suffered a torn ACL during the match, the AEW star has now taken to social media to share a post-surgery update.

Two weeks on from surgery, Grey shared a video to social media, which was her own version of the popular meme "Nobody talks about how hard it is to go from this… to this…" The first "this" was her competing on ROH, with the latter "this" being a clip of herself climbing the stairs with a crutch and leg brace. Calling it the hardest two weeks of her life, Grey wrote: "It's been 2 weeks since my knee surgery. Hardest two weeks of my life. But I'm alright. Working to get better every day. Doing what I gotta do to be who I wanna be."

ACL injuries take a while to recover from, with stars typically out of action anywhere from six to twelve months following surgery. While Leila Grey and AEW haven't provided any specific return date expectations, the March ACL surgery date indicates that she won't be back in a ring until September at the absolute earliest, with it more realistic to expect a return in late 2026 or early 2027.

With several wrestlers from the AEW women's division currently on the shelf, Queen Aminata recently shared a positive update on her neck injury, stating that she is now pain free. The wrestling community continues to monitor Grey's recovery progress closely, with fans and fellow wrestlers extending their support during this challenging period.

Nick Wayne AEW NJPW Best of Super Junior

AEW's Nick Wayne has been confirmed as AEW's representative for the NJPW Best of the Super Juniors 33 tournament, which will run from May 14 through its conclusion on June 7. The ROH World TV Champion will compete in the A-Block as he returns to the tournament for his second consecutive year.

Wayne announced his participation on Twitter, framing the opportunity as his redemption. He stated: "Redemption. Best of the Super Jrs. 33. 1 full year of preparation, concentration, repetition and disciplined hard work done in silence. It was always my dream just to be in the Super Jrs, but my aspiration is to be victorious. Last year, I arrived with a dream and learned a lesson, this year I leave with the aspiration accomplished."

The Best of the Super Jr tournament operates under a round robin format, with competitors in the A-Block facing one another while B-Block wrestlers face each other. The leader from each block advances to the tournament finals on June 7 to determine the overall winner.

Wayne's A-Block competition includes Master Wato, Ryusuke Tasuchi, Kosei Fujita, Robbie X, Francesco Akira, Titan, Valiente Jr, Jun Kasai, and current IWGP Jr Heavyweight Champion DOUKI. This guarantees Wayne will face all nine of these wrestlers, including the division's current champion, in individual matches throughout the tournament.

The B-Block consists of El Desperado, KUSHIDA, YOH, Robbie Eagles, Taiji Ishimori, Jakob Austin Young, SHO, Yoshinobu Kanemaru, Hyo and Daisuke Sasaki.

In the 2025 Best of the Super Juniors, Wayne finished with eight points after defeating El Desperado, Robbie Eagles, Ryusuke Taguchi and SHO. While he technically finished joint-fourth in the B-Block, he was tied with five other wrestlers and only outscored Ryusuke Taguchi. With Wayne positioning this year's entry as his redemption, he will be looking to finish higher in the standings and secure significant victories against top junior heavyweight talent.

A potential historic achievement awaits Wayne this year. If he wins the Best of the Super Juniors 33 tournament, he would become the youngest winner in the event's history, adding to the significance of his redemption narrative.

Wayne's absence from AEW and ROH programming is effectively confirmed by his tournament participation. He has not wrestled for AEW since the July 26, 2025 edition of Collision and last appeared under the Tony Khan banner during a March 22 ROH TV taping. His involvement in the Best of the Super Juniors will keep him away from AEW and ROH tapings from mid-May through early June at the earliest, as the round-robin format will require an intensive wrestling schedule that may make simultaneous domestic appearances impractical.
