AEW Collision viewership

AEW Collision Viewership Increases on Thursday Night Show

On a new night for the week, AEW Collision averaged 473,000 viewers on TNT. The number is up 11.8% from the previous week and was the second highest audience for the show since the Grand Slam Collision episode from Australia on Saturday, February 14. Collision averaged a 0.08 rating in the 18-49 demo, the same rating it did the previous week and tying the second-best rating Collision has achieved in that category since February 14. Viewership was up 26.8% and the 18-49 rating was up 14.3% from the previous Thursday night airing two weeks earlier. Both shows were taped.

The show was moved to Thursday for the week due to WWE WrestleMania 42, but returns to its usual day this week. Collision tied for 20th on the primetime cable charts, going head-to-head with TNA Impact for its second hour as well as the NHL regular season finale on ESPN. As compared to the same week in 2025, overall viewership was up 34% while the 18-49 rating was identical.

The April 16, 2026 episode of AEW Collision on TNT reflected steady upward momentum. For comparison, the April 11, 2026 episode drew 423,000 viewers with a 0.08 rating in the key P18-49 demographic, representing an increase of 50,000 viewers. Compared to the same week in 2025, AEW Collision drew 353,000 viewers and a 0.08 demo rating on April 17, 2025, versus 473,000 viewers and a 0.08 demo rating on April 16, 2026. That represents a year-over-year change of up 34.0% in viewership and up 0.0% in the demo.

The four-week average currently stands at 436,250 viewers. The highest viewership of 2026 was 616,000 on March 22, 2026, while the lowest viewership of 2026 was 253,000 on January 24, 2026. These figures reflect only linear TV viewership on TNT and do not include streaming or digital viewership.

This week's show was up 8.6% in overall viewers compared to recent averages. Recent data shows the 10-week average at 0.08 in the demo rating and 435,400 in overall viewership, not including the most recent week. The company continues to build momentum as it heads toward its next major live event.

F4W/WON · Ringside News

AEW Kota Ibushi injury update

Kota Ibushi's path to recovery has taken a more positive turn following his second surgical procedure. The AEW star suffered a broken femur in a match against Josh Alexander just as he appeared ready to make his 2025 comeback. The initial injury was serious enough that he still appeared to be in considerable pain when he made an appearance at Hiroshi Tanahashi's retirement ceremony at Wrestle Kingdom, an event that took place shortly after his first surgery.

The discovery that Ibushi required a second procedure understandably raised concerns among fans and the wrestling community. However, the former IWGP Heavyweight Champion has since provided updates that suggest this second intervention has yielded much better results than the first. One week prior to the article's publication, Ibushi shared a series of four photos on Instagram documenting his progress.

The images painted an encouraging picture of his rehabilitation. The first photo showed Ibushi flexing to demonstrate his upper body strength, while the second captured him performing squats that he executed with notable ease and minimal apparent discomfort. The third and fourth slides documented his ability to walk up and down a flight of stairs, with observers noting that he displayed very little sign of a limp during these movements. Beyond the visual evidence, Ibushi appeared to be in considerably better spirits compared to his appearance at the Tokyo Dome.

Regarding his timeline for returning to competition, Ibushi has remained somewhat cautious in his public statements. He has not provided a definitive date for his in-ring return. Initially, medical professionals suggested that the femur fracture would sideline him for nearly two years of active competition. However, Ibushi expressed optimism relatively soon after his injury, indicating that he believed he could potentially return before the end of 2026.

Following his second surgery, Ibushi revealed in a March interview that he was targeting a six-month recovery window, a goal that puts his potential return in September if measured from the surgery date. This timeline is notably more ambitious than the one-year prognosis that doctors had provided to him. Despite his optimism, Ibushi acknowledged the realistic limitations he continues to face.

At the time of the March interview, Ibushi admitted that his current mobility remained restricted in certain ways. While he could walk without any difficulty, he disclosed that he was not yet able to run. Looking ahead to his eventual return, Ibushi suggested that his comeback would likely involve matches designed to be "easy," indicating that he would need to work his way back to performing at his previous level of intensity and capability. This measured approach suggests that while his recovery is progressing well, a full return to peak performance will still require additional time and gradual progression through his matches.

Ricky Saints compares AEW and NXT

Ricky Saints is taking a measured look back at his career so far, offering an honest assessment of his time in both AEW and WWE NXT. After spending just over a year in NXT, where he's already captured both the NXT North American Championship and the NXT Championship, Saints reflected on how that run stacks up against his previous stint as Ricky Starks in AEW, which included a reign as AEW Tag Team Champion.

Speaking with David Shoemaker and Kazeem Famiyude of The Ringer, Saints was asked to compare the two chapters of his career and assign each a grade. "That's hard," he admitted. "I loved my run at AEW for all television stuff. You can say what you want about the other stuff. If you look at it in totality, I did enjoy it, I just wanted more out of it. I think I would give it a B."

Saints went on to explain that separating personal expectations from actual output played a key role in how he evaluated his time in AEW. "I have to remove certain personal things of accomplishments that I wanted that I didn't necessarily get. I have to remove that. I have to look at what the actual work was. What was actually done."

When it comes to his current NXT run, however, Saints feels he's edging even closer to top marks. "When compared to NXT, I would give my NXT run a B+. We're edging out an A." The measured grades reflect Saints' professional perspective on his journey through two major wrestling promotions and his satisfaction with his current trajectory.
