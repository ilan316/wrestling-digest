🔥 Hangman Adam Page AEW Return

Hangman Adam Page is making his highly anticipated return to All Elite Wrestling. AEW President Tony Khan announced the news via social media, ending weeks of speculation about Page's future following his loss to MJF at AEW Revolution on March 15, 2026. In that match, Page challenged MJF for the AEW World Championship in a grueling 46-minute Texas Deathmatch. Though he pushed Friedman to the limit, Page came up short in what many considered one of the toughest matches of the year. As a result of his defeat, Page accepted a stipulation that he could never challenge for the AEW World Championship again if he lost. After that devastating loss, he disappeared from television programming entirely.

Now, nearly four months later, Page is finally returning. Khan announced that Page will appear on the July 11, 2026 episode of AEW Collision, where he will open the live show in Roanoke, Virginia. Khan shared the news on social media, confirming that Page will kick off the show in his home state. The announcement emphasized the significance of the location, with Khan noting that fans haven't seen Hangman since Revolution in March and that his return to AEW will occur in his home state of Virginia. The appearance marks one of the most newsworthy segments expected on AEW Collision, a show typically known more for compelling wrestling matches than major storyline announcements.

A lot has changed in AEW since Hangman Adam Page left the company. The AEW World title has changed hands multiple times. Most significantly, on the July 8 episode of AEW Dynamite, Kenny Omega defeated MJF to win the AEW World Championship. Interestingly, Omega managed to win despite facing the same "never challenge again" stipulation that had stopped Page at Revolution. This twist makes Page's return even more intriguing. MJF is no longer champion, but the agreement Page accepted still leaves open questions about whether he can compete for the world title again under the original stipulation or if circumstances have changed.

The landscape of AEW has shifted considerably during Page's absence. The world title went from MJF to Darby Allin to MJF again to Kenny Omega. Will Ospreay is waiting in the wings for his world title shot at Wembley Stadium after winning the Owen Hart Cup. Not to mention that Ospreay is now a member of the Death Riders, a group that was a thorn in Hangman's saddle for so long. Given all these changes, it remains to be seen if Page will address his stipulation or move on to a different rivalry altogether.

The July 11 edition of AEW Collision in Roanoke is already stacked with championship matches. Harley Cameron will challenge Hikaru Shida for the TBS Championship. Speedball Mike Bailey will challenge Mark Davis for the AEW National Championship. Katsuyori Shibata will challenge Bandido for the ROH World Championship. With three title matches already scheduled, Hangman's opening segment is positioned as the marquee attraction that will launch the show.

All eyes will be on what Page has to say when he opens AEW Collision. Hangman's absence has lasted several months, so fans are eager to understand where he has been and what his motivation is moving forward, especially with the stipulation that prevents him from chasing the world title. Whether he addresses his future, targets a new rival, or immediately jumps into a new storyline remains to be seen. However, AEW has made it clear that Page will be the first person fans see when the show goes live from his home state, making his segment the focal point of the broadcast and potentially setting the tone for his next chapter in the company.

🔥 Dark Order Re-Signs with AEW

All Elite Wrestling has re-signed three of its original performers from the Dark Order stable. According to multiple reports, Evil Uno, Alex Reynolds, and John Silver have all agreed to new deals with the promotion. The news comes after earlier reports indicated that several AEW contracts were set to expire, including deals for various Dark Order members. Those concerns have now been resolved with these new signings.

The Dark Order has been part of AEW since the company's inception in May 2019. Evil Uno appeared at the inaugural AEW event, Double or Nothing, where he and Stu Grayson led the Dark Order's so-called "Creepers" in an attack on the Best Friends, Angelico, and Jack Evans. John Silver and Alex Reynolds joined the group shortly after, initially losing to Santana & Ortiz on Dynamite in October 2019. Since their early prominence, the group has made occasional appearances on AEW and Ring of Honor programming, though they have not always been heavily featured on television.

In terms of recent activity, Silver was last seen on AEW television in May as part of a backstage segment involving all available bald wrestlers taunting MJF ahead of his hair match against Darby Allin. Before that, none of the three had wrestled on AEW television since December of the previous year, when they lost a trios match to Hook, Katsuyori Shibata, and Powerhouse Hobbs on Dynamite. However, both Evil Uno and Alex Reynolds have been competing regularly for Ring of Honor, while Evil Uno has also participated in dark matches for AEW throughout the year.

Beyond his in-ring responsibilities, Evil Uno is now expanding his role within the promotion. In addition to remaining under contract as a performer, he has taken on a coaching position with both AEW and Ring of Honor. This backstage addition provides the company with veteran experience and expertise while keeping Uno available for future on-screen storylines.

The re-signings represent AEW's commitment to keeping the core members of the Dark Order together and ensuring the stable's future within the promotion. The company clearly saw sufficient value in these veteran wrestlers to secure their services long-term, even during periods when they have not been featured prominently on television. These new deals remove several names from AEW's list of expiring contracts and provide more stability for storyline planning across both the AEW and Ring of Honor brands.

🔥 Jim Ross Brain Surgery Announcement

Jim Ross is preparing to undergo another major medical procedure as the legendary WWE Hall of Famer continues his battle with serious health issues. Ross revealed on the latest episode of Grilling JR podcast that he is scheduled to have brain surgery within the next week or so to address a condition that has significantly impacted his memory and quality of life.

Speaking with co-host Conrad Thompson, Ross explained his current health status while discussing the upcoming procedure. "I feel pretty good. I think it's getting, I'm getting better every day, but still got a ways to go. I can't drive yet. All that stuff. I'm cutting back on my appearances. I got one big one coming up in Minneapolis in a few weeks," Ross said. He acknowledged the road ahead remains challenging, stating "I'm just battling. I'm getting ready to have another surgery, brain surgery this time. That's going to be an adventure, because I don't think they're going to find much in my brain."

The procedure will involve implanting a shunt to drain excess cerebrospinal fluid from Ross's brain, a condition doctors have determined is responsible for his memory issues and cognitive difficulties. Ross described the gravity of the situation in his own words: "This procedure that I'm getting ready to have done, I think it's called a shunt. It'll go from my brain. Title Changes July 10th History

Pro wrestling has seen considerable championship activity on July 10th throughout its history. According to wrestling data, there have been 225 notable and major title changes recorded on this particular date across various wrestling promotions worldwide. The landscape of these title changes has evolved significantly over the decades, with a notable shift occurring in recent years.

The earliest recorded July 10th title change dates back to 1967, when Lou Albano and Tony Altimore captured the WWWF United States Tag Team Titles. Twenty-one years later in 1988, the tradition continued with two significant changes. Bobby Eaton and Stan Lane won the NWA/WCW United States Tag Team Titles on that date, while Zuleyma claimed the Mexican National Women's Title, demonstrating the multi-promotional nature of title changes on this day.

The early 2000s and 2010s saw continued championship activity on July 10th. In 2001, Jeff Hardy won the WWF Hardcore Title. Three years later in 2004, Yoshinobu Kanemaru captured the GHC Junior Heavyweight Title. The year 2011 proved particularly active, with Go Shiozaki winning the GHC Heavyweight Title and Brian Kendrick claiming the TNA X-Division Title on the same date. In 2012, El Sagrado won the Occidente Light Heavyweight Title, though the ROH World Tag Team Titles were vacated rather than changing hands.

However, the pattern of active title changes appears to have diminished in recent years. In 2024, three championships were recorded as vacated on July 10th rather than being won by new champions.
