MJF Says People Don't Talk About This AEW Star Enough

AEW Men's World Champion MJF is not typically one to label wrestlers as underrated or overrated, but he made an exception during a recent appearance on Late Night Grin when discussing one particular AEW star who he believes deserves more recognition.

"I don't think people talk about Mark Davis enough," MJF said during the interview. "How's that? I think Mark Davis, and he's getting his flowers, but he's not getting enough flowers. That dude is next f*****g level. He's probably one of the best big men in the sport right now. Not probably, I take that back, he's one of the best big men in the sport right now."

Davis originally arrived in AEW as part of Aussie Open alongside Kyle Fletcher. However, the talented big man suffered several significant injury setbacks that resulted in a combined 18 months away from in-ring competition between 2024 and 2025. Following his recovery, Davis has emerged strong in 2026 as a member of the Don Callis Family. While Davis was dealing with his injuries, Fletcher saw his stock rise considerably as a singles competitor and was widely regarded as one of AEW's fastest rising stars before suffering a recent injury of his own.

According to MJF, Davis's injury troubles have directly contributed to him being underrated. The AEW champion even pondered whether Davis might have achieved similar success to Fletcher had the situation been reversed, with Davis remaining healthy while Fletcher dealt with injuries instead.

"That's another reason I think he's underrated," MJF explained on Late Night Grin. "I think if Mark Davis was given these same platform as Kyle was, or if the roles were reversed even. If Kyle got first...who's to say? I mean, they're both so talented. Tremendously different performers and athletes, and looks. But they're both so great. And they both connect in very different ways through very different ways."

MJF's comments highlight how circumstances and timing can significantly impact a wrestler's trajectory and public perception, even when talent levels are comparable.
