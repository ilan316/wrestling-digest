🔥 Cedric Alexander career and matches

Cedric Alexander has become increasingly vocal about the pivotal moments and creative evolution throughout his wrestling career. Speaking in recent interviews, the veteran discussed what made his match against Kota Ibushi during the 2016 WWE Cruiserweight Classic tournament so memorable and impactful. Alexander explained that the match stood out because of its authenticity during a time when many fans were not yet familiar with him as a competitor.

"That's probably the most popular time in my career that people remember, just because of the Kota Ibushi match. Everyone remembers that match," Alexander said. He reflected on what made the encounter special, noting that after considerable thought, he believes authenticity was the key factor. "From that whole tournament, that whole time, dare I say that whole year, that one match sticks out for what made wrestling beautiful, I think. That was a time in my career where no one really knew who I was. People were aware of me, but it was like 'Oh, this guy's cool.' But then they see what I did with Kota and it was like 'Oh no, this guy's amazing.' It really made me feel connected to wrestling more."

The match with Ibushi proved to be the launchpad Alexander needed, ultimately leading to a nine-year run with WWE during which he held the Cruiserweight Championship, the 24/7 Championship, and the Raw Tag Team Championship. He also formed notable alliances with MVP, Shelton Benjamin, and Bobby Lashley, most famously as part of The Hurt Business stable. While some fans hoped to see a reunion of this group in AEW after they split ahead of the pandemic's end, Alexander chose a different path.

In June 2025, Alexander officially joined TNA Wrestling, a decision he has since explained in detail. Rather than reuniting with his former Hurt Business colleagues in AEW, Alexander found himself drawn to TNA's creative environment. He described his previous situation as restrictive, explaining that before coming to TNA, he felt caught within limitations regarding what he could and could not do as a performer.

"I felt like before coming to TNA, I was still caught in that bubble of you can't do this, you can't do that. You know, there's so many barriers put on, you know, things you can and can't do," Alexander stated. However, his experience in TNA has been markedly different. "TNA is really freeing in that respect because if you can imagine it, if it's something that works for you and you can put it out there, let's run with it. And TNA's been very good with allowing me to just express myself. A more authentic version of myself."

Since joining TNA, Alexander has been actively involved in the company's programming and has taken time to evaluate the emerging talent across the wrestling landscape. He expressed particular admiration for three wrestlers who represent the next generation of the industry: Leon Slater from TNA, Je'Von Evans from WWE, and Jason Hotch also from TNA.

Regarding his match against Leon Slater at TNA Emergence in August 2025, Alexander spoke glowingly about the encounter. "The match I had with Leon Slater back at Emergence was one of my favorite matches I've had in a long time. That was the first time I really got to really work with Leon and he surprised me, man. It was really refreshing to see, in this phase of my career being the established veteran going against a younger kid, just kinda seeing, not only do I still have it but man, the younger generation is getting hot right now."

When discussing the broader slate of young talent making their mark, Alexander credited them with making him proud to be a wrestler. "Guys like that really make me proud to be a wrestler. To steal a phrase from my old trainer George South, they did it the right way. They came in, paid their dues, really learned from the business and really adapted their own style, their own personalities. Like a Leon Slater, like a Je'Von Evans, or to even speak more about TNA guys, like a Jason Hotch, who I think is very underrated. Guys like that, they really deserve the spotlight, and I'm glad they're getting that time."

Alexander has also spoken with confidence about his role within TNA's faction landscape. He is currently a member of The System, and he expressed a strong belief in his ability to elevate any group he joins. "When I join factions, they get cool. That's just a fact. The Hurt Business was cool, but then it got better when I jumped in. It got defined and polished. I think The System was the same thing, which is why we got rid of Moose, brought in the true athlete, the true franchise."

Looking ahead, Alexander is scheduled to challenge for the TNA X-Division Championship at the upcoming TNA Rebellion pay-per-view event. His continued presence in TNA signals a wrestler entering a new chapter of his career, one defined by creative autonomy and the satisfaction of working with and mentoring the next wave of wrestling talent.

🔥 NJPW Sakura Genesis AEW involvement

NJPW Sakura Genesis took place on Saturday, April 4, 2026, at the Ryogoku Kokugikan in Tokyo, Japan, and delivered multiple shocking moments with significant inter-promotional implications. The event featured prominent involvement from All Elite Wrestling talent, mystery masked men interfering in matches, and storyline developments that could reshape both promotions' landscapes.

The main event saw history made when Callum Newman, at just 23 years old, defeated IWGP World Heavyweight Champion Yota Tsuji to become the youngest champion in the history of the prestigious title. Tsuji had won the championship at Wrestle Kingdom 20 in January 2026 and was expected to hold it considerably longer. Newman, the current leader of the United Empire faction founded by Will Ospreay, earned a shocking victory by connecting with the Prince's Curse followed by his Make Way finisher to secure the three count and stun the crowd.

Will Ospreay, who had wrestled earlier in the show, was the first to embrace the new champion after the match and put the IWGP Championship around Newman's waist. The show initially closed with all members of United Empire, including Ospreay, posing with the newly crowned champion as the crowd celebrated the historic moment.

However, the celebration did not last long. A hooded figure suddenly appeared at ringside and attacked the recovering Yota Tsuji with a piledriver. The masked assailant then removed his hood and jacket to reveal himself as Gabe Kidd, an AEW talent who is a member of The Dogs alongside David Finlay and Clark Connors. Kidd unzipped his jacket to prominently display an AEW t-shirt, making a clear statement about his current wrestling home. Shingo Takagi came out for the save but Kidd landed a punch before exiting the area. Takagi recovered and eventually crossed paths with Newman, issuing the first challenge for the IWGP World Heavyweight Championship.

Following the match, Kidd declared war on New Japan Pro-Wrestling during backstage comments. He directed his ire at the company's leadership and roster, stating he was declaring war on what he called a "shitty company" and calling out president Hiroshi Tanahashi and chairman Naoki Sugabayashi. Kidd specifically targeted Tsuji, who had positioned himself as the "main character" of New Japan, suggesting that his attack was part of this broader declaration of war. The attack also notably involved the IWGP Global Championship, which Kidd had previously held before losing it to Tsuji back in October.

The inter-promotional angle continued backstage when Will Ospreay approached the newly crowned champion at the post-show press conference. Ospreay expressed that he wanted to discuss something with Newman privately, stating, "There's something I kind of wanted to talk to you guys about. I didn't want to do this in front of you on your big moment." He went on to explain that he has been dealing with numbers game disadvantages in AEW involving Jon Moxley, PAC, and the rest of The Death Riders since returning from surgery. "I've got some trouble at work, and I could really do with some backup," Ospreay said. "I know you just wrestled. I could really use a hand." Newman indicated they would discuss the matter further at a later time.

This approach from Ospreay comes with added context about potential tension within the United Empire itself. Earlier in the evening, when Zane Jay, a United Empire member and U.S. Dojo Young Lion, offered Newman a steel chair to use against Tsuji, Ospreay pleaded with his countryman to win the match fairly. Newman heeded his mentor's advice and won clean, which pleased Ospreay. However, tension emerged after Ospreay's own match earlier in the card when Great-O-Khan and Henare, fellow United Empire originals, launched an assault on opponents Yuya Uemura and El Desperado after their six-man tag team match. Ospreay did not approve of this behavior, suggesting potential internal discord within the faction.

Earlier in the evening, Ospreay had teamed with Great-O-Khan and Henare to defeat Yuya Uemura, Taichi, and El Desperado in a featured six-man tag team match. Ospreay secured the victory by pinning Taichi with his Hidden Blade and Stormbreaker maneuver, marking his first NJPW win since 2023. The post-match assault by O-Khan and Henare on the defeated opponents appeared to plant seeds for a potential eventual split between Ospreay and some United Empire members, suggesting that Ospreay's moral compass may be pulling away from the faction's more aggressive tendencies.

Another inter-promotional angle unfolded during the NJPW World Television Championship match. Konosuke Takeshita, an AEW star and the reigning NJPW World Television Champion, went to a 15-minute time-limit draw in a title defense against Shota Umino. A mystery man in a mask and suit appeared at ringside, hopping up on the ring apron to distract "The Alpha." While Takeshita was distracted, Chase Owens attacked him from behind. Owens continued his assault until Umino came back to the ring for the save. The identity of the masked mystery man remains unclear, adding another layer of intrigue to the evening's events.

Will Ospreay is scheduled to challenge Jon Moxley of The Death Riders for the AEW Continental Championship at the upcoming AEW Dynasty 2026 pay-per-view, which takes place on April 12 from Rogers Arena in Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada. His plea for backup from United Empire and Callum Newman suggests he may be attempting to secure allies from New Japan to help even the odds against The Death Riders' numbers advantage.

The events at Sakura Genesis have set up compelling storylines for both promotions, with AEW's Gabe Kidd now seemingly at war with New Japan, tensions brewing within the United Empire, and Ospreay seeking international support in his battle against The Death Riders.

Billy Jack Haynes Murder Trial

Billy Jack Haynes Set To Stand Trial In Wife's Death After Being Ruled Fit To Face Charges

William Albert Haynes III, known professionally as Billy Jack Haynes, is now set to stand trial in connection with the death of his wife following a court determination that he is mentally fit to proceed. The ruling marks a significant development in a case that has experienced considerable delays since the incident occurred.

According to reports from local news outlets in Portland, the decision to move forward came after Haynes was previously ruled mentally unfit to assist in his own defense in May 2025. That determination paused legal proceedings while he underwent evaluation at Oregon State Hospital, a process originally expected to take between one and three years. However, authorities have now determined he is fit to stand trial, allowing the case to advance toward resolution.

Judge Angela Lucero has set the trial date for April 26 through May 7, 2027. Haynes was charged with second-degree murder and unlawful use of a weapon following the death of his wife, Janette Becraft, which occurred in February 2024.

The incident unfolded when authorities were called to a residence in Portland after receiving reports of a shooting around 9:52 a.m. local time. Upon arrival, officers encountered a tense situation that escalated into a standoff lasting approximately two hours before Haynes was taken into custody. During the incident, neighbors were ordered to shelter in place while tactical teams and crisis negotiators worked to bring the situation under control. After Haynes was detained, authorities confirmed that Becraft, 85, had been found dead inside the home. The medical examiner later ruled her death a homicide caused by a gunshot wound.

A neighbor with close ties to the family spoke publicly about the emotional impact of the tragedy. The neighbor, whose father lived nearby and helped care for the couple, described being closely acquainted with both Haynes and Becraft and having recently assisted with her care during her battle with dementia. The neighbor's comments reflected the shock felt throughout the community following the tragedy.

Beyond the criminal charges, Haynes also faces a civil lawsuit filed by the estate of his late wife. That lawsuit alleges years of abuse and exploitation, including claims that he coerced Becraft into isolating from family members, forced financial changes to her estate, and influenced her medical care decisions. The civil suit also alleges that he pressured her into purchasing a firearm because he was legally prohibited from buying one himself due to a prior criminal record.

Before these legal troubles, Haynes enjoyed a lengthy career in professional wrestling. He was widely known in wrestling circles, particularly in the Pacific Northwest territory, where he built a reputation as a tough competitor. Haynes was known for using the full nelson as his signature finishing move. He later appeared in several national promotions, including WWE in the late 1980s, where he engaged in notable feuds with Hercules Hernandez over their respective finishing holds.

While his wrestling career once brought him national attention and recognition, the upcoming trial now places him back in headlines for circumstances far removed from his athletic accomplishments. With the court determining that he is mentally fit to stand trial, the legal process is expected to move forward after years of delays that have characterized this case.

Gabe Kidd NJPW conflict

Gabe Kidd has dramatically shifted his allegiance, going from passionately representing New Japan Pro-Wrestling to declaring outright war on the company. At NJPW Sakura Genesis on Saturday, Kidd attacked Yota Tsuji after Tsuji lost the IWGP Heavyweight Title to Callum Newman. The assault continued backstage, where Kidd posted footage to social media that captured his scathing criticism of the organization.

In his backstage tirade, Kidd expressed his renewed passion for professional wrestling after what he described as losing his love for the game last year. He stated: "It feels like I'm alive again. I come here from AEW. Let's talk about people talking about my decision and my choice. My love of this game fucking died last year, but I got it back again. I am waging war on this fucking company."

Kidd's grievances extended to NJPW's leadership and personnel, specifically naming Tanahashi and Sugabayashi among those he targeted. He vented his frustration at what he perceived as unfair scrutiny directed at him while other departures went unquestioned. "Nobody wants to question why Ospreay left. Nobody wants to question why EVIL, why Hiromu left, but you all wanna put it on me? Fuck the lot of yous. Fuck every single one of yous," Kidd declared.

The English wrestler's condemnation of the company was comprehensive and unsparing. "New Japan is a fucking joke," he stated, adding that he had no respect for its defenders or young prospects. His declaration became increasingly emphatic as he concluded: "We're waging war on this company. We are waging war on New Japan Pro-Wrestling. Fuck this company."

Beyond his verbal assault, Kidd set his sights on the IWGP Global Championship held by Tsuji. He declared his intention to remove the belt from Japan entirely, stating: "When it comes to this, I'm gonna take this back and I'm gonna keep it well away from Japan. This is a global belt, right? I'm gonna take this to America and make it fucking famous."

Kidd's journey with NJPW began in early 2020, establishing himself as a core talent for the promotion. In the summer of 2023, he joined the Bullet Club War Dogs faction, which became central to NJPW's storytelling for an extended period. However, Kidd eventually departed for AEW alongside faction members Clark Connors and David Finlay, spending considerably more time with AEW than NJPW during 2026.

Despite his current animosity toward the company, Kidd's tenure in NJPW produced significant moments. His match against Kenny Omega at Wrestle Dynasty in 2025, which was built around Kidd representing NJPW against Omega, a wrestler who left to help form AEW, earned widespread acclaim. The bout was voted Pro Wrestling Match of the Year in the 2025 Wrestling Observer Newsletter Awards, cementing it as a highlight of his career.

Mike Santana wrestling

Mike Santana has opened up about multiple aspects of his current run as TNA World Champion, discussing both his distinctive entrance and the responsibility that comes with holding the company's top title.

Regarding his entrance, Santana explained the thought process behind walking through the crowd to reach the ring. He noted that this approach serves multiple purposes, including being a throwback to his original LAX days during his first TNA run when the group would also come through the crowd. However, the primary motivation is more symbolic. "Everything I do is very deliberate. Everything has a purpose," Santana stated. "I always said with this run that I'm on just in my career in general and what I represent and what I'm putting out there, the whole thing is, I'm bringing the fans on this journey and on this ride with me."

Santana believes this connection with the audience is a significant factor in why fans have responded so positively to him. He explained, "I literally walk through the crowd and as I'm walking through the crowd, it's almost like I'm grabbing everyone and putting them on my back and taking them with me to the ring." Beyond the symbolism, Santana emphasized that there is genuine value in the direct interaction with fans. "There's nothing like getting the energy from the audience. That energy that they give is why we do what we do. So I always say, you give me your energy. I'm going to give it right back and we're going to put it together, and boy, oh boy, whoever's standing across the ring from me, they're going to get a hell of a ride."

In discussing his role as TNA World Champion, Santana highlighted that being the face of a promotion extends far beyond in-ring performance. After betting on himself and leaving AEW to join TNA Wrestling, Santana won the TNA World Title back from Frankie Kazarian on the Impact AMC premiere in January, marking his second championship reign. He has since made numerous appearances in WWE NXT as well, including a hometown appearance at The Theater in Madison Square Garden.

When speaking about his position as champion, Santana emphasized the broader responsibilities involved. "Being the champion and the face of a promotion is much more than just what you're doing in the ring. You're in this position because you're trusted to represent the company in all aspects. Whether it's media, whether it's going to another promotion and representing, whether it's being on TV. The fact that you're being placed in this spot and being trusted is everything."

Santana stressed that reliability is a crucial quality in professional wrestling, and he believes that is why the company has placed him in this position. "One thing that you always wanna be in this business is reliable, and I think that's definitely, definitely what the company sees in me. If not, then I wouldn't be in this position, right?"

Reflecting on his journey and mindset, Santana emphasized his commitment to continuous improvement. "I'm always striving to get better. Whoever thinks that they know it all, they're not doing it right. I'm constantly with the mindset of – like my dad used to say – the top of one mountain is the bottom of another."

Looking at his overall position, Santana expressed confidence in his current level while acknowledging that his growth is far from complete. "But overall, yes, I am the champion that I knew I could be. Betting on myself and putting all the chips on the table when it came to myself, and saying, 'Hey, I wanna be at that top level and I'm gonna put in the work, I'm not just gonna say it.' And what I've done since becoming champion is constantly show and prove why I'm at that level. And honestly, it's just the beginning, and I feel like I'm only gonna get better."

Santana also previewed his upcoming TNA World Title defense at TNA Rebellion, continuing his active run as the company's top champion.

Ron Garvin criticism of modern wrestling

The pro wrestling industry is one that's in a constant state of evolution. Spots and angles done in the 1990s and 2000s were alien to the veterans of the time, and today's product is an even greater departure. Former NWA World Heavyweight Champion and bitter rival of Ric Flair Ron Garvin is one of the old school veterans who recently expressed his distaste for the current day scene.

During an appearance on Busted Open Radio, Garvin voiced his concerns about the direction wrestling has taken. "Wrestling has changed so much, so much! Sometimes I don't understand it, and I watch matches and I go, 'Why did they do that?'" he stated. He clarified that while he sees good things in modern wrestling, they are often out of place. "I watch other sports, but wrestling? Because sometimes it's a little boring and I've seen matches – it's embarrassing," he added.

Garvin recounted a specific incident that exemplified his frustration with contemporary wrestling. He was attending a signing event and was asked to stay afterward to watch some of the matches performed at the post-show. However, he simply couldn't bring himself to sit through the wrestling. "I hated it – I don't lie – [the promoter] said: 'What do you think?' ... I said: 'It sucks!' You know? 'It's horrible!'" Garvin recalled. "You know, I wouldn't even put that in front of kids! You know, they'd be laughing, thinking they're chimpanzees or some damn thing."

The veteran's assessment was blunt in his conclusion about the state of professional wrestling. "Good wrestling is – I don't know if it still exists," Garvin stated.

Peter Rosenberg Chris Hero Twitter controversy

Peter Rosenberg's back-and-forth with Chris Hero just took another turn, and this time it ended with a deleted tweet.

The situation stems from the fallout of Iyo Sky's botched dive on the March 30, 2026 episode of WWE RAW, which led Peter Rosenberg to criticize high-risk dives. Hero then mocked Rosenberg by parodying his comments online, turning the debate into a full-blown back-and-forth.

Rosenberg didn't ignore it. He took to Twitter on April 3, 2026 and fired back directly at Hero, making it clear he had something to say and framing it like he was about to lay everything out.

"After this commercial I have some words for everyone who had something to say about my thoughts this week .. @ChrisHero pull up a chair and listen .. schools in session"

The tweet immediately made the rounds, but so did the backlash. Fans pushed back hard on Rosenberg's tone, especially the idea of him "schooling" a veteran like Hero, who's widely respected for his in-ring experience. Not long after that reaction picked up steam, the tweet was deleted.

That move quickly became part of the story itself, with the focus shifting from the original debate about risky dives to Rosenberg's response and why it didn't stay up for long. What started as a discussion about in-ring safety turned into a public back-and-forth, with Rosenberg's response and subsequent deletion becoming the latest talking point.

NJPW Best of Super Juniors 33

NJPW has officially announced the complete lineup for Best of the Super Juniors 33 following a week-long reveal process. The final two participants were unveiled during Saturday's Sakura Genesis event, with Nick Wayne of AEW and Jun Kasai of FREEDOMS officially confirmed as tournament entrants.

Wayne, the current ROH Television Champion, is making his second appearance in the tournament after making his debut last year. He recently returned from a foot injury that sidelined him for several months, marking an important recovery milestone in his career. Kasai is returning to the tournament after generating significant attention last year following a high-profile death match against El Desperado. The two competitors will have an opportunity to team together in the coming months, as they are scheduled to compete as partners during the Deathmatch Invitacional that will take place during WrestleMania weekend, where they will face Nick Gage and Matt Tremont as well as Rina Yamashita and Masashi Takeda in a three-way deathmatch.

Block A of the tournament features DOUKI, Francesco Akira, Robbie X, Nick Wayne, Titan, Valiente Jr., Kosei Fujita, Jun Kasai, Ryusuke Taguchi, and Master Wato.

Block B features El Desperado, YOH, Daisuke Sasaki, KUSHIDA, Robbie Eagles, Jakob Austin Young, Taiji Ishimori, Hyo SHO, and Yoshinobu Kanemaru.

The tournament begins on May 14 at Korakuen Hall and will conclude on June 7 in Ota.

Leon Slater On Being Part Of John Cena's WWE Retirement: 'Like A Dream'

TNA prodigy Leon Slater has already captured the TNA X Division Championship at 21 years old, making him the youngest person to hold the title, especially since he was only 20 when he first won the belt. Slater's quick success has impressed many veterans, like Matt Hardy, but especially John Cena who handpicked him twice during his final run as one of the Men's Iron Survivor Challenge participants and to compete against AJ Styles and Dragon Lee at Cena's retirement show.

During an appearance on the Battleground Podcast, Slater recalled the emotions he felt to represent TNA in WWE. "It was a mad one, you know? It was really, really wild because it was... It almost feels like a dream when I look back on it, you know what I mean?" he said, adding that he still has to pinch himself to confirm that he was on the same match card as Cena.

"WrestleMania happens every year, SummerSlam happens every year, and that doesn't take away from how huge that is, but there's only one John Cena, and there's only one John Cena retirement show," Slater expressed. "The fact that I don't even work there and I was on John Cena's last show, as the X-Division Champion – coming out with it around my neck – representing my brand and my people? It was something that I hold very, very close to my heart."

Slater maintained that he still can't believe it was real, and noted how he saw so many of his childhood wrestling heroes backstage, like Kurt Angle and The Undertaker. "It was the best day of my life, man," he said.

WWE Matches To Miss Out On WrestleMania 42 Card

WWE has been announcing plenty of matches to make this year's WrestleMania 42 card look like one of the most stacked in the history of the event. While there have been several bouts added in the last week, there have also been certain matches that many fans believed would be on the lineup not announced or scheduled to be added before the company heads to Las Vegas on April 18 and 19.

From title bouts to grudge matches to settle scores, some notable contests will not be seen during the two-night event. Here are seven WWE matches to potentially miss out on the WrestleMania 42 card.

On the March 20 edition of Friday Night SmackDown, Carmelo Hayes defeated Ilja Dragunov to retain the WWE United States Championship. Hayes and Dragunov have been telling the story of their competitive rivalry turned friendship over the US Title for the past several months since they traded the gold in December 2025. However, it seems like this bout will not happen again for the gold at WrestleMania 42 after Hayes dropped the United States Championship to Sami Zayn on the March 27 SmackDown. With Zayn now scheduled to face Trick Williams at Mania, Hayes and Dragunov do not have their path to be on the WrestleMania 42 card at the time of this writing.

Over the past few weeks, Tiffany Stratton has set her sights on the WWE Women's United States Championship. Giulia currently holds the gold in her second reign with the title, but Stratton might just be the biggest threat to this run. The Beautiful Madness seemingly turned back this challenge when she defeated Stratton in a non-title singles match on the March 27 episode of Friday Night SmackDown. Thanks to this loss, fans might not see a rematch between Giulia and Stratton for the Women's US Title come WrestleMania 42.

On the March 30 edition of Monday Night Raw, The Vision's Logan Paul and Austin Theory defeated The Usos in a Street Fight to win the World Tag Team Championships thanks to iShowSpeed. Before Paul used his signature brass knuckles to knock out Jimmy Uso, it was iShowSpeed who knocked out LA Knight with those same knucks. This Street Fight from this past Monday may have set the stage for a huge six-man tag team match with The Vision teaming with Speed to face Knight and The Usos at WrestleMania 42. With all four men accompanied in this trios contest, it feels like The Usos and The Vision will not be able to compete for the WWE World Tag Team Championships at The Showcase of the Immortals.
