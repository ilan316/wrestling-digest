🔥 Mick Foley joins AEW

Mick Foley Joins AEW and Reflects on His Debut

More than a week after making his AEW debut at Double or Nothing, Mick Foley posted a video to social media reflecting on his experience joining Tony Khan's promotion. The WWE Hall of Famer took time to thank everyone involved in making his debut special, including the fans who gave him a reaction that felt like genuine appreciation rather than mere nostalgia.

Foley explained that the response he received in New York was distinctly different from a typical nostalgia pop. The reaction conveyed that the audience was genuinely glad to have him there and believed he could still make a meaningful difference in professional wrestling. This reception stood in stark contrast to what he has experienced in recent years, resonating deeply with him on a personal level.

In addressing his transition to AEW, Foley made it explicitly clear that his decision should not be interpreted as criticism of WWE. He emphasized his enduring love and appreciation for the company, stating that he will always be deeply grateful for every opportunity WWE afforded him throughout his legendary career. However, he identified a fundamental difference he observes in AEW's leadership and company culture.

Foley highlighted what he sees as a unique quality in Tony Khan's approach to running the company. Unlike Vince McMahon's different management style, Khan displays an obvious happiness and excitement about being involved in professional wrestling. This enthusiasm and joy permeates throughout the organization in a way that energizes everyone around him.

The AEW environment particularly resonates with Foley because it reminds him of WWE during the Attitude Era. He explained that during that era, wrestlers actively pulled for one another while understanding that striking out was part of the process of hitting monumental home runs. In AEW, wrestlers are encouraged to take chances and try new ideas without constantly worrying about management's approval or disapproval.

Foley noted that he has observed many wrestlers over the years become almost petrified before walking through the curtain because they were unsure whether they would receive a metaphorical thumbs up or thumbs down from management. This fear stifles creativity and risk-taking. In contrast, Tony Khan's visible excitement and passion for wrestling creates an atmosphere where performers feel comfortable taking chances.

He articulated the competitive spirit he sees in AEW's locker room, saying that wrestlers are out there playing to win because it is understood that failure is part of the process. This acceptance of failure as a necessary component of achieving success fundamentally changes how performers approach their craft. The understanding that not every attempt will result in success, but that swinging for the fences is encouraged and valued, creates an environment where innovation can flourish.

At Double or Nothing, Foley served as a co-host on the preshow alongside Renee Paquette. He has officially signed an AEW deal for this broadcasting role, but he views himself as having broader potential within the company. Describing himself as a "Swiss Army knife," Foley indicated he is ready and willing to contribute in a variety of ways to AEW's operations and programming.

During his debut appearance, Foley participated in an angle with MJF that resulted in a low blow, suggesting his involvement may extend beyond broadcasting duties. However, observers have noted hope that any physical involvement remains limited given his history of serious concussions throughout his career. Rather than competing in matches, the preference is for Foley to leverage his storytelling and communication skills to create compelling wrestling narratives.

Foley expressed genuine excitement about his future with AEW and about professional wrestling itself. He stated that he has rediscovered his passion for wrestling in a way he had not experienced in several years. The combination of AEW's environment, creative freedom, and the enthusiasm of both the company's leadership and its roster has reignited his love for the industry.

He concluded by thanking both AEW fans and the AEW product itself for helping him fall in love with professional wrestling all over again. This sentiment underscores the personal significance of this new chapter in his career, representing not just a business decision but an emotional reconnection with the art form that defined his life.

Will Ospreay Styles Clash at AEW Double or Nothing

AJ Styles Commends Will Ospreay's Styles Clash at AEW Double or Nothing

It's no secret that AEW star Will Ospreay is a huge fan of "The Phenomenal" AJ Styles. Ospreay has credited Styles for being one of the reasons he became a wrestler, and has since adopted the Styles Clash as one of his own moves. At the recent AEW Double or Nothing 2026 pay-per-view, Ospreay faced one of Styles' greatest rivals in Samoa Joe during an Owen Hart Cup Tournament match, and even got to hit Joe with the Styles Clash. Styles himself witnessed the clip and shared his thoughts.

During the latest episode of "The Phenomenally Retro Podcast," Styles commended Ospreay for not only hitting the move but also his technique since executing that on someone like Joe is not easy. "I seen the Styles Clash onto Joe. I can tell you hitting Joe with the Styles Clash is not an easy task, I've done it a couple of times in my career. But man, he pulled it off flawlessly," Styles said.

Styles also discussed how his new talent scout position with WWE scratches an itch he has now that he is retired from active in-ring competition. "It scratches that itch so that I don't have to get in the ring again," Styles explained. "I can kind of just live through these guys and these characters that they're doing and just being a part of what they're doing. That's what it's scratching that itch for me. And I'm so proud of these guys and the girls, man, to work with them and then see them do well."

During the same podcast episode, Styles was asked about recent videos of his son training in a wrestling ring and hitting a Spiral Tap, a move that Styles himself couldn't do towards the end of his career. When his co-host mentioned that he feels it's only a matter of time before Ospreay pulls that one out in an AEW match, Styles recalled actually being hit with the move by Ospreay during a previous encounter. "He can do it, I've seen him, I've seen him do it. He hit a standing one on me, not off the top rope, just a standing one on me. When we wrestled each other years ago, not only did he hit me with the leap frog/drop down/drop kick, and then he hit me with my own Spiral Tap too," Styles said.

Styles also reflected on the nature of professional wrestling and the importance of learning from mistakes. He acknowledged that things don't always go perfect for talent, but pointed out how the proper reaction to such situations can help you going forward. "And of course, it's not perfect. It's never going to be perfect. Even when you get on the main roster, it's not going to be perfect. But that's what we do. If you're mad that you screwed something up, something else on the show, great, that's a great attitude. That way you don't make the same mistake twice. So that's a good thing if you're not happy with your match, great. Let's make sure the next one's better. And then you'll learn from mistakes. That's what this whole experience is about," Styles continued.

The likes of Jordynne Grace and Joe Hendry have made their way to WWE and were recently called up to the main roster. Looking at the current TNA Wrestling landscape, there are other names that might be able to make a similar statement upon signing with WWE and make their way up the ladder in a major way.

At number seven on the list is The Righteous, consisting of Vincent and Dutch. Signing with TNA Wrestling in December 2025, The Righteous have become one of the most prominent tag teams in the company. Vincent and Dutch came in with established characters that has made them a regular part of TNA programming. Their rivalry with The Hardys has made The Righteous future challengers for the TNA World Tag Team Championship. With everything they have done to cement themselves as a television act in TNA, The Righteous might be ready for the next stage of their careers by signing with WWE in the near future.

Ranked sixth is Moose, who has been one of the biggest stars in TNA Wrestling since signing with the promotion in 2016. Over the course of a decade with the company, Moose is a three-time TNA World Champion, two-time Grand Champion and a former X-Division Champion. Considering his size, experience and ability in the ring, Moose would be an excellent addition to the WWE roster. Moose signing with WWE feels inevitable and could lead to even more success for his career after everything he has accomplished in TNA for the past ten years.

At number five is Ricky Sosa, one of the most significant signings of 2026 for TNA Wrestling. The European standout is known for his "Bang, Bang, Sosa, Sosa" theme song and entrance, which has caught a ton of attention that has made him an exciting star to sign for any promotion. While he has officially signed with TNA, Sosa has not been on television much and may be awaiting visa clearance. As soon as he catches the eye of WWE, it feels like Sosa would be someone the company would be interested in bringing in long term.

Number four on the list is Elayna Black. At the start of 2026, Elayna Black signed with TNA Wrestling as a major acquisition for the company. The former Cora Jade has become a regular fixture on TNA television and feels like someone who could become a top contender for the Knockouts World Title. At just 25 years old, it always felt like Black could wind up back in WWE after being released from the promotion in May 2025. Black re-signing with WWE would be interesting and might see her rise up the ladder quicker than she did in her first stint with the company.

The Hardys rank at number three on this list. In 2025, The Hardys made their return to WWE as part of the working relationship with TNA Wrestling. In partnership with WWE NXT, Matt and Jeff Hardy made their presence felt by winning the NXT Tag Team Championship and getting into a feud with DarkState. Since returning to TNA in 2024, The Hardys have become among the biggest stars and draws for the company. Despite the status as top stars in the promotion, The Hardys feel likely to re-sign with WWE down the line for one final run to end their legendary careers.

Mike Santana comes in at number two. Since re-signing with TNA Wrestling in 2024, Mike Santana had a rapid rise up the ladder in the promotion. Then in 2025, Santana captured the TNA World Championship for the first time and has become the face of the company. Now a two-time TNA World Champion, Santana has been a vocal leader for the locker room and someone who has grown as a main event level talent. After several appearances on WWE NXT over the past year, Santana feels more than ready to sign with the company when his TNA Wrestling contract expires later this year.

Topping the list at number one is Leon Slater. Leon Slater has become someone pegged as the future of TNA Wrestling by fans, peers and legendary stars. In 2025, Slater made history by becoming the youngest X-Division Champion of all time and then later tied the record for the longest reign in the title's history.

WWE Lawsuit Over ESPN PLE Streaming Access Takes New Turn With Latest Filing

The legal battle over WWE premium live event access through ESPN's streaming platform continues to develop as plaintiffs in a proposed class action lawsuit are now pushing back against efforts to pause the discovery process. The lawsuit, originally filed in January, accuses WWE of engaging in deceptive marketing practices related to PLE availability on ESPN Unlimited.

According to the complaint, some fans who already paid for ESPN through cable packages or other subscription services were still required to purchase an additional monthly subscription in order to watch WWE premium live events. The plaintiffs contend that the requirement conflicted with promotional messaging from both WWE and ESPN, arguing that marketing materials led consumers to believe that existing ESPN subscribers would automatically receive access to WWE's premium live event programming without needing to pay extra fees.

A new filing submitted by the plaintiffs seeks to prevent discovery from being stayed while the case moves forward. The filing argues that WWE should not be trusted to preserve potentially relevant evidence, citing recent legal findings involving WWE President Nick Khan. That argument stems from a separate lawsuit tied to the WWE-UFC merger, where both Vince McMahon and Khan were sanctioned after the court determined evidence had been destroyed. The latest filing suggests those findings raise concerns about allowing a delay in the discovery process.

Meanwhile, WWE has continued to seek a pause in discovery while it attempts to move the dispute into arbitration. The company has pointed to the arbitration clause contained within ESPN's streaming user agreement and argues that the matter should be handled outside of court. ESPN also became more directly involved in the case earlier this year. In March, the media company filed a motion to intervene as the legal proceedings continued to unfold.

For now, the court must decide whether discovery will proceed or be temporarily halted while the arbitration issue is resolved.

Seth Rollins Vs Bron Breakker Rematch To Headline WWE Raw June 1, More Matches Announced

WWE Raw General Manager Adam Pearce has announced new matches for Monday Night Raw, confirming the main event of the upcoming show. The June 1 edition of Raw will emanate from the Inalpi Arena in Turin, Italy, the same venue as the previous night's Clash In Italy premium live event.

With the May 31 PLE now in the rearview mirror, Pearce has announced the lineup for the red brand's first show post-Clash In Italy. In his mini-preview video, Pearce confirmed that Seth Rollins vs Bron Breakker will be the main event for the evening.

Pearce also announced a tag-team match for the card, with Rey Mysterio and Dragon Lee to face off against Ethan Page and Rusev. The GM also reiterated that first round matches from both the King of the Ring and Queen of the Ring tournaments will take place, however he didn't announce the specific matches which will happen in Turin.

The announced card for WWE Raw is as follows: Seth Rollins vs Bron Breakker, Rey Mysterio and Dragon Lee vs Ethan Page and Rusev, King and Queen of the Ring first round matches, and Jacob Fatu to acknowledge Roman Reigns.

This week's main event is a rematch from Backlash, where Breakker defeated Seth Rollins in singles action. While Breakker had said ahead of the May 9 match that he was hoping to put his and Rollins' story to bed at Backlash, Breakker will have to face his former Vision teammate at least once more before he can pursue other goals.

Bron Breakker is currently a WWE World Tag Team Championship, taking Logan Paul's place in the team alongside Austin Theory, following Paul's recent tricep injury. The result of the main event will indicate whether or not WWE intends to have Breakker be a serious contender in the singles division while also holding the tag title, as it would be hard to deny the second-generation star a singles title match if he's able to put Rollins away for a second time.
