🔥 AEW sues TrillerTV over unpaid revenue

AEW Takes Legal Action Against Former Partner Over Alleged $5 Million Dispute

All Elite Wrestling has officially taken legal action against TrillerTV over millions of dollars in alleged unpaid revenue, marking a significant escalation in the breakdown of their business relationship. According to a lawsuit filed on April 29 in Duval County Court in Florida, AEW claims that TrillerTV and its parent company, Triller Group Inc., owe the promotion nearly $5 million tied to AEW pay-per-view sales and the now-defunct AEW Plus subscription service.

The filing paints a damaging picture of Triller's financial situation and business practices. AEW's legal complaint alleges that Triller Group used revenue generated from AEW programming to fund unrelated business ventures instead of paying the wrestling company what it was contractually owed. In particularly pointed language, AEW's attorneys wrote that Triller Group's "strategy of robbing revenues generated by TrillerTV's distribution of AEW content to cover other of Defendants' operating expenses (much of which was spent on the social media platform endeavor) negatively impacted its relationship with and payments owed to AEW."

The lawsuit claims that AEW sent formal payment requests in both January and March 2025 before finally escalating the matter to legal action. Court documents show that AEW claims it is owed exactly $4,988,989.13, with the amount continuing to grow because interest is accrued at 2% per month under the agreement. According to the filing, Triller only made a partial payment on March 1, 2025, despite continuing to collect revenue from fans buying PPVs and subscriptions.

AEW's attorneys further accused Triller of collecting money from AEW viewers while failing to properly remit payments. The complaint states that "Defendants failed to make the full payment due on March 1, 2025, paying a fraction of the total amount owed — despite the remittance being a mere percentage of the total revenue Defendants collected and had in its coffers from AEW viewers before wrongfully spending it on other ventures and expenses."

The court documents reveal the specific revenue split arrangements that governed the AEW and TrillerTV partnership. Under the 2019 agreement, AEW was entitled to 75% of net domestic pay-per-view revenue and 65% of international PPV revenue. Revenue from AEW Plus subscriptions was split 60/40 in AEW's favor. TrillerTV, formerly known as FITE, served for years as one of the main streaming platforms for international AEW fans who could not watch shows through regular television. AEW Plus became especially popular overseas because it gave fans live weekly shows and PPVs through one subscription service.

The partnership recently fell apart when TrillerTV announced that AEW Plus would shut down completely. AEW Plus was officially discontinued in April 2026 after AEW launched its new MyAEW platform in March. The legal action comes shortly after TrillerTV informed subscribers that AEW and ROH pay-per-views would no longer be available for purchase through the service.

The situation is compounded by severe financial distress at Triller itself. Flipps Media Inc., the company that operates TrillerTV, previously filed its own lawsuit against Triller Group Inc. in Delaware Chancery Court on April 13. In that filing, Flipps claims that Triller Group has abandoned the company and left it without a functioning board of directors. According to court documents, "Flipps initiated this action because it is insolvent, cannot protect value for its creditors, and will soon cease being a going concern unless it its board causes it to promptly action. Flipps, however, has been without a board since October 2024 when its directors resigned and, as a result of Defendant Triller Group, Inc.'s abandonment, it cannot install replacement directors without intervention by this Court. As of the filing of this Complaint, Flipps has been without directors for nearly eighteen (18) months."

The Flipps Media lawsuit also claims that Triller Group is insolvent and does not currently have a working board of directors, making it difficult to properly move forward with bankruptcy protection. CEO Kostadin Jordanov and President/COO Eric Winter currently operate the streaming service.

In response to the allegations, Triller Group CEO Wing-Fai Ng released a brief statement. "We take these reports seriously and are reviewing the legal matter thoroughly. At this time, Triller Group Inc. has no comment," the statement read.

The legal conflict is also being pursued on multiple fronts. AEW is pursuing several claims in the lawsuit, including breach of contract, unjust enrichment, and tortious interference. The timing of AEW's legal action comes just weeks after the Flipps Media suit against Triller Group, suggesting a broader unraveling of Triller's business operations and financial obligations.

For many years, TrillerTV served as a crucial international streaming home for AEW fans. Now, with lawsuits flying and financial issues surfacing publicly, the collapse of the partnership appears tied to much larger corporate problems within the Triller Group organization. The situation represents a significant setback for international AEW viewers who relied on the AEW Plus service for access to wrestling programming.

🔥 Thekla re-signs with AEW

Thekla is committing to AEW for the long haul after re-signing with the promotion on a multi-year agreement that came with a substantial raise. The deal was finalized well ahead of when the wrestler would have become a free agent, preventing any potential bidding war between major wrestling companies.

Thekla's original stint with AEW was planned to be temporary when she debuted on May 28, attacking Jamie Hayter on Dynamite. Her first match with the promotion came a few weeks later on Collision, where she defeated Lady Frost. Since then, she has steadily risen through the AEW ranks and currently holds the AEW Women's World Championship. She is scheduled to defend her title at Double or Nothing in Queens, New York, on May 24 in a four-way match against Hikaru Shida, Kris Statlander, and Jamie Hayter.

When asked what made re-signing the right move, Thekla explained that Tony Khan's direct approach played a crucial role. "What made this the right move? I think AEW is a perfect fit for me. So when boss man asked me, 'Hey, would you mind staying with us for a little longer than we originally said?' I was like, 'Yeah, if the circumstances are right and allow it for me to feel lucrative enough, I'm definitely thinking that could be a very good idea.'" She emphasized that once the financial terms aligned with her expectations, the decision became straightforward.

The backstage environment and locker room atmosphere were equally important factors in Thekla's decision to stay. "I love T.K. I love working with him and I love his energy backstage and all the girls are nice. The locker room is quiet and peaceful and I love my job. It's good, you know? I don't think the grass is greener anywhere else because I'm sitting in a f***ing flower garden, you know?" Her comments made it clear she never seriously considered exploring other options once AEW approached her about extending her contract.

Behind the scenes, AEW moved strategically to lock in Thekla's future before she could test the open market. Industry reports indicate that the wrestler's representatives negotiated the deal professionally and secured favorable compensation as part of the new agreement. There was an expectation that if Thekla had reached free agency, there would have been significant outside interest. Specifically, WWE was believed to be a company that likely would have pursued her aggressively in a bidding situation, but AEW finalized the extension early enough to prevent any competitive offers from developing.

Thekla's commitment to AEW reflects her genuine satisfaction with the company's direction and culture. Since arriving in AEW following her departure from STARDOM in 2025, she has become one of the company's top women's stars, capturing the AEW Women's World Championship earlier this year after defeating Kris Statlander in a strap match. With both the company and the wrestler viewing this partnership as a major part of AEW's future, the long-term deal signals confidence in Thekla's continued elevation within the promotion.

In related comments, Thekla also addressed the possibility of other international talent joining AEW. When asked about Kairi Sane potentially landing with AEW following her WWE release, Thekla praised the wrestler's abilities and presentation. "I think she's an outstanding talent, and I could totally see her in AEW. I think that would be cool. I'm always happy to see new people who are good, who have a reputation, who have a look. You know, she knows how to do an entrance." While acknowledging the challenges of adding too much talent to a crowded roster, Thekla believed Sane would elevate the product: "So, of course, you always got to be careful—protect yourself. It's like, you know, the locker room's small. You can't have too many people in there; otherwise, it gets cramped. But Kairi's sick. She'd totally elevate the product if they gave her a chance. The receipt—the receipt is still waiting over here to get paid." The comments suggest Thekla has unfinished business with Sane and would welcome the opportunity to settle it in AEW.

🔥 Young Bucks and New Day potential WWE/AEW crossover match discussions

For years, one of professional wrestling's most coveted dream matches remained unrealized despite genuine efforts from both promotions. According to Matt Jackson of The Young Bucks, serious behind-the-scenes negotiations took place in 2018 to make a crossover clash between The Elite and The New Day happen. The story emerged when Jackson and his brother Nick revisited old episodes of their Being The Elite YouTube channel, reflecting on their long-standing friendly rivalry with the WWE faction.

Jackson explained the full scope of the discussions during the video. "We truly were trying to get a New Day, Young Bucks, Kenny match to happen, an Elite vs. New Day match. Actively," he stated. "They said they were trying on their side, we were talking to our bosses, even to the point in 2018 we got on the phone to Triple H and we talked and said 'we would really like to do this match'."

The proposed six-man tag team match would have featured Matt Jackson, Nick Jackson, and Kenny Omega facing off against Kofi Kingston, Big E, and Xavier Woods. The two groups had developed significant chemistry and a friendly rivalry through various public appearances, including gaming tournaments and online content that generated considerable fan enthusiasm for a potential in-ring encounter.

Jackson provided important context about Triple H's apparent motivations during the 2018 phone conversation. "I don't know if he was necessarily interested in just doing that match. I think he was more interested in pursuing us to work for him, but we talked to him about it. We were serious about doing the match and we thought we'd probably be able to do it, and unfortunately we never did," Jackson revealed. This suggests that while WWE was willing to discuss the match, the primary focus may have been on recruitment rather than enabling a cross-promotional appearance.

The timing of these discussions is particularly significant. In 2018, The Young Bucks and Kenny Omega were featured in an online rivalry with The New Day, even meeting face-to-face at E3 that same year for a Street Fighter V gaming tournament on the UpUpDownDown YouTube channel. The Elite would launch All Elite Wrestling just one month later in January 2019, with the Bucks and Omega serving as executive vice presidents of the company.

Jackson's comments have reignited discussion about this major missed opportunity in professional wrestling. For years, fans speculated about whether promotions might open the forbidden door for such a match, and both groups had teased the possibility through online interactions and social media references. Earlier in 2024, Kofi Kingston acknowledged that facing The Young Bucks remained one of his personal dream matches, and he pointed to WWE's past willingness to allow crossover appearances involving talent from other promotions as precedent.

Recent developments have added new intrigue to the decades-old discussion. Both Kofi Kingston and Xavier Woods recently departed WWE and are currently subject to a 90-day non-compete clause before becoming available to sign elsewhere. The departures have sparked immediate speculation in the wrestling community about possible AEW appearances for the two men. Multiple AEW roster members have publicly expressed interest in bringing Kingston and Woods to the company, with suggestions that they could arrive later in 2024 or early 2025.

While the originally pitched six-man tag team match between The Elite and The New Day may never happen due to Big E still being employed by WWE and his in-ring career being essentially concluded, the possibility of at least a tag team match between The Young Bucks and The New Day has become realistic for the first time. Speculation has emerged about potential dates and venues, with some wrestling observers suggesting that AEW All In at Wembley Stadium could be an ideal setting for such a dream match.

Jackson's revelations underscore how close professional wrestling came to breaking down barriers between major promotions nearly a decade ago. With the landscape continuing to evolve and crossover conversations becoming increasingly common across the industry, the dream of seeing The Young Bucks face The New Day may finally become reality, even if the original six-person format involving Kenny Omega and Big E remains forever in the realm of wrestling's greatest what-if scenarios.

Rather than a standard match conclusion, Asuka broke character and hugged Sky, raising her hand before waving to the fans. This moment immediately sparked retirement rumors, which were fueled further when several WWE wrestlers posted tribute messages on social media and engaged in cryptic online activity. Reports began circulating that Asuka could be preparing to retire or take on a significantly reduced role.

WWE addressed the uncertainty directly on Raw. During a backstage segment, Asuka had a heartfelt conversation with Sky in which she explicitly passed the torch. "I was looking for someone to take over my position. Finally, you've become that person. I'm so glad it was you. I'm proud of you," Asuka told Sky. Sky responded emotionally, saying "Even if we fight, we'll always be family... Goodbye, Asuka-san." Asuka replied with a hug, saying "See you later," before leaving the arena with her suitcase and travel bag. The segment was framed as a farewell, with the camera following Asuka as she walked out of the building.

The presentation created an impression that this could be Asuka's final WWE appearance. However, WWE itself added another layer of uncertainty during the broadcast. Michael Cole addressed the situation on commentary, stating that Asuka's future is "up in the air" and acknowledged that she "appeared to say goodbye tonight, but who knows." This admission reflected genuine uncertainty within WWE about Asuka's status, with reports indicating that even people in creative were unsure whether the Backlash moment was meant to be a final farewell.

PWInsider reported that Asuka is "believed to remain aligned with the company" and that backstage speculation centers on her potentially returning to Japan for an extended period to handle a family matter that would not be publicly disclosed. The report emphasized that this information had not been independently confirmed, but Asuka was present backstage in Knoxville ahead of Raw and available if WWE creative chose to use her.

GCW And Kevin Gill Reach Legal Settlement

Game Changer Wrestling and Kevin Gill have reached a legal settlement, according to a status report filed with the court on behalf of Gill's legal team. The statement indicated that counsel for both parties have executed a final, binding settlement agreement that resolves all claims in the matter. Both parties respectfully requested that if no further letter or request is filed on or before July 13, 2026, the matter be dismissed with prejudice.

The dispute originated when Kevin Gill, a prominent announcer in the wrestling industry, filed a lawsuit against Game Changer Wrestling in November 2024. The lawsuit alleged that Gill was owed money in relation to the GCW Patreon, a subscription-based platform that provides exclusive content to fans.

According to the lawsuit filing, Gill and Brett Lauderdale, acting on behalf of GCW, had agreed to launch a Patreon during the pandemic. Under this agreement, Gill was to receive 20% of the revenue generated by the Patreon. The platform launched in May 2020 during the height of pandemic-related restrictions on live events.

Gill's claims centered on two primary grievances. First, he alleged that he was underpaid during the early months of the Patreon's operation. Second, and more significantly, he claimed that he stopped receiving payments entirely in September 2020, less than four months after the platform launched. This sudden cessation of payments prompted Gill to pursue legal action to recover the funds he believed he was owed.

The settlement brings closure to a dispute that has been ongoing for several months. While the case is technically still open pending the formal dismissal request scheduled for July 13, 2026, the executed settlement agreement represents a resolution of all claims between the two parties. The specifics of the settlement terms have not been disclosed publicly.
